16CHAPTER 97
17LIMITED LINES SELF-STORAGE INSURANCE
18§7501.  Definitions
19 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 
20 have the following meanings.
211.  Customer.  "Customer" means a person who rents or leases a storage space within 
22 a self-storage facility under a rental agreement with a self-storage provider.  "Customer" 
23 includes the sublessee, assignee or successor in interest of the person originally a party to 
24 a rental agreement with a self-storage provider.
252.  Covered customer.  "Covered customer" means a customer who elects to receive 
26 coverage under a self-storage insurance policy.
273.  Limited lines license.  "Limited lines license" means a license to sell or offer a 
28 policy for self-storage insurance.
294.  Location.  "Location" means any physical location in the State or any publicly 
30 accessible website, call center or similar operation directed to residents of the State.
315.  Rental agreement.  "Rental agreement" means a written agreement between a 
32 customer and self-storage provider that establishes or modifies the terms, conditions or 
33 other provisions governing a customer's occupancy and use of a storage space within a self-
34 storage facility owned or operated by the self-storage provider.
356.  Self-storage facility.  "Self-storage facility" means any real property or facility in 
36 which individual storage spaces rented or leased by a self-storage provider to a customer 
37 are located and within which a customer is generally responsible for placing and removing 
38 property the customer stores within a rented or leased storage space.
Page 2 - 130LR0477(01)17.  Self-storage insurance.  "Self-storage insurance" means personal property 
2 insurance authorized under section 705 providing coverage for the repair or replacement of 
3 personal property of a covered customer stored at a self-storage facility or in transit to or 
4 from a self-storage facility against various causes of loss, including loss or damage.  "Self-
5 storage insurance" does not include a homeowner's or renter's insurance, private passenger 
6 automobile insurance, commercial multiple peril insurance or any similar policy.
78.  Self-storage provider.  "Self-storage provider" means a person or business entity, 
8 as defined in section 1151-A, subsection 4, that is the owner, operator, lessor or sublessor 
9 of a self-storage facility.
109.  Supervising entity.  "Supervising entity" means a business entity that is a licensed 
11 insurance producer or insurer.
12§7502.  Licensure of self-storage providers
131.  License required.  A self-storage provider must obtain a limited lines license under 
14 this chapter prior to selling or offering coverage under a policy of self-storage insurance.
152.  Authority provided by license.  A limited lines license issued under this chapter 
16 authorizes any employee or authorized representative of a self-storage provider to sell or 
17 offer coverage under a policy of self-storage insurance to a customer at each location at 
18 which the self-storage provider engages with a customer or prospective customer.
193.  List of locations.  In connection with a self-storage provider's application for a 
20 license under section 7506 and upon request by the superintendent, the self-storage 
21 provider shall provide a list to the superintendent of all locations in this State at which the 
22 self-storage provider offers coverage.
234.  Activities authorized by license.  Notwithstanding any provision of law to the 
24 contrary, a license issued pursuant to this chapter authorizes the licensee and its employees 
25 or authorized representatives to engage only in those activities that are expressly permitted 
26 in this chapter.
27§7503.  Requirements for the sale of self-storage insurance
281.  Brochures.  At each location where a self-storage provider sells or offers self-
29 storage insurance to customers, the self-storage provider shall make available to a 
30 prospective customer brochures or other written materials that:
31 A.  Disclose that self-storage insurance may provide a duplication of coverage already 
32 provided by a customer's homeowner's insurance policy, renter's insurance policy or 
33 other source of coverage;
34 B.  State that the enrollment by the customer in a self-storage insurance policy is not 
35 required in order to rent or lease storage space within a self-storage facility;
36 C.  Summarize the material terms of the insurance coverage, including:
37 (1)  The identity of the insurer;
38 (2)  The identity of the supervising entity;
39 (3)  The amount of any applicable deductible and how it is to be paid; and
40 (4)  Benefits of the coverage;
41 D.  Summarize the process for filing a claim; and
Page 3 - 130LR0477(01)1 E.  State that the customer may cancel enrollment for coverage under a self-storage 
2 insurance policy at any time and the person paying the premium must receive a refund 
3 of any applicable unearned premium.
42.  Periodic basis of coverage.  Self-storage insurance may be offered on a month-to-
5 month or other periodic basis as a group or master commercial inland marine policy issued 
6 to a self-storage provider under which individual customers may elect to enroll for 
7 coverage.
83.  Eligibility and underwriting standards.  Eligibility and underwriting standards 
9 for customers that elect to enroll in self-storage insurance must be established by an insurer 
10 for each self-storage insurance program.
11§7504.  Authority of self-storage providers
121.  Requirements for employees and authorized representatives of self-storage 
13providers.  An employee or authorized representative of a self-storage provider may sell 
14 or offer self-storage insurance to a customer and is not subject to licensure as an insurance 
15 producer under this chapter if:
16 A.  The self-storage provider obtains a limited lines license to authorize its employees 
17 or authorized representatives to sell or offer self-storage insurance pursuant to this 
18 section;
19 B.  The insurer issuing the self-storage insurance either directly supervises or appoints 
20 a supervising entity to supervise the administration of the sale of insurance, including 
21 development of a training program for employees and authorized representatives of the 
22 self-storage providers.
23 (1)  The training must be delivered to all employees and authorized representatives 
24 of the self-storage provider who are directly engaged in the activity of selling or 
25 offering self-storage insurance.  The training may be provided in electronic form.  
26 If the training is conducted in electronic form, the supervising entity shall 
27 implement a supplemental education program that is conducted and overseen by 
28 licensed employees of the supervising entity to supplement the electronic training.
29 (2)  Each employee and authorized representative must receive basic instruction 
30 about the self-storage insurance offered to customers and the disclosures required 
31 under section 7503, subsection 1; and
32 C.  The employee or authorized representative of the self-storage provider does not 
33 advertise, represent or otherwise hold that employee or authorized representative out 
34 as other than a nonlimited lines licensed insurance producer.
352.  Charges.  The charges for self-storage insurance coverage may be billed and 
36 collected by the self-storage provider.  Any charge to the customer for coverage that is not 
37 included in the cost associated with the rental or lease of self-storage or related services 
38 must be separately itemized on the customer's bill.  If the self-storage insurance coverage 
39 is included with the rental or lease of self-storage or related services, the self-storage 
40 provider shall clearly and conspicuously disclose to the customer that the self-storage 
41 insurance coverage is included with the rented or leased storage space.  A self-storage 
42 provider billing and collecting charges for coverage is not required to maintain those funds 
43 in a segregated account as long as the self-storage provider is authorized by the insurer to 
44 hold such funds in an alternative manner and remits the funds to the supervising entity 
Page 4 - 130LR0477(01)45 within 60 days of receipt.  All funds received by a self-storage provider from a customer 
46 for the sale of self-storage insurance are considered funds held in trust by the self-storage 
47 provider in a fiduciary capacity for the benefit of the insurer.  A self-storage provider may 
48 receive compensation for billing and collection services.
5§7505.  Violations
61.  Penalties.  If a self-storage provider or its employee or authorized representative 
7 violates any provision of this chapter, the superintendent may enforce this chapter in 
8 accordance with section 12-A except the superintendent may not impose a fine exceeding 
9 $15,000 for aggregate conduct in violation of this chapter.
102.  Suspension or revocation.  In addition to any other penalties authorized by law, 
11 the superintendent may:
12 A.  Suspend the authority of a self-storage provider to transact self-storage insurance;
13 B.  Suspend the authority of a self-storage provider to transact self-storage insurance 
14 pursuant to this chapter at specific business locations where violations have occurred; 
15 and
16 C.  Suspend or revoke the authority of an individual employee or authorized 
17 representative of a self-storage provider to act under a limited lines license under 
18 section 7502, subsection 2.

201.  Application for license to be filed with superintendent.  A  self-storage provider 
21 must file a sworn application for a license under this chapter with the superintendent on 
22 forms prescribed and furnished by the superintendent.
232.  Contents of application.  In addition to other information required by the

25 A.  Provide the name, residence address and other information required by the 
26 superintendent for an employee or authorized representative of the self-storage

5SUMMARY
6 This bill creates self-storage insurance as a limited line insurance that provides 
7 coverage against loss or damage to the personal property of a self-storage customer that 
8 occurs while the property is inside a self-storage space or in transit to or from a self-storage 
9 facility.  The bill permits certain self-storage providers and their employees to sell or offer 
10 self-storage insurance to customers who rent or lease self-storage space.  The bill describes 
11 the application process for a self-storage insurance limited lines license and requires that 
12 self-storage providers obtain a limited lines license or receive training from a licensed 
13 insurer before selling or offering self-storage insurance to a customer.  The bill requires 
14 that certain disclosures be made to customers when selling or offering self-storage 
15 insurance, describes the duration and fees associated with a limited lines license and 
16 authorizes the Superintendent of Insurance to assess penalties for violations of the 
17 provisions related to self-storage insurance.6
