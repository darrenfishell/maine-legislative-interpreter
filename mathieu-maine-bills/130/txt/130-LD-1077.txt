24Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to provide outreach and 
25education related to COVID-19 vaccines to rural populations and historically 
26vulnerable populations.  Resolved:  That, within existing resources, including any 
27 federal funds, the Department of Health and Human Services shall implement a program 
28 providing outreach and education to rural populations and to historically vulnerable 
29 populations that includes information about where and how to receive a COVID-19 
30 vaccine, vaccine safety and disease prevention.  Outreach under this program must be 
31 tailored to rural populations and to historically vulnerable populations and must use 
32 methods that are likely to reach those populations, including but not limited to direct phone 
33 calls.
34Sec. 2.  Department of Health and Human Services to increase MaineCare 
35reimbursement rates for COVID-19 vaccine administration.  Resolved:  That 
36 the Department of Health and Human Services shall amend rule Chapter 101: MaineCare 
37 Benefits Manual, Chapter I, Section 5, COVID-19 Public Health Emergency Services to 
38 provide that the activities associated with the administration of COVID-19 vaccines are 
39 reimbursed to the maximum extent allowable under federal law.  The Department of Health 
40 and Human Services shall submit a request for a waiver or state plan amendment to the 
41 United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for Medicare and 
42 Medicaid Services as necessary to implement this section.  Rules adopted pursuant to this

3Sec. 3.  Department of Health and Human Services to report to the Joint 
4Standing Committee on Health and Human Services on COVID-19 
5vaccinations.  Resolved:  That beginning 7 days after the effective date of this legislation 
6 and every 6 weeks thereafter until the end of the Governor's declaration of a state of 
7 emergency due to COVID-19, the Department of Health and Human Services shall report 
8 to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services on the progress the State 
9 is making in vaccinating state residents with COVID-19 vaccines.  This report must 
10 include, but may not be limited to, data on the vaccination of state residents generally and 
11 of historically vulnerable populations and the activities around and success of public 
12 education and outreach efforts.
13Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
14 takes effect when approved.
15SUMMARY
16 This resolve requires the Department of Health and Human Services to:
17 1. Implement an outreach and education program, using existing resources, including 
18 federal funding sources, that is tailored to reach rural populations and historically 
19 vulnerable populations with information about where and how to receive a COVID-19 
20 vaccine, vaccine safety and disease prevention;
21 2.  Amend its rules in Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter I, Section 5, 
22 COVID-19 Public Health Emergency Services to provide that the activities associated with 
23 the administration of COVID-19 vaccines under the MaineCare program are reimbursed to 
24 the maximum extent allowable under federal law; and
25 3.  Report to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services on the 
26 progress the State is making in vaccinating state residents with COVID-19 vaccines 
27 beginning 7 days after the effective date of this legislation and every 6 weeks thereafter 
28 until the end of the Governor's declaration of a state of emergency due to COVID-19.1
