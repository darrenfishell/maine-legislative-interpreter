Page 1 - 130LR2677(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, outdoor stadiums are currently authorized to serve alcohol, but only wine 
4 and malt liquor; and
5Whereas, authorizing outdoor stadiums to also serve spirits will benefit the State's 
6 tourism industry; and
7Whereas, the Legislature recognizes the importance of ensuring that outdoor 
8 stadiums are authorized to serve spirits in time for this year's tourism season; and
9Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
10 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
11 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
12 therefore,
13Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
14Sec. 1.  28-A MRSA §2, sub-§15, ¶M, as amended by PL 2011, c. 629, §3, is 
15 further amended to read:
16 M.  "Outdoor stadium" means any commercially operated outdoor facility with 3,000 
17 or more fixed seats designed or used for the playing of any sport or event that is open 
18 to the general public, charges a fee and has adequate facilities for the sale and 
19 consumption of wine and , malt liquor  and spirits.
20Sec. 2.  28-A MRSA §1074, sub-§1, as amended by PL 1997, c. 373, §101, is 
21 further amended to read:
221.  Issuance of licenses.  The bureau may issue licenses under this section for the sale 
23 of wine and , malt liquor  and spirits to be consumed on the premises to outdoor stadiums, 
24 as defined in section 2, subsection 15, paragraph M.  A concessionaire or lessee may be 
25 issued a license under this section, regardless of whether it controls the premises, as long 
26 as that concessionaire or lessee complies with the notice provisions applicable to qualified 
27 catering services in section 1076, subsection 7 prior to exercising the license.
28Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
29 takes effect when approved.
30SUMMARY
31 This bill amends the liquor licensing laws governing outdoor stadiums to allow outdoor 
32 stadiums to serve spirits, in addition to wine and malt liquor as provided in current law.31
