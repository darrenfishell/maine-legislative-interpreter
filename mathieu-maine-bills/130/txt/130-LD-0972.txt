10ยง1700-B.  Rare Disease Advisory Council
111.  Advisory board established.  The Rare Disease Advisory Council, as established 
12 in Title 5, section 12004-I, subsection 48-A and referred to in this section as "the council," 
13 advises the commissioner and provides information to the public on issues regarding rare 
14 diseases.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, "rare disease" 
15 means a condition or illness that affects fewer than 200,000 persons in the United States.
162.  Membership.  The council consists of 14 members appointed by the commissioner 
17 as follows: 
18 A.  Three physicians who practice in the area of cardiology, emergency care, 
19 neurology, oncology, orthopedics, pediatrics or primary care and provide care to 
20 patients with rare diseases;
21 B.  Two registered nurses who provide care to patients with rare diseases;
22 C.  Two administrators of hospitals that provide care to patients with rare diseases, or 
23 their designees;
24 D.  One representative of the department who provides education concerning rare 
25 diseases or the management of chronic conditions;
26 E.  One representative of the department who is responsible for epidemiology services;
27 F.  Two persons over 18 years of age who have suffered from or currently suffer from 
28 a rare disease;
29 G.  Two parents or guardians who each have a child with a rare disease; and
30 H.  One representative of an organization dedicated to providing services to patients 
31 suffering from rare diseases.
32 The council may, by affirmative vote of a majority of its members, request that the 
33 commissioner appoint to the council additional temporary members who have expertise on 
34 issues studied by the council. Such members serve for a period determined by the council.
353.  Terms; quorum; meetings.  Members serve for a term of 3 years and elect a chair 
36 from among the members.  At the end of a term an outgoing member serves until a 
37 successor has been appointed.  A member may serve multiple terms.  The board shall meet 4

65.  Duties of council.  The council shall:
7 A.  Perform a statistical and qualitative examination of the incidence, causes and 
8 economic burden of rare diseases in the State;
9 B.  Receive and consider reports and input concerning rare diseases from the 
10 department and persons whose work relates to rare diseases, including community-
11 based organizations, providers of health care and local and national organizations;
12 C.  Increase awareness of the burden caused by rare diseases in the State;
13 D.  Identify evidence-based strategies to prevent and control rare diseases;
14 E.  Determine the effect of delayed or inappropriate treatment on the quality of life for 
15 patients suffering from rare diseases and on the economy of this State;
16 F.  Study the effect of early treatment for rare diseases on the quality of life for patients 
17 suffering from rare diseases, the provision of services to such patients and 
18 reimbursement for such services;
19 G.  Increase awareness among providers of health care of the symptoms of and care for 
20 patients with rare diseases;
21 H.  Evaluate the systems for delivery of treatment for rare diseases in place in the State 
22 and develop recommendations to increase the survival rates and quality of life of 
23 patients with rare diseases;
24 I.  Determine effective methods of collecting data concerning cases of rare diseases in 
25 the State to conduct epidemiological studies of rare diseases in the State;
26 J.  Establish a comprehensive plan for the management of rare diseases in the State, 
27 which must include recommendations for the department, public and private 
28 organizations and businesses and for potential sources of funding, and update the 
29 comprehensive plan as necessary;
30 K.  Develop a registry of rare diseases diagnosed in the State to determine the genetic 
31 and environmental factors that contribute to such rare diseases; and
32 L.  Compile an annual report, which must include, without limitation, a summary of 
33 the activities of the council and any recommendations of the council for legislation or 
34 other policies.  The council shall: 
35 (1)  Post the report on a publicly accessible website maintained by the department; 
36 and
37 (2)  Submit the report to the Governor and the commissioner and to the Executive 
38 Director of the Legislative Council for transmittal to the Legislature.1

2 This bill creates the Rare Disease Advisory Council to advise the Department of Health 
3 and Human Services and the public on issues regarding rare diseases.  The bill defines a 
4 rare disease as a disease that affects fewer than 200,000 persons in the United States.  The 
5 council is made up of 14 members, who are health care professionals and others involved 
6 with or affected by rare diseases.  The council is directed to study a variety of issues 
7 regarding rare diseases and their treatment within the State.  The council must submit an 
8 annual report to the Governor, the Commissioner of Health and Human Services and the 
9 Legislature and must post the report on a publicly accessible website.2
