2Sec. 1.  22 MRSA c. 255-A is enacted to read:
3CHAPTER 255-A
4AMYOTROPHIC LATERAL SCLEROSIS
5§1411.  Registry established
6 The Maine Center for Disease Control and Prevention shall establish, maintain and 
7 operate a statewide amyotrophic lateral sclerosis incidence registry, referred to in this 
8 chapter as "the registry."
9§1412.  Duty of health care providers
10 A physician, surgeon, nurse practitioner, physician assistant or other health care 
11 practitioner and a hospital or other health care facility that screens for, diagnoses or 
12 provides therapeutic services to patients with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis shall report to 
13 the department all persons diagnosed as having amyotrophic lateral sclerosis no later than 
14 6 months from the date of diagnosis.  The report must include, but is not limited to, 
15 information on each person's usual occupation and industry of employment and other 
16 elements determined by rule to be appropriate.
17§1413.  Confidentiality
18 Information that directly or indirectly identifies individual persons contained within 
19 the registry is confidential and protected by applicable law, including section 42, 
20 subsections 2 and 5 and chapters 401 and 857.  Information within the registry may be 
21 disclosed only in aggregated, de-identified form and in a manner consistent with Maine 
22 Center for Disease Control and Prevention confidentiality policies.  The department may 
23 disclose the minimum information necessary to accomplish a specified research purpose 
24 only upon successful completion of the research disclosure approval process established 
25 by the department and as permitted under applicable human subject research protections, 
26 state and federal laws and the department's confidentiality policies and processes.
27§1414.  Annual report
28 The department shall prepare and submit to the Governor annual reports containing 
29 statewide prevalence and incidence estimates of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, including 
30 any trends occurring over time across the State.  The reports may not contain any 
31 information that directly or indirectly identifies individual persons.
32§1415.  Rules
33 The department shall adopt rules to implement this chapter, including, but not limited 
34 to, rules governing the operation of the registry, reporting to the registry and data release 
35 protocols.  Rules adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical rules as defined in 
36 Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
DRAFT DRAFT DRAFT        Page 2 - 130LR2633(01)        DRAFT DRAFT DRAFT1SUMMARY
2 This bill requires health care practitioners and health care facilities to report diagnoses 
3 of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis to the Department of Health and Human Services in order 
4 for the Maine Center for Disease Control and Prevention to create and maintain a statewide 
5 registry for surveillance of the disease in the State.  The bill provides confidentiality 
6 protections regarding information that directly or indirectly identifies individual persons.  
7 The bill requires the department to complete annual reports containing statewide 
8 prevalence and incidence estimates of amyotrophic lateral sclerosis, including any trends 
9 occurring over time across the State.  The bill also requires the department to adopt routine 
10 technical rules to implement the registry.2
