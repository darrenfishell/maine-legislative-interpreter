10 Amend the amendment by inserting after the title the following: 
11 'Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:

13 Energy Future' ' 
14 Amend the amendment by inserting after section 11 the following: 
15 'Sec. 12. Competitive procurement inquiry. By October 1, 2022, the Public 
16 Utilities Commission shall initiate an inquiry to assess the use of competitive procurement 
17 methods for purchases by transmission and distribution utilities, as defined in the Maine 
18 Revised Statutes, Title 35-A, section 102, subsection 20-B, of specific goods of a quantity 
19 that in the aggregate could reasonably impose significant costs to ratepayers. The inquiry 
20 must consider the activities for which competitive procurement methods may apply, 
21 exceptions to competitive procurement methods, the cost-effectiveness of requiring the use 
22 of competitive procurement methods and any other considerations the commission finds 
23 are necessary to assess the use of competitive procurement methods. Upon the conclusion 
24 of the inquiry, the commission may adopt routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
25 chapter 375, subchapter 2-A regarding competitive procurement methods for transmission 
26 and distribution utilities. On or before February 27, 2023, the commission shall submit a 
27 report regarding the inquiry to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
28 jurisdiction over energy and utilities matters. The committee may report out a bill to the 
29 131 st Legislature in 2023.' 
30 Amend the amendment by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or 
31 section number to read consecutively.
