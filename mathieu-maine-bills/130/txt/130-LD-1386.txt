3Whereas, this resolve requires the Department of Health and Human Services to

6 period expires; and
7Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
8 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
9 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
10 therefore, be it
11Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to develop a plan for 
12specialized bariatric care beds.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and 
13 Human Services, referred to in this resolve as "the department," shall develop a plan to 
14 provide by September 1, 2022, up to 16 bariatric care beds in the State to serve individuals 
15 who are eligible for long-term care and need bariatric care.  In developing the plan, the 
16 department shall do the following.
17 1.  No later than August 31, 2021, the department shall issue a request for information 
18 to determine potential providers of nursing facility bariatric care beds.  Potential providers 
19 must include behavioral health technology partners to provide appropriate behavioral 
20 health services to residents occupying bariatric care beds.
21 2.  No later than October 31, 2021, the department shall review the responses to the 
22 request for information to inform the proposed amendment to the department's rule Chapter 
23 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter III, Section 67, Principles of Reimbursement 
24 for Nursing Facilities with respect to bariatric care beds.
25 3.  No later than December 31, 2021, the department shall adopt rules with rate changes 
26 that build upon the resource utilization group system designed to capture the clinical 
27 intensity needs of residents occupying bariatric care beds.  The rules must include 
28 appropriate weight or body mass index requirements, an enhanced rate of reimbursement 
29 to nursing facilities for residents occupying bariatric care beds and up to 30 bed hold days 
30 for bariatric care residents experiencing hospitalization.  The rules must also include direct 
31 billing to the department by providers of durable medical equipment, transportation and 
32 behavioral health services.  Rules adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical 
33 rules as defined in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
34 4.  No later than March 30, 2022, the department shall issue a request for proposals to 
35 award contracts to provide bariatric care beds.
36 5.  No later than June 30, 2022, the department shall award contracts under the request 
37 for proposals.  Providers who are awarded contracts must offer bariatric care beds to 
38 appropriate residents no later than September 1, 2022 and receive enhanced reimbursement 
39 pursuant to rules adopted pursuant to subsections 2 and 3.
40 The department shall submit a report on progress, including any necessary legislation 
41 to establish the bariatric care beds and any required funding, to the Joint Standing 
42 Committee on Health and Human Services no later than December 1, 2021.
