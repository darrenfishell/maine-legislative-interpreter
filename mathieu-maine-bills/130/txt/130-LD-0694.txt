232.  Coverage for losses or business interruption.  Notwithstanding any law, rule or 
24 policy provision to the contrary, an insurer authorized to do business in this State that 
25 provides a policy insuring against loss or damage to property, including any loss of 
26 occupancy or interruption to the operations of a business, may not:
27 A.  Contain a provision in the policy excluding coverage for any loss of occupancy or 
28 interruption to the operations of a business directly or indirectly resulting from any 
29 pandemic declared by the World Health Organization, or its successor organization, or 
30 declared by the United States Government;
31 B.  Exclude coverage for any loss of occupancy or interruption to the operations of a 
32 business directly or indirectly resulting from the COVID-19 pandemic; or
33 C.  Deny any claim for coverage for any loss of occupancy or interruption to the 
34 operations of a business directly or indirectly resulting from the COVID-19 pandemic 
35 on the basis that:
36 (1)  COVID-19 is a viral disease subject to a policy exclusion for losses resulting 
37 from a virus; or
38 (2)  The insured has not suffered any physical damage or loss of property.

3SUMMARY
4 This bill prohibits insurers that provide a policy insuring against loss or damage to 
5 property from excluding coverage for any loss of occupancy or business interruption 
6 directly or indirectly resulting from the COVID-19 pandemic.  The bill also prohibits 
7 insurers from including a provision in a policy excluding coverage for any loss of 
8 occupancy or business interruption directly or indirectly resulting from a pandemic 
9 declared by the World Health Organization or the United States Government.
10 The bill also makes the requirements of this bill apply retroactively to March 15, 2020, 
11 the date a civil state of emergency was first declared in the State due to the COVID-19 
12 pandemic.4
