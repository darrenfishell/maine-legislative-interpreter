32SUMMARY
33 This bill defines "upland game species."  It provides for the Commissioner of Inland 
34 Fisheries and Wildlife to set a daily bag limit and possession limit for upland game species 
35 and makes violating these limits a Class E crime for which a fine of not less than $200 plus 
36 $50 for each animal taken in violation may be adjudged.
37 It allows the department to enforce the law prohibiting the abuse of another person's 
38 property against persons who abuse another person's property but who may not be involved 
39 in an activity such as hunting, fishing or trapping, as in current law.  It allows a person 
40 found to have violated that law to be ordered to pay restitution to the landowner for 
41 damages.
Page 7 - 130LR0129(01)1 It provides for the revocation of a hunting license of a person convicted of night hunting 
2 and found to have been in the possession of a thermal imaging device at the time of the 
3 offense.
4 It provides for the revocation of any license, permit or registration issued by the 
5 department, not just hunting and fishing licenses, of a person who is convicted of 
6 destroying, tearing down, defacing or otherwise damaging a property posting sign.
7 It prohibits domestic violence offenders and certain nonviolent juvenile offenders from 
8 obtaining or possessing any license or permit issued by the department that authorizes a 
9 person to hunt with a firearm.
10 It allows a person who has lawfully killed or trapped and registered a bear to sell, 
11 without a hide dealer's license, the fat not attached to the meat of that animal.  It clarifies 
12 that a person may not take or possess reptiles or amphibians from the wild for export, sale 
13 or commercial purposes.
14 It specifies that a person may not keep bear, deer, moose or wild turkey without 
15 registering the animal for more than 18 hours after the animal was harvested.
16 It requires all the edible meat of a bear, deer or moose to be presented for registration 
17 along with evidence of the animal's sex.  It also allows those animals to be dismembered 
18 for ease of transportation and allows the hunter to leave parts that are not of use away from 
19 public view.
20 It requires a hide dealer to keep a true and complete record of those buying and selling 
21 bear fat.
22 It allows a special hide dealer's license holder to commercially sell or barter the heads 
23 and untanned hides of bear that are butchered in the license holder's place of business.
24 It makes a special hide dealer's license valid for a full year, commencing January 1st, 
25 rather than a partial year, commencing August 1st.
26 It amends the definition of "owner," for the purpose of registration of a snowmobile, 
27 watercraft or ATV, to include a person having equitable interest in the snowmobile, 
28 watercraft or ATV, which entitles the person to possession of the vehicle.
