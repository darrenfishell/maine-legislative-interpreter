14§711.  Sealing of records of nonviolent crimes
151.  Class E, D and C crimes.  A person convicted of a Class E, Class D or Class C 
16 crime may petition the court in which the conviction was recorded to seal the record of the 
17 conviction after a period of 5 years from the completion of the sentence.  The court shall 
18 order all records of the conviction sealed if:
19 A.  The defendant has not been convicted of a crime in this State or any other 
20 jurisdiction since the conviction subject to the petition and has no formal charging 
21 instrument for a crime pending in this State or any other jurisdiction; and
22 B.  The crime is not a crime:
23 (1)  Under Title 17-A, chapter 11, 12, 25 or 35;
24 (2)  That involved violence or domestic violence; or
25 (3)  That had as an element of the offense a victim who was 17 years of age or 
26 younger or a victim who was 65 years of age or older.
272.  State Bureau of Identification.  Following receipt of a court order to seal a 
28 conviction record under subsection 1, the Department of Public Safety, Bureau of State 
29 Police, State Bureau of Identification shall make the record a confidential criminal history 
30 record and make the necessary arrangements with the identification division of the Federal 
31 Bureau of Investigation to have all references to the sealed crime deleted from the Federal 
32 Bureau of Investigation's identification record and any state materials returned to the 
33 contributing agency.
34SUMMARY
35 This bill allows a person convicted of a Class E, Class D or Class C crime to petition 
36 the court where the person was convicted to seal all records of the crime 5 years after the 
37 completion of the person's sentence by making the record a confidential criminal history 
38 record.  Sealing a record is not available for persons who have subsequent convictions or 
39 pending criminal charges; for crimes involving bribery, corruption, violence or sex 35
