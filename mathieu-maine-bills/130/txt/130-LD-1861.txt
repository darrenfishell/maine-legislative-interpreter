302.  Program established.  The Safe Homes Program is established and administered 
31 by the department to support the safe storage of prescription drugs, firearms and dangerous 
32 weapons in homes by providing educational information and materials and grants in 
33 accordance with this section.
343.  Educational information and materials.  The department shall support designated 
35 groups that provide educational information and materials on the safe storage of

104.  Grant program.  The department shall develop a grant program to provide funding 
11 for the purpose of providing educational information and materials in accordance with 
12 subsection 3 and for purchasing firearm safety devices, including, but not limited to, safes,

14 A.  Grants may be provided under this subsection to designated groups and nonprofit 
15 and community organizations serving designated groups.  
16 B.  The department shall administer the grant program developed pursuant to this 
17 subsection through an agreement with a statewide association of sheriffs.  
18 C.  The department shall adopt rules to implement the grant program.  The rules must 
19 include procedures for assisting applicants with the application process, accepting 
20 applications, approving and disapproving applications and distributing funding, 
21 coupons or vouchers to successful applicants.  Rules adopted pursuant to this paragraph 
22 are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
235.  Safe Homes Program Fund.  The Safe Homes Program Fund is established as a 
24 nonlapsing fund to provide funding to achieve the purposes of this section.  The fund 
25 consists of any funds received from private and public sources.  The fund must be held 
26 separate and apart from all other money, funds and accounts.  Any balance remaining in 
27 the fund at the end of any fiscal year must be carried forward to the next fiscal year.
286.  List.  Within available resources, the Commissioner of Public Safety shall maintain 
29 a list of state, community and private resources and programs that are available to support 
30 the purposes of this section.
31SUMMARY
32 This bill establishes the Safe Homes Program administered by the Department of 
33 Public Safety to support the safe storage of prescription drugs, firearms and dangerous 
34 weapons in homes by providing educational information and materials and grants.  Under 
35 the bill, grants may be provided to public schools, public preschool programs, foster homes, 
36 veterans programs, hospitals or other facilities in which newborns are delivered and 
37 members of the public and to nonprofit and community organizations serving those groups 
38 for the purpose of providing educational information and materials and for purchasing 
39 firearm safety devices.  The bill also designates September as Safe Homes Awareness 
40 Month.1
