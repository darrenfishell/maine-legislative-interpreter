Page 1 - 130LR0731(01)1Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to increase MaineCare 
2rates for chiropractic services for manipulative treatments.  Resolved:  That, no 
3 later than January 1, 2022, the Department of Health and Human Services shall amend its 
4 rules in Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter III, Section 15 to increase 
5 reimbursement rates for chiropractic services for manipulative treatments under procedure 
6 codes 98940, 98941 and 98942 to no less than 70% of the federal Medicare reimbursement 
7 rate for these services as long as the rate is no lower than the rate reimbursed as of January 
8 1, 2021.
9Sec. 2.  Rate study.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding section 1, if the Department 
10 of Health and Human Services conducts a rate study of chiropractic services for 
11 manipulative treatments, the department may adopt new rates for manipulative treatments 
12 that are recommended by the rate study by amending its rules in Chapter 101: MaineCare 
13 Benefits Manual, Chapter III, Section 15.
14Sec. 3.  Routine technical rules.  Resolved:  That rules adopted pursuant to section 
15 1 or 2 are routine technical rules as defined in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 
16 375, subchapter 2-A.
17SUMMARY
18 This resolve requires the Department of Health and Human Services, by January 1, 
19 2022, to amend its rules in Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter III, Section 
20 15 to increase reimbursement rates for chiropractic services for manipulative treatments 
21 under procedure codes 98940, 98941 and 98942 to no less than 70% of the federal Medicare 
22 reimbursement rate for these services as long as the rate is no lower than the rate reimbursed 
23 as of January 1, 2021.  If the department conducts a rate study of chiropractic services for 
24 manipulative treatments, the department may adopt new rates.  The rules adopted are 
25 routine technical rules.
