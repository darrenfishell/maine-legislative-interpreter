28 SUMMARY 
29 This amendment amends Committee Amendment "B" in order to provide that a person 
30 is immune from arrest, prosecution and revocation and termination proceedings if the 
31 person is rendering aid at the location of the suspected drug-related overdose and removes 
32 the provision that provides that a person need only be present at that location in order to 
33 receive immunity. It defines "rendering aid" to mean performing any action that involves 
34 looking after a person who is experiencing a suspected drug-related overdose while the 
3 5 person performing the action is awaiting the arrival of a medical professional or law 
36 enforcement officer to provide assistance, including but not limited to giving first aid or 
37 administering or assisting in the administration of naloxone hydrochloride.

It retains the provision in the committee amendment that provides immunity from 
arrest, prosecution and revocation and termination proceedings if the person in good faith 
calls for assistance for another person experiencing a suspected drug-related overdose and 
is at the location when a medical professional or law enforcement officer arrives. 
The amendment also removes the provision in the committee amendment that allows a 
criminal defendant to move that the court prior to trial determine whether the defendant is 
immune from arrest, but retains the provision that a defendant may move that the court 
determine prior to trial whether the defendant is immune from prosecution or revocation or 
termination proceedings.
