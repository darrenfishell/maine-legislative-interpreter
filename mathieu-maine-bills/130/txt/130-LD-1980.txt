38Sec. 5.  17 MRSA §1837-C is enacted to read:
39§1837-C.  Small nonprofit raffle operator license
401.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
41 following terms have the following meanings.
Page 2 - 130LR2326(01)1 A.  "License holder" means the holder of a small nonprofit raffle operator license.
2 B.  "Nonprofit corporation" means a corporation, no part of the income or profit of 
3 which is distributable to its members, directors or officers, operating under Title 13-B.
42.  Operation of Internet raffle authorized.  Notwithstanding any provision of law 
5 to the contrary, a nonprofit corporation may operate a raffle, including an Internet raffle, if 
6 the nonprofit corporation is a license holder. 
73.  Eligibility.  In order to be eligible for a small nonprofit raffle operator license, a 
8 nonprofit corporation must demonstrate that it:
9 A.  Is a nonprofit corporation operating under Title 13-B; 
10 B.  Has a physical presence in the State; and
11 C.  Receives less than $75,000 per year in gross receipts from Internet raffle ticket 
12 sales.
134.  Application and license term.  A nonprofit corporation seeking a small nonprofit 
14 raffle operator license under this section must submit an application to the Gambling 
15 Control Unit on a form or in a format approved by the Gambling Control Unit.  A small 
16 nonprofit raffle operator license is valid for a period of 2 years from the date the license is 
17 issued. 
185.  Operation by license holder.  A license holder shall:
19 A.  Retain for a period of 3 years a physical copy of a receipt for the sale of each raffle 
20 ticket and each raffle ticket stub generated by the sale of a raffle ticket for each Internet 
21 raffle held by the license holder;
22 B.  Retain for a period of 3 years a physical copy of each raffle ticket stub generated 
23 by the sale of a ticket for a raffle that is not an Internet raffle;
24 C.  Maintain raffle ticket stubs retained under paragraph A or B in a manner organized 
25 by each individual raffle;
26 D.  Provide raffle ticket receipts and raffle ticket stubs retained under this subsection 
27 to the Gambling Control Unit on request; 
28 E.  If the license holder raffles a firearm, process the transfer of the firearm through a 
29 holder of a federal firearms license for a dealer in firearms other than destructive 
30 devices under 18 United States Code, Section 923; and
31 F.  Submit an annual report to the Gambling Control Unit that includes the number of 
32 raffles conducted during the year covered by the report and the amount of gross receipts 
33 for each of those raffles.
346.  Acceptance of credit cards.  A license holder that has dues-paying members may 
35 accept payments for the sale of raffle tickets by credit card.
367.  Violation.  A license holder that fails to comply with subsection 5, paragraph D 
37 commits a civil violation punishable by a fine of not less than $500 and not more than 
38 $5,000.
