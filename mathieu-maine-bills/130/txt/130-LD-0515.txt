18development for education and employment. The Science, Technology, Engineering

20 referred to in this section as "the council," shall engage with the University of Maine

23 Authority of Maine under Title 10, section 964, the Loring Development Authority of

26 education and employment in the State:

31 based at the University of Southern Maine and the University of Maine at Farmington to 
32 develop and prepare educators for remote communication, learning and training platforms; 
33 3. A dual degree program in accounting and green technology at the University of 
34 Maine at Presque Isle;

36 technology that addresses the needs of new residents of the State, including but not limited

38 5. An associate degree program, supported by public-private funding, to train students

40 6. An apprenticeship program in green technology and employment, including but not 
41 limited to apprenticeships in solar farm development and operation;

19SUMMARY
20 This bill requires the Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics Council to 
21 coordinate the development of higher education programs and public-private partnerships 
22 to enhance higher education and employment opportunities in the State in science, 
23 technology, engineering and mathematics. It directs the council to engage with the 
24 University of Maine System, the Maine Community College System, adult education 
25 programs, the Finance Authority of Maine, the Loring Development Authority of Maine 
26 and private sector stakeholders to develop proposals for a series of programs and initiatives 
27 to promote new models of manufacturing education and employment in the State.20
