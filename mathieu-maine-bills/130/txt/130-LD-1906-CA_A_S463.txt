30 SUMMARY 
31 This amendment replaces the bill and makes the following changes to the State's liquor 
32 laws. 
33 1. It requires the State Liquor and Lottery Commission to adopt routine technical rules 
34 establishing procedures by which a spirits supplier may, after the commission establishes 
35 the retail price of that spirits supplier's own spirits product, request a reconsideration of that 
36 retail price determination at an adjudicatory hearing. 
37 2. Under current law, the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, 
38 Bureau of Alcoholic Beverages and Lottery Operations must enter into a contract with a 
39 trade association representing states that control and manage the sale of spirits for the 
40 monthly collection of data, by product code, on spirits sales made by each reselling agent 
41 to establishments licensed to sell spirits for on-premises consumption. The amendment 
42 requires the trade association to compile and make available aggregate data, by product

6 3. It streamlines the paperwork requirements by updating the procedures for purchase

12 in the State, describes the process by which the bureau develops recommended retail prices

14 employed by the bureau to establish such recommended retail prices operates and informs 
15 the committee of the status of the commission's establishment of a process for a spirits 
16 supplier to seek a reconsideration of a commission retail price determination. The
