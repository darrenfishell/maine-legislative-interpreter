Page 1 - 130LR1311(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, the spread of the novel coronavirus disease referred to as COVID-19 has 
4 created a public health emergency; and
5Whereas, the combined effects of the public health emergency and subsequent public 
6 health measures have negatively impacted the ability of nonprofit organizations to sell 
7 liquor for on-premises consumption as permitted by their licenses; and
8Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
9 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
10 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
11 therefore,
12Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
13Sec. 1.  Refund 50% of liquor license fees.  Notwithstanding any provision of the 
14 law to the contrary, a nonprofit charitable, educational, political, civic, recreational, 
15 fraternal, patriotic, religious or veterans' organization that was licensed under the Maine 
16 Revised Statutes, Title 28-A, chapters 41 and 43 to sell liquor for on-premises consumption 
17 on March 18, 2020 and that was not able to remain open in the year 2020 due to emergency 
18 public health restrictions may request, and is eligible to receive, a 50% refund of the initial 
19 licensure or license renewal fees it paid in the year 2020 for a license issued by the 
20 Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Bureau of Alcoholic Beverages and 
21 Lottery Operations under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 28-A.
22Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
23 takes effect when approved.
24SUMMARY
25 This bill allows nonprofit organizations that were licensed by the Department of 
26 Administrative and Financial Services, Bureau of Alcoholic Beverages and Lottery 
27 Operations to sell liquor for on-premises consumption on March 18, 2020 and not able to 
28 remain open in the year 2020 due to emergency public health restrictions to request a 50% 
29 refund of the initial licensure or license renewal fees they paid for a liquor license.25
