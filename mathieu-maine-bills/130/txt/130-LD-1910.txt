20Sec. 2.  24-A MRSA §4320-A, sub-§4 is enacted to read:
214.  Coverage of evidence-based mental health services for children.  A carrier 
22 offering a health plan in this State with an effective date on or after January 1, 2023 shall, 
23 at a minimum, provide coverage for mental health treatment services that use evidence-
24 based practices that are recommended by a provider for a child 18 years of age or younger. 
25 For the purposes of this subsection, "evidence-based practices" means clinically sound and 
26 scientifically based policies, practices and programs that reflect expert consensus on the 
27 prevention, treatment and recovery science published and disseminated by the United 
28 States Department of Health and Human Services, Substance Abuse and Mental Health 
29 Services Administration or its successor organization.
30Sec. 3.  Application. The requirements of this Act apply to all policies, contracts and 
31 certificates executed, delivered, issued for delivery, continued or renewed in this State on 
32 or after January 1, 2023.  For purposes of this Act, all contracts are deemed to be renewed 
33 no later than the next yearly anniversary of the contract date.
34Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
35 takes effect when approved.
36SUMMARY
37 This bill requires health insurance carriers to provide coverage for mental health 
38 treatment services that use evidence-based practices that are recommended by a provider 
39 for a child 18 years of age or younger. The bill defines "evidence-based practices" as 
40 clinically sound and scientifically based policies, practices and programs that reflect expert 37
