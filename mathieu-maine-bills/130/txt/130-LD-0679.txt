9§55.  Search warrants; issuance by justice, judge or justice of the peace
10 A justice of the Superior Court, a judge of the District Court or a justice of the peace 
11 shall issue search warrants for any place in the State for such purposes as the Constitution 
12 of the United States  Constitution and the Constitution of Maine permit, including with 
13 respect to any violation over which the Passamaquoddy Tribe, the Penobscot Nation or the 
14 Houlton Band of Maliseet Indians exercises exclusive jurisdiction under Title 30, section 
15 6209‑A, 6209-B or 6209-C.  The evidence presented to the magistrate in support of the 
16 search warrant may consist of affidavits and other evidence under oath or affirmation that 
17 is capable of being reduced to a record for purposes of review.  The application for the 
18 search warrant and supporting information and evidence must be submitted in accordance 
19 with rules adopted by the Supreme Judicial Court, except that, following the establishment 
20 of a statewide electronic warrant system as provided in Title 4, section 17, subsection 18, 
21 the filing of the application and supporting information and evidence must be done 
22 electronically and the warrant issued and returned electronically.  The Supreme Judicial 
23 Court shall by rule provide the procedure of the application for and issuance of search 
24 warrants.  When no procedure is specified by the Supreme Judicial Court, the justice, judge 
25 or justice of the peace shall proceed in any reasonable manner that is authorized by this 
26 section, that conforms to the requirements of Title 4, section 17, subsection 18 and that will 
27 allow the issuance of a search warrant for any constitutional purpose.  A justice, a judge or 
28 a justice of the peace shall issue a search warrant for a domestic or foreign entity that is a 
29 provider of electronic communication service or a provider of remote computing service in 
30 accordance with the provisions of this section and section 56.
31Sec. 5.  Rule amendments.  The Supreme Judicial Court shall adopt amendments

33 effective on the date of the establishment of the statewide electronic warrant system 
34 pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 4, section 17, subsection 18, to provide for 
35 the application, issuance and return of arrest warrants and search warrants through 
36 electronic means.
37SUMMARY
38 This bill directs the State Court Administrator to establish a secure system for the 
39 application, issuance and return of arrest warrants and search warrants that is electronic and 
40 that provides access to authorized users statewide.  The bill amends the law on search 
41 warrants to require electronic application and issuance once the electronic system has been 
42 established.  The bill directs the Supreme Judicial Court to adopt amendments to the Maine 
43 Rules of Unified Criminal Procedure, Rule 4, Arrest Warrant or Summons, effective on the 
44 date of the establishment of the statewide electronic warrant system, to provide for the 38
