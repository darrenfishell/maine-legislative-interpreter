8Sec. 2.  28-A MRSA §1382 is enacted to read:
9§1382.  Direct shipment of spirits by distilleries
101.  Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
11 following terms have the following meanings.
12 A. "Direct shipper" means a distillery licensed under subsection 2 that holds a current 
13 manufacturer license under section 1355-A or, for a distillery outside the State, a 
14 current manufacturer license from that state, territory or possession of the United 
15 States.
16 B. "Outside the State" means in any other state or territory or possession of the United 
17 States but does not include a foreign country.
182.  Direct shipment of spirits; licensure.  A direct shipper may ship spirits within the 
19 State subject to the licensure requirements of this section.  A distillery that holds a federal 
20 basic spirits manufacturing permit may obtain an annual spirits direct shipper license by 
21 filing with the bureau an application in a form determined by the bureau accompanied by 
22 an application fee of not more than $200 and a copy of the applicant's current federal basic 
23 spirits manufacturing permit.
243.  Direct shipper application.  Before sending a shipment to a resident of this State, 
25 a direct shipper must file an application for a spirits direct shipper license under subsection 
26 2 with the bureau on a form issued by the bureau along with a true copy of its current 
27 manufacturer license under section 1355-A or issued outside the State and a $100 
28 registration fee.
294.  Direct shipment of spirits; requirements.  A direct shipper may ship only spirits 
30 produced by the direct shipper in accordance with the direct shipper's federal permit. A 
31 direct shipper may ship spirits to a recipient only for personal use and not for resale.  A 
32 direct shipper shall label each package to be shipped in accordance with this section so that 
33 it conspicuously reads "CONTAINS ALCOHOL: SIGNATURE OF A PERSON 21 
34 YEARS OF AGE OR OLDER IS REQUIRED FOR DELIVERY."
355.  Common carrier.  Shipments made in accordance with this section must be made 
36 by a common carrier and must be accompanied by a shipping label that clearly indicates 
37 the name of the direct shipper and the name and address of the recipient. The common 
38 carrier shall obtain the signature of a person 21 years of age or older at the address listed 
39 on the shipping label prior to delivery of the shipment. The common carrier shall request 
40 photographic identification from the person signing for the shipment and verify that the 
41 person is 21 years of age or older.
Page 2 - 130LR1546(01)16.  Bottle size and case limit.  A direct shipper may not ship a container of spirits of 
2 less than 750 milliliters and may ship no more than 4 cases, each of which may contain no 
3 more than 9 liters or an equivalent volume, to any one recipient address in a calendar year.
47.  Prohibited shipping areas.  A direct shipper may not ship to any address identified 
5 by the bureau as within a prohibited shipping area or a municipality that has voted in any 
6 particular local option election against the sale of all types of liquor.
78.  License renewal.  A direct shipper may annually renew its spirits direct shipper 
8 license with the bureau by paying a $50 renewal fee and providing the bureau with a true 
9 copy of its manufacturer license under section 1355-A or issued outside the State.
109.  Sales tax registration and payment required.  As a condition of receiving a 
11 certificate of approval, a shipper located outside the State shall comply with the provisions 
12 of Title 36, Part 3, including all requirements relating to registration as a seller and the 
13 collection, reporting and remittance of the sales and use taxes of the State, and shall agree 
14 to be subject to the jurisdiction of the State for purposes of the enforcement of those 
15 obligations. The requirements of this subsection apply notwithstanding any provision of 
16 law of the State to the contrary.
1710.  Payment of excise taxes.  A direct shipper located outside the State shall annually 
18 pay to the bureau an excise tax of $11.96 per gallon of spirits sold to residents of the State 
19 in the preceding year. Spirits shipped by a direct shipper located outside the State are not 
20 subject to any premium or additional tax under Title 28-A or Title 36.
2111.  Report.  A direct shipper shall submit a report to the bureau annually in a manner 
22 and form prescribed by the bureau that includes the total number of cases of spirits shipped 
23 to recipients in the State and the name and residence address of shipment recipients in the 
24 State, the common carrier used to deliver the shipments and the date, quantity and purchase 
25 price of each shipment.
2612.  Audit.  The bureau may perform an audit of a direct shipper's records relevant to 
27 compliance with this section. A direct shipper shall provide copies of any records requested 
28 by the bureau within 10 business days of that request.
2913.  Violation.  A person, including a common carrier, who knowingly causes a direct 
30 shipment in violation of this section is subject to a fine up to $500 for a first offense and 
31 up to $1,000 for any subsequent violation of this section. A direct shipper or common 
32 carrier who knowingly delivers spirits to a person under 21 years of age is subject to a fine 
33 up to $5,000. The bureau may suspend or revoke a spirits direct shipper license for failure 
34 to comply with the shipping limits and reporting requirements required by this section. The 
35 bureau may accept payment of an offer in compromise in lieu of suspension; such payments 
36 must be determined by rules adopted by the bureau.
3714.  Jurisdiction.  A direct shipper, as a condition of licensure, is subject to the 
38 jurisdiction and enforcement authority of the State for the purposes of enforcement of this 
39 section.
4015.  Not subject to beverage container law.  Notwithstanding Title 38, chapter 33, a 
41 container of spirits shipped pursuant to this section does not require a refund value for 
42 beverage container control purposes.

36SUMMARY
37 This bill allows distilleries located inside and outside of the State to ship spirits 
38 manufactured by the distilleries directly to recipients for personal use upon licensure of the 
39 distilleries by the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Bureau of 
40 Alcoholic Beverages and Lottery Operations. Patterned on the provisions in the liquor laws 
41 allowing for direct shipment of wine, direct shipment of spirits requires use of a common 37
