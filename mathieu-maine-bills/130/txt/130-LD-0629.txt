Page 1 - 130LR1047(01)1Sec. 1.  Task force established.  Resolved:  That the Task Force To Study 
2 Improving Safety and Provide Protection from Violence for Health Care Workers in 
3 Hospitals and Mental Health Care Providers, referred to in this resolve as "the task force," 
4 is established.
5Sec. 2.  Task force membership.  Resolved:  That the task force consists of 9 
6 members appointed as follows:
7 1.  Two members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including 
8 members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
9 2.  Three members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
10 House, including members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
11 in the Legislature;
12 3.  Two members who are representatives of entities knowledgeable about or involved 
13 in providing hospital, medical or mental health services, appointed by the President of the 
14 Senate; and
15 4.  Two members who are representatives of entities knowledgeable about or involved 
16 in providing hospital, medical or mental health services, appointed by the Speaker of the 
17 House.
18Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
19 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the task force.
20Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of task force.  Resolved:  That all 
21 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
22 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
23 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
24 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the task force.  If 30 days or more after 
25 the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been made, the 
26 chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for the task 
27 force to meet and conduct its business.
28Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the task force shall review case studies related to 
29 violence or assault cases in health care and mental health care hospitals and facilities and 
30 current security practices and develop a plan to improve future security for health care 
31 workers and mental health care providers.
32Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
33 necessary staffing services to the task force, except that Legislative Council staff support 
34 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
35Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 1, 2021, the task force 
36 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
37 legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
38SUMMARY
39 This resolve establishes the Task Force To Study Improving Safety and Provide 
40 Protection from Violence for Health Care Workers in Hospitals and Mental Health Care 39
