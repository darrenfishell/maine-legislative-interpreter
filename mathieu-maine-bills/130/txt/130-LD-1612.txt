41.  Testimony of a child outside the presence of the defendant.  Upon motion by the 
5 State prior to trial and with reasonable notice to the defendant, a court shall allow a child 
6 who is 14 years of age or younger to testify outside the presence of the defendant pursuant 
7 to this section in a criminal proceeding concerning a crime under Title 17-A, chapter 11 or

92.  Requirements for direct testimony outside the presence of the defendant.  Direct 
10 testimony of a child outside the presence of the defendant under subsection 1 must meet 
11 the following requirements:
12 A.  The testimony must be conducted by way of 2-way closed-circuit television or other 
13 audiovisual electronic means;
14 B.  The testimony must occur at a recognized children's advocacy center with only a 
15 victim or witness advocate present in the room in which the child is testifying; and
16 C.  Live cross-examination of the child must be provided to the defendant's attorney 
17 after the child's direct testimony.
183.  Exception.  This section does not apply if the defendant is an attorney pro se or if 
19 the positive identification of the defendant is required.
