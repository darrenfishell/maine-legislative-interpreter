8SUMMARY
9 This bill reduces the exclusion amount, below which the Maine estate tax does not 
10 apply, to $1,000,000 from $5,600,000 for estates of decedents dying on or after January 1, 
11 2022 and removes the annual adjustment for inflation of that exclusion amount.  The bill 
12 creates an additional exclusion amount from the estate tax for family farms and aquaculture, 
13 fishing and wood harvesting businesses of up to $3,800,000.  This additional exclusion 
14 applies to farmland or depreciable machinery and equipment used in agriculture, 
15 aquaculture, fishing or wood harvesting that is inherited by a family member and remains 
16 in commercial use for 5 years following transfer.  The Treasurer of State must credit 50% 
17 of the revenue generated by the reduction in the exclusion amount beginning January 1, 
18 2022 to the General Fund and 50% the Maine State Housing Authority.  The Maine State 
19 Housing Authority must deposit the funds credited to it in the Housing Opportunities for 
20 Maine Fund.  The bill directs the Maine State Housing Authority in consultation with the 
21 Permanent Commission on the Status of Racial, Indigenous and Maine Tribal Populations 
22 to develop a racial equity assessment tool to use when evaluating project funding and report 
23 to the Joint Standing Committee on Labor and Housing on the development of this tool no 
24 later than October 1, 2022.9
