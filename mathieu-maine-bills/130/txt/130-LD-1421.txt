Page 1 - 130LR1685(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, it is essential for population growth strategies to be supported in concert 
4 with the State's 10-year economic development strategy produced by the Department of 
5 Economic and Community Development; and
6Whereas, applications for a grant that may support these population growth strategies 
7 given by the Northern Border Regional Commission for calendar year 2021 are due before 
8 the expiration of the 90-day period; and
9Whereas, without a grant from the Northern Border Regional Commission, certain 
10 population growth strategies may not be implemented to the detriment of citizens and 
11 regions in the State; and
12Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
13 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
14 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
15 therefore, be it
16Sec. 1.  Department of Economic and Community Development to update 
17its current priorities under the Northern Border Regional Commission's 
18allowable grant requests to include support of population growth strategies 
19around the State in concert with the State's 10-year economic development 
20strategy.  Resolved: That, on the effective date of this resolve, the Department of 
21 Economic and Community Development shall update its priorities under the Northern 
22 Border Regional Commission's allowable grant requests for the State to include support of 
23 population growth strategies around the State consistent with or recommended by the 
24 State's 10-year economic development strategy produced by the department pursuant to the 
25 Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, section 13056.
26Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
27 takes effect when approved.
28SUMMARY
29 This resolve directs the Department of Economic and Community Development to 
30 update its priorities under the Northern Border Regional Commission's allowable grant 
31 requests for the State to include support of population growth strategies around the State 
32 consistent with or recommended by the State's 10-year economic development strategy
