3ยง173.  Installation of smoke and carbon monoxide detectors by fire departments
41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5 following terms have the following meanings.
6 A.  "Device" means a battery-operated or plug-in smoke detector, carbon monoxide 
7 detector or combination smoke and carbon monoxide detector.
8 B.  "Fire department" means the State Fire Marshal, a municipal fire department as 
9 defined in Title 30-A, section 3151, subsection 1, a volunteer fire association as defined 
10 in Title 30-A, section 3151, subsection 3 or a fire district under Title 30-A, chapter 
11 164.
122.  Immunity.  Notwithstanding any provision of any public or private and special law 
13 to the contrary, a fire department or an employee or member of a fire department that 
14 delivers to or installs at residential premises a device or batteries for a device is not liable 
15 for damages for personal injury, wrongful death, property damage or other loss related to 
16 the device if:
17 A.  The device is installed by the fire department and the device is new and meets all 
18 applicable current safety and manufacturing standards;
19 B.  The device or the batteries in the device are installed by the fire department and the 
20 installation is performed in accordance with the manufacturer's instructions; and
21 C.  The installation or delivery is performed in the fire department's official capacity.
22 For purposes of this subsection, "installation" does not include the alteration or installation 
23 of electrical wiring.
243.  Records.  A fire department that installs or delivers a device or batteries for a device 
25 under this section shall keep a record of the installation or delivery for a period of 5 years 
26 after the installation or delivery.
274.  Application.  This section does not limit or otherwise affect an obligation or duty 
28 of an owner or occupier of residential premises receiving an installation or delivery of a 
29 device or batteries for a device under this section.
30SUMMARY
31 This bill provides immunity from liability to a fire department in the residential 
32 installation or delivery of a smoke or carbon monoxide detector or batteries for a smoke or 
33 carbon monoxide detector if the installation or delivery is performed in the fire department's 
34 official capacity.31
