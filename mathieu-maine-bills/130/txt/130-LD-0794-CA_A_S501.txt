12 SUMMARY 
13 This amendment replaces the bill, which is a concept draft, with a resolve. 
14 The amendment directs the Department of Health and Human Services to survey state-
15 contracted providers of therapeutic foster care about their liability insurance coverage, the 
16 costs of liability insurance and any difficulties in obtaining and maintaining liability 
17 insurance coverage due to availability and cost of that insurance coverage. The amendment 
18 also directs the department to survey other states about how therapeutic foster care is 
19 delivered in those states, the costs of liability insurance if services are delivered through 
20 contracts with providers and what difficulties have been identified in those states related to 
21 the availability and cost of liability insurance coverage for providers of therapeutic foster 
22 care. The department is directed to identify potential recommendations, based on the 
23 information from the surveys, to address costs of liability insurance for state-contracted 
24 providers of therapeutic foster care in this State. The department is required to submit the 
25 results of the surveys, along with potential recommendations, to the joint standing 
26 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters 
27 no later than February 15, 2023. The joint standing committee is authorized to submit 
28 legislation related to the report to the First Regular Session of the 131st Legislature. 
29 The amendment also adds an appropriations and allocations section to support the costs 
30 of the survey.
