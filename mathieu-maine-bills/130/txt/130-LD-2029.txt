3Sec. A-1.  Independent review of Maine National Guard policies and 
4procedures; report.  The Adjutant General shall request that the United States National 
5 Guard Bureau, Office of Complex Investigations thoroughly review the manner in which 
6 reports of sexual assault are investigated, prosecuted and adjudicated by the Maine Army 
7 National Guard and the Maine Air National Guard, referred to in this section as "the Maine 
8 National Guard."  If the Adjutant General is unable to secure the assistance of the Office 
9 of Complex Investigations, the Adjutant General shall contract with an independent agency 
10 experienced in evaluating military sexual violence prevention and response to conduct the 
11 review.  To the extent requested by the Office of Complex Investigations or the independent 
12 agency, the Adjutant General shall, to the greatest extent allowed under state and federal 
13 law and in a manner that protects the privacy of victims, provide the office or the agency 
14 access to all Maine National Guard policies, procedures and records related to sexual 
15 assault and sexual harassment, the results of climate command surveys and unit risk 
16 inventory surveys and any other materials requested by the office or the agency.  The 
17 Adjutant General shall direct all members of the Maine National Guard to cooperate fully 
18 with the review, except that no victim of sexual assault or sexual harassment may be 
19 required to participate.  The Adjutant General shall submit a copy of the report prepared by 
20 the Office of Complex Investigations or the independent agency, including the findings and 
21 recommendations for improving Maine National Guard policies, practices and procedures 
22 related to sexual assault and sexual harassment, to the joint standing committee of the 
23 Legislature having jurisdiction over veterans affairs by February 15, 2023.  The joint 
24 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over veterans affairs may report 
25 out legislation regarding the subject matter of the report to the 131st Legislature in 2023.

18SUMMARY
19 This bill is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs 
20 pursuant to Resolve 2021, chapter 21.
21 Part A of the bill requires the Adjutant General to provide for a thorough review of the 
22 manner in which reports of sexual assault are investigated, prosecuted and adjudicated by 
23 the Maine Army National Guard and the Maine Air National Guard, referred to in this 
24 summary as "the Maine National Guard," to be performed either by the United States 
25 National Guard Bureau, Office of Complex Investigations or by an independent agency 
26 experienced in evaluating military sexual violence prevention and response.  The Adjutant 
27 General must submit a copy of the report resulting from the review to the joint standing 
28 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over veterans affairs by February 15, 
29 2023.  The committee may report out legislation regarding the subject matter of the report 
30 to the 131st Legislature in 2023.
31 Part B of the bill:
32 1.  Amends the crime of harassment to provide that a person is guilty of harassment if 
33 the person engages in any course of conduct with the intent to harass, torment or threaten 
34 another person and during the previous year the person, while a member of the Maine 
35 National Guard, was notified, in writing or otherwise, not to engage in such conduct by a 
36 commanding officer, regardless of whether the person remains a member of the state 
37 military forces when the conduct occurs and regardless of where the conduct occurs; 
38 2. Adds a member of the Maine National Guard with experience in sexual assault 
39 response to the Maine Commission on Domestic and Sexual Abuse; 
40 3. Directs the Governor and the Adjutant General to convene the Advisory Council on 
41 Sexual Trauma in the Maine National Guard to review best practices for the prevention of 
42 and response to sexual assault and sexual harassment within the Maine National Guard, 
43 including best practices for assisting state and local law enforcement with the prosecution 19
20
Page 5 - 130LR2672(01)44 of offenders, and to compile a directory of all resources available to assist victims of sexual 
45 assault or sexual harassment who are current or former members of the Maine National 
46 Guard;
4 4. Requires the Adjutant General to provide current and former members of the Maine 
5 National Guard who were victims of sexual assault or sexual harassment while members 
6 of the Maine National Guard with financial assistance to cover the expenses of traveling to 
7 and from and participating in state criminal or civil court proceedings or proceedings under 
8 the Maine Code of Military Justice related to the sexual assault or sexual harassment;
9 5. Requires the Adjutant General to enter into a cooperative agreement with a statewide 
10 coalition against sexual assault for the provision of advocacy and support services to 
11 members of the Maine National Guard and their family members who are victims of sexual 
12 assault or sexual harassment, including a method for such individuals to make anonymous 
13 reports of sexual assault or sexual harassment to the Adjutant General; and
14 6. Requires the Adjutant General to submit an annual report to the joint standing 
15 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over veterans affairs containing specific 
16 data regarding reported incidents of sexual assault or sexual harassment within the Maine 
17 National Guard, to the extent permitted by federal law and in a manner that does not reveal 
18 the identity of victims; a description of all sexual assault and sexual harassment prevention 
19 training provided during the previous year; a description of the current practices and 
20 procedures for the prevention of sexual assault and sexual harassment and investigation of 
21 and disciplinary actions taken in response to reports of sexual assault and sexual harassment 
22 in the Maine National Guard; and a summary of the activities of the Advisory Council on 
23 Sexual Trauma in the Maine National Guard.1
