92.  Fund established.  The Municipal Gigabit Broadband Network Access Fund is 
10 established as a nonlapsing, revolving fund administered by the authority for the purposes

12 the fund must be continuously applied by the authority to carry out this section.  The

183.  Purpose of the fund.  The fund is established to address the need in the State for 
19 access to ultra high-speed broadband infrastructure that will enhance the State's 
20 competitiveness in national and international economies.  To the extent funds are available, 
21 the fund must be used to provide grants to communities, regional partnerships and 
22 municipalities to support public-private partnerships to support a municipal gigabit fiber-
23 optic broadband network in their regions with the following goals:
24 A.  Provide high-speed broadband access to attract, create and grow the State's 
25 economy and market the products and services of businesses in the State in national 
26 and international markets with ultra high-speed symmetric connectivity and address 
27 challenges in geography;
28 B.  Provide expanded health care services by facilitating access to telemedicine, as 
29 defined in Title 24‑A, section 4316, subsection 1, and state and local services for senior 
30 citizens and persons with disabilities;
31 C.  Expand educational opportunities for students across the State through virtual and 
32 distance learning;
33 D.  Facilitate broader access for the public to services provided by municipal and 
34 county governments, including, but not limited to, law enforcement entities, the 
35 judicial system and child, youth and family social services; and
36 E.  Provide expanded residential services to support employment opportunities.
37 In order to facilitate the achievement of the goals and policies of this section, the authority 
38 shall establish and regularly update, after opportunity for public comment and taking into 
39 consideration relevant federal policies, definitions of "gigabit fiber-optic broadband 
40 network" and "ultra high-speed broadband infrastructure."1

30SUMMARY
31 This bill amends the Municipal Gigabit Broadband Network Access Fund, which is a 
32 fund administered by the ConnectMaine Authority to address the need in Maine to access 
33 ultra high-speed broadband infrastructure through the provision of grants to communities, 
34 regional partnerships and municipalities to support a municipal gigabit fiber-optic 
35 broadband network.  This bill makes the following changes to the fund:
36 1.  It limits the grants to a municipality or group of municipalities;
37 2.  It provides definitions of "gigabit fiber-optic network," "symmetrical high-speed 
38 Internet" and "open-access network" and requires the authority, beginning in 2025, to 
39 establish minimum upload and download speeds for a symmetrical high-speed Internet at 
40 least equal to the minimum upload and download speeds available to a majority of urban 
41 census blocks in the United States;31
