COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "ff" to H.P. 1144, L.D. 1539, "An Act To Provide 
Access to Fertility Care" 
Amend the bill in section 1 in the first line (page 1, line 2 in L.D.) by striking out the 
following: "§4320-Q" and inserting the following: '§4320-R' 
Amend the bill in section 1 in §4320-Q in the first line (page 1, line 3 in L.D.) by 
striking out the following: "§4320-0" and inserting the following: '§4320-R' 
Amend the bill in section 1 in §4320-Q in subsection 1 in paragraph C in the first line 
(page 1, line 13 in L.D.) by inserting after the following: "infertility" the following: ', an 
individual or couple who is at increased risk of transmitting a serious inheritable genetic or 
chromosomal abnormality to a child' 
Amend the bill in section 1 in §4320-Q in subsection 1 in paragraph E in the 2nd line 
(page 1, line 26 in L.D.) by inserting after the following: "pregnancy" the following: 'that 
results in a live birth with healthy outcomes and that are provided in a manner' 
Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or section 
number to read consecutively. 
SUMMARY 
This amendment is the majority report of the committee. The amendment makes 
clarifying changes to the definitions of "fertility patient" and "fertility treatment" in the bill.

Requiring health insurance plans to provide coverage for fertility diagnostic care, fertility treatment and fertility 
preservation services will increase General Fund costs to the State Employee Health Plan beginning in the plan year 
that starts July 1, 2023 by $174,818 in fiscal year 2023-24. The bill will also increase Highway Fund costs by $61,542 
in fiscal year 2023-24. These estimates are based on the assumption that this required coverage will increase costs to 
the plan by $1 .41 per member per month (PMPM).
