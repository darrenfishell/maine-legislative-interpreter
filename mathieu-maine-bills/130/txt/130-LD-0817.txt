24Sec. 1.  Commission established.  Resolved:  That the COVID-19 Review 
25 Commission, referred to in this resolve as "the commission," is established.
26Sec. 2.  Commission membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
27 353, the commission consists of 12 members appointed as follows:
28 1. Four members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including 2 
29 members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature; 
30 and
31 2. Eight members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
32 House, including 4 members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
33 in the Legislature.
34Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
35 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
36 commission.
37Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
38 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
39 resolve. The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
40 Council once all appointments have been completed. After appointment of all members, 
41 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission. If 30 days or more 
42 after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
Page 2 - 130LR1219(01)43 made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for 
44 the commission to meet and conduct its business.
3Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall: study the State's response to 
4 coronavirus disease 2019, referred to in this resolve as COVID-19, and state laws, rules 
5 and policies governing that response in the State, including but not limited to executive 
6 orders related to the declared COVID-19 emergency; review contracts entered into related 
7 to executing the Governor's emergency proclamation and related executive orders; review 
8 the data related to COVID-19 collected by the Department of Health and Human Services, 
9 Maine Center for Disease Control and Prevention, including suicide attempts and deaths 
10 and drug-related hospitalizations and deaths; study the Maine Center for Disease Control 
11 and Prevention response, including testing and use of contact tracers; study the policies and 
12 rules connected to COVID-19 implemented by the Department of Health and Human 
13 Services, the Department of Education, the Department of Defense, Veterans and 
14 Emergency Management, Maine Emergency Management Agency and the Department of 
15 Economic and Community Development; and study any other matters related to the 
16 COVID-19 response as required by the commission.
17Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human 
18 Services shall provide necessary staffing services to the commission.
19Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 6, 2021, the commission 
20 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
21 legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services.
22Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
23 takes effect when approved.
24SUMMARY
25 This resolve establishes the COVID-19 Review Commission to study the State's 
26 response to COVID-19 and state laws, rules and policies governing that response in the 
27 State.  The commission must submit a report that includes its findings and 
28 recommendations, including suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on 
29 Health and Human Services no later than December 6, 2021.1
