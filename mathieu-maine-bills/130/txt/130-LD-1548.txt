11Sec. 1.  37-B MRSA §747 is enacted to read:
12§747.  Federal funds received due to the COVID-19 pandemic and appropriated to 
13the Department of Health and Human Services
14 Notwithstanding the provisions of section 744 and Title 5, section 1669, at least 10% 
15 of discretionary funds not otherwise earmarked prior to the effective date of this section 
16 that are received directly by the State from the Federal Government due to the pandemic 
17 related to coronavirus disease 2019, also known as COVID-19, and that are appropriated 
18 or otherwise directed to the Department of Health and Human Services, referred to in this 
19 section as "the department," must be used by the department to address the needs of 
20 historically marginalized racial communities in the State.  Prior to using or obligating the 
21 funds, the department shall consult with and accept recommendations from the Permanent 
22 Commission on the Status of Racial, Indigenous and Maine Tribal Populations, established 
23 by Title 5, section 12004‑I, subsection 74‑J, to ensure the funds are used to address the 
24 needs of historically marginalized racial communities in the State.  By December 15, 2021 
25 and every 6 months thereafter until all of the funds have been used or obligated by the 
26 department, the department shall submit a report to the joint standing committee of the 
27 Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters on the use of the 
28 funds.
29Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
30 takes effect when approved.
31SUMMARY
32 This bill requires that at least 10% of discretionary funds that are received directly by 
33 the State from the Federal Government due to the pandemic related to COVID-19 and that 
34 are appropriated or otherwise directed to the Department of Health and Human Services be 
35 used by the department to address the needs of historically marginalized racial communities 
36 in the State.  The bill requires the department to consult with and accept recommendations 
37 from the Permanent Commission on the Status of Racial, Indigenous and Maine Tribal 
38 Populations to ensure the funds are used to address the needs of historically marginalized 
39 racial communities in the State.  It also requires the department to submit a report to the 
40 joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human 
41 services matters on the use of the funds by December 15, 2021 and every 6 months 
42 thereafter.32
