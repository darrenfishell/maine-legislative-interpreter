8 of emergency department staff and provide appropriate treatment to a person admitted to 
9 an emergency department of a health care facility who exhibits violent behavior, including:
10 A.  Developing and communicating to health care facilities with emergency

12 B.  Requiring the unit within the department responsible for the person to accept the 
13 person once the person is cleared for discharge from the emergency department;
14 C.  Identifying and securing an alternate care site within 24 hours of a health care 
15 facility's request for placement and prior to the person's being cleared for discharge;
16 D.  Requiring a residential care facility to take the person back once the person is 
17 cleared for discharge and no longer needs medical care;
18 E.  Creating an enhanced rate paid to institutions for persons with mental illness or 
19 residential care facilities that are required to close a bed due to a staffing or safety 
20 concern when accepting the person;
21 F.  Covering the costs of security in an emergency department when the person 
22 becomes violent;
23 G.  Working to increase the number of secure residential treatment options in the State; 
24 and
25 H.  Adopting rules to carry out the purposes of this section.  Rules adopted pursuant to 
26 this paragraph are routine technical rules as defined by Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 
27 2-A.
28SUMMARY
29 This bill requires emergency departments of health care facilities to discharge a person 
30 exhibiting violent behavior once the person has been medically stabilized and directs the 
31 Department of Health and Human Services to establish measures to ensure the safety of 
32 emergency department staff and provide appropriate treatment to persons admitted to 
33 emergency departments who exhibit violent behavior.29
