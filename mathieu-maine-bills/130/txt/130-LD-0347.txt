14Sec. 4.  35-A MRSA §3157 is enacted to read:
15§3157.  Electric vehicle charging stations; incentive rates
16 The commission shall review and approve incentive rates for electric vehicle charging 
17 stations in accordance with this section.
181. Proposed incentive rate schedules.  The commission shall direct each transmission 
19 and distribution utility to develop and submit by November 1, 2021 a proposed incentive 
20 rate schedule to promote the installation and operation of electric vehicle charging stations.  
21 A proposed incentive rate schedule:
22 A.  Must be designed to align with and support relevant strategies of the State's climate 
23 action plan adopted and updated under Title 38, section 577 and to help achieve the 
24 State's greenhouse gas emissions reduction levels under Title 38, section 576-A; and
25 B.  Must include an evaluation of the relative direct and indirect costs and benefits 
26 associated with each proposed incentive rate and must account for varying scenarios 
27 of electric vehicle adoption and usage.
282. Review.  The commission, after notice and hearing, shall determine whether a 
29 proposed incentive rate schedule submitted pursuant to subsection 1 meets the requirements 
30 of subsection 1, paragraph A and, using the information provided under subsection 1, 
31 paragraph B and any other information available to the commission, shall evaluate the costs 
32 and benefits of the proposed schedule.  After completing that determination and evaluation, 
33 the commission shall approve, approve with modifications or reject the proposed schedule.  
34 If the commission rejects the proposed schedule, the commission may either order the 
35 utility to implement an incentive rate schedule established by the commission or direct the 
36 utility to submit a new proposed schedule that meets the requirements of this section.
373.  Revisions.  Not more than once every 3 years following the establishment of a 
38 transmission and distribution utility's incentive rate schedule pursuant to this section, the 
39 transmission and distribution utility may file with the commission an application to revise 
40 the schedule.  The application under this subsection must include a description of the 
41 changing costs or conditions necessitating the proposed revision.  The commission shall 
Page 2 - 130LR1178(01)42 review a revision submitted under this subsection in the same manner and using the same 
43 criteria as provided under subsection 2.
3SUMMARY
4 This bill requires each transmission and distribution utility, on or before November 1, 
5 2021, to submit to the Public Utilities Commission a proposed incentive rate schedule to 
6 promote the installation and operation of electric vehicle charging stations.  Such proposals 
7 must be designed to align with and support relevant strategies of the State's climate action 
8 plan adopted and updated under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 38, section 577 and to 
9 help achieve the State's greenhouse gas emissions reduction levels under Title 38, section 
10 576-A.  The commission, after notice and hearing, is required to approve, approve with 
11 modifications or reject each proposed incentive rate schedule.1
