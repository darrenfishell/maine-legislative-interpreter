Page 1 - 130LR0308(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, the Maine Integrated Youth Health Survey, a statewide survey administered 
4 by the Department of Education in collaboration with the Department of Health and Human 
5 Services, is conducted in February of odd-numbered years and quantifies the health-related 
6 behaviors and attitudes of 5th graders through 12 graders; and
7Whereas, the COVID-19 pandemic has drastically changed the school learning 
8 environment for students; and
9Whereas, while it may currently be more difficult to conduct the survey, it is vitally 
10 important that the survey continue and be administered more frequently in order to track 
11 youth mental health as students respond to changing conditions; and
12Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
13 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
14 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
15 therefore, be it
16Sec. 1.  Maine Integrated Youth Health Survey conducted.  Resolved:  That 
17 the Department of Health and Human Services and the Department of Education shall 
18 ensure that the survey referred to as the Maine Integrated Youth Health Survey is conducted 
19 during the 2020-2021 school year in addition to the planned survey in the 2021-2022 school 
20 year in order to track youth mental health during the public health emergency caused by 
21 coronavirus disease, or COVID-19.
22Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
23 takes effect when approved.
24SUMMARY
25 This resolve directs the Department of Health and Human Services and the Department 
26 of Education to ensure that the survey referred to as the Maine Integrated Youth Health 
27 Survey is conducted during the 2020-2021 school year in addition to the planned survey in 
28 the 2021-2022 school year in order to track youth mental health during the COVID-19 
29 public health emergency.
