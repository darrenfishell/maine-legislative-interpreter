25SUMMARY
26 This bill requires the Public Utilities Commission to include developments in the 
27 delivery of crisis response services in the commission’s annual report to the Legislature. 
28 The bill defines "crisis response services" as services offered to individuals experiencing 
29 mental health emergencies, emergencies relating to substance use disorder or other 
30 emergencies for which fire, emergency medical or police services are determined not to be 
31 required.
32 The bill directs the Public Utilities Commission, Emergency Services Communication 
33 Bureau, in consultation with the Department of Public Safety, to develop proposed 
34 legislation to implement the bureau's protocol and procedure recommendations for the 
35 delivery of crisis response services. The bureau must allow affected persons and entities to 
36 provide comments on the proposal. The proposed legislation and comments must be 
37 submitted to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
38 utilities and energy matters, and the committee may report out legislation in connection 
39 with the proposal to the 131st Legislature in 2023.26
