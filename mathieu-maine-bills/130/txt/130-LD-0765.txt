27Sec. 3.  22 MRSA §4038, sub-§8 is enacted to read:
288.  Placement in qualified residential treatment program; hearing within 60 days.  
29 The court shall conduct a hearing to review the status of a child placed in a qualified 
30 residential treatment program and determine the appropriateness of the placement within 
31 60 days after the child enters the program.
32 A.  At the hearing under this subsection, the court shall:
33 (1)  Review a needs assessment of the child conducted by a qualified individual;
34 (2)  Consider whether the needs of the child can be met through an alternative 
35 placement in a family foster home as defined in section 8101, subsection 3;
36 (3)  Consider whether the placement of the child in a qualified residential treatment 
37 program provides effective and appropriate care for the child in the least restrictive 
38 environment; and
Page 2 - 130LR0066(01)1 (4)  Consider whether placement of the child in a qualified residential treatment 
2 program is consistent with the short-term and long-term goals for the child as 
3 specified in the permanency plan of the child protection case pursuant to section 
4 4038-B.
5 B.  The court shall state, in writing, the reasons for its decision to approve or disapprove 
6 under this subsection the continued placement of the child in the qualified residential 
7 treatment program.
8 C.  In a hearing under this subsection, records of evaluations of the child and medical, 
9 behavioral and mental health records of the child are admissible upon showing that the 
10 records contain information relevant to the issues before the court, as long as the 
11 records are made available to counsel at least 10 days prior to the hearing.
12Sec. 4.  22 MRSA §4038, sub-§9 is enacted to read:
139.  Continued placement in qualified residential treatment program; judicial 
14review.  At each review conducted pursuant to this section regarding a child placed in a 
15 qualified residential treatment program, the court shall make judicial findings, by a 
16 preponderance of the evidence, regarding the child's continued placement.  The court shall 
17 conduct a hearing to review the status of a child placed in a qualified residential treatment 
18 program and determine the appropriateness of placement within 60 days after the child 
19 continues placement in the qualified residential treatment program.
20 A.  The court shall:
21 (1)  Determine whether an ongoing needs assessment of the child, as prepared by 
22 qualified individuals, supports continued placement of the child in the qualified 
23 residential treatment program;
24 (2)  Determine whether the documentation about the child regarding the child's 
25 placement in the qualified residential treatment program supports the conclusion 
26 that it is effective and appropriate care for the child in the least restrictive 
27 environment; and
28 (3)  Determine whether the documentation about the child supports the conclusion 
29 that continued placement in the qualified residential treatment program is 
30 consistent with the short-term and long-term goals for the child as specified in the 
31 permanency plan of the child protection case pursuant to section 4038-B.
32 B.  The court shall state, in writing, the reasons for its decision to approve or disapprove 
33 under this subsection the continued placement of the child in the qualified residential 
34 treatment program.
35 C.  In a hearing under this subsection regarding the child's continued placement in a 
36 qualified residential treatment program, records of evaluations of the child and 
37 medical, behavioral and mental health records of the child are admissible upon showing 
38 that the records contain information relevant to the issues before the court, as long as 
39 the records are made available to counsel at least 10 days prior to the hearing.
40SUMMARY
41 In order to claim federal reimbursement for the cost, with respect to a child in state 
42 custody, of placement in a residential care facility under the federal Family First Prevention 
Page 3 - 130LR0066(01)43 Services Act, this bill adds definitions to the Child and Family Services and Child 
44 Protection Act and creates a statutory requirement for a court hearing within 60 days of a 
45 child's placement in a qualified residential treatment program.  Additionally, this bill 
46 ensures that regular reviews of a child's continued placement in a qualified residential 
47 treatment program are conducted by the court.1
