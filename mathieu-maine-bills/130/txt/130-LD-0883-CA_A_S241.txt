§12803. Designation of state endangered and state threatened species 
1. Standards. The commissioner shall recommend to the Legislature a species to be 
listed as state endangered or state threatened under subsection 3 whenever the 
commissioner finds one of the following to exist: 
A. The present or threatened destruction, modification or curtailment of its habitat or 
range; 
B. Overutilization for commercial, sporting, scientific, educational or other purposes; 
C. Disease or predation; 
D. Inadequacy of existing regulatory mechanisms; or 
E. Other natural or manmade factors affecting its continued existence within the State. 
2. Commissioner's duties. In recommending to the Legislature a species to be listed 
as state endangered or state threatened under subsection 3, the commissioner shall: 
A. Make use of the best scientific, commercial and other data available;

endangered or state threatened, naming each species by both its scientific and common 
name, if any, and specifying over what portion of its range each species so designated 
is state endangered or state threatened.

SUMMARY 
This amendment replaces the bill and does the following: 
1. Requires the Commissioner of Marine Resources to designate a marine species as a 
state endangered or state threatened species if the species is listed as an endangered or 
threatened species under the United States Endangered Species Act of 1973, the species 
uses the waters of the State for any part of its life cycle and the species is not already so 
designated in state law or rule; 
2. Requires the Commissioner oflnland Fisheries and Wildlife to designate a species 
as a state endangered or state threatened species if the species is listed as an endangered or 
threatened species under the United States Endangered Species Act of 1973, the species 
uses the land or waters of the State for any part of its life cycle and the species is not already 
so designated in state law or rule; 
3. Removes provisions oflaw that provide that the Legislature is the sole authority to 
designate species as state endangered or state threatened; and 
4. Authorizes the Commissioner of Marine Resources and the Commissioner ofinland 
Fisheries and Wildlife to recommend additional species to the Legislature for listing as 
endangered or threatened species, as in current law.
