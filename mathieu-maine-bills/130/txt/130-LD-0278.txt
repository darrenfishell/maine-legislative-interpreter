7§4710-C.  Establishment or substantial expansion of mandated instruction or training
8 A joint standing committee of the Legislature that considers proposed legislation to 
9 establish mandated instruction or training for students in elementary or secondary school 
10 not required under this Title or rules adopted pursuant to this Title or to substantially 
11 expand mandated instruction or training for students in elementary or secondary school 
12 shall evaluate the proposed legislation in accordance with this section.
131.  Request to commissioner.  Upon the referral to a joint standing committee of the 
14 Legislature of legislation that proposes new or substantially expanded mandated instruction 
15 or training, the joint standing committee shall hold a public hearing and briefly and 
16 informally review the proposal.  Following this informal review, the committee shall:
17 A.  Request that the commissioner convene the review committee established in 
18 subsection 2 and report the review committee's findings and recommendations to the 
19 joint standing committee by a specific date established in consultation with the 
20 department; or
21 B.  Hold a work session to work and vote on the proposed legislation.
222.  Review Committee on Proposed Legislation.  The Review Committee on 
23 Proposed Legislation, referred to in this section as "the review committee," is established.  
24 The commissioner shall appoint members to the review committee including teachers, 
25 administrators and content specialists within the department and a representative of an 
26 association of statewide curriculum leaders to evaluate any proposals governed under 
27 subsection 1.
283.  Evaluation.  Upon a request by a joint standing committee of the Legislature under 
29 subsection 1, paragraph A, the review committee shall review the legislation that proposes 
30 to establish or substantially expand mandated instruction or training and shall identify 
31 whether the subject of the proposal is already addressed within existing courses of study 
32 aligned with the system of learning results as established in section 6209.  If the subject of 
33 the proposal is not already addressed within existing courses of study, the review committee 
34 shall analyze the positive and negative effects of establishing or substantially expanding 
35 the mandated instruction or training contained in the proposal and shall specifically 
36 consider issues of availability of resources, costs to implement the proposal, staff expertise, 
37 the number of required courses of study that exist in accordance with the system of learning 
38 results and the available hours of instruction.  The review committee shall invite the 
39 sponsor of the proposed legislation to participate in the evaluation.
404.  Findings and recommendations.  The commissioner shall submit and present a 
41 final report containing the review committee's findings and recommendations to the joint 
42 standing committee of the Legislature that requested the review pursuant to this section 4

6SUMMARY
7 This bill does the following.
8 1.  It establishes the Review Committee on Proposed Legislation as a board with 
9 minimal authority and provides that members are not entitled to compensation.
10 2.  It requires the joint standing committee of the Legislature that considers proposed 
11 legislation that would establish or substantially expand mandated instruction or training for 
12 students in elementary or secondary school to hold a public hearing on the proposal and 
13 then either vote to request that the Commissioner of Education convene the Review 
14 Committee on Proposed Legislation to review the proposal and report to the joint standing 
15 committee by a specific date chosen in consultation with the Department of Education or 
16 hold a work session to work and vote on the proposed legislation.
17 3.  It requires the Commissioner of Education to appoint the Review Committee on 
18 Proposed Legislation's members, including teachers, administrators and content specialists 
19 within the Department of Education and a representative of an association of statewide 
20 curriculum leaders, and requires the review committee to invite the sponsor of the proposed 
21 legislation to participate in its evaluation.
22 4.  It provides that, as part of the evaluation, the Review Committee on Proposed 
23 Legislation must analyze the positive and negative effects of the proposed legislation and 
24 the costs to implement the proposal.
25 5.  It requires the Commissioner of Education or the commissioner's designee to present 
26 the final report of the Review Committee on Proposed Legislation to the joint standing 
27 committee.1
