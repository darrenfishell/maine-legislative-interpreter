16 amended to read:
172.  Surcharge permitted for governmental entity.  Notwithstanding subsection 1, a 
18 governmental entity may impose a surcharge for payments made with a credit card or debit 
19 card for taxes, fines, charges, utility fees, regulatory fees, registration fees, license or permit 
20 fees or the provision of a specific service or good provided by that governmental entity if 
21 the surcharge:
22 A.  Is disclosed clearly to the consumer prior to payment; and
23 B.  Does not exceed the costs associated with providing the credit card or debit card 
24 service that are directly incurred by the governmental entity or assessed by an 
25 authorized 3rd-party payment service provider for a credit card or debit card 
26 transaction. If there is not a cost assessed by an authorized 3rd-party payment service 
27 provider for a debit card transaction, the governmental entity may not impose a 
28 surcharge associated with a debit card transaction.
29 A governmental entity shall disclose to the consumer that the surcharge may be avoided if 
30 the consumer makes payments by cash, check or other means not a credit card or debit card. 
31 A governmental entity is not subject to any liability to the issuer of a credit card or an 
32 authorized 3rd-party payment service provider for nonpayment of credit card charges by 
33 the consumer. As used in this subsection, "governmental entity" includes, but is not limited 
34 to, a state department or agency, a county established or governed by Title 30-A, Part 1, a 
35 municipality as defined in Title 30-A, section 2001, subsection 8, a quasi-municipal 
36 corporation as defined in Title 30-A, section 2604, subsection 3, the Judicial Department 
37 as described in Title 4, the University of Maine System, the Maine Community College 
38 System and the Maine Maritime Academy.
39SUMMARY
40 This bill amends the administrative procedures and services laws pertaining to state 
41 finances to explicitly allow a state department or agency to impose a surcharge for 
42 payments made by credit card or debit card for taxes, fines, charges, utility fees, regulatory 
Page 2 - 130LR0473(01)43 fees, registration fees, license or permit fees or the provision of a specific service or good 
44 provided by that state department or agency.  It also amends the Maine Consumer Credit 
45 Code – Truth-in-Lending to allow a governmental entity to impose a surcharge for 
46 payments made by credit card or debit card for registration fees and goods and includes 
47 under the definition of "governmental entity" a state department or agency.1
