4§6557.  Maine School Safety Center
5 The Maine School Safety Center, referred to in this section as "the center," is 
6 established within the department to ensure assist schools in their efforts to provide for the 
7 overall safety of schools in this State  their school community.  The primary role of the 
8 center is to provide training, guidance and technical support to schools in this State 
9 regarding safety and security  their efforts to safely mitigate against, prepare for, respond to 
10 and recover from all hazards and threats.
111.  Purposes.  The purposes of the center are to:
12 A.  Serve as a central location for school safety and security information, training and 
13 technical assistance related to successful implementation of school safety and security 
14 programs in schools;
15 B.  Be a resource for the prevention of youth violence;
16 C.  Promote overall school safety by developing recommendations to support a positive 
17 school climate and multiple-hazard mitigation and response plans;
18 D.  Develop and provide school emergency management, training, guidance and 
19 technical support to include information provided by persons with expertise in the 
20 relevant subject matter in the development of the center's school safety specialist 
21 education program;
22 E.  Promote, develop and implement technical support and training for a behavioral 
23 threat assessment program; and
24 F.  Facilitate and assist local schools and public safety stakeholders in preventing, 
25 preparing for and responding to threats and acts of violence, including self-harm, 
26 through a holistic, solution-based approach to improving school safety.
272.  Collaboration.  The center shall work collaboratively with all appropriate 
28 stakeholders in furtherance of its purposes as set out in subsection 1. 
293.  Staff and services.  The center’s staff consists of a director, assistant director and 
30 employees responsible for providing the following services to schools in the State:
31 A.  Training, including school safety specialist training and credentialing in a school 
32 emergency management education program;
33 B.  Mental health and behavioral threat assessment;
34 C.  Site assessment and school climate assessment;
35 D.  Planning and emergency operation plan review and exercises;
36 E.  Training, procedures and best practices for school resource officers and officers that 
37 may have interactions with youth;
38 F.  Legislation and policy recommendations and review;
39 G.  Administrative assistance;

3 I.  Contracted school safety services.
