Page 1 - 130LR1937(01)1Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to develop a 
2comprehensive statewide strategic plan to serve people in the State with 
3behavioral health needs throughout their lifespans.  Resolved: That, within 
4 existing resources, the Department of Health and Human Services shall develop a vision 
5 and comprehensive statewide strategic plan to serve people in the State with behavioral 
6 health needs throughout their lifespans, referred to in this resolve as "the plan."  For 
7 purposes of this resolve, "behavioral health" includes a wide range of mental disorders and 
8 illnesses, substance use disorder and developmental disabilities, including autism spectrum 
9 disorder.
10Sec. 2.  Plan participation.  Resolved: That the department's Office of Child and 
11 Family Services, office of behavioral health and office of aging and disability services shall 
12 draft the plan in collaboration with the Legislature, other agencies of the executive branch, 
13 indigenous and tribal populations of the State, the Permanent Commission on the Status of 
14 Racial, Indigenous and Maine Tribal Populations, consumer groups, service providers, 
15 public safety organizations, law enforcement organizations, hospitals and the LGBTQ 
16 community.  For purposes of this resolve, "LGBTQ" includes but is not limited to lesbian, 
17 gay, bisexual, transgender, queer, questioning, intersex and asexual.
18Sec. 3.  Elements of plan.  Resolved: That, in drafting the plan, the department 
19 shall address, as they relate to behavioral health:
20 1.  Protection of consumer choice;
21 2.  Evidence-based treatments;
22 3.  Early screenings, detection and interventions for all children up to 6 years of age;
23 4.  Identifying and serving children from 6 years of age to 20 years of age in school 
24 settings, including screenings and providing early interventions where appropriate;
25 5.  Public health screenings for families at risk of adverse childhood experiences;
26 6.  Children in state custody;
27 7.  Children who are incarcerated;
28 8.  Best practices for a successful transition from children's to adult services;
29 9.  Adults who are incarcerated;
30 10.  Workforce development at all levels;
31 11.  Availability of diagnosis and treatment in the least restrictive environment at all 
32 levels, including:
33 A.  Hotlines and so-called warmlines;
34 B.  Suicide prevention;
35 C.  Crisis services, including response to 9-1-1 calls, safety assessments and 
36 stabilization;
37 D.  Outpatient treatment;
38 E.  Medication management;
39 F.  Intensive outpatient treatment;

24Sec. 4.  Report.  Resolved: That, by December 7, 2022, the department shall submit 
25 the plan along with any recommendations and suggested legislation to the joint standing 
26 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters.  
27 The joint standing committee having jurisdiction over health and human services matters 
28 may report out legislation based upon the plan to the First Regular Session of the 131st 
29 Legislature.
30SUMMARY
31 This resolve directs the Department of Health and Human Services, including the 
32 department's Office of Child and Family Services, office of behavioral health and office of 
33 aging and disability services and a variety of other interested parties and stakeholders, to 
34 develop a vision and comprehensive statewide strategic plan to serve people in the State 
35 with behavioral health needs throughout their lifespans.31
