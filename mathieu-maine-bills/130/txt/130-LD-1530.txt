71.  Tiny homes.  Regulation of tiny homes is governed by this section.  This section 
8 does not prohibit municipalities from establishing controls on tiny homes that are less 
9 restrictive than are permitted by this section.
102.  Location of tiny homes.  A municipality shall allow a tiny home to be placed or 
11 erected as a primary structure in a number of locations on an undeveloped house lot where 
12 a single-family dwelling is allowed or as a primary or accessory structure on property that 
13 already contains a structure.  No more than one tiny home may be placed on a lot unless 
14 the combined square footage of multiple tiny homes does not exceed 400 square feet.  A 
15 municipality may reduce the setback requirements for a tiny home from those required for 
16 a single-family home.
173.  Municipal inspection.  A tiny home may be placed on a property for 180 days 
18 without an inspection under this subsection.  A tiny home may be used as a dwelling.  A 
19 tiny home may be but is not required to be connected to a utility, including an electric 
20 service, a water service or, if the tiny home is connected to water service, a wastewater 
21 sewer or septic system of an adjoining structure.  A municipality may inspect the following 
22 for a tiny home:
23 A.  That the tiny home meets the requirements of Title 29-A, section 101, subsection 
24 80-C;
25 B.  That the tiny home is placed on a stable surface, ground or footing;
26 C.  Any utility connection to the tiny home; and
27 D.  If a tiny home is connected to a water service, all rules and requirements relating 
28 to wastewater.
294.  Municipal design criteria.  A municipality may establish design criteria for tiny 
30 homes, including a pitched, shingled roof and exterior siding or other exterior features that 
31 are residential in appearance, unless:
32 A.  The criteria have the effect of circumventing the purposes of this section; and
33 B.  The criteria are used to prevent the relocation of a tiny home that is legally sited as 
34 of January 1, 2021.
355.  Property tax.  A municipality may assess for and collect the tax under Title 36, 
36 Part 2 for a tiny home that is:
37 A.  Affixed to the property, including by way of a utility connection;
38 B.  In a state of completeness or permanency; and
39 C.  Located on the property for more than 180 days.

4SUMMARY
5 This bill establishes standards for municipalities to follow regarding tiny homes, which 
6 are structures no larger than 400 square feet constructed on a frame or chassis and designed 
7 for use as permanent living quarters.  The bill allows municipalities to set rules for tiny 
8 homes that are less restrictive than state law, allows tiny homes on undeveloped and 
9 developed housing lots, allows municipal inspection of certain features of tiny homes and 
10 provides for tiny homes to be assessed for property tax purposes after 180 days in certain 
11 circumstances.5
