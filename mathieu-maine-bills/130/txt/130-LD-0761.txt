13Sec. 2.  20-A MRSA §12723 is enacted to read:
14§12723.  Maine Community College System Pell Grant Match Program
15 The Maine Community College System Pell Grant Match Program, referred to in this 
16 section as "the program," is established within the system to assist in funding community 
17 college for eligible students.
181.  Eligibility.  A person is eligible to receive a grant award under subsection 2 if the 
19 person:
20 A.  Is a resident of the State as determined by the system;
21 B.  Has completed an application for federal student financial aid programs and is 
22 eligible for a Federal Pell Grant for the academic year in which the person applies to 
23 the program; and
24 C.  Maintains a cumulative grade point average of 2.0 on a scale of 4.0 as determined 
25 by the college the person attends.
262.  Grant awards.  Beginning in fiscal year 2021-22, the system may award a student 
27 who is eligible under subsection 1 a grant award under this subsection. The grant award 
28 may not exceed 100% of the amount of funds provided by the Federal Pell Grant that the 
29 student receives for the academic year in which the student applies.  The availability of the 
30 grant award and the amount of the grant award are subject to the amount available in the 
31 fund established under subsection 3.
323.  Fund.  The Maine Community College System Pell Grant Match Fund is established 
33 as a nonlapsing fund administered by the system.  Grant awards may be distributed from 
34 this fund only to students who meet the eligibility requirements under subsection 1.
354.  Rulemaking.  The system may adopt rules to implement and administer the 
36 provisions of this section. Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical 
37 rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.

3SUMMARY
8 This bill establishes the Maine Community College System Pell Grant Match Program 
9 and provides that a student is eligible for the program if the student is a resident of the 
10 State, is eligible for a Federal Pell Grant and maintains a minimum grade point average. 
11 The grant award may not exceed 100% of the amount of funds provided by the Federal Pell 
12 Grant that the student receives for the academic year in which the student applies. The 
13 availability of the grant and the amount of the grant are subject to the amount available in 
14 the established fund, and the Maine Community College System may adopt rules to 
15 implement and administer the program and fund.3
