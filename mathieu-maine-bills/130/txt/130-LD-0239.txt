10SUMMARY
11 This bill creates within the Department of the Attorney General the Office of the 
12 Advocate for Real Estate Appraisal, the purpose of which is to assist members of the public 
13 in understanding their rights regarding real estate appraisals.  The office is administered by 
14 the Advocate for Real Estate Appraisal, who is appointed by the Attorney General and who 
15 must be a licensed Maine real estate appraiser.  The duties of the advocate include preparing 
16 and making available on a publicly accessible website interpretive and educational 
17 materials and programs concerning the real estate appraisal process and options available 
18 to consumers to dispute real estate appraisals and to submit complaints regarding licensed 
19 appraisers; providing individual guidance to members of the public to help them understand 
20 their rights in disputing real estate appraisals and submitting complaints regarding licensed 
21 appraisers; receiving and reviewing inquiries regarding real estate appraisals and 
22 determining whether the appraisals were completed in accordance with the Uniform 
23 Standards of Professional Appraisal Practice; and responding with nonbinding written 
24 findings and recommendations and information regarding the consumer's options.  The bill 
25 requires the advocate to submit a report not later than January 15th of each year to the joint 
26 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over consumer affairs.  The 
27 report must include the total number of inquiries received in the previous calendar year; a 
28 summary of outcomes of the inquiries received; and recommendations for future 
29 legislation, if any.
30 The bill also amends the licensing laws for real estate appraisers to require the Board 
31 of Real Estate Appraisers to establish by rule a process for receiving and reviewing 
32 complaints regarding licensees.  It requires the board to include on its publicly accessible 
33 website a description of consumer rights in the real estate appraisal process and a 
34 step-by-step process for the filing of complaints, with links to relevant forms.  It requires 
35 the board to include on its publicly accessible website searchable information regarding 
36 any disciplinary actions, including letters of guidance, taken or issued against licensed 
37 appraisers.  It requires the board to submit a report not later than January 15th of each year 
38 to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over matters related 
39 to licensing of real estate appraisers.  The report must include the total number of 
40 complaints received in the previous calendar year; a summary of outcomes of the 
41 complaints received; and recommendations for future legislation, if any.
