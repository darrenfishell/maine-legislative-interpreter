2Sec. 1.  36 MRSA c. 368 is enacted to read:
3CHAPTER 368
4EXTRACTION OF WATER FOR COMMERCIAL BOTTLING
5§2731.  Bottled water operator defined
6 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, "bottled water operator" 
7 means a person engaged in the business of bottling or packaging water for sale.
8§2732.  Excise tax
91.  Imposition of tax.  Beginning with the extraction of water occurring on or after 
10 November 1, 2021, an excise tax is imposed for the privilege of engaging in the business 
11 of extracting water on each bottled water operator that extracted more than 1,500,000 
12 gallons of groundwater or surface water from springs or other underground sources within 
13 the State in the previous calendar year for commercial bottling for sale.
142.  Rate of tax.  The excise tax under subsection 1 is imposed at a rate of 5¢ per gallon 
15 of water extracted in the State.
163.  Payment.  Each bottled water operator shall prepare and submit to the assessor by 
17 the 15th of each month a return according to procedures and schedules specified by the 
18 assessor reporting the total gallons of water extracted for bottling during the preceding 
19 month and other information required by the assessor and shall pay the excise tax under 
20 this section each month to the assessor at the time the report is submitted.
214.  Records.  A bottled water operator shall preserve for a period of at least 3 years a 
22 record of the amount of all water extracted in this State by that bottled water operator during 
23 that period.
24§2733.  Rules
25 The department may adopt rules to implement this chapter.  Rules adopted pursuant to 
26 this section are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
27Sec. 2.  Application. This Act applies to bottled water operators as defined in the 
28 Maine Revised Statutes, Title 36, section 2731 that extracted more than 1,500,000 gallons 
29 of water in the State in a calendar year beginning with 2020.
30SUMMARY
31 This bill creates an excise tax of 5¢ per gallon on the extraction of groundwater or 
32 surface water for commercial bottling for sale.31
