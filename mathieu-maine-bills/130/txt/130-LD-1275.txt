Page 1 - 130LR0784(01)1Sec. 1.  Violations of emergency orders not resulting in damage or harm to 
2be dismissed.  Resolved: That a violation by a business of an order, rule or guidance 
3 issued pursuant to the state of emergency proclaimed on March 15, 2020, by the Governor 
4 pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 37-B, chapter 13 in response to the ongoing 
5 COVID-19 pandemic, must be dismissed if the agency or department issuing the violation 
6 does not produce evidence of any resulting measurable damage or harm caused by the 
7 violation.  Any fine, suspension, license revocation or other penalty assessed due to a 
8 violation dismissed under this section must be dismissed or reversed and the record of the 
9 violation expunged from any administrative or agency record regarding the business.  Any 
10 fines or penalties paid for a violation dismissed or reversed pursuant to this section must 
11 be returned or refunded to the payor of that fine or penalty.
12Sec. 2.  Application.  Resolved:  That this resolve applies to a violation issued 
13 under the state of emergency proclaimed on March 15, 2020 by the Governor until the state 
14 of emergency is terminated.
15SUMMARY
16 This bill directs that any violation by a business of an order, rule or guidance issued 
17 pursuant to the state of emergency due to COVID-19 proclaimed by the Governor and any 
18 resulting fine, suspension or license revocation be dismissed if the agency or department 
19 that issued the violation produces no evidence of resulting measurable damage or harm 
20 caused by the violation, that any record of the violation be expunged from administrative 
21 and agency records of the business and that any fine or penalty paid be refunded or returned.16
