23SUMMARY
24 This bill provides that a career and technical education center or region may enter into 
25 a memorandum of understanding with a local community college to provide career and 
26 technical education to residents of the municipalities served by that region, center or 
27 satellite program as an alternative organizational method of delivering career and technical 
28 education. The memorandum of understanding must include provisions that the schedule 
29 must be aligned to allow students to fulfill the requirements of the Maine Learning Results, 
30 students must be awarded college credit, where applicable, students must receive automatic 
31 admission to the University of Maine System or Maine Community College System to 
32 complete a postsecondary program that was begun at the career and technical education 
33 center or region and funding must be provided through the Aspirations program.24
