22SUMMARY
23 This bill requires the Department of Health and Human Services to report annually on 
24 the current number and case specifics of children served by the department's Office of Child 
25 and Family Services, including where the department is in the reunification process of 
26 children with whom the Office of Child and Family Services is involved and who have 
27 parents or primary caregivers who are incarcerated in a jail or correctional facility or 
28 detention facility and the number of cases of abuse and neglect that were not opened for 
29 assessment.
30 The bill adds a person with knowledge related to the incarceration of women and a 
31 woman who has been incarcerated in the State and who has had prior involvement with the 
32 Office of Child and Family Services to the board of visitors of each jail.  The bill establishes 
33 a board of visitors for women's services to serve with regard to women who are incarcerated 
34 in Department of Corrections correctional and detention facilities and requires the 
35 Commissioner of Corrections to establish a separate grievance process for complaints 
36 regarding compliance with standards for county and municipal detention facilities, jails and 
37 other detention housing.  The bill prohibits a correctional or detention facility from billing 
38 an indigent client for future payment of services and medications.
39 The bill adds to the rights of a person who is incarcerated in the custody of the 
40 Department of Corrections opportunities and conditions for visitations with the child of the 
41 person that parallel those opportunities and conditions for visitation for families receiving 
42 family reunification services from the Department of Health and Human Services. The bill 
43 requires the Department of Corrections to report annually to the joint standing committee 23
