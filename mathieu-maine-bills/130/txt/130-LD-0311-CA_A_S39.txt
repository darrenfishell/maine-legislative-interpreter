15 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and insetting the following: 
16 'Sec. 1. Rules governing corrosion mitigation methods for bridges. 
17 Resolved: That the Department of Transportation shall adopt rules governing corrosion 
18 mitigation methods for bridges wholly under the control of the State. Rules adopted 
19 pursuant to this resolve must include, but are not limited to, the establishment of processes 
20 for ensuring that conosion mitigation activities are carried out in accordance with 
21 established federal c01rosion mitigation standards and under the supervision of personnel 
22 who are industry-trained and industry-certified in corrosion mitigation methods on an 
23 appropriate substrate or surface; requiring plans to prevent environmental degradation that 
24 might result from corrosion mitigation activities; and requiring compliance with all 
25 applicable state and federal regulations and industry standards established by the Society 
26 for Protective Coatings, the National Association of Corrosion Engineers, the United States 
27 Environmental Protection Agency and the United States Department of Labor, 
28 Occupational Safety and Health Administration. Rules adopted pursuant to this resolve are 
29 not required to apply to routine maintenance work performed by Maine Department of 
30 Transportation employees. 
31 Rules adopted pursuant to this resolve are routine technical rules as defined in the 
32 Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.'

SUMMARY 
This amendment replaces the bill with a resolve that requires the Department of 
Transportation to adopt routine technical rules governing corrosion mitigation methods for 
bridges wholly under the control of the State.
