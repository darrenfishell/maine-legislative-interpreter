5ยง1500-N. Notification of recall of orthopedic medical devices
61.  Definitions. As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
7 following terms have the following meanings.  
8 A.  "Firm" means the manufacturer or seller of an orthopedic medical device. 
9 B.  "Health care entity" means a hospital or ambulatory surgical facility as defined in 
10 Title 22, section 1812-E that is located in this State and that purchases an orthopedic 
11 medical device from a firm. 
12 C.  "Market withdrawal" has the same meaning as in 21 Code of Federal Regulations, 
13 Section 7.3 (2019).
14 D.  "Orthopedic medical device" means any device described in 21 Code of Federal 
15 Regulations, Part 888.
16 E.  "Recall" has the same meaning as in 21 Code of Federal Regulations, Section 7.3 
17 (2019).
182.  Patient notification required. Within 30 days after a health care entity receives 
19 notification of a recall or market withdrawal of an orthopedic medical device that it has 
20 provided to a patient, the health care entity shall provide a written notice to the patient of 
21 the recall or market withdrawal that includes the following information:
22 A.  Identification of the specific orthopedic medical device, including, if applicable, 
23 the lot number, code and serial number and any other pertinent descriptive information 
24 to enable accurate and immediate identification of the orthopedic medical device;
25 B.  A concise explanation of the reason for the recall or market withdrawal, to the 
26 extent known, and the hazards posed by the orthopedic medical device, if any;
27 C.  Specific action steps that the firm, health care entity or United States Food and Drug 
28 Administration advises the patient to take with respect to the orthopedic medical 
29 device; and
30 D.  A point of contact for the patient to obtain further information about the recall or 
31 market withdrawal and action steps under paragraph C.
323.  Civil violation.  An individual or health care entity that violates this chapter 
33 commits a civil violation.  The following penalties apply to violations of this chapter.
34 A.  An individual who violates this chapter commits a civil violation for which a fine 
35 of not less than $500 and not more than $5,000 may be adjudged for each violation.
36 B.  A health care entity that violates this chapter commits a civil violation for which a 
37 fine of not less than $2,000 and not more than $15,000 may be adjudged for each 
38 violation.

2 This bill requires a hospital or ambulatory surgical facility to notify patients when the 
3 hospital or ambulatory surgical facility receives notice that an orthopedic medical device 
4 that the hospital or ambulatory surgical facility has provided to a patient has been subjected 
5 to market withdrawal or United States Food and Drug Administration requested recall. 
6 Notice must be provided to the patient in writing within 30 days and include identification 
7 of the specific device, a concise explanation of the reason for the recall and the hazards 
8 involved, if any, specific action steps and a point of contact for the patient.
9 The bill provides for a civil violation if an individual or entity fails to provide the 
10 notification.  An individual is subject to a fine of not less than $500 and not more than 
11 $5,000 for each violation, and an entity is subject to a fine of not less than $2,000 and not 
12 more than $15,000 for each violation.
