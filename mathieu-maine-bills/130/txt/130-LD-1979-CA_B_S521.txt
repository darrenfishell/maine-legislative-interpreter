21 SUMMARY 
22 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, removes from the bill 
23 the provision that amends the antidegradation policy under the State's water classification 
24 program to provide that, notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, a person that 
25 meets a condition relating to any effect or potential effect on a species that is listed under 
26 the United States Endangered Species Act that is imposed or proposed to be imposed by 
27 the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission in granting a new license pursuant to 18 Code 
28 of Federal Regulations, Part 16, as amended, is considered to have met the State's water 
29 quality standards in the event that no equivalent state standard has been set. The 
30 amendment also adds an appropriations and allocations section.

This bill modifies the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry (ACF) criteria used to develop 
comprehensive river resource management plans. The bill includes appropriations of $485,811 beginning in fiscal year 
2022-23 for one Chief Planner position, two Senior Planner positions and two limited-period Senior Planner positions 
with an end date of June 30, 2024. The bill also includes appropriations of $21,700 and allocations of $19,170 also 
beginning in fiscal year 2022-23 for technology and all other costs associated with the positions.
