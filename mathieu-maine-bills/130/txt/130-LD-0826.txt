15Sec. 1.  Permanent Atlantic Standard Time study and analysis.  Resolved:  
16 That the University of Maine System shall study and analyze the potential effects on the 
17 State's economy of the State adopting permanent Atlantic Standard Time and determine if 
18 that adoption will serve the convenience of commerce as required by the United States 
19 Department of Transportation.  In conducting the study, the University of Maine System 
20 shall examine:
21 1.  The current effects of the practice of setting clocks forward and backward, including 
22 but not limited to physical health, mental health and economic effects as determined by 
23 both scientific research and public opinion; and
24 2.  Whether adopting permanent Atlantic Standard Time will negatively affect business 
25 shipping and receiving of goods and products; the ability of residents to receive important 
26 television and radio broadcasts and timely news and information; bus, passenger rail and 
27 airline services across time zones; residents who engage in work, schooling, recreation, 
28 health care or religious worship outside of the State's time zone; major elements of the 
29 State's economy; and any current federal, state or local plans for economic development.
30 Funding for the study must be through private donations to the University of Maine 
31 System.
32Sec. 2.  Report.  Resolved:  That, by December 31, 2021, the University of Maine 
33 System shall submit a report, including suggested legislation, based on its study and 
34 analysis in section 1 to the Joint Standing Committee on Innovation, Development, 
35 Economic Advancement and Business.  The committee is authorized to submit legislation 
36 related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
37Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
38 takes effect when approved.
