15§6554-A.  Cyberbullying of school employees
161.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
17 following terms have the following meanings.
18 A.  "School employee" means an employee of a school administrative unit, private 
19 school, approved private school or career and technical education center.
20 B.  "Student" means an elementary student or secondary student.
212.  Prohibition.  A student may not use a computer or computer network to:
22 A.  Intentionally intimidate or torment a school employee by:
23 (1)  Creating a fake profile or website;
24 (2)  Initiating a post or encouraging another person to post on the Internet private, 
25 personal or sexual information pertaining to the school employee;
26 (3)  Posting a real or doctored image of the school employee on the Internet;
27 (4)  Accessing, altering or erasing any computer network, computer data, computer 
28 program or computer software, including, but not limited to, breaking into a 
29 password-protected account or stealing or otherwise accessing a password, of the 
30 school employee; or
31 (5)  Using a computer network for repeated, continuing or sustained electronic 
32 communications, including, but not limited to, e-mail or other transmissions, to the 
33 school employee;
34 B.  Make a statement, whether true or false, intended to and likely to provoke a 3rd 
35 party to stalk or harass a school employee;
36 C.  Copy and disseminate or cause to be made in any form an unauthorized copy of 
37 data pertaining to a school employee for the purpose of intimidating or tormenting the 
38 school employee;
Page 2 - 130LR1951(01)1 D.  Sign up a school employee for a pornographic Internet site with the intent to 
2 intimidate or torment the school employee; or
3 E.  Without authorization of the school employee, sign up a school employee for e-
4 mail mailing lists or to receive junk electronic messages or instant messages, with the 
5 intent to intimidate or torment the school employee.
63.  Juvenile crime.  A student who violates this section commits cyberbullying of a 
7 school employee, which is a juvenile crime pursuant to Title 15, section 3103, subsection 
8 1, paragraph I.  Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, if a student pleads 
9 guilty to or is found guilty of an offense under this section, the court may, without entering 
10 a judgment of guilt and with the consent of the student, defer further proceedings and place 
11 the student on probation upon such reasonable terms and conditions as the court may 
12 require.
13SUMMARY
14 This bill prohibits elementary and secondary school students from using a computer or 
15 computer network to take certain actions against or toward a school employee.  A student 
16 who violates the prohibition commits a juvenile crime pursuant to the Maine Juvenile Code.14
