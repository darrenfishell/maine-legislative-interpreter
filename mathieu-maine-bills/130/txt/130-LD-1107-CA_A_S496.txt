13 'Resolve, Directing the Maine Connectivity Authority To Take Further Actions To

15 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following: 
16 'Sec. 1. Regional partnership strategies. Resolved: That the Maine Connectivity 
17 Authority, established in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 35-A, section 9404 and referred 
18 to in this resolve as "the authority," shall seek to encourage and support regional 
19 partnerships among appropriate entities to identify and implement strategies to bring high-
20 speed broadband to unserved areas of the State. The strategies must involve approaches 
21 and models that recognize and address population densities and the high cost of providing 
22 service to low-density areas; ensure the inclusion of services to all individuals and 
23 communities, including the most disadvantaged populations, within the area, commonly 
24 referred to as "digital inclusion"; and ensure the capacity of relevant communities to 
25 undertake a comprehensive approach to bringing broadband to the area. The strategies 
26 must involve consideration of requirements to receive available funding for expanding 
27 high-speed broadband connectivity as well as the critical nature of providing the 
28 connectivity in a time frame that meets the needs of the area. 
29 Sec. 2. Reports. Resolved: That the authority shall, by January 15, 2023, report to 
30 the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over energy, utilities 
31 and technology matters any federally approved plans for funding the activities described in 
32 section 1, including from the United States Department of the Treasury's Capital Projects 
33 Fund or the United States Department of Commerce, National Telecommunications and 
34 Information Administration's Broadband Equity, Access, and Deployment Program funded 
35 by the federal Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act.'

SUMMARY 
This amendment replaces the bill with a resolve that directs the Maine Connectivity 
Authority to: 
1. Encourage regional partnerships among appropriate entities to identify and 
implement strategies to bring high-speed broadband to unserved areas of the State; and 
2. Report to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
energy, utilities and technology matters any federally approved plans for funding these 
activities, including through the United States Department of the Treasury's Capital 
Projects Fund or the United States Department of Commerce, National 
Telecommunications and Information Administration's Broadband Equity, Access, and 
Deployment Program funded by the federal Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act.
