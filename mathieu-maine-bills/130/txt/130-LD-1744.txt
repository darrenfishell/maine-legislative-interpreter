31SUMMARY
32 This bill increases the amount of the fees collected from registrations to distribute a 
33 commercial feed in the State that is deposited in the Animal Welfare Fund from half of the 
34 fees collected to all of the fees collected.  It amends the animal welfare laws regarding 
35 rabies prevention and shelters to increase daily compensation for animal shelters holding 
36 animals pending court decisions from $5 to $15 for dogs and cats, from $8 to $18 for female 
37 cats or dogs with unweaned litters and from $10 to $20 for equines; it also increases from 
38 $8 to $20 the upper limit of daily compensation for holding livestock animals, excluding 
39 equines, fowl and rabbits.32
