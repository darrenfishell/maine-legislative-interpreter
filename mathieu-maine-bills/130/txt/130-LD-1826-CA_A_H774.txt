5 SUMMARY 
6 This amendment changes the title of and replaces the bill, removing the emergency 
7 preamble and emergency clause. It amends the statute governing the Interagency Task 
8 Force on Invasive Aquatic Plants and Nuisance Species to require that, on or before January 
9 15, 2023, and biennially thereafter, the task force submit to the Department of 
10 Environmental Protection, the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife, the 
11 Department of Marine Resources and the joint standing committees of the Legislature 
12 having jurisdiction over environment and natural resources matters, inland fisheries and 
13 wildlife matters and marine resources matters a report containing its findings and 
14 recommendations, including any suggested legislation, on a variety of matters relating to 
15 controlling the introduction of invasive aquatic plants and nuisance species in the State. 
16 After reviewing the report, each joint standing committee may report out legislation related 
1 7 to the report. 
18 The amendment also requires the task force, in developing those findings and 
19 recommendations, to convene a stakeholder group that includes, but is not limited to, task 
20 force members, state natural resources agency staff and other persons with relevant 
21 experience or expertise including representatives of local and regional lake associations 
22 and representatives of lake protection organizations. It provides that for the first report due 
23 January 15, 2023, the task force is required to include additional one-time findings on a 
24 number of specified matters.
