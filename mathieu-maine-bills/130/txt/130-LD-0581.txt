2Services.  Resolved:  That the Department of Labor shall add one new permanent 
3 position in the Bureau of Employment Services to expedite the processing of employer 
4 applications for certification of an employee as a member of a targeted group for purposes 
5 of the employer's qualifying for the federal work opportunity tax credit under Section 51 
6 of the United States Internal Revenue Code of 1986.
7Sec. 2.  Report.  Resolved:  That the Department of Labor shall submit a report to 
8 the Joint Standing Committee on Labor and Housing by February 1, 2022 with the most 
9 recent information available concerning employer applications for certification pursuant to 
10 the federal work opportunity tax credit under Section 51 of the United States Internal 
11 Revenue Code of 1986 submitted to the department and the extent of any backlog in 
12 application processing.
13SUMMARY
14 This resolve directs the Department of Labor to establish a new permanent position in 
15 the Bureau of Employment Services to expedite the processing of employer applications 
16 for certification required for the federal work opportunity tax credit under Section 51 of the 
17 United States Internal Revenue Code of 1986.  The resolve also requires the Department 
18 of Labor to submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on Labor and Housing with 
19 the most recent information available concerning applications submitted by employers, 
20 including information on the extent of any backlog in application processing, by February 
21 1, 2022.
