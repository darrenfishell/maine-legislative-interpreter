4§4706.  Instruction in American history, Maine studies and , Maine Native American

6 Instruction in American history, government, citizenship and , Maine studies and the 
7 history of genocide must be aligned with the parameters for essential instruction and 
8 graduation requirements established under section 6209.
91.  American history.  American history, government and citizenship, including the 
10 Constitution of the United States, the Declaration of Independence, the importance of 
11 voting and the privileges and responsibilities of citizenship, must be taught in and required 
12 for graduation from all elementary and secondary schools, both public and private.
132.  Maine studies.  Maine history, including the Constitution of Maine, Maine 
14 geography and environment and the natural, industrial and economic resources of Maine 
15 and Maine's cultural and ethnic heritage, must be taught.  A required component of Maine 
16 studies is Maine Native American studies, which must be included in the review of content 
17 standards and performance indicators of the learning results conducted in accordance with 
18 section 6209, subsection 4.  The Maine Native American studies must address the following 
19 topics:
20 A.  Maine tribal governments and political systems and their relationship with local, 
21 state, national and international governments;
22 B.  Maine Native American cultural systems and the experience of Maine tribal people 
23 throughout history;
24 C.  Maine Native American territories; and
25 D.  Maine Native American economic systems.
263.  History of genocide.  The history of genocide, including the Holocaust, must be 
27 taught and must be included in the review of content standards and performance indicators 
28 of the system of learning results conducted in accordance with section 6209, subsection 4.  
29 For purposes of this subsection, "Holocaust" means the program of discriminatory and 
30 genocidal laws, policies or actions targeted against discrete groups of individuals based on 
31 race, religion, ethnicity, sexual orientation, physical disability or national origin, from 
32 January 1, 1933 to December 31, 1945, in Nazi Germany or in any European country allied 
33 with or occupied by Nazi Germany.  The teaching of the history of genocide does not have 
34 to be a separate course of study.
35SUMMARY
36 This bill requires that the history of genocide, including the Holocaust, be taught in 
37 schools, aligned with the parameters for essential instruction and graduation requirements 
38 and included in the review of content standards and performance indicators of the system 
39 of learning results.
