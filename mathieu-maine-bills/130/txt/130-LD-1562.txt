16Sec. 5.  Transfer of funds from Multimodal Transportation Fund.  On the 
17 effective date of this Act, the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Office

22Sec. 6.  Transfer of state rail assets. The Department of Transportation and the 
23 Northern New England Passenger Rail Authority shall enter into agreements to transfer all

25 Authority, except for the state rail assets listed under section 7. The department shall 
26 conduct a review of existing laws relevant to state rail assets and submit a report to the

28 legislation necessary to facilitate the transfer of rail assets to the Northern New England

32 corridors for future passenger rail development:
33 1.  The portion of the Mountain Division Trail corridor from the crossing at

35 Fryeburg; and

37 Ayers Junction Road in the Town of Pembroke to the Canadian border in the Town of 
38 Calais.
39Sec. 8.  Passenger rail bonding review.  The Northern New England Passenger 
40 Rail Authority shall analyze and develop recommendations on whether the authority should

2 This bill does the following.
3 1.  It establishes the State Passenger Rail Fund and transfers all funds from the 
4 automobile and truck rental sales tax in the Multimodal Transportation Fund to the State 
5 Passenger Rail Fund.
6 2.  It directs the Northern New England Passenger Rail Authority to hire additional 
7 executive management and staff positions.
8 3.  It creates 2 new director positions on the Northern New England Passenger Rail 
9 Authority board of directors.
10 4.  It directs the Department of Transportation and the Northern New England 
11 Passenger Rail Authority to enter into agreements to transfer all state rail assets the 
12 department controls to the Northern New England Passenger Rail Authority.
13 5.  It directs the Department of Transportation to develop a plan to allow for the 
14 recreational use of certain rail corridors while also preserving the corridors for future 
15 passenger rail development.
16 6.  It directs the Department of Transportation to conduct a review of existing laws 
17 relevant to state rail assets and submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on 
18 Transportation no later than January 1, 2022 identifying any legislation necessary to 
19 facilitate the transfer of rail assets to the Northern New England Passenger Rail Authority.
20 7.  It directs the Northern New England Passenger Rail Authority to look into options 
21 for issuing bonds necessary to carry out its responsibilities under the Maine Revised 
22 Statutes, Title 23, chapter 621 and report its findings to the Joint Standing Committee on 
23 Transportation no later than January 1, 2022.2
