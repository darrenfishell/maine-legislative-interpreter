3Sec. 1. Identification of offensive names for geographic features and other 
4places.  Resolved:  That the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry 
5 Permanent Commission on the Status of Racial, Indigenous and Tribal Populations shall 
6 work jointly with the Permanent Commission on the Status of Racial, Indigenous and 
7 Maine Tribal Populations, jointly referred to in this resolve as "the department and 
8 commission," to review state law regarding offensive place names.  The department and 
9 commission shall:
10 1.  Review the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 1, section 1101 to determine and suggest 
11 any additional words to be included in the current definition of offensive name;
12 2.  Establish a uniform process for renaming geographic features and other places that 
13 have offensive names; and
14 3.  Explore the most effective methods of identifying the individuals, entities and 
15 institutions in the State that profited or benefited in any manner from the global economy 
16 of enslavement.; and
17 4. Create the Advisory Committee on Reconciliation in Place Names within the 
18 commission, and appoint members to the advisory committee, in order to accelerate the 
19 process by which offensive place names in the State are identified and replaced.  This 
20 subsection governs the creation and activities of the advisory committee.
21 A. The commission may determine the number of members that will serve on the 
22 advisory committee.
23 B. The commission may determine the expertise of the members appointed to serve on 
24 the advisory committee, which may include representation from indigenous and tribal 
25 populations; members of the African American community; people with a deep 
26 knowledge of civil rights, anthropology or history; representatives of the Department 
27 of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, Office of the Attorney General or Maine 
28 Human Rights Commission; and members of the public.
29 C. The advisory committee shall consult with local community representatives, 
30 municipalities, historically impacted populations, state, federal and local governments 
31 and the public in order to broadly solicit, review and recommend to the commission 
32 changes that will facilitate the proactive and systematic development and review of 
33 name-change proposals.
34 D. The advisory committee shall establish a process to solicit and assist with proposals 
35 to the United States Board on Geographic Names to change offensive place names.
36 It is the intent of this resolve that the department and commission Permanent 
37 Commission on the Status of Racial, Indigenous and Tribal Populations carry out their its 
38 responsibilities within the existing resources of the department and commission.  The 
39 department and commission may solicit, accept and expend funds to cover any expenses 
40 that are in excess of existing resources.

4SUMMARY
5 This resolve amends Resolve 2021, chapter 98 to extend from December 1, 2021 to 
6 December 1, 2022 the deadline for submitting the required report to the Legislature, 
7 removes the requirement that the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry 
8 work jointly with the Permanent Commission on the Status of Racial, Indigenous and 
9 Tribal Populations to review state law regarding offensive place names and authorizes the 
10 Permanent Commission on the Status of Racial, Indigenous and Tribal Populations to 
11 establish an advisory committee to facilitate the process of identifying offensive place 
12 names.1
