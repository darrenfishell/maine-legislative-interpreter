13Sec. 1.  Commission established.  Resolved: That the Commission To Develop a 
14 Paid Family and Medical Leave Benefits Program, referred to in this resolve as "the 
15 commission," is established.
16Sec. 2.  Commission membership. Resolved: That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
17 353, the commission consists of 11 voting members appointed as follows:
18 1.  Two members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including a 
19 member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
20 2.  Two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
21 House, including a member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
22 in the Legislature;
23 3.  Two members appointed by the President of the Senate as follows:
24 A.  One member with expertise in issues affecting labor and independent contractors; 
25 and
26 B.  One employer with more than 50 employees;
27 4.  Two members appointed by the Speaker of the House as follows:
28 A.  One member with expertise in issues related to family and medical leave benefits; 
29 and
30 B.  One employer with 50 or fewer employees;
31 5.  Two members appointed by the Governor as follows:
32 A.  One member with expertise in issues affecting maternity and postpartum care; and
33 B. One member with expertise in issues affecting elder care; and
34 6.  The Commissioner of Labor or the commissioner's designee.
35Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved: That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
36 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
37 commission.  Notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the chairs may appoint, as nonvoting 
38 members, individuals with expertise in paid family and medical leave, social insurance 
39 programs or related state infrastructure.
Page 2 - 130LR0547(01)1Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
2 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
3 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
4 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
5 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
6 after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
7 made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for 
8 the commission to meet and conduct its business.
9Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved: That the commission shall:
10 1.  Study the paid family and medical leave benefits programs in other states, including 
11 those that have established paid family and medical leave benefits programs.  In its review 
12 of paid family and medical leave benefits programs in other states, the commission shall 
13 consider without limitation the following factors for each program:
14 A.  Equity of the program;
15 B.  Program funding;
16 C.  State partnerships and consortiums, including information technology needs;
17 D.  Education and outreach needs;
18 E.  Oversight and structure of the program; and
19 F.  Relationship between state government and the employers and employees 
20 participating in the program;
21 2.  Solicit and consider public comment on the administration and establishment of a 
22 paid family and medical leave benefits program, including but not limited to purposes for 
23 leave, eligibility, job protection and duration;
24 3.  Develop a plan to implement a paid family and medical leave benefits program by 
25 consulting with other states that have established paid family and medical leave benefits 
26 programs;
27 4.  Contract for and complete an actuarial study of the planned program under 
28 subsection 3, including start-up costs and ongoing costs of the program, the economic 
29 impact on and benefits to the State and the contributions needed to maintain the solvency 
30 of the program; and
31 5.  Based on the actuarial study in subsection 4 and other factors considered by the 
32 commission, make recommendations to implement a paid family and medical leave 
33 benefits program, including any necessary legislation.
34Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Governor's Office of Policy 
35 Innovation and the Future shall provide necessary staffing services to the commission.  
36 Upon request, the Department of Labor, the Department of Health and Human Services 
37 and the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Office of Information 
38 Technology and Bureau of Revenue Services shall provide additional staffing assistance to 
39 the commission.
40Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, no later than 
41 January 15, 2022, the commission shall submit a report that includes its findings and 
Page 3 - 130LR0547(01)42 recommendations, including suggested legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular 
43 Session of the 130th Legislature.
3Sec. 8.  Additional funding sources.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
4 353, the commission may apply for and receive funds, grants or contributions from public 
5 and private sources to support its activities.
6Sec. 9.  Transfers from Department of Professional and Financial 
7Regulation Other Special Revenue Funds balances to General Fund.  
8Resolved: That, at the close of fiscal year 2020-21, the State Controller shall transfer 
9 $200,000 from available balances in Other Special Revenue Funds accounts within the 
10 Department of Professional and Financial Regulation to the General Fund unappropriated 
11 surplus to support the duties of the commission.  The Commissioner of Professional and 
12 Financial Regulation shall determine from which accounts the funds will be transferred so 
13 that the sum equals $200,000 and notify the State Controller and the Joint Standing 
14 Committee on Appropriations and Financial Affairs of the amounts to be transferred from 
15 each account.
16Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
17 takes effect when approved.
18SUMMARY
19 This resolve establishes the Commission To Develop a Paid Family and Medical Leave 
20 Benefits Program.  It requires the State Controller to transfer $200,000 from available 
21 balances in Other Special Revenue Funds accounts within the Department of Professional 
22 and Financial Regulation to the General Fund unappropriated surplus to support the duties 
23 of the commission.1
