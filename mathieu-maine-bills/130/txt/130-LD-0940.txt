18Sec. 4.  2 MRSA §9, sub-§8 is enacted to read:
198.  Appliance and product efficiency standards.  The following provisions apply to 
20 the sale or offering for sale in the State of certain new appliances and products.
21 A.  Beginning January 1, 2023, a person may not sell or offer for sale in the State any 
22 of the following appliances and products that are prohibited from sale in the State of 
23 California pursuant to the California Code of Regulations, Title 20, Division 2, Chapter 
24 4, Article 4, as those regulations were in effect on January 1, 2021 and as amended on 
25 December 9, 2020:
26 (1)  Computers and computer monitors;
27 (2)  General service lamps;
28 (3)  Commercial hot food holding cabinets;
29 (4)  Plumbing fittings that are showerheads, lavatory faucets, kitchen faucets, 
30 public lavatory faucets, metering faucets, kitchen replacement aerators and 
31 lavatory replacement aerators;
32 (5)  Plumbing fixtures that are water closets and urinals;
33 (6)  Portable electric spas;
34 (7)  Spray sprinkler bodies; and
35 (8)  Water dispensers.
36 For the purposes of this paragraph, the appliances and products listed in subparagraphs 
37 (1) to (8) have the same meanings as in the California Code of Regulations, Title 20, 
38 Division 2, Chapter 4, Article 4, as those regulations were in effect on January 1, 2021, 
39 except that "general service lamps" means medium-base incandescent light bulbs that 
40 are: reflector lamps that are ER30, BR30, BR40 or ER40 lamps rated at 50 watts or

22 joint standing committee may report out legislation relating to the report.
23 This subsection does not apply to any appliances and products listed in paragraph A, 
24 subparagraphs (1) to (8) that are manufactured before January 1, 2022 or that are sold or 
25 offered for sale in the State in used condition.

32SUMMARY
33 This bill provides that, beginning January 1, 2023, a person may not sell or offer for 
34 sale in the State any of the following new products that were prohibited from sale in the 
35 State of California under its appliance efficiency regulations, as those regulations were in 
36 effect on January 1, 2021: computers and computer monitors; certain general service lamps; 
37 commercial hot food holding cabinets; plumbing fittings that are showerheads, lavatory 
38 faucets, kitchen faucets, public lavatory faucets, metering faucets, kitchen replacement 
39 aerators and lavatory replacement aerators; plumbing fixtures that are water closets and 
40 urinals; portable electric spas; spray sprinkler bodies; and water dispensers.
41 The Governor's Energy Office is required to enforce this prohibition, and fines for a 
42 violation of the prohibition are specified.  The office is authorized to enter into a 
43 memorandum of understanding or other formalized agreement with the Efficiency Maine 
44 Trust for the provision of technical assistance by the trust to consumers and businesses 1
