10SUMMARY
11 This bill makes changes to the provisions governing property of a debtor exempt from 
12 attachment and execution. 
13 1.  The bill increases the value of the exemption for a debtor's interest in a residence to 
14 the median home price in the State, provides that that amount is $225,000 as of December 
15 2020 and requires that the amount must be adjusted with the median housing value for the 
16 preceding year beginning January 1, 2022. The bill also permits a debtor an allowance of 
17 2% of the value of the residence plus an amount equal to the property taxes payable by the 
18 debtor for the preceding year. 
19 2.  The bill increases the value of certain property that is exempt from attachment and 
20 execution and requires that the maximum value is adjusted to account for inflation 
21 beginning January 1, 2022.  
22 3.  The bill permits a debtor an allowance of 2% of the manufacturer's suggested  retail 
23 price for a motor vehicle plus an amount equal to the excise taxes payable by the debtor for 
24 the preceding year. 
25 The bill also adds a provision requiring that a court must consider the state minimum 
26 wage if it is higher than the federal minimum wage when determining the amount of 
27 installment payments that a debtor must make to a creditor following a money judgment.
