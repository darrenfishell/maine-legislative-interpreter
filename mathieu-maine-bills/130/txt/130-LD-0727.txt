Page 1 - 130LR1611(01)1Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to seek waiver.  
2Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services shall submit a request to 
3 the United States Department of Agriculture to waive the requirements for an asset test for 
4 determining the eligibility of any household under the federal supplemental nutrition 
5 assistance program when the household includes an individual who is 60 years of age or 
6 older.
7Sec. 2.  Home-delivered meals pilot project.  Resolved:  That, no later than 
8 January 1, 2022, the Department of Health and Human Services shall submit an application 
9 to the United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for Medicare and 
10 Medicaid Services to request a demonstration waiver under the federal Social Security Act, 
11 Section 1115 to develop a 2-year pilot project pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 
12 22-A, section 203, subsection 5 to provide home-delivered meals to individuals who are 60 
13 years of age or older and who are homebound or at risk for readmission to a health care 
14 facility.  The pilot project under the waiver is limited to individuals being released from a 
15 health care facility at high risk of readmission who have received a doctor's 
16 recommendation that medically tailored food and nutrition interventions are necessary for 
17 the patient's health. Patients being offered medically tailored food and nutrition 
18 interventions through the pilot project may receive one meal per day for up to 14 days. The 
19 Department of Health and Human Services shall submit a report no later than July 1, 2024 
20 on the results of the pilot project, including, but not limited to, the number of individuals 
21 served by the pilot project; the number of home-delivered meals provided; health care 
22 facility readmission rates of those served; and recommendations for continuing the pilot 
23 project to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health 
24 and human services matters at the conclusion of the pilot project.
25SUMMARY
26 This resolve directs the Department of Health and Human Services to request the 
27 United States Department of Agriculture to waive the asset test for determining the 
28 eligibility for the federal supplemental nutrition assistance program of any household in 
29 which there is an individual who is 60 years of age or older.  It also directs the department 
30 to develop a pilot project to provide home-delivered meals to individuals who are 60 years 
31 of age or older and who are homebound or at risk for readmission to a health care facility.  
32 It requires the department to report to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
33 jurisdiction over health and human services matters at the conclusion of the pilot project.26
