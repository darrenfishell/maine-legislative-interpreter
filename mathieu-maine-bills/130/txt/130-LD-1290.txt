2Sec. 1.  32 MRSA §81-A, last ¶, as amended by PL 2007, c. 274, §1, is further 
3 amended to read:
4 It is the intent of the Legislature to designate that a central agency be responsible for 
5 the coordination and integration of all state activities concerning emergency medical 
6 services and the overall planning, evaluation, coordination, facilitation and regulation of 
7 emergency medical services systems.  Further, the Legislature finds that the provision of 
8 prompt, efficient and effective emergency medical dispatch and emergency medical care, 
9 a well-coordinated trauma care system, effective communication between prehospital care 
10 providers and hospitals and the safe handling and transportation, and the treatment and 
11 nontransport under appropriate medical guidance, of the sick and injured are key elements 
12 of an emergency medical services system.  This chapter is intended to promote the public 
13 health, safety and welfare by providing for the creation of a statewide emergency medical 
14 services system with standards for all providers of emergency medical services.
15SUMMARY
16 This bill amends the statutory purpose of the Maine Emergency Medical Services Act 
17 of 1982 to provide that the treatment and nontransport of the sick and injured is a key 
18 element of an emergency medical services system.16
