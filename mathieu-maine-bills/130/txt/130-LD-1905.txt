51.  Requirements for communication.  To ensure that all waivers of the right to 
6 counsel are made knowingly, voluntarily and intelligently, a prosecutor may not 
7 communicate with an unrepresented defendant concerning the facts, circumstances, merits 
8 or disposition of a pending criminal charge against the defendant unless:
9 A.  The defendant has been informed by a judicial officer of the defendant's right to 
10 counsel, including court-appointed counsel  if the defendant is indigent;
11 B.  The court has provided to the defendant a statement of:
12 (1)  The substance of the charges against the defendant;
13 (2)  The defendant's right to retain counsel, to request the assignment of counsel 
14 and to be allowed a reasonable time and opportunity to consult counsel before 
15 entering a plea;
16 (3)  The defendant's right to remain silent and that the defendant is not required to 
17 make a statement and that any statement made by the defendant may be used 
18 against the defendant;
19 (4)  The maximum possible sentence and any applicable mandatory minimum 
20 sentence; and
21 (5)  The defendant's right to trial by jury; and
22 C.  The defendant has executed a written waiver of waived the right to counsel  in each 
23 prosecution  pursuant to rules adopted by the Supreme Judicial Court.
242.  Exception.  Notwithstanding subsection 1, a prosecutor may communicate with an 
25 unrepresented defendant who has not executed a written waiver of the right to counsel to

333.  Application.  This section does not apply to:
34 A.  The obligation of the State to provide discovery or other information pursuant to

37 B.  Notice by the prosecutor to a person that no charge is being filed.
