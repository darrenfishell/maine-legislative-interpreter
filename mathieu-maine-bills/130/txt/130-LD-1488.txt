Page 1 - 130LR1698(01)1Sec. 1.  Dam study.  Resolved:  That the Department of Defense, Veterans and 
2 Emergency Management, in consultation with other state agencies as required by the Maine 
3 Revised Statutes, Title 37-B, section 1112, appropriate federal agencies, state and local 
4 emergency management officials, hazard assessment and public safety experts, including 
5 dam safety experts, civil engineering organizations, environmental organizations, 
6 municipal dam operators and private dam owners, shall conduct a study of the State's dam 
7 safety efforts under Title 37-B, chapter 24 that assesses the condition of dams in the State 
8 and the ability of the department to ensure the safe operation of dams and to protect public 
9 safety.  The study must include:
10 1.  A review of the State's laws and rules governing dams and dam safety and whether 
11 they are sufficient to ensure that the objectives of Title 37-B, chapter 24 are met;
12 2.  A survey of the State's existing dams, including the:
13 A.  Total number of registered and unregistered dams;
14 B.  Hazard potential and hazard classifications of each dam;
15 C.  Current age and estimated operational lifespan of each dam;
16 D.  Potential risks to downstream communities with respect to dam failures, including 
17 infrastructure and public and private property; and
18 E.  Impact of dams and potential dam failures on public resources, including wildlife 
19 habitats, native ecosystems, water quality and fisheries;
20 3.  An assessment of the resiliency of the State's existing dams and their ability to 
21 withstand historic and projected drought and flood events;
22 4.  A review of the existing limits and needs of the State's dam safety efforts; and
23 5.  A review of local, state and federal loan or grant programs available to dam owners 
24 in the State.
25 The department may use funds from federal or private sources, to the extent available, 
26 to conduct this study and shall disclose those funding sources in its report to the Joint 
27 Standing Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs under section 3.
28Sec. 2.  Dam safety peer review.  Resolved:  That, by December 1, 2021, the 
29 department shall have the Association of State Dam Safety Officials conduct a peer review 
30 of the State's dam safety efforts.
31Sec. 3.  Dam report.  Resolved:  That, by January 14, 2022, the Department of 
32 Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management shall provide a report on the results of its 
33 study under section 1, the peer review under section 2 and recommendations for the 
34 improvement of dam safety, including suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing 
35 Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs.  The committee is authorized to submit a bill 
36 based on the report to the Second Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
37SUMMARY
38 This resolve requires the Department of Defense, Veterans and Emergency 
39 Management to conduct a study of the State's dams and the State's dam safety efforts, 
40 including an assessment of the hazard potential and classification of each dam, the potential 38
