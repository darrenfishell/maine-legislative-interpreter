23Sec. 2.  Rulemaking.  By April 1, 2022, the Department of Health and Human 
24 Services shall adopt emergency rules to implement provisions of the Maine Revised 
25 Statutes, Title 22, section 3174-F.  When adopting rules under this section, the department 
26 shall consider recommendations provided by the stakeholder group established pursuant to 
27 section 3.
28Sec. 3.  Convene stakeholder group.  No later than October 1, 2021, the 
29 Department of Health and Human Services shall convene a stakeholder group to develop a 
30 plan for adult comprehensive preventive, diagnostic and restorative dental benefits under 
31 the MaineCare program.
321.  Members.  The stakeholder group consists of the Commissioner of Health and 
33 Human Services or the commissioner's designee and 12 members appointed by the 
34 commissioner as follows:
35 A.  One member representing a statewide organization representing dentists;
36 B.  One member representing a statewide organization representing dental hygienists;
37 C.  One member representing a federally qualified health center;
38 D.  One member representing a nonprofit dental clinic;
39 E.  One member representing a nonprofit dental managed care organization;
Page 2 - 130LR1172(01)1 F.  One member with policy expertise or experience in promoting access to dental care 
2 for low-income individuals;
3 G.  One member representing a philanthropic organization concerned with oral health;
4 H.  One member with experience in public health policy and oral health;
5 I.  One member with expertise in racial and ethnic health disparities;
6 J.  Two members currently enrolled in the MaineCare program who would benefit from 
7 access to an adult dental benefit; and
8 K.  One member of the general public.
92.  Stakeholder group duties.  The stakeholder group shall consider the matters in 
10 paragraphs A to I and develop a plan that includes:
11 A.  Dental procedures and services that are aligned with evidence-based care, are 
12 medically necessary to maintain good oral and overall health and are appropriate to be 
13 included in an adult dental benefit under the MaineCare program;
14 B.  Current MaineCare expenditures for emergency dental care;
15 C.  Projected expenditures for adult dental services that include an adult comprehensive 
16 preventive, diagnostic and restorative dental benefit;
17 D.  Anticipated cost savings resulting from reduced use of hospital emergency 
18 departments and prescription medication related to the relief of pain and infection as a 
19 result of the implementation of the plan;
20 E.  Delivery and provider reimbursement models that will best ensure adequate access 
21 to quality dental care for all enrollees, improve provider participation, achieve healthy 
22 outcomes and promote fiscal and administrative efficiency for the long-term success 
23 of the MaineCare program;
24 F.  Delivery methods including fee-for-service state administration and risk-based 
25 dental managed care organization carve-out models.  The stakeholder group shall take 
26 into account differences among dental practice settings, including any differences in 
27 service delivery, payment methods and reimbursement amounts between private 
28 practices, community dental clinics, federally qualified and similarly situated health 
29 centers and hospital-affiliated providers;
30 G.  An evaluation of existing oral health disparities for Maine racial minority, 
31 Indigenous and tribal populations and recommendations for strategies to reduce 
32 disparities through the implementation of the adult dental benefit;
33 H.  Strategies to improve oral health education within the MaineCare program;
34 I.  Metrics to measure outcomes including measures of provider participation and 
35 utilization of services; and
36 J.  The plan must be submitted in a report to the Joint Standing Committee on Health 
37 and Human Services no later than January 2, 2022.

34SUMMARY
35 This bill adds comprehensive preventive, diagnostic and restorative dental services to 
36 the limited dental services currently available to MaineCare members 21 years of age and 
37 over.  It requires the Department of Health and Human Services to convene a stakeholder 
38 group no later than October 1, 2021 to assist in creating a plan to implement the adult health 
39 benefit.  It requires the department to work with providers of oral health care and dental 
40 services to encourage participation in the MaineCare program.  It requires the department 
41 to develop public education materials and strategies for the distribution of those materials 
42 relating to the importance of dental services for children and the availability of the 
43 comprehensive adult dental benefit to children covered by the MaineCare program who are 1
