3CHAPTER 810
4THE RIGHT TO PRACTICE FORESTRY ACT
5§9801.  Short title
6 This Act may be known and cited as "the Right To Practice Forestry Act."
7§9802.  Definitions
8 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 
9 have the following meanings.
101.  Department.  "Department" means the Department of Agriculture, Conservation 
11 and Forestry.
122.  Forest.  "Forest" means a parcel of land in which at least 80% of the parcel is 
13 producing or is capable of producing at least 20 cubic feet of merchantable timber per acre 
14 per year.
153.  Forestry operation.  "Forestry operation" means an operation that involves forest 
16 management activities for which generally accepted forest management practices have 
17 been established by the department by rule under section 9806.
184.  Local unit of government.  "Local unit of government" means a county, city or 
19 town located in the State.
20§9803.  Forestry operation not a nuisance
211.  Generally accepted forest management practices.  A forestry operation may not 
22 be considered a public or private nuisance under Title 17, chapter 91:
23 A.  If the forestry operation alleged to be a nuisance is in compliance with generally 
24 accepted forest management practices established by the department by rule under 
25 section 9806.  This paragraph does not apply if an activity associated with a forestry 
26 operation alleged to be a nuisance is found to be the result of negligence; or
27 B.  Solely as a result of the occurrence of any of the following:
28 (1)  Change in ownership or size of the forestry operation;
29 (2)  Cessation or interruption of the forestry operation;
30 (3)  Enrollment of all or part of the forestry operation in a governmental forestry 
31 or conservation program;
32 (4)  Adoption of new forestry technology by the forestry operation;
33 (5)  Visual change due to removal of timber or vegetation;
34 (6)  Normal noise from forestry equipment;
35 (7)  Application of pesticides in a manner consistent with rules for pesticide 
36 application adopted by the Board of Pesticides Control within the department; or
Page 2 - 130LR0544(01)1 (8)  Application of fertilizers in a manner consistent with applicable laws and rules.
2§9804.  Recovery of costs
3 In any court action in which a forestry operation is alleged to be a nuisance, if the 
4 person that was alleged to commit the nuisance prevails, the court may award that person 
5 the actual and necessary costs incurred by the action and reasonable attorney's fees.
6§9805.  Violation of ordinances
7 A local unit of government that allows a forestry operation to operate in that local unit 
8 of government may not regulate that forestry operation in a manner that limits or prohibits 
9 any generally accepted forest management practices established by the department by rule 
10 under section 9806.
11§9806.  Rules
12 The department shall by rule establish generally accepted forest management practices.  
13 Rules adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
14 chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
15SUMMARY
16 This bill enacts the Right To Practice Forestry Act.  The bill provides that a local unit 
17 of government that allows a forestry operation to operate in that local unit of government 
18 may not regulate that forestry operation in a manner that limits or prohibits any generally 
19 accepted forest management practices, which the bill requires the Department of 
20 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to establish by rule.16
