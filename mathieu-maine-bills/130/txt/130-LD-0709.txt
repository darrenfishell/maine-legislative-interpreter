12Sec. 1.  Net energy billing moratorium. Resolved:  That, notwithstanding the 
13 Maine Revised Statutes, Title 35-A, sections 3209-A and 3209-B, until June 30, 2021, the 
14 Public Utilities Commission may not allow any new net energy billing arrangements for a 
15 distributed generation resource with a nameplate capacity between 2 megawatts and 5 
16 megawatts unless:
17 1. An interconnection request involving the distributed generation resource was made 
18 with the commission on or before January 1, 2021; or
19 2. The distributed generation resource is proposed to be sited on a brownfield site or a 
20 landfill site.
21Sec. 2.  Stakeholder group. Resolved: That the Governor's Energy Office shall 
22 convene a stakeholder group to examine the costs and benefits of net energy billing 
23 arrangements associated with a distributed generation resource with a nameplate capacity 
24 between 2 megawatts and 5 megawatts in order to make recommendations regarding the 
25 need for any changes to existing laws relating to net energy billing. The office shall submit 
26 a report to the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and Technology by May 15, 
27 2021 outlining its findings and recommendations, including any proposed legislation, and 
28 the committee may report out a bill based on the report to the First Regular Session of the 
29 130th Legislature.
30Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
31 takes effect when approved.
32SUMMARY
33 This resolve places a brief moratorium on new net energy billing arrangements until 
34 June 30, 2021 associated with a distributed generation resource with a nameplate capacity 
35 between 2 megawatts and 5 megawatts unless an interconnection request has been made 
36 with the Public Utilities Commission on or before January 1, 2021 for that distributed 
37 generation resource or the distributed generation resource is proposed to be sited on a 
38 brownfield or landfill site.
39 It also directs the Governor's Energy Office to convene a stakeholder group to examine 
40 both the costs and benefits of net energy billing arrangements associated with a distributed 
41 generation resource with a nameplate capacity between 2 megawatts and 5 megawatts in 
42 order to make recommendations regarding the need for any changes to existing laws 33
