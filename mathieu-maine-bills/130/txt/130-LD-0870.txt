2Maine Tribal Populations study.  Resolved:  That the Permanent Commission on 
3 the Status of Racial, Indigenous and Maine Tribal Populations, established under the Maine 
4 Revised Statutes, Title 5, section 12004-I, subsection 74-J and referred to in this resolve as 
5 "the commission," shall study the impact of policies regarding agriculture, access to land, 
6 access to grants and access to financing on African-American and indigenous farmers in 
7 the State.  The commission shall report its findings and may submit legislation that serves 
8 to create equity in representation and access to agricultural programs, land, grants and 
9 financing in the State to the Joint Standing Committee on Agriculture, Conservation and 
10 Forestry by January 17, 2022.

18SUMMARY
19 This resolve directs the Permanent Commission on the Status of Racial, Indigenous 
20 and Maine Tribal Populations to study the impact of policies regarding agriculture, access 
21 to land, access to grants and access to financing on African-American and indigenous 
22 farmers in the State.  It directs the commission to report its findings and allows it to submit 
23 legislation that serves to create equity in representation and access to agricultural programs, 
24 land, grants and financing in the State to the Joint Standing Committee on Agriculture, 
25 Conservation and Forestry by January 17, 2022.  It also provides a one-time allocation of 
26 $20,000 in fiscal year 2021-22.18
