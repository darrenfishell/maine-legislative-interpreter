34SUMMARY
35 This bill makes the following changes to the Maine Medical Use of Marijuana Act:  
36 1.  It defines the terms "batch number," "marijuana tincture," "packing slip" and 
37 "telehealth services" and amends the definition of "medical provider"; 
38 2.  It establishes requirements for the inclusion of batch numbers on the packaging or 
39 container or on a packing slip for retail and wholesale transactions of harvested marijuana; 
40 3.  It amends the requirements for medical providers providing written certification to 
41 qualifying patients who are minors including by imposing a requirement for medical 1

Page 5 - 130LR2277(01)42 providers to be available after hours for questions about a minor patient's use of medical 
43 marijuana; 
3 4.  It authorizes the use of telehealth services for medical providers to meet with 
4 patients seeking a written certification for the medical use of marijuana;
5 5.  It adds naturopathic doctors to the list of medical providers that may issue written 
6 certifications and clarifies that medical providers who issue written certifications must be 
7 licensed in Maine; and
8 6.  It requires the Department of Administrative and Financial Services to publish on 
9 its website a monthly report on sales data, as well as a breakdown of revenues generated 
10 by registered caregivers and dispensaries. 
11 The bill makes the following changes to the Marijuana Legalization Act:
12 1.  It defines the term "marijuana tincture"; and
13 2.  It requires the Department of Administrative and Financial Services to publish on 
14 its website a monthly report on retail sales data.1
