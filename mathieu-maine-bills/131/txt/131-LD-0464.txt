3Whereas, persons with disabilities have the right to dignity and to receive the support 
4 they need to work and to be otherwise included in their communities; and
5Whereas, many persons with disabilities have an urgent need for adult home and 
6 community-based services to provide support and to enable their family members to work; 
7 and

11 services ensures dignity and safety; and

14 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
15 therefore,

5SUMMARY
6 This bill provides funding to provide services to individuals with brain injury, other 
7 related conditions, developmental disabilities or autism spectrum disorder on the waiting 
8 lists for community-based services provided under the Department of Health and Human 
9 Services rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapters II and III, Section 21, 
10 Home and Community Benefits for Members with Intellectual Disabilities or Autism 
11 Spectrum Disorder; Section 29, Support Services for Adults with Intellectual Disabilities 
12 or Autism Spectrum Disorder; Section 18, Home and Community-Based Services for 
13 Adults with Brain Injury; and Section 20, Home and Community-Based Services for Adults 
14 with Other Related Conditions. It also provides funding to eliminate the waiting lists for 
15 state-funded services provided under Department of Health and Human Services rule 
16 Chapter 10-149: Office of Aging and Disability Services, Chapter 5, Office of Aging and 
17 Disability Services Policy Manual, Section 63, In-Home and Community Support Services 
18 for Elderly and Other Adults and for services provided under Chapter 14-197: Office of 
19 Aging and Disability Services, Chapter 11, Consumer Directed Personal Assistance 
20 Services.1
