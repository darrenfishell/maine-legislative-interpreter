2Sec. 1.  29-A MRSA §2063, sub-§11, as enacted by PL 2015, c. 164, §7, is amended 
3 to read:
411.  Traffic-control devices other than red lights.  A person operating a bicycle or 
5 roller skis shall obey a traffic-control device other than a red light, unless otherwise directed 
6 by a law enforcement officer.  A traffic-control device conforming to the requirements for 
7 these devices is presumed to comply with this chapter.
8Sec. 2.  29-A MRSA §2063, sub-§11-A is enacted to read:
911-A.  Red lights.  Unless directed to proceed by a law enforcement officer, a person 
10 operating a bicycle or roller skis approaching a red light shall stop and:
11 A.  Yield the right-of-way to a vehicle that has entered the intersection or that is 
12 approaching so closely as to constitute an immediate hazard; and
13 B.  Having yielded, a person operating a bicycle or roller skis may proceed. All other 
14 operators approaching the intersection shall yield the right-of-way to the person 
15 operating a bicycle or roller skis so proceeding.
16Sec. 3.  29-A MRSA §2063, sub-§12, as enacted by PL 2015, c. 164, §7, is amended 
17 to read:
1812.  Stop signs.  Unless directed to proceed by a law enforcement officer or traffic-
19 control device, a person operating a bicycle or roller skis approaching a stop sign shall stop 
20 and:
21 A.  Yield the right-of-way to a vehicle that has entered the intersection or that is 
22 approaching so closely as to constitute an immediate hazard; and
23 B.  Having yielded, a person operating a bicycle or roller skis may proceed.  All other 
24 operators approaching the intersection shall yield the right-of-way to the person 
25 operating a bicycle or roller skis so proceeding.
26SUMMARY
27 This bill allows a person operating a bicycle or roller skis to yield at a stop sign and, at 
28 a red light, to stop and then proceed if clear.27
