6 approved a major disaster declaration for the State of Maine for periods beginning on 
7 January 20, 2020 and continuing; and
8Whereas, on March 13, 2020, the President of the United States declared a national 
9 emergency concerning the coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) pandemic pursuant to 
10 Sections 201 and 301 of the National Emergencies Act and, on February 18, 2022, the 
11 President of the United States continued the national emergency pursuant to Section 202(d) 
12 of the National Emergencies Act; and
13Whereas, on January 31, 2020, the United States Secretary of Health and Human 
14 Services determined that a public health emergency exists and has existed since January 
15 27, 2020 nationwide and, on October 13, 2022, the United States Secretary of Health and 
16 Human Services renewed that determination; and
17Whereas, Maine consumers are experiencing increased energy prices across all fuel 
18 types and experiencing volatility; and
19Whereas, with nearly 60% of homes reliant on heating oil, Maine is the most heating 
20 oil dependent state in the country. Also, the New England electric grid, which supplies 
21 power to a majority of Maine, is over-reliant on natural gas to generate electricity. This 
22 makes Maine distinctly vulnerable to price increases; and
23Whereas, residential energy prices have increased significantly throughout the 
24 pandemic and high winter energy prices will exacerbate the ongoing effects of the 
25 pandemic and pose a threat to Maine's recovery from the pandemic; and
26Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
27 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
28 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
29 therefore,

34SUMMARY
35 This bill provides relief to certain Maine households to assist them with high costs for 
36 home heating.
37 Part A transfers funds totaling $71,000,000 from the unappropriated surplus of the 
38 General Fund to the Maine State Housing Authority no later than June 30, 2023. Of this 
39 amount, $40,000,000 is allocated for heating assistance to households in the State eligible 
40 for the federal Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program, $10,000,000 is allocated as 
41 emergency funds to households to avert an energy crisis and $21,000,000 is allocated for 1

44 Part B establishes the Winter Energy Relief Payment Program and transfers funds on 
45 a one-time basis totaling $398,000,000 from the unappropriated surplus of the General 
46 Fund to the newly created Winter Energy Relief Payment Program Fund Other Special 
47 Revenue Funds account no later than December 31, 2022 to fund the program.  The 
48 program provides relief payments of $450 to individuals who filed a Maine income tax 
49 return as of October 31, 2022 and who had income less than: $200,000, if filing as an 
50 individual filing a married joint return or a surviving spouse permitted to file a joint return; 
51 $150,000, if filing as a head of household; or $100,000, if filing as a single individual or as 
52 a married individual filing a separate return.
53 The State Tax Assessor is required to identify eligible recipients and make relief 
54 payments to those individuals no later than March 31, 2023.  A process is specified to allow 
55 individuals who are eligible but who have not received a relief payment to document 
56 eligibility to the State Tax Assessor.  The relief payment is not considered income for 
57 Maine income tax purposes.
58 Part C transfers $4,433,437 from the unappropriated surplus of the General Fund to the 
59 COVID Pandemic Relief Payment Program no later than June 30, 2023. Part C also 
60 transfers $385,754 from the COVID Disaster Relief Payment Fund Other Special Revenue 
61 Funds account and $6,674,993 from the Homestead Property Tax Exemption 
62 Reimbursement, Other Special Revenue Funds account to the COVID Pandemic Relief 
63 Payment Program. Part C provides the COVID Pandemic Relief Payment Program with an 
64 Other Special Revenue Funds allocation of $11,494,184 in fiscal year 2022-23 to complete 
65 the $850 pandemic relief payment to each eligible resident of the State as authorized in 
66 Public Law 2021, chapter 635, Part L. 
67 Part D includes General Fund deappropriations to the Department of Health and 
68 Human Services of $156,752,586 in fiscal year 2022-23 to adjust funding for the 6.2% 
69 increase in the Federal Medical Assistance Percentage based on the extension of the 
70 national public health emergency related to the COVID-19 pandemic for the first, 2nd and 
71 3rd quarter of fiscal year 2022-23. This adjustment also deallocates $4,154,555 from the 
72 Fund for a Healthy Maine in fiscal year 2022-23. The offsets for these deappropriations 
73 and deallocations are Federal Expenditures Fund allocations of $158,965,759 and Federal 
74 Block Grant Fund allocations of $1,941,382 in fiscal year 2022-23.
75 Part E allows the Commissioner of Environmental Protection to suspend the sulfur 
76 content requirements for liquid and solid fossil fuels if the commissioner finds that the 
77 expected availability of fuel that complies with those requirements is inadequate to meet 
78 the needs of residential, commercial or industrial users in this State and that such expected 
79 unavailability constitutes an immediate threat to public health, safety or the general 
80 welfare.  The commissioner is required to specify in writing the period during which the 
81 suspension is in effect.
82 Part F requires the temporary suspension of the provision of law that requires certain 
83 General Fund revenue and resources that exceed the General Fund appropriation limit to 
84 be transferred to the Maine Budget Stabilization Fund.
