2Sec. 1.  5 MRSA §4612, sub-§6, as amended by PL 1995, c. 462, Pt. A, §7, is further 
3 amended to read:
46.  Right to sue.  If, within 180 days of a complaint being filed with the commission, 
5 the commission has not filed a civil action in the case or has not entered into a conciliation 
6 agreement in the case, the complainant may request a right-to-sue letter, and, if a letter is 
7 given, the commission shall end its investigation.
8 On or after the 181st day of the date a complaint has been filed with the commission, the 
9 commission may issue a right-to-sue letter without a request from the complainant.
10Sec. 2.  5 MRSA §4622, sub-§1, as amended PL 2021, c. 366, §21, is further 
11 amended by enacting at the end a new last blocked paragraph to read:
12 If the commission issued a right-to-sue letter, the plaintiff may not be awarded attorney's 
13 fees, civil penal damages or compensatory and punitive damages unless the plaintiff 
14 establishes that the plaintiff received the right-to-sue letter before filing the civil action.
15SUMMARY
16 This bill allows the Maine Human Rights Commission to issue a right-to-sue letter to 
17 a complainant without a request from the complainant on the 181st day after the complaint 
18 was filed with the commission and makes a conforming change.16
