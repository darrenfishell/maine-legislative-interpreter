31Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
32 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the committee.
33Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of committee.  Resolved:  That, for 
34 appointments made under section 2, subsection 5, the appointing authorities shall make an 
35 effort to represent the racial, ethnic and gender diversity of the State.  All appointments 
36 must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this resolve.  The 
37 appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative Council once 
38 all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, the chairs shall 
39 call and convene the first meeting of the committee.  If 30 days or more after the effective 
40 date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been made, the chairs may

3Sec. 5.  Meetings.  Resolved:  That the committee is authorized to meet 6 times.
4Sec. 6.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the committee shall:
5 1.  Review the history and purpose of the Maine Criminal Justice Academy, the statutes 
6 that govern the Maine Criminal Justice Academy and its board of trustees and any 
7 educational credentials applicable to teachers and students at the Maine Criminal Justice 
8 Academy;
9 2.  Review how the current curriculum of the Maine Criminal Justice Academy is 
10 developed, revised, funded and delivered and, in comparison to other states, the benefits, 
11 challenges and effects of using volunteer or full-time personnel to provide education and 
12 training;
13 3.  Review any job task analysis completed by the Department of Public Safety that 
14 examines the role of rural and urban law enforcement officers in the State and examine the 
15 need for additional training resources, including a comparison of similar training protocols 
16 and delivery approaches in other states and the effect of different models on policing 
17 outcomes and on the quality of life of all residents and visitors;
18 4.  Review strategies, including changes to training and education requirements, to 
19 enhance recruitment for law enforcement employment from underrepresented 
20 communities, including ethnic minorities, women, new immigrants and communities of 
21 color;
22 5.  Review working relationships between the Maine Criminal Justice Academy and 
23 public and private institutions of higher education, opportunities for obtaining credentials 
24 and continuing education and sources for financial aid; 
25 6.  Seek input from municipal, county and state law enforcement agencies regarding 
26 law enforcement officer training at the Maine Criminal Justice Academy and whether 
27 increasing resources and staffing would improve recruitment and retention of staff and the 
28 quality of instruction and further the development of skills and leadership for candidates 
29 and staff; and
30 7.  Compile recommendations for improving the training of law enforcement officers 
31 through community and education partnerships, including by addressing statewide 
32 recruitment needs, racial justice and social issues, mental health and substance use disorder 
33 issues and any identified needs for additional requirements for law enforcement officers 
34 and candidates.
35Sec. 7.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
36 necessary staffing services to the committee, except that the Legislative Council staff 
37 support is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
38Sec. 8.  Report.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, no later than 
39 January 3, 2024, the committee shall submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on 
40 Criminal Justice and Public Safety that includes its recommendations, including suggested 
41 legislation.  After reviewing the report, the joint standing committee may submit legislation 
42 relating to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.1

Page 3 - 131LR2292(01)1SUMMARY
2 This bill establishes the 17-member Law Enforcement Training and Qualifications 
3 Study Committee to review law enforcement officer training.  The committee is authorized 
4 to meet 6 times and is required to report its recommendations for training for law 
5 enforcement officers in racial issues, racial justice and social issues and for additional 
6 requirements for law enforcement officers and candidates by January 3, 2024.  The Joint 
7 Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety is authorized to submit 
8 legislation relating to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.2
