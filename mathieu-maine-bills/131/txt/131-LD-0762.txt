21 a mausoleum, or by cremation. The remains of a human body after cremation may be 
22 deposited in a niche of a columbarium or a crypt of a mausoleum, buried, including by 
23 being buried in a biodegradable container, or disposed of in any manner not contrary to 
24 law. No  A deposit of the bodies or remains of the human dead  shall  may not be made in a 
25 single chamber, vault or tomb partly above and partly below the natural surface of the 
26 ground, unless the part thereof below such surface is of a permanent character, constructed 
27 of materials capable of withstanding extreme climatic conditions, waterproof and air tight, 
28 and capable of being sealed permanently to prevent all escape of effluvia, and unless the 
29 part thereof above the natural surface of the ground is constructed of natural stone of a 
30 standard not less than that required by the United States Government for monuments 
31 erected in national cemeteries, or durability sufficient to withstand all conditions of

24 Department of Professional and Financial Regulation shall update their rules in order to

26SUMMARY
27 This bill authorizes the burial of biodegradable containers, such as a casket or an urn, 
28 containing human remains or cremated remains, including tree pods, which are containers 
29 buried directly underneath the roots of a tree.1
