3SUBCHAPTER 7
4ELECTION FUNDING
5ยง871. Legislative approval
61. Short title. This section may be known and cited as "the State Oversight of Election 
7 Funding Act of 2023."
82. Definition. As used in this section, "new federal election funds" means any federal 
9 funds received on or after October 1, 2023 by a person, group or entity charged by the State 
10 to administer an official election occurring within the State.
113. Notice requirement. A person, group or entity charged by the State to administer 
12 an official election occurring within the State that intends to accept or disburse new federal 
13 election funds shall provide notice to the Legislature of its intent to do so at least 30 days 
14 before accepting or disbursing the funds. 
154. Prohibition on acceptance or disbursement of federal election funds without 
16state legislative approval. New federal election funds must be approved by the Legislature 
17 before they may be accepted or disbursed by a person, group or entity charged by the State 
18 to administer an official election occurring within the State unless explicitly required by 
19 state or federal law. 
205. Presumption of state legislative approval. It is presumed for purposes of 
21 compliance with this section that the Legislature has approved the acceptance and 
22 disbursement of new federal election funds if the Legislature fails to vote on the issue 
23 within 30 days from when notice is provided to the Legislature in accordance with 
24 subsection 3. 
256. Construction. This section may not be construed to pertain to funding that already 
26 possesses an explicit state or federal legal requirement related to its acceptance and 
27 disbursement as of October 1, 2023 or funding provided under the federal Help America 
28 Vote Act of 2002.
297. Penalties. A person, group or entity that violates this section commits a civil 
30 violation for which a fine in the amount of the new federal election funds accepted or 
31 disbursed in violation of this section plus an additional $1,000 may be adjudged.
32SUMMARY
33 This bill requires that new federal election funds be approved by the Legislature before 
34 they may be accepted or disbursed by a person, group or entity charged by the State to 
35 administer an official election occurring within the State unless explicitly required by state 
36 or federal law.33
