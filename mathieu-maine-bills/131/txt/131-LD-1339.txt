Page 1 - 131LR1749(01)1Preamble.  Whereas, one in 4 adults in Maine has a disability; and
2Whereas, the State has a significant role in supporting more inclusive state programs, 
3 communities and health care to help people with or at risk for disabilities to be well and 
4 active in their communities; and
5Whereas, the services for those with disabilities are dispersed throughout state 
6 departments, which can result in lack of effectiveness or utilization; and
7Whereas, other New England states such as Rhode Island and New Hampshire have 
8 successfully integrated their services into a commission on disabilities model to increase 
9 accessibility and transparency for their residents; now, therefore, be it
10Sec. 1.  Task force established.  Resolved:  That the Task Force to Study Forming 
11 the Maine Commission on Disabilities, referred to in this resolve as "the task force," is 
12 established.  
13Sec. 2.  Task force membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
14 353, the task force consists of 14 members as follows:
15 1.  One member of the Senate, appointed by the President of the Senate;
16 2.  One member of the House of Representatives, appointed by the Speaker of the 
17 House;
18 3.  One member representing a statewide organization of consumers of mental health 
19 services, appointed by the President of the Senate;
20 4.  One member representing an organization representing the deaf and hard-of-hearing 
21 community, appointed by the President of the Senate;
22 5.  One member representing an organization representing older adults, appointed by 
23 the President of the Senate;
24 6.  One member who is a municipal code enforcement officer, appointed by the 
25 President of the Senate;
26 7.  One member representing a statewide self-advocacy organization for people with 
27 developmental disabilities, appointed by the Speaker of the House;
28 8.  One member representing an organization representing the blind and visually 
29 impaired community, appointed by the Speaker of the House;
30 9.  One member representing an organization providing independent living services, 
31 appointed by the Speaker of the House;
32 10.  One member representing the Maine Statewide Independent Living Council 
33 administered by the Department of Labor, Bureau of Rehabilitation Services, appointed by 
34 the Executive Director of the Maine Statewide Independent Living Council;
35 11.  One member representing the Maine Municipal Association, appointed by the 
36 Executive Director of the Maine Municipal Association; 
37 12.  The State Fire Marshal or the State Fire Marshal's designee;
38 13.  The Commissioner of Labor or the commissioner's designee; and
39 14.  The Commissioner of Health and Human Services or the commissioner's designee.
Page 2 - 131LR1749(01)1Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the Senate member is the Senate chair and the 
2 House of Representatives member is the House chair of the task force.  
3Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of task force.  Resolved:  That all 
4 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
5 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
6 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
7 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the task force.  If 30 days or more after 
8 the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been made, the 
9 chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for the task 
10 force to meet and conduct its business.  
11Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the task force shall study the formation of a 
12 statewide commission on disabilities and the consolidation of services for persons with or 
13 at risk for disabilities.  The task force shall determine what services are currently provided 
14 by state agencies, including but not limited to the Department of Health and Human 
15 Services, the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife, the Department of Labor, the 
16 Maine State Library and the State Workforce Board, and how a commission on disabilities 
17 model is used in other states to combine those services for greater effectiveness and access.  
18Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
19 necessary staffing services to the task force, except that Legislative Council staff support 
20 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.  
21Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 6, 2023, the task force 
22 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
23 legislation, for presentation to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human 
24 Services.  The Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services is authorized to 
25 introduce legislation related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st 
26 Legislature.  
27SUMMARY
28 This resolve establishes the Task Force to Study Forming the Maine Commission on 
29 Disabilities.  The task force is directed to study the formation of a statewide commission 
30 on disabilities and the consolidation of services for persons with or at risk for disabilities.  
31 The task force must, no later than December 6, 2023, submit a report, including suggested 
32 legislation, for presentation to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human 
33 Services, which may introduce legislation related to the report to the Second Regular 
34 Session of the 131st Legislature.28
