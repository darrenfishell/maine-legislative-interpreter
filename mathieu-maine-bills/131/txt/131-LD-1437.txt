12Sec. 2.  Rules relating to home energy assistance programs.  The Department 
13 of Health and Human Services, the Maine State Housing Authority and the Efficiency 
14 Maine Trust shall amend their rules as necessary to ensure that the Home Energy Assistance 
15 Program and federal Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program resources are 
16 distributed in the most expedited manner possible.
17Sec. 3.  Rules relating to energy efficiency programs.  The Efficiency Maine 
18 Trust and the Maine State Housing Authority shall amend their rules as necessary to more 
19 fully address the needs of low-income individuals relating to energy efficiency programs, 
20 including, but not limited to, by amending their rules to remove the term "low-income" 
21 from home energy assistance program materials, add cooling pumps to references to 
22 heating pumps and authorize mobile homes built after 1976 to qualify for weatherization 
23 assistance programs. The Efficiency Maine Trust and the Maine State Housing Authority 
24 shall consult each other and the Maine Climate Council in the adoption of rules pursuant 
25 to this section. The Efficiency Maine Trust and the Maine State Housing Authority shall 
26 jointly submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and 
27 Technology no later than December 6, 2023 with recommendations, including suggested 
28 legislation, for any statutory changes needed to implement this section.
29Sec. 4.  Rules relating to energy efficiency and weatherization services.  The 
30 Efficiency Maine Trust and the Maine State Housing Authority shall amend their rules as 
31 necessary to prioritize programming that combines energy efficiency and weatherization 
32 services, including prioritizing the distribution of state funds, private funds and bond 
33 measures for that programming and requiring energy auditors to recommend sustainable 
34 heating solutions, including heating and cooling pumps.
35Sec. 5.  Rules relating to weatherization assistance programs and social 
36services.  The Efficiency Maine Trust and the Maine State Housing Authority shall amend 
37 their rules to combine weatherization assistance programs with social services, when 
38 reasonable and feasible, in circumstances that result in a net savings of state funds and that 
39 advance social equity.
