38.  Model residential mechanical ventilation systems codes.  Notwithstanding any 
4 provision of this chapter to the contrary, a municipality may not adopt a standard or code 
5 governing the design and installation of residential mechanical ventilation systems, beyond 
6 what may be specified in the Maine Uniform Building and Energy Code, other than the 
7 Canadian Standards Association Standard for Residential Mechanical Ventilation Systems 
8 F326-M91.  This subsection does not require a municipality to adopt a standard or code 
9 governing the design and installation of residential mechanical ventilation systems beyond 
10 what is specified in the Maine Uniform Building and Energy Code.  A standard or code 
11 governing the design and installation of residential mechanical ventilation systems adopted 
12 by a municipality prior to the effective date of this subsection is not invalidated by this 
13 subsection, but if the municipality replaces that standard or code it must adopt the Canadian 
14 Standards Association Standard for Residential Mechanical Ventilation Systems F326-
15 M91.
16SUMMARY
17 This bill prohibits a municipality from adopting standards for the design and 
18 installation of residential mechanical ventilation systems, beyond what may be specified in 
19 the Maine Uniform Building and Energy Code, other than the Canadian Standards 
20 Association's Standard for Residential Mechanical Ventilation Systems F326-M91.  A 
21 municipality is not required to adopt standards for residential mechanical ventilation 
22 systems but if it does, it must adopt the Canadian Standards Association standard.  The bill 
23 also provides that a standard or code governing the design and installation of residential 
24 mechanical ventilation systems adopted by a municipality prior to the effective date of this 
25 provision is not invalidated, but if the municipality replaces that standard or code it must 
26 adopt the CSA Standard for Residential Mechanical Ventilation Systems F326-M91.17
