12 3. One member appointed by the President of the Senate who is an advocate for or a 
13 professional working with indigent families;
14 4. One member appointed by the President of the Senate who is an advocate for or a 
15 professional working with homeless families;
16 5. One member appointed by the President of the Senate who is an advocate for or a 
17 professional working with victims of domestic violence;
18 6. One member appointed by the President of the Senate who is a professional working

20 7. One member appointed by the President of the Senate who is an advocate for or a 
21 professional working with children;
22 8. One member appointed by the Speaker of the House who is a professional working

24 9. One member appointed by the Speaker of the House who represents an organization

26 10. One member appointed by the Speaker of the House who represents an organization 
27 of therapists or psychologists;
28 11. One member appointed by the Speaker of the House who represents law 
29 enforcement; and
30 12. One member appointed by the Speaker of the House who represents a faith-based 
31 organization.
32Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
33 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
34 commission.
35Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
36 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
37 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
38 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
39 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
40 after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
Page 2 - 131LR1920(01)41 made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for 
42 the commission to meet and conduct its business.
3Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall study the impact of divorce, 
4 child support issues and custody issues on children, parents, health, poverty and housing 
5 insecurity.
6Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
7 necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff support 
8 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
9Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 6, 2023, the commission 
10 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
11 legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
12Sec. 8.  Outside funding.  Resolved:  That the commission shall seek funding 
13 contributions to fully fund the costs of the study.  All funding is subject to approval by the 
14 Legislative Council in accordance with its policies.  If sufficient contributions to fund the 
15 study have not been received within 30 days after the effective date of this resolve, no 
16 meetings are authorized and no expenses of any kind may be incurred or reimbursed.
17SUMMARY
