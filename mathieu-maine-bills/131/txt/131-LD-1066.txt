1410.  Consumption of bottles of wine not sold on the premises.  A restaurant, Class 
15 A restaurant or Class A restaurant/lounge licensed by the State to sell wine on the premises 
16 may give, serve or permit to be served a bottle of wine furnished by a person who has 
17 purchased a full meal to be consumed on the premises by that person or by another person 
18 within the same group as the person who furnished the wine and may charge a fee, not to 
19 exceed $100, for this service.  A restaurant, Class A restaurant or Class A restaurant/lounge 
20 may permit a person who furnished the bottle of wine to remove the partially consumed 
21 bottle of wine from the premises upon departure as long as the person is not visibly 
22 intoxicated as defined in section 2503, subsection 7 and the bottle of wine is removed and 
23 transported in a manner consistent with subsections 4 and 5, respectively.
24SUMMARY
25 This bill allows a restaurant, Class A restaurant or Class A restaurant/lounge licensed 
26 by the State to sell wine on the premises to allow a person who has purchased a full meal 
27 to provide a bottle of wine to consume on the premises. A restaurant, Class A restaurant or 
28 Class A restaurant/lounge may charge a fee, not to exceed $100.  The person may remove 
29 and transport the partially consumed bottle of wine in a manner consistent with the 
30 provisions of law regarding the removal and transport of a partially consumed bottle of 
31 wine purchased from a restaurant, Class A restaurant or Class A restaurant/lounge.25
