2Sec. 1.  20-A MRSA §8309 is enacted to read:
3§8309.  Report on career and technical education needs
4 Using the information in the report provided by the Department of Labor under Title 
5 26, section 1455, each year the department shall compare available career and technical 
6 education courses to the mapping of in-demand job titles and credentials and produce a 
7 report containing recommended modifications and expansions of career and technical 
8 education courses throughout the State, recommendations for budgetary and statutory

10 nonstate money available for accomplishing those modifications and expansions.  The 
11 department shall make the report publicly available and provide the report to the Governor 
12 and the Legislature.
13Sec. 2.  20-A MRSA §10911 is enacted to read:
14§10911.  Credit for career and technical education courses
151. Plan required. The trustees shall create a plan to award a standard amount of credit 
16 for certified career and technical education courses completed by students during secondary 
17 education.  The courses appropriate for certification must include trade and industry, 
18 technology, agriculture, business and marketing, family and consumer science, health 
19 occupational training or other vocational and technical fields.  In creating the plan, the 
20 trustees shall consult with university faculty governing bodies, Maine Community College 
21 System faculty, the commissioner, a representative of an organization of career and 
22 technical education professionals and other stakeholders the trustees consider necessary.  
232. Plan requirements. The plan created under subsection 1 must include:
24 A. For each course, standardized credit offerings and general curriculum requirements; 
25 and
26 B. The documentation that a student must submit in order to receive course credit.  
273. Credit awarding required. Following adoption of the plan, the university shall 
28 award course credit to a student who completes the course requirements and submits the 
29 required documentation.

12SUMMARY
13 This bill requires the Board of Trustees of the University of Maine System to create a 
14 plan to provide course credit for career and technical education courses.  It requires the 
15 Department of Labor to produce a report twice each year concerning existing and projected 
16 workforce needs and requires the Department of Education to use that report to recommend 
17 modifications and expansions to career and technical education courses.13
