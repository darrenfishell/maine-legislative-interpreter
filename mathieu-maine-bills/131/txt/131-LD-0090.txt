15Sec. 1.  12 MRSA §11164 is enacted to read:
16§11164.  Authority for persons 70 years of age or older to use subpermittee to harvest 
17deer
181.  Designation of subpermittee; permit.  A person 70 years of age or older who 
19 possesses a valid license to hunt may obtain a permit to designate a subpermittee to harvest 
20 deer on behalf of the permittee.
212.  Application procedure.  An applicant for a permit under this section shall file a 
22 written application for a permit on a form furnished by the commissioner.  The application 
23 must be accompanied by an application fee of $1.
243.  Subpermittees. An applicant for a permit under this section shall indicate on the 
25 application filed pursuant to subsection 2 the name of a subpermittee-designate who 
26 possesses a valid license to hunt.  Thirty days prior to the start of the applicable deer hunting 
27 season, the subpermittee-designate becomes a subpermittee.  The permittee may authorize 
28 the subpermittee to hunt deer with the permittee and to harvest deer on behalf of the 
29 permittee.  The permittee may choose not to authorize a subpermittee to hunt deer with the 
30 permittee and to harvest deer on the permittee’s behalf.
314.  Subpermittee bag limit.  The hunting or killing of deer pursuant to this section by 
32 a subpermittee does not count toward the subpermittee’s bag limit pursuant to section 
33 11501.
34Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
35 takes effect when approved.
36SUMMARY
37 This bill authorizes a licensed hunter 70 years of age or older to apply for a permit 
38 allowing the designation of a subpermittee to harvest deer on the permittee’s behalf. Acting 
39 as a designated subpermittee does not limit the subpermittee’s ability to hunt deer pursuant 
40 to the subpermittee's own license.37
