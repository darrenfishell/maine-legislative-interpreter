2Sec. 1.  State to negotiate with federally recognized Indian tribes for casino 
3license.  Notwithstanding the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 8, chapter 31, the State shall 
4 negotiate with any federally recognized Indian tribe in this State or any combination of 
5 federally recognized Indian tribes in this State that wishes to own and operate a casino on 
6 tribal land for a license to operate a casino.  The State also may negotiate with all of the 
7 federally recognized Indian tribes in this State for a license to operate a casino on land that 
8 is not owned by the tribes in a county other than Penobscot County or Oxford County.  The 
9 license to operate a casino on land that is not owned by the tribes would be in addition to 
10 licenses negotiated to operate casinos on tribal land.  The negotiations between the State 
11 and a federally recognized Indian tribe, combination of tribes or all of the tribes must 
12 include but are not limited to taxes and regulations governing the casino.
13SUMMARY
14 This bill requires the State to negotiate with any federally recognized Indian tribe in 
15 this State or any combination of federally recognized Indian tribes in this State that wishes 
16 to own and operate a casino on tribal land for a license to operate a casino.  The State also 
17 may negotiate with all of the federally recognized Indian tribes in this State for a license to 
18 operate a casino on land that is not owned by the tribes in a county other than Penobscot 
19 County or Oxford County.  The license to operate a casino on land that is not owned by the 
20 tribes would be in addition to licenses negotiated to operate casinos on tribal land.  The 
21 negotiations between the State and a federally recognized tribe, combination of tribes or all 
22 of the tribes must include but are not limited to taxes and regulations governing the casino.14
