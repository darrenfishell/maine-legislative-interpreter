Page 1 - 131LR0255(01)1Preamble.  Whereas, it is becoming more and more difficult to recruit and retain 
2 qualified law enforcement officers in the State; and
3Whereas, significant cost, time and effort are expended to sponsor cadet law 
4 enforcement officers at the Maine Criminal Justice Academy; and
5Whereas, it is not uncommon for law enforcement officers to leave their sponsoring 
6 agency for another agency after completing the basic law enforcement training at the Maine 
7 Criminal Justice Academy; and
8Whereas, at times cadets at the Maine Criminal Justice Academy suffer injuries that 
9 require workers' compensation claims or end in disability; now, therefore, be it
10Sec. 1. Law enforcement officer training study. Resolved: That the 
11 Commissioner of Public Safety shall conduct a study regarding the number during the last 
12 8 years of cadet law enforcement officer injuries that resulted in lost training time, cadet 
13 law enforcement officer injuries that resulted in workers' compensation claims or resulted 
14 in temporary or permanent disability claims and law enforcement officers who had their 
15 training costs paid by a sponsoring agency but left that agency within one year of 
16 completion of the basic law enforcement training at the Maine Criminal Justice Academy. 
17Sec. 2. Report. Resolved: That, by December 6, 2023, the Commissioner of Public 
18 Safety shall submit a report based on the study in section 1 for presentation to the Second 
19 Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.  The report must include but is not limited to:
20 1.  The number of cadet law enforcement officer injuries that resulted in lost training 
21 time during the last 8 years, reported by year;
22 2.  The number of cadet law enforcement officer injuries that resulted in workers' 
23 compensation claims or resulted in temporary or permanent disability claims during the 
24 last 8 years, reported by year; and
25 3.  The number of law enforcement officers who had their training costs paid by a 
26 sponsoring agency but left that agency within one year of completion of the basic law 
27 enforcement training at the Maine Criminal Justice Academy during the last 8 years, 
28 reported by year. 
29SUMMARY
30 This resolve directs the Commissioner of Public Safety to conduct a study regarding 
31 the number during the last 8 years of cadet law enforcement officer injuries that resulted in 
32 lost training time, cadet injuries that resulted in workers' compensation claims or resulted 
33 in temporary or permanent disability claims and law enforcement officers who had their 
34 training costs paid by a sponsoring agency but left that agency within one year of 
35 completion of the basic law enforcement training at the Maine Criminal Justice Academy.  
36 The commissioner is required to submit a report based on the study by December 6, 2023 
37 for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.30
