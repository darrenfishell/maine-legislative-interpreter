61.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
7 following terms have the following meanings.
8 A. "Closure plan monitor" means a person whose purpose is to ensure that a facility or 
9 hospital is following its closure or reduction plan under subsection 3 and that patient 
10 needs and preferences are being considered and honored whenever possible.
11 B. "Emergency department" means the unit of a hospital licensed in accordance with 
12 this chapter that provides 24-hour emergency medical care services to an unscheduled 
13 patient population.
14 C. "Facility" means a sanatorium, convalescent home, rest home, nursing home or 
15 similar institution.
16 D. "Hospital" means a hospital licensed in accordance with this chapter and any unit 
17 or facility of a hospital providing specialized care services, including, but not limited 
18 to, perinatal care services.
19 E. "Perinatal care services" means maternal and infant health improvement services 
20 and ancillary services that are appropriate for women and infants during the perinatal 
21 period.
22 F. "Perinatal period" means the period of time that begins before conception and ends 
23 on an infant's first birthday.
242.  Notice; timeline.  This subsection governs the required notice of a voluntary closing 
25 of a facility or hospital or reduction in services provided by the facility or hospital governed 
26 by this section.
27 A. Except as provided in paragraph B, a person, including a county or local government 
28 unit, that is licensed in accordance with this chapter to conduct, manage or operate a 
29 facility shall give to the patients and governmental units or institutions that are 
30 primarily responsible for the welfare of those patients at least 60 days' notice of the 
31 voluntary closing of the facility or reduction in services provided by the facility so that 
32 adequate preparation may be made for the orderly transfer of the patients to another 
33 qualified facility.
34 B. A person, including a county or local government unit, that is licensed in accordance 
35 with this chapter to conduct, manage or operate a hospital shall give the following 
36 notice of the voluntary closing of the hospital or reduction in services provided by the 
37 hospital so that adequate preparation may be made for the orderly transfer of the 
38 patients to another qualified hospital:
39 (1) To the department and the Emergency Medical Services' Board under Title 32, 
40 section 88, 180 days' notice; and
Page 2 - 131LR0389(01)1 (2) To the patients and governmental units or institutions that are primarily 
2 responsible for the welfare of those patients, including emergency services 
3 agencies that regularly serve the hospital, 135 days' notice.
43.  Notice; requirements.  Notice provided pursuant to subsection 2 must include the 
5 following information:
6 A. A credible root cause analysis of the factors contributing to the decision to close the 
7 facility or hospital or reduce the services provided by the facility or hospital, including:
8 (1) Interventions considered or implemented by the facility or hospital to prevent 
9 its closure or reduction in services;
10 (2) Analysis of why the interventions implemented were not effective;
11 (3) The rationale for a decision that identified interventions that were considered 
12 but not implemented; and
13 (4) Evidence of what the facility or hospital considered before reaching a decision 
14 regarding whether to contract with a closure plan monitor; and
15 B. A copy of the closure or reduction plan for the services provided by the facility or 
16 hospital that addresses the facility's or hospital's plan to:
17 (1) Make referrals to other facilities or hospitals or other providers in a manner that 
18 includes patient choice and preferences and continuity of care;
19 (2) Monitor and assist patients and their guardians, legal representatives and 
20 families with the psychosocial effect of the closure or reduction in services on 
21 patients;
22 (3) Transfer patients' medical records and pending tests in a manner that includes 
23 continuity of care and procedures to ensure no undue delay in patient care;
24 (4) Address the effect on inpatients relating to transportation to a new facility or 
25 hospital in a manner that promotes appropriate and timely continuity of care and 
26 minimizes the psychosocial effect of the transportation;
27 (5) Notify and coordinate with other facilities and hospitals and other providers 
28 that could be affected by the closure or reduction in services;
29 (6) Notify the public emergency services agencies that regularly serve the hospital 
30 and governmental units or institutions of the planned closure or reduction in 
31 services; and
32 (7) If the closure or reduction in services affects perinatal care services, address 
33 emergencies related to the perinatal period by developing policies and procedures 
34 regarding emergency perinatal care services and training for emergency 
35 department and family practice staff on emergency perinatal care services that 
36 incorporates teaching with examples and simulations.
