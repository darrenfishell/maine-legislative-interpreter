4§4203.  Critical incident stress management and peer team support services for public

61. Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
7 following terms have the following meanings.
8 A. "Affected person" means a firefighter or employee or member of a provider.
9 B. "Director" means the Director of the Maine Emergency Management Agency within 
10 the Department of Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management.
11 C. "Peer team" means a team of employees or members of a public safety agency, fire 
12 department or organization involved in emergency care or response that provides peer 
13 team support to affected persons and that is trained in accordance with standards 
14 established by rule by the director.
15 D.  "Peer team support" means the provision of behavioral health resources to an 
16 affected person by a peer team for the purpose of addressing issues resulting from stress 
17 and trauma associated with being an affected person but not necessarily related to a 
18 critical incident.
19 E.  "Provider" means a public safety agency, fire department or organization involved 
20 in emergency care or response in the State.
212.  Critical incident stress management team services.  The director shall ensure that 
22 critical incident stress management team services are available to all affected persons, 
23 including by:
24 A.  Supporting the establishment of a critical incident stress management team for 
25 every provider or through a collaboration of more than one provider;
26 B.  Providing critical incident stress management team services for every provider that 
27 does not have a critical incident stress management team;
28 C.  Establishing a program for critical incident stress management training for at least 
29 one firefighter in every fire department and at least one employee or member of every 
30 public safety agency or organization involved in emergency care or response; 
31 D.  Beginning July 1, 2024 and as part of preservice and in-service training under 
32 chapter 341, providing educational, training and promotional programs and materials, 
33 including, but not limited to, programs and materials related to trauma, to all affected 
34 persons to increase awareness of or provide support for critical incident stress 
35 management team services; and
36 E. Providing access to behavioral health care services for all affected persons 
37 immediately after a critical incident in which the affected person or provider has 
38 provided care, response or services.  
393.  Peer team support services.  The director shall ensure that peer team support 
40 services are available to all affected persons.1

36SUMMARY
37 This bill directs the Director of the Maine Emergency Management Agency within the 
38 Department of Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management to provide critical incident 
39 stress management team services and peer team support services to all firefighters and 
40 employees or members of a public safety agency, fire department or organization involved 
41 in emergency care or response in the State by supporting the establishment of critical 
42 incident stress management teams; providing critical incident stress management team 
43 services; establishing a program for critical incident stress management training; providing 37
