25SUMMARY
26 This bill strengthens the protections of the Maine Civil Rights Act by prohibiting a 
27 person from intentionally interfering or attempting to intentionally interfere with another 
28 person's exercise or enjoyment of that other person's civil rights by engaging in any course 
29 of conduct that would cause a reasonable person to suffer emotional distress or to fear 
30 violence.  Examples of behavior prohibited under the bill include repeatedly trespassing on 
31 another person's property to hang or burn a figure in effigy or to fumigate the other person's 
32 residential property with pesticides when such conduct is motivated by reason of race, 
33 color, religion, sex, ancestry, national origin, physical or mental disability, sexual 
34 orientation or gender identity.26
