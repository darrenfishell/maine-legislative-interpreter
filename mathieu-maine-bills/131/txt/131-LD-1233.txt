7SUMMARY
8 Current law authorizes, rather than requires, the Maine State Cemetery Preservation 
9 Commission to take certain actions that are identified as duties.  This bill requires the 
10 cemetery preservation commission to take those actions.  The bill adds to the duties of the 
11 cemetery preservation commission a requirement to investigate violations of the laws 
12 governing burying grounds and cemeteries and make recommendations related to those 
13 violations to the relevant municipal officials and the Attorney General.  The bill authorizes 
14 the cemetery preservation commission to hire staff, create a web page on the State's 
15 publicly accessible website, prepare educational materials for distribution to municipalities 
16 and statewide cemetery associations, establish a grant program under which communities 
17 may apply for a grant for the restoration of burying grounds and cemeteries and submit 
18 legislation at the start of each session regarding the care, protection and preservation of and 
19 access to burying grounds and cemeteries.  The bill also directs the Department of Health 
20 and Human Services to increase the fee for a burial-transit permit by $5, with the increase 
21 to be used to fund the operation of the Maine State Cemetery Preservation Commission.8
