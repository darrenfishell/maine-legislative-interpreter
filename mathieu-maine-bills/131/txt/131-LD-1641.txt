22 the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;

35Sec. 3.  Chairs; quorum. Resolved: That the first-named Senate member is the 
36 Senate chair and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of 
37 the commission.  A quorum is 9 members and a quorum must be present to start a meeting 
38 but is not required to continue a meeting.
Page 2 - 131LR2124(01)1Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission. Resolved: That all 
2 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
3 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
4 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
5 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
6 after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
7 made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for 
8 the commission to meet and conduct its business.
9Sec. 5.  Duties. Resolved:  That the commission shall review the report submitted 
10 to the Legislature by the Commission to Study the Role of Water as a Resource in the State 
11 of Maine pursuant to Resolve 2021, chapter 185.  The commission shall develop findings 
12 and recommendations, including any suggested legislation, to ensure that there is adequate 
13 clean, safe and accessible drinking water for the State's residents and to meet the needs of 
14 commercial and business interests now and in the future, which may include, but are not 
15 limited to, consideration of the following matters:
16 1.  Promoting public access to information about the State's water resources; 
17 consolidating and centralizing access to existing water use and water resources data; and 
18 enhancing or supplementing water use and water resources monitoring and data collection 
19 and the analysis of that data;
20 2.  Enhancing drought preparedness and drought resilience by agricultural producers 
21 and others;
22 3.  Reviewing the legal status of groundwater rights and ownership in Maine, including, 
23 but not limited to, further review of the Law Court's opinion in Maddocks v. Giles, 1999 
24 ME 63, 728 A.2d 150, and the approaches to this issue taken by other states;
25 4.  Protecting public and private drinking water sources from perfluoroalkyl and 
26 polyfluoroalkyl substances contamination and other contaminants; and
27 5.  Identifying any changes to state laws or rules regulating the large-scale extraction 
28 of groundwater for public or private use that may be necessary to ensure adequate supply 
29 for all users.
30Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
31 necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff support 
32 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
33Sec. 7.  Report. Resolved: That, no later than December 15, 2023, the commission 
34 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including any 
35 suggested legislation, to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction 
36 over taxation matters. After reviewing the report, the joint standing committee may report 
37 out legislation related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
38SUMMARY

40 the State of Maine to develop findings and recommendations, including any suggested 
41 legislation, to ensure that there is adequate clean, safe and accessible drinking water for the 
42 State's residents and to meet the needs of commercial and business interests now and in the 
43 future. This study is designed to build off the work of the Commission to Study the Role 39
