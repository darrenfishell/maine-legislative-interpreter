11 Amend the bill by inserting after the title and before the enacting clause the following: 
12 'Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
13 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and 
14 Whereas, climate change is making the availability of water more unpredictable for 
15 farmers, and it has been well documented that extreme weather patterns will continue; and 
16 Whereas, of the State's 4 most recent droughts, 3 of them have occurred in 2020, 
17 2021 and 2022, and the State's farmers face a very uncertain future when it comes to 
18 sufficient water for crops; and 
19 Whereas, this legislation makes necessary changes to immediately support farmers to 
20 move forward to develop sustainable water sources and invest in irrigation to mitigate risk 
21 of a changing climate and to help secure the State's food supply; and 
22 Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
23 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
24 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
25 therefore,'

35 emergency preamble and emergency clause to the bill. 
36 The amendment provides a one-time appropriation of $35,000 in fiscal year 2023-24 
37 in order for the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, Maine Land Use

This bill includes ongoing General Fund appropriations to the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry 
(AFC) of $154,085 in fiscal year 2023-24 and $215,772 in fiscal year 2024-25 for one Public Service Coordinator I 
position, one Environmental Specialist II position and associated costs. It includes a one-time General Fund
