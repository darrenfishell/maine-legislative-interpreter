Page 1 - 131LR1429(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, this legislation establishes the Task Force to Study Barriers to Achieving 
4 Behavioral Health Integration and Parity to study barriers imposed by insurers and 
5 experienced by providers in attempting to deliver behavioral health services; and
6Whereas, the study must be initiated before the 90-day period expires in order that 
7 the study may be completed and a report submitted in time for submission to the next 
8 legislative session; and
9Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
10 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
11 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
12 therefore, be it
13Sec. 1.  Task force established.  Resolved:  That the Task Force to Study Barriers 
14 to Achieving Behavioral Health Integration and Parity, referred to in this resolve as "the 
15 task force," is established.
16Sec. 2.  Task force membership. Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
17 353, the task force consists of 13 members appointed as follows:
18 1.  Two members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including a 
19 member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
20 2.  Three members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
21 House, including members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
22 in the Legislature;
23 3.  Two members representing entities knowledgeable about or involved in providing 
24 behavioral health services, appointed by the President of the Senate;
25 4.  Three members who are providers of behavioral health services and represent a 
26 small group practice, a federally qualified health center and a large group practice, 
27 appointed by the Speaker of the House; and
28 5.  Three members representing entities knowledgeable about or involved in insurance 
29 coverage for behavioral health treatment, including at least one member from the 
30 Department of Professional and Financial Regulation, Bureau of Insurance, appointed by 
31 the President of the Senate.
32Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
33 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the task force.
34Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of task force.  Resolved:  That all 
35 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
36 resolve. The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
37 Council once all appointments have been completed. After appointment of all members, 
38 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the task force. If 30 days or more after 
39 the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been made, the 
40 chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for the task 
41 force to meet and conduct its business.
Page 2 - 131LR1429(01)1Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the task force shall study barriers imposed by 
2 insurers and experienced by providers in attempting to deliver behavioral health services 
3 and develop a plan, including specific legislative recommendations, to address and remove 
4 those barriers, allowing for better integrated care and parity in behavioral health treatment. 
5Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
6 necessary staffing services to the task force, except that Legislative Council staff support 
7 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
8Sec. 7.  Reports.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, no later than 
9 December 6, 2023, the task force shall submit a report that includes findings and 
10 recommendations, including suggested legislation, for presentation to the Joint Standing 
11 Committee on Health and Human Services and the Joint Standing Committee on Health 
12 Coverage, Insurance and Financial Services. The committees may report out legislation to 
13 the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
14Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
15 takes effect when approved.
16SUMMARY
17 This resolve establishes the Task Force to Study Barriers to Achieving Behavioral 
18 Health Integration and Parity. The task force is directed to submit a report and any 
19 suggested legislation for presentation to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and 
20 Human Services and the Joint Standing Committee on Health Coverage, Insurance and 
21 Financial Services by December 6, 2023. The committees are authorized to report out 
22 legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.17
