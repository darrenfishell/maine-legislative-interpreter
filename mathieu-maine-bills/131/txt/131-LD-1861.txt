5ยง5171. Chronically absent students
61. Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
7 following terms having the following meanings.
8 A. "Absence" means an excused absence, unexcused absence or disciplinary absence, 
9 as those terms are defined by the state board by rule.
10 B. "Chronically absent student" means a student who is enrolled in a school under the 
11 jurisdiction of a school administrative unit and whose total number of absences at any 
12 time during a school year is equal to or greater than 10% of the total number of days 
13 that that student has been enrolled at that school during that school year.
14 C. "School chronic absenteeism rate" means the total number of chronically absent 
15 students for a school in the previous school year divided by the total number of students 
16 enrolled in that school for that school year.
17 D. "Unit chronic absenteeism rate" means the total number of chronically absent 
18 students under the jurisdiction of a school administrative unit in the previous school 
19 year divided by the total number of students under the jurisdiction of that unit for that 
20 school year.
212. School administrative unit. A school administrative unit that:
22 A. Has a unit chronic absenteeism rate of 10% or higher shall establish an attendance 
23 review team under subsection 3 or establish a committee to review chronic absence for 
24 the school administrative unit;
25 B. Has under its jurisdiction a school with a school chronic absenteeism rate of 15% or 
26 higher shall establish an attendance review team under subsection 3 at that school;
27 C. Has under its jurisdiction more than one school with a school chronic absenteeism 
28 rate of 15% or higher shall establish an attendance review team under subsection 3 for 
29 the school administrative unit or at each such school; or
30 D. Has a unit chronic absenteeism rate of 10% or higher and has one or more schools 
31 under its jurisdiction with a school chronic absenteeism rate of 15% or higher shall 
32 establish an attendance review team under subsection 3 for the school administrative 
33 unit or at each such school.
343. Attendance review teams. An attendance review team established under this 
35 subsection may consist of school administrators, guidance counselors, school counselors, 
36 school social workers, teachers and representatives from community-based programs who 
37 address issues related to student attendance by providing programs and services to students 
38 who are truant and chronically absent students and their parents or guardians. An 
39 attendance review team is responsible for reviewing the cases of students who are truant

7SUMMARY
8 This bill requires school administrative units to establish attendance review teams or 
9 committees to address chronic absenteeism if chronic absenteeism rates exceed certain 
10 levels. An attendance review team may consist of school administrators, guidance 
11 counselors, school counselors, school social workers, teachers and representatives from 
12 community-based programs who address issues related to student attendance by providing 
13 programs and services to students who are truant and chronically absent students and their 
14 parents or guardians.1
