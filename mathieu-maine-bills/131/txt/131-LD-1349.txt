7§1809.  Commission to Review State-owned Lands and Waterways
81.  Established.  The Commission to Review State-owned Lands and Waterways, 
9 referred to in this section as "the commission," is established.
102.  Members.  The members of the commission are as follows:
11 A.  One member representing the bureau, appointed by the commissioner;
12 B.  One member representing the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife, 
13 appointed by the Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife;
14 C.  One member representing the Department of Marine Resources, appointed by the 
15 Commissioner of Marine Resources;
16 D.  One member representing the Maine Indian Tribal-State Commission established 
17 in Title 30, section 6212, subsection 1, appointed by the chair of the commission;
18 E.  One member representing the Passamaquoddy Tribe, appointed by the 
19 Passamaquoddy Tribe;
20 F.  One member representing the Penobscot Nation, appointed by the Penobscot 
21 Nation;
22 G.  One member representing the Houlton Band of Maliseet Indians, appointed by the 
23 Houlton Band of Maliseet Indians; 
24 H.  One member representing the Mi'kmaq Nation, appointed by the Mi'kmaq Nation; 
25 and
26 I.  Three members of the public, one each appointed by the Governor, the President of 
27 the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives.
283.  Duties.  The commission shall:
29 A.  Identify which state-owned lands or waterways have sacred, traditional or other 
30 significance to the Passamaquoddy Tribe, Penobscot Nation, Houlton Band of Maliseet 
31 Indians or Mi'kmaq Nation;
32 B.  Examine the processes used in other states and by the Federal Government to return 
33 lands and waterways to tribes and develop a process to return lands and waterways to 
34 the Passamaquoddy Tribe, Penobscot Nation, Houlton Band of Maliseet Indians and 
35 Mi'kmaq Nation.  The commission also shall identify resources to fund the cost to 
36 transfer these lands and waterways to the Passamaquoddy Tribe, Penobscot Nation, 
37 Houlton Band of Maliseet Indians and Mi'kmaq Nation;
38 C.  Examine the process used by the Federal Government to designate lands and 
39 waterways as federal protected areas; and4

2 additional members who should be added to the commission.
34.  Meetings.  The commission shall hold public meetings and hearings throughout the 
4 State and shall accept testimony and written comments from the public in a manner 
5 determined by the commission.  The commission shall invite the participation of 
6 stakeholders as determined by the commission and also invite the participation of large 
7 private landowners, land trusts and conservation organizations.
85.  Confidentiality.  The commission may designate as confidential records pertaining 
9 to state-owned lands or waterways described in subsection 3, paragraph A.
106.  Report.  Beginning in 2024, the commission shall submit a report no later than 
11 November 15th of each year to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
12 jurisdiction over conservation matters, the joint standing committee of the Legislature 
13 having jurisdiction over inland fisheries and wildlife matters and the joint standing 
14 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over marine resources matters.  The joint 
15 standing committees may report out legislation related to the report of the commission.
16SUMMARY
17 This bill establishes the Commission to Review State-owned Lands and Waterways to 
18 identify which state-owned lands or waterways have sacred, traditional or other 
19 significance to the Passamaquoddy Tribe, Penobscot Nation, Houlton Band of Maliseet 
20 Indians or Mi'kmaq Nation and develop a process to return lands and waterways to the 
21 Passamaquoddy Tribe, Penobscot Nation, Houlton Band of Maliseet Indians and Mi'kmaq 
22 Nation.  The commission must hold public meetings and hearings throughout the State and 
23 must accept testimony and written comments from the public in a manner determined by 
24 the commission.  The commission must invite the participation of stakeholders as 
25 determined by the commission and also invite the participation of large private landowners, 
26 land trusts and conservation organizations.  Beginning in 2024, the commission must 
27 submit a report no later than November 15th of each year to the joint standing committee 
28 of the Legislature having jurisdiction over conservation matters, the joint standing 
29 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over inland fisheries and wildlife matters 
30 and the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over marine 
31 resources matters.  The joint standing committees may report out legislation related to the 
32 report of the commission.17
