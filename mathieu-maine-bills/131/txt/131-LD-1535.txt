4ยง454.  Maine Criminal Justice Sentencing Institute
51.  Institute established; purpose.  There is established the Maine Criminal Justice 
6 Sentencing Institute, referred to in this section as "the institute," under the administrative 
7 supervision of the State Court Administrator. The purpose of the institute is to provide a 
8 continuing forum for the regular discussion of the most appropriate methods of sentencing 
9 convicted offenders and adjudicated juveniles.
102.  Membership.  All Supreme Judicial Court, Superior Court and District Court 
11 Justices and Judges, all District Attorneys and all attorneys within the Criminal Division of 
12 the Office of the Attorney General are considered members of the institute.  In addition, 
13 the Chief Justice of the Supreme Judicial Court shall invite, at a minimum, as members of 
14 the institute, law enforcement and correctional personnel, representatives of advisory and 
15 advocacy groups, including persons with expertise in sentencing reform and restorative 
16 justice and with experience in substance use disorder counseling, and representatives of the 
17 defense bar.
183.  Meetings; notice; public participation.  When sufficient funding is allocated by 
19 the Legislature, but no less often than once every 2 years, the institute shall meet, at the call 
20 of the Chief Justice of the Supreme Judicial Court, for a 2-day period to discuss 
21 recommendations for changes in the sentencing authority and policies of the State's 
22 criminal and juvenile courts, in response to law enforcement problems and the available 
23 alternatives for criminal and juvenile rehabilitation within the State's correctional system. 
24 The Chief Justice shall post on the publicly accessible website of the judicial branch notice 
25 of the meeting, including the date, time and place of the meeting and the agenda, no later 
26 than 2 weeks prior to the meeting.  The meeting must be open to the public, and the Chief 
27 Justice shall allow participation and input by the public.
284.  Compensation.  Members of the institute are not entitled to receive compensation 
29 for their services but are allowed, out of any appropriation or other fund made available for 
30 the purpose, reimbursement for expenses for clerical and other services, travel and 
31 incidentals as the Chief Justice of the Supreme Judicial Court may authorize.
325.  Biennial report.  Beginning January 15, 2024 and every 2 years thereafter, the 
33 Chief Justice of the Supreme Judicial Court shall submit a report to the joint standing 
34 committees of the Legislature having jurisdiction over judiciary matters and over criminal 
35 justice and public safety matters detailing, at a minimum, the membership of the institute, 
36 the matters discussed at the most recent meeting of the institute and any recommendations 
37 of the institute regarding matters considered by the institute.  The joint standing committees 
38 may report out legislation based on the report.

3SUMMARY
4 This bill implements the recommendations of the Commission to Examine 
5 Reestablishing Parole regarding the Maine Criminal Justice Sentencing Institute by:
10 1.  Modifying the membership to require that the Chief Justice of the Supreme Judicial 
11 Court invite, as members, representatives of advisory and advocacy groups, including 
12 persons with expertise in sentencing reform and restorative justice and experience in 
13 substance use disorder counseling;
14 2. Requiring that the institute meet at least once every 2 years;
15 3.  Requiring that a meeting of the institute be advertised on the publicly accessible 
16 website of the judicial branch at least 2 weeks prior to the meeting. The notice must include 
17 the time, date and place of the meeting as well as the agenda;
18 4.  Requiring that the meeting be open to the public, with public participation and input 
19 allowed; 
20 5.  Requiring the Chief Justice to submit a report to the joint standing committees of 
21 the Legislature having jurisdiction over judiciary matters and over criminal justice and 
22 public safety matters detailing, at a minimum, the membership of the institute, the matters 
23 discussed at the most recent meeting of the institute and any recommendations of the 
24 institute regarding matters considered by the institute.  The joint standing committees are 
25 authorized to report out legislation based on the report; and
26 6.  Making technical changes to the statute for readability and clarity.
27 This bill also provides for ongoing funding for the institute to allow it to meet 
28 biennially.3
