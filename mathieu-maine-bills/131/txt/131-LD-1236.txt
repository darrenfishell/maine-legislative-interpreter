13Sec. 2.  Department of Health and Human Services to increase availability 
14of children's behavioral health services in rural areas.  The Department of Health 
15 and Human Services, referred to in this section as "the department," shall increase the 
16 availability of children's behavioral health services for families involved in the child 
17 welfare system.  The department shall do the following. 
18 1.  No later than January 1, 2024, the department shall amend its rules in Chapter 101:  
19 MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter III, Sections 28 and 65 to establish reimbursement 
20 rates for providers of behavioral health services to children in families in rural areas 
21 involved in the child welfare system.  The rural reimbursement rate must be sufficient to 
22 allow for the additional travel time required to serve children in rural areas.
23 2.  The department shall offer grants or other incentives to children's behavioral health 
24 providers to expand programs into rural areas and serve children in families involved in the 
25 child welfare system.
26 3.  The department shall establish a method of reimbursing providers of services to 
27 families involved in the child welfare system to meet together and coordinate services for 
28 each family.
29Sec. 3.  Family team meetings.  The Department of Health and Human Services 
30 shall fully implement the family team meetings plan that is established in the Maine Family 
31 First Prevention Services State Plan.  The department shall develop training for child 
32 welfare staff to act as neutral facilitators.  The department shall ensure that all providers of 
33 services to families involved in the child welfare system participate in family team 
34 meetings.
35SUMMARY
36 This bill requires the Department of Health and Human Services to expand children's 
37 behavioral health services for children in families involved in the child welfare system in 
38 rural areas.  It requires the department to amend its rule in Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits 
39 Manual, Chapter III, Sections 28 and 65 to establish a rural reimbursement rate that 
40 includes travel time for providers to rural areas to provide services.  It requires the 
41 department to offer grants or other incentives to existing providers to expand services into 36
