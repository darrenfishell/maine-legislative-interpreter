3ยง458.  Courthouse facility dogs
41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5 following terms have the following meanings.
6 A.  "Assistance dog organization" means an organization that is a member of an 
7 internationally recognized assistance dog association that has the primary purpose of 
8 granting accreditation for assistance dog acquisition, dog training, dog handler training 
9 and dog placement.
10 B.  "Child advocacy center" has the same meaning as in Title 22, section 4019, 
11 subsection 1, paragraph B.
12 C.  "Courthouse facility dog" means a dog that meets the requirements of subsection 2 
13 and is handled to do specific, skilled tasks to provide support to a vulnerable witness 
14 in the physical spaces of a criminal justice agency.
15 D.  "Courthouse facility dog handler" means a person who meets the requirements of 
16 subsection 3 and who handles a courthouse facility dog to provide a calming influence 
17 on a vulnerable witness during the investigation and prosecution of a crime or other 
18 legal proceeding and to provide a comforting presence in those situations.
19 E.  "Courthouse facility dog team" means a paired courthouse facility dog and 
20 courthouse facility dog handler that meets the requirements of subsection 4.
21 F.  "Criminal justice agency" means a state or local agency that performs the 
22 administration of criminal justice, including a court, a law enforcement agency, the 
23 Department of the Attorney General, a district attorney's office and a child advocacy 
24 center.
25 G.  "Independent professional dog trainer" means a private dog trainer who provides 
26 dog training and dog handler training and who has been approved to be an evaluator of 
27 a nationally recognized dog obedience program, which may include the American 
28 Kennel Club Canine Good Citizen, Community Canine and Urban Canine Good 
29 Citizen programs.
302.  Requirements for courthouse facility dog.  In order to be a courthouse facility 
31 dog, a dog must be at least 2 years of age and:
32 A.  Have graduated from an assistance dog organization training program; or
33 B.  Be certified by an independent professional dog trainer as:
34 (1)  Having completed at least 100 hours of individualized training focused on 
35 obedience and the skills needed for providing quiet companionship to a vulnerable 
36 witness in a legal setting without causing a disruption in a legal proceeding and 
37 demonstrating competence in those skills, including having a calm demeanor, 
38 being able to work in a high-stress environment and being able to sit or lie down 
39 beside a witness for a minimum of 90 minutes; and
Page 2 - 131LR0755(01)1 (2)  Having successfully completed 3 nationally recognized dog obedience 
2 programs, which may include the American Kennel Club Canine Good Citizen, 
3 Community Canine and Urban Canine Good Citizen programs.
43.  Requirements for courthouse facility dog handler.  In order to be a courthouse 
5 facility dog handler, an individual must be a professional working in the criminal justice 
6 system.
74.  Requirements for courthouse facility dog team.  In order to be a courthouse 
8 facility dog team, a courthouse facility dog handler and the courthouse facility dog that is 
9 part of the team must:
10 A.  Have completed a minimum of 40 hours of training together as a team in addition 
11 to the training for the dog required by subsection 2 in a training program run by an 
12 assistance dog organization or with an independent professional dog trainer in skills 
13 related to working in public spaces with a wide variety of people and distractions; and
14 B.  Have demonstrated by means of graduating from a training program run by an 
15 assistance dog organization or by means of certification by an independent professional 
16 dog trainer that the courthouse facility dog team has successfully completed 3 
17 nationally recognized dog obedience programs, which may include the American 
18 Kennel Club Canine Good Citizen, Community Canine and Urban Canine Good 
19 Citizen programs.
205.  Access to criminal justice agency premises.  Except as provided in subsection 6, 
21 a criminal justice agency may not exclude from the publicly accessible areas of the agency's 
22 premises a courthouse facility dog team or a dog in training to be a courthouse facility dog 
23 that is in the presence of a trainer affiliated with an assistance dog organization or an 
24 independent professional dog trainer.
256.  Access to courtrooms during proceedings.  A courthouse facility dog team may 
26 have access to a courtroom during a legal proceeding if authorized by the judicial officer 
27 presiding over that proceeding. Either party in a proceeding may request an order from the 
28 court to allow a courthouse facility dog to be present with a vulnerable witness, regardless 
29 of whether that witness's testimony is in person or by means of 2-way closed-circuit 
30 television. The presiding judicial officer may issue the requested order if the presiding 
31 judicial officer determines that the courthouse facility dog will aid the vulnerable witness 
32 in providing testimony and that aid is consistent with the rules of court. The order may 
33 include provisions to preserve the fairness of the proceedings, including by imposing 
34 restrictions on and instructing the jury regarding the presence of the courthouse facility 
35 dog.
36SUMMARY
37 This bill provides a system to allow a trained dog to accompany a witness in courtroom 
38 appearances and other interactions where criminal justice is administered.37
