31SUBCHAPTER 3-D
32TRANSITIONAL HOUSING COMMUNITIES
33§4768. Transitional Housing Community Construction Program and Fund
341. Definitions.  As used in this subchapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
35 following terms have the following meanings.
36 A. "Dwelling unit" has the same meaning as in Title 12, section 682, subsection 11.
37 B. "Transitional housing" means housing designed to provide a homeless individual or 
38 family with interim stability and support to successfully move to and maintain 
Page 2 - 131LR1022(01)39 permanent housing. Transitional housing is typically used for up to 24 months and 
40 generally includes accompanying support services.
3 C. "Transitional housing community" means more than one transitional housing 
4 dwelling unit located collectively in one building or in multiple buildings on one site 
5 with community space.
62. Program established.  The Transitional Housing Community Construction 
7 Program, referred to in this section as "the program," is established within the Maine State 
8 Housing Authority. The goals of the program are to create transitional housing 
9 communities to reduce state and municipal costs and reliance on hotels, motels and other 
10 transient lodging accommodations to provide temporary housing to homeless populations, 
11 to provide stability for homeless individuals and families and to create efficiencies for 
12 service providers in the provision of services to the homeless populations.
133. Program activities.  The activities of the program include providing short-term or 
14 long-term assistance in the form of:
15 A. Grants or loans to private entities to incentivize and assist with the development and 
16 construction of transitional housing communities;
17 B. Creating transitional housing communities;
18 C. Purchasing dwelling units to be used as transitional housing in transitional housing 
19 communities;
20 D. Purchasing land on which to locate transitional housing communities;
21 E. Purchasing buildings in which to locate transitional housing dwelling units or 
22 community space associated with a transitional housing community;
23 F. Conducting site work in preparation for a transitional housing community; or
24 G. Purchasing furniture, fixtures or equipment for transitional housing.
254. Fund established.  The Transitional Housing Community Construction Program 
26 Fund, referred to in this section as "the fund," is established and administered by the Maine 
27 State Housing Authority. The purpose of the fund is to support the Transitional Housing 
28 Community Construction Program. The fund consists of appropriations and allocations and 
29 grants, donations and revenue from other public and private sources. Amounts in the fund 
30 may be invested, and interest earned by the investments is credited to the fund.  Funds in 
31 the fund do not lapse but must be carried forward to the next fiscal year. The Maine State 
32 Housing Authority may expend funds in the fund for reasonable costs in administering the 
33 fund.
345. Reporting.  Beginning February 1, 2024 and quarterly thereafter, the Maine State 
35 Housing Authority shall submit a report of expenditures from the fund and the goals and 
36 achievements of the program to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
37 jurisdiction over housing matters.

Page 5 - 131LR1022(01)1SUMMARY
2 This bill creates the Transitional Housing Community Construction Program Fund 
3 under the Maine State Housing Authority to create transitional housing communities, 
4 which are clusters of transitional housing dwelling units for homeless individuals and 
5 families to provide stability and to allow the individuals and families to transition to 
6 permanent housing.  The bill directs that transitional housing communities be created 
7 throughout the State designed to cover all 16 counties. The bill directs the Department of 
8 Health and Human Services, through independent housing services under its administration 
9 of assisted housing programs, to coordinate, arrange or provide services including job 
10 training and health care services for residents of transitional housing communities. The bill 
11 also provides one-time funding to the Maine State Housing Authority to create transitional 
12 housing communities and funding to the department to provide independent housing 
13 services to residents of transitional housing.1
