41.  Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5 following terms have the following meanings.
6 A. "Commercial agricultural production" has the same meaning as in section 2013, 
7 subsection 1, paragraph A.
8 B.  "Commercial aquacultural production" has the same meaning as in section 2013, 
9 subsection 1, paragraph A-1.
10 C.  "Commercial fishing" has the same meaning as in section 2013, subsection 1, 
11 paragraph B.
12 D.  "Credit base" means the amount claimed as a deduction by the taxpayer under 
13 Section 170 of the Code for the tax year for charitable contributions of agricultural, 
14 aquacultural or fishing products intended for human consumption to a qualified donee.
15 E.  "Low-income individual" means an individual who is a member of a lower income 
16 household as defined in Title 30-A, section 5002, subsection 11 or who is homeless as 
17 defined in Title 30-A, section 5002, subsection 6.
18 F.  "Qualified donee" means an organization described in the Code, Section 170(c)(2) 
19 or (3) that is located in this State and that provides food to low-income individuals in 
20 this State without charging a fee for the food or requiring any other type of 
21 compensation from low-income individuals.
222. Credit.  For tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2024, a person engaged in 
23 commercial agricultural production, commercial aquacultural production or commercial 
24 fishing may claim a credit against the taxes imposed by this Part equal to 10% of the credit 
25 base.  The credit under this section may not exceed $5,000 for a taxable year.  A person 
26 applying for a credit under this section must submit with the application a written statement 
27 from the qualified donee that the products donated will be used by the qualified donee for 
28 charitable purposes and for the care of individuals who are ill, needy or malnourished.
29SUMMARY
30 This bill provides an income tax credit up to $5,000 for persons engaged in commercial 
31 agricultural production, commercial aquacultural production or commercial fishing for 
32 donations to a qualified organization that provides food to low-income individuals without 
33 charging a fee for the food or requiring any other type of compensation from the low-
34 income individuals.30
