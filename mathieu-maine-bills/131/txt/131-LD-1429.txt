5ยง4087-E.  Resource family navigator
6 The resource family navigator, referred to in this subchapter as "the navigator," is 
7 established as an independent navigator within the department who shall provide resources 
8 and information to resource families.  The navigator shall consider and promote the best 
9 interests of the child and assist resource families and children in the child welfare system 
10 receiving services from the department.
111.  Term.  The commissioner shall appoint one person as the navigator to administer 
12 the navigator program for a 6-year term.  The navigator may only be removed or dismissed 
13 for cause.  The navigator may hire additional personnel if resources are available.
142.  Services.  The navigator may provide the following services for resource families 
15 and children in the child welfare system who request assistance or make inquiries of the 
16 navigator:
17 A.  Identify and coordinate existing resources;
18 B.  Advocate within the department for assistance and to solve problems; and
19 C.  Provide referrals to available resources and information about the means of 
20 obtaining services from the department.
21 The navigator shall develop collaborative working relationships within offices within the 
22 department, social service agencies and other organizations providing services.
233.  Access to records.  As necessary for carrying out the navigator's duties, the 
24 department shall provide access to the files and records of the department to the navigator 
25 for the purposes of providing the services in subsection 2.  The navigator shall maintain the 
26 confidentiality of all information and records obtained under this subsection.
274.  Confidentiality of records.  Information held by and records and case-specific 
28 reports maintained by the navigator are confidential.  Disclosure may be made as allowed 
29 or required in accordance with the provisions of section 4008, subsections 2 and 3.  
30 Unlawful dissemination is subject to the provisions of section 4008, subsection 4.
31Sec. 2. Report.  The resource family navigator established in the Maine Revised 
32 Statutes, Title 22, section 4087-E shall, no later than December 6, 2023, submit a report to 
33 the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services with the number of people 
34 served by the resource family navigator and a description of the issues addressed by the 
35 navigator.  The committee may report out legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 
36 131st Legislature relating to the report.
Page 2 - 131LR0683(01)1SUMMARY
2 This bill establishes a navigator position within the Department of Health and Human 
3 Services to provide assistance to resource families and foster families caring for children 
4 involved in the child welfare system.  The position is appointed by the Commissioner of 
5 Health and Human Services for a 6-year term and may be dismissed only for cause.  The 
6 navigator provides assistance to resource families, foster parents and children who request 
7 assistance with identifying resources, advocating within the department and agencies 
8 providing resources and providing referrals. The navigator must be given access to 
9 department records and files for the purposes of providing services; those records remain 
10 confidential and may be disclosed only in accordance with existing law.
11 The bill also requires the navigator to report to the Joint Standing Committee on Health 
12 and Human Services and authorizes the committee to report out legislation related to the 
13 report.2
