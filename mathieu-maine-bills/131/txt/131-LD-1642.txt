8§13.  Wabanaki Studies Commission
9 The Wabanaki Studies Commission, referred to in this section as "the commission," is 
10 established to ensure the inclusion of Wabanaki studies in education curricula as required 
11 by the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, section 4706, subsection 4.
121. Membership.  The commission consists of the following 19 members:
13 A. Two members appointed by the Chief of the Penobscot Nation;
14 B. Two members appointed by the Chief of the Houlton Band of Maliseet Indians;
15 C. Two members appointed by the Chief of the Mi'kmaq Nation;
16 D. Two members appointed by the Chief of the Passamaquoddy Tribe at Sipayik;
17 E. Two members appointed by the Chief of the Passamaquoddy Tribe at 
18 Motahkomikuk;
19 F. One member appointed by the Maine Indian Tribal-State Commission;
20 G. Seven members appointed by the Commissioner of Education, including an 
21 elementary school teacher; a middle school teacher; a high school teacher; a curriculum 
22 director; a superintendent or principal; and an employee of a nationally accredited or 
23 state-approved teacher education program, after consultation with deans, directors and 
24 chairs of those programs; and
25 H. One member appointed by the Chancellor of the University of Maine System.
262. Terms; vacancies.  The terms of office of commission members are as follows.  
27 A. All members are appointed for 3-year terms.
28 B. Members shall serve until their successors are appointed and take office.
29 C. Vacancies must be filled in the same manner as original appointments, except that 
30 any person appointed to fill a vacancy shall serve for the remainder of the unexpired 
31 term of the vacancy.
323.  Compensation. The members of the commission are entitled to receive legislative 
33 per diem plus compensation for travel costs associated with participation in commission 
34 meetings and other activities of the commission.
354.  Duties.  The commission shall:
36 A.  Assist school administrative units and educators in the exploration of a wide range 
37 of educational materials and resources relating to Wabanaki studies;
38 B.  Identify materials and resources for implementing Wabanaki studies;4

16SUMMARY
17 This bill establishes the Wabanaki Studies Commission to ensure the inclusion of 
18 Wabanaki studies in education curricula.  It amends the statutes relating to required courses 
19 about Wabanaki studies, including amending provisions relating to the substance of the 
20 courses and the requirement that the courses be funded.  It directs the Department of 
21 Education to establish and fund teacher education programs in Wabanaki studies.  It directs 
22 the State Board of Education to adopt rules to require the completion of a Wabanaki studies 
23 component in the qualifications for certifying teachers.1
