41. Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5 following terms have the following meanings.
6 A. "By-name list" means a comprehensive list of every person experiencing 
7 homelessness in an area, updated in real time using information collected and shared 
8 with the person's consent, containing information about the person, including the 
9 person's name, homeless history, health and housing needs.
10 B. "Client" means a person receiving services from an emergency housing or shelter 
11 program.
12 C. "Emergency housing or shelter program" means any organization that operates a 
13 facility with the primary purpose of providing temporary shelter for persons 
14 experiencing homelessness.
15 D. "Housing first" means an approach to serving persons experiencing homelessness 
16 that:
17 (1) Recognizes that a person experiencing homelessness must first be able to access 
18 a decent, safe place to live;
19 (2) Does not limit length of stay before stabilizing, improving health, reducing 
20 harmful behaviors or increasing income of the person;
21 (3) Prioritizes connecting the person to a permanent home as quickly as possible;
22 (4) Removes barriers to accessing housing, including requirements for sobriety or 
23 absence of criminal history of the person; and
24 (5) Values the person's choices, including where to live and whether to participate 
25 in support services and does not require the person to participate in support services 
26 to access or retain housing.
27 E. "Mainstream resources" means housing, health, social services, employment, 
28 education and youth programs for which individuals and families experiencing 
29 homelessness and individuals and families at risk of homelessness may be eligible, 
30 including the MaineCare program, the Temporary Assistance for Needy Families 
31 program, the federal State Children's Health Insurance Program and other federal 
32 programs that assist homeless, indigent or underserved populations.
33 F. "Program" means the Emergency Housing Matching Grant Program established in 
34 subsection 2.
35 G. "Rapid rehousing" means rapidly connecting individuals and families experiencing 
36 homelessness to permanent housing through a tailored package of assistance that may 
37 include the use of time-limited financial assistance and targeted supportive services.
382. Program established.  The Emergency Housing Matching Grant Program is 
39 established within the Maine State Housing Authority.  The program requires applicants to 
40 match grant requests under the program on a one-to-one basis with funds from a source 
41 other than state funding.

44. Recipient criteria. A recipient of a grant under the program must:
5 A. Be a nonprofit corporation in good standing in the State and qualify as a tax-exempt 
6 organization under 26 United States Code, Section 501(c)(3) or a municipal 
7 corporation;
8 B. Be eligible for funding under the McKinney-Vento Homeless Assistance Act, 42 
9 United States Code, Section 11301 et seq. (2009);
10 C. Be a provider of homeless services with at least one year of experience providing 
11 emergency housing, street outreach, homeless prevention or rapid rehousing activities;
12 D. Be a regular and active participant in a state-based continuum eligible for 
13 homelessness funding from the United States Department of Housing and Urban 
14 Development and be compliant with the continuum's governance charter and 
15 performance criteria; 
16 E. Be a regular and active participant in a regional homeless service hub administered 
17 by the Maine State Housing Authority serving the region of the recipient by 
18 participating in hub meetings and providing relevant data to the hubs for the generation 
19 of a by-name list of persons experiencing homelessness in an area served by the 
20 recipient;
21 F. Have board or advisory board representation of persons experiencing homelessness 
22 or having experienced homelessness who are involved in policy making or planning of 
23 the recipient; 
24 G. Participate in a coordinated entry process that links persons who are experiencing 
25 homelessness or who are at risk of homelessness with appropriate housing or services 
26 by acting as an access point to the coordinated entry process, administering common 
27 tools to assess the needs of the persons, placing eligible persons on a housing 
28 prioritization list, participating in case conferencing meetings and making housing 
29 referrals using the processes and procedures designated by the continuum in paragraph 
30 D;
31 H. Have the administrative and financial management capacity necessary to administer 
32 and to account for the use of the grant from the program in accordance with the grant's 
33 funding requirements; 
34 I. Operate in accordance with the homelessness strategy outlined in the most recent 
35 consolidated housing and community development plan submitted by the Maine State 
36 Housing Authority to the United States Department of Housing and Urban 
37 Development outlining the State's goals and strategies for using federal funds to meet 
38 housing and community development needs; 
39 J. If applicable, meet the procedural and administrative objectives for a grant from the 
40 program subject to recapture on certain terms and for certain purposes determined by 
41 the Maine State Housing Authority;
42 K. If the recipient is a victim services provider, participate in and meet the performance 
43 and reporting requirements of a homeless management information system that is a

205. Emergency housing or shelter program requirements. An emergency housing or 
21 shelter program of a recipient of a grant under the program must:
22 A. Provide access 365 days per year to assist clients to meet basic emergency shelter 
23 needs;
24 B. Provide adequate sleeping space or beds and a clean and functioning shower and 
25 toilet facility; 
26 C. Provide safe and nutritious food, including breakfast or access to breakfast and, if 
27 open 24 hours per day, provide lunch and dinner or access to lunch and dinner;
28 D. Treat all clients with dignity and respect, regardless of religious or political beliefs, 
29 cultural background, disability, gender identity or sexual orientation;
30 E. Provide shelter or housing services based upon rapid rehousing or a housing first 
31 approach; 
32 F. Have admittance and stay policies that are appropriate for the population served and 
33 do not create unnecessary barriers to clients staying at the facility;
34 G. Provide access to community resources such as health care, job readiness and 
35 employment services, mainstream resources and educational services to assist clients 
36 in achieving housing stability;
37 H. Assess clients for emergency housing or shelter program eligibility and services to 
38 enable mobility to permanent housing with adequate supports; 
39 I. Inform a client of the client's rights and responsibilities, including specific shelter 
40 policies or house rules;1

29SUMMARY
30 This bill establishes the Emergency Housing Matching Grant Program to award grants 
31 at $10,000 per emergency housing bed up to $1,000,000 per recipient to program providers 
32 to construct, renovate or acquire new or existing buildings to house emergency housing or 
33 shelter programs, including emergency housing 24 hours per day, 7 days per week and 
34 housing navigation and case management services in the building after occupancy. 17
