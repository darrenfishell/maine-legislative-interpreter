18Sec. 2.  32 MRSA §2600-G is enacted to read:
19§2600-G.  Balance billing of Medicare beneficiaries prohibited
201. Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
21 following terms have the following meanings.
22 A. "Balance bill" means to charge to or collect from a Medicare beneficiary any amount 
23 in excess of the Medicare-approved amount for a service, procedure or treatment paid 
24 to a physician.
25 B. "Medicare" means the federal Health Insurance for the Aged Act, Title XVIII of the 
26 Social Security Amendments of 1965, as amended. 
27 C. "Medicare beneficiary" means a person who is a beneficiary of health insurance 
28 under Medicare.
292.  Balance billing prohibited.  A physician licensed under this chapter may not 
30 balance bill a Medicare beneficiary for any service, procedure or treatment provided to the 
31 Medicare beneficiary.
323.  Notice; posting.  A physician who treats Medicare beneficiaries shall post a 
33 summary of the provisions of this section in a conspicuous place in the physician's office.  
34 The summary must include the statement that any person aggrieved by a physician's failure 
35 to comply with the provisions of this section may file a complaint with the board and must 
36 include toll-free telephone numbers to be used for this purpose.
374.  Complaints; violations.  An individual may file a complaint with the board seeking 
38 disciplinary action concerning a violation of this section.  The board shall investigate or 
39 cause to be investigated and shall resolve a complaint on its own motion or upon receipt of 
40 a written complaint.  The board shall conduct its actions in accordance with the Maine 
41 Administrative Procedure Act.

25SUMMARY
26 This bill requires an insurer offering Medicare supplement insurance in this State to 
27 disclose whether the Medicare supplement insurance plan covers the costs of Medicare Part 
28 B excess charges.
29 The bill also prohibits physicians from balance billing Medicare beneficiaries for any 
30 amount in excess of the Medicare-approved amount for a service, procedure or treatment 
31 paid to that physician under the Medicare program.26
