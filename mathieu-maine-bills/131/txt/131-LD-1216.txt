7§3906-D.  Animal Cruelty Task Force
81.  Task force established.  The Animal Cruelty Task Force, established in Title 5, 
9 section 12004‑I, subsection 2‑J and referred to in this section as "the task force," shall 
10 provide a coalition of trained professionals to assist with and enhance the enforcement of 
11 animal cruelty laws, including the investigation of cases of animal cruelty.
122.  Membership.  The commissioner shall appoint the members of the task force. 
13 Members must include at least the following:
14 A.  One member who is an animal control officer;
15 B.  One member who is a humane agent;
16 C.  One member representing a statewide association of chiefs of police;
17 D.  One member representing a statewide association of sheriffs;
18 E.  One member who is a district attorney;
19 F.  One member representing the judicial branch;
20 G.  One member representing the Maine Municipal Association;
21 H.  One member representing the Maine Fire Chiefs Association;
22 I.  One member who is a licensed clinical social worker as defined in Title 32, section 
23 7001-A, subsection 6;
24 J.  One member who is a veterinarian licensed to practice in the State;
25 K.  One member representing an animal shelter licensed in accordance with chapter 
26 723; and
27 L.  One member with experience responding to large-scale animal cruelty cases and 
28 natural disasters.
293.  Consultants; experts.  Whenever the task force considers it appropriate, the task 
30 force may seek the advice of consultants or experts on a case-by-case basis in fields related 
31 to its duties.
324.  Terms; vacancies.  A member is appointed for a term of 2 years and may be 
33 reappointed. In the case of a vacancy for any reason, the commissioner shall appoint a 
34 member representing the same interest to fill the unexpired term.
355.  Meetings; officers.  The task force shall organize initially and annually thereafter 
36 by electing a chair and a vice-chair from among its members. The vice-chair shall also 
37 serve as secretary. The task force shall meet at the call of the chair at least twice a year and 
38 may meet at such other times as may be reasonably necessary to carry out its duties.4

Page 2 - 131LR1970(01)16.  Staff.  The department shall provide necessary staffing services to the task force.
27.  Funding.  When a member of the task force who is an employee of a state agency, 
3 except for a member who is a humane agent, is assigned to investigate a case of animal 
4 cruelty under this section, that state agency is responsible for establishing the salary and 
5 benefits, including overtime, of that member, and the State shall reimburse that state agency 
6 monthly through the animal welfare auxiliary fund established under section 3906-B, 
7 subsection 16.  The task force is also authorized to accept donations made to the 
8 investigation of an animal cruelty case.
98.  Powers and duties.  The task force has the following powers and duties.
10 A.  At the request of a municipality, the task force may provide guidance and support 
11 for animal cruelty cases that involve a large number of animals, involve safety concerns 
12 or are complex in nature.
13 B.  The task force shall identify ways to assist municipalities with the identification 
14 and handling of animal cruelty cases.
15 C.  The task force shall identify ways to assist with the prosecution of animal cruelty 
16 cases, including the prompt holding of a full hearing regarding the possession of the 
17 animal in accordance with Title 17, section 1021, subsection 3 and seeking petitions 
18 for the posting of a security in accordance with Title 17, section 1027.
19 D.  The task force shall recommend measures to streamline reporting of animal cruelty 
20 to local law enforcement agencies for immediate documentation and assessment.
21 E.  The task force shall identify and pursue collaborations among law enforcement 
22 agencies, the criminal justice system, animal welfare organizations, behavioral health 
23 liaisons, community resources and others to aid in the prevention and addressing of 
24 animal cruelty.
25 F.  A law enforcement agent serving on the task force may be deputized and may serve 
26 a municipality if there is no acting agent for the municipality in the jurisdiction of an 
27 animal cruelty case or in the event the animal control officer for the municipality does 
28 not have the authority to issue a criminal summons.
29 G.  The task force shall review the handling of prior large-scale animal cruelty cases to 
30 identify and recommend ways to improve investigation and enforcement.
319.  Media policy.  Members of the task force are not authorized to release to the public 
32 or a media outlet any information on an animal cruelty case.  Requests for information from 
33 a media outlet or the public must be referred to the public information officer of the 
34 appropriate law enforcement agency.  For purposes of this subsection, "media outlet" 
35 includes broadcast media, print media and Internet-based media.
36Sec. 3. Animal Cruelty Task Force first meeting.  The Commissioner of 
37 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry shall call the first meeting of the Animal Cruelty 
38 Task Force, established under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 7, section 3906-D, no later 
39 than December 31, 2023.
40SUMMARY
41 This bill establishes the Animal Cruelty Task Force to provide a coalition of trained 
42 professionals to assist with and enhance the enforcement of animal cruelty laws.41
