3Sec. 4.  Deed and restrictions.  Resolved:  That the property must be conveyed 
4 by quitclaim deed without covenants, subject to the following deed restrictions: The Town 
5 of Carrabassett Valley shall in perpetuity retain title to the property and may not sell or 
6 otherwise transfer any interest, in whole or in part, in the property except that the Town of 
7 Carrabassett Valley may lease portions of the property as long as the uses are consistent 
8 with the uses specified in this resolve.  The Except as otherwise provided in this section, 
9 the property must remain open and available for use and enjoyment by the public at large.  
10 Use and use of the property must be dedicated for purposes of public outdoor recreation, 
11 including, but not limited to: natural history study; hiking; camping; cross-country skiing; 
12 hunting; fishing; fisheries and wildlife management; skating; timber management and 
13 harvesting under a management plan prepared by a licensed professional forester; and 
14 attendant roads and parking. The property must be maintained in an essentially natural and 
15 undeveloped condition, except that up to 25 contiguous acres in the aggregate, including 
16 any development in existence on the effective date of this resolve, may be developed for 
17 any municipal facility and except that up to 5 contiguous acres in the aggregate may be 
18 developed for workforce housing if the development occurs before July 1, 2033. 
19 Development for workforce housing may not interfere with the public outdoor recreation 
20 uses of the property. The following uses are expressly prohibited: residential development 
21 of any type other than workforce housing; and development for overnight accommodations, 
22 except camping, including commercial camping if development plans for commercial 
23 camping are approved by the State. Workforce housing on property developed under this 
24 section must be affordable to households earning between 60% and 120% of the median 
25 income for the area. The Town of Carrabassett Valley shall demonstrate to the Bureau of 
26 Parks and Lands that a proposed workforce housing development project meets the 
27 requirements of this section before construction on the development process begins. If the 
28 Town of Carrabassett Valley fails to comply with any of the conditions or restrictions, in 
29 whole or in part, contained in this resolve, the State may give written notice to the Town 
30 of Carrabassett Valley, and if the Town of Carrabassett Valley fails to comply within 30 
31 days, then the title to the property reverts to the State; such a reversion may not be effective 
32 until the State records a notice of the reversion in the Franklin County Registry of Deeds.
33SUMMARY
34 This bill allows up to 5 acres of a portion of land owned by the Town of Carrabassett 
35 Valley to be developed for workforce housing to serve households earning between 60% 
36 and 120% of the median income for the area if the development occurs before July 1, 2033.34
