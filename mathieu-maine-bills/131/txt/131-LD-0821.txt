Page 1 - 131LR1833(01)1Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to provide 
2neurobehavioral beds.  Resolved:  That, no later than September 1, 2024, the 
3 Department of Health and Human Services shall provide 16 new neurobehavioral beds in 
4 one or more neurobehavioral centers to serve individuals with significant behavioral 
5 challenges and complex medical needs who need short-term treatment before transitioning 
6 to a long-term care environment.  A center shall serve individuals with dementia, brain 
7 injury, intellectual disabilities or autism and accompanying behavioral issues who cannot 
8 be served appropriately in the community or in a nursing facility lacking specialized 
9 neurobehavioral services but are not in need of hospitalization.  A center shall provide 
10 evaluation and short-term treatment for no longer than one year and shall facilitate 
11 transition to a long-term care environment in the community or in a long-term care facility 
12 with appropriate treatment supports.
13SUMMARY
14 This resolve requires the Department of Health and Human Services to provide by 
15 September 1, 2024 16 new neurobehavioral beds in one or more neurobehavioral centers 
16 to serve individuals with significant behavioral challenges and complex medical needs who 
17 need evaluation and short-term treatment before transitioning to a long-term care 
18 environment either in the community or in a long-term care facility.14
