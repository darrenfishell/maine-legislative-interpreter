3Resolved: That the Commissioner of Economic and Community Development, referred 
4 to in this resolve as "the commissioner," shall convene a working group, referred to in this 
5 resolve as "the group," to improve the State's 10-year economic development strategy in 
6 order to develop a diverse and sustainable economy that raises the State's standard of living; 
7 raises the State's average weekly wage; reduces poverty; attracts outside investment and 
8 venture capital in order to compete with other states regionally and nationally; attracts 
9 research and development opportunities; improves the State's regulatory environment; 
10 improves the State's competitive position; creates and retains jobs that add economic value 
11 or bring in money from outside the State, supporting public sector and service jobs; 
12 provides economic development that reduces greenhouse gas emissions and addresses 
13 climate change; supports individuals' aspirations and enables each person to maximize that 
14 person's contribution to productive work, community and personal fulfillment; and makes 
15 the State an economic contributor to the country's national and international goals.
16Sec. 2.  Membership. Resolved: That the commissioner shall appoint to the group 
17 at least 12 members who have knowledge and experience in economic and community 
18 development in the State. The commissioner or the commissioner's designee shall serve as 
19 an ex officio member of the group. All appointments must be made within 30 days of the 
20 effective date of this resolve. The members of the group shall select a chair from the group's 
21 membership. Members of the group serve without compensation. The Department of 
22 Economic and Community Development shall provide administrative support to the group. 
23 The group shall disband after completing its duties under section 3.
24Sec. 3.  Meetings; duties. Resolved: That the chair shall schedule the first meeting 
25 of the group within 30 days of the date of the final appointment to the group. The group 
26 shall meet at other times the chair determines necessary to perform and complete the duties 
27 of the group. In its work, the group shall consider and develop improvements to the State's 
28 10-year economic development strategy, including, but not limited to:
29 1.  The development of workforce skills;
30 2.  The attraction and retention of a diverse workforce;
31 3.  The creation and retention of quality jobs that add economic value or bring in money 
32 from outside the State;
33 4.  The attraction of outside investment and venture capital and how the State can 
34 successfully compete with other states for investment and venture capital;
35 5.  The attraction of research and development opportunities;
36 6.  The connection to and development of sectors tied to the 21st century economy;
37 7.  Establishing connections to cutting-edge growth sectors;
38 8.  Providing education that produces career or college-ready graduates and provides 
39 training for 21st century jobs and integrating all educational institutions for appropriate 
40 lifetime learning; 
41 9.  The best ways to promote stability and certainty in the economic environment; 
42 10.  Incentives and penalties that should be put in place to help attract and support jobs; 
Page 2 - 131LR1460(01)1 11.  Potential solutions to the economic disparity between the urban and rural areas of 
2 the State; and
3 12.  Identifying the strategy's potential impacts on taxes, regulation, health care, energy 
4 costs and labor laws, as well as anything that affects the State's ability to compete for 
5 innovative companies and jobs that add economic value or bring in money from outside

7 Upon completion of its work, the group shall submit its work to the commissioner for 
8 review and make any changes to its proposed improvements that the commissioner

10Sec. 4.  Report. Resolved: That, no later than December 6, 2023, the commissioner 
11 shall submit a report including the proposed improvements to the 10-year economic 
12 development strategy as described in section 3 and any recommended legislation to the 
13 Joint Standing Committee on Innovation, Development, Economic Advancement and 
14 Business. The joint standing committee may report out legislation concerning 
15 improvements to the 10-year economic development strategy to the Second Regular 
16 Session of the 131st Legislature.
17SUMMARY
18 This resolve directs the Commissioner of Economic and Community Development to 
19 establish a working group to improve the State's 10-year economic development strategy 
20 in order to develop a diverse and sustainable economy that raises the State's standard of 
21 living; raises the State's average weekly wage; reduces poverty; attracts outside investment 
22 and venture capital in order to compete with other states regionally and nationally; attracts 
23 research and development opportunities; improves the State's regulatory environment; 
24 improves the State's competitive position; creates and retains jobs that add economic value 
25 or bring in money from outside the State in order to sustain public sector and service jobs; 
26 provides economic development that reduces greenhouse gas emissions and addresses 
27 climate change; supports individuals' aspirations and enables each person to maximize that 
28 person's contribution to productive work, community and personal fulfillment; and makes 
29 the State an economic contributor to the country's national and international goals.  The 
30 resolve directs the commissioner to submit the proposed improvements to the 10-year 
31 economic development strategy to the Joint Standing Committee on Innovation, 
32 Development, Economic Advancement and Business by December 6, 2023 and authorizes 
33 the joint standing committee to report out legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 
34 131st Legislature.18
