183.  Posting required.  For at least 10 days before each general election, an employer 
19 shall post notice of the requirements of this law in the workplace in a manner likely to be

22 For the purposes of this section, "employee" means a person in the service of an

25SUMMARY
26 This bill designates Election Day, the Tuesday following the first Monday in 
27 November in an even-numbered year, as a state holiday.  It also establishes a requirement 
28 that employers provide an employee 3 consecutive hours in which to vote on the day of the 
29 general election.  If the employee's working hours do not allow the employee 3 consecutive 
30 hours in which to vote, the employer shall provide the employee paid time off sufficient to 
31 allow the employee 3 consecutive hours in which to vote.  It also requires that an employer, 
32 at least 10 days before each general election, post notice of these requirements in the 
33 workplace in a manner likely to be seen by all employees.  The Secretary of State is directed 
34 to prepare and supply employers the notice that must be posted.26
