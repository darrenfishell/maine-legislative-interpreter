9Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within

12 therefore,
13Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
14Sec. 1.  29-A MRSA §2382, sub-§1, as enacted by PL 1993, c. 683, Pt. A, §2 and 
15 affected by Pt. B, §5, is amended to read:
161.  Overlimit movement permits issued by State.  The Secretary of State, acting 
17 under guidelines and advice of the Commissioner of Transportation, may grant permits to 
18 move nondivisible objects having a length, width, height or weight greater than specified 
19 in this Title over a way or bridge maintained by the Department of Transportation. Permits 
20 issued under this subsection must allow the movement of the nondivisible objects from 1/2 
21 hour before sunrise to 10 p.m. on any day, including a holiday, other than Sunday.
22Sec. 2.  29-A MRSA §2382, sub-§3, as amended by PL 2003, c. 452, Pt. Q, §73 
23 and affected by Pt. X, §2, is further amended to read:
243.  County and municipal permits.  A county commissioner or municipal officer may 
25 grant a permit, for a reasonable fee, for travel over a way or bridge maintained by that 
26 county or municipality. Permits issued under this subsection must allow the travel from 1/2 
27 hour before sunrise to 10 p.m. on any day, including a holiday, other than Sunday.
28Sec. 3.  29-A MRSA §2382, sub-§5, as amended by PL 2019, c. 335, §9, is further 
29 amended to read:
305.  Long-term permits.  The Secretary of State may grant permits for up to one year 
31 for trucks, truck tractors, semitrailers, heavy duty recovery vehicles and Class A special 
32 mobile equipment.   Notwithstanding Title 5, section 8071, subsection 2, paragraph A, the 
33 Secretary of State, in consultation with the Commissioner of Transportation, shall establish 
34 the permit terms and limitations and fee schedule by rule.  Rules adopted pursuant to this 
35 subsection must provide for permits to be issued to vehicles up to 12 feet in width and must 
36 allow travel by any permitted vehicle between 1/2 hour before sunrise and 10 p.m. on any 
37 day, including a holiday, other than Sunday. Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are 
38 routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2‑A.
39Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
40 takes effect when approved.
