75.  Sector-specific greenhouse gas emissions limits.  The department shall adopt 
8 sector-specific biennial limits on greenhouse gas emissions from energy sources in the 
9 commercial sector, industrial sector, residential sector and transportation sector and for 
10 electricity and combined heat and power plants whose primary business is to sell electricity 
11 or electricity and heat to the public.  The department may adopt sector-specific greenhouse 
12 gas emissions limits for any other sector or source the department designates.  These limits 
13 must collectively ensure compliance with the annual greenhouse gas emissions levels 
14 established by subsections 1 to 3. The department shall calculate and impose biennial limits 
15 under this paragraph for a period of 10 years.
16 By December 31, 2025 and every 4 years thereafter, the department shall adjust the sector-
17 specific greenhouse gas emissions limits as necessary taking into consideration updated 
18 greenhouse gas emissions data and reflecting the policies contained in the most recent 
19 updated climate action plan under section 577. The adjusted greenhouse gas emissions 
20 limits must be imposed for a period of 10 years.
21 Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
22 chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
23SUMMARY
24 This bill requires the Department of Environmental Protection to adopt rules setting 
25 sector-specific biennial limits on greenhouse gas emissions from energy sources in the 
26 commercial sector, industrial sector, residential sector and transportation sector and for 
27 electricity and combined heat and power plants whose primary business is to sell electricity 
28 or electricity and heat to the public. The bill also requires the Department of Transportation 
29 to adopt rules as necessary to ensure compliance with the State's greenhouse gas emissions 
30 levels.  24
