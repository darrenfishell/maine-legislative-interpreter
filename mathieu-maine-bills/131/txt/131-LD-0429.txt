26SUMMARY
27 Part A amends certain Maine motor vehicle laws in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 
28 29-A, limiting the sentence for the crime of manufacturing or reproducing registration 
29 plates without the consent of the Secretary of State to a fine and changing some crimes to 
30 traffic infractions.  Crimes reduced to traffic infractions include certain violations of laws 
31 related to:
32 1.  Motor vehicle registration certificates and plates and driver's licenses;
33 2.  Removal of a vehicle without the consent of the owner or person in charge of the 
34 property where the vehicle is located;
35 3.  Unlawful ownership or display of a revoked, fictitious or fraudulently altered 
36 driver's license or identification card;
37 4.  Unlawful use of a driver's license or an identification card;
38 5.  Improper use of registration plates; and27

2 Part B amends the Maine Criminal Code provision governing civil violations to 
3 explicitly provide that evidence obtained pursuant to an unlawful search and seizure is not 
4 admissible in any civil violation proceeding, not just those arising from cannabis possession 
5 by a person under 21 years of age or possession of butyl nitrite or isobutyl nitrite, and to 
6 permit a law enforcement officer who has probable cause to believe that a violation of the 
7 law has taken place or is taking place to make application for a search warrant.
8 Part C establishes an effective date of January 1, 2024.
