5 The department shall implement and administer a system, referred to in this section as 
6 "the tracking system," for the tracking of cannabis plants, adult use cannabis and adult use 
7 cannabis products from immature cannabis plant to the point of retail sale, disposal or 
8 destruction. The tracking system must allow for cannabis plants at the stage of cultivation 
9 and upon transfer from the stage of cultivation to another licensee to be tracked by group , 
10 including, but not limited to, by plant canopy, to the point of retail sale, disposal or 
11 destruction.  The department  may  shall implement a tracking system that allows adult use 
12 cannabis or adult use cannabis products to be tracked by group.
13 The department shall ensure that the system implemented and administered under this 
14 section, whether tracking individually or by group, maintains a detailed record at every

161.  Data submission requirements.  The tracking system must allow licensees to 
17 submit tracking data for adult use cannabis or adult use cannabis products to the department 
18 through manual data entry or through the use of tracking system software commonly used 
19 within the cannabis industry as determined by the department.
201-A.  Group tracking.  Cannabis plants at the same stage of growth that are of the 
21 same varietal or cultivar of the plant genus Cannabis may be tracked by group if they:
22 A.  Are planted in the same specific area at the same time;
23 B.  Are transplanted to the same specific area at the same time; or
24 C.  Include cannabis plants that were planted in a specific area and cannabis plants that 
25 were transplanted to the same specific area. ; or
26 D.  Are within a plant canopy.
27 For cannabis plants that are tracked as a group, a licensee shall designate the square footage 
28 of the specific area in which the plants are planted or transplanted.  Cannabis plants may 
29 not be tracked as a group unless they are intended for harvest as a group.
301-B.  Tagging.  A licensee shall affix a tag containing the identifying information 
31 required by the department by rule to each group of cannabis plants tracked under this 
32 section.  The department may not require cannabis plants that are being tracked as a group 
33 to be individually affixed with a tag during cultivation or subsequent transfer  to another 
34 licensee.
351-C.  Group transfers.  When a group of cannabis plants tracked under this section is 
36 transferred to another licensee  person, the licensee transferring the group of cannabis plants 
37 must provide a manifest that lists every cannabis plant within the group and any other 
38 relevant information required by the department by rule.
392.  Rules.  The department shall adopt rules regarding the implementation and 
40 administration of the tracking system and tracking requirements for licensees.  Rules 
41 adopted under this section must include, but are not limited to, the following:

11SUMMARY
12 This bill provides that group tracking of cannabis plants, currently provided for under 
13 the Cannabis Legalization Act through wholesale transactions, is extended through to retail 
14 sale, disposal or destruction. It further provides that the contract of the Department of 
15 Administrative and Financial Services' office of cannabis policy, with Metrc, LLC to 
16 implement the Maine Cannabis Enforcement Tracking Reporting and Compliance webpage 
17 is void and of no effect if, on the effective date of this legislation, the webpage is not in 
18 compliance with the requirements of the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 28-B, section 105 
19 as in effect on January 1, 2023.12
