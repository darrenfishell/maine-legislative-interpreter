COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "If" to H.P. 235, L.D. 384, "An Act Addressing 
Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry in Maine" 
Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
'An Act to Provide Emergency Aid to Dairy Farmers Affected by the Crisis in 
Production Costs' 
Amend the bill by inserting after the title and before the enacting clause the following: 
'Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and 
Whereas, market instability, labor shortages, inflation, limits on milk production, 
supply chain delays and distribution disruptions are driving Maine dairy farmers out of 
business; and 
Whereas, Maine has lost approximately one-quarter of its dairy farms since 2020; and 
Whereas, dairy farms are integral to Maine's rural communities and agricultural 
economy; and 
Whereas, it is critically important that this legislation take effect before the expiration 
of the 90-day period; and 
Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
therefore,' 
Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the 
following:

relief for dairy farmers. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, on or 
before July 30, 2023, the State Controller shall transfer $5,329,667 from the unappropriated

25 SUMMARY 
26 This amendment replaces the bill, which is a concept draft. The amendment provides 
27 one-time pandemic volatility payments to Maine milk producers who produced milk and 
28 reported production information to the Maine Milk Commission in calendar year 2022 and 
29 are currently participating in the Maine Dairy Stabilization Program, also known as the 
30 Tier Program. The amendment provides a one-time appropriation at a rate of $1 per 
31 hundredweight of milk production in fiscal year 2023-24 from the unappropriated surplus 
32 of General Fund to the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, Milk 
33 Commission program to provide one-time payments to Maine milk producers.
