20SUMMARY
21 This bill provides that if a sheriff, deputy or constable serves a notice in connection 
22 with an eviction, that sheriff, deputy or constable does not need to be present at the court 
23 hearing to testify to the service of the notice.  It also provides that after one good faith effort 
24 has been made to serve the defendant with a writ of possession, service may be 
25 accomplished by both mailing the notice by first-class mail to the defendant's last known 
26 address and leaving the writ of possession at the defendant's last and usual place of abode.  
27 Current law requires 3 good faith efforts on 3 different days to serve the defendant.21
