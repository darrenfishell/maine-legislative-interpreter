32.  Substance use disorder.  "Substance use disorder" means a clinical and significant

6Sec. 2.  22 MRSA §3088, as repealed and replaced by PL 2011, c. 293, §3, is 
7 amended to read:
8§3088.  Comprehensive neurorehabilitation service system
9 The department shall, within the limits of its available resources, develop a 
10 comprehensive neurorehabilitation service system designed to assist, educate and 
11 rehabilitate the person with an acquired brain injury to attain and sustain the highest 
12 function and self-sufficiency possible using home-based and community-based treatments, 
13 services and resources to the greatest possible degree.  The comprehensive 
14 neurorehabilitation service system must include, but is not limited to, care management and 
15 coordination, crisis stabilization services, physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech 
16 therapy, neuropsychology, neurocognitive retraining, positive neurobehavioral supports 
17 and teaching, social skills retraining, counseling, substance use disorder counseling, 
18 vocational rehabilitation and independent living skills and supports.  The comprehensive 
19 neurorehabilitation service system may include a posthospital system of nursing, 
20 community residential facilities and community residential support programs designed to 
21 meet the needs of persons who have sustained an acquired brain injury and assist in the 
22 reintegration of those persons into their communities.
23Sec. 3.  Department of Health and Human Services to include counseling 
24for substance use disorder in rehabilitative services for MaineCare members 
25who have sustained an acquired brain injury. The Department of Health and Human 
26 Services shall amend its rules to include counseling for substance use disorder co-occurring 
27 with an acquired brain injury as part of rehabilitative services for eligible MaineCare 
28 members who have sustained an acquired brain injury under the department's rule Chapter 
29 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter II, Section 102, Rehabilitative Services. For 
30 purposes of this section, "substance use disorder" means a clinical and significant 
31 functional impairment, including a health problem or a disability or an inability to meet 
32 major responsibilities at work, home or school, due to the use of alcohol or a drug.
33SUMMARY
34 This bill adds substance use disorder counseling to the comprehensive 
35 neurorehabilitation service system administered by the Department of Health and Human 
36 Services and directs the department to include counseling for substance use disorder in 
37 rehabilitative services for MaineCare members who have sustained an acquired brain 
38 injury.34
