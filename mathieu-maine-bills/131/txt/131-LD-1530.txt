10Sec. 2.  22 MRSA §2430-I is enacted to read:
11§2430-I.  On-site consumption
12 A registered caregiver, caregiver retail store or registered dispensary may permit the 
13 consumption of harvested cannabis for medical use by a qualifying patient only in 
14 accordance with the following.
151. Department permit. The registered caregiver, caregiver retail store or registered 
16 dispensary shall obtain a permit from the department authorizing on-site consumption of 
17 harvested cannabis.  The department, by rule, shall establish the process for issuing permits 
18 under this subsection as well as the permit fee, which may not exceed the administrative 
19 cost of issuing the permit.  Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are major substantive 
20 rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
212. Compliance.  The registered caregiver, caregiver retail store or registered dispensary 
22 shall comply with all applicable requirements under this chapter and the rules adopted 
23 pursuant to this chapter. 
243. Physically separate area.  In the case of a caregiver retail store or registered 
25 dispensary, on-site consumption of harvested cannabis must occur only in an area 
26 physically separate from all other areas of the caregiver retail store or registered dispensary, 
27 including the sales area. 
284.  Security.  In the case of a caregiver retail store or registered dispensary, the store 
29 or registered dispensary shall implement appropriate security measures to deter and prevent 
30 unauthorized entrance into areas where on-site consumption occurs.
315.  Assistants.  A registered caregiver, caregiver retail store or registered dispensary 
32 may hire any number of assistants to assist in on-site consumption, but those assistants 
33 must be 21 years of age or older. 
346.  Signs, advertising and marketing.  Signs, advertising and marketing used by or 
35 on behalf of a registered caregiver, caregiver retail store or registered dispensary 
36 advertising or marketing on-site consumption of harvested cannabis must comply with 
37 section 2429-B.
387.  Sales tax.  The registered caregiver, caregiver retail store or registered dispensary 
39 shall ensure that the tax imposed on the sale of harvested cannabis to a consumer pursuant 
40 to Title 36, section 1811 is collected and remitted in accordance with the requirements of 
41 Title 36, Part 3 and the rules adopted pursuant to Title 36, Part 3.

3 a qualifying patient in a caregiver retail store, in a registered dispensary or with a registered 
4 caregiver.  The Department of Administrative and Financial Services must establish, by 
5 major substantive rule, the process for issuing permits for on-site consumption and the 
6 permit fee, which may not exceed the administrative cost of issuing the permit.2
