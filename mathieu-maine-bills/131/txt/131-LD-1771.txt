4SPEEDY TRIALS
5ยง1491. Time for commencement of trial 
61.  Defendant in custody.  Trial must commence for a defendant who has been 
7 detained in a jail or a correctional facility for at least 30 total days while awaiting trial 
8 within the following time limits:
9 A.  For a defendant charged with murder or a Class A, Class B or Class C crime, trial 
10 must commence within 180 days from arraignment or first appearance, whichever is 
11 earlier; and
12 B.  For a defendant charged with a Class D or Class E crime and not charged with a 
13 related Class C or higher crime, trial must commence within 45 days from arraignment 
14 or first appearance, whichever is earlier.
152.  Defendant not in custody.  Trial must commence for a defendant who has not been 
16 detained in a jail or a correctional facility while awaiting trial, or who has been detained in 
17 a jail or correctional facility for fewer than 30 total days while awaiting trial, within the 
18 following time limits:
19 A. For a defendant charged with murder or a Class A, Class B or Class C crime, trial 
20 must commence within 270 days from arraignment or first appearance, whichever is 
21 earlier; and
22 B. For a defendant charged with a Class D or Class E crime and not charged with a 
23 related Class C or higher crime, trial must commence within 60 days from arraignment 
24 or first appearance, whichever is earlier.
253.  Time excluded.  The following time periods are excluded in computing the time 
26 limits in subsections 1 and 2:
27 A. Delay resulting from a continuance granted at the defendant's request. Before 
28 granting a continuance under this paragraph, the court shall advise the defendant of the 
29 defendant's right to a speedy trial and the effect of the continuance; 
30 B. Delay resulting from an interlocutory appeal;
31 C. Delay resulting from a proceeding relating to the transfer of a case or the removal 
32 of any defendant from another county under the Maine Rules of Criminal Procedure, 
33 Rule 21 or succeeding rule;
34 D. Delay resulting from transportation of a defendant from another county or to and 
35 from a place of examination or hospitalization, except that any time consumed in 
36 excess of 5 days from the date of an order of removal or an order directing 
37 transportation under this paragraph and the defendant's arrival at the destination is 
38 presumed to be unreasonable;
Page 2 - 131LR2158(01)1 E. Delay reasonably attributable to a period not to exceed 30 days during which any 
2 proceeding concerning the defendant is under advisement by the court;
3 F. Delay during which prosecution is deferred by the State pursuant to written 
4 agreement with the defendant, with the approval of the court, to allow the defendant to 
5 demonstrate the defendant's good conduct;
6 G. Delay resulting from the defendant being mentally incompetent or physically unable 
7 to stand trial; and
8 H. A reasonable delay when the defendant is joined for trial with a codefendant as to 
9 whom the time for commencement of trial has not expired and no motion for severance 
10 has been granted.
114. Effect of mistrial or order of new trial.  Trial must commence for a defendant 
12 within 180 days from the date of mistrial or order granting a new trial.
135. Effect of refiling.  If the prosecution moves to dismiss the charge and subsequently 
14 refiles the same or a similar charge, the refiling does not reset or extend the original time 
15 limit under this chapter.
16ยง1492. Waiver of speedy trial right 
17 A defendant may waive the defendant's right to a speedy trial under this chapter.  The 
18 waiver is not effective unless the court has advised the defendant of the defendant's right 
19 to a speedy trial and the effect of the waiver.
20ยง1493. Remedy for noncompliance with time limit 
21 If the trial of a defendant does not commence within the applicable time limits under 
22 this chapter, the court shall dismiss the case with prejudice.
23Sec. 2.  Effective date. This Act takes effect January 1, 2024.
24SUMMARY
25 This bill establishes time limits for the commencement of a defendant's criminal trial 
26 and provides remedies for when the time limits have been violated.25
