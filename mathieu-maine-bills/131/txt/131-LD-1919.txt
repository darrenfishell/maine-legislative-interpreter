2Sec. 1.  12 MRSA §12706, sub-§1, ¶C-1 is enacted to read:
3 C-1.  Blue Hill Falls Sanctuary: The following described territory situated in the Town 
4 of Blue Hill in the County of Hancock: The area enclosed by a boundary line drawn as 
5 follows: Beginning at Stills Point at the easterly end of Blue Hill Neck, extending to 
6 the north in an arc to the easternmost point of the island called The Nub, thence follow 
7 along the high tide mark in a generally south direction parallel to the Blue Hill Falls

13Sec. 2.  12 MRSA §12707, sub-§2, ¶I is enacted to read:
14 I.  Subsection 1-A applies to Blue Hill Falls Sanctuary from the 15th day of October of 
15 each year to the first day of April following.  From April 2nd until October 14th of 
16 each year, hunting wild animals and wild birds is permitted.  From the 15th day of 
17 October of each year to the first day of April following, a person may not lure or attempt 
18 to lure common eider out of Blue Hill Falls Sanctuary.  From the 15th day of October 
19 of each year to the first day of April following, a person may not engage in sustained 
20 recreational or commercial activity in Blue Hill Falls Sanctuary.  For purposes of this 
21 paragraph, "sustained recreational or commercial activity" means activity lasting more 
22 than 30 minutes on more than one day from the 15th day of October of each year to the 
23 first day of April following.  From April 2nd until October 14th of each year, no more 
24 than a total of 20 wild animals and wild birds per day may be taken from the Blue Hill 
25 Falls Sanctuary.  From April 2nd until October 14th of each year, a person may not 
26 take wild animals or wild birds from the Blue Hill Falls Sanctuary on more than one 
27 day in a calendar week.
28SUMMARY
29 This bill designates a state wildlife sanctuary at Blue Hill Falls in Hancock County and 
30 places restrictions on certain activities in the wildlife sanctuary.29
