155.  Payments to managed care organizations.  The department shall pay managed 
16 care organizations on the basis of per MaineCare member, per month payments negotiated 
17 pursuant to this subsection.  Payments must be at risk-adjusted rates based on historical 
18 utilization and spending data, projected and adjusted to reflect the eligibility category, 
19 geographic area and clinical risk profile of the MaineCare members with the provision for 
20 subsequent adjustment based on actual enrollments and encounter data when available.  In 
21 negotiating rates with the managed care organizations, the department shall consider any 
22 adjustments necessary to encourage the managed care organizations to use the most cost-
23 effective means of improving outcomes and providing specialized management of 
24 particular subgroups of populations with complex or high-cost needs.
256.  Ratesetting.  The department shall establish rates in the contracts in accordance 
26 with this section that include the following:
27 A.  Rates that are actuarially sound, including utilization assumptions that are 
28 consistent with industry and local standards.  Rates must be adjusted for risk and

31 B.  Appropriate rate floors for in-network primary care and specialty care providers 
32 and pharmacy dispensing fees to ensure the achievement of goals; and
33 C.  Rates for services in the remaining fee-for-service programs.

16SUMMARY
17 This bill establishes a managed care program for all covered MaineCare services.  It 
18 requires the Department of Health and Human Services to issue a request for proposals for 
19 at least 3 managed care organizations that are able to operate the managed care program on 
20 a statewide basis.  By July 1, 2025, the Medicaid expansion population, those covered by 
21 the children's health insurance program and members categorically eligible for the 
22 Temporary Assistance for Needy Families program must be enrolled in the managed care 
23 program.  By July 1, 2027, all eligible MaineCare members must be enrolled in the 
24 managed care program.  The bill establishes the requirements for contracts for managed 
25 care organizations.  It requires the Department of Health and Human Services to apply for 
26 any state plan amendments or waivers required by January 1, 2024.  It requires the 
27 department to explore options to create a plan that integrates Medicaid and Medicare 
28 benefits for individuals into a managed care program.1
