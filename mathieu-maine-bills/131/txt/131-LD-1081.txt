5§1299.  Sale of water in bulk
6 A person selling water that is withdrawn from a lake or pond in this State may not

10Sec. 2.  38 MRSA §1844 is enacted to read:
11§1844.  Lake conservation program
121. Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
13 following terms have the following meanings.
14 A. "Lake association" means a nonprofit organization incorporated under state law the 
15 corporate purpose of which includes maintenance or improvement of water quality or 
16 public safety on a lake or pond, management of water levels or other social, 
17 educational, stewardship or advocacy efforts to benefit users of or the natural 
18 environment of a lake or pond.
19 B. "Production fee" means the fee authorized by subsection 2.
20 C. "Program" means the program established under subsection 4.
212.  Production fee.  The department shall impose a production fee on water withdrawn 
22 from a lake or pond for commercial purposes at a rate of 10% of the retail price of the water 
23 withdrawn.  
243. Disposition of fees; account.  A production fee collected under this section must be 
25 deposited in a nonlapsing Other Special Revenue Funds account to be used only for the 
26 purposes of the program. This account may receive money from grants, gifts, bequests and 
27 donations.
284. Lake conservation program.  The department shall develop and implement a 
29 program to provide grants to lake associations for conservation efforts.  The department 
30 may give grants to lake associations in an amount equal to the total amount of the 
31 production fees collected for the water withdrawn from the lake or pond served by the lake 
32 association.  Grant money awarded under this section may be used only for conservation 
33 efforts by the lake association.
345. Rules.  The department shall adopt rules to implement this section. Rules adopted 
35 pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, 
36 subchapter 2‑A.

2 This bill prohibits a person from charging a fee for water withdrawn from a lake or 
3 pond in the State and allows a delivery fee for that water.  The bill requires the Department 
4 of Environmental Protection to impose a production fee on water withdrawn from a lake or 
5 pond for commercial purposes and directs the department to use the money collected to 
6 give grants to lake associations for conservation efforts.2
