2 Programs and Policies Regarding Natural Resources and Environmental Protection, 
3 referred to in this resolve as "the commission," is established to develop updated, united 
4 and holistic natural resources programs and functions throughout State Government.
5Sec. 2.  Commission membership.  Resolved:  That the commission consists of 
6 the following members:
7 1.  The Commissioner of Environmental Protection or the commissioner's designee;
8 2.  The Commissioner of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry or the commissioner's 
9 designee;
10 3.  The Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife or the commissioner's designee; 
11 4.  The Commissioner of Marine Resources or the commissioner's designee; and
12 5.  The Director of the Office of Program Evaluation and Government Accountability 
13 or the director's designee.
14Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the chair of the commission and an assistant chair 
15 of the commission are appointed by the Governor.
16Sec. 4.  Convening of commission.  Resolved:  That the chair shall call and 
17 convene the first meeting of the commission no later than 30 days following the effective 
18 date of this resolve.
19Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall  review natural resources 
20 programs and functions throughout State Government, including the natural resources 
21 agencies' interactions with federal agencies, municipalities, counties and nongovernmental 
22 organizations.  For the purposes of this resolve, "the natural resources agencies" includes 
23 the Department of Environmental Protection, the Department of Agriculture, Conservation 
24 and Forestry, the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife and the Department of 
25 Marine Resources.  The commission shall:
26 1.  Identify redundancies, inefficiencies and lack of potential internal and external 
27 coordination among the natural resources agencies;
28 2.  Examine methods and issue recommendations to bring all natural resources and 
29 environmental protection functions of the natural resources agencies in line with statewide 
30 priorities, streamline overall operations and identify overlooked, unsupported and 
31 emerging priorities. Such examinations may include examining the nature of how the 
32 natural resources agencies evaluate land and water conservation priorities, the methods for 
33 establishing new state parks, the ways forests are managed and how climate change and 
34 emerging methods for protecting forest ecosystems are managed; and
35 3.  Receive and consider public comment related to the commission's duties.
36Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the members in section 2 shall provide 
37 necessary staff services to the commission.  At a minimum, each member identified in 
38 section 2 shall designate a staff member from that member's agency or office to provide 
39 staff support to the commission.
40Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than November 6, 2024, the commission 
41 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
Page 2 - 131LR2334(01)42 legislation, for presentation to the Governor and the First Regular Session of the 132nd 
43 Legislature in 2025.
3SUMMARY
4 This resolve establishes the Commission to Examine Programs and Policies Regarding 
5 Natural Resources and Environmental Protection to review natural resources programs and 
6 functions throughout State Government, including the natural resources agencies' 
7 interactions with federal agencies, municipalities, counties and nongovernmental 
8 organizations.  The natural resources agencies include the Department of Environmental 
9 Protection, the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, the Department of 
10 Inland Fisheries and Wildlife and the Department of Marine Resources.   Members of the 
11 commission include the commissioners of the natural resources agencies and the Director 
12 of the Office of Program Evaluation and Government Accountability.1
