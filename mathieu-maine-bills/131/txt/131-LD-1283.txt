14Sec. 1.  Task force established.  Resolved:  That the Task Force to Study the 
15 Creation of a Comprehensive Career and Technical Education System, referred to in this 
16 resolve as "the task force," is established.
17Sec. 2.  Task force membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
18 353, the task force consists of 20 members as follows:
19 1.  Six members appointed by the President of the Senate as follows:
20 A.  Two members of the Senate, including one member from each of the 2 parties 
21 holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature, one of whom is a member of the 
22 Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs;
23 B.  One member who is a current career and technical education high school 
24 administrator;
25 C.  One member who is on the State Board of Education; 
26 D.  One member who is a member of a skilled trades union or representative of a skilled 
27 trades business or industry; and
28 E.  One member who is a principal of a secondary school;
29 2.  Six members appointed by the Speaker of the House as follows:
30 A.  Two members of the House of Representatives, including one member from each 
31 of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature, one of whom is 
32 a member of the Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs;
33 B.  One member who is a current career and technical education high school 
34 administrator;
35 C.  One member who is on the State Board of Education; 
36 D.  One member who is a member of a skilled trades union or representative of a skilled 
37 trades business or industry; and
38 E.  One member who is a superintendent of a school administrative unit;
39 3.  Seven members appointed by the Governor as follows:
40 A.  One member who is an administrator at a community college;
Page 2 - 131LR0744(01)1 B.  One member who is on a local board of education in a Maine community;
2 C.  One member who is an officer of the Maine Education Association;
3 D.  Three members who are members of a skilled trades union or representatives of a 
4 skilled trades business or industry; and
5 E.  One member who is an administrator at the University of Maine System; and
6 4. The Commissioner of Education or the commissioner's designee.
7Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
8 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the task force.
9Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of task force.  Resolved:  That, 
10 notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the appointing authorities shall notify the Executive 
11 Director of the Legislative Council once all appointments have been completed.  After 
12 appointment of all members, the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the task 
13 force.
14Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the task force shall:
15 1.  Examine the feasibility of establishing a comprehensive 4-year high school career 
16 and technical education program to provide a technical high school setting for middle 
17 school students to attend at the completion of the 8th grade, including but not limited to the 
18 advantages and disadvantages of a comprehensive 4-year high school career and technical 
19 education model, obstacles to implementation of a comprehensive 4-year high school 
20 career and technical education model and other models for comprehensive 4-year high 
21 school career and technical education that exist around the State and on a national level; 
22 and
23 2.  Examine increasing crosswalks and intersections between technical and 
24 occupational knowledge and curricula and academic standards in order to promote multiple 
25 pathways for awarding content area credit to students enrolled in career and technical 
26 education programs, including but not limited to building on prior and current work among 
27 the Department of Education, superintendents of school administrative units and career and 
28 technical education administrators.
29Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
30 necessary staffing services to the task force, except that Legislative Council staff support 
31 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
32Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 6, 2023, the task force 
33 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
34 legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
35Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
36 takes effect when approved.
37SUMMARY
