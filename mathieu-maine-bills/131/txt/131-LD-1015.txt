17 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
18 commission.
19Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
20 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
21 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
22 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
23 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
24 after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
25 made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for 
26 the commission to meet and conduct its business.
27Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That in conducting the study the commission shall 
28 consider:
29 1.  The laws and state agency rules governing the foreclosure process, the various 
30 timelines of specific procedures in the foreclosure process and how those timelines affect 
31 the availability of foreclosed properties for affordable housing;
32 2.  The effect on the foreclosure process of mortgages sold in the secondary mortgage 
33 market by large financial institutions and the ability of small financial institutions in the 
34 State to work to keep property owners out of foreclosure and to make foreclosed property 
35 available for sale quickly; and
36 3.  How problems in the foreclosure process can be mitigated without losing existing 
37 consumer protections.
38Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
39 necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff support 
40 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.

6SUMMARY
7 This resolve establishes the Commission to Study the Foreclosure Process, which is a 
8 7-member commission directed to consider the laws and state agency rules governing the 
9 foreclosure process, the various timelines of specific procedures in the foreclosure process 
10 and how those timelines affect the availability of foreclosed properties for affordable 
11 housing; the effect on the foreclosure process of mortgages sold in the secondary mortgage 
12 market by large financial institutions and the ability of small financial institutions in the 
13 State to work to keep property owners out of foreclosure and to make foreclosed property 
14 available for sale quickly; and how problems in the foreclosure process can be mitigated 
15 without losing existing consumer protections.  The commission is required to submit a 
16 report no later than December 6, 2023, including suggested legislation, for presentation to 
17 the Joint Select Committee on Housing, which is authorized to submit legislation to the 
18 Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.7
