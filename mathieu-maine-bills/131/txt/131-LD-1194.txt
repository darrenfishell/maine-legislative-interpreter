62.  Work requirement. Except as provided in subsection 3, an individual is not eligible 
7 to participate in the MaineCare program unless that individual is:
8 A.  Working 20 hours or more per week, averaged monthly;
9 B.  Participating in and complying with the requirements of a work program 20 hours 
10 or more per week, as determined by the department;
11 C.  Volunteering 20 hours or more per week, as determined by the department;
12 D.  Meeting any combination of working and participating in a work program for a 
13 total of 20 hours or more per week, as determined by the department; or
14 E.  Participating in and complying with the requirements of a workfare program as 
15 implemented by the department.
163.  Exemptions from work requirement. The work requirement under subsection 2 
17 does not apply to a MaineCare member if the member:
18 A.  Has not attained 19 years of age;
19 B.  Is 65 years of age or older;
20 C.  Is medically certified as physically or mentally unfit for employment;
21 D.  Is pregnant;
22 E.  Is a parent or caregiver responsible for the care of a dependent child under one year 
23 of age;
24 F.  Is a parent or caregiver personally providing care for a dependent child with a 
25 serious medical condition or disability, as determined by the department;
26 G.  Is receiving unemployment compensation and complying with work requirements 
27 that are part of the unemployment compensation system under Title 26, chapter 13; or
28 H.  Is participating in substance use disorder treatment, including alcohol use disorder 
29 treatment.
304.  Rules.  The department shall adopt rules to implement this section. Rules adopted 
31 pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, 
32 subchapter 2-A.
33Sec. 2.  State plan amendments and waivers.  To the extent necessary to carry 
34 out the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 22, section 3173-K, the Department of Health and 
35 Human Services shall request federal approval from the United States Department of 
36 Health and Human Services, Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services for a Section 
37 1115 demonstration waiver under the United States Social Security Act by January 1, 2024. 
38 If federal approval for the waiver is denied or withdrawn, the department shall resubmit a 
39 request for approval within 24 months of each denial or withdrawal.
