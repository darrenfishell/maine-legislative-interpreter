15Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of task force. Resolved:  That, 
16 notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, all appointments must be made no later than 30 days 
17 following the effective date of this resolve. The appointing authorities shall notify the 
18 Executive Director of the Legislative Council once all appointments have been completed. 
19 After appointment of all members, the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the 
20 task force. If 30 days or more after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not 
21 all appointments have been made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative 
22 Council may grant authority for the task force to meet and conduct its business.
23 Each public member of the task force under section 2, subsection 11 serves for a term 
24 of 2 years except that, of the members first appointed, 3 members serve for an initial term 
25 of one year and 3 members serve for an initial term of 2 years.  Each public member serves 
26 for the term of appointment until the member's successor is appointed.  A public member 
27 who has completed a term of membership may be reappointed to the task force.  Vacancies 
28 in the task force must be filled in the same manner as the original appointments were made.
29Sec. 5.  Duties; meetings. Resolved:  That the task force shall convene a public 
30 process to study, examine and review all aspects of traffic safety, with a particular focus 
31 on access, equity and mobility for all road users using the safe system approach, and to 
32 advise the Governor, the Legislature, the Department of Transportation and the Maine 
33 Turnpike Authority regarding policies, programs, research and priorities to help achieve 
34 the goal of eliminating traffic fatalities and serious injuries.  To fulfill this purpose and 
35 these duties, the task force shall:
36 1.  Develop a comprehensive and coordinated action plan to help achieve the goal of 
37 eliminating traffic fatalities and serious injuries on all public roadways in the State through 
38 engineering, education and enforcement systems that analyze physical transportation 
39 designs, with a focus on the equitable treatment of all transportation users;
40 2.  Identify short-term and long-term data-driven strategies and street design best 
41 practices with measurable goals and target dates to reduce traffic fatalities and serious 
42 injuries, with the goal of eliminating all traffic fatalities and serious injuries by 2035, 
43 including studying the processes by which the Department of Transportation sets speed 
44 limits across the State and recommending evidence-based alternative methods;1

3 For the purposes of this resolve, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following 
4 terms have the following meanings.
5 "Active transportation" means mobility options that are powered primarily by human 
6 energy, including pedestrian mobility, such as walking and running; mobility assistive 
7 devices, such as wheelchairs, bicycles and tricycles; and small personal devices, such as 
8 skateboards and foot scooters, and that include accommodations pursuant to the federal 
9 Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990.
10 "Safe system approach" means evaluating traffic safety and designing a transportation 
11 system with the goal of eliminating fatal and serious injuries for all road users by 
12 acknowledging that traffic fatalities and serious injuries are unacceptable, humans make 
13 mistakes, humans are vulnerable, responsibility is shared, safety is proactive and 
14 redundancy is crucial.
15 "Vision Zero strategies" means actionable strategies using the safe system approach, 
16 including, but not limited to, prioritizing roadway design, focusing on speed management, 
17 ensuring equitable enforcement and using effective education strategies.
18Sec. 6.  Staff assistance. Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353,  the 
19 Legislative Council shall provide necessary staffing services to the task force, except that 
20 Legislative Council staff support is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or 
21 special session.
22Sec. 7.  Reports.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, no later than 
23 December 6, 2023, the task force shall submit a report that includes its initial findings and 
24 recommendations, the number of road traffic fatalities and serious injuries and a review of 
25 the implementation of the action plans under section 5 and any suggested legislation for 
26 presentation to the Governor and the Joint Standing Committee on Transportation.  The 
27 Joint Standing Committee on Transportation may submit legislation for presentation to the 
28 Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.  Updated reports must be submitted to 
29 the Governor and the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
30 transportation matters in 2024 and 2025.
