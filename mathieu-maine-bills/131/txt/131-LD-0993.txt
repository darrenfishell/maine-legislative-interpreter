8§8007.  Maine Forest Advisory Board established
9 The Maine Forest Advisory Board, as established in Title 5, section 12004-I, 
10 subsection 24-H and referred to in this section as "the board," advises the bureau and 
11 provides input in the development, updating and implementation of the state forest action 
12 plan required under the federal Food, Conservation, and Energy Act of 2008, Public Law 
13 110-234 and Public Law 110-246.

242.  Terms; quorum; meetings.  Except for the member appointed under subsection 1, 
25 paragraph C, subparagraph (1), members serve for a term of 3 years. At the end of a term 
26 an outgoing member serves until a successor has been appointed. A member may serve 
27 multiple terms.  The board shall meet at least 4 times a year to conduct business and to elect 
28 officers. A quorum consists of at least 11 members. The affirmative vote of a majority of 
29 the members present is necessary to decide any business brought before the board.  
303.  Duties of board.  The board shall:  
31 A.  Advise the bureau on conditions and trends in the forests of the State;
32 B.  Facilitate dialogue among diverse interests involving forestry matters;
33 C.  Promote cooperation among state agencies involving forestry matters;
34 D.  Advise the bureau on forestry policy development and implementation; 
35 E.  Ensure public participation in the development of forestry policy; 
36 F.  Provide input in the development, updating and implementation of the state forest 
37 action plan required under the federal Food, Conservation, and Energy Act of 2008, 
38 Public Law 110-234 and Public Law 110-246; and
39 G.  Submit annual reports to the Legislature on its work under this section.

5SUMMARY
6 This bill creates the Maine Forest Advisory Board to advise the Department of 
7 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, Bureau of Forestry on a variety of forestry issues 
8 and to provide input on the state forest action plan required under the federal Food, 
9 Conservation, and Energy Act of 2008.  The board is directed to submit annual reports to 
10 the Legislature, including a report outlining conditions and trends in the State's forests to 
11 be submitted by December 6, 2023 to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.6
