16SUMMARY
17 This bill accomplishes the following.
18 1.  It amends the definition of “scallop” to include bay scallops.
19 2.  It amends a definition of “shellfish” to include scallops.
20 3.  It amends the definition of “whole scallop” to exclude the adductor muscle on the 
21 shell.
22 4.  It clarifies that a license-holding member of the Passamaquoddy Tribe, Penobscot 
23 Nation, Aroostook Band of Micmacs or Houlton Band of Maliseet Indians is not required 
24 to complete the lobster fishery apprentice program if the license applicant has completed 
25 an apprentice program that satisfies the tribe, nation or band.
26 5. It replaces a license exemption for personal use to the commercial halibut fishing 
27 license, adds a violation for fishing for halibut without a commercial halibut fishing license 
28 and requires the Commissioner of Marine Resources to adopt rules for commercial halibut 
29 fishing licenses.
30 6. It allows the holder of an enhanced retail certificate to purchase shellfish from the 
31 holder of an aquaculture license.
32 7. It corrects cross-references in the administrative suspension process for licenses and 
33 certificates issued under marine resources laws.17
