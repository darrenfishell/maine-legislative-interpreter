14Sec. 1.  Commission established.  Resolved:  That the Commission to 
15 Recommend Cost-effective, Safe, Administrable and Healthful Programs to Reduce 
16 Violence in Prisons and Jails, referred to in this resolve as "the commission," is established.
17Sec. 2.  Commission membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
18 353, the commission consists of 15 members as follows:
19 1.  Two members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including one 
20 member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
21 2.  Three members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
22 House, including at least one member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number 
23 of seats in the Legislature;
24 3.  The Commissioner of Corrections or the commissioner's designee;
25 4.  A member representing the Maine Medical Association, appointed by the President 
26 of the Senate; 
27 5.  A member representing a statewide organization advocating for the interests of 
28 people who are incarcerated, appointed by the President of the Senate;
29 6.  A member representing the organization responsible for collective bargaining for 
30 correctional officers in the State, appointed by the President of the Senate;
31 7.  A member representing an organization advocating for the interests of persons with 
32 developmental disabilities or behavioral health issues, including substance use disorder, 
33 appointed by the President of the Senate;
34 8.  A member representing a public health organization, appointed by the President of 
35 the Senate;
36 9.  A member representing the Maine Sheriffs' Association, appointed by the Speaker 
37 of the House;
38 10.  A member with lived experience of solitary confinement, appointed by the Speaker 
39 of the House;
Page 2 - 131LR2081(01)1 11.  A member representing an organization advocating for the interests of racial 
2 minorities, appointed by the Speaker of the House; and 
3 12.  A member representing the Maine Association of Psychiatric Physicians, 
4 appointed by the Speaker of the House.
5 The commission may request that individuals with specific expertise in methods, costs 
6 and impacts of reducing violence in prisons and jails, including but not limited to current 
7 staff of the Department of Corrections, members of the State Sheriffs' Association and other 
8 experts, serve as consultants to the commission.
9Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
10 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
11 commission.
12Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
13 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
14 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
15 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
16 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
17 after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
18 made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for 
19 the commission to meet and conduct its business.
20Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall examine violence in prisons 
21 and jails in the State, including resident or inmate on staff violence, resident or inmate on 
22 resident or inmate violence, staff on resident or inmate violence, self-harm by residents or 
23 inmates and self-harm by staff, and the current methods of responding to that violence.  In 
24 conducting its examination, the commission shall consider:
25 1.  The conditions, including, but not limited to housing, health, health treatment and 
26 program status, surrounding the violence in each case;
27 2.  Different models of care and management, both domestic and international, used to 
28 reduce violence in prisons and jails;
29 3.  The costs and savings of instituting model administrable violence reduction 
30 programs in prisons and jails in the State;
31 4.  The effects of violence in prisons and jails on staff retention and recruitment, health 
32 care costs, reentry success and recidivism; and
33 5.  The development of measures to reduce violence in prisons and jails in the State.
34 The Commissioner of Corrections shall ensure that members of the commission have 
35 complete access to the inside of the jails or prisons in the State as needed during the course 
36 of the commission's performance of its duties under this section and have the ability to 
37 interview any persons who are incarcerated.
38Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
39 necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff support 
40 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
Page 3 - 131LR2081(01)1Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 6, 2023, the commission 
2 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
3 legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
4Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
5 takes effect when approved.
6SUMMARY
7 This resolve establishes the Commission to Recommend Cost-effective, Safe, 
8 Administrable and Healthful Programs to Reduce Violence in Prisons and Jails.  The 
9 commission is directed to examine the issue of violence in prisons and jails in the State, 
10 including the development of measures to reduce that violence, and to submit a report by 
11 December 6, 2023 for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.7
