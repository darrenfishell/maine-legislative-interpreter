3ยง683.  Dangerous weapons at voting places
41. Prohibition.  A person may not carry or discharge a dangerous weapon:
5 A. While present in a voting place or on public property within 250 feet of a voting 
6 place during the time that the polls are open until the completion of election duties at 
7 the voting place on election day;
8 B. In a location where absentee voting is occurring in the presence of the clerk or on 
9 public property within 250 feet of that location; or
10 C. In a location where the clerk's office is receiving or emptying absentee ballots via a 
11 secured drop box or on public property within 250 feet of that location. 
12 For purposes of this section, "dangerous weapon" has the same meaning as defined in Title 
13 17-A, section 2, subsection 9.
142. Exception. Subsection 1 does not apply to:
15 A. A law enforcement officer in the performance of the officer's official duties; or
16 B. A dangerous weapon in a locked motor vehicle that is not removed from the motor 
17 vehicle or carried by a person.
183. Penalty. A person who violates this section commits a civil violation for which a 
19 penalty of not more than $1,000 may be adjudged.
20SUMMARY
21 This bill restricts dangerous weapons at voting places. The bill provides an exception
