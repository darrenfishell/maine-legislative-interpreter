6SUMMARY
7 This bill prohibits a public utility from including in operating expenses costs, whether 
8 incurred by the public utility or an affiliated interest, as defined in the Maine Revised 
9 Statutes, Title 35-A, section 707, subsection 1, paragraph A, associated with educational 
10 expenditures in excess of $1,000, political contributions and charitable contributions.  A 
11 public utility may include in operating expenses costs for educational expenditures in 
12 excess of $1,000 with the preapproval of the Public Utilities Commission. The bill requires 
13 a public utility to file an annual report with the commission of the political, charitable and 
14 educational expenses of the utility and its affiliated interests. If a public utility or an 
15 affiliated interest engages in major political activities, it must also file a quarterly report 
16 with the commission. The bill requires commission rules regarding charitable contributions 
17 and educational expenditures by a public utility or an affiliated interest to be major 
18 substantive rules.1
