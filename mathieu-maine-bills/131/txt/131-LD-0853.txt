7Constitutional referendum procedure; form of question; effective date.  
8Resolved:  That the municipal officers of this State shall notify the inhabitants of their 
9 respective cities, towns and plantations to meet, in the manner prescribed by law for holding 
10 a statewide election, at a statewide election held in the month of November following the 
11 passage of this resolution, to vote upon the ratification of the amendment proposed in this 
12 resolution by voting upon the following question:
13 "Do you favor amending the Constitution of Maine to declare that all 
14 individuals have a natural, inherent and unalienable right to housing?"
15 The legal voters of each city, town and plantation shall vote by ballot on this question 
16 and designate their choice by a cross or check mark placed within the corresponding square 
17 below the word "Yes" or "No."  The ballots must be received, sorted, counted and declared 
18 in open ward, town and plantation meetings and returns made to the Secretary of State in 
19 the same manner as votes for members of the Legislature.  The Governor shall review the 
20 returns.  If it appears that a majority of the legal votes are cast in favor of the amendment, 
21 the Governor shall proclaim that fact without delay and the amendment becomes part of 
22 the Constitution of Maine on the date of the proclamation.
23Secretary of State shall prepare ballots.  Resolved:  That the Secretary of State 
24 shall prepare and furnish to each city, town and plantation all ballots, returns and copies of 
25 this resolution necessary to carry out the purposes of this referendum.
26SUMMARY
27 This resolution proposes to amend the Constitution of Maine to declare that all
