Page 1 - 131LR2075(01)1Sec. 1. Subdivision laws review required.  Resolved:  That the Department of 
2 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, in coordination with the Department of 
3 Environmental Protection, shall convene a stakeholder group to review the subdivision 
4 laws in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 12, chapter 206-A and Title 38, chapter 3, 
5 subchapter 1, article 6.  The stakeholder group shall recommend a comprehensive overhaul 
6 and modernization of the subdivision laws that promotes development in growth areas; 
7 reduces development pressure in rural areas; protects public health, safety and welfare; 
8 advances state goals to produce affordable housing; and streamlines the development 
9 review process.  At a minimum, the stakeholder group must include representatives from 
10 municipal government, municipal planners, real estate developers, municipal law attorneys 
11 and representatives of conservation organizations.  By December 6, 2023, the Department 
12 of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, in coordination with the Department of 
13 Environmental Protection, shall submit a report that includes its findings and 
14 recommendations, including suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on 
15 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry and the Joint Standing Committee on Environment 
16 and Natural Resources.  The joint standing committees may report out legislation related 
17 to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
18SUMMARY
19 This resolve requires the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, in 
20 coordination with the Department of Environmental Protection, to convene a stakeholder 
21 group to review and recommend a comprehensive overhaul and modernization of the 
22 subdivision laws in the Maine Revised Statutes, Titles 12 and 38.  The departments must 
23 submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry 
24 and the Joint Standing Committee on Environment and Natural Resources, and the joint 
25 standing committees may report out legislation related to the report to the Second Regular 
26 Session of the 131st Legislature.19
