10Sec. 2.  22 MRSA §1600 is enacted to read:
11§1600.  Prohibition on abortion-inducing drugs or devices
121. Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
13 following terms have the following meanings.
14 A. "Abortion" has the same meaning as in section 1598, subsection 2, paragraph A.
15 B. "Abortion-inducing drug" means a drug prescribed or dispensed with the intent of 
16 inducing an abortion, including the off-label use of a drug known to have abortion-
17 inducing properties that is prescribed specifically with the intent to induce an abortion.
182. Prohibitions.  A person may not:
19 A. Prescribe an abortion-inducing drug or a device designed to produce an abortion by 
20 telehealth, telephone, the Internet or other type of digital communication; or
21 B. Ship an abortion-inducing drug or a device designed to produce an abortion by mail, 
22 express delivery service or other means of interstate commerce.
233. Violation. A person who violates this section commits a Class C crime.
244. Civil remedies.  A person who violates this section is subject to Maine law 
25 governing civil liability for wrongful death and medical malpractice and is subject to any 
26 relevant professional disciplinary proceeding.
27SUMMARY
28 This bill prohibits the prescription of an abortion-inducing drug or a device designed 
29 to produce an abortion by telehealth, telephone, the Internet or other type of digital 
30 communication and the shipment of an abortion-inducing drug or a device designed to 
31 produce an abortion by mail, express delivery service or other means of interstate 
32 commerce and makes a violation of this prohibition a Class C crime and subjects a person 
33 who violates the prohibition to civil liability for wrongful death and medical malpractice 
34 and to any relevant professional disciplinary proceeding.28
