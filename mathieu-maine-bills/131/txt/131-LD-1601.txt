6Sec. 6.  22 MRSA §7926 is enacted to read:
7§7926.  Visitation policies
8 No later than January 1, 2024, each long-term care facility and hospice provider must 
9 establish a visitation policy for visitors and designated visitors as follows.
101.  Circumstances for in-person visit requirements.  The policy must allow in-person 
11 visitation in all of the following circumstances unless the resident objects:
12 A. End-of-life situations;
13 B. A resident who was living with family before being admitted to the long-term care 
14 facility or hospice and is struggling with the change in environment and lack of in-
15 person family support;
16 C. A resident is making one or more major medical decisions;
17 D. A resident is experiencing emotional distress or grieving the loss of a friend or 
18 family member who recently died;
19 E. A resident needs cueing or encouragement to eat or drink that was previously 
20 provided by a family member or caregiver;
21 F. A resident who used to talk and interact with others is seldom speaking; and
22 G. The resident is under 18 years of age.
232. Designated visitors.  In addition to a policy for in-person visitation, the policy must 
24 allow for in-person visits to a resident by the resident's designated visitor for at least 2 hours 
25 daily.  This section does not require a designated visitor to provide necessary care to the 
26 resident, and providers may not require a designated visitor to provide such care.
273.  Infection control and education.  The policy must, at a minimum, include infection 
28 control and education policies for visitors; screening, personal protective equipment and 
29 other infection control protocols for visitors; permissible length of visits and numbers of

34 consensual physical contact between a resident and visitors.

8SUMMARY
9 This bill requires long-term care facilities, hospice providers and hospitals to adopt a 
10 visitation policy that is provided to the Department of Health and Human Services.  The 
11 policy must be adopted by January 1, 2024.  The policy must permit in-person visitation in 
12 certain circumstances.  The policy must include infection control and education policies 
13 for visitors and may not require visitors to provide proof of vaccinations.  It also requires 
14 the policy to allow for a resident of a facility to designate a family member, friend, guardian 
15 or other person as a designated visitor that may visit in person for at least 2 hours a day in 
16 addition to other visitors.  The policy must be posted on the hospital's website and a long-
17 term care facility's or hospice provider's website if such a website exists.  The department 
18 must post information on visitation requirements on its website and establish a process for 
19 the public to report complaints against providers.1
