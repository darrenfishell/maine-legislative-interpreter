3SUBCHAPTER 7
4ELECTION FUNDING
5ยง871.  Private funding prohibited
61.  Short title. This section may be known and cited as "the Prohibition on the Private 
7 Funding of Elections Act."
82.  Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
9 following terms have the following meanings.
10 A.  "Election" means an election held to nominate or elect a candidate to a public office.
11 B.  "Person" means an individual, proprietorship, firm, partnership, joint venture, 
12 syndicate, labor union, business trust, company, association, committee or corporation, 
13 whether or not operated for profit, or any other organization or group of persons acting 
14 in concert or any other nongovernmental 3rd-party entity.
15 C.  "Public funds" means funds derived from taxes, fees and other sources of public 
16 revenue lawfully appropriated by the Legislature.
173.  Prohibition on private funds.  Notwithstanding any provision of law to the 
18 contrary, a public official may not solicit, take or otherwise accept from any person a 
19 contribution, donation or anything else of value for purposes of conducting state or local 
20 elections. All costs and expenses relating to state or local elections must be paid for with 
21 public funds.
224.  Construction.  This section may not be construed to apply to the donation or use of 
23 a location for voting purposes, services that are provided without remuneration or goods 
24 that have a nominal value of less than $100.
255.  Violation.  A violation of this section is a Class C crime.
26SUMMARY
27 This bill provides that a public official may not solicit, take or otherwise accept from 
28 any person a contribution, donation or anything else of value for purposes of conducting 
29 state or local elections.  It requires that all costs and expenses relating to state or local 
30 elections must be paid for with public funds.  A violation of the prohibition is a Class C 
31 crime.27
