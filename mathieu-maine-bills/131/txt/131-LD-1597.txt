Page 1 - 131LR1688(01)1Sec. 1.  Study commission established.  Resolved:  That the Gagetown Harmful 
2 Chemical Study Commission, referred to in this resolve as "the study commission," is 
3 established.
4Sec. 2.  Study commission membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint 
5 Rule 353, the study commission consists of 10 members appointed as follows:
6 1.  Two members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including 
7 members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
8 2. Two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
9 House, including members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
10 in the Legislature;
11 3. Two members who represent veterans' advocacy organizations, one appointed by the 
12 President of the Senate and one appointed by the Speaker of the House;
13 4. One member who is a family member of a veteran who served at the Canadian 
14 military support base in Gagetown, New Brunswick, Canada, appointed by the President 
15 of the Senate;
16 5. One member with expertise processing veterans' claims for benefits related to 
17 harmful chemicals, appointed by the Speaker of the House; and
18 6. Two members who served at Gagetown and were exposed to harmful chemicals 
19 during their service, one appointed by the President of the Senate and one appointed by the 
20 Speaker of the House.
21Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
22 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the study 
23 commission.
24Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of study commission.  Resolved:  That all 
25 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
26 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
27 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
28 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the study commission.  If 30 days or 
29 more after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have 
30 been made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant 
31 authority for the study commission to meet and conduct its business.
32Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the study commission shall study the impacts of 
33 exposure to harmful chemicals on veterans who served at the Canadian military support 
34 base in Gagetown, New Brunswick, Canada.
35Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
36 necessary staffing services to the study commission, except that Legislative Council staff 
37 support is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
38Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 6, 2023, the study 
39 commission shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including 
40 suggested legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 131st 
41 Legislature.
