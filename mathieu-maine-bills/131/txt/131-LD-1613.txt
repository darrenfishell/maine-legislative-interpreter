6 the following terms have the following meanings.
7 A.  "Civil rights officer" means a law enforcement officer who has received special 
8 training approved by the Attorney General in identifying and investigating civil rights 
9 violations.
10 B.  "Law enforcement agency" means an agency in the State charged with enforcement

18 or customs laws, including, but not limited to, a law enforcement officer who possesses 
19 a valid certificate issued by the Board of Trustees of the Maine Criminal Justice 
20 Academy pursuant to Title 25, section 2803-A.  
212.  Required civil rights officer.  A law enforcement agency shall select, assign and 
22 secure training for a civil rights officer, notify the Attorney General of the name and contact 
23 information for the civil rights officer and notify the Attorney General of any change in 
24 assignment of the civil rights officer as soon as practicable after the change is made.  The 
25 law enforcement agency shall make the contact information for the civil rights officer 
26 publicly available.

323.  Profiling.  "Profiling" means relying, to any degree, on actual or perceived race, 
33 gender, ethnicity, religion, socioeconomic status, ancestry, sexual orientation, gender 
34 identity, color, physical or mental disability or national origin in targeting an individual for 
35 routine or spontaneous investigatory activities or in deciding upon the scope and substance 
36 of law enforcement activity following the initial investigatory procedure, except in the 
37 event the officer or agency is relying on trustworthy information that is relevant to the 
38 locality and time frame and that links a person with a particular characteristic described in 
39 this section to a specific criminal incident or scheme.

25SUMMARY
26 In Part A, this bill requires law enforcement agencies to select, assign and train civil 
27 rights officers and to keep the Attorney General informed of the identity of the civil rights 
28 officer.  It requires law enforcement agencies to make contact information for their civil 
29 rights officers publicly available.  In Part B, the bill prohibits profiling by a law 
30 enforcement officer or agency based on race, gender, ethnicity, religion, socioeconomic 
31 status, ancestry, sexual orientation, gender identity, color, physical or mental disability or 
32 national origin.  In Part C, the bill changes from January 1, 2023 to January 1, 2024 the 
33 date by which the Attorney General must adopt rules for the recording, retention and 
34 reporting of information regarding traffic infractions and from January 15, 2024 to January 
35 15, 2025 the date by which the Attorney General must begin submitting the information to 
36 the joint standing committees of the Legislature having jurisdiction over judiciary matters 
37 and criminal justice and public safety matters.  It also makes the changes in Part C 
38 retroactive to December 31, 2023.26
