5ยง3631. Peer respite centers
61. Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
7 following terms have the following meanings.
8 A. "Certified peer support specialist" means a peer who has successfully completed an 
9 intentional peer support training offered through the department.
10 B. "Intentional peer support" means support offered by a certified peer support 
11 specialist through a community-based and evidence-based program.
12 C. "Peer" means a person who has experience living with mental health crises, 
13 including trauma, and has experience being a consumer of the mental health system.
14 D. "Peer respite center" or "center" means a residential living environment managed 
15 and staffed by peers that offers an intentional peer support program offered by a 
16 certified peer support specialist to persons with mental health needs.
172. Establishment of peer respite centers required.  The department shall establish 
18 through contracts 2 peer respite centers in the State for persons 18 years of age or older.  
19 The centers must provide 24-hour intentional peer support to persons in need of mental 
20 health services on a voluntary, short-term basis.  The centers must be:
21 A. In geographically diverse locations in the State;
22 B. Located in areas of the State that have populations with a high utilization rate of 
23 crisis services, according to data maintained by the department; and
24 C. Operated by:
25 (1) A peer-run organization; or
26 (2) An organization with a board of directors or an advisory group in which 51% 
27 or more of its members are peers.
283. Rulemaking.  The department shall prescribe by rule the requirements for its 
29 contracts with peer respite centers, including required data reporting or other reports to 
30 allow the department to monitor and evaluate the services provided by the centers.  The 
31 department shall adopt other rules as necessary to implement this section.  Rules adopted 
32 pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, 
33 subchapter 2-A.
