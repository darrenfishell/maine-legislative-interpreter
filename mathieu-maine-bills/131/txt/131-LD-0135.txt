4§1909-D.  Animal-drawn vehicles
5 This section governs the required equipment for certain animal‑drawn vehicles 
6 operated on a public way.
71.  Nighttime equipment.  An animal‑drawn vehicle operated on a public way during 
8 nighttime must be equipped with the following:
9 A.  An oil lantern or electric lights attached to the left side of the vehicle that displays 
10 a red light toward the rear of the vehicle and a white light toward the front of the 
11 vehicle; and
12 B.  Grade DOT‑C2 white reflective tape as described in 49 Code of Federal 
13 Regulations, Section 571.108, in effect on March 26, 2019, applied to the vehicle as 
14 follows:
15 (1)  Seventy‑two inches of reflective tape applied in segments that outline the rear 
16 frame of the vehicle;
17 (2)  Thirty‑six inches of reflective tape on each side of the vehicle, applied in 
18 segments that outline the frame on each side with at least 2 segments applied to the 
19 upper borders of the frame; and
20 (3)  Forty‑two inches of reflective tape applied in segments that outline the front 
21 frame of the vehicle.
222.  Manure bags.  A horse-drawn vehicle operated on a public way must be equipped 
23 with manure bags of sufficient capacity to hold the amount of manure produced by each 
24 horse in one day.  The operator of a horse-drawn vehicle may not empty a manure bag at a 
25 location other than the location where the horse is stabled.
26SUMMARY
27 This bill requires a horse-drawn vehicle operated on a public way to be equipped with 
28 manure bags of sufficient capacity to hold the amount of manure produced by each horse 
29 in one day.  It prohibits the operator of a horse-drawn vehicle from emptying a manure bag 
30 at a location other than the location where the horse is stabled.27
