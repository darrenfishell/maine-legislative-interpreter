3CHAPTER 613
4EMPOWERMENT SCHOLARSHIP ACCOUNTS
5§17201.  Definitions
6 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 
7 have the following meanings.
81.  Eligible student.  "Eligible student" means a resident of this State who is eligible 
9 to enroll in an elementary or secondary public school.
102.  Empowerment scholarship account.  "Empowerment scholarship account" means 
11 an account established under this chapter.
123.  Participating student.  "Participating student" means an eligible student who is 
13 participating in the program.
144.  Program.  "Program" means the Empowerment Scholarship Account Program 
15 established under section 17202.
16§17202.  Empowerment Scholarship Account Program
171.  Establishment of program.  The department shall establish the Empowerment 
18 Scholarship Account Program to create empowerment scholarship accounts to which 
19 money is deposited by the department as described in subsection 3.
202.  Use of empowerment scholarship accounts.  A parent or legal guardian of a 
21 participating student may withdraw money from an empowerment scholarship account to 
22 pay for education expenses to educate the participating student as provided by this 
23 subsection. A parent or legal guardian of a participating student must agree to use the funds 
24 deposited in the student's empowerment scholarship account only for tuition and fees at a 
25 private school.
26 Money in an empowerment scholarship account may not be refunded or rebated to or shared 
27 with a parent or legal guardian of a participating student or a participating student. A refund 
28 or rebate for goods or services purchased with money from an empowerment scholarship 
29 account must be credited directly to that empowerment scholarship account.
303.  Deposits into empowerment scholarship accounts.  The department shall deposit 
31 into an empowerment scholarship account an amount equal to 50% of the average amount 
32 spent by the State on public school students in the previous school year.
33 Money deposited in an empowerment scholarship account does not constitute taxable 
34 income for the parent or legal guardian of the participating student or the participating 
35 student for the purposes of State income taxes.
36§17203.  Application for program
371.  Application.  A parent or legal guardian of an eligible student may apply to the 
38 department to establish an empowerment scholarship account for the eligible student.  The 
Page 2 - 131LR1631(01)39 department shall accept applications year round and shall establish procedures for 
40 approving applications in a timely manner.
32.  Approval.  The department shall approve an application for an empowerment 
4 scholarship account if:
5 A.  The parent or legal guardian of an eligible student submits an application for an 
6 empowerment scholarship account in accordance with application procedures 
7 established by the department; and
8 B.  The application is for an empowerment scholarship account to be created for an 
9 eligible student.
10§17204.  Rules
11 The department shall adopt rules to implement this chapter. Rules adopted pursuant to 
12 this paragraph are major substantive rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 
13 2‑A.
14Sec. 2.  Eligibility.  A student who is enrolled in a private school on the effective 
15 date of this Act is not eligible to participate in the Empowerment Scholarship Account 
16 Program created under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, chapter 613 until the 
17 2025-2026 school year.
18SUMMARY
19 This bill establishes a program in the Department of Education to create empowerment 
20 scholarship accounts for participating students to be funded by the State and used to pay 
21 expenses for private school.1
