39.  Emergency funding for nonprofit food banks.  During a state of emergency 
4 declared in accordance with Title 37-B, section 742, subsection 1, the Governor may 
5 distribute up to $400,000 from the stabilization fund to nonprofit entities, including food 
6 banks, food pantries and soup kitchens, that provide or distribute food to low-income, 
7 indigent or unemployed individuals or households without charge. The Commissioner of 
8 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry shall adopt rules to prescribe the manner in which 
9 the funds distributed under this subsection must be disbursed.  The commissioner may 
10 leverage funds distributed under this subsection to match any relevant federal funding 
11 available for qualifying food programs in the State.  Rules adopted pursuant to this 
12 subsection are routine technical rules as described in chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
13SUMMARY
14 This bill authorizes the Governor to distribute up to $400,000 from the Maine Budget 
15 Stabilization Fund during a state of emergency to nonprofit entities, including food banks, 
16 food pantries and soup kitchens, that provide or distribute food to low-income, indigent or 
17 unemployed individuals or households without charge.
18 It directs the Commissioner of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to adopt rules 
19 to prescribe the manner in which the emergency funding for nonprofit entities must be 
20 disbursed and authorizes the commissioner to leverage emergency funding for nonprofit 
21 entities to match any relevant federal funding available for qualifying food programs in the 
22 State.14
