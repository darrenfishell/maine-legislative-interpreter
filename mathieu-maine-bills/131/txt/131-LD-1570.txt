3ยง420-F.  Storm Water Infrastructure Repair and Replacement Fund
41.  Fund established.  The Storm Water Infrastructure Repair and Replacement Fund, 
5 referred to in this section as "the fund," is established in the department to issue grants to 
6 municipalities to repair or replace storm water infrastructure.  A grant may be issued to a 
7 municipality to repair or replace storm water infrastructure that is damaged, is nearing the 
8 end of its life expectancy, has failed or is a hazard, or for any other purpose determined by 
9 rule by the department.  The fund consists of money received as appropriations, allocations 
10 and contributions from private and public sources.  The fund, to be accounted within the 
11 department, must be held separate and apart from all other money, funds and accounts.  
12 Eligible investment earnings credited to the assets of the fund become part of the assets of 
13 the fund.  Any unexpended balances remaining in the fund at the end of any fiscal year do 
14 not lapse and must be carried forward to the next fiscal year.  For the purposes of this 
15 subsection, "storm water infrastructure" includes, but is not limited to, culverts, pipes, catch 
16 basins, treatment ponds, treatment devices and storm water infiltration systems.
172.  Rules.  The department shall adopt routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
18 chapter 375, subchapter 2-A to carry out the purposes of the fund.
19Sec. 2. Storm Water Infrastructure Repair and Replacement Fund; 
20alternative funding sources.  The Department of Environmental Protection shall 
21 explore alternative stable funding sources for the Storm Water Infrastructure Repair and 
22 Replacement Fund established in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 38, section 420-F.  By 
23 December 6, 2023, the department shall submit a report and recommendations, including 
24 any suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Environment and Natural 
25 Resources.  The committee may submit legislation related to the report to the Second 
26 Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
27Sec. 3. Transfer from unappropriated surplus of General Fund to Storm

30 1, 2023 from the unappropriated surplus of the General Fund to the Storm Water 
31 Infrastructure Repair and Replacement Fund, Other Special Revenue Funds account in the 
32 Department of Environmental Protection.

10SUMMARY
11 This bill establishes the Storm Water Infrastructure Repair and Replacement Fund in 
12 the Department of Environmental Protection for the purpose of issuing grants to 
13 municipalities to repair or replace storm water infrastructure. It provides funding for grants 
14 and one Environmental Engineer position. It also requires the department to explore 
15 alternative stable funding sources for the fund and to submit recommendations to the Joint 
16 Standing Committee on Environment and Natural Resources. The committee is authorized 
17 to submit legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.1
