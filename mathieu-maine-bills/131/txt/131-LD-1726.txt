7§3731-A.  Maine Child Care Scholarship Program
8 The Maine Child Care Scholarship Program is established in the department to provide 
9 child care subsidies under this chapter in accordance with the principles described under 
10 section 3732 to support the quality child care needs of children and working families while 
11 also providing a living wage to early childhood educators.  Eligibility for child care 
12 subsidies under the Maine Child Care Scholarship Program must be determined in 
13 accordance with this chapter and rules adopted pursuant to this chapter.  Rules adopted 
14 pursuant to this paragraph are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, 
15 subchapter 2-A.

9Sec. 7.  22 MRSA §3740-B is enacted to read:
10§3740-B.  Quality child care costs estimator tool
11 The department, in consultation with the Children's Cabinet established under Title 5, 
12 section 19131, subsection 1 and the council, shall create and maintain an estimator tool in 
13 accordance with this section to calculate the actual cost of quality child care.
141. Requirements.  The estimator tool must:
15 A. Include a living wage compensation scale for early childhood educators pursuant to 
16 the recommendations of the Child Care Task Force established in section 3740-E;
17 B. Include regional differences in costs;
18 C. Include employee salaries and benefits;
19 D. Include facility costs;
20 E. Include costs associated with compliance with statutory and rule requirements, 
21 including the child care quality rating system participation under section 3737, 
22 subsection 3 and specific costs associated with each step of the rating system and any 
23 quality indicators used; and
24 F. Use data and research available from existing studies and reports.
252. Considerations.  In developing the estimator tool, the department shall:
26 A. Evaluate the effectiveness of current boundaries of child care subsidy regions;
27 B. Consider differentiating child care subsidy rates by child age and geographic region; 
28 and
29 C. Examine alternatives to the current system under paragraph B, such as zip code level 
30 regions or regionalization based on urban, suburban or rural designations.
313. Accessibility.  The estimator tool must be publicly accessible.  The department's 
32 process for making changes to the tool must be transparent to the public.

12Sec. 11.  Department of Health and Human Services to make changes to 
13the child care subsidy program.  The Department of Health and Human Services shall:
14 1. Review department rules, department materials and department publications, 
15 including on the department's publicly accessible website, and replace references to "Child 
16 Care Subsidy Program" with "Maine Child Care Scholarship Program"; 
17 2. Promote the Maine Child Care Scholarship Program, established in the Maine 
18 Revised Statutes, Title 22, section 3731-A, statewide with a plan to reach underserved and 
19 marginalized populations; and
20 3. Streamline and simplify the online invoicing and billing systems for child care 
21 providers to accurately reflect reimbursements, including quality bumps, payments to child 
22 care programs and parent copays, and to isolate payments for the changes to eligibility 
23 under the department's child care subsidy rules from state funding.
24Sec. 12.  Child care subsidy rules.  The Department of Health and Human Services 
25 shall amend its rules in Child Care Subsidy Program Rules, Chapter 6 to:
26 1. Increase eligibility for child care subsidies from 85% of the State's median income 
27 to 125% of the State's median income;
28 2. Add eligibility for an employee of a licensed child care center or family child care 
29 provider if the applicant otherwise meets the eligibility requirements after the department 
30 disregards all of the employee's earned and unearned income and verifies the employee's 
31 employment; and
32 3. Reimburse child care providers that accept child care subsidies based on enrollment 
33 and not attendance.

Page 8 - 131LR1449(01)1SUMMARY
2 This bill establishes the Maine Child Care Scholarship Program within the Department 
3 of Health and Human Services to encompass the former child care subsidy program and 
4 new eligibility for child care providers.  The bill directs the department to amend its 
5 procedures regarding eligibility and reimbursement, submit an annual report relating to 
6 child care subsidies to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction 
7 over health and human services matters and to implement various departmental 
8 improvements to the child care subsidy program.  The bill establishes the Child Care Task 
9 Force to make recommendations to the department regarding quality child care.  The bill 
10 provides funding to increase eligibility and wages for child care services and to implement 
11 the improvements to child care subsidies.  The bill also directs the department, by 2030, to 
12 implement a program to achieve affordable child care.1
