Page 1 - 131LR2155(01)1Sec. 1.  Commission established.  Resolved:  That the Commission to Study the 
2 Constitution of Maine, referred to in this resolve as "the commission," is established.
3Sec. 2.  Commission membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
4 353, the commission consists of 13 members as follows:
5 1.  Three members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including 
6 members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
7 2.  Three members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
8 House, including members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
9 in the Legislature;
10 3.  Two active or retired judges or justices appointed by the Chief Justice of the 
11 Supreme Judicial Court;
12 4.  Two constitutional law scholars with expertise in the history of the Constitution of 
13 Maine appointed by the President of the Senate;
14 5. One member of a federally recognized Indian tribe in the State appointed by the 
15 Speaker of the House;
16 6. One representative of the state chapter of a national civil liberties organization 
17 appointed by the Speaker of the House; and
18 7. The Secretary of State or the Secretary of State's designee.
19Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
20 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
21 commission.
22Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
23 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
24 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
25 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
26 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
27 after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
28 made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for 
29 the commission to meet and conduct its business.
30Sec. 5.  Duties; meetings.  Resolved:  That the commission is authorized to meet 
31 12 times and shall study and review the Constitution of Maine and recommend changes 
32 and amendments as considered necessary and desirable. 
33 Proposed changes and amendments for the commission to consider include, but are not 
34 limited to, provisions for:
35 1.  Strengthening Maine's Declaration of Rights, Article I of the constitution;
36 2.  Removing procedural minutiae from the constitution that are better left to statute;
37 3.  Creating the position of Lieutenant Governor;
38 4.  Enabling the popular election of constitutional officers;
39 5.  Establishing 4-year terms for Senators;
40 6.  Reducing the number of members of the Legislature;
Page 2 - 131LR2155(01)1 7.  Establishing a unicameral Legislature;
2 8.  Allowing constitutional amendments to be initiated directly by the people of the 
3 State; and
4 9.  Examining any constitutional resolutions introduced in the First Regular Session or 
5 First Special Session of the 131st Legislature.
6Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
7 necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff support 
8 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
9Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 6, 2024, the commission 
10 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
11 resolutions to amend the Constitution of Maine, for presentation to the joint standing 
12 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over judiciary matters for the First Regular 
13 Session of the 132nd Legislature.
14SUMMARY
15 This resolve creates the Commission to Study the Constitution of Maine to study and 
16 review the Constitution of Maine and recommend changes and amendments as considered 
17 necessary and desirable.15
