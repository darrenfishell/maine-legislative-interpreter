24Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of task force.  Resolved:  That all 
25 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
26 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
27 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
28 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the task force.  If 30 days or more after 
29 the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been made, the 
30 chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for the task 
31 force to meet and conduct its business.
32Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the task force shall study accessibility to appropriate 
33 communication methods for deaf and hard-of-hearing patients in health care settings and 
34 how that accessibility may be improved.  The task force shall consider, but is not limited 
35 to, the following:
36 1.  The availability of American Sign Language interpreters in health care settings;
37 2.  The availability of other communication technologies in health care settings, such 
38 as video interpreters, automatically generated voice transcriptions and automatically 
39 generated captions;
40 3.  Staff education and training programs on overcoming barriers to health care 
41 experienced by deaf and hard-of-hearing patients; and
Page 2 - 131LR0262(01)1 4.  Successful models for overcoming barriers to health care experienced by deaf and 
2 hard-of-hearing patients.
3Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
4 necessary staffing services to the task force, except that Legislative Council staff support 
5 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
6Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 6, 2023, the task force 
7 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
8 legislation, for presentation to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human 
9 Services.  The Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services is authorized to 
10 report out legislation related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st 
11 Legislature.
12SUMMARY
13 This resolve establishes the Task Force on Accessibility to Appropriate 
14 Communication Methods for Deaf and Hard-of-hearing Patients, which is directed to study 
15 accessibility to appropriate communication methods for deaf and hard-of-hearing patients 
16 in health care settings and how that accessibility may be improved.  The task force is 
17 required to submit a report and suggested legislation by December 6, 2023 to the Joint 
18 Standing Committee on Health and Human Services, which is authorized to report out 
19 legislation based on the report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.13
