34SUMMARY
35The bill makes the following changes to the licensing laws of certain professions and 
36occupations.
371.  It provides authority for the Director of the Office of Professional and 
38Occupational Regulation within the Department of Professional and Financial Regulation 
39to reduce license fees by order.
Page 6 - 129LR2369(01)-112.  It adds failure by a licensee to provide treatment records to a patient within a 
2reasonable time when requested by the patient in writing as a new ground for discipline.
33.  It increases from one to 2 the number of helper electricians that may be supervised 
4by a master, journeyman or limited electrician at any one time and corrects an error in 
5hours of work experience required for a journeyman-in-training to apply for a master 
6electrician license.
74.  It repeals current license requirements for real estate appraisers and replaces them 
8with less stringent license requirements adopted by rule by the federally authorized 
9appraiser qualifications board.
105.  It authorizes the issuance of licenses to speech-language pathology assistant 
11applicants who have a degree higher than an associate degree and exempts temporary 
12licensees, speech-language pathology assistants and trainee licensees from continuing 
13education requirements at the time of license renewal.
