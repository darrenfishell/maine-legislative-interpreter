30SUMMARY
31This bill requires private and public secondary schools with more than 300 students 
32to enter into contracts with secondary schools with 300 or fewer students to establish 
33academic opportunity programs that make available to resident students of the smaller 
34schools the courses and academic programs of the larger schools, including, but not 
35limited to, advanced placement courses, international baccalaureate programs and early 
36college programs. The bill also requires the secondary schools with 300 or fewer students 
37to inform their resident students of the academic opportunity program and provides that 
38the schools participating in an academic opportunity program are not responsible for 
39providing transportation to participating students.
