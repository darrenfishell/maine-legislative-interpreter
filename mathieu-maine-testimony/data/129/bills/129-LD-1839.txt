2become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, assisted living facilities received one-time funding in Public Law 2017, 
4chapter 460 for the 2018-19 fiscal year and additional funding is immediately necessary; 
5and
6Whereas, almost 200 elderly residents of assisted living facilities in Sanford, Saco, 
7Portland, Camden, Augusta, Bangor and Millinocket are vulnerable if additional funding 
8is not immediately provided; and
9Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
10the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
11immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
12therefore, be it
13Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to increase 
14reimbursement.  Resolved:  That, no later than July 1, 2019, the Department of 
15Health and Human Services shall increase the total reimbursement paid to the 7 assisted 
16living facilities that have contracts with the department by $569,111 in each year.
17Sec. 2.  Increase to medication passes.  Resolved:  That the Department of 
18Health and Human Services shall amend Section 63 of the Office of Elder Services Policy 
19Manual, 10-149 C.M.R. Chapter 5, for residents who reside in one of the assisted living 
20facilities that have contracts with the department to increase the number of medication 
21passes from 3 medication passes per consumer per day to 6 medication passes per 
22consumer per day.  Rules adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical rules as 
23defined in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.  The rules 
24must allow for reimbursement under this section beginning July 1, 2019.

Page 2 - 129LR2552(01)-11SUMMARY
2This emergency bill includes the recommendations of the sustainable funding review 
3conducted by the Department of Health and Human Services pursuant to Public Law 
42017, chapter 460, Part H.  The bill requires the Department of Health and Human 
5Services, by July 1, 2019, to increase the total reimbursement amount by $569,111 in 
6each year to the 7 assisted living facilities that have contracts with the department.  The 
7Department of Health and Human Services shall also amend its Section 63 rules of the 
8Office of Elderly Services Policy Manual, 10-149 C.M.R. Chapter 5, to increase the 
9number of medication passes per consumer per day from 3 to 6.  The rules must allow for 
10reimbursement for this service beginning July 1, 2019.
