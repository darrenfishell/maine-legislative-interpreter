14§12723.  Maine Community College System No-cost Tuition Program
15The Maine Community College System No-cost Tuition Program is established 
16within the Maine Community College System to fully fund community college for 
17eligible students.
181.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
19following terms have the following meanings.
20A.  "Eligible program of study" means a curriculum leading to an associate or 
21baccalaureate degree for which federal funds under the federal Higher Education Act 
22of 1965, 20 United States Code, Chapter 282, Subchapter IV are available.
23B.  "Eligible student" means a person who meets the requirements of subsection 2.
24C.  "Fund" means the Maine Community College System No-cost Tuition Fund 
25established in subsection 4.
26D.  "Program" means the Maine Community College System No-cost Tuition 
27Program.
282.  Eligibility.  A person is eligible to receive a grant for tuition and fees under 
29subsection 3 if the person meets the requirements of paragraph A or B.
30A.  A person is eligible to receive a grant under subsection 3 if the person:
31(1)  Is a resident of the State;
32(2)  Is admitted to a college and enrolled in courses leading to completion of an 
33eligible program of study at that college;
34(3)  Has not previously earned an associate or baccalaureate degree or has earned 
35an associate degree in an eligible program of study that directly leads to a 
36baccalaureate degree in an eligible program of study;
Page 2 - 129LR0331(01)-11(4)  Has completed an application for federal student financial aid programs for 
2which the person may be eligible for the academic year in which the person 
3applies through the programs; and
4(5)  Has not previously enrolled in a college at no cost under this section.
5B.  A person who previously enrolled in a college at no cost under this section is 
6eligible to receive a grant if the person:
7(1)  Meets the requirements of paragraph A, subparagraphs (1) to (4);
8(2)  Maintains a minimum cumulative grade point average of 2.0 on a scale of 4.0 
9as determined by the college the person attends;
10(3)  Maintains continuous enrollment at a college as at least a part-time student, 
11unless the person is approved by the college for a medical or personal leave of 
12absence and the person enrolls in a college within 2 semesters of the end of the 
13approved medical or personal leave of absence; and
14(4)  First received a grant under the program within the previous 5 years.
153.  Grant awards.  Beginning in fiscal year 2020-21, the system may award an 
16eligible student a grant under this section.  The award is limited to the amount of the cost 
17of tuition and mandatory fees charged to all students enrolled in courses leading to 
18completion of an eligible program of study on at least a part-time basis at the college in 
19which the student is enrolled, less all other financial aid received from the Federal Pell 
20Grant or any other merit-based or needs-based grant or scholarship, not including a grant 
21awarded under this section, that the recipient is not required to repay. The availability of 
22the grant and the amount of the grant are subject to the amount available in the fund.
234.  Maine Community College System No-cost Tuition Fund.  The Maine 
24Community College System No-cost Tuition Fund is established as a nonlapsing fund 
25administered by the system.  Grants may be distributed from this fund only to eligible 
26students in accordance with this section.
275.  Rulemaking.  The system may adopt rules to implement this section. Rules 
28adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
29chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
30SUMMARY
31This bill establishes the Maine Community College System No-cost Tuition Program.  
32Under the program, Maine residents who are determined to be eligible students and who 
33are enrolled in an eligible course of study at a college within the Maine Community 
34College System are eligible for a grant to cover the cost of tuition and mandatory fees, 
35less any federal financial aid or other financial assistance that the student receives that is 
36not required to be repaid.  The Maine Community College System must include in its 
37biennial budget for presentation to the Governor and the Legislature the estimated full 
38funding for the Maine Community College System No-cost Tuition Program.
