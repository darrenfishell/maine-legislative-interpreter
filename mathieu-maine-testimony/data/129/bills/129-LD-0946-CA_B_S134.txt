14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following: 
15 'Sec. 1. Study of online customer privacy issues. Resolved: That the 
16 Attorney General shall, in consultation with industry and customer stakeholders, study 
17 and make recommendations regarding the State's role in the regulation of the privacy of 
18 online customer information. The study shall include consideration of: 
19 1. Regulations to protect the privacy of customers of broadband Internet access 
20 service providers; 
21 2. Designation of a chief privacy officer for the State and what the responsibilities of 
22 that officer would be; ·, 
23 3. Whether to regulate businesses that handle the data of customers with whom they 
24 have a direct relationship; and 
25 4. Other relevant customer data privacy issues identified by the Attorney General. 
26 Sec. 2. Report. Resolved: That, no later than January 31, 2020, the Attorney 
27 General shall submit a report, including findings and recommendations from the study in 
28 section 1, to the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and Technology. The 
29 committee may report out a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature 
30 related to the report. 
31 Sec. 3. Appropriations and allocations. Resolved: That the following

11 SUMMARY 
12 This amendment replaces the bill with a resolve. The resolve directs the Attorney 
13 General, in consultation with industry and customer stakeholders, to study and make 
14 recommendations regarding the State's role in the regulation of the privacy of online 
15 customer information. It requires the Attorney General to submit a report to the Joint 
16 Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and Technology by January 31, 2020 and 
17 authorizes the committee to report out a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 129th 
18 Legislature related to the report. The amendment also adds an appropriations and 
19 allocations section.
