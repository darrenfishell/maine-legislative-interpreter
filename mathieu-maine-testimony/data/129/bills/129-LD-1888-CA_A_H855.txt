29SUMMARY
30The bill prohibited the application of nonselective herbicides; this amendment, which 
31strikes and replaces the bill, bans only the use of glyphosate within 75 feet of school 
32grounds.  The prohibition in the amendment does not apply to residential property or land 
33used for commercial farming.
34The amendment also directs the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and 
35Forestry, Board of Pesticides Control to establish an environmental risk advisory 
36committee to assess the environmental and human health risks associated with the use of 
37glyphosate.  The advisory committee is required to submit an interim report no later than 
38February 1, 2022 and a final report no later than February 1, 2023 with findings and

Fiscal Detail and NotesFiscal Note
This bill bans the use of glyphosate under certain conditions  and directs the Board of Pesticides Control (BPC), 
within the Department of Agriculture , Conservation and Forestry, to establish an environmental risk advisory 
committee to assess the environmental and human risks associ ated with the use of glyphosate and complete a final 
report of their findings by February 1, 2023.  The BPC has i ndicated that it will require additional staffing to complete 
these tasks.  The bill includes additional allocations of $103, 540 to the BPC to allow expenditure of existing resources 
for one toxicologist, contracted staffi ng, environmental sampling and literature review.  The impact of these new 
unfunded costs on other programs in the BPC cannot be determined at this time.129th MAINE LEGISLATURE
