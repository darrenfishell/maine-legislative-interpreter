31SUMMARY
32This bill amends the bottle redemption laws to require a dealer to accept from a 
33consumer or other person and to pay the refund value on any beverage container that was 
34sold by the dealer if no approved bottle redemption centers are located within 5 miles 
35from the property line of the dealer.  Subject to prior approval of the Department of 
36Environmental Protection, a dealer shall affix to the beverage containers it sells a sticker 
37or similar device indicating that the beverage container is sold by the dealer and may 
38refuse to accept from a consumer or other person and to pay the refund value on any 
Page 2 - 129LR1775(01)-11beverage container that does not have the sticker or similar device affixed to it.  A dealer 
2may also limit the hours or days on which it accepts beverage containers for redemption.
3Under the bottle redemption laws, a dealer is a person who sells, offers to sell or 
4engages in the sale of beverages in beverage containers to a consumer, and a dealer may, 
5but is not currently required to, accept from a consumer or other person and pay the 
6refund value on any beverage container.
