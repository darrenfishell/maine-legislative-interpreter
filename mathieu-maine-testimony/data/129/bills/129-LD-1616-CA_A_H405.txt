25 SUMMARY 
26 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, clarifies the 
27 activities of the Vaccine Consumer Protection Office in investigating a death that is 
28 suspected to have been caused by the administration of a vaccine. It also requires a 
29 health care provider who provides primary care to a child under the age of 18 to provide 
30 to the parent, guardian or custodian of the child a form for the parent, guardian or 
31 custodian to complete requesting the child's most recent immunization and the date of 
32 that immunization and requires the health care provider to remove the personally 
33 identifying information of the child from the form and submit it to the Vaccine Consumer 
34 Protection Office. The Vaccine Consumer Protection Office is directed to submit a report 
35 annually to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health 
36 and human services matters regarding information received on the f01ms and any trends. 
37 The amendment also repeals Public Law 2019, chapter 154, "An Act To Protect Maine

Fiscal Detail and Notes 
The bill includes General Fund appropriations to the Department of Health and Human Services of $607,788 in fiscal 
year 2019-20 and $600,746 in fiscal year 2020-21 foi' one Public Service Manager II position, one Public Health 
Physician position, one Planning and Research Associate II position, one Health Program Manager position and one 
Office Associate II position to run the new program. Additionally, the funding provides for contracts to prepare and 
complete the 2 annual forums and the development of a database and website and on-going funding to host the 
database. 
Additional costs to the licensing boards within or affiliated with the Department of Professional and Financial 
Regulation that are responsible for the licensing of a health care provider associated with the rulemaking process can 
be absorbed within existing budgeted resources
