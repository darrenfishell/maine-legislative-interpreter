23pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
24Sec. 4.  Title XIX waivers and state plan amendments; collaboration with 
25Maine State Housing Authority; stakeholder group.  Resolved:  That the 
26Department of Health and Human Services shall review any waivers or state plan 
27amendments available under Title XIX of the United States Social Security Act to 
28stabilize access to safe and affordable housing for individuals with disabilities, older 
29adults, individuals needing long-term services and supports and persons experiencing 
30chronic homelessness through the provision of housing transition, tenancy sustainment 
31and case management services.  The department shall collaborate with the Maine State 
32Housing Authority to determine how existing Maine State Housing Authority resources 
33may be leveraged more effectively to address housing needs under this section.
34In performing the review under this section, the department shall examine relevant 
35data and evidence-based practices and convene a stakeholder group to solicit input 
36regarding methods to improve health outcomes through greater housing stability for 
37individuals with disabilities, older adults, individuals needing long-term services and 
38supports and persons experiencing chronic homelessness.
39SUMMARY
40This resolve directs the Department of Health and Human Services to apply for 
41waivers to provide housing-related services, including housing transition and tenancy 
Page 2 - 129LR1916(01)-11sustaining services, to individuals with disabilities, older adults needing long-term 
2services and supports and persons experiencing chronic homelessness under the 1915(b) 
3and 1915(c) Medicaid waiver provisions. It also directs the department to amend 
4MaineCare rules to implement the waivers once approved. These allowable benefits are  
5set forth in the United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for 
6Medicare and Medicaid Services, Center for Medicaid and CHIP Services Informational 
7Bulletin dated June 26, 2015 on Coverage of Housing-Related Activities and Services for 
8Individuals with Disabilities.
9The resolve also directs the department to review any waivers or state plan 
10amendments available under Title XIX of the United States Social Security Act to 
11stabilize access to safe and affordable housing for individuals with disabilities, older 
12adults, individuals needing long-term services and supports and persons experiencing 
13chronic homelessness through the provision of housing transition, tenancy sustainment 
14and case management services and to collaborate with the Maine State Housing Authority 
15to determine how resources may be leveraged more effectively to address housing needs.  
16As part of its review, the department is required to convene a stakeholder group to solicit 
17input regarding methods to improve health outcomes through greater housing stability.
