19Sec. 2.  22 MRSA §4301, sub-§1, as amended by PL 1991, c. 9, Pt. U, §1, is 
20further amended to read:
211.  Basic necessities.  "Basic necessities" means food, potable water, clothing, 
22shelter, fuel, electricity, nonelective medical services as recommended by a physician, 
23nonprescription drugs, telephone where it is necessary for medical reasons and any other 
24commodity or service determined essential by the overseer in accordance with the

29Sec. 3.  30-A MRSA §1654, as amended by PL 2015, c. 44, §6, is further amended 
30to read:
31§1654.  Supplies for jails; accounts audited
32The county commissioners of the several counties shall, without extra charge or 
33commission to themselves or to any other person, procure all necessary supplies, 
34including necessary food, potable water, fuel, bedding and clothing for the jails and the 
35prisoners in the jails, to be furnished and purchased under their direction and at the 
36expense of the counties.  A county commissioner may not be interested directly or 
37indirectly in the purchase of any such supplies or in any contract for such supplies made 
38by the board of which and while the county commissioner is a member, and all contracts 
39made in violation of this provision are void.  A suitable person must be employed to 
Page 2 - 129LR1653(01)-11prepare the foods of the prisoners in each county at the expense of the county.  The 
2service of the food to the prisoners is under the general direction of the jailer, master or 
3keeper.  The sheriff shall appoint the person employed to prepare the food of the 
4prisoners subject to the approval of the county commissioners.  The county 
5commissioners may at any time direct specific rations or articles of food, clothing, soap, 
6fuel or other necessities to be provided to the prisoners.  The bills and accounts for 
7supplies furnished and the items of expense incurred in preparing and serving these 
8supplies must be audited pursuant to section 951.
9SUMMARY
10This bill adds potable water to lists of necessities in the Maine Revised Statutes in 
11laws governing profiteering in necessities, municipal general assistance and supplies for 
12jails.
