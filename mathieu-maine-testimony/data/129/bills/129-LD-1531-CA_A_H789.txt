9Sec. 2.  Maine agriculture, food system and forestry infrastructure 
10investment; assessment.  The Commissioner of Agriculture, Conservation and 
11Forestry shall undertake a Maine agriculture, food system and forestry infrastructure 
12investment assessment either directly or with a suitable contractor.  The purpose of the 
13assessment is to support the duties of the commissioner under the Maine Revised 
14Statutes, Title 7, chapter 10-C.  The commissioner shall conduct a comprehensive review 
15of current state and external partners' efforts to achieve the duties under Title 7, chapter 
1610-C.
171. Definitions.  For purposes of this section, the following terms have the following 
18meanings.
19A.  "Food system" means all processes, infrastructure and persons involved in feeding 
20a population, including growing, harvesting, collecting, processing, packaging, 
21transporting, marketing, selling, consuming and disposing of food and food-related 
22items in the State.
23B.  "Supply chain" means the chain of distribution by which agricultural, food and 
24forest products in the State are moved from the producer to the consumer and may 
25include intermediate entities such as processors, packagers, warehouse operators, 
26distributors, wholesalers and retailers.
27C.  "Value chain coordination" means an approach to product development that 
28connects the existing elements within the supply chain and identifies key gaps in the 
29supply chain in existing infrastructure or services and opportunities for effective 
30investment.
312.  Assessment outcomes. The commissioner may:
32A.  Recommend necessary improvements or changes to current state grant and loan 
33programs that support the agriculture, food and forestry sectors of the State, including 
34any recommendations for the creation of new financing programs based on other 
35successful state models;
36B.  Review and organize existing recommendations from trade groups and others that 
37pertain to Maine's agriculture, food and forestry economy infrastructure, including 
38but not limited to processing, transportation, storage and warehousing, distribution 
39and wholesale and retail sales; and
40C.  Identify gaps, undertake additional data collection and outline short-term and 
41long-term actions that can be taken by the Legislature, the Department of Agriculture,
