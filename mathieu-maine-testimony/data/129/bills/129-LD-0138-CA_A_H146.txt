18 SUMMARY 
19 This amendment requires the Department of Economic and Community 
20 Development, when determining grants from the Maine Coworking Development Fund, 
21 to solicit applications through a competitive bid process, instead of a request for proposal 
22 process. The amendment allows for funds to be provided to entities to establish new 
23 collaborative workspaces and directs the department to prioritize development of new 
24 workspaces in the competitive application process. The amendment replaces the 
25 requirement for an annual report by the department to the President of the Senate and the 
26 Speaker of the House of Representatives with a report to the joint standing committee of 
27 the Legislature having jurisdiction over innovation, development, economic advancement 
28 and business matters. The amendment directs the department to engage in at least 2 
29 rounds of competitive applications and also directs that the 2019-20 appropriation be 
30 carried over into 2020-21.
