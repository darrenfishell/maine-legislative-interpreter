COMMITTEE AMENDMENT1SUMMARY
2This amendment is the majority report of the committee.  It replaces the bill, which is 
3a concept draft.  It requires the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, 
4Bureau of General Services to inventory all state-owned buildings where state employees 
5perform job duties to determine the presence of asbestos, lead, black mold or other 
6substances that may be harmful to human health.  It requires property owners of buildings 
7leased to the State to conduct a similar inventory and report the results to the bureau. The 
8amendment requires the bureau to establish and maintain a database containing the results 
9of the inventories and make the database easily accessible electronically to employee 
10collective bargaining agents, employees working in state office buildings with asbestos, 
11lead, black mold or other substances that may be harmful to human health, individuals 
12who perform maintenance, repair or custodial services, firefighters, the Department of 
13Labor, the Department of Health and Human Services, Maine Center for Disease Control 
14and Prevention and members of the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
15jurisdiction over state and local government matters.  It requires the database to be 
16consulted before maintenance of or repair to a state-owned building by a state employee 
17and the provision of protective gear to the employee if appropriate.  The department is 
18required to submit a biennial report and may adopt rules to implement these requirements.
19The amendment also provides funding for personnel in the bureau to carry out the 
20requirements of this legislation.

buildings where state employees work for the presence of ha rmful substances. It also requires DAFS to establish and 
maintain a database of the results from this inventory and make that inventory available to multiple different state 
departments and agencies as well as organizations. DAFS ha s indicated it will require two full time positions to meet 
these requirements. The bill establishes two full time Engi neering Technician IV positions and provides a General
