122-D.  Lottery for aquaculture purposes; additional quota.  If the Atlantic States 
13Marine Fisheries Commission establishes an elver quota to be allocated specifically for 
14use in aquaculture, the commissioner shall establish a lottery under which a person who is 
15licensed under this section may become eligible to fish this elver quota. An applicant to 
16the lottery shall submit a lottery application together with a $35 nonrefundable 
17application fee no later than January 15th of the same calendar year as the lottery. An 
18applicant may not submit more than one application for the lottery established under this 
19subsection.  In any year in which a lottery is held for this purpose, the lottery must be 
20held on or before February 15th.
21The commissioner shall assign the quota allocated specifically for use in aquaculture in 5-
22pound increments to each successful lottery applicant. A successful applicant shall sell all 
23elvers caught pursuant to this subsection to an aquaculture facility approved by the 
24commissioner.
25The commissioner may adopt rules to implement the lottery under this subsection, 
26including provisions for the method and administration of the lottery. Rules adopted 
27pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, 
28subchapter 2-A.
29Twenty-five dollars of the application fee collected under this subsection must be 
30deposited in the Eel and Elver Management Fund established in section 6505-D and used 
31to fund a life-cycle study of the elver fishery. Ten dollars of the application fee may be 
32used by the department to fund the costs of administering the lottery.
33Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
34legislation takes effect when approved.
35SUMMARY
36This bill requires the Commissioner of Marine Resources to establish an elver fishing 
37lottery if the Atlantic States Marine Fisheries Commission allocates an elver quota 
38specifically for use in aquaculture.  To be eligible to apply to the aquaculture lottery, a 
Page 2 - 129LR1759(01)-11person must hold a valid elver fishing license.  The bill requires the commissioner to 
2allocate the quota in 5-pound increments to each successful lottery applicant and requires 
3that elvers caught as a result of this lottery be sold to an aquaculture facility approved by 
4the commissioner.  Twenty-five dollars of the $35 application fee is required to be 
5deposited in the Eel and Elver Management Fund for a life-cycle study of the elver 
6fishery, and $10 of the fee may be used by the department to fund the costs of 
7administering the lottery.
