3CHAPTER 224
4TRAFFIC SAFETY EDUCATION
5§6701.  Traffic safety education defined
6As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, "traffic safety 
7education" means education designed to provide instruction regarding traffic safety laws 
8and rules, including best practices for users of public and private ways, including motor 
9vehicle operators, pedestrians, bicyclists and other vulnerable users, as that term is 
10defined in Title 29-A, section 101, subsection 91-A.
11§6702.  Traffic safety education required
12A school administrative unit shall provide at least one hour of age-appropriate traffic 
13safety education annually to students in grades 2 to 12.  The traffic safety education must 
14meet the minimum requirements specified in section 6703.
15§6703.  Traffic safety education program minimum requirements
16A traffic safety education program, referred to in this chapter as "the program," 
17offered by a school administrative unit must meet, at a minimum, the following 
18requirements adjusted as appropriate for each grade.
191.  Pedestrian instruction.  The program must provide instruction on traffic laws and 
20rules applicable to pedestrians and safe pedestrian use of public and private ways, 
21including:
22A.  Use of sidewalks and crosswalks;
23B.  Travel on the shoulders of public ways, including direction of travel;
24C.  Entering, exiting and crossing public and private ways; and
25D.  Conspicuity.
262.  Bicyclist instruction.  The program must provide instruction on traffic laws and 
27rules applicable to bicyclists and safe bicycle operation on public and private ways, 
28including:
29A.  Helmet requirements and use;
30B.  Lighting and reflector use;
31C.  Hand signals and use;
32D.  The meaning of and the proper response to traffic control devices as defined in 
33Title 29-A, section 101, subsection 84;
34E.  Travel on public ways, including direction of travel; and
Page 2 - 129LR0253(01)-11F.  Entering, exiting and crossing public and private ways.
23.  Motorist instruction.  The program must provide instruction on traffic laws and 
3rules applicable to motor vehicle operators and passengers in motor vehicles, including:
4A.  Awareness of, operating around and safely passing pedestrians, bicyclists and 
5other vulnerable users of public and private ways;
6B.  Prohibitions against distracted driving;
7C.  Prohibitions against operating under the influence of intoxicating liquor and 
8drugs; and
9D.  Speed limits.
10§6704.  Limits on instruction
11The program is not intended to supplant a driver education course offered pursuant to 
12chapter 316 and may not include any practical instruction in the operation of a motor 
13vehicle.
14§6705.  Contract with 3rd party
15A school administrative unit may contract with a 3rd party to provide traffic safety 
16education.
17SUMMARY
18The purpose of this bill is to provide students in Maine with traffic safety education 
19from an early age with the goal of teaching students safe practices and respect for all 
20users of the road in order to reduce the unacceptable number of pedestrian and bicyclist 
21fatalities and make Maine's roadways safer and more enjoyable for all users.
22This bill requires all school administrative units to provide at least one hour annually 
23of age-appropriate traffic safety education to students in grades 2 to 12.  The traffic safety 
24education program must provide, at a minimum, instruction on the use of public and 
25private ways by pedestrians, bicyclists and motor vehicle operators and on the laws and 
26rules regarding that use.  A school administrative unit may contract with a 3rd party to 
27provide the traffic safety education.
