11Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to increase 
12MaineCare reimbursement for outpatient psychosocial treatments for 
13children.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services shall 
14increase reimbursement rates under rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, 
15Chapters II and III, Section 65 for multisystemic therapy, multisystemic therapy for 
16problem sexualized behavior and functional family therapy by 20% for the period from 
17the effective date of this resolve to June 30, 2020.
18Sec. 2.  Rate study.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human 
19Services shall contract with a 3rd party to conduct a rate study of reimbursement rates 
20under rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapters II and III, Section 65 for 
21multisystemic therapy, multisystemic therapy for problem sexualized behavior and 
22functional family therapy.  The rate study must develop a rate that is set on a per case per 
23week basis.  The rate study must also take into account the costs to providers of 
24delivering the services, including additional training, and maintenance of fidelity to the 
25treatment models.  The rate study must be completed no later than December 1, 2019.
26Sec. 3.  Report.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services 
27shall submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services 
28with the findings of the rate study conducted pursuant to section 2 no later than January 
2930, 2020.
30Sec. 4.  Rulemaking.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human 
31Services is authorized to adopt rules to implement new rates developed pursuant to the 
32rate study in section 2 as long as those rates are no lower than the rates of reimbursement 
33that exist on April 1, 2019 and the federal Department of Health and Human Services, 
34Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services approves the reimbursement rates.  Rules 
35adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical rules as defined in the Maine 
36Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
37Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
38legislation takes effect when approved.

2This resolve requires the Department of Health and Human Services to increase 
3reimbursement rates for multisystemic therapy, multisystemic therapy for problem 
4sexualized behavior and functional family therapy by 20% until June 30, 2020.  It 
5requires the department to contract for a 3rd-party rate study of the reimbursement rates 
6for those therapies, including developing a rate set on a per case per week basis rather 
7than the current 15-minute increments.  The rate study must also take into account the 
8costs to providers of delivering the services, including additional training, and 
9maintenance of fidelity to the treatment models.  The rate study must be completed no 
10later than December 1, 2019.  The department must submit a report on the results of the 
11study to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services no later than 
12January 30, 2020.  The department is authorized to implement new rates through 
13rulemaking as long as the rates are no lower than those that exist on April 1, 2019 and the 
14rates are approved by the federal Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for 
15Medicare and Medicaid Services.
