8volunteer driver networks funded in section 3 to work with regional transportation

11charge modest fares; and serve all interested residents.  The Department of Transportation 
12shall create a pilot purchase of service program in a selected region of the State funded in 
13section 3 and coordinate the program with related efforts of the Statewide Independent 
14Living Council.  The Department of Transportation shall distribute the funding for 
15regional transportation providers in section 3 using the same formula as current state 
16public transportation funding is distributed:  50% distributed to urban areas following the 
17United States Department of Transportation, Federal Transit Administration formula and 
1850% distributed to rural areas following the standards governing the Federal Transit 
19Administration formula grants for rural areas under 49 United States Code, Section 5311, 
20which include the number of square miles in the service area, the population of the 
21service area and the percentage of low-income individuals in the service area.  Regional 
22transportation providers shall give preference in the allocation of funding to addressing 
23the needs of the workforce in their region.

21SUMMARY
22This bill requires the quinquennial locally coordinated plan for regional transit 
23submitted by each regional public transportation agency to focus on meeting workforce 
24needs.
25This bill also provides funding to the Department of Transportation to support and 
26expand local volunteer driver networks; to create a pilot purchase of service program in a 
27selected region of the State to provide senior citizens and persons with disabilities 
28vouchers to purchase their own transportation services; and for regional transportation 
29providers throughout the State, split evenly between rural and urban areas, to expand their
