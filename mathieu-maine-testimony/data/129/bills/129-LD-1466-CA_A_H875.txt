COMMITTEE AMENDMENT30 Program is established to support persons with presumed substance use disorder by 
31 providing grants to municipalities and, counties and community-based organizations in 
32 partnership with municipal or county law enforcement agencies to carry out programs 
33 designed to reduce substance use, substance use-related crimes and recidivism.  As used in 
34 this chapter, "community-based organization" means a nonprofit community organization 
35 that provides substance use disorder services to individuals, including, without limitation, 
36 substance use assessment, treatment, education and support group services.
82.  Eligibility; program targets; programs.  Grants may be awarded to:
9 A.  Municipal or county governments or, regional jails or community-based 
10 organizations in partnership with municipal or county law enforcement agencies for 
11 programs designed to assist persons with presumed substance use disorder by using 
12 liaison strategies both before and after arrest to refer alleged low-level offenders into 
13 community-based treatment and support services.  Programs may include, but are not 
14 limited to:
15 (1)  Referral of participants in the Substance Use Disorder Assistance Law 
16 Enforcement Assisted Diversion Program under subsection 1 to evidence-based 
17 treatment programs, including medically assisted treatment; and
18 (2)  Provision of case management services to participants in the Substance Use 
19 Disorder Assistance Law Enforcement Assisted Diversion Program under 
20 subsection 1 in order to secure appropriate treatment and support services such as 
21 housing, health care, job training and mental health services for participants in the 
22 Substance Use Disorder Assistance Law Enforcement Assisted Diversion 
23 Program;
24 B.  County governments for programs in county jails designed to facilitate the 
25 accessing by persons with presumed substance use disorder of post-adjudication 
26 diversion and reentry programs.  Programs may include, but are not limited to:
27 (1)  Provision of evidence-based treatment programs, including medically assisted 
28 treatment, to jail inmates; and
29 (2)  Provision of case management or other support services to participants in the 
30 Substance Use Disorder Assistance Law Enforcement Assisted Diversion Program 
31 under subsection 1 to assist in transition from jail upon release; and
32 C.  Municipal governments or community-based organizations in partnership with 
33 municipal or county law enforcement agencies for programs designed to facilitate 
34 pathways to community-based treatment, recovery and support services for persons 
35 with substance use disorder who present themselves to municipal law enforcement 
36 agencies and request assistance and referral to evidence-based treatment programs, 
37 including medically assisted treatment.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT14.  Selection of grant recipients.  The Commissioner of Public Safety shall review 
2 applications submitted by municipalities, counties and, regional jails and community-based 
3 organizations in partnership with municipal or county law enforcement agencies for grants 
4 under this chapter.  Preference must be given to collaborative approaches that include 
5 treatment providers or community-based organizations in partnership with municipal or 
6 county law enforcement agencies.
75.  Administration of funds.  The policy board established in this State to carry out 
8 the State's responsibilities under the federal Justice Assistance Act of 1984, the federal 
9 Anti-Drug Abuse Act of 1986, the federal Anti-Drug Abuse Act of 1988 and the federal 
10 Violent Crime Control and Law Enforcement Act of 1994, known as "the Justice 
11 Assistance Council," shall administer grant funds appropriated for use under this chapter.
126.  Reports.  A recipient of a grant under subsection 4 shall report to the Commissioner 
13 of Public Safety annually on the anniversary date of the grant award regarding the status of 
14 the program for which the grant was awarded.  The report must include a description of 
15 how the grant funds were spent, the results of the program and any recommendations for 
16 modification of the program, including any available information concerning the program's 
17 effectiveness in reducing substance use disorder and recidivism.'

20SUMMARY
21 This amendment is the majority report of the committee.  The amendment changes the 
22 name of the Substance Use Disorder Assistance Program to the Law Enforcement Assisted 
23 Diversion Program.  The amendment retains all of the provisions of the bill that include 
24 community-based organizations as entities that are eligible for grants under the Law 
25 Enforcement Assisted Diversion Program, requiring that they apply and operate in 
26 partnership with municipal or county law enforcement agencies.  The amendment updates 
27 the language in the bill in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 25, section 5101 to reflect 
28 amendments to the section that were enacted in Public Law 2019, chapter 343, Part 
29 CCCCC.
