14Sec. 1.  Task force established.  Resolved:  That the Task Force To Study and 
15Plan for the Implementation of Maine's Early Childhood Special Education Services, 
16referred to in this resolve as "the task force," is established to study and plan for the 
17implementation of Maine's early childhood special education programs and services 
18provided for young children from 3 years of age to 5 years of age.

Page 3 - 129LR0991(01)-11Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate 
2chair and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the task 
3force.
4Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of task force.  Resolved:  That all the 
5appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
6resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
7Council once all appointments have been completed.  Within 15 days after appointment 
8of all members, the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the task force.  If 30 
9days or more after enactment of this legislation a majority of but not all of the 
10appointments have been made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative 
11Council may grant authority for the task force to meet and conduct its business.
12Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the task force shall examine:
131.  National trends and relevant models of governing and delivering early childhood 
14special education systems in other states and jurisdictions that hold the potential for 
15enhancing the effectiveness, efficiency or accountability of the early childhood special 
16education system in the State;
172.  The short-term and long-term costs and benefits of the Department of Education's 
18proposed plan to restructure the Child Development Services System as presented by the 
19Commissioner of Education to the Legislature in Legislative Document 1870 in the 
20Second Regular Session of the 128th Legislature;
213.  The findings and recommendations of the Subcommittee To Study Early 
22Childhood Special Education in its January 2007 report;
234.  The findings and recommendations of the Office of Program Evaluation and 
24Government Accountability in its July 2012 report on child development services; and
255.  Any other issues the task force determines to be useful or necessary concerning 
26early childhood special education systems matters.
27Sec. 6.  Development of recommendations; plan.  Resolved:  That the task 
28force shall develop recommendations for an early childhood special education services 
29program plan for the State, which must include, but is not limited to:
301.  Models of best practices;
312.  Fiscally sound budget forecasting, including all possible revenue streams and 
32updated costs;
333.  Transportation services;
344.  Data systems, including a billing system, a system that allows coordination with 
35the MaineCare program and a case management documentation system;
365.  A timeline for the implementation of the plan under this section;
Page 4 - 129LR0991(01)-116.  A procedure for data collection and analysis conducted by the Maine Education 
2Policy Research Institute;
37.  A method for assessing a school administrative unit's capacity for implementing 
4early childhood special education programs;
58.  Training requirements for service providers and leaders;
69.  Public information communication strategy for implementation of the plan; and
710.  Identification of potential revisions to the Department of Health and Human 
8Services rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual.
9Sec. 7.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the 
10Legislative Council shall provide staffing assistance to the task force, except that 
11Legislative Council staff support is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or 
12special session.
13Sec. 8.  Compensation.  Resolved:  That legislative members of the task force are 
14entitled to receive the legislative per diem and reimbursement for travel and other 
15necessary expenses related to their attendance at authorized meetings of the task force.  
16Notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, other members of the task force may not receive 
17compensation for their participation on the task force.
18Sec. 9.  Report.  Resolved: That, no later than December 4, 2019, the task force 
19shall submit a report containing its findings, recommendations and plan, including 
20suggested legislation, for presentation to the Joint Standing Committee on Education and 
21Cultural Affairs.  The Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs may 
22submit a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
23Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
24legislation takes effect when approved.
25SUMMARY
26This resolve establishes the Task Force To Study and Plan for the Implementation of 
27Maine's Early Childhood Special Education Services to examine the national trends and 
28relevant models of governing and delivering early childhood special education systems 
29and the short-term and long-term costs and benefits to the Department of Education's 
30proposed plan to restructure the Child Development Services System and to make 
31recommendations for an early childhood special education services program plan.
