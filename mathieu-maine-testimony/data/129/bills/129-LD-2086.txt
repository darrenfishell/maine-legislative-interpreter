14Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate 
15chair and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
16review committee.
17Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of review committee.  Resolved:  That all 
18appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
19resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
20Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
21the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the review committee.  If 30 days or 
22more after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have 
23been made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant 
24authority for the review committee to meet and conduct its business.
25Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the review committee shall:
261.  Review activities in other states that address the expungement of, sealing of, 
27vacatur of and otherwise limiting public access to criminal records;
282.  Consider whether the following convictions should be subject to different 
29treatment:
30A.  Convictions for conduct that has been decriminalized in this State over the last 10 
31years and conduct that is currently under consideration for decriminalization; and
32B.  Convictions for conduct that was committed by victims and survivors of sexual 
33exploitation and sex trafficking;
343.  Consider whether there is a time limit after which some or all criminal records 
35should not be publicly available; 
364.  Invite comments and suggestions from interested parties, including but not limited 
37to victim advocates and prison and correctional reform organizations;
Page 3 - 129LR3119(01)-115.  Review existing information about the harms and benefits of making criminal 
2records confidential, including the use and dissemination of those records;
36.  Invite comments and suggestions concerning the procedures and processes to limit 
4public accessibility of criminal records;
57.  Consider who, if anyone, should continue to have access to criminal records that 
6are not publicly available; and
78.  Develop options to manage criminal records.
8Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
9necessary staffing services to the review committee, except that Legislative Council staff 
10support is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
11Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than November 4, 2020, the review 
12committee shall submit to the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary a report that 
13includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested legislation, for 
14presentation to the First Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
15Sec. 8.  Outside funding.  Resolved:  That the review committee shall seek 
16funding contributions to fully fund the costs of the study.  All funding is subject to 
17approval by the Legislative Council in accordance with its policies.  If sufficient 
18contributions to fund the study have not been received within 30 days after the effective 
19date of this resolve, no meetings are authorized and no expenses of any kind may be 
20incurred or reimbursed.
21Sec. 9.  Appropriations and allocations.  Resolved:  That the following
