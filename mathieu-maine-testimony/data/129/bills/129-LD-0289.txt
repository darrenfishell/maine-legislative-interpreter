3ยง1652-A.  Disposable food service containers
41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Disposable food service container" means service ware designed for one-time 
7use.  "Disposable food service container" includes service ware for take-out foods, 
8packaged meat, eggs, bakery products and leftovers from partially consumed meals 
9prepared by food vendors.
10B.  "Polystyrene foam" means blown polystyrene and expanded or extruded foams 
11using a styrene monomer.
12C.  "Service ware" means a container, bowl, plate, tray, carton, cup, lid or other item 
13designed to be used for prepared foods.
14D.  "Store" means a retail establishment in the State, including, but not limited to, a 
15convenience store, restaurant, grocery store, pharmacy, seasonal or temporary 
16business, farm stand and seller of merchandise and dry goods to the ultimate 
17consumer for direct use or consumption and not for resale.
182.  Prohibition.  Beginning January 1, 2020, a store may not sell or distribute in the 
19State a disposable food service container that is composed in whole or in part of 
20polystyrene foam.
213.  Rules.  The department shall adopt rules to implement the provisions of this 
22section.  Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined 
23in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
24SUMMARY
25Beginning January 1, 2020, this bill prohibits the sale or distribution in the State of 
26disposable food service containers composed in whole or in part of polystyrene foam.  
27The bill requires the Department of Environmental Protection to adopt rules to implement 
28these statutory provisions.
