'Sec. 2. 36 MRSA c. 725 is enacted to read: 
CHAPTER 725 
MARINE FLARE DISPOSAL FEE 
§5011. Marine flare disposal fee imposed 
A fee is imposed on the retail sale in this State of marine flares in the amount of 25¢ 
per flare for the purpose of offsetting the cost to the General Fund of paying for the 
marine flare disposal, education and collection programs pursuant to Title 25, chapter 
318-A. 
1. Marine flare. As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, 
"marine flare" means a device designed to produce a bright flame for use as a signal or 
marker on the inland or coastal waters of the State or at sea. 
2. Exemption. Transactions that. under the laws of this State, are not subject to 
taxation in accordance with Part 3 are exempt from the fee imposed by this section. 
3. Administration. The fee imposed by this section is administered as provided in 
chapter 7 and Part 3, with the fee imposed pursuant to this chapter to be considered as 
imposed under Part 3. The revenue derived from the fee imposed by this chapter must be 
deposited in the General Fund. 
4. Effective date. This chapter takes effect January 1, 2020. 
Sec. 3. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and 
allocations are made.

The bill creates a fee of $.25 per marine flare sold in the State and would increase General Fund revenue by $2,500 in 
fiscal year 2019-20 and $5,000 in fiscal year 2020-21. It includes a one-time General Fund appropriation of $43,500 
in fiscal year 2019-20 to the Department of Public Safety for one the1mal destruction unit to assist with safely 
disposing of expired marine flares. It also includes a one-time General Fund appropriation of $16,000 in fiscal year 
2019-20 to the Department of Administrative and Financial Services for computer programming to update the sales 
tax return. Additional costs to the Department associated with establishing an education campaign and updating the 
website can be absorbed within existing budgeted resources.
