13(2)  In a public or private educational institution, kindergarten to grade 12;

15(4)  In a federally qualified health center;
16(5)  In a community-based behavioral health center;
17(6)  In a public interest law service;
18(7)  In a public child care facility;
19(8)  In a public service for individuals with disabilities;
20(9)  In a public service for the elderly;
21(10)  In a public service for veterans; or
22(11) At an organization exempt from taxation under the United States Internal 
23Revenue Code of 1986, Section 501(c)(3) or 501(c)(6);
24E.  Submit an application to the authority, which must include but is not limited to

26community involvement; and

28practice area in the State for a minimum of 5 years after acceptance into the program 
29in a form acceptable to the authority.

28SUMMARY
29This bill establishes and provides funding for the Maine Mental Health Providers 
30Loan Repayment Program to be administered by the Finance Authority of Maine.  Under 
31the program, all social workers licensed by the State Board of Social Worker Licensure 
32and all counselors licensed by the Board of Counseling Professionals Licensure may 
33apply for student loan repayment assistance, as long as the applicants agree to practice for 
345 years in one of a list of underserved practice areas in the State.  The bill also repeals the 
35currently unfunded Social Work Education Loan Repayment Program in the Maine
