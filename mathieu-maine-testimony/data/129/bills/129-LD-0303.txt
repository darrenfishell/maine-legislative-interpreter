11Sec. 3.  22 MRSA §2353, sub-§4-A is enacted to read:
124-A.  Housing-based programs; standing orders for naloxone hydrochloride.  
13Acting under standing orders from a licensed health care professional authorized by law 
14to prescribe naloxone hydrochloride, a housing-based program shall operate in 
15accordance with rules adopted by the department and the provisions of this subsection.
16A.  Notwithstanding any other provision of law, a housing-based program shall store 
17and dispense naloxone hydrochloride and is not subject to the provisions of Title 32, 
18chapter 117.  The housing-based program shall store on site at least one unit of 
19naloxone hydrochloride for every 3 residents of the housing-based program.
20B.  A housing-based program shall provide training in administration of naloxone 
21hydrochloride that meets the protocols and criteria established by the department, and 
22full-time employees shall successfully complete the training.
23C.  A housing-based program shall distribute unit-of-use packages of naloxone 
24hydrochloride and the medical supplies necessary to administer the naloxone 
25hydrochloride to a full-time employee of the housing-based program who has 
26successfully completed the training described in paragraph B so that the employee 
27may possess and administer naloxone hydrochloride to an individual who appears to 
28be experiencing an opioid-related drug overdose.
29The department shall adopt rules to implement this subsection.  Rules adopted pursuant to 
30this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 
312-A.
32SUMMARY
33This bill requires organizations that provide housing to persons with substance use 
34disorder to store one unit of naloxone hydrochloride for every 3 residents.  It also requires 
35these organizations to provide training to full-time employees so that an employee may 
36possess and administer naloxone hydrochloride to an individual who appears to be 
37experiencing an opioid-related drug overdose.  It directs the Department of Health and 
38Human Services to adopt rules to implement these requirements.
