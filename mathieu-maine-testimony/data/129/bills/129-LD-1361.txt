2Sec. 1.  32 MRSA §2112 is enacted to read:
3§2112.  Medical standards; prohibition on government intervention
41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Evidence-based" means that the best available clinical evidence from systematic 
7research is used to make decisions about the care of an individual patient.
8B.  "Medically accurate," with respect to information, means:
9(1)  Verified or supported by the weight of peer-reviewed medical research 
10conducted in compliance with accepted scientific methods;
11(2)  Recognized as correct and objective by leading medical organizations with 
12relevant expertise; or
13(3)  Recommended by or affirmed in the medical practice guidelines of a 
14nationally recognized accrediting organization.
15C.  "Medically appropriate," with respect to information, means consistent with 
16applicable legal, health and professional standards, the patient's clinical and other 
17circumstances and the patient's reasonably known wishes and beliefs.
182.  Prohibition.  The State may not:
19A.  Require a person licensed under this chapter to provide a patient with the 
20following:
21(1)  Information that is not medically accurate or medically appropriate for the 
22patient; or
23(2)  A medical service that is not evidence-based and appropriate for the patient; 
24or
25B.  Prohibit a person licensed under this chapter from providing a patient with the 
26following:
27(1)  Information that is medically accurate and medically appropriate for the 
28patient; or
29(2)  A medical service that is evidence-based and appropriate for the patient.
303.  Professional standards.  This section may not be construed to alter professional 
31standards of care or affect the duty of a person licensed under this chapter to meet the 
32applicable standard of care.

26SUMMARY
27This bill prevents the State from requiring persons licensed by the Board of 
28Osteopathic Licensure or the Board of Licensure in Medicine, including physician 
29assistants, or the State Board of Nursing to give patients information that is medically 
30inaccurate or perform medical services that are inconsistent with evidence-based 
31standards.  It also provides that the State may not prohibit a person licensed under these 
32professional boards from providing patients with medically accurate information or 
33evidence-based medical services.
