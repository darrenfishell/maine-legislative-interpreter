2PART A
3Sec. A-1.  Deorganization of Magalloway Plantation.  Notwithstanding any 
4contrary requirement of the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 30-A, chapter 302, if in 
5accordance with Title 30-A, section 7207 a majority of the voters in Magalloway 
6Plantation approve the deorganization procedure developed in accordance with Title 
730-A, section 7205, the question of Magalloway Plantation's deorganization is approved 
8by the registered voters of Magalloway Plantation pursuant to section 8 of this Part and 
9Magalloway Plantation has executed a withdrawal agreement with Regional School Unit 
10No. 78, Magalloway Plantation in Oxford County is deorganized, except that the 
11corporate existence, powers, duties and liabilities of the plantation survive for the 
12purposes of prosecuting and defending all pending suits to which the plantation is, or may 
13be, a party and all needful process arising out of any suits, including provisions for the 
14payment of all or any judgments or debts that may be rendered against the plantation or 
15exist in favor of any creditor.
16Sec. A-2.  Financial obligations and other liabilities.  Any financial 
17obligations or other liabilities that were incurred by Magalloway Plantation as a 
18municipality or that were incurred by Magalloway Plantation as a member of Regional 
19School Unit No. 78 are hereby excepted and reserved in accordance with the Maine 
20Revised Statutes, Title 30-A, section 7303 and remain liabilities for the inhabitants of 
21lawful age residing in the territory included in the deorganized Magalloway Township for 
22the duration of the liabilities.  The State Tax Assessor shall assess taxes against the 
23property owners in the deorganized Magalloway Township to provide funds to satisfy any 
24municipal or educational obligations or other liabilities.  These financial obligations or 
25other liabilities are not the responsibility of either the Department of Education or the 
26taxpayers in the Unorganized Territory Tax District as described in Title 36, chapter 115.
27Sec. A-3.  Deorganization procedure.  The deorganization of Magalloway 
28Plantation must be conducted in accordance with the approved deorganization procedure 
29for the plantation dated October 23, 2018 that was developed in accordance with the 
30Maine Revised Statutes, Title 30-A, section 7205 and approved by a majority of the 
31plantation's voters as required in Title 30-A, section 7207, subsection 2.
32Sec. A-4.  Unexpended school funds.  The treasurer of Magalloway Plantation or 
33any other person who has custody of the funds of the plantation shall pay the Treasurer of 
34State all unexpended school funds that, together with the credits due the plantation for 
35school purposes, are to be used by the State Tax Assessor to settle any school obligations 
36incurred by the plantation before deorganization.  The State Tax Assessor shall approve 
37any written requests or invoices for payments and submit the approved documents to the 
38fiscal administrator of the unorganized territory within the Office of the State Auditor to 
39process through the Office of the State Controller.  Any unexpended school funds 
40remaining with the Treasurer of State after all the obligations have been met must be 
41deposited to the Unorganized Territory Education and Services Fund, as established in 
42the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 36, chapter 115.

11Sec. A-7.  Assessment of taxes.  The State Tax Assessor shall assess the real and 
12personal property taxes in Magalloway Plantation as of April 1, 2021 as provided in the 
13Maine Revised Statutes, Title 36, section 1602.
14Sec. A-8.  Referendum; certificate to Secretary of State.  This Part takes 
15effect 90 days after its approval only for the purpose of permitting its submission by the 
16plantation officers of Magalloway Plantation to the legal voters of the plantation by ballot 
17at the next general election to be held in November.  This election must be called, 
18advertised and conducted according to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 30-A, sections 
192528 and 2532.  The plantation clerk shall prepare the required ballots on which the clerk 
20shall reduce the subject matter of this Part to the following question:
21"Shall Magalloway Plantation be deorganized?"
22The voters shall indicate their opinion on this question by a cross or check mark 
23placed against the word "Yes" or "No."  Before becoming effective, this Part must be 
24approved by at least 2/3 of the legal voters casting ballots at the general election, and the 
25total number of votes cast for and against the acceptance of this Part at the election must 
26equal or exceed 50% of the total number of votes cast in the plantation for Governor at 
27the last gubernatorial election.
28The plantation officers of Magalloway Plantation shall declare the result of the vote.  
29The plantation clerk shall file a certificate of the election result with the Secretary of State 
30within 10 days after the date of the election.
31Sec. A-9.  Effective date.  Sections 1 to 7 of this Part take effect July 1, 2021 if the 
32legal voters of Magalloway Plantation approve the referendum under section 8 of this 
33Part.

7SUMMARY
8This bill provides for the deorganization of Magalloway Plantation in Oxford County, 
9subject to approval at local referendum and execution of a withdrawal agreement from 
10Regional School Unit No. 78.
