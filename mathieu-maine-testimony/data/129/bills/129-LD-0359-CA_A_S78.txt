26 SUMMARY 
27 The bill requires a school administrative unit with a public school in which at least 
28 50% of students qualified for a free or reduced-price lunch during the preceding school 
29 year to operate an alternative breakfast delivery service. 
30 This amendment, which is the majority report, clarifies that the alternative breakfast 
31 delivery service provides breakfast after the start of the school day and before any lunch 
32 period in the school begins for students at that public school. The amendment also 
33 provides that a school administrative unit or a public school in which at least 70% of 
34 students who are eligible for free and reduced-price meals participate in the breakfast 
35 program is exempt from the requirement of providing an alternative breakfast delivery 
36 service. The amendment revises the process as proposed in the bill for a school

This bill includes ongoing General Fund appropriations of $500,000 per year beginning in fiscal year 2019-20 to the 
School Finance and Operations program within the Department of Education to provide funding to those school 
administrative units that operate alternative breakfast delivery services that provide breakfast after the start of the 
school day. 
This fiscal note assumes that the public hearings required to be held in order for a school administrative unit to opt out 
of the alternative breakfast delivery service will be held during a regularly scheduled meeting of the local school 
administrative unit's governing board and that the required public notice will be provided as part of the regular notice 
of a meeting of the governing board.
