22SUMMARY
23 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, replaces the resolve 
24 and removes the emergency preamble and clause.  As in the majority report, the amendment 
25 requires the Department of Health and Human Services to review nonmedical 
26 transportation programs in other states that provide services under Medicaid to older adults 
27 with disabilities and other adults with disabilities, brain injury or other related conditions 
28 to determine options for providing nonmedical transportation services in this State.  The 
29 department is required to review the New York Medicaid program and any other relevant 
30 programs, determine options for modifying or amending home and community-based 
31 waiver programs, and develop possible programs, including pilot projects, for use in this 
32 State.  The department is required to submit a report to the joint standing committee of the 
33 Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters no later than 
34 February 28, 2021, and the committee is authorized to report out legislation to the First 
35 Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
36 This amendment also requires the Department of Health and Human Services to 
37 develop a pilot project lasting 18 months that provides nonmedical transportation services 
38 to individuals receiving services pursuant to Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, 
39 Section 19 up to $1,000 in addition to currently permissible medical transportation services.  
40 The department is required to submit a report with the costs and effectiveness of the 
41 program and the future viability of the program no later than January 30, 2023.  One-time 
42 funding for the development of the pilot project is provided.1
