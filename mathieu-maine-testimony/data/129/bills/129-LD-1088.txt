3ยง2917.  Eligibility for law enforcement memorial
41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Killed in the line of duty" means died as a direct and proximate result of a 
7personal injury sustained while undertaking any action that the deceased law 
8enforcement officer was obligated or authorized to perform by law, rule, regulation or 
9written condition of employment as a law enforcement officer or for which the law 
10enforcement officer was compensated by the public entity employing the law 
11enforcement officer.
12B.  "Law enforcement officer" has the same meaning as in Title 29-A, section 101, 
13subsection 30.
142.  Eligibility.  A deceased law enforcement officer's name is eligible to be included 
15on the law enforcement memorial located at the State Capitol complex if:
16A.  The law enforcement officer was killed in the line of duty and at the time of death 
17was a resident of the State or employed as a law enforcement officer in the State; and
18B.  The law enforcement officer's:
19(1)  Name is approved for inclusion in the National Law Enforcement Officers 
20Memorial in Washington, D.C.; or
21(2)  Death meets the criteria for benefits under section 1612.
223.  Application process.  Upon application, the commissioner shall add a name of a 
23law enforcement officer eligible under subsection 2 to the law enforcement memorial 
24located at the State Capitol complex.  An application under this subsection must include a 
25letter detailing the correct spelling of the law enforcement officer's name and official 
26documentation that the requirement of subsection 2, paragraph B, subparagraph (1) or (2) 
27has been met.
28SUMMARY
29This bill requires the Commissioner of Public Safety to add to the list of names on the 
30law enforcement memorial located at the State Capitol complex the name of a law 
31enforcement officer who was killed in the line of duty, who at the time of death was a 
32resident of the State or employed in the State as a law enforcement officer and whose 
33name is approved for inclusion on the National Law Enforcement Officers Memorial or 
34whose death meets the criteria for line-of-duty death benefits.
