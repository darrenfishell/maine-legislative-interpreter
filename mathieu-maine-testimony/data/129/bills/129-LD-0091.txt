18ยง3209-A.  Net energy billing
19The commission may adopt or amend rules governing net energy billing. Rules 
20adopted or amended under this section are routine technical major substantive rules as 
21defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.  "Net energy billing" means a billing and 
22metering practice under which a customer is billed on the basis of net energy over the 
23billing period taking into account accumulated unused kilowatt-hour credits from the 
24previous the difference between the kilowatt-hours delivered by a transmission and 
25distribution utility to the customer over a billing period and the kilowatt-hours delivered 
26by the customer to the transmission and distribution utility over the billing period, taking 
27into account accumulated unused kilowatt-hour credits from the previous billing period.
28Sec. 2.  Rules. Within 60 days of the effective date of this Act, the Public Utilities 
29Commission shall amend its net energy billing rules adopted pursuant to the Maine 
30Revised Statutes, Title 35-A, section 3209-A to be substantively equivalent to the rules in 
31effect on January 1, 2017.  Notwithstanding Title 35-A, section 3209-A, rules adopted for 
32this purpose are routine technical rules, as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.

3SUMMARY
4This bill clarifies the definition of "net energy billing." It requires the Public Utilities 
5Commission, within 60 days of this legislation becoming effective, to amend its net 
6energy billing rules adopted pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 35-A, section 
73209-A to be substantively equivalent to the rules in effect on January 1, 2017.  It 
8provides that all customers that entered into a net energy billing arrangement on or after 
9March 29, 2017 are governed by the rules that are to be adopted pursuant to this 
10legislation.
