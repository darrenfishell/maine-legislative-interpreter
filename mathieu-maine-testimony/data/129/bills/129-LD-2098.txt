13SUMMARY
14This bill is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Marine Resources 
15pursuant to Resolve 2019, chapter 23, section 2. The resolve directed the Commissioner 
16of Marine Resources to allow a person to raise or haul any lobster trap from September 1, 
172019 to October 31, 2019 during any time of the day in an area in the Bay of Fundy, 
18referred to as "the gray zone," that encompasses approximately 210 square miles around 
19Machias Seal Island where there are overlapping claims of sovereignty by the United 
20States and Canada if that person is authorized to fish in the lobster management zone in 
21which the area described is located.  The resolve required the commissioner to submit a 
22report to the committee describing the results of this limited allowance, and this bill 
23implements the findings of that report.
24This bill allows a person who holds a lobster and crab fishing license to raise or haul 
25any lobster trap during any time of the day from September 1st to October 31st in the 
26gray zone if that person is authorized to fish in that area. The bill also requires the 
27commissioner to define this area in rule to ensure that the boundaries of this area are 
28clearly delineated.

Fiscal Detail and Notes
This bill allows lobster and crab fishing licensees who are authorized to fish in a specific area of the Bay of Fundy 
that is subject to overlapping claims of sovereignty between the United States and Canada to commercially fish at any 
time of day between September 1st to October 31st. The bill also requires the Department of Marine Resources 
(DMR) to conduct rulemaking to define the boundaries of th e specific area subject to overlapping sovereignty. Any 
additional costs to the DMR as a result of this bill are antic ipated to be minor and can be absorbed within existing
