15C.  "Employment in the field of study" means employment in a job:

28D.  "For-profit college" means a postsecondary institution that has degree-granting

173.  Evaluation.  The state board shall by rule establish metrics to determine adequate 
18educational standards that must be met by for-profit colleges.  The metrics must include 
19at least the following annual evaluation criteria.
20A.  The state board shall evaluate spending priorities by using information supplied 
21pursuant to subsection 2, paragraph A.  If the state board finds a for-profit college 
22annually spends less than 50% of its total spending on instruction or more than 15% 
23of its total spending on advertising, the state board must find that the for-profit 
24college fails to meet adequate educational standards.
25B.  The state board shall determine whether a for-profit college has received 
26necessary accreditations to allow graduates to meet professional licensing or other 
27career standards related to the educational programs for which degrees or certificates 
28are granted by the for-profit college.  If the state board finds a for-profit college has 
29not received these necessary accreditations, the state board must find that the 
30for-profit college fails to meet adequate educational standards.
314.  Rules.  The state board shall adopt rules necessary to implement this section.  
32Rules adopted under this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
33chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
345.  Termination of degree-granting authority.  Pursuant to section 10712, the state 
35board, after completing the evaluation under subsection 3, shall terminate the 
36degree-granting authority of a for-profit college that the board finds does not meet 
37adequate educational standards.

24SUMMARY
25This bill requires annual review of for-profit colleges by the State Board of Education 
26to ensure that they are meeting adequate educational standards.  If the board finds that a 
27for-profit college is not meeting the standards, the board may terminate the 
28degree-granting authority of the for-profit college.
