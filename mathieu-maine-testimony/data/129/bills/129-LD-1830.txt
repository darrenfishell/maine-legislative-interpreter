4CHAPTER 310-A
5OFFICE OF POLICY INNOVATION AND THE FUTURE 
6§3121.  Definitions
7As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 
8have the following meanings.
91.  Director.  "Director" means the Director of the Office of Policy Innovation and 
10the Future appointed pursuant to section 3123.
112.  Office.  "Office" means the Office of Policy Innovation and the Future established 
12by section 3122.
13§3122.  Office of Policy Innovation and the Future; establishment; purposes
14The Office of Policy Innovation and the Future is established in the Executive 
15Department to: support the creation of a coherent system of policy planning and 
16coordinated implementation as one function and responsibility of the executive branch; 
17serve the Governor as a research, advisory, consultative, coordinating and administrative 
18agency; and advance policies that support a sustainable future for the State's people, 
19communities, natural resources, physical infrastructure, industries, businesses and 
20institutions by:
211.  Assistance; data; policy.  Providing technical assistance and data to the Governor 
22by undertaking special studies and plans as directed and preparing policy;
232.  Coordination.  Facilitating general intergovernmental coordination;
243.  Innovation.  Supporting state efforts to encourage innovation and policy that 
25facilitate innovation in the public and private sectors;
264.  Technology.  Supporting improved technology use for government programs and 
27advancing responsible state data policies;
285.  Resources.  Supporting the development of the State's economy and energy 
29resources with the conservation of its natural resources; and
306.  Analysis.  Conducting ongoing demographic, economic, workforce and other 
31needed analyses to support state policy makers.
Page 2 - 129LR2541(01)-11§3123.  Director
21.  Appointment; salary; qualifications.  The Director of the Office of Policy 
3Innovation and the Future is appointed by the Governor, serves at the pleasure of the 
4Governor and is paid a salary fixed by the Governor.  The director must be qualified by 
5education, training and experience in policy planning or public administration.
62.  Duties.  The director shall exercise the powers of the office and is responsible for 
7the execution of the duties of the office. The director shall:
8A.  Appoint and remove the staff of the office and prescribe the duties of the staff as 
9necessary to implement the purposes of this chapter.
10(1)  The director is authorized to hire professional personnel competent by 
11education, training and experience in the fields of economics and economic 
12development, local and regional planning, statistics, human resources, natural 
13resources, climate science, energy, transportation, finance, taxation, health, 
14education and general science and policy making.
15(2)  The director is authorized to employ office assistance as required and 
16authorized by the budget;
17B.  Supervise and administer the affairs of the office and advise the Governor and 
18other officials of State Government on matters of statewide policy planning, and 
19consult with them about policy planning and development matters and projects that 
20affect the future of the State and its people;
21C.  At the request of the Governor, act for the State in the initiation of or participation 
22in any multi-governmental agency program related to the purposes of this chapter;
23D.  At the request of the Governor, prepare and submit a budget for the office; and
24E.  At the request of the Governor, report on the activities of the office and, after 
25consultation with and approval by the Governor, submit such recommendations for 
26legislative action as are determined necessary to further the purposes of this chapter.
27Any department, agency, authority, board, commission or other instrumentality of the 
28State or other governmental unit may assist in the gathering of information, reports and 
29data that relate to state policy planning and development. Upon request by the director, 
30the Governor may assign to the office on a temporary basis personnel from any other 
31state department or agency needed for a project assigned to the office.

34A.  Provide technical assistance to the Governor in identifying intermediate and long-
35range goals and policies for the State and appropriate measures to achieve these 
36goals;
37B.  Conduct continuing analysis of the economy, human resources, natural resources

3C.  Participate with other states or their subdivisions in interstate policy planning and

102.  Contracts; agreements; funding.  The office may:
11A.  With the consent of the Governor, employ expert and professional consultants and 
12contract for services as the director determines necessary, within the limits of the 
13funds provided and consistent with the powers and duties of the office;
14B.  Enter into agreements with the Federal Government and other agencies and 
15organizations as will promote the objectives of this chapter; and
16C.  Accept funds from the Federal Government or from any individual, foundation or 
17corporation and expend these funds for purposes consistent with this chapter.
18Sec. 3.  Construction. Nothing in this Act or in the establishment of the Office of 
19Policy Innovation and the Future under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 
20310-A may be construed to restrict, limit or alter any policy or planning authority 
21conferred by law to any state department or agency, director of a state department or 
22agency, instrumentality of the State, regional planning agency or municipality, except as 
23provided in this chapter.
24Sec. 4.  Maine Revised Statutes amended; revision clause.    Wherever in the 
25Maine Revised Statutes the words "Governor's Office of Policy and Management" appear 
26or reference is made to that entity or those words, those words are amended to read or 
27mean, as appropriate, "Office of Policy Innovation and the Future" and the Revisor of 
28Statutes shall implement this revision when updating, publishing or republishing the 
29statutes.
30SUMMARY
31This bill eliminates the Governor’s Office of Policy and Management and establishes 
32the Office of Policy Innovation and the Future to support the creation of a coherent 
33system of policy planning and coordinated implementation as one function and 
34responsibility of the executive branch of State Government, serve as a research, advisory, 
35consultative, coordinating and administrative agency and advance policies that support a 
36sustainable future for Maine's people, communities, natural resources, physical 
37infrastructure, industries, businesses and institutions.
