6SUMMARY
7This bill provides a tax credit for the production of renewable chemicals by the 
8conversion of renewable biomass from the forest, farms, the sea or solid waste.  The 
9credit is equal to 7¢ per pound of renewable chemical produced in the State, 9¢ per pound 
10of renewable chemical produced in the State if the taxpayer demonstrates to the 
11satisfaction of the Department of Economic and Community Development that the 
12contractors hired or retained by a landowner to harvest renewable biomass used in 
13production of the renewable chemicals are 3rd-party certified by the Northeast Master 
14Logger program or successor program and at least 50% of the contractors' employees are 
15residents of the United States or 12¢ per pound of renewable chemical produced in the 
16State if the taxpayer demonstrates to the satisfaction of the Department of Economic and 
17Community Development that the contractors hired or retained by a landowner to harvest 
18renewable biomass used in such production are 3rd-party certified by the Northeast 
19Master Logger program and at least 75% of the contractors' employees are residents of 
20the United States.  Renewable chemicals are defined to include chemicals, polymers, 
21plastics and formulated products, and to exclude substances used for food, feed or fuel, 
22with limited exceptions.  The bill also reinstates the tax credit for commercial production 
23and use of biofuels.
