30Sec. 3.  5 MRSA c. 337-D is enacted to read:
31CHAPTER 337-D
32MAINE FAIR CHANCE HOUSING ACT
33§4711.  Short title
34This chapter may be known and cited as "the Maine Fair Chance Housing Act."
Page 2 - 129LR0378(01)-11§4712.  Declaration of policy
2It is the declared public policy of the State to ensure that equitable access to safe, 
3decent and affordable housing extends to all residents of the State, including individuals 
4involved in the criminal justice system and their families.
5§4713.  Definitions
6As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 
7have the following meanings.
81.  Criminal history information.  "Criminal history information" includes criminal 
9history record information as defined in Title 16, section 703, subsection 3 and other 
10information regarding the criminal history of an applicant, from whatever source.
112.  Housing provider.  "Housing provider" means an entity that owns, leases, 
12manages or develops housing in the State and any agent, such as a property management 
13company, that makes tenancy decisions on behalf of such an entity.
143.  Political subdivision.  "Political subdivision" has the same meaning as in Title 
1530-A, section 2252.
164.  State.  "State" means the State or any office, department, agency, authority, 
17commission, board, institution, hospital or other instrumentality of the State.
18§4714.  Limitations on use and disclosure of criminal history information
191.  Inquiry only after conditional offer of housing.  A housing provider may not 
20inquire into or consider an applicant's criminal history information until the housing 
21provider has determined that the applicant meets all other qualifications for tenancy.
222.  Confidential.  Criminal history information obtained or in the possession of the 
23State or any housing provider is confidential. A housing provider may not distribute or 
24disseminate criminal history information except as required by law. Information that 
25pertains to an applicant's criminal background check obtained in conjunction with a 
26housing decision by the State or a political subdivision is confidential unless disclosure is 
27specifically required by law.
28§4715.  Violation by a private housing provider
291.  Maine Human Rights Commission.  An individual who is aggrieved by a private 
30housing provider's violation of this chapter may file a complaint with the Maine Human 
31Rights Commission pursuant to section 4635.  A private housing provider who violates 
32section 4714 is subject to fines and shall submit to an investigation by the commission, as 
33specified in section 4635, subsection 1.
342.  Civil action.  An aggrieved individual may bring a civil action in a court of 
35competent jurisdiction against a private housing provider that has violated section 4714 
36and, upon prevailing, is entitled to such legal or equitable relief as the court may 
37determine appropriate and reasonable attorney's fees and costs.
Page 3 - 129LR0378(01)-11§4716.  Violation by a public housing provider
2An individual who is aggrieved by a violation of section 4714 by the State or a

7§4717.  Application
8The provisions of this chapter apply to all housing providers notwithstanding any 
9provision of law to the contrary, except to the extent the other provision of law contains 
10additional limitations on the consideration of criminal history information of applicants 
11for housing.
12SUMMARY
13This bill establishes the Maine Fair Chance Housing Act, the purpose of which is to 
14ensure that a person is not denied housing based solely on the existence of a history of 
15criminal convictions.  This bill prohibits a housing provider from considering an 
16applicant’s criminal history until after the housing provider determines that the applicant 
17meets all other qualifications for tenancy.
18A person who is aggrieved by a violation of the Maine Fair Chance Housing Act by a 
19housing provider may file a grievance with the Maine Human Rights Commission and, if 
20it is a violation by a private housing provider, may bring a civil action in court.
