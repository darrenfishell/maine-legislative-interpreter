26SUMMARY
27This bill permits the Department of Public Safety, Gambling Control Board, 
28beginning January 1, 2020, to issue a license to a charitable nonprofit organization that is 
29a veterans' organization that is tax-exempt under the United States Internal Revenue Code 
30of 1986 to operate up to 3 slot machines on premises that have been owned, rented or 
31leased by the organization for at least 2 consecutive years, that serve as its primary 
32administrative operations headquarters and that are located in a municipality that has, by 
33referendum of the voters, approved the operation of slot machines in that municipality. 
34The charitable nonprofit veterans' organization must be able to demonstrate that it has a 
35cash reserve of $1,000 for each machine the organization intends to operate.
36A charitable nonprofit veterans' organization that wishes to apply prior to January 1, 
372020 may file a declaration of intent to apply with the Gambling Control Board. An 
38application must include a refundable $2,500 deposit. The initial application fee for a slot 
39machine operator license is $500, and the annual renewal fee is $175.
Page 8 - 129LR0709(01)-11A slot machine operated by a charitable nonprofit veterans' organization is subject to 
2the same central site monitoring that applies to casinos and slot machine facilities at 
3harness racing tracks. The total number of slot machines allowed to be operated by 
4charitable nonprofit veterans' organizations statewide between January 1, 2020 and 
5December 31, 2020 is 80; beginning January 1, 2021 the number increases to 150.
6The bill provides that 10% of the net slot machine income from a charitable nonprofit 
7veterans' organization is required to be deposited directly with the Gambling Control 
8Board for administrative expenses; 8% goes directly to the General Fund; 10% goes to 
9the host municipality; and 2% is dedicated to gambling addiction prevention and 
10treatment. A charitable nonprofit veterans' organization that is licensed to operate slot 
11machines is required to establish a separate account, from which the board may withdraw 
12funds to distribute the net revenue percentages. The remaining revenue generated from 
13the slot machines must be used to support the charitable purposes of the veterans' 
14organization.
