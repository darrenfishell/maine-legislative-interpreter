14 'Sec. 1. 36 MRSA §2729 is enacted to read: 
15 §2729. Credit 
16 1. Purpose. It is the public policy of this State, as a market participant in 
17 commercial forestry. to make a credit available to those taxed under this chapter. The 
18 credit is an incentive to promote fair and just commercial forestry in the State, preserve 
19 numerous opportunities for jobs for people of the State and ensure the preservation of an 
20 improved economy in the State and for its people. 
21 2. Credit. An owner of commercial forest land subject to tax under this chapter is 
22 entitled to an annual credit equal to 100% of the taxes assessed under this chapter if the 
23 landowner meets the conditions specified in subsection 3. 
24 3. Conditions of eligibility. A landowner subject to tax under this chapter is eligible 
25 for a credit under this section if the landowner demonstrates to the State Tax Assessor 
26 that all businesses, including forest road construction and trucking businesses, and 
27 contractors. including subcontractors, hired or retained by the landowner in the harvesting 
28 of timber on commercial forest land owned by the landowner are based in the United 
29 States and that the landowner has obtained from each business and contractor affidavits 
30 stating that: 
31 A. At least 7 5% of the employees of the business or contractor are residents of the 
32 United States; and 
33 B. The business or contractor is current in the payment of all taxes due the State or 
34 political subdivisions of the State, including, but not limited to, payroll taxes, 
35 unemployment taxes and personal property taxes. This paragraph may not be

14 SUMMARY 
15 This amendment requires a landowner seeking the credit against the commercial 
16 forestry excise tax to obtain affidavits indicating that businesses or contractors retained 
17 by the landowner are based in the United States, that at least 75% of their employees are 
18 United States residents and that they are current in the payment of all state and local 
19 taxes. It makes other technical changes to the bill.
