13'Sec. 1.  38 MRSA §1612 is enacted to read:
14§1612.  Hydrofluorocarbon use restrictions
151.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
16following terms have the following meanings.
17A.  "Aerosol propellant" means a compressed gas or vapor contained in a container 
18that, upon release of pressure and expansion through a valve, carries another 
19substance from the container as a mist or spray.
20B.  "Air conditioning equipment" means chillers designed for the comfort cooling of 
21occupied spaces.
22C.  "Built-in household refrigerator or freezer" means a refrigerator, refrigerator-
23freezer or freezer designed for residential use that:
24(1)  Has 7.75 cubic feet or greater total volume and 24 inches or less depth not 
25including doors, handles and custom front panels;
26(2) Has sides that are not finished and are not designed to be visible after 
27installation; 
28(3)  Is designed, intended and marketed exclusively to be installed completely 
29encased by cabinetry or panels that are attached during installation and securely 
30fastened to adjacent cabinetry, walls or floor; and
31(4)  Is equipped with an integral factory-finished face or that accepts a custom 
32front panel.Date: (Filing No. H-          )

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT1D.  "Capital cost" means an expense incurred in the production of goods or in the 
2rendering of services, including, but not limited to, the cost of engineering; the cost of

4contractor and construction fees.

7cooling.  "Centrifugal chiller" does not include air conditioning equipment used for 
8industrial process cooling and refrigeration.

10dairy products and other products prior to the delivery of those products to other 
11locations for sale to the ultimate consumer.
12G.  "Compact household refrigerator or freezer" means a refrigerator, refrigerator-
13freezer or freezer designed for residential use that has a total refrigerated volume of 
14less than 7.75 cubic feet.
15H.  "Component" means a part of a refrigeration system, including, but not limited to, 
16a condensing unit, compressor, condenser, evaporator or receiver and all of the 
17refrigeration system's connections and subassemblies without which the refrigeration 
18system would not properly function or would be subject to failure.
19I.  "End use" means a process or class of specific applications within an industry 
20sector.
21J.  "Flexible polyurethane" means a nonrigid synthetic foam containing polymers of 
22urethane radicals, including, but not limited to, foam used in furniture, bedding, chair 
23cushions and shoe soles.
24K.  "Foam-blowing agent" or "foam" means a product or substance used to produce a 
25product with a cellular structure formed via a foaming process, including materials 
26that undergo hardening or phase transition, such as polymers and plastics.
27L.  "Heat pump" means a device designed for the comfort heating or cooling of 
28residential or commercial spaces, whether air sourced, water sourced or ground 
29sourced, including, but not limited to, a mini-split heat pump.
30M.  "Household refrigerator or freezer" means a refrigerator, refrigerator-freezer,

32residential use.  "Household refrigerator or freezer" does not include a compact 
33household refrigerator or freezer or a built-in household refrigerator or freezer.

35polymers of urethane radicals, including, but not limited to, foam used in shoe soles 
36and automobile steering wheels and dashboards.

38defined in Title 5, section 1812-E.
39P.  "Metered dose inhaler" means a device that delivers a measured amount of 
40medication as a mist that an individual can inhale and that consists of a pressurized 
41canister of medication in a case with a mouthpiece.

13the date of an applicable prohibition under subsection 2 on the selling, leasing, 
14renting, installing or entering into commerce in the State of that product or equipment 
15may be sold in, imported into, exported from, distributed in, installed in and used in 
16the State after that date.

18person to sell, lease, rent, install or enter into commerce in the State for a period of

22process by which a person may submit such a waiver request and the criteria to be 
23used by the department in assessing and approving or denying such waiver requests.
