111-A.  Critical incident stress management peer support.  "Critical incident stress 
12management peer support" means services provided to an employee of or emergency 
13dispatcher for a public safety agency or to an employee's or dispatcher's family member 
14when that employee or dispatcher has been involved in a critical incident that can 
15reasonably have a devastating, long-lasting effect on a person or the employee or 
16dispatcher has had cumulative exposure to excessive stress and traumatic incidents and 
17the employee's or dispatcher's work performance or general well-being has been affected.  
18"Critical incident stress management peer support" includes assisting the employee or 
19dispatcher to appropriately process the trauma and stress and connecting the employee or 
20dispatcher to appropriate resources.
211-B.  Critical incident stress management peer support person.  "Critical incident 
22stress management peer support person" means a person who is trained in accordance 
23with evidence-based standards and certified in crisis intervention training and mental 
24health first aid who provides one-on-one critical incident stress management peer support.

15SUMMARY
16This bill clarifies the qualifications and duties of members of critical incident stress 
17management teams and provides that the services of critical incident stress management 
18teams are provided to employees of state, county or municipal government entities that 
19provide or have the authority to provide fire, emergency medical or police services.
