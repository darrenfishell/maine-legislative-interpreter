41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Operator" means a person designated by the owner of a tanning facility or the 
7lessee of a tanning device to operate, or to assist and instruct in the operation and use 
8of, a tanning facility or tanning device.
9B.  "Phototherapy device" means equipment that emits ultraviolet radiation and is 
10used in the diagnosis or treatment of disease or injury.
11C.  "Tanning device" means equipment that emits electromagnetic radiation having 
12wavelengths in air between 200 and 400 nanometers that is used for the tanning of 
13human skin and any equipment used with that equipment, including but not limited to 
14protective eyewear, timers and handrails.  "Tanning device" includes a sunlamp, 
15tanning booth or tanning bed but does not include a phototherapy device used or 
16prescribed for use by a physician.
17D.  "Tanning facility" means a location, place, area, structure or business that 
18provides persons access to a tanning device, including tanning salons, health clubs, 
19apartments and condominiums, regardless of whether a fee is charged for access to 
20the tanning device.
212.  Prohibition.  An owner of a tanning facility, a lessee of a tanning device or an 
22operator may not allow an individual under 18 years of age to use a tanning device. Proof 
23of age may be satisfied with a driver's license or other government-issued identification 
24containing the date of birth and a photograph of the individual.
253.  Notice.  An owner of a tanning facility or a lessee of a tanning device shall post in 
26a conspicuous place in the tanning facility notice, in a form developed by the department:
27A.  That it is unlawful for a tanning facility, a lessee of a tanning device or an 
28operator to allow an individual under 18 years of age to use a tanning device;
29B.  That a tanning facility, a lessee of a tanning device or an operator that violates the 
30provisions of this section is subject to penalties;
31C.  That an individual may report a violation of this section to the local law 
32enforcement agency or radiation control program of the Maine Center for Disease 
33Control and Prevention; and
34D.  That the health risks associated with tanning include but are not limited to skin 
35cancer, premature aging of the skin, burns to the skin and adverse reactions to certain 
36medications, foods and cosmetics.
374.  Written statement.  An owner of a tanning facility, a lessee of a tanning device 
38or an operator shall provide to every customer prior to that customer's first use in that 
39calendar year of that particular tanning device a written statement that must be signed by

85.  Duties of owner.  An owner of a tanning facility, a lessee of a tanning device or 
9an operator shall ensure that:
10A.  An individual under 18 years of age is not permitted to use the tanning facility;
11B.  There is present at the tanning facility during its hours of operation an operator 
12who is able to inform customers about, and assist customers in, the proper use of 
13tanning devices;
14C.  Each tanning device is properly sanitized after each use;
15D.  Before a customer uses a tanning device, the customer is provided, at no cost, 
16with properly sanitized and securely fitting protective eyewear that protects the 
17customer's eyes from ultraviolet radiation and allows enough vision to maintain 
18balance;
19E.  A customer is not allowed to use a tanning device unless the customer uses 
20protective eyewear;
21F.  A customer is shown how to use physical aids including handrails and markings 
22on the floor to maintain a proper exposure distance from the tanning device as 
23recommended by the manufacturer;
24G.  A timing device that is accurate within 10% of any selected timer interval is used 
25and is remotely located so customers cannot set their own exposure time;
26H.  Each tanning device is equipped with a mechanism that allows the customer to 
27turn the tanning device off;
28I.  A customer is limited to the maximum exposure time recommended by the 
29manufacturer for that customer's skin type;
30J.  A customer is not allowed to use a tanning device more than once every 24 hours;
31K.  The interior temperature of the tanning facility does not exceed 100 degrees 
32Fahrenheit; and
33L.  The following records are maintained:  copies of all consent forms signed by 
34customers; a record of a customer's total number of uses of a tanning device at the 
35facility; the dates and durations of uses of a tanning device; and any injury reports for 
36a period of 3 years after tanning device use for each customer.
376.  Duties of customer.  A customer may not use a tanning device of a tanning

67.  Certificate of registration.  A person may not operate a tanning facility without

108.  Violation; penalty.  A person who violates this section is subject to civil penalties

139.  Local ordinance.  This section does not preempt local ordinances that provide for 
14more restrictive regulation of tanning facilities than required in this section.
15SUMMARY
16This bill: 
171.  Prohibits a tanning facility from allowing an individual who has not attained 18 
18years of age to use a tanning device;
192.  Requires that the owner of a tanning facility or the lessee of a tanning device post 
20a conspicuous notice regarding the laws governing tanning and the health risks associated 
21with tanning;
223.  Requires that each customer, prior to that customers first use in that calendar year 
23of that tanning device, sign an acknowledgment that the customer understands the posted 
24notice and agrees to use protective eyewear; and
254.  Authorizes municipalities to adopt more restrictive regulations than required in 
26this bill.
