41.  Determination of wage and benefit rates.  The Bureau of Labor Standards shall 
5investigate and determine the prevailing hourly wage and benefits rate paid in the 
6construction industry in this State. To determine the prevailing hourly wage and benefit 
7rate, the bureau shall collect one set of data by conducting a survey of wages and benefits 
8during the 2nd and 3rd week of September July of each year and a 2nd set of data though 
9certified payroll submissions on state construction projects during the 2nd and 3rd week 
10of July of each year; the bureau shall use the higher wage and benefit information of the 2 
11data sets to determine the prevailing hourly wage and benefit rate.  The bureau may also 
12use wage and benefit information received from construction trade associations in its 
13determination of prevailing rates.  Prevailing wages and benefits must be determined in 
14September 1999 and become effective upon determination.  In determining the prevailing 
15rates, the bureau may ascertain and consider the applicable wage and benefits rates 
16established by collective bargaining agreements, if any, and those rates that are paid 
17generally in the locality where the construction of the public works is to be performed.  
18For purposes of this subsection, "benefits" means health and welfare contributions, 
19pension or individual retirement account contributions and vacation and annuity 
20contributions, per diem in lieu of wages and any other form of payment, except for 
21wages, made to or on behalf of the employee.  If a defined contribution amount is not 
22established, the most accurate estimated value of contributions must be included.

30SUMMARY
31This bill directs the Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Standards, when 
32determining prevailing hourly wages and benefits, to collect one set of data through 
33conducting a survey of wages and benefits and a 2nd set of data through certified payroll 
34submissions on state construction projects during 2 weeks in July of each year and to use 
35the higher wage and benefit information of the 2 data sets to determine the prevailing 
36hourly wage and benefit rate.  This bill also increases from $50 to $250 the penalty for 
37failing to provide requested information to the bureau.
