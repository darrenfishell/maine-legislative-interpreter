16SUMMARY
17This bill amends the laws relating to the State's regulation of the shoreland zone as 
18follows.
191.  It requires the Department of Economic and Community Development, Office of 
20Community Development to provide both basic and advanced training to code 
21enforcement officers in the technical and legal aspects of code enforcement.  Current law 
22only requires that the office provide basic training to code enforcement officers.
232.  It increases the maximum per day civil penalty for a specific violation of a 
24municipal land use law or ordinance from $2,500 to $5,000 and increases the maximum 
25per day civil penalty for a specific violation of a municipal shoreland zoning ordinance 
26occurring within an area zoned for resource protection from $5,000 to $10,000.
273.  It provides that a municipal shoreland zoning ordinance must require an applicant 
28for a permit for development within the shoreland zone to provide to the municipal 
29permitting authority preconstruction and postconstruction photographs of the shoreline 
30vegetation and development site.
