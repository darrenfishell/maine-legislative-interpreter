4§4008.  School counselors and school social workers
51.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context indicates otherwise, the 
6following terms have the following meanings.
7A.  "Client" means a person who has actively sought or is in the process of seeking 
8professional help from a school counselor or school social worker.
9A-1.  "Direct services" means in-person interactions between a school counselor or 
10school social worker and students, including, but not limited to, delivering the school 
11guidance curriculum, guiding individual student planning and providing responsive 
12services.
13A-2.  "Indirect services" means services provided by a school counselor or school 
14social worker on behalf of students as a result of a school counselor's or school social 
15worker's interactions with the students and others, including, but not limited to, 
16providing referrals for additional assistance and consultation and collaboration with 
17parents, teachers and other educators.
18B.  "School counselor" means a person who is employed as a school counselor in a 
19school setting and who: 
20(1)  Is certified as a school counselor by the department; or 
21(2)  Possesses a minimum of a master's degree in an approved program in 
22guidance and counseling.
23C.  "School social worker" means a person who is employed as a school social 
24worker in a school setting and who:
25(1)  Is licensed as a social worker by the State Board of Social Worker Licensure; 
26or
27(2)  Possesses a bachelor's degree and has been granted a conditional license from 
28the State Board of Social Worker Licensure.
292.  Privileged communication.  A school counselor or school social worker may not 
30be required, except as provided by this section subsection 3, to divulge or release 
31information gathered during a counseling relation with a client or with the parent, the 
32guardian or a person or agency having legal custody of a minor client.  A counseling 
33relation and the information resulting from it shall must be kept confidential consistent 
34with the professional obligations of the counselor or social worker.
353.  Exceptions.  This section shall not apply to the extent that disclosure of 
36information Notwithstanding subsection 2, a school counselor or school social worker 
37may be required to disclose information that is necessary: 
38A.  To comply with Title 22, chapter 1071; and or
Page 2 - 129LR0653(01)-11B.  To report to an appropriate authority or to take an appropriate emergency measure 
2when: 
3(1)  The client's condition requires others to assume responsibility for the client; 
4or 
5(2)  There is clear and imminent danger to the client or others.
64.  Direct and indirect services to students.  Each school counselor and school 
7social worker shall deliver a comprehensive school counseling program in which the 
8school counselor or school social worker spends at least 80% of the school counselor's or 
9school social worker's time providing direct services or indirect services to students.  The 
10commissioner shall adopt rules to implement this subsection.  Rules adopted pursuant to 
11this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 
122-A.
13SUMMARY
14This bill requires each school counselor and school social worker to spend at least 
1580% of that counselor's or social worker's time providing direct or indirect services to 
16students and requires the Commissioner of Education to adopt rules to implement this 
17provision.
