ยง10706-A. For-profit college and university review 
1. Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
following terms have the following meanings. 
A. "Adequate educational standards" means educational offerings and spending 
priorities that the state board finds meet the metrics established under subsection 3. 
B. "Employed" means a person has obtained permanent employment and: 
(1) The position is paid. If the position is an entry-level position, the salary must 
be at least 80% of the entry-level salary for the position as listed by the 
Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor Standards in its most recent report on 
industry employment and wages; 
(2) The position requires at least 32 hours of work per week; 
(3) The person has worked in the position for at least 30 days; and 
(4) The position is not affiliated with a for-profit college or university from 
which the person graduated, its parent company or a subsidiary or affiliate of its 
parent company. 
C. "Employment in the field of study" means employment in a job: 
(1) That is included on a list of job titles for which a graduate of a program of 
study is qualified, as published by the for-profit college or university, and in the

8 3. Evaluation. The commissioner shall by rule establish metrics to determine 
9 adequate educational standards that must be met by for-profit colleges and universities. 
1 O The metrics must include at least the following annual evaluation criteria. 
11 A. The commissioner shall evaluate spending priorities by using information 
12 supplied pursuant to subsection 2. paragraph A. If the commissioner finds a 
13 for-profit college or university spends less than 50% of its total spending on 
14 instruction or more than 15% of its total spending on advertising. the commissioner 
15 must find that the for-profit college or university fails to meet adequate educational 
16 standards. 
17 B. The commissioner shall determine whether a for-profit college or university has 
18 received necessary accreditations to allow graduates to meet professional licensing or 
19 other career standards related to the educational programs for which degrees or 
20 certificates are granted by the for-profit college or university. If the commissioner 
21 finds a for-profit college or university has not received these necessary accreditations, 
22 the commissioner must find that the for-profit college or university fails to meet 
23 adequate educational standards. 
24 C. The commissioner shall evaluate the design and implementation of student 
25 support services. including whether the complaint process by which student 
26 complaints are handled is transparent. includes an appropriate official to receive 
27 complaints and provides appropriate and timely responses to complaints. If the 
28 commissioner finds that a for-profit college or university does not have adequate or 
29 appropriate student support services. the commissioner must find that the for-profit 
30 college or university fails to meet adequate educational standards. 
31 4. Rules. The commissioner shall adopt rules necessary to implement this section. 
32 The rules must include definitions of "educational instruction." "advertising" and 
33 "executive salaries." Rules adopted under this subsection are routine technical rules as 
34 defined in Title 5. chapter 375. subchapter 2-A. 
35 5. Termination of degree-granting authority. The commissioner, after completing 
36 the evaluation under subsection 3, shall terminate the degree-granting authority of a 
37 for-profit college or university that the commissioner finds does not meet adequate

SUMMARY 
2 This amendment, which is the majority report, clatifies that the bill applies to 
3 for-profit universities as well as colleges and expands the definition of "for-profit college 
4 or university" to include a postsecondary institution that is regulated by the Department 
5 of Education or the State Board of Education, is eligible to participate in federal student 
6 aid programs and is operated by a private, for-profit business. The amendment requires 
7 the Commissioner of Education, instead of the State Board of Education, to review a 
8 for-profit college or university and adds a requirement that student support services, 
9 including the process by which student complaints are handled, be reviewed by the 
10 Commissioner of Education to determine whether adequate educational standards are 
11 being met by a for-profit college or university ..
