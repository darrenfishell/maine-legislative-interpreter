6established.  The Independent Board To Review Law Enforcement Officer-involved 
7Deaths, as established in Title 5, section 12004-J, subsection 18, is an independent board 
8responsible for reviewing investigations of incidents of officer-involved death.
93.  Membership of board; appointment.  The board consists of 7 members 
10appointed by the Attorney General as follows:
11A.  Two employees in the Office of the Attorney General;
12B.  Two persons recommended by a recognized statewide organization representing 
13criminal defense attorneys;
14C.  One member of the clergy recommended by a recognized statewide 
15nondenominational organization representing religious interests;
16D.  One person recommended by the Maine Sheriffs' Association or successor 
17organization; and
18E.  One person recommended by a nationally recognized organization that advocates 
19for civil rights and minorities.
204.  Term; chair.  Members are appointed to serve a 3-year term.  The members shall 
21elect one member to serve as chair for at least a 2-year term.
225.  Staff assistance; support.  The Office of the Attorney General shall provide 
23necessary staff assistance and administrative support to the board.
246.  Compensation.  A member of the board serves without compensation but must be 
25reimbursed for expenses, including travel expenses, incurred in the performance of the 
26member's duties.
277.  Duties.  In a case of an officer-involved death, the board shall determine the facts 
28of the incident, including reviewing all notes, data and reports of the law enforcement 
29officer and the law enforcement agency involved in or responding to the incident; 
30interviewing witnesses and experts; and collecting other information concerning the 
31incident that the board determines relevant.  The board may collaborate with or seek 
32guidance from other state and federal law enforcement agencies with experience in 
33officer-involved deaths, including an incident review team convened by the Maine 
34Criminal Justice Academy.  After reviewing the information the board determines 
35relevant, the board shall issue recommendations to the prosecuting attorney of the county 
36in which the incident occurred or, if the prosecuting attorney is the Attorney General, the 
37Attorney General within 90 days of the incident unless the board requests an extension of 
38time from the Attorney General.  The board shall also issue a public report with its 
39findings and recommendations that the Attorney General shall publish on its publicly 
40accessible website.

9SUMMARY
10This bill establishes the Independent Board To Review Law Enforcement Officer-
11involved Deaths to review investigations by law enforcement agencies concerning deaths 
12involving law enforcement officers and to issue recommendations to the prosecuting 
13attorneys or Attorney General.  The board consists of 7 members appointed to 3-year 
14terms, and members of the board are conferred immunity in performing their duties on 
15the board, except when the plaintiff is the State.  The bill requires the board to issue a 
16public report of its findings and conclusions for every officer-involved death in the State.
