2Sec. 1.  14 MRSA c. 15 is enacted to read:
3CHAPTER 15
4UNIFORM INTERSTATE DEPOSITIONS AND DISCOVERY ACT
5§401.  Short title
6This chapter may be known and cited as "the Uniform Interstate Depositions and 
7Discovery Act."
8§402.  Definitions
9As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 
10have the following meanings.
111.  Foreign jurisdiction.  "Foreign jurisdiction" means a state other than this State.
122.  Foreign subpoena.  "Foreign subpoena" means a subpoena issued under authority 
13of a court of record of a foreign jurisdiction.
143.  Person.  "Person" means an individual, corporation, business trust, estate, trust, 
15partnership, limited liability company, association, joint venture, public corporation, 
16government or governmental subdivision, agency or instrumentality or any other legal or 
17commercial entity.
184.  State.  "State" means a state of the United States, the District of Columbia, Puerto 
19Rico, the United States Virgin Islands, a federally recognized Indian tribe or any territory 
20or insular possession subject to the jurisdiction of the United States.
215.  Subpoena.  "Subpoena" means a document, however denominated, issued under 
22authority of a court of record requiring a person to:
23A.  Attend and give testimony at a deposition;
24B.  Produce and permit inspection and copying of designated books, documents, 
25records, electronically stored information or tangible things in the possession, 
26custody or control of the person; or
27C.  Permit inspection of premises under the control of the person.
28§403.  Issuance of subpoena
29The issuance of a subpoena is governed by this section.
301.  Request issuance.  To request issuance of a subpoena under this section, a party 
31must submit a foreign subpoena to the clerk of a District Court in the district or to the 
32clerk of the Superior Court of the county in which the discovery is to be conducted.  A 
33request for the issuance of a subpoena under this Act does not constitute an appearance in 
34the courts of the State.
Page 2 - 129LR1425(01)-112.  Submission of foreign subpoena.  When a party submits a foreign subpoena to a 
2clerk of court in the State, the clerk, in accordance with that court's procedure, shall 
3promptly issue a subpoena for service upon the person to which the foreign subpoena is 
4directed.
53.  Requirements.  A foreign subpoena submitted under subsection 2 must:
6A.  Incorporate the terms used in the foreign subpoena; and
7B.  Contain or be accompanied by the names, addresses and telephone numbers of all 
8counsel of record in the proceeding to which the subpoena relates and of any party 
9not represented by counsel.
10§404.  Service of subpoena
11A subpoena issued by a clerk of court under section 403 must be served in 
12compliance with the Maine Rules of Civil Procedure, Rule 45.
13§405.  Deposition, production and inspection
14The Maine Rules of Civil Procedure and the provisions of Title 16, sections 101, 102 
15and 251 apply to depositions and discovery carried out under this Act and subpoenas 
16issued under section 403.
17§406.  Application to court
18An application to the court for a protective order or to enforce, quash or modify a 
19subpoena issued by a clerk of court under section 403 must comply with the Maine Rules 
20of Civil Procedure and be submitted to the District Court in the district or to the Superior 
21Court of the county in which the discovery is to be or is being conducted.
22§407.  Uniformity of application and construction
23In applying and construing this Act, consideration must be given to the need to 
24promote uniformity of the law with respect to its subject matter among states that enact it.
25§408.  Application to pending actions
26Notwithstanding Title 1, section 302, this Act applies to requests for discovery in 
27cases pending on the effective date of this Act.
28SUMMARY
29This bill enacts in Maine law the Uniform Interstate Depositions and Discovery Act.
