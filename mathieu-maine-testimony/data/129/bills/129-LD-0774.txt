2This bill amends the State's coastal management policies laws to ensure that those 
3policies address the effects of coastal erosion on the State's beaches and shoreline and 
4ensure the health of the State's beaches and shoreline for habitat protection for coastal 
5wildlife species and for protection against storms.
6The bill also creates an advisory group on beach management, to be convened by the 
7Commissioner of Environmental Protection for the purpose of developing and 
8recommending objectives, strategies, legislation and other actions to mitigate coastal 
9erosion in the State and to keep beaches healthy for storm protection, habitat protection 
10for coastal wildlife species and for recreational use.  The group is directed to review the 
11report titled "Integrated Beach Management Program Working Group Report," dated 
12January 2017 and submitted to the Joint Standing Committee on Environment and 
13Natural Resources in the First Regular Session of the 128th Legislature, update the data 
14and findings contained in that report, if necessary, and review and update the specific 
15recommendations for legislative, regulatory or other actions contained in that report, if 
16necessary.  No later than February 15, 2020, the commissioner is required to submit a 
17report to the Joint Standing Committee on Environment and Natural Resources 
18containing the findings and recommendations of the group following its review of the 
192017 report, including any proposed legislation necessary to implement those 
20recommendations.  After reviewing the report, the committee may report out legislation 
21to implement those recommendations.
