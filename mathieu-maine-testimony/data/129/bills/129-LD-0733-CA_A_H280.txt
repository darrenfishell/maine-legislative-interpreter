1Sec. 1. 26 MRSA §§599-A and 599-B are enacted to read: 
§599-A. Noncompete agreements 
1. Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
following terms have the following meanings. 
A. 11Federal poverty level11 means the nonfarm income official poverty line for an 
individual, as defmed by the federal Office of Management and Budget and revised 
annually in accordance with the Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act of 1981, Section 
673(2). 
B. 11Noncompete agreement11 means a contract or contract provision that prohibits an 
employee or prospective employee from working in the same or a similar profession 
or in a specified geographic area for a certain penod of time following termination of 
employment. 
2. Public policy; enforceability of noncompete agreements. Noncompete 
agreements are contrary to public policy and are enforceable only to the extent that they 
are reasonable and are no broader than necessary to protect one or more of the following 
legitimate business interests of the employer: 
A. The employer1s trade secrets, as defined in Title 10, section 1542, subsection 4; 
B. The employer's confidential information that does not qualify as a trade secret; or 
C. The employer1s goodwill. 
A noncompete agreement may be presumed necessary if the legitimate business interest 
cannot be adequately protected through an alternative restrictive covenant, including but

13 SUMMARY 
14 This amendment strikes and replaces the bill. It moves the language in the bill to 
15 another location in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 26 and restricts the enforceability of 
16 noncompete agreements to the extent that they are reasonable and are no broader than 
17 necessary to protect a legitimate business interest of the employer, such as trade secrets, 
18 confidential information or goodwill. It also adds a presumption that a noncompete 
19 agreement is necessary if the legitimate business interest cannot be adequately protected 
20 through an alternative restrictive covenant. As in the bill, it also provides for ongoing 
21 appropriations to provide for enforcement of the provisions.
