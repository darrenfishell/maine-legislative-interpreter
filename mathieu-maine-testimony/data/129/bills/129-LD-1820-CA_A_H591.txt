14 SUMMARY 
15 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, does the following. 
16 1. It clarifies that "covered investigation" means an investigation by a school entity 
17 into the conduct of a holder of a credential that a school entity has a reasonable 
18 expectation would affect the credential holder's employment or contracted service 
19 because the alleged conduct involves alcohol, illegal drugs, physical abuse, emotional 
20 abuse, inappropriate contact between a credential holder and a student, stalking or similar 
21 behavior that endangers the health, safety or welfare of a student. It removes the phrase 
22 "violating boundaries" from the definition. 
23 2. It provides that a credential holder who is the subject of a final report produced in 
24 support of a school entity's decision to discipline, suspend or terminate the credential 
25 holder may submit to the Department of Education a written rebuttal to the report and that 
26 the written rebuttal must be placed in the department's investigative file. 
27 3. It requires the department to destroy copies of all records and reports related to a 
28 finding resulting in discipline, suspension or termination of a credential holder if the 
29 finding resulting in that discipline, suspension or termination is reversed upon appeal at 
30 the school entity level. 
31 4. It directs the department to convene a work group to study and report to the Joint 
32 Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs no later than February 1, 2020 on 
33 recommendations and suggested legislation to improve the law regarding investigations 
34 into credential holders.
