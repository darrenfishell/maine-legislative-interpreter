26 SUMMARY 
27 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, replaces the bill. 
28 This amendment provides that, beginning January 1, 2020, a person licensed as a dentist 
29 or dental hygienist with dental hygienist therapy authority may place a mercury amalgam 
30 filling in a tooth as part of a procedure that is covered by MaineCare only if the patient or 
31 the patient's parent or legal guardian signs a consent form. The signed form must be 
32 submitted with the request for reimbursement under MaineCare and a copy of the signed 
33 form must be retained in the patient's records for a period of at least 3 years for review 
34 during MaineCare recertification. This amendment also includes an appropriations and 
35 allocations section.
