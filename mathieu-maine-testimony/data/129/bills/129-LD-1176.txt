19§1101-B.  Ancient burying grounds
201.  Access to ancient burying grounds.  The owner of an ancient burying ground or 
21a person who owns a parcel of land that contains an ancient burying ground shall provide 
22a municipality or its the caretaker designated pursuant to section 1101 access necessary to 
23perform the duties pursuant to section 1101 and Title 30-A, section 2901.  A municipality 
24or the caretaker designated pursuant to section 1101 may access an ancient burying 
25ground surrounded by privately owned land annually or as often as determined necessary 
26by the municipality or the designated caretaker to provide care and maintenance of the 
27headstones, monuments and markers so as to protect and preserve the ancient burying 
28ground.  Any unreasonable denial to provide of access may result in the owner being held 
29responsible for any fines, court costs and attorney's fees incurred by municipalities in 
30legally obtaining access or for failing to meet the requirements of section 1101.
312.  Maintenance by landowner.  A person who owns a parcel of land that contains 
32an ancient burying ground and chooses to deny access to the municipality or its the 
33caretaker designated pursuant to section 1101 shall assume the duties as described in 
34section 1101 and Title 30-A, section 2901, subsection 1.  Maintenance of an ancient 
35burying ground by the owner or a person who owns a parcel of land that contains an 
36ancient burying ground exempts the municipality from performing the duties as described 
37in section 1101.

40ancient burying ground within the municipality in order to determine if the ancient 
41burying ground is being maintained in good condition and repair.  If an ancient burying

9SUMMARY
10This bill allows a descendant of an individual or a veteran buried in an ancient or 
11public burying ground to appoint a caretaker to maintain the burying ground when the 
12municipality is unable to care for the burying ground or to appoint a caretaker.
