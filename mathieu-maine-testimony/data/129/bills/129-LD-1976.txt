414.  Temporary unemployment; work search.  Notwithstanding any other 
5provisions of this chapter to the contrary, any otherwise eligible individual who is 
6temporarily laid off by an employer that has given that individual a definite recall date 
7may not be denied benefits for any week based on the individual's failure to meet the 
8requirements of subsection 2 or 3 for a period of 6 weeks during that temporary layoff, so 
9long as the individual remains in contact with and able and available to work for that 
10employer.
11This subsection applies to a person employed as a school bus driver for a public or private 
12educational institution whose employer has set a definite recall date of not more than 12 
13weeks from the date of the temporary layoff.
14An individual may not receive more than 6 weeks of benefits in a benefit year pursuant to 
15this subsection unless approved by the Department of Labor.
16SUMMARY
17This bill exempts a school bus driver who has been temporarily laid off from work 
18search requirements in the unemployment compensation laws for up to 6 weeks if the 
19driver's employer sets a definite recall date of not more than 12 weeks from the date of 
20the temporary layoff.
