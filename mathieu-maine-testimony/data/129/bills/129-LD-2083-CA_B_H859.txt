18SUMMARY
19This amendment strikes and replaces the bill.  The amendment directs the Department 
20of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, Board of Pesticides Control to establish an 
21environmental risk advisory committee to evaluate the environmental risks associated 
22with the use of neonicotinoids, particularly the impact the use of neonicotinoids has on 
23pollinator species, including, but not limited to, native Maine bee species and other insect 
24pollinators and bird species.  The advisory committee is required to submit an interim 
25report by March 2021 and a final report by February 2022 with findings and 
26recommendations to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction 
27over agricultural matters.  The joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
28jurisdiction over agricultural matters has the authority to submit a bill relating to the 
29subject matter of the report during the Second Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
30The amendment also provides funding necessary to carry out the requirements of this 
31legislation.

Fiscal Detail and NotesFiscal Note
This bill directs the Board of Pesticides Control (BPC),  within the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and 
Forestry, to establish an environmental risk advisory comm ittee to assess the environmental risks associated with the 
use of neonicotinoids and complete a final report of their fi ndings by February 1, 2022.  The BPC has indicated that it 
will require additional staffing to complete these tasks.   The bill includes additional allocations of $103,540 to the 
BPC to allow expenditure of existing resources for one toxi cologist, contracted staffing, environmental sampling and 
literature review.  The impact of these new unfunded costs on other programs in the BPC cannot be determined at this
