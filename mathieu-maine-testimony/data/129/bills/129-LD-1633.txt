2This bill establishes a framework for the regulation of historical instant racing and 
3keno.  The bill creates a single license for the operation of historical instant racing and 
4keno.  Casinos, commercial tracks, off-track betting facilities and high-stakes beano 
5facilities are each eligible to apply for the license, issued by the Gambling Control Board.
6The bill directs the board to adopt rules governing the conduct of historical instant 
7racing and keno, including rules related to the prevention of fraud or deception, 
8authorized wager amounts, the maximum percentage of all wagers that may be retained 
9by the licensee and methods for verifying that a person who makes a historical instant 
10racing wager or who plays keno is at least 21 years of age.  The bill further requires that 
111% of the net income from historical instant racing and keno be used for administrative 
12expenses of the board and that 10% of the net revenue be distributed to the Treasurer of 
13the State to be credited to the Department of Education for essential programs and 
14services for kindergarten to grade 12.
