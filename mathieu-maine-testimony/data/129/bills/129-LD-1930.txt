7SUMMARY
8This bill amends the aquaculture leasing and licensing statutes to:
91.  Provide that the Department of Environmental Protection receives notices only of 
10those lease applications that involve activities that have a discharge;
112.  Expand the reasons under which the Commissioner of Marine Resources may 
12initiate lease revocation proceedings to include operating in a manner substantially 
13injurious to public health or violating minimum lease standards;
143.  Reduce the number of days in advance of which an individual must apply for the 
15renewal of a lease from 90 days prior to the expiration to 30 days prior to the expiration;
164.  Clarify notice requirements when a standard lease is proposed for renewal;
175.  Require the fee for a lease transfer to be paid upon application for the transfer 
18instead of at the execution of the lease;
196.  Specify that a person may not apply for an expansion of a lease until the person 
20has held that lease for a minimum of 2 years;
217.  Move the responsibility for notifying riparian landowners of an application for a 
22lease expansion from the applicant to the Department of Marine Resources and move the 
23responsibility for providing public notice in the newspaper from the department to the 
24applicant;
258.  Establish the rule-making authority for the commissioner to establish fees for 
26services provided by the department to lease holders if they request testing or studies to 
27ensure their products are safe for human consumption;
289.  Broaden the language allowing changes to leases and require the commissioner to 
29establish a fee for making changes to a lease;
3010.  Remove the commissioner's rule-making authority regarding changes to limited-
31purpose leases;
3211.  Limit the ability of the holder of a limited-purpose aquaculture license to have 
33unlicensed individuals participate in the licensed activities by requiring their direct 
34supervision by the license holder; and
Page 6 - 129LR3015(01)-1112.  Raise the fee for a limited-purpose aquaculture license from $50 to $100 for a 
2resident and from $300 to $400 for a nonresident.
