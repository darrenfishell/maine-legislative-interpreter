11developmental needs of children who enter into the child protection system, especially 
12the developmental needs of children in foster care.

302.  Core components.  The core components of the safe children court team 
31programs established under this chapter include the following:
32A.  Judicial leadership in focusing on achieving timely, permanent resolution of child 
33placement issues and resolving the issues that brought families into the system;
34B.  A development specialist with education and expertise in developmental 
35psychology;

2community responds to the needs of children who are subject to abuse or neglect;
3D.  Comprehensive services offered to each child, including screening for 
4developmental delays and disabilities, medical care and mental health services that 
5focus on the parent-child relationship;
6E.  Services addressing the needs of parents and the wide variety of traumatic 
7stressors in parents' lives;
8F.  Concurrent planning that places equal emphasis on supporting a 2nd permanent 
9family if reunification is ruled out;
10G.  Training and support for the foster parents, mentors and extended family prior to 
11and while foster parents are engaged with a child and the child's family;
12H.  Preremoval conferences and monthly family team meetings, the purpose of which 
13is to resolve issues that may lead to removal of a child from home;
14I.  Frequent family time to permit a child and parent to maintain their bond and to 
15improve the parent's responsiveness to the child's needs;
16J.  A continuum of mental health services to provide intervention applicable to the 
17specific characteristics of the parent and child as well as the level of preexisting 
18trauma in their relationship and in the parent's own childhood experiences.  
19Recommended interventions include:
20(1)  An assessment of the parent-child relationship;

28SUMMARY
29This bill authorizes the Judicial Department to establish safe children court team 
30programs, modeled on the "Safe Babies Court Team" approach supported by the Quality 
31Improvement Center for Research-based Infant-Toddler Court Teams funded by the 
32United States Department of Health and Human Services, Administration for Children 
33and Families, Children's Bureau.  The purpose is to develop a community-based system 
34to provide local systems and services for children and families that interact with the 
35State's child protection system.  The core components of such programs are listed.
