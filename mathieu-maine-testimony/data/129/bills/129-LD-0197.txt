17SUMMARY
18This bill imposes a 2-year moratorium, beginning November 1, 2019, on new 
19contracts or agreements by a consumer-owned water utility, municipality, state agency or 
20other governmental entity involving the extraction of more than 75,000 gallons of 
21groundwater during any week or more than 50,000 gallons of groundwater on any day.  
22The bill also directs the Commissioner of Environmental Protection to convene a working 
23group to develop the statutory and regulatory framework for the establishment of the 
24Maine Water Trust, which must be designed to ensure a safe and plentiful drinking water 
25supply for all residents of the State by regulating the use of groundwater for commercial 
26purposes under laws that establish the absolute control and dominion of the State over all 
27groundwater supplies in the State.  The commissioner must, on or before January 15, 
282021, report the recommendations of the working group to the joint standing committee 
29of the Legislature having jurisdiction over environment and natural resources matters, 
30which may report out a bill to implement those recommendations to the First Regular 
31Session of the 130th Legislature.
