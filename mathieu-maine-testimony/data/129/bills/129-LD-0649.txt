44-A.  Landowner relations program.  The commissioner shall develop and 
5implement a landowner relations program, referred to in this section subsection as "the 
6program."
7A.  The program must:
8(1)  Encourage landowners to allow outdoor recreationists access to their 
9property to hunt, fish or engage in other outdoor recreational pursuits;
10(2)  Foster good relationships between landowners and outdoor recreationists; 
11and
12(3)  Promote high standards of courtesy, respect and responsibility by outdoor 
13recreationists in their relations with landowners.
14B.  The commissioner shall appoint a landowner relations coordinator to oversee the 
15program and any other landowner relations activities of the department.
16C.  To the extent resources allow, the program must include the following elements:
17(1)  Building and maintaining an educated and motivated group of outdoor 
18recreationists who meet and promote high standards of courtesy, respect and 
19responsibility in their relations with landowners and who are willing to volunteer 
20in program-related projects or efforts to improve landowner relations;
21(2)  Issuing a certificate to persons wishing to support or participate in the 
22program. The commissioner may establish a fee for the certificate, which may not 
23exceed $20.  All proceeds from the fees must be deposited in the Landowner 
24Relations Fund established in section 10265;
25(3)  Developing and disseminating to outdoor recreationists a code of ethics or 
26other information promoting high standards of courtesy, respect and 
27responsibility in their relations with landowners;
28(4)  Developing an outreach program that provides educational materials and 
29signs and that disseminates information to landowners and land users about 
30landowner rights, landowner liability protections, the tradition of allowing 
31outdoor recreationists to use private land and law enforcement resources 
32available to landowners; 
33(5)  Engaging organizations and companies representing landowners, 
34conservation groups, recreationists, land trusts and other organizations involved 
35in outdoor recreation in developing, implementing and publicizing the program; 
36(6)  Organizing or otherwise promoting landowner appreciation events;
37(7)  Working with representatives of various state agencies to promote and 
38broaden public access to private lands for recreational use and to enhance 
39enforcement of applicable laws; and

2D.  All state agencies and in particular the Department of Agriculture, Conservation 
3and Forestry, the Department of Marine Resources and the Department of Economic 
4and Community Development shall collaborate with the department to implement the 
5program.  The department shall organize the collaboration to distribute as evenly as 
6practicable the burden experienced by the various agencies arising from the public 
7use of private lands.
8SUMMARY
9This bill directs all state agencies and in particular the Department of Agriculture, 
10Conservation and Forestry, the Department of Marine Resources and the Department of 
11Economic and Community Development to collaborate with the Department of Inland 
12Fisheries and Wildlife to implement the landowner relations program.  This bill directs 
13the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife to organize the collaboration to distribute 
14as evenly as practicable the burden experienced by the various agencies arising from the 
15public use of private lands.
