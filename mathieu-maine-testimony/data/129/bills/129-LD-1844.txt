Sec. 1.  Public Utilities Commission evaluation. Resolved:  That the Public 21 
Utilities Commission shall evaluate the proposal for creation of the Maine Power 22 
Delivery Authority as presented in L.D. 1646, An Act To Restore Local Ownership and 23 
Control of Maine's Power Delivery Systems, referred to in this resolve as "the proposal."  24 
The evaluation must include but is not limited to:  25 
1.  Analysis of the costs and benefits, both short -term and long -term, of the proposal;  26 
2.  Examination of legal, regulatory, technical, financial and operational issues related 27 
to the proposal and its imple mentation;  28 
3.  Assessment of anticipated impacts, both positive and negative, of the proposal on 29 
the State, including but not limited to impacts on electricity rates, utility employees and 30 
ratepayers; and  31 
4.  Development of alternatives or amendments to th e proposal to address any 32 
identified obstacles to its implementation.  33 
The commission shall contract with an independent consultant or consultants with 34 
relevant expertise to assist the commission with the evaluation.  For the purposes of this 35 
section, "rel evant expertise" includes, but is not limited to, expertise in utility valuation 36 
and finance and other legal, regulatory, technical, financial and operational issues related 37 
to the proposal.  The commission may seek public input and may consult with the Of fice 38

Fiscal Detail and Notes
This resolve requires that the Public Utilities Commission evaluate and report back on the proposal to create the 
Maine Power Delivery Authority as presented in LD 1646, An Act to Restore Local Ownership and Control of Maine's Power Delivery System. This resolve includes a one-time allocation of $500,000 to the PUC Reimbursement Fund to allow expenditures related to the cost of the evaluation.  The PUC Reimbursement Fund has sufficient cash available to fund the additional allocation. 129th MAINE LEGISLATURE
