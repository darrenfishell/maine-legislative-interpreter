4§1051.  Legal holidays
5Court may not be held on Sunday or any day designated for the annual Thanksgiving; 
6New Year's Day, January 1st; Martin Luther King, Jr., Day, the 3rd Monday in January; 
7Washington's Birthday, the 3rd Monday in February; Patriot's Day, the 3rd Monday in

10Labor Day, the first Monday of September; First Responders Day, September 11th; 
11Indigenous Peoples Day, the 2nd Monday in October; Veterans Day, November 11th; or 
12on Christmas Day.  The Chief Justice of the Supreme Judicial Court may order that court 
13be held on a legal holiday when the interests of justice and judicial economy in any 
14particular case will be served.  The public offices in county buildings may be closed to 
15business on the holidays named in this section.  When any one of the holidays named in 
16this section falls on Sunday, the Monday following must be observed as a holiday, with 
17all the privileges applying to any of the days named in this section.
18Sec. 2.  5 MRSA §59 is enacted to read:
19§59.  First Responders Day established as state holiday
20In recognition of the service and sacrifice of law enforcement officers, firefighters 
21and emergency medical technicians; active members of the United States Armed Forces; 
22and those veterans of the United States Armed Forces who served during the military 
23campaigns in Afghanistan and Iraq and in recognition of those who were victims of the 
24September 11, 2001 terrorist attacks, the State designates September 11th of each year as 
25First Responders Day and as a state holiday.  All nonessential state offices must be closed 
26on First Responders Day.

31SUMMARY
32This bill establishes September 11th of each year as First Responders Day and as a
