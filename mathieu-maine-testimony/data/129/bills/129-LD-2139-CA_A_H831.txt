171.  Refusal to confirm existence or nonexistence.  A Maine criminal justice agency 
18 may refuse to confirm the existence or nonexistence of intelligence and investigative record 
19 information, whether or not the record exists or does not exist, only if there is a reasonable 
20 possibility that the fact of the record's existence or nonexistence would:
21 A.  Interfere with law enforcement proceedings related to crimes;
22 B.  Endanger the life or physical safety of an individual, including, but not limited to, 
23 a victim of domestic violence, a victim of abuse, a confidential source or law 
24 enforcement personnel;
25 C.  Constitute an unwarranted invasion of personal privacy of one or more individuals; 
26 or
27 D.  Disclose specific investigatory techniques that are not known to the general public 
28 and that, if disclosed, would cause the use of those techniques to be compromised.  For 
29 the purposes of this paragraph "specific investigatory techniques" does not include 
30 types of technology that might be used for investigative purposes.
312.  Appeal.  A person aggrieved by the decision of a Maine criminal justice agency 
32 under subsection 1 to refuse to confirm the existence or nonexistence of intelligence and 
33 investigative record information may, within 30 calendar days of the receipt of written 
34 notice from the agency, appeal that decision to the Superior Court for the county where the

23SUMMARY
24 This amendment is the majority report of the committee.  The amendment enacts 
25 authority for a Maine criminal justice agency to refuse to confirm the existence or 
26 nonexistence of confidential intelligence and investigative record information in specific 
27 circumstances.  The amendment enacts an appeal process for a person aggrieved by a 
28 decision of an agency not to confirm existence or nonexistence.
