5ยง850-A.  Maine Paid Family Leave Insurance Program
61.  Definitions.  As used in this subchapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, 
7the following terms have the following meanings.
8A.  "Employee" has the same meaning as in section 843, subsection 1.
9B.  "Employer" has the same meaning as in section 843, subsection 3.
10C.  "Family medical leave" has the same meaning as in section 843, subsection 4.
11D.  "Program" means the Maine Paid Family Leave Insurance Program created in this 
12subchapter.
132.  Program created.  The Maine Paid Family Leave Insurance Program is created 
14within the Department of Labor.  The program provides wage-replacement benefits to 
15eligible persons who are on family medical leave from employment.
163.  Eligibility.  To be eligible for benefits under the program, a person must be 
17employed and must have had earnings from employment and made contributions to the 
18program in at least 12 of the prior 52 weeks.  The person must file a written notice with 
19the person's employer at least 30 days prior to commencement of the family medical 
20leave.  Failure by the person to file the written notice may result in delay or reduction in 
21the benefits, except in the event the time of the leave is unforeseeable or changes due to 
22unforeseeable circumstances.
234.  Benefits provided.  An eligible person under this subchapter receives a weekly 
24benefit of 2/3 of that person's average weekly wage in the 2 quarters in the preceding 52 
25weeks in which the person's wage was highest, except that the maximum amount of the 
26weekly benefit is 100% of the state average weekly wage.  Benefits are provided for up to 
276 weeks in any 12-month period.  Benefits must be reduced by any available workers' 
28compensation benefits and sick pay. Benefits may not be reduced by other disability 
29benefits, pension payments or other earnings.  The waiting period before benefits begin is 
307 days, except that a benefit payment for the first 7 days must be made retroactively if the 
31family medical leave lasts 3 weeks or more.
325.  Fund created.  The Paid Family Leave Insurance Fund is created to collect 
33contributions and fund benefits under this subchapter.  The fund may be used to pay 
34administrative costs of the program and to pay benefits.  The fund does not lapse but must 
35be carried forward to implement this subchapter.
Page 2 - 129LR1851(01)-116.  Contributions.  The Department of Labor shall determine the actuarially 
2appropriate total contribution needed each year to fund benefits under this section.  The 
3department shall collect the entire required amount from employees.  The aggregate 
4employee contribution must be collected from individual employees on a sliding scale 
5based on wages, as determined by the department.
67.  Restoration of employment.  A person who exercises that person's right to 
7benefits under this subchapter is, upon the expiration of those benefits, entitled to be 
8restored by the person's employer to the position held by the person when the family 
9medical leave commenced, or to a position with equivalent seniority, status, employment 
10benefits, pay and other terms and conditions of employment including any other benefits 
11and service credits that the person had been entitled to at the commencement of leave.
128.  Maintenance of employee health benefits.  During a family medical leave taken 
13pursuant to this subchapter, the employer shall maintain any existing health benefits of 
14the employee in effect for the duration of the leave as if the employee had continued 
15employment from the date the employee commenced the leave until the date the leave 
16terminates as long as the employee continues to pay any employee shares of the costs of 
17health benefits as required prior to the commencement of the leave.
189.  Concurrent leave.  Benefits provided under this subchapter must be provided in 
19accordance with the federal Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993, 29 United States 
20Code, Section 2601 et seq. and the requirements for family medical leave under state law 
21in subchapter 6-A.  An employer may require an employee who is entitled to leave under 
22federal or state law who takes family medical leave pursuant to this subchapter to take 
23that leave concurrently with any leave taken pursuant to the federal Family and Medical 
24Leave Act of 1993 or to subchapter 6-A.
2510.  Rules.  The Department of Labor shall adopt rules necessary to administer the 
26program and determine the method of collecting and depositing contributions in the fund 
27created in subsection 5.  Rules adopted under this subsection are routine technical rules as 
28defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
29SUMMARY
30This bill creates the Maine Paid Family Leave Insurance Program to provide wage-
31replacement benefits to persons who qualify for family medical leave.  The program is 
32funded by employee contributions and provides 2/3 of a person's average weekly wage or 
33100% of the state average weekly wage, whichever is lower, for up to 6 weeks in any 12-
34month period.  Employee contributions are collected on a sliding scale based on wages.
