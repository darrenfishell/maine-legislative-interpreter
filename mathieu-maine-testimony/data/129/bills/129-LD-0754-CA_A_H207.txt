This bill would establish a limit on energy efficiency spending from the Regional Greenhouse Gas Initiative Trust 
Fund (RGGI), within the Efficiency Maine Trust (EMT), of $5 per carbon dioxide allowance and would the transfer 
revenue in excess of $5 per carbon dioxide allowance from the RGGI to the Public Utilities Commission (PUC) to be 
used for disbursements to affected customers, energy-intensive businesses and ratepayers. EMT estimates that the 
excess revenue from the sale of carbon dioxide allowances will generate $1,767,282 in fiscal year 2019-20 and 
$1, 703,266 in fiscal year 2020-21. The bill includes an Other Special Revenue Funds allocation of $3,470,548 in 
fiscal year 2020-21 to the PUC to allow the expenditure of the money from EMT for disbursements to customers, 
business and ratepayers.
