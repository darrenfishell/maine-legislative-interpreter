COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A" to H.P. 486, L.D. 665, Bill, "An Act To Enact 
the Campus Free Expression Act" 
Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
'Resolve, Directing the Office of the Attorney General To Review Free Speech on 
Public College and University Campuses' 
Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following: 
'Sec. 1. Office of the Attorney General to review free speech on public 
college and university campuses. Resolved: That the Office of the Attorney 
General shall review whether there have been any instances in the State in which the 
University of Maine System, Maine Community College System or Maine Maritime 
Academy has violated or otherwise restricted a student's free speech rights protected 
under the United States Constitution, Amendment I and the Constitution of Maine, 
Article I, review whether additional protections are necessary under state law and report 
to the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary no later than January 1, 2020. The Joint 
Standing Committee on Judiciary may report out a bill relating to the report to the Second 
Regular Session of the 129th Legislature. 
Sec. 2. Appropriations and allocations. Resolved: That the following 
appropriations and allocations are made. 
ATTORNEY GENERAL, DEPARTMENT OF THE 
Administration -Attorney General 0310 
Initiative: Establishes one limited-period Assistant Attorney General position through 
January 31, 2020 to support the office in reviewing free speech considerations.

This bill requires the Office of the Attorney General (OAG) to investigate whether there have been any instances in 
the state where the University of Maine System, the Maine Community College System or the Maine Maritime 
Academy has violated or restricted a student's free speech rights. The bill also requires the OAG to detennine if any 
additional protections are necessaiy under state law and report to the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary by 
January 1, 2020. The OAG has indicated it requires an additional position to meet these requirements. The bill 
includes one limited-period Assistant Attorney General position and provides General Fund appropriations of 
$43,100 in fiscal year 2019-20.
