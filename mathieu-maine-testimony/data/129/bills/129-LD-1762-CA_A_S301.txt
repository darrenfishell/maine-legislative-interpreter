COMMITTEE AMENDMENT" A "to S.P. 594, L.D. 1762, "Resolve, Authorizing 
the Transfer of a Plot of Land from the State to the Town of Allagash" 
Amend the resolve by striking out all of section 1 and inserting the following: 
'Sec. 1. Director of Bureau of Parks and Lands to convey land. Resolved: 
That the Director of the Bureau of Parks and Lands within the Department of Agriculture, 
Conservation and Forestry shall offer to convey by quitclaim deed with covenant, at the 
cost of $1,500, to the Town of Allagash, County of Aroostook for the establishment of a 
fueling facility for the local logging industry and off-road recreational vehicles, 
including, but not limited to, snowmobiles and all-terrain vehicles, the parcel of land 
situated on Route 161, otherwise known as Allagash Road, and Old Route 161 in the 
Town of Allagash and described in the records of the bureau as the Allagash Southeast 
project, Original Reservation parcel and having identification number 86-2298. The use 
of the property is restricted to the operation of a fueling facility and if at any time the 
property following conveyance is no longer used for the purpose as provided under this 
section, the title reverts to the State of Maine without cost and, upon request of the State 
of Maine, the site must be restored to its condition at the time of conveyance by the 
owners at their own cost. If the Town of Allagash does not establish a fueling facility as 
described by September 1, 2020, the title reverts to the State of Maine without cost and, 
upon request of the State of Maine, the site must be restored to its condition at the time of 
conveyance by the owners at their own cost.

8 SUMMARY 
9 This amendment, which is the majority report, requires the Director of the Bureau of 
10 Parks and Lands within the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to 
11 offer to convey to the Town of Allagash a parcel of land situated on Route 161 and Old 
12 Route 161 in the Town of Allagash for the establishment of a fueling facility for the local 
13 logging industry and off-road recreational vehicles, including, but not limited to, 
14 snowmobiles and all-terrain vehicles. The amendment also stipulates that the use of the 
15 property is restricted to the operation of a fueling facility and if the property is no longer 
16 used as a fueling facility, title to the property reverts to the State of Maine without cost. 
17 The amendment also adds an appropriations and allocations section.

This resolve directs the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to offer to convey a parcel ofland to 
the Town of Allagash for $1,500. The parcel ofland conveyed must be used to establish a fueling facility by 
September 1, 2020 for the local logging industry and off-road recreational vehicles or title will revert back to the 
State. Title will also revert back to the State if the Town of Allagash no longer uses the property for the operation of 
a fueling facility. The bill includes an Other Special Revenue Funds allocation to the DACF of $1,500 to allow 
expenditure of the revenue.
