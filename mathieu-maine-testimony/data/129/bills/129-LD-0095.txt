16SUMMARY
17This bill clarifies that the primary residence of a candidate for election as a State 
18Senator or State Representative must be located in the district the candidate seeks to 
19represent on the date of the candidate's nomination for placement on a primary, general or 
20special election ballot.  The bill also clarifies that the primary residence of a candidate for 
21election as a State Senator or State Representative must be located in the district the 
22candidate seeks to represent for the 3 months immediately preceding the general election 
23and, if the candidate is elected, throughout the candidate's term of office.
