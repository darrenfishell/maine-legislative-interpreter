COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "4,, to H.P. 1138, L.D. 1576, Bill, "An Act To 
Improve the Department of Corrections' Response to Emergency Situations by Including 
Vehicles Operated by Certain Department Employees in the Definition of Authorized 
Emergency Vehicles" 
Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
'An Act To Improve the Department of Corrections' Response to Emergency 
Situations by Amending Provisions Related to Vehicles Operated by Certain 
Department Employees' 
Amend the bill in section 1 in paragraph B by striking out all of subparagraph (6) 
(page 1, lines 13 to 17 in L.D.) and inserting the following: 
1(6) A Department of Corrections vehicle used for responding to the escape of or 
perfo1ming the high-security transfer of a prisoner, juvenile client or juvenile 
detainee or a Department of Corrections vehicle operated by a person who is 
certified by the Board of Trnstees of the Maine Criminal Justice Academy as a 
law enforcement officer;' 
Amend the bill by adding after section 1 the following: 
'Sec. 2. 29-A MRSA §2054, sub-§5, 1C, as amended by PL 2001, c. 360, §7, is 
further amended to read: 
C. Exceed the maximum speed limits as long as life or property is not endangered, 
except that .!! capital security officers officer and employees of the a person operating 
.!! Department of Corrections vehicle who is not certified as a law enforcement officer 
by the Board of Trnstees of the Maine Critninal Justice Academy may not exercise 
this privilege;' 
Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or 
section number to read consecutively.

1 SUMMARY 
2 This amendment expands the definition of an authorized emergency vehicle to 
3 include a Department of Corrections vehicle operated by a person certified by the Board 
4 of Trustees of the Maine Criminal Justice Academy as a law enforcement officer and 
5 allows a person certified by the Board of Trustees of the Maine Criminal Justice 
6 Academy as a law enforcement officer who is operating a Department of Corrections 
7 vehicle to exceed the maximum speed limits as long as life or property is not e.ndangered.
