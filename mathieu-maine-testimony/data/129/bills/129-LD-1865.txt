41.  Protection order; consent agreement.  The court, after a hearing and upon 
5finding that the defendant has committed the alleged abuse as defined in section 4002, 
6subsection 1 or engaged in the alleged conduct described in section 4005, subsection 1, 
7may grant a protective order or, upon making that finding, approve a consent agreement 
8to bring about a cessation of abuse or the alleged conduct.  This subsection does not 
9preclude the parties from voluntarily requesting a consent agreement without a finding of 
10abuse or a finding that the alleged conduct occurred.  The court may enter a finding that 
11the defendant represents a credible threat to the physical safety of the plaintiff or a minor 
12child residing in the plaintiff's household.  The court may enter a finding of economic 
13abuse.  Relief granted under this section may include:
14A.  Directing the defendant to refrain from threatening, assaulting, molesting, 
15harassing, attacking or otherwise abusing the plaintiff and any minor children 
16residing in the household;
17A-1.  Directing the defendant not to possess a firearm, muzzle-loading firearm, bow, 
18crossbow or other dangerous weapon for the duration of the order;
19A-2.  Prohibiting the defendant from the use, attempted use or threatened use of 
20physical force that would reasonably be expected to cause bodily injury against the 
21plaintiff or a minor child residing in the household;
22B.  Directing the defendant to refrain from going upon the premises of the plaintiff's 
23residence;
24C.  Directing the defendant to refrain from repeatedly and without reasonable cause:
25(1)  Following the plaintiff;

27employment; or

30the plaintiff;

25SUMMARY
26This bill amends the protection from abuse laws to provide that parties are not 
27precluded from voluntarily requesting a consent agreement without a finding by the court 
28that the alleged conduct described in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 19-A, section 
294005, subsection 1 occurred.
