7SUMMARY
8This resolve imposes a delay on the adoption by the Department of Health and 
9Human Services of any rule that proposes to change provisions of 10-144 C.M.R. Chapter 
10101: MaineCare Benefits Manual governing school-based services.
11It requires the department, before initiating rulemaking, to complete an impact study 
12setting forth the rationale for the proposed changes, the financial impacts on affected 
13families and providers of school-based services, potential service delays and other issues 
14relating to state and federal law.  The department is required to seek and consider input 
15from affected provider groups, schools, children and family advocacy groups, the 
16Department of Education, the Child Development Services System and other stakeholders 
17and is also required to incorporate into the study the recommendations and 
18implementation plan for early intervention services in public schools that will be 
19forthcoming from the review of the State's early childhood special education services 
20being carried out pursuant to Public Law 2019, chapter 343, Part VVVV. This report, 
21produced by an independent entity, is due to be completed on December 1, 2020.
22It requires the department to submit the completed study and a further report 
23regarding any proposed rules or modifications to current rule pertaining to medically 
24necessary school-based services to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
25jurisdiction over health and human services matters no later than April 1, 2021.  The joint 
26standing committee is authorized to report out legislation relating to the subject matter of 
27the study and the report to the First Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
28It authorizes the department, following completion of its study and report but no 
29earlier than July 1, 2021, to initiate rulemaking to change rules governing school-based 
30services provided under 10-144 C.M.R. Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual and 
31specifies that any rules adopted are major substantive rules pursuant to the Maine Revised 
32Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
