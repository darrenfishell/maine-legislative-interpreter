2Sec. 1.  7 MRSA §4155, sub-§3-A is enacted to read:
33-A.  Health problem; alternative remedies; dogs. As an alternative to the 
4remedies under subsection 3, the purchaser of a dog may select a remedy from a seller as 
5follows:
6A.  For a dog with less than one year of life expectancy, as determined by a 
7veterinarian pursuant to subsection 1, full reimbursement of the original purchase

12Return of the dog to the seller and reimbursement of veterinary fees by the seller are not 
13required under this subsection.
14Sec. 2.  7 MRSA §4155, sub-§5, as amended by PL 2007, c. 702, §27, is further 
15amended to read:
165.  Sellers not exempt.   Sellers may not, contractually or otherwise, exempt 
17themselves from the remedies provided by this section for deaths or health problems 
18caused by infectious, contagious, parasitic or communicable disease or for deaths or 
19health problems in dogs caused by hereditary or congenital defects as described in 
20subsection 1. 
21SUMMARY
22This bill provides alternative remedies for a purchaser of a dog that has a health 
23problem causing diminished life expectancy.  For a dog with a life expectancy of less 
24than one year as determined by a veterinarian, the purchaser may choose to receive a 
25refund of the full purchase price of the dog.  For a dog with a life expectancy of one to 5 
26years as determined by a veterinarian, the purchaser may choose to receive a refund of 
271/2 of the purchase price of the dog.  Return of the dog to the seller and reimbursement of 
28veterinary fees by the seller are not required.
