171.  Reimbursement.  A postsecondary educational institution in the State that 
18temporarily suspends classes and requires students to remain off campus due to safety 
19concerns caused by an infectious disease, including the novel coronavirus disease referred 
20to as COVID-19 by the World Health Organization and referred to in this section as 
21"COVID-19," shall reimburse a student on a pro rata basis any unused fees previously 
22paid to the institution by the student, including, but not limited to, the cost of room and 
23board and fees for parking, student activities, labs and technology and, if classes are not 
24conducted remotely or through an alternate method as determined appropriate by the 
25institution, tuition.
262.  Plan for certain students' housing and dining.  A postsecondary educational 
27institution in the State that temporarily suspends classes and requires students to remain 
28off campus due to safety concerns caused by an infectious disease, including COVID-19, 
29shall develop a plan to allow certain students, based on a student's individual 
30circumstances, to remain on campus and to provide housing and dining options to those 
31students.
323.  Plan for staff and faculty.  A postsecondary educational institution in the State 
33that temporarily suspends classes and requires staff and faculty to remain off campus due 
34to safety concerns caused by an infectious disease, including COVID-19, shall develop a 
35plan, in consultation with the department and the Department of Labor, to compensate 
36staff and faculty for lost compensation due to the temporary suspension.
37Sec. 2.  Retroactivity.  This Act applies retroactively to February 1, 2020.

3SUMMARY
4This bill requires a postsecondary educational institution in the State that temporarily 
5suspends classes and requires students, staff and faculty to remain off campus due to an 
6infectious disease, including COVID-19, to:
71.  Reimburse students on a pro rata basis any unused fees previously paid to the 
8institution by the student, and, if classes are not conducted remotely or through an 
9alternate method as determined appropriate by the institution, tuition;
102.  Develop a plan to allow certain students, based on a student's individual 
11circumstances, to remain on campus and to provide housing and dining options to those 
12students; and
133.  Develop a plan, in consultation with the Department of Labor and the Department 
14of Education, to compensate staff and faculty for lost compensation due to the temporary 
15suspension.
16This bill applies retroactively to February 1, 2020.
