9Sec. 2.  Care coordination services units.  Resolved:  That, by January 1, 
102020, the Department of Health and Human Services shall amend its rule Chapter 101: 
11MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter II, Section 18, Home and Community-Based 
12Services for Adults with Brain Injury, to allow for 400 units of care coordination in each 
13year rather than limiting it to 200 units after one year.  Rules adopted pursuant to this 
14section are routine technical rules pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 
15375, subchapter 2-A.
16Sec. 3.  Department of Health and Human Services to explore telehealth 
17opportunities.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services shall 
18explore opportunities to provide additional reimbursable services using telehealth to 
19members currently receiving services under the brain injury waiver administered by the 
20department's office of aging and disability services, including care coordination services, 
21that could be provided by health care providers, including licensed medical personnel and 
22nonlicensed personnel.
23SUMMARY
24This resolve directs the Department of Health and Human Services to increase the 
25rates for services provided to MaineCare members receiving Home Support (Residential 
26Habilitation) Level I under the brain injury waiver, rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits 
27Manual, Chapter III, Section 18, to no less than $8.63 per quarter hour.  It also allows up 
28to 400 units of care coordination each year rather than only in the first year of receiving 
29services under the waiver.  The Department of Health and Human Services is directed to 
30explore opportunities to provide additional telehealth services, including care 
31coordination services, provided by both licensed medical personnel and nonlicensed 
32personnel.
