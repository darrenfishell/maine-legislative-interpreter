Page 4 - 129LR2504(01)-11§24051.  Maine Children's Cabinet Early Childhood Advisory Council
21.  Definitions.  As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
3following terms have the following meanings.
4A.  "Cabinet" means the Children's Cabinet established in section 19131.
5B.  "Council" means the Maine Children's Cabinet Early Childhood Advisory 
6Council established in subsection 2.
7C.  "Young children" means children from birth until they complete grade 3.
82.  Establishment.  The Maine Children's Cabinet Early Childhood Advisory 
9Council, as established in section 12004-J, subsection 18, is created to develop, maintain 
10and evaluate under the direction of the cabinet a plan for sustainable social and financial 
11investment in healthy development of the State's young children and their families.
123.  Membership.  The council consists of the members listed in this subsection, who 
13must have a strong interest in early childhood and early care and education and must be 
14influential in their communities:
15A.  One member of the Senate, appointed by the President of the Senate;
16B.  One member of the House of Representatives, appointed by the Speaker of the 
17House;
18C.  The Governor or the Governor's designee;
19D.  One person who is the parent of a young child, appointed by the Governor;
20E.  Two persons with experience in public funding and philanthropy, appointed by 
21the Governor;
22F.  One person representing child abuse and neglect prevention, appointed by the 
23Governor;
24G.  One person representing postsecondary education, appointed by the Governor;
25H.  Three persons representing statewide, membership or constituent organizations 
26that advance the well-being of young children and their families, including early care 
27and education programs, child care centers, Head Start programs, family child care 
28providers, resource development centers, programs for school-age children, child 
29development services, physicians and child advocates, nominated by their 
30organizations and appointed by the cabinet;
31I.  One person representing a business roundtable on early childhood investment, 
32appointed by the Governor;
33J.   One person with expertise in children's health or public health, appointed by the 
34Governor;
35K.  Two ex officio nonvoting members who are employees of the Department of 
36Education and direct or work in programming that affects young children, appointed 
37by the Commissioner of Education; and
Page 5 - 129LR2504(01)-11L.  Two ex officio nonvoting members who are employees of the Department of 
2Health and Human Services and direct or work in programming that affects young 
3children, appointed by the Commissioner of Health and Human Services. 
44.  Terms of appointment.  Except for ex officio members, members of the council 
5are appointed for terms of 3 years.  Members who are Legislators are appointed for the 
6duration of the legislative terms in which they were appointed. Members who are not 
7Legislators may serve beyond their designated terms until their successors are appointed.
85.  Chair. The Governor shall appoint a chair for the council.
96.  Staffing; funding.  The council may hire staff as necessary for its work and as 
10resources permit.  The council may accept grant funding and other funding as may be 
11available for the work of the council from the Federal Government, any department, 
12office or political subdivision of the State or any individual, foundation or corporation.
13§24052.  Duties
141.  Duties.  The duties of the council include, but are not limited to:
15A.  Reviewing and addressing recommendations of the cabinet regarding young 
16children in the following areas:
17(1)  Early childhood policy implementation to ensure an effective public and 
18private service delivery system and reporting recommendations based upon the 
19review to the cabinet; and
20(2)  The delivery of early childhood services by the departments of the cabinet 
21and recommending improvements to increase efficiencies in the delivery of 
22services;
23B.  Advising the Department of Health and Human Services regarding:
24(1)  The coordination of child care services in the State; and
25(2)  The preparation of any application, amendment, waiver request, plan or other 
26document submitted by the department to the Federal Government regarding 
27child care funding or other early childhood services grants;
28C.  Reviewing and commenting on initiatives and plans of the cabinet related to early 
29childhood;
30D.  Submitting annually to the cabinet recommendations for changes to laws and 
31rules to reduce barriers to collaboration and coordination among federally funded and 
32state-funded programs and services for young children and the children's families and 
33to improve the quality of programs and services for young children statewide.  
34Recommendations must include, but are not limited to, methods to:
35(1)  Promote the availability of services and programs for young children and the 
36children's families across the State;
37(2)  Promote innovative programs for young children and the children's families 
38including funding models for early childhood and education programs and 
39services; and
Page 6 - 129LR2504(01)-11(3)  Enhance and improve quality and outcomes of early childhood and education 
2programs and services, including child development services, home visitation, 
3child care, education services, supervision services, health services and social 
4services; and
5E.  Developing and periodically updating a process for evaluating the council's 
6effectiveness in performing the council's duties, including advising the cabinet.

21SUMMARY
22This bill:
231.  Amends certain provisions governing the Children's Cabinet;
242.  Eliminates the Maine Children's Growth Council;
253.  Eliminates the Child Care Advisory Council; and
264.  Establishes the Maine Children's Cabinet Early Childhood Advisory Council to 
27develop and evaluate under the direction of the Children's Cabinet a plan for the healthy 
28development of the State's young children and their families.
