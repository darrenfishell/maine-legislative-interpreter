8SUMMARY
9This bill requires the Director of the Maine Bureau of Veterans' Services within the 
10Department of Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management to establish a process for 
11a veteran who separated from service without an honorable discharge due solely to the 
12veteran's sexual orientation, gender identity or gender expression or to statements, 
13consensual sexual conduct or consensual acts relating to sexual orientation, gender 
14identity or gender expression to have that discharge treated as an honorable discharge for 
15purposes of determining the veteran's eligibility for rights, privileges and benefits under 
16state law.
17The bill further requires the director to explore whether other states have established 
18processes for reviewing and potentially upgrading the discharge status, for state law 
19purposes, of veterans who have been diagnosed with post-traumatic stress disorder or 
20traumatic brain injury or who have been diagnosed with psychological trauma resulting 
21from sexual assault or sexual harassment during military service as described in 38 
22United States Code, Section 1720D and who separated from service without an honorable 
23discharge.  The director is required to report, no later than January 15, 2021, to the joint 
24standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over veterans affairs on both 
25the potential for implementing such a process in the State and the resources that would be 
26required to implement the process. The committee may report out legislation to the First 
27Regular Session of the 130th Legislature based on the director's report.
