Page 5 - 129LR1034(01)-11SUMMARY
2This bill does the following.
3Part A requires competitive electricity providers to demonstrate, by 2040, that their 
4portfolios of supply sources for retail electricity sales in this State are 80% accounted for 
5by renewable resources.  It also amends the State's goals for long-term reduction of 
6greenhouse gas emissions.
7Part B creates the Task Force for a Green New Deal, which consists of 11 members 
8including representatives of State Government, climate science, renewable energy, youth, 
9labor and business.  The task force is charged with creating a plan to advance 
10environmental sustainability, renewable energy and economic growth for the State.  The 
11plan must include, but is not limited to, a renewable resources strategy to achieve 80% 
12reliance on renewable resources for electricity supply by 2040; a job training strategy, 
13including a training program to prepare workers for green jobs; and a residential energy 
14strategy that provides incentives for installation of solar energy systems and heat pumps.  
15The task force is required to submit a report on its plan by January 15, 2020 to the 
16Governor, the Joint Standing Committee on Innovation, Development, Economic 
17Advancement and Business, the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and 
18Technology and the Joint Standing Committee on Environment and Natural Resources.
19Part C requires the Public Utilities Commission and the Efficiency Maine Trust to 
20submit a report by January 1, 2020 that includes draft legislation to establish a virtual net 
21metering program to encourage installation of solar photovoltaic energy systems on 
22public school buildings. 
23Part D creates the Commission on a Just Transition to a Low-carbon Economy.  The 
24commission includes 13 members.  The purpose of the commission is to ensure that the 
25State's transition to a low-carbon economy benefits all residents fairly and equitably.  The 
26commission is required to submit an annual report to the Legislature.
