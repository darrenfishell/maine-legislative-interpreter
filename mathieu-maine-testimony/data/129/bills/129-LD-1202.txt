Page 1 - 129LR1291(01)-11Preamble. Whereas, the United States Department of Health and Human Services, 
2Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services adopted home and community-based 
3settings rules in 2014 that the State must comply with by March 17, 2022; and
4Whereas, the rules are intended to ensure that individuals receiving long-term 
5services and supports through Medicaid home and community-based service programs 
6have full access to the benefits of community living and the opportunity to receive 
7services in the most integrated setting; and
8Whereas, the rules require the Department of Health and Human Services to 
9develop, with stakeholder input and Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services 
10approval, a transition plan to bring the State into full compliance; and
11Whereas, the State submitted proposed transition plans in 2014 and 2016 but has 
12not received approval from the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services; and
13Whereas, implementing the rules will be a complex process requiring significant 
14changes to how services are delivered in the State; now, therefore, be it
15Sec. 1.  Stakeholder group for working plan created.  Resolved:  That the 
16Department of Health and Human Services, referred to in this resolve as "the 
17department," shall convene a stakeholder group to develop a working plan to implement 
18the United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for Medicare and 
19Medicaid Services home and community-based settings rules in the State.  The 
20department shall provide staff support for the stakeholder group, including but not limited 
21to a facilitator and administrative support.
22Sec. 2.  Membership.  Resolved:  That the membership of the stakeholder group 
23under section 1 consists of the following:
241.  Two members of the Senate, appointed by the President of the Senate;
252.  Two members of the House of Representatives, appointed by the Speaker of the 
26House; and
273.  A member receiving services under each home and community-based waiver, 
28including department rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapters II and III, 
29Sections 18, 19, 20, 21 and 29.
30The department shall also invite the participation of at least one representative from 
31each of the following organizations:  Developmental Disabilities Partners for Positive 
32Change within Disability Rights Maine; the Maine Developmental Disabilities Council; 
33the University of Maine Center for Community Inclusion and Disability Studies; 
34Speaking Up For Us of Maine; the Acquired Brain Injury Advisory Council under the 
35Maine Revised Statutes, Title 34-B, section 19001; the Maine Long-Term Care 
36Ombudsman Program; and the Maine Association for Community Service Providers.
37Sec. 3.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the stakeholder group under section 1 shall 
38investigate and develop a plan for the department to fully comply with the United States 
Page 2 - 129LR1291(01)-11Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services 
2home and community-based settings rules and bring best practices to the State by:
31.  Reviewing information about home and community-based settings best practices 
4in other states;
52.  Reviewing the State's most recently submitted transition plan and making 
6recommendations on how to revise the plan for resubmission to the Centers for Medicare 
7and Medicaid Services; and
83.  Reviewing and making specific recommendations regarding:
9A.  Changes needed to the person-centered planning process;
10B.  Combining multiple target populations into one home and community-based 
11settings waiver;
12C.  System changes needed to ensure home and community-based services recipients 
13have community access to the same extent as others, including but not limited to 
14individuals' having leases, choice of providers, opportunities to seek employment, 
15opportunities to engage in community life and to control personal resources and 
16choice of roommates;
17D.  Identification of the support service providers who will need to come into full 
18compliance with the home and community-based settings rules; and
19E.  Quality assurance mechanisms and regulatory changes needed, and the fiscal 
20implications of those changes, to enable the implementation of the home and 
21community-based settings rules. 
22Sec. 4.  Report.  Resolved:  That the department shall submit a report on the 
23findings and recommendations of the stakeholder group under section 1, including any 
24recommended legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human 
25Services by January 15, 2020.  The committee may submit a bill related to the report to 
26the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
27SUMMARY
28This resolve directs the Department of Health and Human Services to convene a 
29stakeholder group to review the Medicaid home and community-based settings rules, 
30review past efforts by the State to come into compliance with those rules and make 
31recommendations about how to bring the State into compliance and recommendations on 
32necessary statutory and regulatory changes.  The department is required to submit a report 
33by January 15, 2020 to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services, 
34which may submit a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
