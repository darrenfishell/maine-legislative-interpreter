12Sec. 1.  34-B MRSA c. 16 is enacted to read:
13CHAPTER 16
14EARLY INTERVENTION FOR PSYCHOTIC DISORDERS
15ยง16001.  Definitions
16As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 
17have the following meanings.
181.  Coordinated specialty care.  "Coordinated specialty care" means an evidence-
19based recovery-oriented treatment model for individuals in the early years of a psychotic 
20disorder.  Coordinated specialty care promotes shared decision making with a team of 
21specialists to work with an individual to develop a personalized treatment plan.
222.  Psychotic disorder.  "Psychotic disorder" means a diagnosis of schizophrenia 
23spectrum disorder and other psychotic disorders or mood disorders with prominent 
24psychotic features.
25ยง16002.  Coordinated specialty care
26No later than January 1, 2020, the department, in cooperation with the Department of 
27Education and the Department of Labor, shall establish a funding mechanism to 
28reimburse for the treatment of individuals within the first 3 years of onset of a psychotic 
29disorder using the coordinated specialty care model.  Services provided as part of 
30coordinated specialty care include, but are not limited to, the following:
311.  Case management.  Case management to manage services to help the individual 
32develop problem solving skills and manage medication;
332.  Family support and education.  Family support to give the family of the 
34individual information and skills to support the individual receiving treatment and 
35recovery services;
Page 2 - 129LR0436(01)-113.  Psychotherapy.  Psychotherapy services that teach resiliency, managing the 
2psychotic disorder, promoting wellness and developing coping skills;
34.  Medication management.  Medication management to determine the most 
4effective medication and the lowest dosage to be effective;
55.  Supported education and employment.  Support services to help an individual 
6continue education or employment or return to education or employment; and
76.  Peer support.  Peer support services to connect the individual with others who 
8have similar experiences.
9ยง16003.  Evaluation
10The department shall ensure that an organization providing coordinated specialty care 
11provides program evaluation assessments to the department, including external ratings of 
12fidelity to the coordinated specialty care model and reports of clinical and functional 
13outcomes.
14ยง16004.  Rulemaking
15The department may adopt rules to accomplish the requirements of this chapter.  
16Rules adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
17chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
18Sec. 2.  Federal approval.  The Department of Health and Human Services shall 
19submit any necessary applications to the United States Department of Health and Human 
20Services, Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services for waiver or state plan 
21amendments to implement the coordinated specialty care services established under the 
22Maine Revised Statutes, Title 34-B, chapter 16.  The department shall maximize coverage 
23of services under the MaineCare program and private insurance.
24Sec. 3.  Community mental health services block grant.  The Department of 
25Health and Human Services shall seek federal funding from the United States Department 
26of Health and Human Services, Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services 
27Administration through the community mental health services block grant to cover 
28services provided pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 34-B, chapter 16 that are 
29not otherwise covered by the MaineCare program or private insurance.  The community 
30mental health services block grant funding may be used in any other manner allowable 
31under federal law to establish and support coordinated specialty care services.
32Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
33legislation takes effect when approved.
34SUMMARY
35This bill requires the Department of Health and Human Services to establish a 
36funding mechanism and reimbursement rate for the treatment of individuals showing 
37early signs of a psychotic disorder using a coordinated specialty care model.  Services

3the treatment of individuals in cooperation with the Department of Education and the

5apply to the United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for 
6Medicare and Medicaid Services for any necessary waivers and state plan amendments
