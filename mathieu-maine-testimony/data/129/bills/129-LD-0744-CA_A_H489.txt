13 following: 
14 1Sec. 1. Authority to convey state property. Resolved: That, 
15 notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the State, by and through the 
16 Commissioner of Administrative and Financial Services, referred to in this resolve as 11the 
17 commissioner, 11 may: 
18 1. Convey by sale any or a portion of the interests of the State in the state property 
19 described in section 2, referred to in this resolve as 11the state property,11 with the buildings 
20 and improvements, together with all appurtenant rights and easements, and all personal 
21 property located on that property, including vehicles, machinery, equipment and supplies. 
22 The State1s interest in the state property does not include timber rights; 
23 2. Negotiate, draft, execute and deliver any documents necessary to settle any 
24 boundary line discrepancies of the state property; 
25 3. Exercise, pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 23, chapter 3, subchapter 
26 3, the power of eminent domain to quiet for all time any possible challenges to ownership 
27 of the state property; 
28 4. Negotiate, draft, execute and deliver any easements or other rights that, in the 
29 commissioner1s discretion, may contribute to the value of a proposed sale of the State1s 
30 interests in the state property; and 
31 5. Release any interests in the state property that, in the commissioner1s discretion, 
32 do not contribute to the value of the remaining state property. 
33 Sec. 2. Property interests that may be conveyed. Resolved: That the state

13 Sec. 5. Appraisal. Resolved: That the commissioner shall have the current 
14 market value of the state property determined by a broker opinion of value and current 
15 comparative market analysis. The commissioner may list the state property for sale with 
16 a private real estate broker and negotiate any sale, solicit bids or sell directly to a 
17 purchaser. The commissioner may reject any offer. 
18 Sec. 6. Right of first refusal. Resolved: That the Town of East Millinocket has 
19 the right of first refusal to purchase the state property at the broker opinion of value 
20 determined pursuant to section 5. The commissioner shall provide written notice to the 
21 clerk of the Town of East Millinocket of the intent to sell the state property and the terms 
22 of sale. The Town of East Millinocket has 90 days to notify the commissioner in writing 
23 whether the Town of East Millinocket will purchase the state property at the broker 
24 opinion of value. The Town of East Millinocket has 60 days from the date of invoking its 
25 right of first refusal to complete the purchase of the state property. 
26 Sec. 7. Proceeds. Resolved: That any proceeds from the sale of the state 
27 property pursuant to this resolve must be deposited into the Department of Administrative 
28 and Financial Services, Bureau of General Services' capital repair and improvement 
29 account for capital improvements. 
30 Sec. 8. Repeal. Resolved: That this resolve is repealed 5 years from its effective 
31 date.' 
32 SUMMARY 
33 This amendment adds a right of first refusal to purchase the state property to the 
34 Town of East Millinocket. It clarifies that the State does not own the timber rights to the 
35 property described in the resolve. It removes the lease option and changes the form of 
36 several references.

Fiscal Detail and Notes 
This resolve authorizes the Commissioner of the Department of Administrative and Financial Services to sell a parcel 
of state property located in the Town of East Millinocket. Proceeds from the potential sale of this property will be 
credited to the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Bureau of General Services -Capital 
Construction and Improvement Reserve Fund. According to a preliminary assessment conducted in March of 2018, 
the parcel was valued between $225,000-$260,000.
