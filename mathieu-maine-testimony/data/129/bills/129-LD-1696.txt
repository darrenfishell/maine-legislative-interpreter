3Whereas, the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A requires

6Whereas, a major substantive rule has been submitted to the Legislature outside the 
7legislative rule acceptance period; and
8Whereas, immediate enactment of this resolve is necessary to record the 
9Legislature's position on final adoption of the rule; and
10Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
11the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
12immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
13therefore, be it
14Sec. 1.  Adoption.  Resolved:  That final adoption of portions of Chapter 101: 
15MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter III, Section 28: Allowances for Rehabilitative and 
16Community Support Services for Children with Cognitive Impairments and Functional 
17Limitations, a provisionally adopted major substantive rule of the Department of Health 
18and Human Services that has been submitted to the Legislature for review pursuant to the 
19Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A outside the legislative rule 
20acceptance period, is authorized.
21Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
22legislation takes effect when approved.
23SUMMARY
24This resolve provides for legislative review of portions of Chapter 101: MaineCare 
25Benefits Manual, Chapter III, Section 28: Allowances for Rehabilitative and Community 
26Support Services for Children with Cognitive Impairments and Functional Limitations, a 
27major substantive rule of the Department of Health and Human Services that was filed 
28outside the legislative rule acceptance period.
