9therefore, be it
10Sec. 1.  Definitions. Resolved:  That, as used in this resolve, unless the context 
11otherwise indicates, the following terms have the following meanings.
121.  "Commissioner" means the Commissioner of Administrative and Financial 
13Services.
142.  "State property" means the real estate described in section 3 with the buildings and 
15improvements, together with all appurtenant rights and easements, and all personal 
16property located on that property, including vehicles, machinery, equipment and supplies.
17Sec. 2.  Authority to convey state property.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding 
18any other law, the State, by and through the commissioner, may:
191.  Convey the interests of the State in the state property to the Town of South 
20Thomaston;
212.  Negotiate, draft, execute and deliver any documents necessary to settle any 
22boundary line discrepancies;
233.  Exercise, pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 23, chapter 3, the power of 
24eminent domain to quiet for all time any possible challenges to ownership of the state 
25property;
264.  Negotiate, draft, execute and deliver any easements or other rights that, in the 
27commissioner's discretion, may contribute to the value of a proposed conveyance of the 
28State's interests; and
295.  Release any interests in the state property that, in the commissioner's discretion, 
30do not contribute to the value of the remaining state property.
31Sec. 3.  Property interests that may be conveyed.  Resolved:  That the state 
32property authorized to be conveyed is a parcel of land described in a deed dated June 14, 
331954 from Amos Makinen of South Thomaston to the Maine School Building Authority, 
34recorded in Book 336, Page 135, Knox County Registry of Deeds.
35Sec. 4.  Property to be conveyed "as is."  Resolved:  That the commissioner 
36may convey the state property without consideration upon terms the commissioner 
Page 2 - 129LR0742(01)-11considers appropriate; however, the state property must be conveyed "as is," with no 
2representations or warranties.
3Title must be transferred by quitclaim deed without covenant and executed by the 
4commissioner.
5Sec. 5.  Exemptions.  Resolved:  That any conveyance pursuant to this resolve is 
6exempt from any statutory or regulatory requirement that the state property first be 
7offered to the Maine State Housing Authority or another state or local agency.
8Sec. 6.  Repeal.  Resolved:  That this resolve is repealed 5 years from its effective 
9date.
10Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
11legislation takes effect when approved.
12SUMMARY
13This resolve authorizes the State to transfer ownership of the Guilford Butler School 
14property in South Thomaston to the Town of South Thomaston.
