41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "EEO-1 report" means the federal Equal Employment Opportunity Commission's 
7Employer Information Report or a successor report.
8B.  "EEO-3 report" means the federal Equal Employment Opportunity Commission's

10C.  "EEO-4 report" means the federal Equal Employment Opportunity Commission's 
11State and Local Government Report or a successor report.
12D.  "EEO-5 report" means the federal Equal Employment Opportunity Commission's 
13Elementary-Secondary Staff Information Report or a successor report.
14E.  "EEO reports" means the EEO-1 report, EEO-3 report, EEO-4 report and EEO-5 
15report.
16F.  "Employees" means all employees for whom information is required to be 
17reported in EEO reports.
18G.  "Gender, race and ethnicity" means gender, race and ethnicity identifications 
19required to be reported in EEO reports.
20H.  "Job category" means the job categories required to be reported in EEO reports.
212.  Wage data report.  By March 31, 2020 and by March 31st each year thereafter, 
22an entity required to file an EEO-1 report, EEO-3 report, EEO-4 report or EEO-5 report 
23with the federal Equal Employment Opportunity Commission shall submit a wage data 
24report to the Maine Human Rights Commission, which must include for the entity:
25A.  The number of employees in each job category by gender, race and ethnicity;
26B.  The aggregate number of male employees and the aggregate number of female 
27employees;
28C.  The aggregate number of white, non-Hispanic employees and the aggregate 
29number of all other employees;
30D.  The median wage of employees in each job category by gender, race and 
31ethnicity;
32E.  The median wage of employees in the groups described in paragraphs B and C;
33F.  The percentage difference between the median wage of employees in each job 
34category between white male employees and employees in other gender, racial and 
35ethnic groups; and
36G.  The percentage difference between the median wage of employees in the groups 
37described in paragraphs B and C.
Page 2 - 129LR0942(01)-11Wage data included in the report must be based on a single pay period from October, 
2November or December immediately preceding the reporting deadline.  Wage data must 
3be reported on an hourly basis.  For salaried employees, hourly wages may be calculated 
4by dividing annual wages by 2,080.  For all other employees, hourly wages must be 
5calculated based on actual hours worked and wages received during the pay period.  For 
6the purposes of this subsection, wages include base pay, tips and incentive-based pay 
7such as commissions.  Wages do not include overtime pay.
83.  Availability of reports; redaction of certain data.  By June 30th of each year, 
9the commission shall post wage data reports filed that year pursuant to this section on the 
10commission's publicly accessible website, except that when the number of employees 
11within a gender, ethnic or racial group is less than 5, data in the wage data report for that 
12group must be redacted.
134.  Retention of reports.  The commission shall retain the original, or exact copies 
14of, wage data reports filed under this section for at least 10 years.
155.  Use of reports in employment discrimination claims.  The commission may use 
16wage data reports filed under this section in the commission's adjudication of 
17employment discrimination claims.
18SUMMARY
19This bill requires certain employers, including, but not limited to, state agencies, to 
20annually submit wage data reports regarding employee gender, race and ethnicity to the 
21Maine Human Rights Commission.
