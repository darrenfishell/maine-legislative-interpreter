64.  Phase-out of credit.  The credit allowed under this section is phased out as 
7follows.
8A.  For single individuals, the credit is reduced by $40 for every $500 or portion 
9thereof that exceeds $50,000 of the income.
10B.  For unmarried individuals or legally separated individuals who qualify as heads of 
11households, the credit is reduced by $40 for every $750 or portion thereof that 
12exceeds $75,000 of the income.
13C.  For individuals filing married joint returns or surviving spouses permitted to file 
14joint returns, the credit is reduced by $40 for every $1,000 or portion thereof that 
15exceeds $100,000 of the income.
165.  Refundability of credit.  The tax credit allowed under this section is refundable.
17SUMMARY
18This bill provides, beginning in 2021, a refundable income tax credit of up to $2,000 
19to a taxpayer who personally provides at least 150 hours per year of personal care 
20assistance services for the care and support of an eligible family member 18 years of age 
21or older.
22The 150-hour annual threshold may be met by providing care to one or more eligible 
23family members or, if the taxpayer is married filing a joint return, by combining the hours 
24of both taxpayers providing care to one or more eligible family members.  The credit is 
25subject to reduction based on the income and length of Maine residency of the individual.
26An eligible family member must require assistance with at least one activity of daily 
27living, as certified by a qualified licensed health care practitioner, and qualify as a 
28dependent of, spouse of, registered domestic partner of, parent of or other relative by 
29blood or marriage of the eligible family caregiver or a registered domestic partner of the 
30eligible family caregiver.
