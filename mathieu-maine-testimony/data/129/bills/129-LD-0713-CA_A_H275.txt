SUMMARY 
2 This amendment replaces the bill. This amendment: 
3 1. Provides that for conviction of certain Class E crimes of negligence related to 
4 endangered or threatened species, the maximum allowable fine of $1,000 must be 
5 adjudged; 
6 2. Removes the requirement that, for a first-time offense of negligently feeding, 
7 setting bait for or harassing any endangered or threatened species, a law enforcement 
8 officer must issue a warning; 
9 3. Provides that for conviction of certain Class D crimes involving intentional acts 
10 related to endangered or threatened species, the maximum allowable fine of $2,000 must 
11 be adjudged; 
12 4. Removes the requirement that, for a first-time offense of intentionally feeding, 
13 setting bait for or harassing any endangered or threatened species, a law enforcement 
14 officer must issue a warning; 
15 5. Provides that for conviction of certain Class D crimes involving intentional 
16 conduct related to a delisted species, the maximum allowable fine of $2,000 must be 
17 adjudged; 
18 6. Removes the requirement that, for a first-time offense of intentionally harassing a 
19 delisted species, a law enforcement officer must issue a warning; and 
20 7. Removes the prohibition on intentionally feeding or setting bait for a delisted 
21 species.
