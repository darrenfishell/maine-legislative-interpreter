27 SUMMARY 
28 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, makes the following 
29 changes to the bill. 
30 1. It keeps the maximum eligibility level for family income at 200% of the federal 
31 poverty level, which is the amount established in current law. 
32 2. It directs the Department of Health and Human Services to submit any waivers or 
33 state plan amendments to accomplish the eligibility criteria established in this legislation, 
34 including but not limited to removing the 3-month waiting peiiod for emollment in the 
35 Cub Care program following the loss of health insurance or coverage under an employer-
36 based plan.

1 3. It removes the provision that eligibility is not subject to an asset test because 
2 current rules do not make eligibility subject to an asset test.

5 5. It removes the requirement that the department contract for outreach activities and

8 6. It removes the requirement for the department to submit a state plan amendment to 
9 the United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for Medicare and 
10 Medicaid Services to implement the use of the express lane eligibility option. 
11 7. It makes the repeal of the waiting period and premium payment requirements 
12 under the Cub Care program contingent on the approval of a waiver of those requirements 
13 by the United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for Medicare 
14 and Medicaid Services. 
15 8. It also adds an appropriations and allocations section.
