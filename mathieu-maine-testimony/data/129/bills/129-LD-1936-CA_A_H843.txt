14 'Sec. 1.  22 MRSA §3174-EEE is enacted to read:
15§3174-EEE.  In-home personal care services to minors
161.  Reimbursement for parent.  A parent of a child who is eligible for in-home 
17 personal care services under the MaineCare program or a similar state-funded program may 
18 receive reimbursement for providing those services to the child only if the parent is hired 
19 and supervised by an employer pursuant to subsection 2 and in accordance with rules 
20 adopted by the department.
212.  Employer designation.  In order for a parent to be reimbursed under subsection 1, 
22 an employer must be designated by the department.  The employer must be approved by 
23 both the department and the parent to act in the child's best interest.  The employer may be 
24 another person who is not the parent of the child or may be a personal care agency as 
25 defined in section 1717, subsection 1, paragraph C or a placement agency as defined in 
26 section 1717, subsection 1, paragraph C-1.  
273.  Rulemaking.  The department shall adopt rules to implement this section.  Rules 
28 adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 
29 375, subchapter 2-A.
30Sec. 2.  Department of Health and Human Services to seek federal 
31approval.  The Department of Health and Human Services shall seek any necessary state 
32 plan amendments or waivers from the United States Department of Health and Human 
33 Services, Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services to implement the Maine Revised 
34 Statutes, Title 22, section 3174-EEE.  The department shall submit the requests for approval 
35 no later than 6 months after the effective date of this Act.
