COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A" to H.P. 809, L.D. 1105, Bill, "An Act To 
Allow Acupuncture Detoxification Specialists To Administer an Auricular Acupuncture 
Treatment for Addiction" 
Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
'An Act To Allow Acupuncture Detoxification Specialists To Administer an 
Auricular Acupuncture Treatment for Substance Use and Co-occurring Disorders' 
Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the 
following: 
'Sec. 1. 32 MRSA §12501, sub-§1-B is enacted to read: 
1-B. Acupuncture detoxification specialist. "Acupuncture detoxification 
specialist" means an individual licensed under this chapter to practice auricular 
acupuncture detoxification. 
Sec. 2. 32 MRSA §12501, sub-§6-K is enacted to read: 
6-K. National acupuncture detoxification association. "National acupuncture 
detoxification association" means the national organization that provides training in 
auricular acupuncture detoxification, as specified in rules by the board. 
Sec. 3. 32 MRSA c. 113-B, sub-c. 5 is enacted to read: 
SUBCHAPTER 5 
AURICULAR ACUPUNCTURE DETOXIFICATION LICENSING 
REQUIREMENTS AND SCOPE OF PRACTICE 
§12551. License required; licensee title; scope of practice; limitations 
1. License required. A person may not practice, offer to practice or profess to be 
authorized to practice auricular acupuncture detoxification or represent to the public that

3 2. Title. A person licensed under this subchapter may use the title "licensed

5 may not represent to the public that the person is an acupuncturist. 
6 3. Scope of practice. Auricular acupuncture detoxification is the subcutaneous 
7 insertion of sterile, single-use disposable acupuncture needles in consistent, 
8 predetermined bilateral locations on the outer ear according to national acupuncture 
9 detoxification · association protocol for the purpose of treatment of substance use and 
10 co-occurring disorders. The practice is limited to the outer ear. 
11 4. Limitations. An acupuncture detoxification specialist: 
12 A. May not perform acupuncture outside of the scope of practice of auricular 
13 acupuncture detoxification; 
14 B. May provide auricular acupuncture detoxification only under the general 
15 supervision of a licensed acupuncturist whose license is in good standing. The 
16 supervising acupuncturist must. at a minimum. be available by telephone or 
17 electronic means during business hours and shall conduct at least 2 in-person visits or 
18 visits through videoconferencing with the acupuncture detoxification specialist 
19 during the first year the supervising acupuncturist oversees the work of the 
20 acupuncture detoxification specialist; and 
21 C. May provide auricular acupuncture detoxification in, or in collaboration with, a 
22 program for substance use and co-occurring disorders or other state-approved 
23 program. An acupuncture detoxification specialist shall provide documentation to the 
24 program administrator demonstrating that the acupuncture detoxification specialist 
25 possesses a record of completion of training in auricular acupuncture detoxification 
26 from the national acupuncture detoxification association or completion of other 
27 board-approved auricular acupuncture detoxification training.

30 specialist under this subchapter. the applicant must hold a valid unrestricted Maine

33 B. Physician or physician assistant: 
34 C. Nurse or nurse practitioner: 
35 D. Professional counselor or clinical professional counselor: 
36 E. Psychologist; or 
37 F. Licensed social worker. conditional licensed social worker, licensed clinical social

7 §12553. Rulemaking 
8 The board may adopt rules necessary to implement this subchapter and set standards 
9 for acupuncture detoxification specialists. Rules adopted pursuant to this section are 
10 routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A. 
11 §12554. Fees and renewal 
12 1. Fees. The Director of the Office of Professional and Occupational Regulation 
13 within the department may establish by rule fees for the purposes authorized under this 
14 subchapter in amounts that are reasonable and necessary for their respective purposes, 
15 except that the fee for initial and renewal licensure may not exceed $675 annually. Rules 
16 adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules pursuant to Title 5, chapter 
17 375, subchapter 2-A. 
18 2. Renewal. A license issued under this subchapter expires on the stated expiration 
19 date as determined by the commissioner. To maintain licensure, prior to expiration of a 
20 license, a licensee shall apply for renewal, pay the required fee and identify the 
21 supervising licensed acupuncturist in accordance with section 12551. subsection 4, 
22 paragraph B. 
23 3. Late renewal. A license may be renewed up to 90 days after the date of 
24 expiration upon payment of a late fee in addition to the renewal: fee as set pursuant to 
25 subsection 1. A person who submits an application for renewal more than 90 days after 
26 the date of expiration is subject to all requirements governing new applicants under this 
27 subchapter, except that the board, giving due consideration to the protection of the public, 
28 may waive any such requirement if that renewal application is received, together with the 
29 late fee and renewal fee, within 2 years from the date of the expiration.' 
30 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or 
31 section number to read consecutively. 
32 SUMMARY 
33 This amendment replaces the bill and directs the Department of Professional and 
34 Financial Regulation, Board of Complementary Health Care Providers to license 
35 acupuncture detoxification specialists. The amendment also establishes standards for the 
36 provision of auricular acupuncture detoxification.
