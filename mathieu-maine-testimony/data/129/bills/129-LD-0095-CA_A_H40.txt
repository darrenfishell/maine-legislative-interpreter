Amend the bill by inserting after the enacting clause and before section 1 the 
following: 
'Sec. 1. 21-A MRSA §1, sub-§32-A is enacted to read: 
32-A. Primary residence. "Primary residence" has the same meaning as 
"residence," except that a person's primaiy residence may be a nontraditional residence, 
including but not limited to a shelter, park or underpass. A person may not have more 
than one primaiy residence at a time. If a person claims a homestead exemption on 
property under Title 36, chapter 105, subchapter 4-B, that property is presumed to be the 
person's primary residence until the person: 
A. Claims a homestead exemption on another property, at which time the other 
property is presumed to be the person's primary residence; or 
B. No longer claims a homestead exemption.'

10 SUMMARY 
11 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, adds a definition of 
12 11primary residence11 to the bill and specifies that, when a person who is a State Senator or 
13 State Representative, or a candidate for those offices, claims a homestead exemption 
14 under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 36, chapter 105, subchapter 4-B, that property is 
15 presumed to be the person's primary residence until the person claims a homestead 
16 exemption on another property or until the person no longer claims a homestead 
17 exemption on any property. A person who files a primary petition or a nomination 
18 petition for these offices or who seeks to be declared a write-in candidate for these offices 
19 must declare, under oath, that the person's primary residence is in the district the person 
20 seeks to represent. 
21 The amendment also clarifies that a. vacancy occurs in the office of State Senator or 
22 State Representative when the incumbent no longer maintains a primary residence in the 
23 district the person represents.
