21SUMMARY
22This bill amends the law governing the record-keeping requirements of the Public 
23Utilities Commission.  Current law requires that the commission maintain a complete 
24record of proceedings, investigations and formal public hearings.  The bill adds the 
25requirement to maintain a record of complaints.  It also specifies that the required records 
26be permanently maintained and include results and conclusions, including orders, 
27findings, decisions and settlement agreements.  Finally, the bill requires the commission, 
28upon request of a public utility customer, to reconstruct, to the extent possible, the record 
29of a proceeding, investigation, formal public hearing or complaint that was disposed of 
30prior to the requirement that a permanent record be maintained.
