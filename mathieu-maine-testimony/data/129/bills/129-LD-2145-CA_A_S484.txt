3SUMMARY
4 This amendment strikes and replaces the bill, which is a concept draft.
5 Part A of the amendment helps veterans access jobs in the following ways.
6 1.  It requires the Director of the Office of Professional and Occupational Regulation 
7 within the Department of Professional and Financial Regulation to review the examination 
8 and licensing eligibility of applicants for licensure who are veterans with military service, 
9 experience and training and to waive any applicable examination and licensing fees for 
10 these veterans.  Under current law, the director has discretion to conduct these reviews and 
11 to grant these fee waivers for individuals who self-identify as veterans.
12 2.  It requires each professional licensing board within or affiliated with the department 
13 to grant a license by endorsement or a temporary license to a person who holds a 
14 comparable license in another state and who is a returning military veteran, the qualified 
15 spouse of a returning military veteran or the qualified spouse of an active duty service 
16 member.  Under current law, these professional licensing boards may, but are not required 
17 to, provide licensure by endorsement or temporary licenses in these circumstances.
18 3.  It requires each professional licensing board within or affiliated with the department 
19 to permit a returning military veteran who applies for a license less than 2 years after being 
20 discharged from active duty and whose military training and experience qualify the veteran 
21 for a license in this State to acquire a temporary license until the full license is issued.
22 4.  It clarifies that a returning military veteran who is eligible for professional and 
23 occupational licensing assistance is a veteran of the Armed Forces of the United States who 
24 was discharged from active duty with either an honorable discharge or a general discharge 
25 under honorable conditions.
26 5.  It authorizes the Department of Public Safety, Emergency Medical Services' Board 
27 to waive examination and initial licensing fees for a veteran with military service, 
28 experience and training who is an applicant for licensure as an emergency medical services 
29 person.
30 6. It authorizes the Commissioner of Public Safety to waive initial licensing fees for a 
31 veteran with military service, experience and training who is an applicant for licensure as 
32 a private security guard.
33 Part B of the amendment helps veterans access postsecondary education in the State by 
34 requiring the University of Maine System, the Maine Community College System and the 
35 Maine Maritime Academy to each designate liaisons to provide academic counseling and 
36 benefits assistance to students who are members of the United States Armed Forces or who 
37 are veterans of the United States Armed Forces and to designate a specific location at each 
38 university or college where those students may congregate to study, to provide support to 
39 their peers and to receive assistance from the liaison.

41 of Health and Human Services, in coordination with the Director of the Maine Bureau of
