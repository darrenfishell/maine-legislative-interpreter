2Sec. 1.  20-A MRSA §10014 is enacted to read:
3§10014.  Homeless youth
41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Homeless youth" means a person 21 years of age or younger who is 
7unaccompanied by a parent or legal guardian and is without shelter where appropriate 
8care and supervision are available, whose parent or legal guardian is unable or 
9unwilling to provide shelter and care or who lacks a fixed, regular and adequate 
10nighttime residence.  For the purposes of this paragraph, "fixed, regular and adequate 
11nighttime residence" does not include residence in an institution of higher education's 
12on-campus housing.  "Homeless youth" does not include a person housed in a 
13correctional facility, jail or detention facility.
14B.  "Institution of higher education" means an educational institution that offers an 
15accredited postsecondary educational degree program.
162.  Homeless liaison.  Each institution of higher education shall designate a staff 
17member to serve as a liaison to assist homeless youth who are enrolled in that institution 
18of higher education.
193.  On-campus housing.  Each institution of higher education that has a residential 
20campus shall:
21A.  Give homeless youth who are enrolled in that institution of higher education 
22priority for on-campus housing, including but not limited to on-campus housing that 
23remains open during school breaks;
24B.  Develop a plan for housing homeless youth who are enrolled in that institution of 
25higher education during school breaks; and
26C.  Allow homeless youth who are enrolled part-time in that institution of higher 
27education to access on-campus housing during the homeless youth's first year of 
28school.

8SUMMARY
9This bill requires institutions of higher education in the State to designate an existing 
10staff member to serve as a liaison for homeless youth who are enrolled in that institution 
11of higher education.  The bill also requires institutions of higher education to give 
12homeless youth priority for on-campus housing, develop a plan to provide homeless 
13youth housing during school breaks and allow homeless youth who are enrolled part-time 
14to access on-campus housing during the homeless youth's first year of school. The bill 
15also expands the tuition waiver for state postsecondary educational institutions to include 
16tuition waivers for homeless youth.
