19SUMMARY
20This bill authorizes pharmacists to prescribe and dispense epinephrine autoinjectors 
21at no cost to a person of any age who the pharmacist reasonably believes is at risk of 
22experiencing severe allergic reactions, including anaphylaxis, or to a person who is in a 
23position to assist the other person if the other person is at risk of experiencing severe 
24allergic reactions, including anaphylaxis.  It creates a fund within the Department of 
25Health and Human Services to reimburse a pharmacist who has prescribed and dispensed 
26an epinephrine autoinjector and directs the department to adopt routine technical rules to 
27implement the fund.
