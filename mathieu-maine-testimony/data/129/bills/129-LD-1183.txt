Page 1 - 129LR2402(01)-11Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, the Freedom of Access Act is silent as to whether members of public 
4bodies may participate in public proceedings while not physically present, thus providing 
5no guidance as to how remote participation can meet the government transparency and 
6accountability goals of the Act; and
7Whereas, this resolve establishes the Study Committee on Remote Participation to 
8resolve questions concerning legal and accountable remote participation of members of 
9public bodies at all levels of government throughout the State; and
10Whereas, the study must be initiated before the 90-day period expires in order that 
11the study may be completed and a report submitted in time for submission to the next 
12legislative session; and
13Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
14the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
15immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
16therefore, be it
17Sec. 1.  Study committee established.  Resolved:  That the Study Committee 
18on Remote Participation, referred to in this resolve as "the study committee," is 
19established.
20Sec. 2.  Study committee membership.  Resolved:  That the study committee 
21consists of 7 members appointed as follows:
221.  Three members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including 
23members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature; 
24and
252.  Four members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
26House, including members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
27in the Legislature.
28Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate 
29chair and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
30study committee.
31Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of study committee.  Resolved:  That all 
32appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
33resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
34Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
35the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the study committee.  If 30 days or 
36more after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have 
37been made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant 
38authority for the study committee to meet and conduct its business.
Page 2 - 129LR2402(01)-11Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the study committee shall:
21.  Collect information about the current use of remote participation by members of 
3public bodies in this State, including any policies public bodies have adopted to govern 
4their use of remote participation.  The study committee may hold a public hearing to 
5gather public comments on whether the use of remote participation meets the public's 
6needs for government accountability, public participation and government efficiency;
72.  Review the use of remote participation, including any laws and policies governing 
8such practices, in other jurisdictions;
93.  Review the history and underlying goals of the Freedom of Access Act to 
10determine the best way to guarantee government transparency and accountability when 
11participation in public proceedings is conducted by members not physically present at the 
12proceeding; and
134.  Make recommendations concerning the use of remote participation, which may 
14include prohibition of or limitation on the use of remote participation, including any 
15guidance and best practices.
16Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
17necessary staffing services to the study committee, except that Legislative Council staff 
18support is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
19Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 4, 2019, the study 
20committee shall submit to the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary a report that 
21includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested legislation, for 
22presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
23Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
24legislation takes effect when approved.
25SUMMARY
26This resolve establishes the Study Committee on Remote Participation to explore the 
27use of remote participation by members of public bodies in this State and other 
28jurisdictions and determine the best way to guarantee government transparency and 
29accountability, goals of the Freedom of Access Act, when considering the use of remote 
30participation.  The study committee consists of 3 Senators and 4 members of the House of 
31Representatives.  The study committee may hold a public hearing to collect public 
32testimony on the use of remote participation.  The study committee is required to make 
33recommendations, which may include prohibition of or limitations on the use of remote 
34participation.  The study committee is required to submit a report, including any 
35legislative recommendations, to the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary no later than 
36December 4, 2019.
