Page 1 - 129LR3134(01)-11Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, beginning in April 2020, Mars Hill, Bridgewater and Blaine will be 
4without emergency medical services; and
5Whereas, this situation places the entire population of those towns at enormous risk; 
6and
7Whereas, it is imperative that this situation be rectified immediately; and
8Whereas, in order to secure emergency medical services for Mars Hill, Bridgewater 
9and Blaine it is necessary to create a quasi-municipal corporation to provide such 
10services; and
11Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
12the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
13immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
14therefore,
15Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
16Sec. 1.  Establishment.  The Central Aroostook County Emergency Medical 
17Services Authority, referred to in this Act as "the authority," is established to facilitate the 
18provision of emergency medical services to the citizens of Mars Hill, Bridgewater and 
19Blaine.
20Sec. 2.  Board of directors.  The authority is governed by a board of directors, 
21referred to in this Act as "the board."  The board consists of 9 members:
221.  Three from Mars Hill, selected by its municipal officers;
232.  Three from Bridgewater, selected by its municipal officers; and
243.  Three from Blaine, selected by its municipal officers.
25For the purposes of this section, "municipal officers" has the same meaning as in the 
26Maine Revised Statutes, Title 30-A, section 2001, subsection 10.
27The members of the board are appointed for terms of 2 years, except that one of the initial 
28members from each municipality is appointed for a term of 3 years.  The members may 
29be reappointed at the pleasure of the appointing authority.
30Sec. 3.  Powers.  The authority may:
311.  Employ and compensate personnel, consultants, technical and professional 
32assistants and an emergency medical services medical director;
Page 2 - 129LR3134(01)-112.  Make and enter into contracts and agreements and, pursuant to the bylaws of the 
2authority, purchase or lease all vehicles and equipment necessary to provide emergency 
3medical services to members;
43.  Hold public hearings and sponsor public forums;
54.  Sue and be sued in its own name;
65.  Accept funds, grants and services from federal, state, county and municipal 
7governments or any agency thereof, gifts and stipends from its member towns and private 
8gifts from individuals and entities; and
96.  Apply for and accept loans and allocate and disburse funds received to carry out 
10the purposes of the authority.
11Debts of the authority authorized under this Act do not constitute or create any debt or 
12liability on behalf of the State.  Debts incurred under this Act do not directly, indirectly or 
13contingently obligate the State to levy or to pledge any form of taxation or to make any 
14appropriation for their payment.  This section may not be construed to prevent the 
15authority from pledging its full faith and credit to the payment of loans or other debts 
16authorized pursuant to this Act.
17Sec. 4.  Duties.  The authority shall:
181.  Prepare an annual budget and require an annual audit that is made available for 
19public inspection;
202.  Follow uniform standards provided in the Maine Revised Statutes insofar as they 
21relate to Department of Public Safety rules;
223.  Make provisions for emergency medical services in Mars Hill, Bridgewater and 
23Blaine and, on a contract basis, other areas where new services are to be provided; 
24however, where emergency medical services are already provided by existing services, 
25they are not abolished by this Act without the express consent of the governing body of 
26the area where services are being provided and vote of the existing medical services' 
27board;
284.  Implement a subscription membership program unless otherwise prohibited by 
29law; and
305.  Set and annually adjust an approved cost-basis schedule that is uniform 
31throughout Mars Hill, Bridgewater and Blaine.
32Sec. 5.  Organization; conduct of business.  The board must be organized and 
33its business must be conducted in accordance with the following.
341.  The board shall elect a chair, vice-chair, secretary and treasurer from among its 
35members.
Page 3 - 129LR3134(01)-112.  The secretary shall keep a record of the board's meetings.  These records are 
2public records as defined in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 1, section 402, subsection 
33.
43.  The treasurer shall keep records of the board's transactions.  These records are 
5public records as defined in Title 1, section 402, subsection 3.
64.  The treasurer must be bonded in an amount to be determined by the board.
75.  A quorum of the board is established in the bylaws of the authority and must 
8include the chair or vice-chair and the secretary or treasurer of the board.
96.  The board shall adopt such bylaws and mission statements as are necessary for the 
10legal operation and proper management of the authority.
11Sec. 6.  Meetings.  The bylaws must establish the annual meeting of the board.  
12Additional meetings may be scheduled at the call of the chair or at the written request of 
13any 5 members of the board.
14A member who fails to attend board meetings 5 consecutive times may be replaced 
15by the appointing authority.  Notification of such absences from the secretary of the board 
16to the appointing authority is sufficient to trigger the appointment of a replacement board 
17member by the appointing authority.
18Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this Act takes 
19effect when approved.
20SUMMARY
21This bill establishes the Central Aroostook County Emergency Medical Services 
22Authority to facilitate the provision of emergency medical services to the citizens of Mars 
23Hill, Bridgewater and Blaine.  The bill includes an emergency preamble and an 
24emergency clause.
