2Sec. 1.  22 MRSA §1833 is enacted to read:
3§1833.  Emergency medical services persons working in a hospital setting
4A hospital licensed under this chapter may authorize an emergency medical services 
5person, as defined in Title 32, section 83, subsection 12, to provide to a patient of the 
6hospital in a hospital setting treatment that is within the scope of practice of the 
7emergency medical services person.
8Sec. 2.  32 MRSA §83, sub-§13, as amended by PL 1985, c. 730, §§8 and 16, is 
9repealed and the following enacted in its place:
1013.  Emergency medical treatment.  "Emergency medical treatment" means those 
11skills, techniques and judgments, as defined by the board, that are directed to 
12maintaining, improving or preventing the deterioration of the medical condition of the 
13patient and that are appropriate to be delivered by trained persons:
14A.  At the scene of a patient's illness or injury outside the hospital and during 
15transportation to the hospital; or
16B.  In a hospital setting if the hospital has authorized emergency medical services 
17persons to provide emergency medical treatment in the hospital under Title 22, 
18section 1833.
19SUMMARY
20This bill allows a hospital to authorize an emergency medical services person to 
21provide to a patient of the hospital in a hospital setting treatment that is within the scope 
22of practice of the emergency medical services person.
23The bill repeals and replaces the definition of "emergency medical treatment" in the 
24Maine Emergency Medical Services Act of 1982 to allow that treatment to be provided 
25by emergency medical services persons in a hospital setting if the hospital has authorized 
26treatment in the hospital by emergency medical services persons.
