12SUMMARY
13This bill accomplishes the following.  It:
141.  Generally prohibits selling, furnishing, giving away or offering to sell, furnish or 
15give away electronic smoking devices and nicotine liquid;
162.  Allows a registered dispensary under the Maine Medical Use of Marijuana Act to 
17continue to sell electronic smoking devices as part of its authorized activity as a 
18dispensary;
193.  Allows tobacco retailers to sell electronic smoking devices and nicotine liquid 
20after the Department of Health and Human Services has adopted governing rules.  These 
21rules are permitted only after the United States Department of Health and Human 
22Services, Food and Drug Administration approves the use of electronic smoking devices 
23as an evidence-based tobacco cessation strategy and promulgates regulations relating to 
24the manufacture, testing, sale and use of the devices and the federal Secretary of Health 
25and Human Services issues an order authorizing the devices to be introduced or delivered 
26for introduction into interstate commerce;
274.  Allows marijuana stores to sell electronic smoking devices after the Department of 
28Administrative and Financial Services adopts rules governing the sale of the devices by a 
29marijuana store.  The rules may be adopted only after the federal Secretary of Health and 
30Human Services issues an order authorizing the devices to be introduced or delivered for 
31introduction into interstate commerce; and
325.  Prohibits the sale of other new tobacco products until the Department of Health 
33and Human Services adopts rules governing their sale.  The rules governing a new 
34tobacco product may be adopted only after the federal Secretary of Health and Human 
35Services issues an order authorizing the new product to be introduced or delivered for 
36introduction into interstate commerce.
