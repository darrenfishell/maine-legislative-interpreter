4Except Until the 130th Legislature, except as provided in this section, each member 
5of the Senate and House of Representatives is entitled to a meal allowance in the amount 
6of $32 and a housing allowance in the amount of $38 for each day in attendance at 
7sessions of the Legislature and for each day the member occupies overnight 
8accommodations away from home either immediately preceding or immediately 
9following attendance at daily sessions of the Legislature.  Beginning with the 130th 
10Legislature, each member of the Senate and House of Representatives is entitled to a 
11meal allowance of $32 and a housing allowance in an amount determined annually by the 
12Legislative Council, which must be no less than the average nightly rate of lodging in the 
13Augusta area, for each day in attendance at sessions of the Legislature and for each day 
14the member occupies overnight accommodations away from home either immediately 
15preceding or immediately following attendance at daily sessions of the Legislature.  The 
16presiding officers may establish reasonable policies regarding allowances for meals and 
17overnight accommodations for the day immediately preceding the session, which may 
18include policies regarding whether an allowance is paid, whether the full amount or a 
19portion of the statutory allowance is paid and whether receipts are required.  In lieu of the 
20meal and housing allowance, each member is entitled to a daily meal allowance in the 
21amount of $32 and actual daily mileage allowances in an amount up to but not exceeding 
22$38 per day.  Each member of the Senate also receives an annual allowance for 
23constituent services in the amount of $2,000, $1,300 in January and $700 in the month 
24following adjournment of the regular session.  Each member of the House of 
25Representatives also receives an annual allowance for constituent services in the amount 
26of $1,500, $1,005 in January and $495 in the month following adjournment of the regular 
27session.  Notwithstanding this section, during the first regular session of each legislative 
28biennium, a member of the Senate or the House of Representatives may elect to receive 
29the first payment of the annual allowance for constituent services in December following 
30convening of the Legislature by notifying the Executive Director of the Legislative 
31Council in the manner prescribed by the executive director.  The Executive Director of 
32the Legislative Council shall inform Legislators of the choice available for payment of 
33the allowance for constituent services in the first year of the legislative biennium and of 
34any tax implications associated with exercising that choice.
35SUMMARY
36This bill changes, beginning with the 130th Legislature, the per diem housing 
37allowance from a set amount of $38 per day to an amount that is established annually by 
38the Legislative Council based on the average rate of lodging in the Augusta area.
