7SUMMARY
8This amendment does the following.
91. It clarifies that, with regard to the revocation of a lease for the violation of 
10minimum lease standards, the standards are those lease maintenance standards that have 
11been adopted by rule by the Commissioner of Marine Resources pursuant to the Maine 
12Revised Statutes, Title 12, section 6072, subsection 13, paragraph A.
132. It specifies that a person who has held a lease for at least 2 years from the date the 
14lease was originally executed may apply for an expansion of a research and aquaculture 
15lease.
163. It specifies that fees collected pursuant to Title 12, section 6072, subsection 13 for 
17certain testing or study services provided by the Department of Marine Resources must 
18be deposited in the Shellfish Fund.
194. It clarifies that the commissioner is authorized to adopt rules to allow changes to 
20research and aquaculture leases to include modifications in operations as a result of 
21changes in species and gear authorizations.
225. It includes an effective date of January 1, 2021 for the increase in fees for limited-
23purpose aquaculture licenses.
246. It also includes an effective date of January 1, 2023 for the provisions of the bill 
25that require a limited-purpose aquaculture license holder to directly supervise an 
26unlicensed individual that assists with licensed activities and allow the commissioner to 
27provide an exception to direct supervision to a license holder who is also the holder of or 
28a majority shareholder in a corporation that holds a lease or a license holder using 
29specific gear types specified in rules adopted by the commissioner.
307.  It adds an ongoing allocation to the Department of Marine Resources for 
31administrative costs.

Fiscal Detail and Notes
This bill amends certain rules regardin g aquaculture leasing and licenses. It also increases the fee for a resident 
limited-purpose aquaculture license from $50 to $100 and increases the fee for a nonresident limited-purpose aquaculture license from $300 to $400 starting January 1, 2021. This will increase Other Special Revenue Funds revenue to the Aquaculture Management  Fund (AMF) in the Department of Marine Resources (DMR) by $20,000 in 
fiscal year 2020-21 and by $40,000 in subs equent years. The bill includes a corresponding ongoing allocation to the 
DMR to allow for expenditure of the additional revenue.Fiscal Note129th MAINE LEGISLATURE
