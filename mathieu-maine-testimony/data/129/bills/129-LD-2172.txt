17SUMMARY
18This bill, which is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Environment and 
19Natural Resources pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 38, section 1772, 
20subsection 5, implements the recommendations of the Department of Environmental 
21Protection regarding the State's container redemption law as contained in a report 
22submitted to the Joint Standing Committee on Environment and Natural Resources 
23pursuant to that same public law.
24The bill reallocates the annual license fee for redemption centers from Title 38, 
25section 352 to the container redemption law and incorporates in the container redemption 
26law other existing application and licensing fees that are assessed by the department.  It 
27amends the definition of "manufacturer" under the container redemption law to include 
28persons that bottle, can or otherwise place beverages in beverage containers for sale to 
29distributors or dealers.  It incorporates into the beverage container label registration 
30provisions under the container redemption law the registration fees that are assessed by 
31the department and clarifies the process for addressing changes to a universal product 
32code or international article number on a beverage container label following its 
33registration with the department.
34The bill also requires the department, using information reported to the department by 
35initiators of deposit and pick-up agents that are not initiators of deposit pursuant to Title 
3638, section 3119, or other available information, to assess the efficiency and convenience 
37of the beverage container redemption system and develop recommendations to improve 
38efficiencies in the handling and transportation of beverage containers and to ensure 
39convenient collection of beverage containers for consumers.  On or before February 15, 
402021, the department is required to submit a report to the joint standing committee of the
