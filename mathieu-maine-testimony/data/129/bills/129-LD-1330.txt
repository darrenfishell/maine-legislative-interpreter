41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Average annual daily traffic" means the average annual daily traffic as 
7determined by the department using accepted engineering practices.
8B.  "Crosswalk" has the same meaning as in the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control 
9Devices published by the United States Department of Transportation, Federal 
10Highway Administration under 23 Code of Federal Regulations, Part 655, Subpart F 
11as adopted by the department.
12C.  "High-use roundabout" means a roundabout with an average annual daily traffic 
13of over 10,000 motor vehicles.
14D.  "Roundabout" has the same meaning as in the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control 
15Devices published by the United States Department of Transportation, Federal 
16Highway Administration under 23 Code of Federal Regulations, Part 655, Subpart F 
17as adopted by the department.
182.  Crosswalks; high-use roundabouts.  The department shall whenever practicable 
19install a crosswalk at a high-use roundabout on any state highway at least 150 feet from 
20the edge of the roundabout.  The crosswalk marking must comply with the latest edition 
21of the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices published by the United States 
22Department of Transportation, Federal Highway Administration under 23 Code of 
23Federal Regulations, Part 655, Subpart F as adopted by the department.
24SUMMARY
25This bill requires the Department of Transportation to install whenever practicable a 
26crosswalk at a high-use roundabout on a state highway at least 150 feet from the edge of 
27the roundabout.
