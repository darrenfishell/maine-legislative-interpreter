COMMITTEE AMENDMENT1SUMMARY
2 This amendment replaces the bill and is the majority report of the committee.  The 
3 amendment includes the provisions of the bill and requires that the sexual and reproductive 
4 health care and education that is provided in a jail or other county detention or correctional 
5 facility take into account history of trauma, address gender-specific health care needs in a 
6 corrections setting and meet nationally recognized standards.  The amendment requires the 
7 county and municipal detention facility advisory committee to establish a subcommittee to 
8 advise the committee in reviewing and developing adequate standards, policies and materials 
9 for the provision of sexual and reproductive health care and education for persons who are 
10 incarcerated as required by the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 30-A, section 1566 and Title 34-
11 A, section 3031, subsection 10 and requires the committee to undertake the review and 
12 development of standards, policies and materials.  The subcommittee must consist of a 
13 representative of the Department of Corrections; a woman who has been detained or 
14 incarcerated in a county jail; a woman who has been detained or incarcerated in a state 
15 correctional facility; a sexual and reproductive health care provider; a representative of a 
16 statewide association of sheriffs; a representative of an organization with the primary mission 
17 to advance racial justice; and a medical provider who treats pregnant people who are 
18 incarcerated. The committee must submit a report to the joint standing committee of the 
19 Legislature having jurisdiction over criminal justice matters by January 1, 2021 regarding the 
20 work of the subcommittee and any actions taken by the county and municipal detention facility 
21 advisory committee.
