2Sec. 1.  38 MRSA §417-B is enacted to read:
3§417-B.  Reporting discharges of aqueous film-forming foam
41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Aqueous film-forming foam" or "AFFF" means a fire suppressant foam 
7containing fluorinated surfactants intended for extinguishing flammable liquid fires.
8B.  "Discharge" includes, but is not limited to, any spilling, leaking, pumping, 
9pouring, spraying, emitting, disposing, escaping, emptying or dumping, whether 
10intentional or unintentional.
11C.  "Person" means any natural person, firm, association, partnership, corporation, 
12trust, the State and any agency of the State, governmental entity, quasi-governmental 
13entity, the United States and any agency of the United States and any other legal 
14entity.
152.  Reporting.  A person causing a discharge of AFFF into or upon any coastal 
16waters, estuaries, tidal flats, beaches and lands adjoining the seacoast of the State, or into 
17or upon any lake, pond, river, stream, sewer, surface water drainage, ground water or 
18other waters of the State or any public or private water supply or onto lands adjacent to, 
19on or over such waters of the State shall report the discharge to the department as soon as 
20practicable, but no later than 24 hours after the discharge occurs.

21Sec. 3.  38 MRSA §1612 is enacted to read:
22§1612.  Products containing PFAS
231.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
24following terms have the following meanings.
25A.  "Final product" means any product, including its components, sold or distributed 
26for personal, residential, commercial or industrial use, including materials used to 
27manufacture other products.
28B.  "Intentionally added PFAS" means PFAS added to a final product or one of its 
29product components to provide a specific characteristic, appearance or quality or to 
30perform a specific function.  "Intentionally added PFAS" includes any degradation 
31by-products of those PFAS.
32C.  "Manufacturer" means any person who created a final product or whose brand 
33name is affixed to the product. In the case of a product that was imported into the 
34United States, "manufacturer" includes the importer or first domestic distributor of 
35the product if the person who manufactured or assembled the product or whose brand 
36name is affixed to the product does not have a presence in the United States.
37D.  "Perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances" or "PFAS" means any member of 
38the class of fluorinated organic chemicals containing at least one fully fluorinated 
39carbon atom.
40E.  "Product" means an item manufactured, assembled, packaged or otherwise 
41prepared for sale to consumers.
Page 3 - 129LR3246(01)-11F.  "Product component" means a portion of a final product.  A product component 
2may be created by the same manufacturer that created the final product or by a 
3different manufacturer.
42.  Notification.  The following provisions govern notification.
5A.  Effective January 1, 2023, a manufacturer of a final product for sale in the State 
6that contains intentionally added PFAS shall submit to the department a written 
7notification, unless the notification requirement is waived by the department pursuant 
8to this section.  The written notification must include:
9(1)  A brief description of the final product;
10(2)  The purpose for which PFAS are used in the final product, including its 
11product components;
12(3)  The amount of PFAS, by individual chemical abstracts service registry 
13number, in each final product, reported as an exact quantity determined using 
14commercially available analytical methods or as falling within a range approved 
15for reporting purposes by the department; and
16(4)  The name and address of the manufacturer, and the name, address and phone 
17number of a contact person for the manufacturer.
18B.  With the approval of the department, a manufacturer may supply the information 
19required in paragraph A for a product category rather than an individual final product.
20C.  The manufacturer shall update and revise the information in the written 
21notification whenever there is significant change in the information, as set forth in 
22department rules adopted under subsection 9, or when requested by the department.
233.  Waiver of notification; extension of deadline.  The department may waive all or 
24part of the notification requirement under subsection 2 if the department determines that 
25substantially equivalent information is already publicly available. The department may 
26extend the deadline for submission by a manufacturer of the information required under 
27subsection 2 if the department determines that more time is needed by the manufacturer 
28to comply with the submission requirement.
294.  Exemptions.  The following provisions govern exemptions.
30A.  A retailer is exempt from subsection 6 unless that retailer knowingly sells a 
31product containing PFAS after that retailer has received notification in accordance 
32with subsection 7, paragraph B.
33B.  The following products are exempt from this section:
34(1)  A final product for which federal law governs notice of the presence of PFAS 
35in the product in a manner that preempts state authority; and
36(2)  A product subject to Title 32, chapter 26-A or 26-B.
375.  Fees.  The department may assess a fee payable by a manufacturer upon 
38submission of the notification required under subsection 2 to cover the department's 
Page 4 - 129LR3246(01)-11reasonable costs in managing the information collected. Fees must be established in 
2department rules as set forth in subsection 9.
36.  Failure to provide notice.  A final product containing PFAS may not be sold, 
4offered for sale or distributed for sale in this State if the manufacturer has failed to 
5provide information required under subsection 2. The department shall exempt a final 
6product from this prohibition if, in the department's judgment, the lack of availability of 
7the final product could pose an unreasonable risk to public health, safety or welfare.
87.  Certificate of compliance.  If the department has evidence that a final product is 
9being offered for sale in violation of this section, the department may request the 
10manufacturer of the final product to provide a certificate of compliance with this section. 
11Within 30 days of receipt of a request under this subsection, the manufacturer shall:
12A.  Provide the department with the certificate of compliance attesting that the final 
13product does not contain intentionally added PFAS; or
14B.  Notify persons who sell the final product in this State that the sale of the final 
15product is prohibited and provide the department with a list of the names and 
16addresses of those notified.
178.  Confidentiality.  Information submitted to the department pursuant to this section 
18is subject to the confidentiality provisions under section 1310-B.
199.  Rules.  The department shall adopt rules to implement this section. Rules adopted 
20pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, 
21subchapter 2-A.
22SUMMARY
23This bill requires manufacturers of products with intentionally added perfluoroalkyl 
24and polyfluoroalkyl substances to report the presence of those substances in those 
25products to the Department of Environmental Protection beginning in 2023.  This bill also 
26requires any person who causes a discharge of aqueous film-forming foam into waters of 
27the State to report that discharge to the department within 24 hours.
