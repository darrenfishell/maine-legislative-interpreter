Page 1 - 129LR0340(01)-11Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, this legislation establishes the Commission To Study and Recommend a 
4Minimum Age for Participation in Tackle Football; and
5Whereas, the study must be initiated before the 90-day period expires in order that 
6the study may be completed and a report submitted in time for submission to the next 
7legislative session; and
8Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
9the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
10immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
11therefore, be it
12Sec. 1.  Commission established.  Resolved:  That the Commission To Study 
13and Recommend a Minimum Age for Participation in Tackle Football, referred to in this 
14resolve as "the commission," is established.
15Sec. 2.  Commission membership.  Resolved:  That the commission consists of 
1613 members appointed as follows:
171.  Three members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including 
18members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
192.  Four members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
20House, including members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
21in the Legislature;
223.  A representative of the Maine School Superintendents Association, appointed by 
23the President of the Senate;
244.  A representative of the Maine Principals' Association, appointed by the Speaker of 
25the House;
265.  A representative of the Maine Athletic Trainers' Association, appointed by the 
27President of the Senate; and
286.  Three members of the public, 2 members appointed by the President of the Senate 
29and one member appointed by the Speaker of the House.
30Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate 
31chair and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
32commission.
33Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
34appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
35resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
36Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
Page 2 - 129LR0340(01)-11the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
2after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
3made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority 
4for the commission to meet and conduct its business.
5Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall study and recommend a 
6minimum age at which a person may participate in tackle football.
7Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
8necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff 
9support is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
10Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 4, 2019, the commission 
11shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
12legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
13Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
14legislation takes effect when approved.
15SUMMARY
16This resolve establishes the Commission To Study and Recommend a Minimum Age 
17for Participation in Tackle Football.   The commission is required to submit a report, 
18including suggested legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 
19129th Legislature.
