3ยง394.  Background checks of firearm buyers
41.  Requirement.  If neither the seller nor buyer in a transaction involving the 
5transfer or sale of a firearm, as defined in Title 17-A, section 2, subsection 12-A, is a 
6federally licensed firearms dealer, referred to in this section as "the dealer," the 
7transaction must be facilitated by such a dealer.  The dealer shall perform a background 
8check of the buyer in the same manner as if the dealer were the seller of the firearm that 
9is the subject of the transaction.  If the background check reveals that the buyer is 
10prohibited from purchasing a firearm, the dealer shall notify the seller of that fact.  The 
11dealer may charge a reasonable fee for serving as the facilitator.
122.  Limitations.  This section applies only to transactions in which:
13A.  The seller offers to sell or transfer the firearm at a gun show, event or function:
14(1)  The purpose of which is to facilitate the purchase and sale of firearms;
15(2)  At which 3 or more vendors of firearms offer firearms for sale; and
16(3)  At which 25 or more firearms are offered for sale; or
17B.  Pursuant to an advertisement, posting, display or other listing on the Internet or in 
18a publication, the seller offers to sell or transfer, or indicates an intent to sell or 
19transfer, a firearm.
20SUMMARY
21This bill requires background checks of purchasers of firearms for all private sales at 
22gun shows or private sales resulting from advertising or marketing.
