6SUBCHAPTER 8
7PRESIDENTIAL PRIMARY ELECTIONS
8§441.  Determination and date of primary; voter eligibility
91.  Determination of primary.  No later than November 1st of the year prior to a 
10presidential election year, the Secretary of State shall set the date of the presidential 
11primary election, which must be held on a Tuesday in March of the year in which a 
12presidential election is held.  Whenever the state committee of a party certifies that there 
13is a contest among candidates for nomination as the presidential candidate, the Secretary 
14of State shall consult with the state committee of each party to determine the date of the 
15presidential primary.
162.  Eligible voter.  Notwithstanding section 340, subsection 1, only a voter who is 
17enrolled in a party may vote in the party's presidential primary election.
18§442.  Petitions
19On or before November 1st of the year prior to a presidential election year, the 
20Secretary of State shall prepare and make available petitions for circulation by a person 
21desiring to be a contestant in the Maine presidential primary election of any party.  This 
22petition must be completed and filed no later than 5:00 p.m. on December 21st of the year 
23prior to a presidential election year in the manner provided in sections 335 and 336.
24§443.  Ballot preparation
25The Secretary of State shall prepare ballots for a presidential primary election.  A 
26ballot must include the name of a person who files with the Secretary of State a petition 
27in accordance with section 442.  The Secretary of State shall determine if a petition meets 
28the requirements of sections 335, 336 and 442, subject to challenge and appeal under 
29section 337.
30SUMMARY
31This bill makes permanent the temporary provisions in law for presidential primaries 
32that were repealed on December 1, 2018.
