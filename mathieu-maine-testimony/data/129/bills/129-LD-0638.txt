2Sec. 1.  12 MRSA §10659, sub-§3 is enacted to read:
33.  Exception; supplemental minerals.  Notwithstanding subsection 1, from March 
41st to July 31st, both days inclusive, a person may place supplemental minerals for deer 
5if:
6A.  The supplemental minerals are placed on the person's own land or, if the person is 
7placing supplemental minerals on another person's land, the person has obtained oral 
8or written permission of the owner of that land to place the supplemental minerals; 
9and
10B.  The supplemental minerals do not contain any grain or food products.
11Sec. 2.  12 MRSA §11452, sub-§1, as affected by PL 2003, c. 614, §9 and 
12amended by c. 655, Pt. B, §171 and affected by §422, is further amended to read:
131.  Prohibitions.  A Except as otherwise permitted by section 10659, subsection 3, a 
14person may not, during an open hunting season on deer: 
15A.  Place salt or any other bait or food in a place to entice deer to that place; or
16B.  Hunt from an observation stand or blind overlooking salt, grain, fruit, nuts or 
17other foods known to be attractive to deer.  This prohibition does not apply to hunting 
18from an observation stand or blind overlooking:
19(1)  Standing crops;
20(2)  Foods that are left as a result of normal agricultural operations or as a result 
21of a natural occurrence; or
22(3)  Bear bait that is placed at a bear hunting stand or blind in accordance with 
23section 11301, subsection 1.
24SUMMARY
25This bill allows a person to place supplemental minerals for deer from March 1st to 
26July 31st if the supplemental minerals are placed on that person's own land, or on another 
27person's land with the other person's oral or written permission, and the supplemental 
28minerals do not contain any grain or food products.
