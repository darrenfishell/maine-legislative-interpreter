41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Beverage" means any of the following products that are in liquid, ready-to-drink 
7form and are intended for human consumption: beer and malt beverages; wine or 
8distilled spirits coolers; carbonated and noncarbonated water; soda; carbonated and 
9noncarbonated soft drinks and fruit drinks; coffee and tea drinks; and vegetable juice 
10in plastic beverage containers of 16 ounces or less.
11B.  "Manufacturer" means a person, partnership, association, corporation or other 
12entity that, through its own action or through contract or control of another entity, is 
13primarily responsible for the production of a beverage held in a single-use plastic 
14beverage container and sold, offered for sale or distributed for sale in the State.
15C.  "Plastic" means a synthetic material made from fossil fuel or organic-based 
16polymers, such as polyethylene, polystyrene, polypropylene and polycarbonate, that 
17can be molded or blown into specific shapes.
18D.  "Plastic beverage cap" means a cap that is composed wholly or in large part of 
19plastic and that is screwed onto or otherwise affixed to a single-use plastic beverage 
20container. "Plastic beverage cap" does not include a cap that is composed wholly or 
21in large part of a nonplastic material but includes a plastic seal.
22E.  "Postconsumer recycled plastic" means plastic produced from the recovery, 
23separation, collection and reprocessing of plastic that would otherwise be disposed of 
24or processed as waste.
25F.  "Single-use plastic beverage container" means an individual bottle, can, jar, carton 
26or other container that:
27(1)  Is composed wholly or in large part of plastic;
28(2)  Contains one liter or less of a beverage; and
29(3)  Is intended to be disposed of after one use.
30"Single-use plastic beverage container" does not include a container that is composed 
31of nonplastic materials only but includes a plastic beverage cap; a cup or other similar 
32open or loosely sealed container; or a container that is intended by the producer to be 
33reused for the same purpose multiple times.
342.  Single-use plastic beverage containers.  Beginning January 1, 2020, a 
35manufacturer may not sell, offer for sale or distribute for sale in the State a single-use 
36plastic beverage container unless the container is composed of at least 15% postconsumer 
37recycled plastic.  Beginning January 1, 2022, a manufacturer may not sell, offer for sale 
38or distribute for sale in the State a single-use plastic beverage container unless the 
39container is composed of at least 20% postconsumer recycled plastic.  Beginning January

2single-use plastic beverage container unless the container is composed of at least 25% 
3postconsumer recycled plastic.
43.  Plastic beverage caps.  Beginning January 1, 2020, a manufacturer may not sell, 
5offer for sale or distribute for sale in the State a single-use plastic beverage container with 
6a plastic beverage cap unless the cap is composed of the same plastic as the beverage 
7container in accordance with the Standard Practice for Coding Plastic Manufactured 
8Articles for Resin Identification of the American Society for Testing and Materials, 
9ASTM D7611, and:
10A.  The cap is tethered to the container in a manner that prevents the separation of the 
11cap from the container when the cap is removed; or
12B.  The cap includes an opening from which the beverage can be consumed while the 
13cap remains screwed onto or otherwise affixed to the container.
144.  Manufacturer compliance.  This subsection governs compliance by 
15manufacturers with the requirements of this section.
16A.  A manufacturer that produces single-use plastic beverage containers shall provide 
17to the department upon request all information necessary for the department to 
18determine the manufacturer's compliance with the requirements of this section.
19B.  In determining whether an entity meets the definition of "manufacturer" under 
20subsection 1, paragraph B, the department shall consider, without limitation, the 
21following factors:
22(1)  The ownership of the brand name of a beverage contained in a single-use 
23plastic beverage container;
24(2)  The primary control or influence over the design of a beverage contained in a 
25single-use plastic beverage container; and
26(3)  The primary control or influence over the design of a single-use plastic 
27beverage container in which a beverage is contained.
28C.  An entity with a legally recognized corporate relationship to a manufacturer that 
29is subject to the requirements of this section may, on behalf of that manufacturer and 
30following notification to the department, assume the manufacturer's responsibilities 
31under this section.
32D.  The requirements of this section do not apply to a manufacturer of beer, wine or 
33spirits that annually produces 50,000 gallons or less of its product or to a 
34manufacturer of beverages other than beer, wine or spirits that annually produces 
35250,000 or fewer single-use plastic beverage containers containing its product.
365.  Administration and enforcement; rules.  The department shall administer and 
37enforce this section.  The department may adopt rules to implement this section.  Rules 
38adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
39chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.

2This bill prohibits, beginning January 1, 2020, a manufacturer from selling, offering 
3for sale or distributing for sale in the State a single-use plastic beverage container unless 
4the container is composed of at least 15% postconsumer recycled plastic.  Beginning 
5January 1, 2022, this threshold for the percentage of postconsumer recycled plastic in 
6single-use plastic beverage containers increases to 20% and, beginning January 1, 2024, 
7the threshold increases to 25%.
8The bill also prohibits, beginning January 1, 2020, a manufacturer from selling, 
9offering for sale or distributing for sale in the State a single-use plastic beverage container 
10with a plastic beverage cap unless the cap is composed of the same plastic as the beverage 
11container and the cap is tethered to the container in a manner that prevents the separation 
12of the cap from the container when the cap is removed or the cap includes an opening 
13from which the beverage can be consumed while the cap remains screwed onto or 
14otherwise affixed to the container.
