22CHAPTER 606
23DRUG DONATION AND REDISPENSING PROGRAM
24§2700-B.  Drug donation and redispensing program
251.  Program established.  The department shall establish a drug donation and 
26redispensing program, referred to in this chapter as "the program."  For purposes of this 
27chapter, "drug" means a prescription drug or legend drug under Title 32, section 
2813702-A, subsection 30.
292.  Program components.  To the extent authorized by federal law, components of 
30the program include:
31A.  Any person, including a drug manufacturer, licensed physician, pharmacy, health 
32care facility licensed under this Title or the Unused Pharmaceutical Disposal Program 
33under section 2700, may donate a drug to the program;
34B.  A drug may be donated at:
35(1)  A donation site provided by the department under subsection 4, paragraph A;
36(2)  A take-back event approved by the United States Department of Justice, 
37Drug Enforcement Administration; or
Page 2 - 129LR2470(01)-11(3)  A pharmacy, licensed health care provider's or professional's office or a 
2licensed health care facility that elects to participate in the program and meets 
3criteria established by the department under subsection 4, paragraph E;
4C.  The program may redispense a donated drug only if the drug is in the drug's 
5original, unopened, sealed packaging or, if the outside packaging is opened or 
6disturbed, the contents are one or more single-unit doses that are individually 
7contained in unopened, tamper-evident packaging;
8D.  The program may not redispense a donated drug if the drug is within 2 months of 
9the drug's expiration date, if the drug appears to be adulterated or misbranded or if the 
10drug is a scheduled drug as defined in Title 17-A, section 1101, subsection 11;
11E.  The program shall inventory all drugs donated and deliver the drugs to a 
12pharmacy that elects to participate in the program and meets criteria established by 
13the department under subsection 4, paragraph F; and
14F.  A pharmacy shall dispense a drug under paragraph E to a qualified recipient under 
15subsection 3 and may charge the recipient a handling fee.
163.  Qualifications of recipients.  A person is a qualified recipient and may receive a 
17donated drug from a pharmacy under subsection 2, paragraph E if the person has a family 
18income equal to or less than 350% of the federal poverty level, the person's family incurs 
19unreimbursed expenses for drugs that equal 5% or more of family income or the person's 
20total unreimbursed medical expenses equal 15% or more of family income.  For purposes 
21of this subsection, "federal poverty level" means that measure defined by the federal 
22Department of Health and Human Services and updated annually in the Federal Register 
23under authority of 42 United States Code, Section 9902(2).
244.  Department duties.  The department shall:
25A.  Provide donation sites in locations convenient to consumers throughout the State;
26B.  Implement by rule procedures for the documentation of all donated drugs;
27C.  Implement by rule procedures for the transfer to the Unused Pharmaceutical 
28Disposal Program under section 2700 of all drugs determined unfit for redispensing;
29D.  Provide for public education of the availability of the program, including the 
30location of donation sites and pharmacies that participate in the redispensing of the 
31drugs;
32E.  Implement by rule procedures by which a person can participate as a donor in the 
33program;
34F.  Implement by rule procedures by which a pharmacy can participate as a dispenser 
35of donated drugs under the program; and
36G.  Administer the program.
375.  Rules.  The department may adopt rules to carry out the purposes of the program.  
38Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined by Title 5, 
39chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
Page 3 - 129LR2470(01)-11SUMMARY
2This bill establishes the drug donation and redispensing program under the 
3Department of Health and Human Services.  The program collects donations of unused 
4prescription and legend drugs from health care providers, health care facilities and other 
5sources, including at drop-off locations throughout the State, and redispenses the drugs 
6through participating pharmacies to qualified low-income persons.
