5§19151.  Substance Use Disorders Cabinet
61.  Establishment.  The Substance Use Disorders Cabinet, referred to in this chapter 
7as "the cabinet," is established, no later than January 15, 2020, to promote 
8interdepartmental collaboration on policy development and program implementation and 
9to support the delivery of services for prevention, harm reduction and treatment of 
10substance use disorders for citizens of the State that are planned, managed and delivered 
11in an integrated manner.

25§19152.  Duties of the cabinet
26Within existing resources, the cabinet shall collaborate to create, manage and 
27promote coordinated policies, programs and service delivery systems that address 
28prevention, harm reduction and treatment of substance use disorders consistent with the 
29purposes of this chapter.  To accomplish these purposes, the cabinet shall:
301.  Regional cabinets.  Appoint regional cabinets to ensure that the purposes of this 
31chapter are implemented at the regional and local levels;
322.  Subcommittees.  Appoint subcommittees, which may include members from any 
33public or private agency or advisory committee or any citizen who has appropriate 
34interest and expertise, as may be necessary to carry out the work of the cabinet;

15§19153.  Implementation and oversight
16Within existing resources, the cabinet shall initiate, implement and oversee programs, 
17policies and services consistent with the purposes of this chapter, which may include but 
18are not limited to programs, policies and services designed to:
191.  Community resources.  Support a collaborative effort between communities and 
20State Government in order to effectively address the problem of substance use disorders 
21facing the State's citizens by organizing and combining the resources of State 
22Government with resources and leadership at the community level;
232.  Effectiveness indicators.  Identify indicators to measure progress in prevention, 
24harm reduction and treatment of substance use disorders to be used by policy makers at 
25the state and local levels;
263.  Reducing overdose.  Reduce the incidence of drug overdose among citizens of 
27the State and improve access to appropriate services for prevention, harm reduction and 
28treatment of substance use disorders;
294.  Access to information and referral.  Ensure easy access to information and 
30referral for services for treatment of substance use disorders;
315.  Service coordination and access.  Coordinate services for prevention, harm 
32reduction and treatment of substance use disorders as an integrated whole and facilitate 
33access to services; and
346.  Pooled funds.  Provide services for prevention, harm reduction and treatment of 
35substance use disorders using appropriate funds pooled from each department represented 
36in the cabinet.

6§19155.  Annual report
7Beginning in 2021, no later than January 31st of each year, the cabinet shall, within 
8existing resources, provide a report to the joint standing committees of the Legislature 
9having jurisdiction over appropriations and financial affairs, criminal justice and public 
10safety matters, education and cultural affairs, health and human services matters, 
11judiciary matters, labor matters and state and local government matters.  The cabinet shall 
12make the report available to the public on the Department of Health and Human Services' 
13publicly accessible website.
14Sec. 2.  Initial chair of the cabinet.  Notwithstanding the Maine Revised

18SUMMARY
19This bill establishes the Substance Use Disorders Cabinet. The cabinet consists of the 
20Commissioner of Corrections, the Commissioner of Education, the Commissioner of 
21Health and Human Services, the Commissioner of Labor and the Commissioner of Public 
22Safety, the Chief Justice of the Supreme Judicial Court and, at the discretion of the 
23Governor, one member of the public. The initial chair of the cabinet is the Commissioner 
24of Health and Human Services or the commissioner's designee. The cabinet is established 
25to promote interdepartmental collaboration on substance use disorders policy 
26development and program implementation and support service delivery in an integrated 
27manner. The duties of the cabinet include coordinating funding, conducting long-term 
28planning and policy development, coordinating service delivery, assessing resource 
29capacity, reviewing programs and policies and communicating the work of the cabinet. 
30The cabinet is authorized to solicit, receive and pool funds from the Federal Government, 
31subdivisions of the State and individuals, foundations or corporations. The cabinet is 
32required to submit an annual report to the joint standing committees of the Legislature 
33having jurisdiction over appropriations and financial affairs, criminal justice and public 
34safety matters, education and cultural affairs, health and human services matters, 
35judiciary matters, labor matters and state and local government matters and to make the 
36report available to the public.  The cabinet is required to carry out its duties within 
37existing resources.
