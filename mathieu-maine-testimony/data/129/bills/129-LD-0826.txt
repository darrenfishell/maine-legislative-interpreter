11The Secretary of State may issue a facsimile plate that is valid for a 90-day period during

14If a person does not have a vehicle to register on May 1st, a registration number may be 
15held for a maximum of 2 registration years by depositing with the Secretary of State $15 
16for each year; except that the registered owner of an antique vehicle may reserve the 
17antique registration assigned to that person for 4 years by depositing the sum of $15 for 
18each registration year. These fees are not refundable and may not be applied against the 
19registration fee.
20All numbers other than those reserved must be released and issued in rotation after July 
211st.
22A person wishing to select a number out of rotation may do so by paying the registration 
23fee and a reserved number fee of $15.
24A holder of vanity registration plates must pay the sum of $15 to reserve those letters or 
25combination of letters and numbers, which is credited toward the renewal fee.
26SUMMARY
27This bill provides that the $15 fee imposed to retain a motor vehicle registration 
28number in a year in which new registration plates are issued does not apply for the 
29retention of the registration number of a special veterans registration plate or a special 
30disability registration plate for veterans.
