12Violation of this subparagraph is a Class B crime;
13(4)  The value of the property is more than $1,000 but not more than $10,000.  
14Violation of this subparagraph is a Class C crime;
15(5)  The value of the property is more than $500 but not more than $1,000.  
16Violation of this subparagraph is a Class D crime; or
17(6)  The person has 2 or more prior convictions for any combination of the Maine 
18offenses listed in this subparagraph or for engaging in substantially similar 
19conduct to that of the Maine offenses listed in this subparagraph in another 
20jurisdiction.  The Maine offenses are:  theft; any violation of section 401 in which 
21the crime intended to be committed inside the structure is theft; any violation of 
22section 405 in which the crime intended to be committed inside the motor vehicle 
23is theft; any violation of section 651; any violation of section 702, 703 or 708; or 
24attempts to commit any of these crimes.  Section 9-A governs the use of prior 
25convictions when determining a sentence.  Violation of this subparagraph is a 
26Class C crime.
27SUMMARY
28This bill makes the crime of theft by deception when the victim is 66 years of age or 
29older at the time of the offense a Class B crime.
