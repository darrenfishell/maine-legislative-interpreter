8 SUMMARY 
9 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, specifies that a 
10 violation of the section on tanning facilities is subject only to civil penalties. It requires 
11 the Department of Health and Human Services to adopt routine technical rules to 
12 implement the law and otherwise regulate tanning facilities and directs the department to 
13 amend its rules in 10-144 C.M.R. Chapter 223 to be consistent with the law.

Correctional and Judicial Impact Statements 
The additional workload associated with the minimal number of new cases filed in the court system does not require 
additional funding at this time. 
The collection of additional fine revenue will increase General Fund and dedicated revenue by minor amounts. 
Fiscal Detail and Notes 
This bill may decrease the number of registered tanning facilities over time, thus decreasing income from registration 
fees. Any loss of revenue to the Department of Health and Human Services is expected to be minor and can be 
absorbed within existing budgeted resources.
