41.  Reimbursement.  The department shall provide reimbursement under the 
5MaineCare program for pasteurized donor breast milk provided to an infant receiving 
6benefits under this chapter if a physician or physician assistant licensed under Title 32, 
7chapter 36 or 48 or an advanced practice registered nurse licensed under Title 32, chapter 
831 signs an order stating that such milk is medically necessary and:
9A.  The infant is medically or physically unable to receive maternal breast milk or 
10participate in breastfeeding; or
11B.  The infant's mother is medically or physically unable to produce maternal breast 
12milk in quantities sufficient for the infant.
132.  Rulemaking.  The department shall adopt rules to implement this section.  Rules 
14adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined by Title 5, 
15chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
16Sec. 2.  Medicaid state plan amendment or waiver.  The Department of 
17Health and Human Services shall prepare and submit a Medicaid state plan amendment or 
18waiver as necessary to the United States Department of Health and Human Services, 
19Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services no later than January 1, 2021 that provides 
20or requests, as appropriate, Medicaid coverage for medically necessary donor breast milk 
21for infants.
22SUMMARY
23This bill requires that the Department of Health and Human Services provide 
24reimbursement under the MaineCare program for pasteurized donor breast milk provided 
25to an infant if a physician, physician assistant or advanced practice registered nurse signs 
26an order stating that such milk is medically necessary and the infant is medically or 
27physically unable to receive maternal breast milk or participate in breastfeeding or the 
28infant's mother is medically or physically unable to produce maternal breast milk in 
29quantities sufficient for the infant.
