316.  Special education circuit breaker reimbursement program.  The special 
4education circuit breaker reimbursement program is established.  Beginning in fiscal year 
52019-20 and in each subsequent year, the department shall in accordance with this 
6subsection reimburse school administrative units, for a student whose eligible special 
7education costs exceed 4 times the statewide EPS per-pupil rate, 75% of the amount of 
8the special education costs that are above 4 times the statewide EPS per-pupil rate.
9A.  For purposes of this subsection, "special education costs" include only those costs 
10directly attributable to providing the special education services in a student's 
11individual education plan and do not include transportation costs.
12B.  To be eligible for reimbursement under this program, a school administrative unit 
13must:
14(1) Include an estimate of a student's special education costs that the school 
15administrative unit expects to be eligible for reimbursement under this program 
16in the school administrative unit's school budget document prepared pursuant to 
17section 15693; 
18(2) Annually report special education costs eligible for reimbursement under this 
19subsection to the department for each eligible student on forms provided by the 
20department; and
21(3) Notify the department within 30 days of any change in previously approved 
22special education costs, including but not limited to any changes in a student's 
23enrollment status or individual education plan.
24C.  The department shall distribute reimbursements on a quarterly basis.
25D.  Funds received by a school administrative unit pursuant to this subsection must be 
26deposited in a separate revolving account and may be spent only on eligible special 
27education costs.
28E.  The department may not reimburse a school administrative unit for a student's 
29special education costs for which a school administrative unit has otherwise received 
30or expects to receive payment under this chapter, including but not limited to 
31payment received pursuant to section 15689-A.
32SUMMARY
33This bill establishes the special education circuit breaker reimbursement program, in 
34which the Department of Education reimburses school administrative units, for a student 
35whose eligible special education costs exceed 4 times the statewide EPS per-pupil rate, 
3675% of the amount of the special education costs that are above 4 times the statewide 
37EPS per-pupil rate. The bill requires the department to distribute reimbursements to 
38eligible school administrative units on a quarterly basis, and the reimbursements must be
