122.  Provider agreement.  A state agency or instrumentality of the State may not 
13commit state funds in a manner that the agency or instrumentality knows would result in 
14a direct payment to an Internet service provider unless:
15A.  The Internet service provider agrees in writing to conform to the requirements of 
16the Federal Communications Commission order, FCC 15-24, adopted on February 
1726, 2015, known as the Open Internet Order;
18B.  The agency or instrumentality notifies the State Controller of its intent to commit 
19state funds in a manner that would result in a direct payment to an Internet service 
20provider and provides at the same time to the State Controller a written agreement 
21from the Internet service provider that conforms to the requirements of paragraph A; 
22and
23C.  The State Controller finds that the requirements of paragraphs A and B have been 
24satisfied and authorizes the state agency or instrumentality to commit state funds.
25Nothing in this section limits the authority of the State Controller under any other 
26provision of law to limit or prohibit a state agency or instrumentality of the State from 
27committing state funds.
28Upon receipt of information or complaint from any person that an Internet service 
29provider may be failing to meet the requirements of an agreement made under this 
30section, the Attorney General may undertake an investigation and take any action the 
31Attorney General determines appropriate, including, but not limited to, action pursuant to 
32section 192.
33SUMMARY
34This bill prohibits a state agency or instrumentality from committing state funds in a 
35manner that the agency or instrumentality knows would result in a direct payment to an 
36Internet service provider unless the Internet service provider agrees in writing to conform 
37to the requirements of the Federal Communications Commission order, FCC 15-24, 
38adopted on February 26, 2015, known as the Open Internet Order.
