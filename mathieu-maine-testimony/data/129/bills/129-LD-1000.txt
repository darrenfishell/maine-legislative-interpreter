21SUBCHAPTER 10
22WASHINGTON COUNTY PRERELEASE CENTER
23§4301.  Establishment
24There is established the Washington County Prerelease Center, referred to in this 
25subchapter as "the center," which must be located in Washington County as a minimum 
26security unit of the Mountain View Correctional Facility.
27§4302.  Purposes
28The purposes of the center include vocational training and rehabilitative programs, 
29including work release and work involving public restitution.DEPARTMENT TOTAL - ALL

Page 11 - 129LR2406(01)-11§4303.  Superintendent
21.  Chief administrative officer.  The chief administrative officer of the center is the 
3superintendent of the Mountain View Correctional Facility as described in section 4103, 
4subsection 1, referred to in this subchapter as "the superintendent."
52.  Duties.  In addition to other duties set out in this chapter, the superintendent has 
6the following duties.
7A.  The superintendent shall exercise proper supervision over the employees, 
8grounds, buildings and equipment at the center.
9B.  The superintendent shall supervise and control the prisoners at the center in 
10accordance with departmental rules.
11§4304.  Prisoners generally
121.  Confinement of prisoners transferred to center.  All prisoners transferred to the 
13center must be detained and confined in accordance with the sentences of the court and 
14the rules of the department.
152.  Work release.  The superintendent shall work collaboratively with private 
16employers as necessary to ensure the provision of suitable work release opportunities for 
17prisoners at the center.
183.  Employment.  The commissioner may authorize the employment of prisoners at 
19the center on public works with any department, agency or entity of state, county or local 
20government and may authorize the use of prisoners to provide assistance in the 
21improvement of property owned by nonprofit organizations.
22A.  The commissioner shall adopt those rules as the commissioner considers proper to 
23ensure the care and treatment of the prisoners and the safe working conditions of 
24prisoners and departmental employees. Rules adopted pursuant to this paragraph are 
25routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
26B.  The purpose of the employment authorized in this subsection is to provide 
27training to the prisoners and to be a form of public restitution for the crimes 
28committed by the prisoners.
29C.  A prisoner employed under this subsection may not be compensated monetarily 
30for work performed.
31D.  The commissioner may request that a nonprofit organization pay for the 
32transportation of the prisoners it employs and pay the per diem compensation of 
33correctional officers or instructors who must accompany the prisoners or oversee the 
34work to be performed.
354.  Escape.  A prisoner who escapes from the center or from any assignment beyond 
36the grounds of the center, including assignment with community rehabilitative programs, 
37is guilty of escape under Title 17-A, section 755.
Page 12 - 129LR2406(01)-11§4305.  Employees of the center
2Employees of the center have the same power as deputy sheriffs in their respective 
3counties to search for and apprehend a prisoner who has escaped from the center when 
4those employees are authorized to do so by the superintendent.
5Sec. D-3.  Authority for property purchase for Washington County 
6Prerelease Center.  The Commissioner of Corrections may purchase a parcel of land 
7and buildings in Washington County for the Washington County Prerelease Center by 
8warranty deed with or without covenants to be conveyed to the State of Maine 
9Department of Corrections and recorded in the Washington County Registry of Deeds. 
10The commissioner may purchase the property and negotiate any sales, solicit bids or 
11directly enter a purchase and sale agreement. A purchase of property pursuant to this 
12section must be approved by the Director of the Bureau of General Services within the 
13Department of Administrative and Financial Services.

11SUMMARY
12PART A
13This Part makes appropriations and allocations.
14PART B
15This Part repeals the limited lifetime maximum coverage and reimbursement of 24 
16months under the MaineCare program for buprenorphine and naloxone combination 
17drugs for the treatment of addiction.
18PART C
19This Part repeals the limited lifetime maximum reimbursement of 24 months under 
20the MaineCare program for methadone for the treatment of addiction.
21PART D
22This Part repeals the laws governing the former Downeast Correctional Facility and 
23establishes a Department of Corrections prerelease center in Washington County.
24PART E
25This Part transfers the remaining funds in the cost recovery fund of the Public 
26Utilities Commission to the unappropriated surplus of the General Fund in fiscal year 
272018-19.
28PART F
29This Part requires the State Controller to transfer any remaining balance specifically 
30related to federal supplemental nutrition assistance program overpayments in the Food 
31Supplement Administration program, Federal Expenditures Fund to the Food Supplement 
32Administration program, Other Special Revenue Funds.
33PART G
Page 14 - 129LR2406(01)-11This Part imposes an additional assessment on electric utility entities for fiscal year 
22018-19 to provide funding to the Office of the Public Advocate in order to fund the costs 
3of representing ratepayers in rate cases.
