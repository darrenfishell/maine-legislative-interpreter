Page 1 - 129LR1665(01)-11Sec. 1.  Commissioner of Health and Human Services to convene study 
2group on crisis response system.  Resolved:  That the Commissioner of Health and 
3Human Services shall convene a study group of interested parties to review the crisis 
4response system in the State.  The study group members must be selected to ensure 
5geographic diversity and must include representatives of advocates for persons with 
6mental illness and representatives of persons with lived experience of mental illness, 
7providers of crisis services, hospitals, providers of residential and community-based 
8behavioral health treatment and nursing care, jail administrators, the Maine Sheriffs' 
9Association and police departments.
10Sec. 2.  Study group duties.  Resolved:  That in conducting the review of the 
11crisis response system in the State, the study group convened under section 1 shall 
12evaluate:
131. Average call times;
142. The average time spent on hold to connect to referral services;
153. Wait times for mobile units;
164. Any decline in the number of crisis teams and crisis stabilization unit beds 
17available across the State and where those declines have occurred;
185. The average length of stay in crisis stabilization units; and
196. Other data considered pertinent by the study group.
20Sec. 3.  Report.  Resolved:  That the study group convened under section 1 shall 
21develop recommendations regarding ways to structure state funding of child and adult 
22crisis services for behavioral health.  The study group shall submit a report, including any 
23recommended legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human 
24Services no later than December 15, 2019.  The joint standing committee may submit 
25legislation related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
26SUMMARY
27This resolve requires the Commissioner of Health and Human Services to convene a 
28study group of interested parties to review the crisis response system in the State.  The 
29study group is required to submit its report, including any recommended legislation, to 
30the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services no later than December 15, 
312019.  The joint standing committee may submit legislation related to the report to the 
32Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
