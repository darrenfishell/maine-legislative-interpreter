Page 1 - 129LR1668(01)-11Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, the Task Force To Study the Creation of a Comprehensive Career and 
4Technical Education System is established pursuant to this legislation to study the 
5feasibility of establishing a comprehensive 4-year high school career and technical 
6education program to provide a technical high school setting for students; and
7Whereas, the study must be initiated before the 90-day period expires in order that 
8the study may be completed and a report submitted in time for submission to the next 
9legislative session; and
10Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
11the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
12immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
13therefore, be it
14Sec. 1.  Task force established.  Resolved:  That the Task Force To Study the 
15Creation of a Comprehensive Career and Technical Education System, referred to in this 
16resolve as "the task force," is established.
17Sec. 2.  Task force membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
18353, the task force consists of 10 members appointed as follows:
191.  Three members appointed by the President of the Senate as follows:
20A.  One member of the Senate who is a member of the Joint Standing Committee on 
21Education and Cultural Affairs;
22B.  One member who is a current career and technical education high school 
23administrator; and
24C.  One member who is on the State Board of Education;
252.  Three members appointed by the Speaker of the House as follows:
26A.  One member of the House of Representatives who is a member of the Joint 
27Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs;
28B.  One member who is a current career and technical education high school 
29administrator; and
30C.  One member who is on the State Board of Education; and
313.  Four members appointed by the Governor as follows:
32A.  One member who attends a community college;
33B.  One member who is on a local board of education in a Maine community;
34C.  One member who is an officer of the Maine Education Association; and
35D.  One member who is a member of a trade union.
Page 2 - 129LR1668(01)-11Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate 
2chair and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the task 
3force.
4Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of task force.  Resolved:  That all 
5appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
6resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
7Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
8the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the task force.  If 30 days or more 
9after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
10made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority 
11for the task force to meet and conduct its business.
12Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the task force shall examine the feasibility of 
13establishing a comprehensive 4-year high school career and technical education program 
14to provide a technical high school setting for middle school students to attend at the 
15completion of the 8th grade.
16Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
17necessary staffing services to the task force, except that Legislative Council staff support 
18is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
19Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 4, 2019, the task force 
20shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
21legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
22Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
23legislation takes effect when approved.
24SUMMARY
25This resolve establishes the Task Force To Study the Creation of a Comprehensive 
26Career and Technical Education System to examine the feasibility of establishing a 
27comprehensive 4-year high school career and technical education program to provide a 
28technical high school setting for middle school students to attend at the completion of the 
298th grade.
