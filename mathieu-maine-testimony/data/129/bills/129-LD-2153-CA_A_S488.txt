30SUMMARY
31This amendment changes the provisions of the bill establishing the Accidental Drug 
32Overdose Review Panel to: update the name of the office of behavioral health in the 
33Department of Health and Human Services; limit the representation of families affected 
34by drug overdose deaths to one member; add a member who is a person in recovery from 
35substance use disorder, to be appointed by the Governor; and remove the provision 
36authorizing the Attorney General and the Governor to appoint additional members.  This 
37amendment also requires that the appointments to the panel take into consideration the 
38racial and ethnic diversity of the State.
39The amendment changes the date by which the first meeting is to be convened from 
40July 1, 2020 to January 1, 2021.

COMMITTEE AMENDMENT1The amendment provides that when the subset of all drug overdoses is selected for 
2review, the racial and ethnic composition of the population of individuals associated with 
3those deaths must be considered.
4The amendment defines "accidental drug overdoses" to mean overdoses that are 
5presumed to be self-administered by individuals and excludes any overdose that occurs 
6within a licensed health care facility.  Overdoses in licensed health care facilities are 
7currently subject to review as sentinel events under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 22, 
8chapter 1684.
9The amendment removes the authority of the panel to request and receive 
10confidential criminal history record information.
