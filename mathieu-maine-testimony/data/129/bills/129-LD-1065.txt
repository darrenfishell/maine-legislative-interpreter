2Sec. 1.  5 MRSA §285, sub-§7, ¶M is enacted to read:
3M.  For an employee in a seasonal or other regularly recurring position available only 
4for a portion of a year, the State shall pay a share of the individual premium for the 
5standard plan identified and offered by the commission as follows.
6(1) For an employee whose base annual rate of pay is projected to be less than or 
7equal to $30,000 on July 1st of the state fiscal year for which the premium 
8contribution is being determined, the State shall pay 95% of the individual 
9premium for the standard plan identified and offered by the commission 
10regardless of whether the employee is in active work status unless the employee 
11is covered by another health insurance plan.
12(2) For an employee whose base annual rate of pay is projected to be greater than 
13$30,000 and less than $80,000 on July 1st of the state fiscal year for which the 
14premium contribution is being determined, the State shall pay 90% of the 
15individual premium for the standard plan identified and offered by the 
16commission regardless of whether the employee is in active work status unless 
17the employee is covered by another health insurance plan.
18(3) For an employee whose base annual rate of pay is projected to be $80,000 or 
19greater on July 1st of the state fiscal year for which the premium contribution is 
20being determined, the State shall pay 85% of the individual premium for the 
21standard plan identified and offered by the commission regardless of whether the 
22employee is in active work status unless the employee is covered by another 
23health insurance plan.
24SUMMARY
25This bill requires the State to pay its share of the individual premium for the state 
26employee health insurance plan for a seasonal or session-only employee regardless of 
27whether the employee is in active work status unless the seasonal or session-only 
28employee has health coverage under another plan.
