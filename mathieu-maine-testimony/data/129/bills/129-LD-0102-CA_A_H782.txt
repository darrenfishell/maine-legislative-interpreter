8SUMMARY
9This amendment replaces the bill with a resolve and changes the title.  The 
10amendment requires the Department of Environmental Protection to study the 
11establishment of minimum recycled plastic content requirements for single-use plastic 
12beverage containers and develop findings and recommendations regarding such 
13requirements, including any proposed legislation.  The department is required to report 
14those findings and recommendations, including any proposed legislation, to the joint 
15standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over environment and natural 
16resources matters by January 15, 2021, and the committee is authorized to report out 
17legislation relating to that report.  The amendment also adds an appropriations and 
18allocations section.

Fiscal Detail and Notes
This bill requires the Department of Environmental Protecti on (DEP) to study the establishment of minimum recycled 
plastic content requirements for single-use plastic beverage  containers and to develop recommendations regarding the 
implementation of these requirements. DEP has indicated that it is unable to complete these directives within existing 
resources and would need to hire cont racted staffing to complete the study. The bill includes a one-time appropriation 
of $40,000 in fiscal year 2020-21 to DEP for contracted staffing services.  Fiscal Note129th MAINE LEGISLATURE
