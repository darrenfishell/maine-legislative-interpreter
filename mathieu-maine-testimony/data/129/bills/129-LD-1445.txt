21There is established the Debt-free Educational Opportunities for Maine Residents 
22program, administered by the authority and referred to in this chapter as "the program."

272.  Accepted as undergraduate.  Has been accepted for enrollment as an 
28undergraduate or is in good standing as an undergraduate at an eligible school in an 
29eligible program of study and has not received a baccalaureate degree;
303.  Applied for grants. Has applied for a grant under this chapter according to 
31schedules and procedures and on forms developed by the authority by rule; and
324.  State residency requirements.  Has been a state resident for at least one year.  To 
33establish residency, the person must provide:
34A.  Proof of attendance at a secondary school in the State;

27§12943.  Grant awards
28Beginning in fiscal year 2019-20, the authority shall provide an eligible student a 
29grant under this chapter in the amount of the cost of tuition and other expenses related to 
30the cost of attendance at an eligible school, including the cost of room and board, books, 
31supplies and fees, charged to a student enrolled in courses leading to completion of an 
32eligible program of study on at least a part-time basis at an eligible school in which the 
33eligible student is enrolled, less any gift aid the eligible student receives.
341.  Length of grant.  A grant is for the cost of tuition and other expenses related to 
35the cost of attendance at an eligible school, including the cost of room and board, books, 
36supplies and fees, charged to a student for a period not to exceed one academic year.  An 
37eligible student may apply for a grant each year during the period required for completion 
38of an eligible program of study being pursued by that student.

18§12945.  Rulemaking
19The authority shall adopt rules to implement this chapter.  Rules adopted pursuant to

21SUMMARY
22This bill establishes the Debt-free Educational Opportunities for Maine Residents 
23program.  The bill requires, beginning in fiscal year 2019-20, the Finance Authority of 
24Maine to provide eligible Maine residents a grant in the amount of the cost of tuition and 
25other expenses related to the cost of attendance at an eligible school, including the cost of 
26room and board, books, supplies and fees, charged to a student enrolled in courses 
27leading to completion of an eligible program of study on at least a part-time basis at a 
28public institution of higher education in the State in which the student is enrolled, less all 
29other gift aid.  An eligible program of study means a curriculum leading to a 
30baccalaureate degree, associate degree or certificate.  An eligible full-time or part-time 
31student may receive a grant for one academic year and may receive grants for the 
32equivalent of a 4-year baccalaureate program at the eligible school the student is 
33attending.
