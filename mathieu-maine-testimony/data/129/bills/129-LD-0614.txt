41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Battery electric vehicle" means a fully electric, zero-emission vehicle that has an 
7on-board electrical energy storage device that is designed to be recharged using an 
8external energy source.
9B.  "Eligible electric vehicle" means a battery electric vehicle that:
10(1)  Is sold or offered for sale in new condition by its original equipment 
11manufacturer or an authorized licensee of the original equipment manufacturer;
12(2)  Has a manufacturer's suggested retail price of not more than $50,000; and
13(3)  Is listed by the trust in accordance with subsection 4.
14C.  "Fund" means the Electric Vehicle Rebate Fund established in subsection 2.
15D.  "Program" means the program established pursuant to subsection 3.
162.  Fund established.  The Electric Vehicle Rebate Fund is established as a 
17nonlapsing fund administered by the trust to support the program.  Any interest earned on 
18funds in the fund must be credited to the fund, and funds not spent in any fiscal year 
19remain in the fund to be used in accordance with this section.  The trust may receive and 
20deposit in the fund funds from the following sources:
21A.  Federal funds and awards that may be used for the purposes of this section;
22B.  Any interest earned on investment of fund balances; and
23C.  Any other funds from public or private sources received in support of the 
24purposes for which the fund is established.
253.  Program established.  In accordance with the provisions of this section, the trust 
26shall establish and administer a program that, beginning July 1, 2020, allows a person 
27who purchases or leases an eligible electric vehicle to apply for and, as resources within 
28the fund allow, receive a direct rebate of $2,500 if the person meets the following 
29eligibility criteria:
30A.  The person is a resident of the State;
31B.  On or after July 1, 2020, the person purchases the eligible electric vehicle or 
32enters into a lease of the eligible electric vehicle with a term of 36 months or more.  
33A person who purchases or enters into a lease of an eligible electric vehicle outside of 
34the State is eligible for a direct rebate under this section as long as the person 
35registers the vehicle as a new vehicle with the State within the 3 months following the 
36date the person purchases or leases the vehicle and as long as the person meets all 
37other eligibility requirements under this section;
Page 2 - 129LR0862(01)-11C.  The person submits within the 3 months following the date the person purchases 
2or leases the eligible electric vehicle an application for the rebate using forms 
3developed and made available by the trust; and
4D.  The person certifies that the person intends to retain ownership, whether through 
5purchase or lease, of the eligible electric vehicle for at least 36 months from the date 
6the person purchases or leases the vehicle.  The trust may require a person who 
7receives a direct rebate under this section but who does not retain ownership of the 
8eligible electric vehicle for at least 36 months to repay to the trust up to the full 
9amount of the rebate. 
104.  List of eligible electric vehicles; applications.  The trust shall develop, make 
11available on its publicly accessible website and periodically update a list of eligible 
12electric vehicles included in the program.  The trust shall develop and make available at 
13its offices and on its publicly accessible website all forms and other documents necessary 
14for a person to apply for and receive a direct rebate under this section.
155.  Rules.  The trust may adopt rules to implement this section.  Rules adopted 
16pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, 
17subchapter 2-A.

30SUMMARY
31This bill establishes an electric vehicle rebate program to be administered by the 
32Efficiency Maine Trust that, beginning July 1, 2020, allows a person who purchases or 
33leases an eligible battery electric vehicle to apply for and, as resources allow, receive a 
34direct rebate of $2,500 if the person meets certain eligibility criteria.  The bill also 
35provides a General Fund appropriation of $500,000 to support the direct rebates to be 
36made available under the program.GENERAL FUND 2019-20 2020-21
