18SUMMARY
19This amendment replaces the bill and creates a temporary exception for employees 
20and students of schools and postsecondary educational institutions in the State to the 
21requirement that a person hold a Maine guide license to conduct certain outdoor 
22recreation trips.  As long as the educational institution's outdoor recreational trips 
23occurred as part of programming prior to this legislation, and as long as any outdoor 
24recreation trip conducted by the educational institution does not include fishing, hunting, 
25trapping, whitewater rafting or the use of snowmobiles or all-terrain vehicles, employees 
26and students of the educational institution do not require a Maine guide license.  The bill 
27establishes a task force of stakeholders consisting of representatives of schools, 
28postsecondary educational institutions and Maine guides and the Department of Inland 
29Fisheries and Wildlife to develop recommendations for a new certification as an 
30alternative to Maine guide licensing for outdoor recreation trips.
