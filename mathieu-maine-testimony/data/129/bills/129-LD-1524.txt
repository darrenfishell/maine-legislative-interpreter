41.  Wage theft; defined.  For the purposes of this section, "wage theft" means a 
5violation of section 621-A, 622, 623, 626, 629 or 629-A.
62.  Injunction; damages.  An employee, multiple similarly situated employees or the 
7Attorney General on behalf of an employee or multiple similarly situated employees may 
8file a civil action for injunctive relief, for any damages incurred and for any lost wages 
9and other benefits against an employer who commits wage theft.  If the employee, 
10employees or Attorney General prevails, the employer is liable to pay, in addition to the 
11unpaid wages or health benefits adjudged to be due, a reasonable rate of interest, costs of 
12suit including a reasonable attorney's fee and an additional amount equal to triple the 
13amount of unpaid wages as liquidated damages.
143.  Stop-work order.  On determination by the Commissioner of Labor or the 
15commissioner's designee that an employer is committing wage theft, the commissioner or 
16designee may issue a stop-work order to the employer.  The issuance of the stop-work 
17order constitutes final agency action.  The stop-work order is effective when served upon 
18the employer at the place of business or employment.  Any person who is aggrieved by 
19the imposition of a stop-work order has 10 days from the date of its service to make a 
20request to the commissioner or commissioner's designee for a hearing.
214.  Remedies in addition.  The injunction, damages and stop-work order available 
22under this section against an employer who commits wage theft are in addition to 
23penalties available under sections 626-A, 670, 670-A and 671, and any action under this 
24section may be combined with an action under section 626-A, 670, 670-A or 671.
25SUMMARY
26This bill addresses wage theft by an employer by creating additional remedies, 
27including injunctive relief, treble damages to be paid to affected employees and a 
28stop-work order against an employer who is engaging in wage theft.  These remedies are 
29in addition to any existing penalties, and the actions may be combined.
30"Wage theft" is defined as a violation of specific statutes that include the 
31requirements of timely and full payment of wages, payment of wages upon cessation of 
32employment, payments defined as "unfair agreements" and fringe benefits.
