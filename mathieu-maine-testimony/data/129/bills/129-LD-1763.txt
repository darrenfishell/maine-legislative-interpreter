9Sec. 5.  12 MRSA §6085, as repealed by PL 2017, c. 94, §5, is reenacted to read:
10§6085.  Marine organism aquaculture license
111.  License required.  The commissioner may require a license for aquaculture of 
12marine organisms in facilities that are not located in the coastal waters of the State but are 
13located in the State.
142.  Licensed activities.  The holder of a license under this section may possess 
15marine organisms the holder has raised by means of aquaculture.  The holder of such a 
16license is exempt from any requirement regarding the time of taking or possessing, 
17minimum or maximum length or other minimum or maximum size requirement for any 
18marine organism the holder has raised by means of aquaculture.
193.  Permit denial.  The commissioner may refuse to issue a license under this section 
20if the commissioner finds that the aquaculture activity presents an unreasonable risk to 
21indigenous marine life or its environment.  In determining whether or not to refuse to 
22issue a license, the commissioner shall consider factors, including, but not limited to:
23A.  Risk of accidental or intentional introduction of marine organisms or marine 
24organism products into the coastal waters of the State;
25B.  Risk of the introduction or spread of disease within the State; and
26C.  Interference with the enforcement of possession, size or season limits for wild 
27marine organisms.
284.  Monitoring and revocation.  The commissioner shall monitor licensed facilities 
29under this section on an annual basis.  If the commissioner determines following an 
30annual review or at any other time that the licensed aquaculture activity presents an 
31unreasonable risk to indigenous marine life or its environment, the commissioner may 
32revoke the license after the licensee has been given an opportunity for a hearing before 
33the department.
345.  Reporting.  The commissioner may require the holder of a license under this 
35section to file periodic reports regarding the aquaculture practices and production of the 
36facility.  Information obtained pursuant to this provision is considered fisheries statistics 
37for the purposes of section 6173, except that information about marine organism health 
38reported pursuant to section 6071 may not be considered fisheries statistics.

8SUMMARY
9This bill transfers authority for the licensing of land-based aquaculture from the 
10Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to the Department of Marine 
11Resources.
