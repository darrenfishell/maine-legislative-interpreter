6(1)  Completed at least 2,000 work hours in the field of electrical installations as 
7a licensed helper electrician under the direct supervision of a master electrician, 
8journeyman electrician or limited electrician; graduated from an accredited 
9community college electrical program or a vocational-electrical program of a 
10state department of corrections or completed a career and technical electrical 
11education program approved pursuant to Title 20-A, section 8306-B as a 
12secondary student; and completed a course of 45 hours in the current National 
13Electrical Code; or
14(2)  Comparable work experience or education or training, or a combination of 
15work experience, education and training, completed within the State or outside 
16the State, that is acceptable to the board.
17SUMMARY
18This bill allows the Electricians' Examining Board to accept satisfactory evidence of 
19completion of a career and technical electrical education program approved pursuant to 
20the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, section 8306-B as a secondary student when 
21issuing a journeyman-in-training electrician license.
