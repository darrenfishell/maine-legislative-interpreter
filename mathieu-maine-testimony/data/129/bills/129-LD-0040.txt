14Sec. 1.  Commission established.  Resolved:  That the Commission To Study 
15Children's Mental Health, referred to in this resolve as "the commission," is established.
16Sec. 2.  Commission membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
17353, the commission consists of 11 members appointed as follows:
181.  Two members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including a 
19member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
202.  Two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
21House, including members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
22in the Legislature;
233.  Two parents of children with mental health issues, one appointed by the President 
24of the Senate and one appointed by the Speaker of the House;
254.  Three representatives of providers of mental health services to children in the 
26State, 2 appointed by the President of the Senate and one appointed by the Speaker of the 
27House;
285.  The Commissioner of Health and Human Services or the commissioner's designee; 
29and
306.  The Commissioner of Education or the commissioner's designee.
31Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate 
32chair and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
33commission.
34Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
35appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
36resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
Page 2 - 129LR0453(01)-11Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
2the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
3after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
4made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority 
5for the commission to meet and conduct its business.
6Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall study the mental health of 
7children in the State and federal and state laws, regulations, rules and policies governing 
8the diagnosis and treatment of children with mental health issues in the State.
9Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human 
10Services shall provide necessary staffing services to the commission.
11Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 4, 2019, the commission 
12shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
13legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
14Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
15legislation takes effect when approved.
16SUMMARY
17This resolve establishes the Commission To Study Children's Mental Health to study 
18the mental health of children in the State and federal and state laws, regulations, rules and 
19policies governing the diagnosis and treatment of children with mental health issues.
