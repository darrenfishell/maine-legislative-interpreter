COMMITTEE AMENDMENT 
COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A" to H.P. 910, L.D. 1249 
1 SUMMARY 
2 This amendment replaces the bill and provides a new title. The amendment adds new 
3 variants to the crime of endangering the welfare of a dependent person. The new variants 
4 are the Class D crime of recklessly infringing on a dependent person's rights of 
5 association, including but not limited to the right to receive visitors, mail or telephone or 
6 electronic communication, for the purpose of establishing or maintaining undue influence 
7 over that person and the Class C crime of intentionally and knowingly infringing on a 
8 dependent person's rights of association for the purpose of establishing or maintaining 
9 undue influence over that person. The amendment also provides a definition for "undue 
10 influence."

Correctional and Judicial Impact Statements 
Changes certain definitions of crimes; minor increases to conectional and judicial costs. 
The collection of additional fine revenue will increase General Fund and dedicated revenue by minor amounts. 
Fiscal Detail and Notes 
The bill adds to the crime of endangering the welfare of a dependent person. It is anticipated that this modification 
will not increase the number of cases or incarcerated individuals by significant amounts and any increases in costs to 
the judicial branch, the Department of Corrections, the Office of the Attorney General, and the Maine Commission on 
Indigent Legal Services can be absorbed within existing resources.
