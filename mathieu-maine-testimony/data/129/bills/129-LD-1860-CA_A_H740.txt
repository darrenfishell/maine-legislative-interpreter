23 §4710-C. Establishment or substantial expansion of mandated instruction or 
24 training 
25 A joint standing committee of the Legislature that considers proposed legislation to 
26 establish mandated instruction or training for students in elementary or secondary school 
27 not required under this Title or rules adopted pursuant to this Title or to substantially 
28 expand mandated instruction or training for students in elementary or secondary school 
29 shall evaluate the proposed legislation in accordance with this section. 
30 1. Request to commissioner. Upon the referral to a joint standing committee of the 
31 Legislature of legislation that proposes new or substantially expanded mandated 
32 instruction or training, the joint standing committee shall hold a public hearing and

29 standing committee of the Legislature that requested the review pursuant to this section

2. It requires the joint standing committee of the Legislature that considers proposed 
legislation that would establish or substantially expand mandated instruction or training 
for students in elementary or secondary school to hold a public hearing on the proposal 
and then either vote to request that the Commissioner of Education convene the Review 
Committee on Proposed Legislation to review the proposal and report to the joint 
standing committee by a specific date chosen in consultation with the Department of 
Education or hold a work session to work and vote on the proposed legislation. 
3. It requires the Commissioner of Education to appoint the Review Committee on 
Proposed Legislation's members, including teachers, administrators and content 
specialists within the Department of Education and a representative of an association of 
statewide curriculum leaders, and requires the review committee to invite the sponsor of 
the proposed legislation to participate in its evaluation. 
4. It adds that, as part of the evaluation, the Review Committee on Proposed 
Legislation must analyze the positive and negative effects of the proposed legislation and 
the costs to implement the proposal. 
5. It requires the Commissioner of Education or the commissioner's designee to 
present the final report of the Review Committee on Proposed Legislation to the joint 
standing committee.
