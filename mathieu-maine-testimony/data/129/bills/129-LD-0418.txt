3CHAPTER 17
4INTERSTATE COMPACT:  AGREEMENT AMONG THE STATES TO ELECT 
5THE PRESIDENT OF THE UNITED STATES BY NATIONAL POPULAR VOTE
6§1301.  Membership - Article 1
7Any state of the United States and the District of Columbia may become a member of 
8this agreement by enacting this agreement.
9§1302.  Right of people in member states to vote for President and Vice President - 
10Article 2
11Each member state shall conduct a statewide popular election for President and Vice 
12President of the United States.
13§1303.  Manner of appointing presidential electors in member states - Article 3
14Prior to the time set by law for the meeting of and voting by the presidential electors, 
15the chief election official of each member state shall determine the number of votes for 
16each presidential slate in each state of the United States and in the District of Columbia in 
17which votes have been cast in a statewide popular election and shall add such votes 
18together to produce a national popular vote total for each presidential slate.
19The chief election official of each member state shall designate the presidential slate 
20with the largest national popular vote total as the national popular vote winner.
21The presidential elector certifying official of each member state shall certify the 
22appointment in that official's own state of the elector slate nominated in that state in 
23association with the national popular vote winner.
24At least 6 days before the day fixed by law for the meeting of and voting by the 
25presidential electors, each member state shall make a final determination of the number of 
26popular votes cast in the state for each presidential slate and shall communicate an 
27official statement of such determination within 24 hours to the chief election official of 
28each other member state.
29The chief election official of each member state shall treat as conclusive an official 
30statement containing the number of popular votes in a state for each presidential slate 
31made by the day established by federal law for making a state's final determination 
32conclusive as to the counting of electoral votes by Congress.
33In the event of a tie for the national popular vote winner, the presidential elector 
34certifying official of each member state shall certify the appointment of the elector slate 
35nominated in association with the presidential slate receiving the largest number of 
36popular votes within that official's own state.

12§1304.  Contingent effective date; withdrawal; notification; severability - Article 4
13This agreement takes effect when states cumulatively possessing a majority of the 
14electoral votes have enacted this agreement in substantially the same form and the 
15enactments by such states have taken effect in each state.
16Any member state may withdraw from this agreement, except that a withdrawal 
17occurring 6 months or less before the end of a President's term does not become effective 
18until a President or Vice President has been qualified to serve the next term.
19The chief executive of each member state shall promptly notify the chief executive of 
20all other states when this agreement has been enacted and has taken effect in that official's 
21state, when the state has withdrawn from this agreement and when this agreement takes 
22effect generally.
23This agreement terminates if the electoral college is abolished.
24If any provision of this agreement is held invalid, the remaining provisions are not 
25affected.
26§1305.  Definitions - Article 5
27As used in this agreement, the following terms have the following meanings.
281.  Chief election official.  "Chief election official" means the state official or body 
29that is authorized to certify the total number of popular votes for each presidential slate.
302.  Chief executive.  "Chief executive" means the governor of a state of the United 
31States or the Mayor of the District of Columbia.
323.  Elector slate.  "Elector slate" means a slate of candidates who have been 
33nominated in a state for the position of presidential elector in association with a 
34presidential slate.
354.  Presidential elector.  "Presidential elector" means an elector for President and 
36Vice President of the United States.

46.  Presidential slate.  "Presidential slate" means a slate of 2 persons, the first of 
5whom has been nominated as a candidate for President of the United States and the 2nd 
6of whom has been nominated as a candidate for Vice President of the United States, or

12a statewide basis.
13Sec. 2.  Legislative intent.  The text and numbering of the Interstate Compact:  
14Agreement among the States To Elect the President of the United States by National 
15Popular Vote have been changed to conform to Maine statutory conventions.  The 
16changes are technical in nature, and it is the intent of the Legislature that this Act be 
17interpreted as substantively the same as the original interstate compact.
18SUMMARY
19This bill proposes to adopt an interstate compact to elect the President of the United 
20States by national popular vote.  Under the compact, the presidential candidate who 
21receives the most popular votes in all 50 states and the District of Columbia is elected 
22President.  Under the compact, all of a state's electoral votes would be awarded to the 
23presidential candidate who receives the most popular votes in all 50 states and the District 
24of Columbia.  This bill takes effect only if enacted by states possessing a majority of the 
25electoral votes, that is, enough electoral votes to elect a President, which is 270 of 538.
