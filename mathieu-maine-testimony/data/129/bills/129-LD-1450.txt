Page 1 - 129LR0492(01)-11Sec. 1.  Pilot project.  Resolved:  That, beginning January 1, 2020 and ending 
2December 1, 2021, the Department of Health and Human Services shall establish a pilot 
3project to provide housing to adults with intellectual disabilities or autism spectrum 
4disorder who are on the waiting list for home and community-based benefits provided 
5under the department's rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter II, Section 
621, Home and Community Benefits for Members with Intellectual Disabilities or Autism 
7Spectrum Disorder.  The pilot project must be modeled on a program operated in the 
8Town of Kittery by the nonprofit corporation A House for ME.  The pilot project must 
9provide housing to 2 to 3 residents in each home and, with the assistance of community 
10volunteers, assist those residents in becoming active, contributing members of the 
11community.  Housing provided under the pilot project must be staffed by a volunteer 
12resident companion who lives at the house rent-free and is employed outside of the house.
13Sec. 2.  Funding.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services 
14may receive funds from any public or private source for the purposes for which the pilot 
15project under section 1 has been established.
16Sec. 3.  Report.  Resolved:  That, by December 1, 2021, the Department of Health 
17and Human Services shall submit a written report on the results of the pilot project under 
18section 1, including findings and recommendations, to the joint standing committee of the 
19Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters.  The joint 
20standing committee may submit a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 130th 
21Legislature relating to the subject matter of the report.
22SUMMARY
23This resolve establishes a 2-year pilot project to provide housing to adults with 
24intellectual disabilities or autism spectrum disorder who are on the waiting list for home 
25and community-based benefits provided under the Department of Health and Human 
26Services rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter II, Section 21.  The pilot 
27project must be modeled on a program operated in the Town of Kittery by A House for 
28ME and must provide housing to 2 to 3 residents in each home and, with the assistance of 
29community volunteers, assist those residents in becoming active, contributing members of 
30the community.
