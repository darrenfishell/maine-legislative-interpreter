10§1805.  Maine Coastal Risks and Hazards Commission
111.  Establishment and purpose.  The Maine Coastal Risks and Hazards 
12Commission, referred to in this section as "the commission," established by Title 5, 
13section 12004-I, subsection 24-G, is created for the purpose of developing and 
14recommending objectives, strategies, legislation and rules and recommending other 
15actions to address and prepare for coastal and coastal watershed hazards identified by the 
16commission, including, but not limited to, increased storm surges, extreme precipitation 
17and other extreme weather events, projected sea level rise and increased river flooding 
18and storm water runoff and the risks such hazards pose to municipalities, the coastal 
19economy and state assets.
202.  Membership.  The commission consists of the following 21 members:

23House of Representatives;

25D.  The Commissioner of Transportation or the commissioner's designee;

27within the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry or the director's 
28designee;
29F.  The director of the Maine coastal zone management program within the 
30Department of Marine Resources or the director's designee;
31G.  A representative of a greater Portland council of governments, appointed by the 
32Speaker of the House of Representatives upon the recommendation of the council;

283.  Chairs.  At the first meeting of the commission, the commission shall select a 
29chair from among its members and may select a new chair annually.
304.  Staffing.  The Legislative Council shall provide staff support for the operation of 
31the commission, except that Legislative Council staff support is not authorized when the 
32Legislature is in regular or special session.  In addition, the commission may contract for 
33administrative, professional and clerical services if funding permits.
345.  Quorum.  For purposes of holding a meeting, a quorum is a simple majority of all 
35members appointed to the commission under subsection 2.  A quorum must be present to 
36start a meeting or to vote but not to continue or adjourn a meeting.
376.  Terms; vacancies.  Members of the commission serve for a term of 2 years and 
38may be reappointed.  A vacancy must be filled in the same manner as the original 
Page 3 - 129LR0411(01)-11appointment.  Members may continue to serve until their replacements are designated.  A 
2member may designate an alternate to serve on a temporary basis.
37.  Consultation.  Whenever the commission considers it appropriate, the 
4commission may seek the advice of experts in fields related to its duties.
58.  Powers and duties.  The commission shall:
6A.  Meet at least 4 times annually.  The Executive Director of the Legislative Council 
7shall call the first meeting of the commission as soon as funding permits and all 
8appointments have been made;
9B.  Review the best available science relating to current and future coastal and coastal 
10watershed hazards, including, but not limited to, increased storm surges, extreme 
11precipitation and other extreme weather events, projected sea level rise and increased 
12river flooding and storm water runoff. The commission shall make available on a 
13publicly accessible website all publications and information reviewed or compiled for 
14review by the commission;
15C.  Identify coastal assets that are vulnerable to current and future coastal and coastal 
16watershed hazards and determine the risks to be considered by the commission in 
17developing a set of objectives and recommendations for action under paragraphs D 
18and E;
19D.  Develop or identify a set of objectives, actionable strategies and best practices to 
20protect, adapt and sustain coastal assets that are vulnerable to current and future 
21coastal and coastal watershed hazards identified by the commission; and
22E.  Develop recommended legislation, rules and other actions in accordance with the 
23set of objectives, strategies and best practices developed or identified under 
24paragraph D to reduce the vulnerability of coastal assets and to adapt to current and 
25future coastal and coastal watershed hazards identified by the commission.
269.  Report and recommendations.  On or before November 1, 2022, and every 4 
27years thereafter, the commission shall submit a report to the joint standing committee of 
28the Legislature having jurisdiction over environmental and natural resources matters 
29containing its recommendations, including draft legislation, regarding actions to be taken 
30by the State to address and prepare for coastal and coastal watershed hazards identified by 
31the commission.
3210.  Compensation.  Legislative members of the commission are entitled to receive 
33the legislative per diem, as defined in Title 3, section 2, and reimbursement for travel and 
34other necessary expenses for their attendance at authorized meetings of the commission.  
35Public members not otherwise compensated by their employers or other entities that they 
36represent are entitled to receive reimbursement of necessary expenses and, upon a 
37determination of financial hardship, a per diem equal to the legislative per diem for their 
38attendance at authorized meetings of the commission.
3911.  Outside funding.  The commission may seek outside funding to fund the cost of 
40public hearings, conferences, workshops, other meetings and other activities of the 
41commission.  All contributions are subject to approval by the Legislative Council in 
Page 4 - 129LR0411(01)-11accordance with its policies.  All funds accepted must be forwarded to the Executive 
2Director of the Legislative Council along with an accounting record that includes the 
3amount of funds, the date the funds were received, from whom the funds were received 
4and the purpose of and any limitation on the use of those funds.  The executive director 
5shall administer any funds received by the commission and accepted as approved by the 
6Legislative Council.
7SUMMARY
8This bill establishes the Maine Coastal Risks and Hazards Commission, the purpose 
9of which is to develop and submit legislative recommendations regarding the actions to 
10be taken by the State to address and prepare for coastal and coastal watershed hazards 
11identified by the commission, including, but not limited to, increased storm surges, 
12extreme precipitation and other extreme weather events, projected sea level rise and 
13increased river flooding and storm water runoff.  On or before November 1, 2022, and 
14every 4 years thereafter, the commission must submit a report to the joint standing 
15committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over environmental and natural 
16resources matters containing its recommendations, including any draft legislation, to 
17address and prepare for identified coastal and coastal watershed hazards.
