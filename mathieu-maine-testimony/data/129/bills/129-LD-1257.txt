3SUMMARY
4This bill authorizes the Secretary of State to require the owner of an electric vehicle 
5to pay an electric vehicle registration surcharge of $25 per year when at least 3,000 
6electric vehicles are annually registered in the State.  The amount of the surcharge 
7increases to $50 per year when at least 10,000 electric vehicles are annually registered in 
8the State.  The municipality that collects the annual electric vehicle registration surcharge 
9is required to expend no less than 50% of the surcharge to construct or modify public 
10infrastructure that facilitates the charging of electric vehicles for use by the general public 
11or for other purposes that encourage or support the purchase and use by the general public 
12of electric vehicles.
13The bill also directs the Governor's Energy Office to convene an electric vehicle task 
14force, which is charged with reviewing a number of matters relating to electric vehicles 
15and electric vehicle infrastructure.  The director of the office is required to report by 
16February 15, 2020 to the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and Technology 
17and the Joint Standing Committee on Transportation regarding any recommendations of 
18the task force, including proposed legislation.  After reviewing the report, the committees 
19may report out legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
20The bill also directs the Commissioner of Transportation to take a number of actions 
21regarding short-term and long-term planning for road infrastructure and highway funding, 
22including a review of potential new vehicle or road use fees.  The commissioner is 
23directed to report by February 15, 2021 to the joint standing committees of the 
24Legislature having jurisdiction over energy, utilities and technology matters and 
25transportation matters regarding those actions and including any findings and 
26recommendations and proposed legislation necessary to implement those 
27recommendations.  After reviewing the report, the committees may report out legislation 
28to the First Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
