4Constitution, Art. II, §4 is amended to read:
5Section 4.  Time of state election; absentee voting.  The election of Senators

7biennially forever and the election of, except as provided in this section.  Beginning with

9the Tuesday following the first Monday of November every 4 years.  The Legislature 
10under proper enactment shall authorize and provide for voting by citizens of the State 
11absent therefrom in the Armed Forces of the United States or of this State and for voting 
12by other citizens absent or physically incapacitated for reasons deemed sufficient.
13Constitution, Art. IV, Pt. First, §2 is amended to read:
14Section 2.  Number of Representatives; biennial terms; division of the 
15State into districts for House of Representatives.  The House of Representatives 
16shall consist of 151 members, to be elected by the qualified electors, and hold their office 
172 years from the day next preceding the first Wednesday in December following the 
18general election.  Beginning in 2022, a person may not serve more than 6 consecutive 
19terms as a member of the House of Representatives.  The Legislature which convenes in 
202013, and also the Legislature which convenes in 2021 and every 10th year thereafter, 
21shall cause the State to be divided into districts for the choice of one Representative for 
22each district.  The number of Representatives shall be divided into the number of 
23inhabitants of the State exclusive of foreigners not naturalized according to the latest 
24Federal Decennial Census or a State Census previously ordered by the Legislature to 
25coincide with the Federal Decennial Census, to determine a mean population figure for 
26each Representative District.  Each Representative District shall be formed of contiguous 
27and compact territory and shall cross political subdivision lines the least number of times 
28necessary to establish as nearly as practicable equally populated districts.  Whenever the 
29population of a municipality entitles it to more than one district, all whole districts shall 
30be drawn within municipal boundaries.  Any population remainder within the 
31municipality shall be included in a district with contiguous territory and shall be kept 
32intact.
33Constitution, Art. IV, Pt. Second, §§1 and 5 are amended to read:
34Section 1.  Number of Senators; terms.  The Senate shall consist of an odd 
35number of Senators, not less than 31 nor more than 35, elected at the same time and for 
36the same term as Representatives by the qualified electors of the districts into which the 
37State shall be from time to time divided except that, beginning with the general election 
38held in 2022, Senators are elected as provided in Article II, Section 4.  Beginning in 2022, 
39a person may not serve more than 3 consecutive terms as a member of the Senate.
40Section 5.  Determination of Senators elected; procedure for filling 
41vacancies.  The Senate shall, on said first Wednesday of December, biennially until the

8Constitutional referendum procedure; form of question; effective date. 
9Resolved:  That the municipal officers of this State shall notify the inhabitants of their 
10respective cities, towns and plantations to meet, in the manner prescribed by law for 
11holding a statewide election, at a statewide election held in the month of November 
12following the passage of this resolution, to vote upon the ratification of the amendment 
13proposed in this resolution by voting upon the following question:
14"Do you favor amending the Constitution of Maine to change the term of 
15office for State Senators from 2 years to 4 years and change the number 
16of consecutive years members of the State Senate and House of 
17Representatives may serve from 8 years to 12 years?"
18The legal voters of each city, town and plantation shall vote by ballot on this question 
19and designate their choice by a cross or check mark placed within the corresponding 
20square below the word "Yes" or "No."  The ballots must be received, sorted, counted and 
21declared in open ward, town and plantation meetings and returns made to the Secretary of 
22State in the same manner as votes for members of the Legislature.  The Governor shall 
23review the returns.  If it appears that a majority of the legal votes are cast in favor of the 
24amendment, the Governor shall proclaim that fact without delay and the amendment 
25becomes part of the Constitution of Maine on the date of the proclamation.
26Secretary of State shall prepare ballots. Resolved: That the Secretary of State 
27shall prepare and furnish to each city, town and plantation all ballots, returns and copies 
28of this resolution necessary to carry out the purposes of this referendum.
29SUMMARY
30This resolution proposes to amend the Constitution of Maine to change the term of 
31office for State Senators from 2 years to 4 years and change the number of consecutive 
32years members of the State Senate and House of Representatives may serve from 8 years 
33to 12 years.
