'Sec. 2. 38 MRSA c. 3, sub-c. 2-C is enacted to read: 
SUBCHAPTER 2-C 
PROHIBITION ON OIL AND NATURAL GAS EXPLORATION, 
DEVELOPMENT AND PRODUCTION 
§570-AA. Definitions 
As used in this subchapter, unless the context othe1wise indicates, the following 
terms have the following meanings. 
1. Development. 11Development11 means the activities conducted subsequent to the 
exploration for and discovery of oil or natural gas resources, but prior to the production of 
those resources, to facilitate the production of those resources, including. but not limited 
to, geophysical activities, drilling. platform construction, pipeline construction and the 
operation of all onshore support facilities specifically constrncted or designed to support 
those activities. 
2. Exploration, 11Exploration11 means the activities conducted to locate oil or natural 
gas resources. prior to the development or production of those resources. including, but 
not limited to, the drilling of wells for the purpose of locating and determining the size 
and scope of those resources. 
3. Federal waters. "Federal waters" means those waters and submerged lands lying 
seaward to the waters of the State that are subject to federal jurisdiction and control. 
4. Oil terminal facility. "Oil terminal facility" has the same meaning as in section 
542, subsection 7.

24 SUMMARY 
25 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, amends the bill to 
26 clarify the prohibition on oil or natural gas exploration, development or production 
27 activities as follows. 
28 1. It amends certain definitions to more clearly delineate the activities regulated and 
29 to specify that the transfer of oil or natural gas resources to or from the waters of the 
30 State, including both onloading and offloading of oil or natural gas resources between an 
31 oil terminal facility and a vessel or between vessels, is not prohibited under the bill. 
32 2. It removes language specifically prohibiting exploration, development or 
33 production activities that may adversely affect the waters of the State.
