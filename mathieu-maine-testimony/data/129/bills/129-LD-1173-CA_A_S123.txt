22 SUMMARY 
23 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment allows an entity that generates 
24 electricity to construct a transmission line for the purpose of interconnecting and 
25 distributing electricity to serve a commercial or industrial consumer without becoming 
26 subject to regulation by the Public Utilities Commission as a transmission and 
27 distribution utility or a competitive electricity provider when the commercial or industrial 
28 consumer is located on the property where the generator is located; on abutting property; 
29 or on a commercial or industrial site served by the generator or its predecessor without 
30 using the transmission and distribution plant of a public utility prior to December 31, 
31 2018. The amendment requires the Public Utilities Commission to report every 3 years, 
32 beginning in 2022, on the incidence of direct sales of electricity by · an entity that 
33 generates electricity to commercial or industrial consumers in the instances allowed under 
34 this amendment. Finally, the amendment clarifies that this legislation does not modify or 
35 nullify the framework and precedent for analyzing when an entity is a transmission and 
36 distribution utility or a competitive electricity provider established by the Public Utilities 
37 Commission in related cases.
