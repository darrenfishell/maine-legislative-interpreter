9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT " fr" to S.P. 331, L.D. 1099, Bill, "An Act To 
10 Reduce Suicides and Violent Crimes by Requiring a 72-hour Waiting Period after the 
11 Sale of a Firearm" 
12 Amend the bill in section 1 in §2015 by striking out all of subsection 4 (page 1, lines 
13 20 to 29 in L.D.) and inserting the following: 
14 '4. Application. This section does not apply to: 
15 A. The sale of a firearm if the seller knows that the person to whom the seller is 
16 selling the firearm is: 
17 (1) A law enforcement officer as defined by Title 17-A, section 2, subsection 17; 
18 (2) A corrections officer as defined by section 2801-A, subsection 2; or 
19 (3) A person who is employed by a contract security company or proprietary 
20 security organization as defined by Title 32, section 9403 or a person who is 
21 licensed as a private security guard under Title 32, chapter 93; or 
22 B. The sale of a firearm to a firearm dealer. 
23 A firearm dealer, as defined in 18 United States Code, Section 921, is subject to the 
24 requirements of conducting national instant criminal background checks under 18 United 
25 States Code, Section 922(t).' 
26 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or 
27 section number to read consecutively. 
28 SUMMARY 
29 This amendment is the minority report of the committee. The amendment provides 
30 cross-references to define the listed professions of persons who are exempt from the law 
31 and adds corrections officers to the list.
