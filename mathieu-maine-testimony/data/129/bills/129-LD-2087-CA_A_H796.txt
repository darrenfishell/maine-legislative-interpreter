25SUMMARY
26Like the bill, the amendment prohibits an employer from requesting criminal history 
27record information on an initial employee application form or stating on an initial 
28employee application form or advertisement or specifying prior to determining a person is 
29otherwise qualified for the position that a person with a criminal history may not apply or 
30will not be considered for a position.
31The amendment provides exceptions to those prohibitions, including instances in 
32which federal or state law or regulation or rule mandates for a position that a criminal 
33conviction disqualifies an applicant, or imposes an obligation on an employer not to hire 
34an applicant who has been convicted of a certain type of offense, or requires that an 
35employer conduct a criminal history record check.
36An employer who violates this prohibition is subject to a penalty of not less than 
37$100 nor more than $500 for each violation, to be enforced by the Department of Labor.
