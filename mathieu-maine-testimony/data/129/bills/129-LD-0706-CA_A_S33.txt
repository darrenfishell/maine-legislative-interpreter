14 §3174-BBB. Medical nutrition therapy; rules 
15 The department shall provide for the reimbursement under the MaineCare program 
16 for medical nutrition therapy services to treat any medical diagnosis, including, but not 
17 limited to, obesity, unhealthy weight, hypertension, diabetes, prediabetes, disorders of 
18 lipid metabolism, HIV infection, unintended weight loss in older adults, chronic kidney 
19 disease, gastrointestinal conditions, malnutrition and inappropriate eating habits. Medical 
20 nutrition therapy services may be provided by a physician licensed pursuant to Title 32, 
21 chapter 36 or 48, a physician assistant licensed pursuant to Title 32, chapter 36 or 48, a 
22 dietitian licensed pursuant to Title 32, chapter 104 or a nurse practitioner licensed 
23 pursuant to Title 32, chapter 31 in any office, facility or other setting in which the 
24 physician, physician assistant, dietitian or nurse practitioner practices. 
25 As used in this section, "medical nutrition therapy" means a therapeutic approach to 
26 treating medical conditions and their associated symptoms by use of counseling and a 
27 specifically tailored diet devised and monitored by a physician licensed pursuant to Title 
28 32, chapter 36 or 48, a physician assistant licensed pursuant to Title 32, chapter 36 or 48, 
29 a dietitian licensed pursuant to Title 32, chapter 104 or a nurse practitioner licensed 
30 pursuant to Title 32, chapter 31. 
31 The department shall adopt rules to implement this section. Rules adopted pursuant 
32 to this paragraph are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 
33 2-A. .

16 SUMMARY 
17 This amendment, which is the unanimous report of the committee, amends the bill by 
18 changing the term "medical nutritional therapy" to "medical nutrition therapy" to 
19 accurately reflect common usage of the term. It adds physician assistants to the group of 
20 medical providers authorized to provide medical nutrition therapy that is reimbursable 
21 under MaineCare. It clarifies that medical nutrition therapy is reimbursable by 
22 MaineCare in any setting in which the authorized medical provider practices. It also 
23 removes the provisions of the bill that provide for the reimbursement under the 
24 MaineCare program for certain prescription drugs to treat obesity.
