9amended to read:
102-A.  Children under 2 years of age.  When a child who is less than 2 years of age 
11is being transported in a motor vehicle that is required by the United States Department of 
12Transportation to be equipped with seat belts, the operator shall ensure that the child is 
13properly secured in a rear-facing child restraint system or convertible child restraint

15system manufacturer's instructions and the vehicle manufacturer's instructions, except if 
16the child is in a convertible child restraint system and the child exceeds the manufacturer

18properly secured in a forward-facing position in accordance with the child restraint 
19system manufacturer's instructions and the vehicle manufacturer's instructions.  Violation 
20of this subsection is a traffic infraction for which a fine of $50 for the first offense, $125

22imposed under this subsection may not be suspended by the court.

26child who is 2 years of age or older and who weighs less than 55 pounds is being

Page 2 - 129LR2753(01)-11Sec. 5.  29-A MRSA §2081, sub-§4, ¶A-2 is enacted to read:
2A-2.  The requirements of subsections 2-A, 2-B and 3 do not apply if a child 
3passenger has a medical condition that, in the opinion of a physician, nurse 
4practitioner, physician assistant or child passenger safety technician with special 
5needs training, necessitates that a different child restraint system be used to improve 
6the safety of the child.  An opinion rendered pursuant to this paragraph must be made 
7in writing by the physician, nurse practitioner, physician assistant or child passenger 
8safety technician with special needs training and must explain the basis of the 
9opinion.
10SUMMARY
11This bill amends the laws governing motor vehicle child restraint systems to provide 
12exceptions for children who exceed a manufacturer's recommended height limit and for 
13children who have a medical condition that necessitates a different child restraint system.
