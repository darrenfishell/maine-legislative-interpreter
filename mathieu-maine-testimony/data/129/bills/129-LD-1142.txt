2become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, preparation of amendments to federal waivers under the Social Security 
4Act are labor intensive and require time to prepare; and
5Whereas, a stakeholder group to develop a plan for transportation services for the 
6elderly must begin as soon as possible in order to have sufficient time to develop a report 
7to be acted on during the next legislative session; and
8Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
9the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
10immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
11therefore, be it
12Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to amend the Section 
1319 waiver.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services shall seek 
14to amend the existing 1915(c) waiver to allow individuals receiving services under the 
15department's rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Section 19 to receive 
16transportation services necessary to access services specified in an individual's service 
17plan, in addition to any currently permissible medical transportation, when the individual 
18has no other means of transportation.  The department shall submit a waiver amendment 
19request to the United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for 
20Medicare and Medicaid Services no later than October 1, 2019.
21Sec. 2.  Rulemaking.  Resolved:  That, upon approval of the waiver amendment 
22pursuant to section 1, the department shall amend its rule Chapter 101: MaineCare 
23Benefits Manual, Section 19 to allow for the transportation services allowed by the 
24amendment to the waiver.  Rules adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical 
25rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.  The department shall submit a 
26progress report on the waiver application and rulemaking to the Joint Standing 
27Committee on Health and Human Services  no later than January 1, 2020.  
28Sec. 3.  Department of Health and Human Services to study nonmedical 
29transportation for seniors.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human 
30Services shall convene a stakeholder group to develop a plan to provide nonmedical 
31transportation services to travel to destinations to meet basic needs to persons who are 61 
32years of age or older with no other means of transportation and who are MaineCare 
33members or receive state-funded services under the department's rule Chapter 5: Office of 
34Elder Services Policy Manual, Section 63.  The department shall submit a report with a 
35proposed plan including funding estimates and sources of funding as well as any 
36suggested legislation to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services no 
37later than January 30, 2020.
38Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
39legislation takes effect when approved.

2This emergency resolve requires the Department of Health and Human Services to 
3submit an amendment request to the United States Department of Health and Human 
4Services, Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services to the 1915(c) waiver that provides 
5services to the elderly under the department's rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits 
6Manual, Section 19 to allow for coverage of transportation services required to access 
7services specified in the individual's service plan that are nonmedical in nature.  The 
8waiver request must be submitted no later than October 1, 2019.  Upon approval, the 
9department shall amend its rules to cover the new service.  The department shall submit a 
10progress report to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services regarding 
11the waiver request and rulemaking.  The resolve also requires the Department of Health 
12and Human Services to convene a stakeholder group to develop a plan to provide 
13nonmedical transportation services to travel to destinations to meet basic needs to persons 
14who are 61 years of age or older with no other means of transportation and who are 
15MaineCare members or receive state-funded services under the department's rule Chapter 
165: Office of Elder Services Policy Manual, Section 63.  The plan must be submitted to the 
17Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services no later than January 30, 2020.
