9Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the committee shall study and develop 
10recommendations to address guardianship, conservatorship and authorization of 
11transaction challenges that result in extended hospitalization of patients clinically 
12qualified for discharge from a hospital.
13Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human 
14Services shall provide necessary staffing services to the committee.
15Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 4, 2019, the committee 
16shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
17legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
18SUMMARY
19This resolve establishes the Committee To Study and Develop Recommendations To 
20Address Guardianship Challenges That Delay Patient Discharges from Hospitals.  The 
21committee is required to study and develop recommendations to address guardianship, 
22conservatorship and authorization of transaction challenges that result in extended 
23hospitalization of patients clinically qualified for discharge from a hospital.
