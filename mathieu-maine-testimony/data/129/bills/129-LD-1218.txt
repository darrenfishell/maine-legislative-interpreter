2Sec. 1.  22 MRSA §2422, sub-§7-B is enacted to read:
37-B.  Plant canopy.  "Plant canopy" means the total surface area within a cultivation 
4area that is dedicated to the cultivation of mature marijuana plants.  The surface area of 
5the plant canopy must be calculated in square feet and measured using the outside 
6boundaries of the area and must include all of the area within the boundaries.  If the 
7surface area of the plant canopy consists of noncontiguous areas, each component area 
8must be separated by identifiable boundaries.  If a tiered or shelving system is used in the 
9cultivation area the surface area of each tier or shelf must be included in calculating the 
10area of the plant canopy.  Calculation of the area of the plant canopy may not include the 
11areas within the cultivation area that are used to cultivate immature marijuana plants and 
12seedlings and that are not used at any time to cultivate mature marijuana plants.
13Sec. 2.  22 MRSA §2423-A, sub-§2, ¶B, as amended by PL 2017, c. 452, §4, is 
14further amended to read:
15B.  Cultivate up to 30 mature marijuana plants or 500 square feet of plant canopy, 60 
16immature marijuana plants and unlimited seedlings;
17SUMMARY
18This bill amends the Maine Medical Use of Marijuana Act provision governing how 
19much medical marijuana caregivers may cultivate.  It defines the term "plant canopy" and 
20adds language allowing caregivers to cultivate up to 30 mature marijuana plants or 500 
21square feet of plant canopy, 60 immature marijuana plants and unlimited seedlings.
