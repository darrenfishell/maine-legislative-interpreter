19 SUMMARY 
20 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, replaces the bill and 
21 provides that, beginning January 1, 2022, a person may not sell or offer for sale in the 
22 State any of the following new products that are manufactured on or after January 1, 2022 
23 and that were prohibited from sale in the State of California under its appliance efficiency 
24 regulations, as those regulations were in effect on January 1, 2020: computers and 
25 computer monitors; general service lamps; commercial hot food holding cabinets; 
26 plumbing :fittings that are showerheads, lavatory faucets, kitchen faucets, public lavatory 
27 faucets, metering faucets, kitchen replacement aerators and lavatory replacement aerators; 
28 plumbing fixtures that are water closets and urinals; portable electric spas; spray sprinkler 
29 bodies; and water dispensers. 
30 The Governor's Energy Office is required to enforce this prohibition, and fines for a 
31 violation of the prohibition are specified. The office is authorized to enter into a 
32 memorandum of understanding or other formalized agreement with the Efficiency Maine 
33 Trust for the provision of technical assistance by the trust to consumers and businesses 
34 regarding this prohibition. The office is also required to submit an annual report to the 
35 joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over energy, utilities and 
36 technology matters describing any recent or anticipated changes to the California 
37 appliance efficiency regulations and including recommendations as to whether, based on 
38 such changes or for other specified reasons, the state provision requires amendment.

Fiscal Detail and Notes 
This bill prohibits the sale of certain appliances in Maine on or after January 1, 2022 that have been prohibited from 
sale in California. The bill authorizes the Governor's Energy Office (GEO) to create the prohibition program, enforce 
the prohibition and to enter into an agreement with the Efficiency Maine Trnst to provide technical assistance to 
educate customers and businesses regarding the prohibition program. The GEO has indicated that to fulfill the 
prohibition program requirements it would require additional staff resources. The bill includes General Fund 
appropriations of $174,055 in fiscal year 20-21 for two Management Analyst II positions and associated costs.
