2Sec. 1.  22 MRSA §1471-B, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2011, c. 119, §1 and 
3affected by §2 and amended by c. 657, Pt. W, §5, is further amended to read:
41.  Board established.  The Board of Pesticides Control is established by Title 5, 
5section 12004-D, subsection 3, within the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and 
6Forestry.  Except as provided in this chapter, the board must be composed of 7 members, 
7appointed by the Governor, subject to approval by the joint standing committee of the 
8Legislature having jurisdiction over agricultural matters and confirmation by the Senate.  
9To provide the knowledge and experience necessary for carrying out the duties of the 
10board, the board must consist of the following members:  one person with practical 
11experience and knowledge regarding the agricultural use of chemicals; one person who 
12has practical experience and knowledge regarding the use of chemicals in forest 
13management; one person from the medical community; a scientist from the University of 
14Maine System having practical experience and expertise in integrated pest management; 
15one commercial applicator; and 2 persons appointed to represent the public.  The 2 
16members appointed to represent the public must have a demonstrated interest in 
17environmental protection and represent different geographic areas of the State.  The term 
18must be for 4 years, except that of the initial appointees, 2 shall serve 4-year terms, 2 
19shall serve 3-year terms, 2 shall serve 2-year terms and one shall serve serves a one-year 
20term.  Any vacancy must be filled by an appointment for the remainder of the unexpired 
21term.
22Sec. 2.  Application.  This Act does not require the terms of members serving on 
23the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, Board of Pesticides Control 
24on the effective date of this Act to be terminated.
25SUMMARY
26This bill restores the requirement that the 2 public members of the Department of 
27Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, Board of Pesticides Control have a demonstrated 
28interest in environmental protection.  The change does not require the termination of the 
29terms of current members of the board.
