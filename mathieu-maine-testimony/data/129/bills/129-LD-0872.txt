71.  Definitions.  For the purposes of this subchapter, unless the context otherwise 
8indicates, the following terms have the following meanings.
9A.  "Federally designated health professional shortage area" means a geographic area 
10experiencing a health care provider shortage as determined by the federal Department 
11of Health and Human Services, Health Resources and Services Administration, 
12Bureau of Health Workforce.
13B.  "Fund" means the Health Care Professional Education Loan Repayment Fund 
14established in subsection 6.
15C.  "Program" means the Health Care Professional Education Loan Repayment 
16Program established in subsection 2.
172.  Program established.  The Health Care Professional Education Loan Repayment 
18Program is established for the purpose of increasing the number of health care 
19professionals practicing in the State.
203.  Criteria.  For an applicant to participate in the program, the applicant must:
21A.  Be a health care professional licensed under Title 32, chapter 31, 36 or 48 or a 
22comparable provision in another jurisdiction;
23B.  Possess outstanding education debt;
24C.  Submit an application to the authority; and
25D.  Have signed a statement of intent in a form acceptable to the authority to live and 
26work as a health care professional in the State for a minimum of 5 years after 
27acceptance into the program.
284.  Debt repayment.  The authority shall repay the education debt of a health care 
29professional who meets the criteria in subsection 3 and is approved by the authority in the 
30following manner:
31A.  If the place of employment of the health care professional in the State is not in a 
32federally designated health professional shortage area, the authority shall repay the 
33education debt of the health care professional in the amount of 20% of the 
34outstanding debt owed at the time of application or $25,000, whichever is lower, per 
35year of the health care professional's participation in the program up to a total of 
36$125,000; or
Page 2 - 129LR1189(01)-11B.  If the place of employment of the health care professional in the State is in a 
2federally designated health professional shortage area, the authority shall repay the 
3education debt of the health care professional in the amount of 20% of the 
4outstanding debt owed at the time of application or $30,000, whichever is lower, per 
5year of the health care professional's participation in the program up to a total of 
6$150,000.
7The authority shall make payments under the program to a health care professional for a 
8period of 5 years or until the health care professional is no longer participating in the 
9program, whichever is earlier.
105.  Administration.  The program is administered by the authority. The authority 
11shall make a payment from the fund against an education debt under subsection 4 upon 
12the conclusion of the fully completed year to which the payment attaches. The authority 
13may adopt rules to carry out the purposes of this subchapter. Rules adopted pursuant to 
14this subsection are routine technical rules pursuant to Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 
152-A.
166.  Fund established.  The Health Care Professional Education Loan Repayment 
17Fund is established in the authority to support the repayment of education debt under the 
18program.  The fund consists of money received from proceeds from the renewal of the 
19contract for the operations of the State's wholesale spirits business under Title 28-A, 
20section 90, existing funding for other authority programs that may be combined with the 
21program and appropriations, allocations and contributions from private and public 
22sources.  The fund, to be accounted within the authority, must be held separate and apart 
23from all other money, funds and accounts.  Eligible investment earnings credited to the 
24assets of the fund become part of the assets of the fund.  Any unexpended balances 
25remaining in the fund at the end of any fiscal year do not lapse and must be carried 
26forward to the next fiscal year.  The authority may deduct from the fund the authority's 
27reasonable costs in administering the program.
28Sec. 2.  Contingent effective date.  Notwithstanding the provisions of the Maine 
29Revised Statutes, Title 30-A, section 6054, this Act does not take effect unless the Health 
30Care Professional Education Loan Repayment Fund established by this Act is capitalized 
31by the proceeds from the renewal of the contract for the operations of the State's 
32wholesale spirits business under Title 28-A, section 90.  The Finance Authority of Maine 
33shall notify the Revisor of Statutes upon the capitalization of the Health Care Professional 
34Education Loan Repayment Fund under this section.
35SUMMARY
36This bill creates the Health Care Professional Education Loan Repayment Program 
37administered by the Finance Authority of Maine to repay the education debt of certain 
38health care professionals who agree to live and work in the State for a minimum of 5 
39years.  Under the program, the authority will pay 20% of the education debt or $25,000, 
40whichever is lower, for each year of participation in the program up to a total of $125,000 
41for a health care professional who is not employed in a federally designated health 
42professional shortage area and 20% of the education debt or $30,000, whichever is lower, 
43for each year of participation in the program up to a total of $150,000 for a health care 
Page 3 - 129LR1189(01)-11professional who is employed in a federally designated health professional shortage area.
