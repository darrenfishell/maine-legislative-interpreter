2Sec. 1.  36 MRSA §3203, sub-§1-C is enacted to read:
31-C.  Diesel fuel tax differential.  In addition to the tax imposed on diesel fuel under 
4subsection 1-B, beginning November 1, 2019, an additional excise tax of 5¢ per gallon is 
5imposed on all diesel fuel sold or used in this State.  This subsection is repealed 
6November 1, 2022.
7Sec. 2.  36 MRSA §3203, sub-§4-A is enacted to read:
84-A.  Diesel fuel tax differential accounting; report.  Taxes collected under 
9subsection 1-C and credited to the Highway Fund under subsection 4 must be separately 
10accounted for and deposited into a special account within the Highway Fund that may be 
11used only for the construction, reconstruction, maintenance and repair of public highways 
12and bridges.
13Sec. 3.  36 MRSA §3203-C, as amended by PL 2009, c. 652, Pt. B, §8 and 
14affected by §9, is further amended to read:
15§3203-C.  Inventory tax
16On the date that any increase in the rate of tax imposed under this chapter takes 
17effect, an inventory tax is imposed upon all distillates that are held in inventory by a 
18supplier, wholesaler or retail dealer as of the end of the day prior to that date on which the 
19tax imposed by section 3203, subsection subsections 1-B and 1-C has been paid.  The 
20inventory tax is computed by multiplying the number of gallons of tax-paid fuel held in 
21inventory by the difference between the tax rate already paid and the new tax rate.  
22Suppliers, wholesalers and retail dealers that hold such tax-paid inventory shall make 
23payment of the inventory tax on or before the 15th day of the next calendar month, 
24accompanied by a form prescribed and furnished by the State Tax Assessor.  In the event 
25of a decrease in the tax rate, the supplier, wholesaler or retail dealer is entitled to a refund 
26or credit, which must be claimed on a form designed and furnished by the assessor.
27Sec. 4.  Report; legislation.  The Commissioner of Transportation shall submit a 
28report to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
29transportation matters by March 1, 2022 identifying the amount of revenue collected 
30under subsection 1-C and the purposes for which the revenue was or will be used.  The 
31committee may submit legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 130th Legislature 
32to retain, repeal or amend provisions relating to the diesel fuel tax differential.
33SUMMARY
34This bill imposes an additional 5¢ per gallon fuel tax on diesel fuel and requires that 
35revenue from the tax be used only for construction, reconstruction, maintenance and 
36repair of public highways and bridges.  The additional tax is repealed on November 1, 
372022.  The Commissioner of Transportation is required to submit a report to the joint 
38standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over transportation matters by
