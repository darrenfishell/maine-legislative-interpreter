8§1083.  Online sports pool operation; rulemaking
9The board shall adopt rules relating to the conduct of online sports pools, including 
10but not limited to rules governing the following:
111.  Fraud or deception.  The prevention of any fraud or deception upon a person 
12placing a wager with an online sports pool;
132.  Comprehensive house rules; approval.  Comprehensive house rules to be 
14adopted by an online sports pool operator governing sports wagering transactions that 
15specify the amounts to be paid on winning wagers and the effect of sports event schedule 
16changes and that identify the maximum wager amount, prohibited sports events and 
17persons prohibited from making wagers with the online sports pool under section 1082.  
18The rules must, at a minimum, require an online sports pool operator to submit the house 
19rules to the board for approval prior to commencing operations and to conspicuously 
20display the house rules on any publicly accessible website or mobile application through 
21which the operator conducts an online sports pool;
223.  Conduct of online sports pool.  The methods of operation of online sports pools, 
23including but not limited to the number of publicly accessible websites and mobile 
24applications a single online sports pool operator may employ, the types of wagers 
25permitted, the maximum wager that may be accepted from any one person on a single 
26sports event, ensuring that wagers are not accepted on prohibited sports events and 
27informing customers of state and federal tax obligations on certain winnings;
284.  Servers and other equipment.  Requirements for servers and other equipment 
29used to operate the online sports pool.  The rules must, at a minimum, require that the 
30servers or other equipment used by the online sports pool operator meet minimum 
31security standards and be located in the State;
325.  Identification of customers.  Required methods for verifying the identity and age 
33of a person who places a wager with the online sports pool and for verifying that the 
34person making a wager is not prohibited from making a wager under section 1082.  The 
35rules must, at a minimum, require that an online sports pool operator that discovers that a 
36person prohibited from making a wager under section 1082 has made a wager shall 
37immediately refund the wager and any entry fee made by the person;
386.  Location of transaction.  Prohibiting the acceptance of any wager by an online

22SUMMARY
23This bill authorizes the Department of Public Safety, Gambling Control Board to 
24issue licenses to federally recognized Indian tribes to operate online sports pools.  A tribe 
25that is licensed by the board may enter into a written contract, approved by the board, 
26with a licensed online sports pool vendor to operate the online sports pool on behalf of 
27the tribe.  Under the bill, an online sports pool is described as a device or system 
28established for the acceptance of wagers on a sports event by any system or method of 
29wagering in which the wagers are made via electronic device and transmitted by any 
30means that involves the use, at least in part, of the Internet.
31Online sports pool operators may accept wagers on all professional or amateur sports 
32events except high school sports events or other events in which a majority of the 
33participants are minors.  Online sports pool operators may not accept wagers from 
34persons whose identity they cannot verify; persons under 21 years of age; the director, 
35officers and employees of the online sports pool operator and relatives living in the same 
36household as those persons; athletes, coaches, referees and umpires participating in the 
37sports event; other interested persons if the sports event upon which the wager is placed is 
38overseen by the person's league or sports governing body; persons with confidential 
39information that could affect the outcome of the sports event; persons who voluntarily 
40request to be prohibited from making online sports wagers; and persons who make 
41wagers on behalf of another person.
Page 14 - 129LR2212(01)-11The bill requires the board to adopt rules regulating the operation of online sports 
2pools, including rules restricting the types of wagers permitted, establishing the 
3maximum wagers that may be accepted from any one person on a single sports event, 
4imposing requirements for servers and other equipment used to operate the online sports 
5pool, establishing methods for verifying the identity and age of persons placing wagers 
6online and prohibiting the acceptance of wagers from outside the State as required by 
7federal law.
8The bill further requires that 1% of net online sports pool income be used for 
9administrative expenses of the board and that 10% of net online sports pool income be 
10distributed to the Treasurer of State to be credited to the Department of Education for 
11essential programs and services for kindergarten to grade 12.
12Finally, the bill provides that the laws governing unlawful gambling and games of 
13chance do not apply to online sports pool operators that comply with the laws governing 
14online sports pools.
