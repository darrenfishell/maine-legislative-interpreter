41.  Fund established.  The Fund To Support Local Fruits and Vegetables Purchasing, 
5referred to in this section as "the fund," is established in the department to provide 
6incentives to federal food and nutrition assistance program participants for the purchase 
7of locally grown fruits and vegetables and to support outreach for and administration of 
8programs that offer nutrition incentives to participants of federal food and nutrition 
9assistance programs.  The fund consists of money received as appropriations, allocations 
10and contributions from private and public sources.  The fund, to be accounted within the 
11department, must be held separate and apart from all other money, funds and accounts.  
12Eligible investment earnings credited to the assets of the fund become part of the assets of 
13the fund.  Any unexpended balances remaining in the fund at the end of any fiscal year do 
14not lapse and must be carried forward to the next fiscal year. For purposes of this section, 
15"local" means within the State.
162.  Fund recipients.  An organization based in the State that supports local food 
17producers, local food production or low-income individuals in receiving food and 
18nutrition assistance may receive proceeds from the fund upon application with the 
19department.  The department shall prioritize an applicant that has a demonstrated history 
20of incentivizing the use of federal food and nutrition assistance programs to purchase 
21locally grown fruits and vegetables or that demonstrates the ability to leverage the 
22proceeds to match or receive additional funds from local, state, federal or private sources.  
23The department shall periodically post a request for applications for eligible organizations 
24to apply for fund proceeds.
253.  Report; audit.  The department shall require a periodic report from a recipient 
26under subsection 2 detailing the use of fund proceeds and the federal food and nutrition 
27assistance programs involved and to ensure that the funds are expended appropriately 
28pursuant to this section.  The department may audit a recipient to carry out the purposes 
29of this subsection.
304.  Rules.  The department may adopt routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
31chapter 375, subchapter 2-A to carry out the purposes of the fund, including application 
32criteria and procedures for recipients, disbursement of funds to recipients and for 
33outreach and administration purposes and reporting and audit procedures for recipients.

6SUMMARY
7This bill establishes the Fund To Support Local Fruits and Vegetables Purchasing 
8within the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to provide incentives to 
9federal food and nutrition assistance program participants for the purchase of locally 
10grown fruits and vegetables and to support outreach for and administration of programs 
11that offer nutrition incentives to participants of federal food and nutrition assistance 
12programs. Fund recipients must be state-based organizations that support local food 
13producers, local food production or low-income individuals in receiving food and 
14nutrition assistance, with priority to organizations that have a demonstrated history of 
15incentivizing the use of federal food and nutrition assistance programs to purchase locally 
16grown fruits and vegetables or that demonstrate the ability to leverage the proceeds to 
17match or receive additional funds from local, state, federal or private sources.  The bill 
18provides reporting and audit requirements for recipients to ensure proper use of the funds 
19and appropriates funds to capitalize the fund.GENERAL FUND 2019-20 2020-21
