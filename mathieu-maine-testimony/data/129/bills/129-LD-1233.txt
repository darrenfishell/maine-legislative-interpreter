2Sec. 1.  14 MRSA §103 is enacted to read:
3§103.  Offers to stipulate to entry of judgment
41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Actual costs" means recoverable costs as described in section 1502-B and 
7discretionary costs as described in section 1502-C.
8B.  "Adjusted verdict" means the verdict plus interest and costs from the filing of the 
9notice of claim, or, if not applicable, from the filing of the complaint, through the 
10date of the offer.
11C.  "Average offer" means the sum of an offer and a counteroffer, divided by 2.  If no 
12counteroffer is made, the offer must be used as the average offer.
13D.  "Counteroffer" means a written reply to an offer, served within 21 days after 
14service of the offer, in which a party rejects an offer of the adverse party and makes 
15that party's own offer.
16E.  "Offer" means a written notification to an adverse party of the offeror's 
17willingness to stipulate to the entry of judgment in a sum certain, which is deemed to 
18include all costs and interest then accrued.  If a party has made more than one offer, 
19the most recent offer controls for the purposes of this section.
20F.  "Verdict" includes:
21(1)  A jury verdict;
22(2)  A judgment by the court after a nonjury trial; and
23(3)  A judgment entered as a result of a ruling on a motion after rejection of the 
24offer of judgment.
252.  Offer.  Prior to 28 days before trial, a party may serve on the adverse party a 
26written offer to stipulate to the entry of judgment for the whole or part of the claim, 
27including interest and costs then accrued.
283.  Acceptance or rejection of offer.  The following provisions govern the 
29acceptance or rejection of an offer.
30A.  To accept an offer, the adverse party, within 21 days after service of the offer, 
31must serve on the other parties a written notice of agreement to stipulate to the entry 
32of judgment offered and file the offer, the notice of acceptance and proof of service 
33of the notice with the court.  The court shall enter a judgment according to the terms 
34of the stipulation.
35B.  An offer is rejected if the offeree:
36(1)  Expressly rejects it in writing; or
37(2)  Does not accept it as provided in paragraph A.
Page 2 - 129LR1956(01)-11A rejection does not preclude a later offer by either party.
2C.  A counteroffer may be accepted or rejected in the same manner as an offer.
34.  Imposition of costs following rejection of offer.  If an offer is rejected, costs are 
4payable as follows.
5A.  If the adjusted verdict is more favorable to the offeror than the average offer, the 
6offeree must pay to the offeror the offeror's actual costs incurred in the prosecution or 
7defense of the action.
8B.  If the adjusted verdict is more favorable to the offeree than the average offer, the 
9offeror must pay to the offeree the offeree's actual costs incurred in the prosecution or 
10defense of the action.  An offeree who has not made a counteroffer may not recover 
11actual costs unless the offer was made less than 42 days before trial.
12C.  The court shall determine the actual costs incurred.
13D.  Evidence of an offer is admissible only in a proceeding to determine costs.
14E.  Proceedings under this section do not affect a contract or relationship between a 
15party and that party's attorney.
16F.  A request for costs under this subsection must be filed and served within 28 days 
17after the entry of judgment or entry of an order denying a timely motion:
18(1)  For a new trial;
19(2)  To set aside the judgment; or
20(3)  For rehearing or reconsideration.

29SUMMARY
30This bill provides that prior to 28 days before trial, a party to a lawsuit may serve on 
31the adverse party a written offer to stipulate to the entry of judgment for the whole or part 
32of the claim.  It sets forth how an offer or counteroffer may be accepted or rejected.  It 
33provides for the payment of costs if an offer is rejected.  It provides that reasonable expert 
34witness fees and expenses included as discretionary costs include fees associated with 
35consulting, preparation and testifying at deposition or trial.
