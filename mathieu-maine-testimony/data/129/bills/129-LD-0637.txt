17§2112-B.  Open container; using marijuana in a vehicle prohibited 
181.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
19following terms have the following meanings. 
20A.  "Open container" means a receptacle that contains any amount of marijuana or 
21consumable products made from marijuana and has a broken seal or a receptacle from 
22which the contents have been partially removed.
23B.  "Passenger area" means the area designed to seat the operator and passengers 
24while a vehicle is in operation and any area readily accessible to the operator or a 
25passenger, including, but not limited to, the glove compartment, while the operator 
26and passengers are in their seated positions.
27C.  "Public way" means a way, including a right-of-way, owned and maintained by 
28the State, a county or a municipality over which the general public has a right to pass.
292.  Violation.  The operator of a vehicle on a public way commits a traffic infraction 
30if the operator or a passenger in the passenger area of the vehicle:
31A.  Consumes marijuana or a marijuana product;
32B.  Possesses an open container; or
33C.  Possesses marijuana or a marijuana product that has been placed in a container 
34labeled by the manufacturer of the container as containing a nonmarijuana substance.
353.  Exceptions.  An operator of a vehicle is not in violation of this section if:
36A.  The operator or a passenger possesses an open container in a vehicle not equipped 
37with a trunk if the open container is located behind the last upright seat of the vehicle 
38or in an area not normally occupied by the operator or passenger; or

9SUMMARY
10This bill does the following:
111.  It allows as admissible evidence indicating whether a person is operating a motor 
12vehicle under the influence of intoxicants whether the person has an alcohol level of 0.05 
13grams or less of alcohol per 100 milliliters of blood or 210 liters of breath and has a trace 
14amount of any drug or the metabolite of any drug within the person's blood or urine;
152.  It establishes a permissible inference of operating under the influence of 
16intoxicants when a person operates a motor vehicle and has an alcohol level in excess of 
170.05 grams of alcohol but less than 0.08 grams of alcohol per 100 milliliters of blood or 
18210 liters of breath and has a trace amount of any drug or the metabolite of any drug 
19within the person's blood or urine;
203.  It establishes a 0.00 nanogram level for THC for a person under 21 years of age 
21who is operating a motor vehicle; and
224.  It creates a new traffic infraction for a person who consumes marijuana or a 
23marijuana product in a vehicle, possesses an open container of marijuana or a marijuana 
24product in the passenger area of a vehicle or possesses marijuana or a marijuana product 
25that has been placed in a container labeled by the manufacturer of the container as 
26containing a nonmarijuana substance.
