29SUMMARY
30This amendment, which strikes and replaces the bill, bans the use of synthetic 
31herbicides, including, but not limited to, glyphosate, within 75 feet of school grounds.  
32The prohibition does not apply to agricultural land or residential property.
33The amendment also directs the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and 
34Forestry, Board of Pesticides Control to establish an environmental risk advisory 
35committee to assess the environmental and human health risks associated with the use of 
36glyphosate.  The advisory committee is required to submit an interim report no later than 
37February 1, 2022 and a final report no later than February 1, 2023 with findings and 
38recommendations to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction 
39over agricultural matters.  The joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
40jurisdiction over agricultural matters has the authority to submit a bill relating to the 
41subject matter of the report during the First Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.OTHER SPECIAL REVENUE FUNDS 2019-20 2020-21

Fiscal Detail and Notes
This bill bans the use of glyphosate under certain conditions a nd directs the Board of Pesticides Control (BPC), within 
the Department of Agriculture, Conserva tion and Forestry, to establish an environmental risk advisory committee to 
assess the environmental and human risks associated with th e use of glyphosate and complete a final report of their 
findings by February 1, 2023.  The BPC ha s indicated that it will require additional staffing to complete these tasks.  
The bill includes additional allocations of $103,540 to the BP C to allow expenditure of existing resources for one 
toxicologist, contracted sta ffing, environmental sampling and literature review.  The impact of these new unfunded 
costs on other programs in the BPC cannot be determined at this time.Fiscal Note129th MAINE LEGISLATURE
