16Sec. 1.  Working group on safe and inclusive learning environments.  
17Resolved:  That the Commissioner of Education shall convene the Working Group on 
18Safe and Inclusive Learning Environments, referred to in this resolve as "the working 
19group."
20Sec. 2.  Working group membership.  Resolved:  That the Commissioner of 
21Education shall include at least the following in the membership of the working group:  
22students; educators; representatives of legal or advocacy groups with experience 
23supporting the LGBTQ community; members of the Department of Education with 
24expertise in curriculum and in social and emotional development; and representatives of 
25stakeholder groups such as the Maine Education Association, the Maine Principals' 
26Association, the Maine Administrators of Services for Children with Disabilities and the 
27Maine School Management Association.  For purposes of this resolve, "LGBTQ" 
28includes but is not limited to lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer, questioning, 
29intersex and asexual.
30Sec. 3.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the working group shall review and develop 
31recommendations on at least the following:
321.  Classroom-based practices that are inclusive of students and educators who 
33identify as LGBTQ;
342.  Hiring and recruiting practices that are inclusive of educators who identify as 
35LGBTQ;
363.  Exemplar policies for inclusive practices for students and educators who identify 
37as LGBTQ;
Page 2 - 129LR2130(01)-114.  Creation of a database of effective instructional practices that are inclusive of 
2students and families who identify as LGBTQ;
35.  Professional development and cultural competency training for educators; and
46.  Creation of a curriculum audit document in which school administrative units map 
5places where LGBTQ voices, stories and histories are included in the curriculum.
6Sec. 4.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 1, 2019, the 
7Commissioner of Education shall submit a report that includes the working group's 
8findings and recommendations, including suggested legislation, for presentation to the 
9Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.  The Joint Standing Committee on 
10Education and Cultural Affairs may report out legislation to the Second Regular Session 
11of the 129th Legislature based on the recommendations.
12Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
13legislation takes effect when approved.
14SUMMARY
15This resolve directs the Commissioner of Education to convene a working group to 
16review and develop recommendations to ensure safe and inclusive learning environments 
17for LGBTQ students and educators in Maine schools.  The commissioner is required to 
18submit the working group's report to the Legislature by December 1, 2019.  The Joint 
19Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs may report out legislation to the 
20Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature based on the recommendations.
21For purposes of this resolve, "LGBTQ" includes but is not limited to lesbian, gay, 
22bisexual, transgender, queer, questioning, intersex and asexual.
