COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A-" to H.P. 960, L.D. 1332, "RESOLUTION, 
Proposing an Amendment to the Constitution of Maine To Require State and 
Congressional Elections To Be Decided by a Plurality of Votes Cast" 
Amend the resolution by striking out all of Section 26 (page 1, lines 14 to 16 in L.D.) 
and inserting the following: 
'Section 26. Congressional and presidential elections. A primary, general or 
special election for United States Representative or United States Senator must be 
decided by a plurality of votes cast. A primary election for President of the United States 
and a general election for presidential elector must be decided by a plurality of votes cast.' 
Amend the resolution by striking out all of the question (page 1, lines 23 to 27 in 
L.D.) and inserting the following: 
"'Do you favor amending the Constitution of Maine to require that a 
primary election for President of the United States, State Representative, 
State Senator or Governor be decided by a plurality of votes cast, that a 
general election for presidential elector be decided by a plurality of votes 
cast and that a primary, general or special election for United States 
Representative or United States Senator be decided by a plurality of 
votes cast?"' 
SUMMARY 
This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, provides that a 
primary election for President of the United States and a general election for presidential 
elector must be decided by a plurality of votes cast.
