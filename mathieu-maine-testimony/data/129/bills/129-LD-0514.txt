3further amended to read:
42.  Representation of parties.  The municipal officers shall consider the following 
5for appointment as election clerks.
6A.  The municipal officers shall consider persons nominated by the municipal, county 
7or state committees of the major parties to serve as election clerks.  The municipal 
8officers shall appoint at least one election clerk from each of the major parties to 
9serve at each voting place during the time the polls are open.  The municipal officers 
10shall also appoint a sufficient number of election clerks to serve as counters after the 
11polls close.  The election clerks must be selected so that, of the total number of 
12election clerks selected, 33% are selected from one major party does not exceed the 
13number of election clerks, 33% are selected from another major party by more than 
14one and the remaining 34% are selected without regard to party enrollment.
15B.  The municipal officers shall appoint at least one election clerk nominated by the 
16municipal committee of a qualified minor party represented on the last general 
17election ballot for each voting place at the committee's request.
18C.  Notwithstanding subsection 1, the municipal officers may also consider persons 
19who are 17 years of age to serve as student election clerks for a specific election.  A 
20student election clerk may perform all the functions of an election clerk as prescribed 
21by this Title.
22All nominations for election clerks must be submitted to the municipal officers no later 
23than April 1st of each general election year.  If a municipal committee of a major party 
24fails to submit a list of nominees to serve as election clerks, the municipal officers may 
25appoint registered voters enrolled in that party to serve as election clerks.
26If the municipal officers are unable to appoint a sufficient number of election clerks as set 
27forth in paragraphs A, B and C, they may appoint any other registered voter, as long as 
28the balance between representation of major political parties is maintained as described in 
29this subsection.
30SUMMARY
31This bill requires election clerks in a municipality to be selected so that 33% of clerks 
32are from one major party and 33% from another, with 34% of clerks being selected 
33without regard to party enrollment.
