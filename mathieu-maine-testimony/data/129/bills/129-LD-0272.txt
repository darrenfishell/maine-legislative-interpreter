23later than 30 days following the effective date of this section.  The appointing authorities 
24shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative Council once all appointments have

26shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more after the 
27effective date of this section a majority of but not all appointments have been made, the 
28chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for the 
29commission to meet and conduct its business.
304.  Duties.  The commission shall make recommendations for implementing voting 
31by mail in the State for all primary and general elections for President of the United 
32States, United States Senator, United States Representative to Congress, Governor, State 
33Senator and State Representative as well as all elections on people's veto questions, 
34initiated measures, bond issues, constitutional amendments and other legislatively 
35proposed referenda.  Prior to making its recommendations, the commission shall examine 
36the laws, rules and implementation of voting by mail in Oregon and gather information 
37on past or current proposals for voting by mail in other states.

20SUMMARY
21This bill requires that, beginning November 1, 2020, all primary and general elections 
22for President of the United States, United States Senator, United States Representative to 
23Congress, Governor, State Senator and State Representative as well as all elections on 
24people's veto questions, initiated measures, bond issues, constitutional amendments and 
25other legislatively proposed referenda be conducted by mail.  The bill also establishes the 
26Commission to Implement Voting by Mail to make recommendations, including 
27proposed legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs, 
28which may report out legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature 
29for implementing voting by mail.
