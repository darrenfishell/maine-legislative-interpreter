41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the

11injury, disease or illness that causes death within a reasonable period of time in 
12accordance with accepted medical standards and for which the application of life-
13sustaining treatment serves only to prolong the process of dying.
142.  Temporary terminal condition medical allowance.  Notwithstanding section 
156421, upon request the commissioner may issue a temporary terminal condition medical 
16allowance that permits an individual to fish under the authority of the license of a Class I, 
17Class II or Class III lobster and crab fishing license holder if the following criteria are 
18met:
19A.  The individual who will be fishing has successfully completed an apprentice 
20program under section 6422;
21B.  The individual who will be fishing is the child, spouse or domestic partner of the 
22individual who holds the Class I, Class II or Class III lobster and crab fishing license;
23C.  The holder of the Class I, Class II or Class III lobster and crab fishing license has 
24been diagnosed within the previous year with a terminal condition by at least one 
25health care professional.  The holder of the Class I, Class II or Class III lobster and 
26crab fishing license shall provide the commissioner with written documentation of the 
27diagnosis from a health care professional; and
28D.  The holder of the Class I, Class II or Class III lobster and crab fishing license 
29documents to the commissioner that the license holder harvested a minimum of 1,000 
30pounds of lobsters within one year prior to the request for the temporary terminal 
31condition medical allowance.
32A request for a temporary terminal condition medical allowance must be in writing and 
33must specify the dates for which the temporary terminal condition medical allowance is 
34requested. The holder of the Class I, Class II or Class III lobster and crab fishing license 
35on which the temporary terminal condition medical allowance is based must maintain a 
36valid license during the duration of the temporary terminal condition medical allowance. 
37The holder of the Class I, Class II or Class III lobster and crab fishing license is liable for 
38the activities of the individual fishing under the temporary terminal condition medical 
39allowance.

16condition medical allowance upon a request in writing from the holder of the Class I, 
17Class II or Class III lobster and crab fishing license on which the temporary terminal 
18condition medical allowance is based.
19SUMMARY
20This bill creates a temporary terminal condition medical allowance that allows an 
21individual to fish under the authority of the license of a Class I, Class II or Class III 
22lobster and crab fishing license holder when the individual is the child, spouse or 
23domestic partner of the license holder and has completed the lobster apprentice program 
24and the license holder has been diagnosed with a terminal condition and harvested a 
25minimum of 1,000 pounds of lobsters within one year prior to the request for the 
26temporary terminal condition medical allowance.  The bill allows the license holder to 
27continue to fish pursuant to the license despite having been issued an allowance but 
28permits only one person, the license holder or the designated individual, to fish under the 
29license at any one time.  The temporary terminal condition medical allowance may not 
30exceed one year in duration and may be renewed at the discretion of the Commissioner of 
31Marine Resources.
