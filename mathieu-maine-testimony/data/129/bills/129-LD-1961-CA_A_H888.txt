37 limited to, race, ethnicity, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, class, zip code, age 
38 and disability.

281.  Establishment; purposes.  The Trust for a Healthy Maine is established for the 
29 purposes of receiving all settlement funds and other funds, redistributing that money to 
30 state agencies or designated agents of the State to fund tobacco use prevention and control 
31 at levels recommended by the United States Department of Health and Human Services, 
32 Centers for Disease Control and Prevention and to ensure adequate resources for other 
33 disease prevention efforts and promoting public health. The purposes of the trust also 
34 include supporting state agencies in planning and delivering public health and prevention 
35 programs and services, supporting accreditation of the Department of Health and Human 
36 Services, Maine Center for Disease Control and Prevention and supporting public health 
37 workforce development. The trust also provides public health expertise and evidence-based 
38 information to the Legislature.
392.  Governance; board.  The trust is created as a body corporate and politic and a 
40 public instrumentality of the State and is governed by the Trust for a Healthy Maine Board 
41 in accordance with this subchapter.

284.  Terms; vacancies.  Trustees serve 3-year terms.  Trustees may serve no more than 
29 3 consecutive terms.  A trustee serves on the board until the trustee is replaced.  If a trustee 
30 is unable to complete a term, the Governor shall consult with the board and appoint a 
31 replacement for the remainder of the unexpired term.  The replacement trustee must hold 
32 the same qualifications, set forth in subsection 3, as that of the departing trustee.
335.  Chair.  The board shall elect a chair, a vice-chair, a secretary and a treasurer from 
34 among the trustees. Each officer serves a one-year term in that office and is eligible for 
35 reelection.
366.  Meetings; quorum; remote participation.  The board shall meet at least 4 times 
37 each year at regular intervals and may meet at other times at the call of the chair or the 
38 Governor.  A majority of the trustees constitutes a quorum.  Meetings of the board are 
39 public proceedings as provided by Title 1, chapter 13, subchapter 1.  Notwithstanding any 
40 other law to the contrary, a trustee who is not physically present may participate by 
41 telephone or other remote-access technology in accordance with procedures established by 
42 the board.

16 administration of the trust.  Upon accepting appointment as a trustee, each trustee must 
17 acknowledge the fiduciary duty to use the trust fund only for the purposes set forth in this 
18 subchapter. It is the duty of each trustee to ensure that the purposes of the trust set forth in 
19 this subchapter are fulfilled.
2013.  Conflict of interest.  A trustee is deemed to be an executive employee for purposes 
21 of Title 5, sections 18, 18-A and 19.  In the operation or dissolution of the trust, a trustee, 
22 employee of the trust or officer of the trust or a spouse or dependent child of any of those 
23 individuals may not receive any direct personal benefit from the activities of the trust, 
24 except that the trust may pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and otherwise 
25 hold, manage and dispose of the trust's property in furtherance of the purposes of the trust.  
26 This subsection does not prohibit corporations or other entities with which a trustee is 
27 associated by reason of ownership or employment from participating in activities funded 
28 directly or indirectly by the trust if ownership or employment is made known to the board 
29 and the trustee abstains from all matters directly relating to that participation immediately 
30 upon discovery of the association.

11§1517.  Funding disbursement plan
121.  Funding disbursement plan.  By December 31, 2022 and every 2 years thereafter, 
13 the board shall develop and approve a funding disbursement plan to disburse settlement 
14 funds and other funds it may hold or receive in the subsequent biennium.  The funding 
15 disbursement plan must advance the purposes of this subchapter and be based on the most 
16 recent state health plan and the most recent data available to the board.
172.  Input from interested parties.  Prior to adopting a funding disbursement plan 
18 under subsection 1 or substantially amending an existing funding disbursement plan, the 
19 trust shall hold at least one public hearing to receive input from interested parties, including, 
20 but not limited to, the Department of Health and Human Services, Maine Center for Disease 
21 Control and Prevention, other state agencies, organizations engaged in smoking cessation 
22 and public health efforts, other nongovernmental organizations, interested stakeholders, 
23 patients and members of the public.  The board shall establish the procedure and timelines 
24 for seeking input from interested parties.  The board shall determine the necessary 
25 circumstances, consistent with this subsection, to initiate a public hearing.  When 
26 considering the input of interested parties, the trust must consider zero-based budgeting 
27 principles and long-term returns on investment.
283.  Funding disbursement plans.  The funding disbursement plan approved by the 
29 board under subsection 1 for fiscal year 2023-24 must disburse an amount equal to 0.40 of 
30 the settlement funds projected to be received in fiscal year 2023-24 for the purpose of 
31 providing medical care.  The funding disbursement plan approved by the board under 
32 subsection 1 for fiscal year 2024-25 must disburse an amount equal to 0.20 of the settlement 
33 funds projected to be received in fiscal year 2024-25 for the purpose of providing medical 
34 care.  The funding disbursement plan approved by the board under subsection 1 for fiscal 
35 year 2025-26 and all subsequent fiscal years may not disburse funds for the purpose of 
36 providing medical care.  When approving other elements of the funding disbursement 
37 plans, the board shall work with the Department of Health and Human Services, Maine 
38 Center for Disease Control and Prevention regarding the transition of funds and consider 
39 funding levels in the most recent fiscal year and disburse funding in amounts that minimize 
40 disruption of existing programs and ensure smooth and efficient transitions to the funding 
41 levels required under subsection 4.

35§1518.  Restrictions; construction
36 The trust's activity is restricted to receiving and disbursing funds and any actions 
37 necessary and appropriate to receive and disburse funds.  The trust may not create, manage 
38 or operate public health or health delivery programs.  Nothing in this subchapter may be 
39 construed to empower the trust to direct, manage or oversee any program, fund or activity 
40 of any other state agency.

26SUMMARY

28 1.  Changes the process of the appointment of trustees to the Trust for a Healthy Maine 
29 Board, including the appointment process and removal of trustees;

31 with expertise in systemic racism and structural inequity who is serving on the Permanent

35 industry;

37 respond to public health emergencies and systemic health disparities;
