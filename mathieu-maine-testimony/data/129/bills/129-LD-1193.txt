2evaluation.  Resolved:  That the Maine Community College System shall evaluate the 
3unmet need and capacity for expanding workforce training options in Waldo County with 
4the goal of providing additional capacity for training in the most accessible platform 
5possible in industries with identified employee shortages.  In conducting the evaluation, 
6the Maine Community College System shall examine the following industries and trades:
71.  Nursing or other medical trades;
82.  Composites manufacturing;
93.  Carpentry;
104.  Electrical trades;
115.  Millwright trades; and
126.  Ventilation, air conditioning, refrigeration, plumbing and heating.
13The Maine Community College System shall use its established methods and 
14practices in assessing the need for expanded workforce training options in Waldo County.
15Sec. 2.  Strategies for expanding workforce training options.  Resolved:  
16That, if the Maine Community College System, while conducting the study in section 1, 
17finds that a need for workforce training options exists in any of the industries and trades 
18examined, the Maine Community College System shall assess the best way to increase 
19capacity and opportunity.  Potential strategies for the consideration include but are not 
20limited to:
211.  Expanding access through Maine Quality Centers under the Maine Revised 
22Statutes, Title 20-A, section 12727;
232.  Long-distance learning options; and
243.  Colocation or collaboration with other learning institutions.
25Sec. 3.  Report.  Resolved:  That, by December 4, 2019, the Maine Community 
26College System shall submit a report based on its evaluation in section 1 identifying if 
27and where needs exist and outlining the best options to meet those needs to the Joint 
28Standing Committee on Innovation, Development, Economic Advancement and 
29Business.  The report, including suggested legislation, must include but is not limited to:
301.  What unmet workforce training needs exist;
312.  What industries should be prioritized for expanded workforce training options;
323.  The best methods for expanding workforce training options;
334.  Costs and facility needs for expanding workforce training programming; and
Page 2 - 129LR0307(01)-115.  A timeline for implementation of expanded workforce training options.
2The Joint Standing Committee on Innovation, Development, Economic Advancement 
3and Business is authorized to submit legislation related to the report to the Second 
4Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
5SUMMARY
6This resolve directs the Maine Community College System to evaluate the unmet 
7need and capacity for expanding workforce training options in Waldo County with the 
8goal of providing additional capacity for training in industries with identified employee 
9shortages.  The Maine Community College System is required to submit a report with 
10suggested legislation by December 4, 2019 to the Joint Standing Committee on 
11Innovation, Development, Economic Advancement and Business, which may submit 
12legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
