33SUMMARY
34This bill creates the position of Director of Aging in the Governor's Office of Policy 
35and Management to oversee the planning and policy development for all functions and 
36activities conducted or supported in the State that relate to aging, older adults and family 
37care partners of older adults, including, but not limited to, promoting intergovernmental 
38collaboration in meeting established aging policy objectives and managing the 
Page 8 - 129LR1181(01)-11coordination of multiple-agency initiatives related to policy objectives.  The bill amends 
2the requirements for preparing and implementing a comprehensive state plan relating to 
3Maine's aging population and incapacitated and dependent adults.  The bill establishes a 
4State Commission on Aging to advise the Governor, Legislature and state agencies on 
5planning, research and intergovernmental cooperation related to the needs of older adults 
6in the State.  The bill authorizes the Revisor of Statutes, in preparing legislation, to 
7change any terms that refer to "elderly," "elder" or "senior" individuals to refer instead to 
8"older" individuals.
