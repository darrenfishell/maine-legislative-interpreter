25 SUMMARY 
26 This amendment provides authority for the Passamaquoddy Tribe and the Penobscot 
27 Nation to extend the jurisdiction of their respective tribal courts over certain criminal 
28 offenses committed by an individual, regardless of whether the individual is a member of 
29 a federally recognized Indian tribe. The criminal offenses are domestic violence offenses 
30 in the Maine Criminal Code and criminal violation of a protection from abuse order. The 
31 criminal offenses are Class D crimes, and the tribe's and nation's jurisdictions are 
32 concurrent with the State's jurisdiction for the crimes. 
33 The Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary has authority to report out legislation to 
34 the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature concerning the extension of tribal 
35 court jurisdiction to felony domestic violence offenses consistent with the federal 
36 Violence Against Women Reauthorization Act of 2013 and the Tribal Law and Order Act 
37 of 2010. 
38 The tribal courts are required to participate in uniform crime reporting by reporting 
39 certain information to the Department of Public Safety, State Bureau of Identification, 
40 and the bureau will share its annual reports with tribal law enforcement agencies.
