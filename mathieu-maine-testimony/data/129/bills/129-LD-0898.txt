6SUMMARY
7This bill proposes to improve teacher quality in the State by amending the current 
8statutes related to programs involving teacher preparation, support systems for 
9conditionally certified teachers and educational specialists, teacher certification and 
10teacher salaries and funding college loans for students enrolled in a program of study 
11leading to certification as a teacher.
12The bill expands the student teaching experience and academic achievement 
13requirements for candidates for a professional teacher certificate. It specifies the number 
14of hours of practicum an applicant must complete and clarifies the time frame during 
15which the student teaching experience must occur.  The bill enacts a grade point average 
16requirement with respect to applicants recommended by educational institutions offering 
17teacher preparation programs.
18The bill strengthens the support and mentoring systems required to provide assistance 
19for teachers, including teachers certified for less than 5 years, and requires the 
20Commissioner of Education to pay salary supplements to teachers who are involved in 
21the operation of such systems.  The bill provides that a school administrative unit may not 
22employ a conditionally certified teacher or educational specialist unless it has an 
23approved, locally designed support and mentoring system or has received specific 
24authorization from the commissioner.
25The bill increases the minimum salary for certified teachers to $40,000 beginning 
26with the 2020-2021 school year and provides that a certified teacher's salary may not 
27remain at the minimum level for longer than 2 years.  The bill includes in the school 
28funding formula state funding for the incremental costs of meeting this minimum in fiscal 
29year 2020-21, state funding for 66% of these incremental costs in fiscal year 2021-22 and 
30state funding for 33% of these incremental costs in fiscal year 2022-23.
31The bill requires the State Board of Education to amend its rule Chapter 114: 
32Purpose, Standards and Procedures for the Review and Approval of Preparation Programs 
33for Education Personnel to articulate and outline clear standards for a memorandum of 
34understanding between student teachers, teacher preparation programs and participating 
35school administrative units.
36The bill directs the Governor to appoint members to vacant positions on the 
37Professional Standards Board by November 1, 2019 and directs the board to convene a
