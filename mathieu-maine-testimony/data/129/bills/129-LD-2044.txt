16Beginning July 1, 2021 and annually thereafter, the benefit amount must be indexed to 
17the Consumer Price Index whenever there is a percentage increase in the Consumer Price 
18Index from July 1st to June 30th of the previous year.  A firefighter, law enforcement

20paid the benefit amount as indexed immediately prior to that firefighter's, law 
21enforcement officer's or emergency medical services person's death.  The Department of 
22Administrative and Financial Services shall adopt rules to calculate the annual percentage 
23increase in the death benefit.

27B.  If there is a surviving child or children and a surviving spouse of the firefighter,

29child or children in equal shares and 1/2 to the surviving spouse;
30C.  If there is no surviving spouse of the firefighter, law enforcement officer or 
31emergency medical services person, to the child or children in equal shares; or
32D.  If there is no surviving child or spouse, to the parent or parents of the firefighter, 
33law enforcement officer or emergency medical services person in equal shares.

16recipient or recipients of any interim payment under subsection 2 are liable for repayment 
17of the amount received. The State Fire Marshal in the case of a firefighter, the chief in the

19services person may waive all or part of the repayment if that official determines that 
20undue hardship would result from that repayment.
215.  Execution or attachment prohibited.  A benefit paid under this section is not 
22subject to execution or attachment.
236.  Other benefits.  The $50,000 death benefit payable under this section may not be 
24considered a benefit paid under "similar law" for purposes of Title 5, sections 18005 and 
2518605 and may not be used to reduce any accidental death benefit amount payable under 
26those provisions or under any other provision of law.
277.  Payment from the Maine Budget Stabilization Fund.  Benefits are payable 
28from the Maine Budget Stabilization Fund as provided in Title 5, section 1532, 
29subsection 6. If funds in the Maine Budget Stabilization Fund are insufficient to pay a 
30death benefit when due, the benefit must be paid as soon as a sufficient balance exists.
318.  Rulemaking.  The State Fire Marshal, the chief and the Emergency Medical 
32Services' Board shall adopt rules to carry out the purposes of this section, except when the 
33Department of Administrative and Financial Services adopts rules as described in this 
34section.  Rules adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical rules as defined in 
35Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter II-A 2-A.
36SUMMARY
37This bill increases the death benefit for a law enforcement officer, firefighter or 
38emergency medical services person who has died while in the line of duty from $50,000 
39to $75,000 through June 30, 2021.  For deaths occurring after June 30, 2021, the 
40Department of Administrative and Financial Services is required to adopt rules to
