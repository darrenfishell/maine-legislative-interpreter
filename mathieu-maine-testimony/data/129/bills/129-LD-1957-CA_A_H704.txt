18 SUMMARY 
19 This amendment, which is the unanimous report of the committee, amends the bill to 
20 provide that the period of time following delivery of a baby for which a woman may be 
21 eligible for services under MaineCare is 6 months. The amendment directs the 
22 Department of Health and Human Services to submit a waiver or state plan amendment 
23 request no later than January 1, 2021 to the United States Department of Health and 
24 Human Services, Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services to implement the 
25 provisions of this legislation that extend MaineCare coverage to a woman following 
26 delivery of a baby from 60 days to 6 months. The department is required to take all 
27 reasonable and necessary steps to seek approval of the waiver or state plan amendment. 
28 In the event the waiver or state plan amendment is not granted, the department is directed 
29 to implement the coverage provisions using federal funds, if allowable, and then using the 
30 General Fund. Upon approval or denial of the waiver or state plan amendment, the 
31 department is directed to adopt rules no later than 180 days after the decision of the 
32 United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for Medicare and 
33 Medicaid Services. The department is required to report on a quarterly basis beginning 
34 April 1, 2021 to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
35 health and human services matters its progress in seeking a waiver or state plan 
36 amendment until the decision by the United States Department of Health and Human 
37 Services, Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services has been made. The committee is 
38 authorized to report out legislation related to each report.

The bill directs the Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS) to submit a waiver or state plan amendment 
request no later than January 1, 2021 to the United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for 
Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) to extend MaineCare coverage to a woman following delivery of a baby from 
60 days to 6 months. Upon approval or denial of the waiver or state plan amendment the department is directed to 
adopt rules no later than 180 days after the decision of the CMS. Since the CMS will only be able to approve the 
waiver after it's submittal on 01/01/21, the earliest the DHHS will be required to adopt rules for the program is in 
State Fiscal Year 2022. No appropriations are made at this time.
