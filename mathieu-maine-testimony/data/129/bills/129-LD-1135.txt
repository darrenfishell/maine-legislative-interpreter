11Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to increase 
12MaineCare reimbursement for adults with serious mental illness.  Resolved:  
13That the Department of Health and Human Services shall increase reimbursement rates 
14under rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapters II and III, Section 17 for 
15assertive community treatment by 25% for the period from the effective date of this 
16resolve to June 30, 2020.
17Sec. 2.  Rate study.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human 
18Services shall contract with a 3rd party to conduct a rate study of reimbursement rates 
19under rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapters II and III, Section 17 for 
20assertive community treatment.  The rate study must develop a rate that is set on a per 
21member per month basis.  The rate study must also take into account the costs to 
22providers of delivering the services, including the addition of peer supports to the 
23treatment team.  The rate study must be completed no later than December 1, 2019.
24Sec. 3.  Report.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services 
25shall submit a report to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction 
26over health and human services matters with the findings of the rate study conducted 
27pursuant to section 2 no later than January 30, 2020.
28Sec. 4.  Rulemaking.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human 
29Services is authorized to adopt rules to implement new rates developed pursuant to the 
30rate study in section 2 as long as those rates are no lower than the rates of reimbursement 
31in effect on April 1, 2019 and the federal Department of Health and Human Services, 
32Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services approves the reimbursement rates.  Rules 
33adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical rules as defined in the Maine 
34Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
35Sec. 5.  Appropriations and allocations.  Resolved:  That the following 
36appropriations and allocations are made.

6SUMMARY
7This resolve requires the Department of Health and Human Services to increase the 
8MaineCare reimbursement rates for assertive community treatment by 25%, contract with 
9a 3rd party to conduct a rate study of reimbursement rates for assertive community 
10treatment and report with findings by January 30, 2020.  The department is authorized to 
11set new rates based on the rate study as long as the rates are no lower than those in effect 
12on April 1, 2019.
13The resolve also includes an appropriations and allocations section.
