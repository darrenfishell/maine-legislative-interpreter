'Sec. 1. 35-A MRSA §10124 is enacted to read: 
§10124. Agricultural fair assistance program 
1. Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
following terms have the following meanings. 
A. "Agricultural fair" has the same meaning as in Title 7. section 81. subsection 1. 
B. "Demand charge" means a charge on an electric bill that is based on the 
customer's peak demand for electricity. 
C. "Fund" means the agricultural fair assistance program fund established under 
subsection 3. 
D. "Program" means the agricultural fair assistance program established under 
subsection 2. 
2. Program established. The trust shall establish and administer an agricultural fair 
assistance program to help agricultural fairs reduce electricity costs through the most 
cost-effective opportunities available. Under the program, the trust shall: 
A. Conduct outreach and provide technical assistance to agricultural fairs to: 
(1) Identify opportunities to reduce electricity costs. including but not limited to 
opportunities to reduce peak electricity demand; and

30 Sec. 2. Rate design; proceeding; report. The Public Utilities Commission shall 
31 open a proceeding to examine rate design and related issues for electricity customers that 
32 have seasonal, limited-duration, concentrated load profiles, including but not limited to 
33 agricultural fairs, seasonal festivals and other similar entities. In this proceeding, the 
34 commission shall examine options for alternative rate design, with particular attention to 
35 electricity demand charges, and identify other types of electricity customers, in addition 
36 to agricultural fairs, that may benefit from a program similar to that established under the 
37 Maine Revised Statutes, Title 35-A, section 10124. No later than December 1, 2019, the 
38 commission shall submit a report on its findings and recommendations under this section 
39 to the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and Technology. The committee 
40 may report out a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature based on the

1 SUMMARY 
2 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment establishes a program 
3 administered by the Efficiency Maine Trust to help agricultural fairs reduce electricity 
4 costs through efficiency and conservation. The program includes outreach and technical 
5 assistance to agricultural fairs to identify opportunities to lower electricity costs and 
6 enroll agricultural fairs in existing programs offered by the trust as appropriate. The 
7 program also provides custom financial incentives to agricultural fairs to implement 
8 electric efficiency and conservation measures, including but not limited to measures to 
9 reduce peak electricity demand. Funds for the program are set at the total amount paid in 
10 demand charges by agricultural fairs in the State during the prior year and are collected 
11 from electricity customers by transmission and distribution utilities. The trust is required 
12 to report to the Legislature on the program in January 2022 and January 2024, and the 
13 program has a sunset date of June 30, 2024. 
14 The amendment also directs the Public Utilities Commission to examine rate design 
15 and related issues for electricity customers that, like agricultural fairs, have seasonal, 
16 limited-duration, concentrated load profiles. It requires the commission to submit a 
17 report on its findings and recommendations to the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, 
18 Utilities and Technology and authorizes the committee to report out a bill to the Second 
19 Regular Session of the 129th Legislature based on the rep01t.

Minor cost increase -Efficiency Maine Trust 
Minor cost increase -Other Special Revenue Funds 
Any additional costs to the Efficiency Maine Trust (EMT) as a result of the implementation of a program to work 
with agricultural fair organizations to identify opportunities to lower electricity costs are anticipated to be minor and 
can be absorbed within the existing resources of the EMT. Any additional cost to the Public Utilities Commission 
(PUC) to examine the rate design and related issues of seasonal, limited duration, concentrated load profile customers 
and to report back on the findings is anticipated to be minor and can be absorbed within the existing budgeted 
resources of the PUC.
