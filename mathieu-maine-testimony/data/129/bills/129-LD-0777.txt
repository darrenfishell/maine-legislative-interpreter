15§25001.  Commission established
16The Permanent Commission on the Status of Racial and Ethnic Populations, 
17established by section 12004-I, subsection 74-J and referred to in this chapter as "the 
18commission," is an independent commission.  The commission shall promote, carry out 
19and coordinate programs designed to improve opportunities for racial and ethnic 
20populations in the State.

28the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature; and

12In making appointments, the Governor, the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the 
13House shall consider and appoint residents of the State who have a knowledge of 
14problems facing racial and ethnic populations in the State, who have experience in 
15advocacy relating to racial and ethnic populations' issues and who provide leadership in 
16programs or activities that improve opportunities for racial and ethnic populations. The 
17members of the commission must be chosen from throughout the State, and the majority 
18of members must be members of racial and ethnic populations.
19§25003.  Term of office
20Members of the commission are appointed for 2-year terms.  A member may serve 
21multiple terms.
22Members of the commission may serve after the expiration of their terms until their 
23successors have taken office.  The Governor, the President of the Senate and the Speaker 
24of the House may terminate the membership of their respective appointees for good 
25cause.  The reason for the termination must be communicated in writing to a member 
26whose membership is terminated.  The membership of any member of the commission 
27must be terminated if the member is absent from 3 consecutive meetings without 
28communicating good cause to the chair of the commission.
29§25004.  Vacancies
30A member appointed to fill a vacancy occurring prior to the expiration of the term for 
31which the member's predecessor was appointed serves only for the remainder of that term 
32and must be appointed by the same appointing authority.  Any vacancy on the 
33commission does not affect its powers.
34§25005.  Chair
35The Governor shall select a chair from among the members of the commission.  The 
36chair is authorized to appoint subcommittees.
Page 3 - 129LR0322(01)-11§25006.  Powers and duties
2The commission shall advise and consult with the Governor and the Legislature 
3about, and assist them in improving, opportunities for historically disadvantaged racial 
4and ethnic populations in the State by:
51.  Research; policy proposals.  Carrying out research necessary to determine the 
6status of historically disadvantaged racial and ethnic populations in the State and 
7considering policy proposals. The commission shall:
8A.  Study income levels of and opportunities available to historically disadvantaged 
9racial and ethnic populations in the State and examine quantitative and qualitative 
10data associated with those populations regarding business ownership, household 
11assets, debts and income, housing, employment and access to inherited wealth, capital 
12and benefits; and
13B.  Consider policy proposals to diversify business ownership to include historically 
14disadvantaged racial and ethnic populations in the State and to bridge the gaps in 
15wealth acquisition, including, but not limited to, proposals to:
16(1)   Develop a program to provide to aspiring and new business owners technical 
17assistance workshops and training on topics such as the creation of business 
18plans, accessing capital and funding and securing job training;
19(2)  Provide assistance to vendors to become registered with the State and create 
20a clearinghouse to serve as a resource for state-registered vendors seeking 
21government contracts; and
22(3)  Establish an office to coordinate the educational and technical assistance 
23needs of emerging small businesses and to provide information to small 
24businesses seeking to open, operate or expand their businesses;
252.  Activities.  Promoting and coordinating activities on state and local levels 
26designed to meet the problems of historically disadvantaged racial and ethnic populations 
27in the State;
283.  Advocate.  Serving as an advocate for historically disadvantaged racial and ethnic 
29populations in making recommendations on proposed budgetary, legislative and policy 
30actions to the Governor, the Legislature and other officials of the State and the Federal 
31Government with respect to state and federal policies, programs and other activities 
32affecting or relating to historically disadvantaged racial and ethnic populations in the 
33State;
344.  Information.  Informing the public about the presence or absence of opportunities 
35for historically disadvantaged racial and ethnic populations in the State;
365.  Meetings.  Conducting public hearings, conferences, workshops and other such 
37meetings to obtain information about, discuss and publicize the needs of and solutions to 
38the problems of historically disadvantaged racial and ethnic populations; and
396.  Reports.  Beginning January 1, 2020 and biennially thereafter, reporting to the 
40Governor and the Legislature concerning the work and interests of the commission.

6§25007.  Meetings
7The commission shall meet at the call of the chair not less than 4 times each year.
8§25008.  Staff support
9The Secretary of State shall provide staffing support as required.
10Sec. 3.  Initial members.  Notwithstanding the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, 
11section 25003, of those public members first appointed to the Permanent Commission on 
12the Status of Racial and Ethnic Populations, one appointed by the Governor, one 
13appointed by the President of the Senate and one appointed by the Speaker of the House 
14must be appointed for one-year terms.  The term of office of each member must be 
15designated at the time of appointment.
16Sec. 4.  Initial report.  No later than January 1, 2020, the Permanent Commission 
17on the Status of Racial and Ethnic Populations established in the Maine Revised Statutes, 
18Title 5, section 12004-I, subsection 74-J shall submit a report that includes the 
19commission's findings and recommendations on the commission's research and 
20consideration of policy proposals pursuant to Title 5, section 25006, subsection 1, 
21including suggested legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular Session of the 
22129th Legislature.
23SUMMARY
24This bill creates the Permanent Commission on the Status of Racial and Ethnic 
25Populations to promote, carry out and coordinate programs designed to improve 
26opportunities for racial and ethnic populations.
