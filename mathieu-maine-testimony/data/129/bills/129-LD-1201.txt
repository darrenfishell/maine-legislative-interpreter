6ยง4763.  Rental subsidy program for persons experiencing long-term homelessness
71.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
8following terms have the following meanings.
9A.  "Fixed, regular and adequate nighttime residence" has the same meaning as in 
10Title 22, section 4099-D, subsection 2.
11B.  "Person experiencing chronic homelessness" means a person with a disability who 
12has been either:
13(1)  Continually homeless for at least 12 months; or
14(2)  Homeless on at least 4 separate occasions in the last 3 years with a total 
15period of homelessness equal to at least 12 months.
16C.  "Person experiencing literal homelessness" means a person who lacks a fixed, 
17regular and adequate nighttime residence.
18D.  "Person experiencing long-term homelessness" means a person who is 
19experiencing chronic homelessness, a person experiencing literal homelessness or a 
20person who is a long-term stayer.
21E.  "Person who is a long-term stayer" means a person who, over the course of one 
22year, stays over 180 days in shelters or outdoors.
232.  Rental subsidy program established.  A rental subsidy program, referred to in 
24this section as "the program," is established within the Maine State Housing Authority.  
25The purpose of the program is to assist persons experiencing long-term homelessness 
26with housing assistance and rental subsidies.
273.  Eligibility.  The Maine State Housing Authority shall determine the eligibility of 
28and provide assistance under the program to a person experiencing long-term 
29homelessness in the following order of priority:
30A.  To a person who is a long-term stayer;
31B.  To a person experiencing chronic homelessness; and
32C.  To a person experiencing literal homelessness.
334.  Rental subsidy.  A rental subsidy must be provided to a person eligible for the 
34program pursuant to subsection 3.  The Maine State Housing Authority shall adopt rules 
35establishing the amount of the rental subsidy available to a person eligible for the 
Page 2 - 129LR1290(01)-11program and the length of time the rental subsidy is available to a person eligible for the 
2program.
35.  Services.  Services provided to a person eligible for the program pursuant to 
4subsection 3 must be administered by regional committees organized by the Maine State 
5Housing Authority.  The regional committees shall provide a person eligible for the 
6program assistance with navigation of available services, housing placement and housing 
7retention support services.
86.  Fund.  The rental subsidy program fund, referred to as "the fund," is established 
9as a nonlapsing fund within the Maine State Housing Authority pursuant to the following 
10conditions.
11A.  All money appropriated for inclusion in the fund and private donations for the 
12program may be included in the fund.
13B.  The Maine State Housing Authority may apply money in the fund for purposes 
14authorized by this subchapter.
157.  Report.  Beginning January 15, 2020, and annually thereafter, the Maine State 
16Housing Authority shall submit a report to the joint standing committees of the 
17Legislature having jurisdiction over housing matters and health and human services 
18matters that includes performance measures of the program, including, but not limited to, 
19housing stability and retention for persons eligible for the program pursuant to subsection 
203.
218.  Rules.  The Maine State Housing Authority shall adopt rules to implement this 
22section.  Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined 
23in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
24SUMMARY
25This bill establishes a rental subsidy program within the Maine State Housing 
26Authority for persons experiencing long-term homelessness.  The rental subsidy program 
27provides a rental subsidy and housing retention services to a person eligible for the 
28program.
