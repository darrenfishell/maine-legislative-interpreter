4Constitution, Art. II, §4 is amended to read:
5Section 4.  Time of state election; absentee voting.  The election of Senators 
6and Representatives shall be on the Tuesday following the first Monday of November 
7biennially forever and the election of, except as provided in this section.  Beginning with 
8the general election held in 2022, the election of Senators and the Governor shall be on 
9the Tuesday following the first Monday of November every 4 years.  The Legislature 
10under proper enactment shall authorize and provide for voting by citizens of the State 
11absent therefrom in the Armed Forces of the United States or of this State and for voting 
12by other citizens absent or physically incapacitated for reasons deemed sufficient.
13Constitution, Art. IV, Pt. Second, §1 is amended to read:
14Section 1.  Number of Senators.  The Senate shall consist of an odd number of 
15Senators, not less than 31 nor more than 35, except that, on or after December 7, 2022, 
16the Senate shall consist of 32 Senators, elected at the same time and for the same term as 
17Representatives by the qualified electors of the districts into which the State shall be from 
18time to time divided except that beginning with the general election held in 2022, 
19Senators are elected as provided in Article II, Section 4.
20Constitution, Art. IV, Pt. Second, §2, first ¶ is amended to read:
21Section 2.  Submission of reapportionment plan to Secretary of Senate; 
22Legislature's action on commission's plan; division of State into Senatorial 
23Districts; division by Supreme Judicial Court.  The Legislature which shall 
24convene in the year 2013, and also the Legislature which shall convene in the year 2021 
25and every tenth year thereafter, shall cause the State to be divided into districts for the 
26choice of a Senator from each district, using the same method as provided in Article IV, 
27Part First, Section 2 for apportionment of Representative Districts, and the Legislature 
28which shall convene on January 5, 2022 shall reapportion the State so that the Senatorial 
29Districts coincide with the counties of the State, resulting in the voters of each county 
30having the right to vote for 2 Senators.
31Constitution, Art. IV, Pt. Second, §5 is amended to read:
32Section 5.  Determination of Senators elected; procedure for filling 
33vacancies.  The Senate shall, on said first Wednesday of December, biennially until the 
34general election held in 2022 and quadrennially thereafter determine who is elected by a 
35plurality of votes to be Senator in each district.  All vacancies in the Senate arising from 
36death, resignation, removal from the State or like causes, and also vacancies, if any, 
37which may occur because of the failure of any district to elect by a plurality of votes the 
38Senator to which said district shall be entitled shall be filled by an immediate election in 
39the unrepresented district.  The Governor shall issue a proclamation therefor and therein 
40fix the time of such election.

17Constitutional referendum procedure; form of question; effective date. 
18Resolved:  That the municipal officers of this State shall notify the inhabitants of their 
19respective cities, towns and plantations to meet, in the manner prescribed by law for 
20holding a statewide election, at a statewide election held in the month of November 
21following the passage of this resolution, to vote upon the ratification of the amendment 
22proposed in this resolution by voting upon the following question:
23"Do you favor amending the Constitution of Maine to reduce the size of 
24the Senate from no more than 35 members to 32 members by 
25reapportioning senatorial districts to coincide with the counties of the 
26State and to increase the Senate term from 2 to 4 years, effective 
27December 7, 2022?"
28The legal voters of each city, town and plantation shall vote by ballot on this question 
29and designate their choice by a cross or check mark placed within the corresponding 
30square below the word "Yes" or "No."  The ballots must be received, sorted, counted and 
31declared in open ward, town and plantation meetings and returns made to the Secretary of 
32State in the same manner as votes for members of the Legislature.  The Governor shall 
33review the returns.  If it appears that a majority of the legal votes are cast in favor of the 
34amendment, the Governor shall proclaim that fact without delay and the amendment 
35becomes part of the Constitution of Maine on the date of the proclamation.
36Secretary of State shall prepare ballots. Resolved: That the Secretary of State 
37shall prepare and furnish to each city, town and plantation all ballots, returns and copies 
38of this resolution necessary to carry out the purposes of this referendum.
