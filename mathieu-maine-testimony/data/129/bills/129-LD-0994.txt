3Whereas, in 2010, the 124th Legislature enacted An Act To Implement the 
4Recommendations of the Governor's Ocean Energy Task Force, Public Law 2009, 
5chapter 615; and
6Whereas, Public Law 2009, chapter 615, Part A, section 6 directed the Public 
7Utilities Commission, in accordance with the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 35-A, section 
83210-C, to conduct a competitive solicitation for proposals for long-term contracts to 
9supply installed capacity and associated renewable energy and renewable energy credits 
10from one or more deep-water offshore wind energy pilot projects or tidal energy 
11demonstration projects; and
12Whereas, in July 2013, the Public Utilities Commission issued a supplemental 
13request for proposals for long-term contracts for deep-water offshore wind energy pilot 
14projects; and
15Whereas, in August 2013, Maine Aqua Ventus I, GP, LLC, a partnership led by the 
16University of Maine System, submitted a proposal to the Public Utilities Commission in 
17response to the request for proposals; and
18Whereas, the Public Utilities Commission evaluated the Maine Aqua Ventus 
19proposal and, by orders issued in February 2014, selected Maine Aqua Ventus as the 
20seller to transfer transmission and distribution capacity and associated energy produced 
21by its deep-water offshore wind energy pilot project, subject to certain terms and 
22conditions; and
23Whereas, consistent with the terms and conditions set forth by the Public Utilities 
24Commission in its February 2014 orders, Maine Aqua Ventus and Central Maine Power 
25Company negotiated and drafted a long-term contract for capacity and associated energy 
26following a series of meetings involving Maine Aqua Ventus, Central Maine Power 
27Company and commission staff and legal counsel and filed the final draft with the Public 
28Utilities Commission in December 2017; and
29Whereas, in January 2018, the Public Utilities Commission delayed the contract's 
30approval and solicited public comment on whether to reopen the request for proposals; 
31and
32Whereas, in June 2018, the Public Utilities Commission voted to not address the 
33long-term contract between Maine Aqua Ventus and Central Maine Power Company filed 
34with the commission in December 2017; and
35Whereas, the Legislature finds that it is in the best interest of the State to approve 
36the December 2017 long-term contract between Maine Aqua Ventus and Central Maine 
37Power Company so that the deep-water offshore wind energy pilot project may move 
38forward and generate the benefits to the State and its people previously sought by the 
39Legislature; and

5Sec. 1.  Public Utilities Commission to approve long-term contract 
6between Maine Aqua Ventus and Central Maine Power Company. Resolved:  
7That, within 30 days of the effective date of this resolve, the Public Utilities Commission 
8shall approve the long-term contract for capacity and associated energy between Maine 
9Aqua Ventus I, GP, LLC and Central Maine Power Company filed with the commission 
10in December 2017.
11Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
12legislation takes effect when approved.
13SUMMARY
14This resolve directs the Public Utilities Commission to approve the long-term 
15contract for capacity and associated energy between Maine Aqua Ventus I, GP, LLC and 
16Central Maine Power Company submitted to the commission in December 2017.
