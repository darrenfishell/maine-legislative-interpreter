14Sec. 1.  20-A MRSA c. 610 is enacted to read:
15CHAPTER 610
16SCHOOL REVOLVING MAINTENANCE FUND
17ยง16001.  School Revolving Maintenance Fund
181.  Fund established.  The School Revolving Maintenance Fund, referred to in this 
19section as "the fund," is established as a nonlapsing fund within the department.
202.  Administration.  The department shall administer the fund and invest the fund 
21balances. The fund balances must be invested in the same manner as permitted for 
22investment of funds belonging to the State or held in the State Treasury.  The fund must 
23be established and held separate and apart from any other funds or money of the State or 
24the department and must be used and administered exclusively for the purposes 
25authorized in this section. The fund consists of:
26A.  Sums that may be appropriated by the Legislature or transferred to the fund by the 
27Treasurer of State;
28B.  Principal and interest received from the repayment of loans made from the fund;
29C.  Interest earned from the investment of fund balances; and
30D.  Other funds from any public or private source received for use for any of the 
31purposes for which the fund has been established.
323.  Purposes.  The fund may be used:
33A.  To make loans to school administrative units for minor school infrastructure 
34maintenance and construction projects;
Page 2 - 129LR0817(01)-11B.  To invest available fund balances and credit the net interest income on those 
2balances to the fund; and
3C.  To pay the costs of the department associated with the administration of the fund 
4and projects financed by the fund.
54.  Priorities.  Periodically, and at least annually, the department shall prepare a 
6project priority list of those school administrative units whose projects are eligible for 
7loans under this section.  In establishing the priority list, the department shall grant 
8special consideration to projects that include urgent health and safety needs.
95.  Eligibility terms.  The department shall develop by rule the terms of repayment of 
10loans made pursuant to this section, which may not carry an interest rate higher than 0%. 
11A loan may be made only if a project is on the priority list prepared under subsection 4. 
12The repayment period may vary depending upon the financial condition of a school 
13administrative unit as identified by the department.
146.  Establishment of accounts.  The department may establish accounts and 
15subaccounts within the fund as it determines desirable to effectuate the purposes of this 
16section.
177.  Rules.  The department shall adopt rules necessary to implement this section, 
18including rules on the types of projects that are considered minor school infrastructure 
19maintenance and construction projects and on project funding limits.  Rules adopted by 
20the department to implement this section are major substantive rules pursuant to Title 5, 
21chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
22Sec. 2.  Major substantive rules; School Revolving Maintenance Fund.  By 
23June 1, 2019, the Department of Education shall provisionally adopt and submit to the 
24Legislature the rules required under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, section 
2516001.
26Sec. 3.  Transfer from General Fund unappropriated surplus; School 
27Revolving Maintenance Fund.  Notwithstanding any other provision of law, the State 
28Controller shall transfer $30,000,000 from the General Fund unappropriated surplus to 
29the School Revolving Maintenance Fund, Other Special Revenue Funds account within 
30the Department of Education no later than September 1, 2019.
31Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
32legislation takes effect when approved.
33SUMMARY
34This bill establishes the School Revolving Maintenance Fund as a nonlapsing fund 
35administered by the Department of Education to make loans to school administrative 
36units for minor school infrastructure maintenance and construction projects.  The bill 
37requires the Department of Education to adopt major substantive rules and transfers 
38$30,000,000 from the General Fund unappropriated surplus to the School Revolving 
39Maintenance Fund.
