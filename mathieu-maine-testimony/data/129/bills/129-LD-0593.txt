2become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, behavioral health services are important for the overall health and 
4well-being of the recipients of those services; and
5Whereas, unequal rates of reimbursement for employees providing direct care 
6services create huge barriers to employment and retention; and
7Whereas, instability of the direct care services workforce reduces access to services 
8by those in need of them; and
9Whereas, a lack of receipt of these behavioral health services could lead to a need 
10for more expensive treatments; and
11Whereas, this legislation requires that an amendment to rules to address this 
12inequality be made by July 1, 2019; and
13Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
14the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
15immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
16therefore, be it
17Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to increase 
18MaineCare rates. Resolved: That, no later than July 1, 2019, the Department of 
19Health and Human Services shall amend rule Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, 
20Chapter III, Section 65, Behavioral Health Services to increase reimbursement rates by 
218%.  The rule must specify that the increase in reimbursement rates must be applied to 
22wages and benefits for employees who provide direct care services and not to 
23administrators or managers.
24Sec. 2.  Increase to employee wages. Resolved: That to qualify for the 
25reimbursement rate increase pursuant to section 1, an agency providing services must 
26demonstrate, to the satisfaction of the Department of Health and Human Services, that an 
27increase in wages and benefits has been granted to employees providing direct care 
28services that equals the amount of the projected increase in reimbursement to be received.  
29The increase in wages and benefits must be paid or granted retroactively from the date the 
30department begins reimbursing at the increased rates.
31Sec. 3.  Appropriations and allocations. Resolved: That the following 
32appropriations and allocations are made.

11SUMMARY
12This resolve provides funding to increase rates by 8% in rule Chapter 101: 
13MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter III, Section 65, Behavioral Health Services.  The 
14resolve also specifies that the increase in reimbursement rates must be applied to wages 
15and benefits for employees who provide direct care services and not to administrators or 
16managers and that to qualify for the rate increase an agency providing services must 
17demonstrate, to the satisfaction of the Department of Health and Human Services, that an 
18increase in wages and benefits has been granted to employees providing direct care
