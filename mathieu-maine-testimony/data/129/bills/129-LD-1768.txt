14SUMMARY
15This bill makes changes to the laws governing barbering and cosmetology.
16It eliminates the demonstrator license.
17It eliminates the barber license and renames the "limited barbering" license the 
18"barber hair styling" license.
19It clarifies provisions regarding establishment owners and booth owners by creating 
20level 1 establishments and level 2 establishments and providing that leased space is 
21considered an establishment.
22It replaces references to "shop" with "establishment."
23It clarifies that the cosmetology scope of practice includes shaving.
24It exempts certain cosmetology-associated activities from licensure requirements.
25It allows the practice of cosmetology, barber hair styling, aesthetics and nail 
26technology on inmates of institutions of the Department of Corrections.
27It changes the 7-day reporting requirement for the change of ownership or location of 
28an establishment to a 10-day reporting requirement for consistency with general reporting 
29requirements of the Department of Professional and Financial Regulation, Office of 
30Professional and Occupational Regulation under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 10, 
31section 8003-G.  Where applicable, the bill adds the word "calendar" to other reporting 
32requirements.  The bill eliminates the necessity of submitting a new application and fee 
33for an establishment location change and makes a location change of a level 1 
34establishment or level 2 establishment a reporting requirement.  The new location is still 
35subject to meeting all current laws and rules and inspection requirements.
36It repeals provisions regarding the age and minimum education requirements for 
37initial practice licenses.
Page 11 - 129LR2370(01)-11It clarifies reporting requirements for trainees for a change in employer or qualified 
2supervisor and also clarifies the number of trainees per establishment that may be trained 
3at one time.
4It authorizes the Director of the Office of Professional and Occupational Regulation 
5to issue a license to a person who holds a valid license in another jurisdiction of the 
6United States.
7Current law requires that a student enrolling in a course of study be at least 16 years 
8of age.  The bill adjusts the minimum school enrollment age to 15 years of age with the 
9stipulation that the student turns 16 years of age at some point during the student's course 
10of study.
11It exempts career and technical education centers that do not collect student tuition 
12for a course of study from surety bond and financial audit requirements.
