3SUMMARY
4This bill authorizes a person to provide alternative therapy or collaborative treatment 
5to an animal only if the person holds a license, registration or certification from the entity 
6in the State responsible for issuing licenses, certifications or registrations for the 
7alternative therapy or collaborative treatment or meets certain educational requirements, 
8has a referral to provide the alternative therapy or collaborative treatment from a licensed 
9veterinarian and is under the direct or indirect supervision of a licensed veterinarian.  The 
10bill also defines "alternative therapy or collaborative treatment" to include acupuncture, 
11dentistry, homeopathic or chiropractic procedures and physical or massage therapy.
