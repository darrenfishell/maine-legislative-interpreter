1 SUMMARY 
2 This amendment strikes and replaces the bill and makes the following changes: 
3 1. Lists as mandatory the specific codes that must comprise the Maine Uniform 
4 Building and Energy Code; 
5 2. Requires that each new version of a code or standard that is part of the Maine 
6 Uniform Building and Energy Code must be reviewed as it is published and that the 
7 Technical Building Codes and Standards Board must adopt either the most recent edition 
8 or the edition previous of each code or standard; 
9 3. Makes the Maine Uniform Building and Energy Code applicable statewide but 
10 does not require municipalities under 4,000 residents to enforce the code; and 
11 4. Provides that the Director of the Efficiency Maine Trust serves as a nonvoting 
12 member of the Technical Building Codes and Standards Board.
