16SUMMARY
17This amendment replaces the bill.  It requires that owners and managers of log yards 
18and mill sites must present a copy of a trip ticket to a forest ranger in the log yard or mill 
19site upon request.  The amendment adds a requirement related to the transportation of 
20forest products by providing that a landowner of 50,000 or more acres of forest land in 
21the State may not allow the transportation of forest products harvested on the landowner's 
22land from a location in the State to another location in the State in violation of federal law 
23or regulation or an international trade agreement that prohibits the transportation of goods 
24from a location in the United States to another location in the United States.  The 
25amendment provides that a landowner with 2 prior violations of the new transportation 
26requirement is ineligible for classification of the landowner's land under the Maine Tree 
27Growth Tax Law and the landowner may not receive certain tax incentives or state grants 
28or other state funding.
