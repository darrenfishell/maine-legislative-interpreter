9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT" A "to S.P. 556, L.D. 1691, Bill, "An Act To Ban 
10 Use of Aerial Herbicide Spraying for the Purpose of Deforestation" 
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
12 'Resolve, Directing the Board of Pesticides Control To Work with the Forest 
13 Products Industry To Monitor Aerial Herbicide Applications' 
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following: 
15 'Sec. 1. Monitoring of aerial herbicide applications. Resolved: That the 
16 Board of Pesticides Control, established in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 22, section 
17 1471-B, shall work with representatives of the forest products industry who conduct 
18 aerial application of herbicides for the purpose of silviculture, including reforestation, 
19 regeneration or vegetation control, to monitor aerial applications of herbicides through a 
20 neutral 3rd-party entity determined by the board. The one-time monitoring of aerial 
21 applications required under this section is contingent upon the receipt of outside funds for 
22 this purpose. The board shall report to the Joint Standing Committee on Agriculture, 
23 Conservation and Forestry with findings and recommendations related to the monitoring 
24 of aerial herbicide application no later than February 1, 2020. The monitoring of aerial 
25 applications of herbicides must conclude upon submission of the report to the joint 
26 standing committee. The joint standing committee may submit a bill relating to the 
27 subject matter of the report to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.' 
28 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or 
29 section number to read consecutively. 
30 SUMMARY

32 replaces it with a resolve. The amendment requires the Board of Pesticides Control 
33 within the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to work with
