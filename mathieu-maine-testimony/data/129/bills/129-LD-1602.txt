Page 1 - 129LR0438(01)-11Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, this resolve establishes the Working Group on Mental Health to review 
4the State's mental health system and propose a mental health plan for the State; and
5Whereas, community-based mental health services are the foundation for a healthy 
6Maine, and the State is currently housing too many people with behavioral health needs 
7in jails and emergency rooms; and
8Whereas, the review must be initiated before the 90-day period expires in order that 
9the study may be completed and a report submitted in time for submission to the next 
10legislative session; and
11Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
12the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
13immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
14therefore, be it
15Sec. 1.  Working group established.  Resolved:  That the Working Group on 
16Mental Health, referred to in this resolve as "the working group," is established to review 
17the State's mental health system and propose a mental health plan for the State.
18Sec. 2.  Working group membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint 
19Rule 353, the working group consists of 11 members as follows:
201.  Two members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, one of 
21whom serves on the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services and one of 
22whom serves on the Joint Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety;
232.  One member of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
24House who serves on the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary;
253.  One member representing consumers of mental health services appointed by the 
26Speaker of the House;
274.  One member representing the National Alliance on Mental Illness Maine 
28organization appointed by the President of the Senate;
295.  One member representing providers of homeless shelter services appointed by the 
30Speaker of the House;
316.  One member representing hospitals in the State appointed by the President of the 
32Senate;
337.  One member representing county sheriffs appointed by the Speaker of the House;
348.  The Attorney General or the Attorney General's designee;

310.  The Commissioner of Corrections or the commissioner's designee.
4The chairs of the working group shall invite the participation on the working group of 
5a representative of the Judicial Department.
6Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate 
7chair and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
8working group.
9Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of working group.  Resolved:  That all 
10appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
11resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
12Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
13the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the working group.  If 30 days or 
14more after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have 
15been made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant 
16authority for the working group to meet and conduct its business.
17Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the working group shall review the State's mental 
18health system and propose a mental health plan for the State.  As part of its review, the 
19working group shall examine:
201.  Information on total state and federal dollars spent on children's and adult 
21behavioral health care as coded by Medicaid and where those dollars are currently spent;
222.  Access to mental health care in the State, including issues associated with waiting 
23lists, geographic barriers to access and lack of adequate reimbursement to community-
24based programs that prevents those programs from reaching optimum capacity;
253.  The quality of outcomes;
264.  The costs required to provide mental health services in emergency rooms, 
27inpatient settings, homeless shelters, jails and prisons as compared with the costs required 
28to provide mental health services such as medication management, daily living support, 
29peer support and other therapies provided in community-based settings;
305.  An assessment of assets and deficits; and
316.  The projected effect of MaineCare expansion on the provision of mental health 
32services.
33Based on the information collected pursuant to this section, the working group shall 
34propose a mental health plan for the State.
35Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human 
36Services shall provide necessary staffing services to the working group.

7Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
8legislation takes effect when approved.
9SUMMARY
10This resolve establishes the Working Group on Mental Health to assess the State's 
11capacity to serve Maine citizens with behavioral health needs and propose a 
12comprehensive mental health plan for the State.
