11Sec. 2.  30-A MRSA §2605-A is enacted to read:
12§2605-A.  Conflicts of interest; contracts for professional services
13A person contracted to provide professional services to a municipality may not have a 
14direct or indirect pecuniary interest in any benefit that may arise from the professional 
15services provided to the municipality other than payment under the contract.  For 
16purposes of this section, a person is deemed to have a pecuniary interest in any benefit 
17that may arise from professional services provided to the municipality under a contract if 
18the person has an ownership interest in more than 10% of the land area within the 
19municipality.  For purposes of this section, "professional services" includes, but is not 
20limited to, services provided by accountants, architects, attorneys, consultants and 
21engineers.

Page 2 - 129LR2063(01)-11SUMMARY
2This bill minimizes conflicts of interest in municipal government operations.  The bill 
3precludes the municipal treasurer and tax collector, or town officer performing the duties 
4of either, from simultaneously acting as the municipality's code enforcement officer.  The 
5bill requires the town manager to implement a training program for municipal officers 
6and employees on the ethical standards applicable to and the performance risks associated 
7with hiring professional consultants.  The bill prohibits a person contracted to provide 
8professional services to a municipality from having a direct or indirect pecuniary interest 
9in the business of the municipality.
