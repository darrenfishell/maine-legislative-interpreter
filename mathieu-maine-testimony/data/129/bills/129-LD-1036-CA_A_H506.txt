COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "JI" to H.P. 766, L.D. 1036, "Resolve, Establishing

Amend the resolve by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
'Resolve, Establishing a Task Force To Study the Creation of a Comprehensive 
Career and Technical Education System and Increased Crosswalks for Academic 
Credit between Secondary Schools and Career and Technical Education Programs'

353, the task force consists of 14 members as follows:

Education and Cultural Affairs; 
B. One member who is a current career and technical education high school 
administrator; 
C. One member who is on the State Board of Education; and 
D. One member who is a principal of a secondary school;

A. One member of the House of Representatives who is a member of the Joint 
Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs; 
B. One member who is a current career and technical education high school 
administrator; 
C. One member who is on the State Board of Education; and 
D. One member who is a superintendent of a school administrative unit; 
3. Five members appointed by the Governor as follows:

Fiscal Note 
Legislative Cost/Study 
Legislative Cost/Study 
The general operating expenses of this study are projected to be $1,250 in fiscal year 2019-20. An estimated $6,190 is 
available in each of fiscal years 2019-20 and 2020-21 in the Legislature's proposed budget for legislative studies as 
well as any balances canied over from prior years for this purpose. Whether these amounts are sufficient to fund all 
studies will depend on the number of studies authorized by the Legislative Council and the Legislature. The additional 
costs of providing staffing assistance to the study during the interim can be absorbed utilizing existing budgeted staff 
resources.The task force is required to seek outside funding contributions to fully fund the costs of the study. If 
sufficient funding is not received within 30 days of the effective date of this resolve, no meetings are authorized and 
no expenses may be incuned. 
Fiscal Detail and Notes 
Additional costs to the State Board of Education, the Department of Education and the University of Maine System to 
have representatives serve on the task force can be absorbed within existing budgeted resources.
