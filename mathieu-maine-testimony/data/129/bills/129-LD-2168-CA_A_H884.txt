5SUMMARY
6 This amendment replaces the bill, which is a concept draft.
7 This bill requires all law enforcement agencies, beginning July 1, 2022, to collect 
8 information about each person stopped for a traffic violation.  The information must include 
9 the characteristics of race, color, ethnicity, gender and age of those persons.  The 
10 identification of such characteristics must be based on the observation and perception of 
11 the law enforcement officer responsible for reporting the stop.  The person stopped may 
12 not be required to provide the information.  The information must also include whether a 
13 warning or citation was issued, an arrest was made or a search was conducted as a result of 
14 the stop.  Each law enforcement agency must report the information to the Attorney 
15 General, who is required to report the information, along with analysis and any 
16 recommendations, to the joint standing committees of the Legislature having jurisdiction 
17 over judiciary matters and criminal justice and public safety matters beginning January 15, 
18 2023 and annually thereafter.  The report must be made available to the public.
19 The Attorney General is directed to adopt rules governing the recording, retention and 
20 reporting of the information.  In adopting the rules, the Attorney General must consult with 
21 the Commissioner of Public Safety and other interested parties, including law enforcement 
22 agencies and community, professional, research, civil liberties and civil rights 
23 organizations and persons with lived experience of being profiled.  The Attorney General 
24 must ensure that the parties consulted represent the racial and ethnic diversity of the State. 
25 The name and personally identifiable information of a law enforcement officer or any 
26 other person involved in any activity for which information is collected and compiled under 
27 these provisions is confidential.
