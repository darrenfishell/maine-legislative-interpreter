12 Amend the resolve by striking out all of section 1 and inserting the following: 
13 'Sec. 1. Department of Health and Human Services to convene a 
14 stakeholder group. Resolved: That the Department of Health and Human Services, 
15 in consultation with the Department of Education, shall convene a stakeholder group to 
16 determine the capacity of the State to respond to its child fmd responsibilities as required 
17 under the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act and to provide early and 
18 periodic screening, diagnostic and treatment services, referred to in this resolve as 
19 "screening, diagnostic and treatment services," to children from birth to 8 years of age, 
20 the gaps in services and the costs of addressing those gaps. The Department of Health 
21 and Human Services shall coordinate the collection and analysis of data from across the 
22 department and from organizations that conduct developmental screenings or offer 
23 services or treatment to children from birth to 8 years of age, including through Head 
24 Start programs, Early Head Start programs, the Child Development Services System as 
25 defmed in section 7001, public health nursing organizations, the Department of Health 
26 and Human Services' home visiting service and the Department of Education. The 
27 Department of Health and Human Services shall submit a report with the findings to the 
28 Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services. The report must include the 
29 following: 
30 1. Data on the number, ages and geographic locations of children from birth to 8 
31 years of age receiving screening, diagnostic and treatment services; 
32 2. The capacity of the State to ensure that all children from birth to 8 years of age 
33 who require screening, diagnostic and treatment services are located and receive services; 
34 3. Current levels of federal and state funding designated or available to support child 
35 find services, screening, diagnostic and treatment services and early intervention and 
36 special education services for children from birth to 8 years of age;
