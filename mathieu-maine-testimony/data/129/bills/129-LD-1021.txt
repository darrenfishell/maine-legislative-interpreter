4The Maine Commission on Indigent Legal Services, established by Title 5, section

6efficient, high-quality representation to indigent criminal defendants, juvenile defendants,

11competent counsel in a manner that is fair and consistent throughout the State and to 
12ensure adequate funding of a statewide system of indigent legal services, which must be 
13provided and managed in a fiscally responsible manner, free from undue political 
14interference and conflicts of interest.

2This bill provides that when a probate court appoints an attorney for a party in a 
3guardianship or protective proceeding, if the party is indigent or a minor, the attorney's 
4fees must be paid by the Maine Commission on Indigent Legal Services.
