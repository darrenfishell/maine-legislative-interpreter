Page 2 - 129LR2696(01)-11SUMMARY
2This bill provides that, prior to administering or supervising a pelvic examination on 
3an anesthetized or unconscious patient, a physician must obtain the patient's informed 
4consent to the pelvic examination unless the examination is within the scope of the 
5procedure or examination for which the patient has already consented, the pelvic 
6examination of an unconscious patient is required for diagnostic purposes and is 
7medically necessary or the pelvic examination is authorized pursuant to the implied 
8consent provision in the Maine Health Security Act relating to forensic examinations of 
9unconscious alleged victims of sexual assault.
