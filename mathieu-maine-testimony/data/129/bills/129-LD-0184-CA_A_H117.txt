COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A" to H.P. 147, L.D. 184, Bill, "An Act To 
Amend the Veterans' Homelessness Prevention Coordination Program" 
Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and before the summary and 
inserting the following: 
'Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and 
Whereas, the need to support homeless veterans is immediate and crucial for their 
health, safety and welfare; and 
Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislatlll'e, these facts create an emergency within 
the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
therefore, 
Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows: 
Sec.1. 37-B MRSA §513, as enacted by PL 2011, c. 329, §1, is repealed. 
Sec. 2. 37-B MRSA §513-A is enacted to read: 
§513-A. Veterans' homelessness prevention coordination 
1. Veterans' homelessness prevention partnership. The director shall, through 
one or more collaborative agreements, establish a program of partnerships with human 
services-based volunteer organizations to provide transitional housing to homeless 
veterans and coordinate efforts to remedy and prevent homelessness among veterans in 
this State. The volunteer organizations must have as their core programs addressing 
homelessness and veterans' services and have been active in the State for at least 2 years. 
Priority must be given to an organization founded, chartered or organized in the State. 
The director may accept donations from outside solU'ces and state and federal funding to 
accomplish the priorities of the partnerships. To the extent state, federal or outside

1 SUMMARY 
2 This amendment strikes and replaces the bill. It: 
3 1. Directs the Director of the Bureau of Maine Veterans' Services to establish a 
4 program of partnerships, through one or more collaborative agreements, with human 
5 services-based volunteer organizations to provide transitional housing to homeless 
6 veterans and coordinate efforts to remedy and prevent homelessness among veterans in 
7 this State; 
8 2. Provides that one of the priorities of the partnerships is to provide reimbursement 
9 to human services-based volunteer organizations that provide transitional housing to 
10 homeless veterans pursuant to collaborative agreements; 
11 3. Authorizes the Bureau of Maine Veterans' Services to adopt rules necessary to 
12 implement this partnership program, including to defme "veterans" for purposes of the 
13 program, to govern collaborative agreements with human services-based volunteer 
14 organizations and to govern the reimbursement of organizations that provide transitional 
15 housing to homeless veterans through disbursements from the Veterans' Homelessness 
16 Prevention Partnership Fund; 
17 4. Establishes the Veterans' Homelessness Prevention Partnership Fund and provides 
18 that it is to be used to reimburse human services-based volunteer organizations that 
19 provide transitional housing to homeless veterans and to otherwise carry out the purposes 
20 of the partnership program; 
21 5. Provides ongoing funding for reimbursement to human services-based volunteer 
22 organizations that provide transitional housing to homeless veterans; 
23 6. Creates 2 new positions in the Bureau of Maine Veterans' Services to help 
24 administer this partnership program; and 
25 7. Includes an emergency preamble and clause.

The bill includes an ongoing General Fund appropriation of $600,000 beginning in fiscal year 2019-20 to the 
Department of Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management for reimbursements to human services-based volunteer 
organizations that provide transitional housing to homeless veterans. It also includes General Fund appropriations of 
$171,429 in fiscal year 2019-20 and $176,272 in fiscal year 2020-21 for one Office Associate II position to provide 
auditing and accounting services and for one Public Service Coordinator I position to manage and administer the 
veterans' homelessness prevention partnership program. 
The bill establishes the Veterans' Homelessness Prevention Partnership Fund within the Department of Defense, 
Veterans and Emergency Management with a base Other Special Revenue Funds allocation of $500 annually 
beginning in fiscal year 2019-20 to allow expenditure of any revenue received from any source for the purposes of the 
program.
