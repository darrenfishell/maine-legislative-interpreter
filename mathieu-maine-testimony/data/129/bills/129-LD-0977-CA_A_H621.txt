Sec. 5. 36 MRSA §5219-L, sub-§6 is enacted to read: 
6. Evaluation; specific public policy objectives; performance measures. The 
credit provided under this section is subject to ongoing legislative review in accordance 
with Title 3, chapter 37. In developing evaluation parameters to perform the review. the 
Office of Program Evaluation and Government Accountability shall consider: 
A. That the specific public policy objectives of the credit provided under this section 
are: 
(1) To create high-quality jobs in the State by encouraging investments in 
research and development in this State and to encourage the recruitment and 
training of employees; and 
(2) To directly and indirectly improve the overall economy of the State by 
expanding the number of businesses conducting and investing in research and 
development in the State; and 
B. Performance measures, including. but not limited to: 
(1) The number, geographic distribution and income of full-time employees 
added or retained during the period being reviewed who would not have been 
added or retained in the absence of the credit: 
(2) The number and amount of investments in research and development made 
by credit recipients during the review period; and 
(3) Direct and indirect improvement in the economy of the State attributable to 
activities entitled to a credit under this section.' 
SUMMARY 
This amendment restores the super credit for substantially increased research and 
development for tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2019. The amendment also 
provides reporting and evaluation requirements to permit evaluation of the credit in 
meeting its intended purposes.

Fiscal Detail and Notes 
The bill restores the super credit for research and development expenses and would result in a reduction in Gener.al 
Fund revenue of$836,000 in fiscal year 2019-20 and $893,000 in fiscal year 2020-21. It would also result in a 
reduction in Local Government Fund revenue of $44, 000 in fiscal year 2019-20 and $4 7 ,000 in fiscal year 2020-21. 
Additional costs to the Department of Economic and Community Development to provide the required information to 
the Office of Program Evaluation and Government Accountability can be absorbed within existing budgeted 
resources.
