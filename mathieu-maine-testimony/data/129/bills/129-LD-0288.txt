10the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
11immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
12therefore, be it
13Sec. 1.  Commission established.  Resolved:  That the Commission To Create a 
14Statewide Economic Development Plan, referred to in this resolve as "the commission," 
15is established.
16Sec. 2.  Commission membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
17353, the commission consists of 20 members appointed as follows:
181.  Two members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including 
19members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
202.  Two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
21House, including members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
22in the Legislature;
233.  The Chancellor of the University of Maine System or the chancellor's designee;
244.  The President of the Maine Community College System or the president's 
25designee;
265.  An individual representing a private organization focused on promoting economic 
27growth in the State appointed by the President of the Senate;
286.  An individual representing a private research institution appointed by the Speaker 
29of the House;
307.  The Commissioner of Economic and Community Development or the 
31commissioner's designee;
328.  The State Economist or the State Economist's designee;
339.  A member of the Maine Economic Growth Council, established in the Maine 
34Revised Statutes, Title 10, section 929-A, appointed by the President of the Senate;

14Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate 
15chair and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
16commission.
17Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
18appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
19resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
20Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
21the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
22after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
23made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority 
24for the commission to meet and conduct its business.
25Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall create a strategic, regionally 
26focused economic development plan for the State, including:
271.  An inventory and analysis of the State's competitive advantages and an inventory 
28and analysis of factors that tend to retard economic development in the State;
292.  Identification and analysis of state, national and international economic trends 
30affecting the State's economic development;
313.  Development of strategies for the promotion of business formation, expansion, 
32recruitment and retention through aggressive marketing, international development and 
33export assistance and any other strategies identified as advantageous that lead to more 
34and better jobs and higher wages for all geographic regions and disadvantaged 
35communities and populations of the State, including rural areas;
364.  Development of policies and programs to further economic diversity in the State, 
37its regions and their associated industries;
Page 3 - 129LR0394(01)-115.  Stimulation of economic development and job creation in rural areas and smaller 
2economic regions of the State, including strategies for rural marketing and the 
3development of infrastructure, both physical and electronic, in rural areas;
46.  Promotion of the successful long-term economic development of the State with 
5increased emphasis in market research and information;
67.  Plans for the generation of foreign investment in the State that creates jobs paying 
7above-average wages and that results in reverse investment in the State, including 
8programs that establish viable foreign markets, assist in meeting the financing 
9requirements of export-ready firms, broaden opportunities for international joint-venture 
10relationships, use the resources of academic and other institutions, coordinate trade 
11assistance and facilitation services and facilitate availability of and access to education 
12and training programs that ensure that requisite skills and competencies necessary to 
13compete successfully in the global marketplace are taught;
148.  Identification of business sectors that are of current or future importance to the 
15State's economy and to the State's global business image and development of specific 
16strategies to promote the development of these sectors;
179.  Strategies for talent development necessary to encourage economic development 
18growth in the State, taking into account factors such as the State's talent supply chain, 
19education and training opportunities and available workforce; and
2010.  Identification of and a strategy for investing in research and development for 
21innovation in manufacturing as well as other types of business development.
22Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Department of Economic and 
23Community Development shall provide necessary staffing services to the commission.
24Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, no later than 
25January 15, 2020, the commission shall submit a preliminary report, including suggested 
26legislation, for presentation to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
27jurisdiction over economic development matters in the Second Regular Session of the 
28129th Legislature.  No later than October 1, 2020, the commission shall submit a final 
29report that includes its findings and recommendations, which may include suggested 
30legislation to implement the plan under section 5 of this resolve and to establish a 
31permanent commission to monitor the progress of the plan, for presentation to the joint 
32standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over economic development 
33matters in the First Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
34Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
35legislation takes effect when approved.
36SUMMARY
37This resolve establishes the Commission To Create a Statewide Economic 
38Development Plan to study economic development issues and create a strategic, 
39regionally focused economic development plan.
