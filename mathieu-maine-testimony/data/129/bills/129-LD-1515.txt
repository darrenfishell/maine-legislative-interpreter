21SUMMARY
22This bill authorizes the Department of Public Safety, Gambling Control Board to 
23issue licenses to operate sports pools to commercial tracks and off-track betting facilities 
24in the State.  Under the bill, wagers on sports events must be made in person at a sports 
25wagering lounge and may not be transmitted over the Internet from a remote location.
26Sports pool operators may accept wagers on all professional or amateur sports events 
27except high school sports events and other events in which a majority of the participants 
28are minors.  Sports pool operators may not accept wagers from persons whose identity 
29they cannot verify; persons under 18 years of age; the director, officers and employees of 
30the sports pool operator and relatives living in the same household as those persons; 
31athletes, coaches, referees and umpires participating in the sports event; other interested 
32persons including persons who own more than 50% of the legal or beneficial interest in 
33any team if the sports event upon which the wager is placed is overseen by the person's 
34league or sports governing body; persons with confidential information that could affect 
35the outcome of the sports event; persons who are on a list of persons who are to be 
36excluded or removed from a sports wagering lounge established by the board, including 
37persons who voluntarily request to be excluded; and persons who make wagers on behalf 
38of another person.
39The bill requires the board to adopt rules regulating the operation of sports pools, 
40including rules restricting the types of wagers permitted, establishing the maximum 
Page 14 - 129LR2036(01)-11wagers that may be accepted from any one person on a single sports event, regulating the 
2design and minimum security standards for in-person sports wagering lounges located 
3within a commercial track or off-track betting facility and establishing record-keeping, 
4reporting and auditing requirements.
5The bill further requires that 18% of net sports pool income be transferred to the 
6board for distribution as follows: 1% for administrative expenses of the board; 2% for 
7primary and secondary school education in the State; 2% for the Maine Community 
8College System's scholarships program; 2% to the Agricultural Fair Support Fund; 2% to 
9the fund used to supplement harness racing purses; 2% to the Fund to Encourage Racing 
10at Maine's Commercial Tracks; 2% to the Fund to Stabilize Off-track Betting Facilities; 
11and 5% to be divided equally among the tribal governments of the federally recognized 
12Indian tribes in the State.
13Finally, the bill provides that the laws governing unlawful gambling and games of 
14chance do not apply to sports pool operators that comply with the laws governing sports 
15pools.
