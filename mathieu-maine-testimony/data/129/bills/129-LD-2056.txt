Page 1 - 129LR3086(01)-11Sec. 1.  Frequent Users System Engagement Collaborative established.  
2Resolved:  That the Frequent Users System Engagement Collaborative, referred to in 
3this resolve as "the collaborative," is established.
4Sec. 2.  Membership.  Resolved:  That the collaborative consists of the director 
5of the Maine State Housing Authority or the director's designee, who shall serve as chair, 
6and other members appointed by the director as follows:
71.  The Commissioner of Health and Human Services or the commissioner's designee;
82.  The Commissioner of Corrections or the commissioner's designee;
93.  One member representing the Statewide Homeless Council;
104.  One member representing the Maine Sheriffs' Association;
115.  One member representing providers of emergency health services; and
126.  Any other members with relevant expertise as determined by the director.
13Sec. 3.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the collaborative shall develop a plan to provide 
14stable housing and community services to 200 persons who are homeless or at risk of 
15homelessness who are the most frequent consumers of high-cost services, such as 
16psychiatric hospitals, emergency shelters, emergency rooms, police, jails and prisons.
17Sec. 4.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than January 1, 2021, the collaborative 
18shall submit a report to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction 
19over health and human services matters.  The report must include the collaborative's plan 
20to provide stable housing and community services developed pursuant to section 3, 
21including the cost savings that could be achieved by reductions in the use of high-cost 
22services and any recommendations developed by the collaborative on providing stable 
23housing and community services to persons consuming high-cost services.  The joint 
24standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services 
25matters may report out a bill to the First Regular Session of the 130th Legislature related 
26to the report.
27SUMMARY
28This resolve establishes the Frequent Users System Engagement Collaborative in 
29order to develop a plan to provide stable housing and community services to 200 persons 
30who are homeless or at risk of homelessness who are the most frequent consumers of 
31high-cost services, such as psychiatric hospitals, emergency shelters, emergency rooms, 
32police, jails and prisons.  The collaborative must submit a report to the joint standing 
33committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters 
34no later than January 1, 2021 on its plan and recommendations.  The joint standing 
35committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters 
36is authorized to report out a bill to the First Regular Session of the 130th Legislature 
37related to the report.
