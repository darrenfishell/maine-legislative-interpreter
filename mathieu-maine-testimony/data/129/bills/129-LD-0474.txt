7Sec. 2.  Home-delivered meals pilot project.  Resolved:  That, no later than 
8January 1, 2020, the Department of Health and Human Services shall submit an 
9application to the United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for 
10Medicare and Medicaid Services to request a demonstration waiver under the federal 
11Social Security Act, Section 1115 to develop a 2-year pilot project pursuant to the Maine 
12Revised Statutes, Title 22-A, section 203, subsection 5 to provide home-delivered meals 
13to individuals who are 60 years of age or older and who are homebound or at risk for 
14readmission to a health care facility.  The program under the waiver is limited to 
15individuals being released from a health care facility at high risk of readmission and have 
16received a doctor's recommendation that medically tailored food and nutrition 
17interventions are necessary for the patient's health. Patients being offered medically 
18tailored food and nutrition interventions may receive one meal per day for up to 14 days. 
19The Department of Health and Human Services shall submit a report no later than July 1, 
202022 on the results of the pilot project, including, but not limited to, the number of 
21individuals served by the pilot project; the number of home-delivered meals provided; 
22health care facility readmission rates of those served; and recommendations for 
23continuing the pilot project to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
24jurisdiction over health and human services matters at the conclusion of the pilot project.

18SUMMARY
19This bill directs the Department of Health and Human Services to request the United 
20States Department of Agriculture to waive the asset test for determining the eligibility for 
21the federal supplemental nutrition assistance program of any household in which there is 
22an individual who is 60 years of age or older. It also directs the department to develop a 
23pilot project to provide home-delivered meals to individuals who are 60 years of age or 
24older and who are homebound or at risk for readmission to a health care facility.  It 
25requires the department to report to the joint standing committee of the Legislature 
26having jurisdiction over health and human services matters at the conclusion of the pilot 
27project. The bill also provides funding to eliminate the waiting list for home-delivered 
28meals to homebound individuals who are over 60 years of age who cannot prepare meals
