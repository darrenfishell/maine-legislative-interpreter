3ยง419-E.  Coal tar sealant products
41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Coal tar" means a viscous substance obtained by the destructive distillation of 
7coal and containing levels of polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons in excess of 10,000 
8milligrams per kilogram.  "Coal tar" includes, but is not limited to, refined coal tar, 
9high temperature coal tar and coal tar pitch.
10B.  "Coal tar sealant product" means a surface-applied sealant product that contains 
11coal tar or coal tar pitch volatiles.
12C.  "Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons" means a group of compounds that are 
13by-products of incomplete combustion, that include several carcinogens and that, in 
14high quantities, are characterized as toxic hazardous waste.
152.  Sale of coal tar sealant products prohibited.  Beginning October 1, 2020, a 
16person may not sell at wholesale or retail a coal tar sealant product that is labeled as 
17containing coal tar.
183.  Application of coal tar sealant products prohibited.  Beginning October 1, 
192021, a person may not apply a coal tar sealant product on a driveway or parking area.
204.  Exemptions.  A person may request an exemption from the prohibitions in 
21subsections 2 and 3 by submitting a written request to the commissioner.  The request 
22must include the reason an exemption is needed.  The commissioner may exempt a 
23person from the prohibitions in subsections 2 and 3 if the commissioner determines that  
24the person is researching the effects of a coal tar sealant product on the environment or 
25the person is developing an alternative technology and the use of a coal tar sealant 
26product is required for research or development.
275.  Penalties.  A person who violates the provisions of this section is subject to 
28penalties under section 349.
29SUMMARY
30This bill prohibits the sale of coal tar sealant products beginning October 1, 2020 and 
31prohibits the application of coal tar sealant products on driveways or parking areas 
32beginning October 1, 2021.  The Commissioner of Environmental Protection may grant 
33an exemption from the prohibitions for research or development purposes.
