24 SUMMARY 
25 This amendment clarifies the bill regarding the exemption from certain state overtime 
26 pay requirements of executive branch and judicial branch employees engaged in fire 
27 protection activities and law enforcement activities by specifying that those employees 
28 are not entitled to overtime for working more than 40 hours in any one week only if those 
29 employees are eligible to have overtime pay calculated and paid in accordance with the 
30 standards set forth under the specified federal law. It also provides that parties are not 
31 prohibited from negotiating an agreement that provides for the payment of overtime pay 
32 that exceeds federal law.

Fiscal Note 
Potential current biennium savings -All Funds 
The bill excludes executive branch and judicial branch employees engaged in fire protection and law enforcement 
activities from state laws pertaining to payment of overtime only if they are eligible to have overtime pay calculated 
and paid in accordance with the standards set forth in federal law. It also allows. parties to negotiate an agreement 
allowing them to receive overtime that exceeds the requirements of federal law. To the extent that a group of affected 
employees chooses not to negotiate such an agreement or are unable to, savings could be realized. An estimate of the 
potential savings cannot be detennined at this time.
