51.  Reimbursement.  By December 31, 2019, the department shall provide for an 
6enhanced crisis stabilization reimbursement rate under the MaineCare program for 
7medically monitored crisis support and intervention provided to a person 18 years of age 
8or older with mental health and co-occurring substance use disorders who seeks treatment 
9voluntarily.  Medically monitored crisis support and intervention includes, but is not 
10limited to: psychiatric medical evaluation; psychiatric stabilization; substance 
11detoxification; 24-hour nursing care; medication stabilization, monitoring, administration 
12and education; and individual, group and family counseling.
132.  Rulemaking.  The department shall adopt rules to implement this section.  Rules 
14adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
15chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
16SUMMARY
17This bill directs the Department of Health and Human Services to provide for an 
18enhanced crisis stabilization reimbursement rate under the MaineCare program for 
19medically monitored crisis support and intervention provided to persons 18 years of age 
20or older with mental health and co-occurring substance use disorders who voluntarily 
21seek treatment.
