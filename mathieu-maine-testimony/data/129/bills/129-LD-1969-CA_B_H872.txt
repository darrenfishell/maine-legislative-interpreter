COMMITTEE AMENDMENT1SUMMARY
2This amendment is the minority report of the committee.  It replaces the bill, which is 
3a concept draft.  It requires the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, 
4Bureau of General Services to inventory all state-owned office buildings to determine the 
5presence of asbestos, lead, black mold or other substances that may be harmful to human 
6health.  It requires property owners of buildings leased to the State to conduct a similar 
7inventory and report the results to the bureau. The amendment requires the bureau to 
8establish and maintain a database containing the results of the inventories and make the 
9database easily accessible electronically to employee collective bargaining agents, 
10employees working in state office buildings with asbestos, lead, black mold or other 
11substances that may be harmful to human health, individuals who perform maintenance, 
12repair or custodial services, firefighters, the Department of Labor, the Department of 
13Health and Human Services, Maine Center for Disease Control and Prevention and 
14members of the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over state 
15and local government matters.  It requires the database to be consulted before 
16maintenance of or repair to a state-owned building by a state employee and the provision 
17of protective gear to the employee if appropriate.  The department is required to submit a 
18biennial report and may adopt rules to implement these requirements.
19The amendment also provides funding for personnel in the bureau to carry out the 
20requirements of this legislation.

Fiscal Detail and Notes
The bill requires the Department of Administrative and Fi nancial Services (DAFS) to inventory all state-owned 
buildings where state employees work for the presence of ha rmful substances. It also requires DAFS to establish and 
maintain a database of the results from this inventory and make that inventory available to multiple different state 
departments and agencies as well as organizations. DAFS ha s indicated it will require two full time positions to meet 
these requirements. The bill establishes two full time Engi neering Technician IV positions and provides a General 
Fund appropriation in fiscal year 2020- 21 of $95,243 for these positions, assuming a start date of January 1, 2021. Fiscal Note129th MAINE LEGISLATURE
