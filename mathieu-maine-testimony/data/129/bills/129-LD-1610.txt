4Sec. 4.  Deed and restrictions.  Resolved:  That the property must be conveyed 
5by quit claim deed without covenants, subject to the following deed restrictions:  The 
6Town of Carrabassett Valley shall in perpetuity retain title to the property and may not 
7sell or otherwise transfer any interest, in whole or in part, in the property except that the 
8Town of Carrabassett Valley may lease portions of the property as long as the uses are 
9consistent with the uses specified in this resolve.  The property must remain open and 
10available for use and enjoyment by the public at large.  Use of the property must be 
11dedicated for purposes of public outdoor recreation, including, but not limited to: natural 
12history study; hiking; camping, other than in motor vehicles; cross-country skiing; 
13hunting; fishing; fisheries and wildlife management; skating; timber management and 
14harvesting under a management plan prepared by a licensed professional forester; and 
15attendant roads and parking.  The property must be maintained in an essentially natural 
16and undeveloped condition, except that up to 25 contiguous acres in the aggregate, 
17including any development in existence on the effective date of this resolve, may be 
18developed for any municipal facility.  The following uses are expressly prohibited: 
19residential development of any type; development for overnight accommodations, except 
20camping; development for any type of commercial service center, shops, restaurants or 
21other commercial development; or development for any purpose that will change the 
22natural character of the area, except that those uses presently made of the Outdoor and 
23Touring Center located on the property may continue.  If the Town of Carrabassett Valley 
24fails to comply with any of the conditions or restrictions, in whole or in part, contained in 
25this resolve, the State may give written notice to the Town of Carrabassett Valley, and if 
26the Town of Carrabassett Valley fails to comply within 30 days, then the title to the 
27property reverts to the State; such a reversion may not be effective until the State records 
28a notice of the reversion in the Franklin County Registry of Deeds.
29SUMMARY
30This resolve amends the deed transferring land from the State to the Town of 
31Carrabassett Valley to remove the prohibition on camping in motor vehicles.
