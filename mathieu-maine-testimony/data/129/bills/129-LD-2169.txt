 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 1 
become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and  2 
Whereas, acts of this and previous Legislatures have resulted in certain technical 3 
inconsistencies,  conflicts and errors in the laws of Maine; and  4 
Whereas, these inconsistencies, conflicts and errors create uncertainties and 5 
confusion in interpreting legislative intent; and  6 
Whereas, it is vitally necessary that these uncertainties and this confusion be 7 
resolved in order to prevent any injustice or hardship to the citizens of Maine; and  8 
Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 9 
the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 10 
immed iately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 11 
therefore,  12 
Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:  13

§2042.  Powers and duties of the board  25 
1.  Prescription drug spending targets.  The board has the following powers and 26 
duties.  For the purposes of this section, the term "public payor" means any division of 27 
state, county  or municipal government that administers a health plan for employees of 28

or municipal employers that administers a health plan for its employees, except for the 30 
Maine Care program.  The board shall:  31 
A.  Beginning for the year 2021 and in consultation with the advisory council 32 
established under section 2041, subsection 10, determine annual spending targets for 33 
prescription drugs purchased by public payors based upon a 10 -year rolling average 34 
of the medical care services component of the United States Department of Labor, 35 
Bureau of Labor Statistics Consumer Price Index medical care services index plus a 36 
reasonable percentage for inflation and minus a spending target determ ined by the 37 
board for pharmacy savings;  38

accomplish its duties under this section:  6 
A.  A public payor's prescription drug spending data, which the 3rd -party 7

§2045.  Authorization  21 
The Wholesale Prescription Drug Importation Program, referred to in this chapter as 22 
"the program," is established to provide for the wholesale importation of prescription 23 
drugs from Canada by or on behalf of the State.  The program mu st be designed in 24 
accordance with the requirements of this chapter.  The program may not be implemented 25 
unless the State obtains approval and certification, pursuant to section 2046, subsection 3, 26 
from the United States Department of Health and Human Servi ces. 27 
§2046.  Design of program  28 
1.  Design requirements.  The Department of Health and Human Services, in 29 
consultation with appropriate federal and other state agencies, other states and interested 30 
parties, shall design the program to comply with the applic able requirements of 21 United 31 
States Code, Section 384, including requirements regarding safety and cost savings.  The 32 
program design must:  33 
A.  Designate a state agency to become a licensed drug wholesaler or to contract with 34 
a licensed drug wholesaler in  order to seek federal certification and approval, 35 
pursuant to subsection 3, to import safe prescription drugs and provide cost savings to 36 
consumers in the State;  37 
 
 Page 8 - 129LR3217(01) -1 
 B.  Use prescription drug suppliers in Canada regulated under the laws of Canada or 1 
of one or  more Canadian provinces, or both;  2 
C.  Ensure that only prescription drugs meeting the federal Food and Drug 3 
Administration's safety, effectiveness and other standards are imported by or on 4 
behalf of the State;  5 
D.  Import only those prescription drugs expe cted to generate substantial cost savings 6 
for consumers in the State;  7 
E.  Ensure that the program complies with the transaction and tracing requirements of 8 
21 United States Code, Sections 360eee and 360eee -1 to the extent feasible and 9 
practical prior to im ported prescription drugs coming into the possession of the 10 
licensed drug wholesaler and that the program complies fully with those federal 11 
requirements after imported prescription drugs are in the possession of the licensed 12 
drug wholesaler;  13 
F.  Consider w hether the program may be developed on a multistate basis through 14 
collaboration with other states;  15 
G.  Prohibit the distribution, dispensing or sale of imported prescription drugs outside 16 
of the State;  17 
H.  Recommend a charge per prescription or another met hod of financing to ensure 18 
that the program is adequately funded in a manner that does not jeopardize significant 19 
cost savings to consumers, including adequate funding for the initial start -up costs of 20 
the program;  21 
I.  Apply for and receive funds, grants o r contracts from public and private sources; 22 
and 23 
J.  Include an audit function.  24 
2.  Rules.  The Department of Health and Human Services shall adopt rules to design 25 
the program in accordance with the requirements of subsection 1 no later than January 1, 26 
2020. Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are major substantive rules as defined in 27 
chapter 375, subchapter 2 -A. 28 
3.  Request for federal approval and certification.  The Department of Health and 29
