24 SUMMARY 
25 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, changes the title and 
26 renames the program the Maine Community College System Pell Grant Match Program. 
27 The amendment establishes the program and provides that a student is eligible for the 
28 program if the student is a resident of the State, is eligible for a Federal Pell Grant and 
29 maintains a minimum grade point average. The grant award may not exceed 100% of the 
30 amount of funds provided by the Federal Pell Grant that the student receives for the 
31 academic year in which the student applies. The availability of the grant and the amount 
32 of the grant are subject to the amount available in the established fund, and the Maine 
33 Community College System may adopt rules to implement and administer the program 
34 and fund. 
35 The amendment also adds an appropriations and allocations section with an 
36 appropriation of $3,600,000.

Fiscal Detail and Notes 
This bill includes ongoing General Fund appropriations of $3,600,000 per year beginning in fiscal year 2020-21 to the 
Maine Community College System (MCCS) to establish the Maine Community College System Pell Grant Match 
Program and Fund to provide grant awards to eligible students to match the amount of funds provided by the Federal 
Pell Grant that the student receives for the academic year in which the student applies. The estimate provided by 
MCCS assumes 7,200 Pell Grant recipients are eligible to receive a matching grant of $500 each year.
