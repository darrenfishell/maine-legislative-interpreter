162.  Special raffles; prizes more than $10,000 but not more than $75,000.    The 
17following provisions apply to special raffles licensed under this subsection.
18A.  The Gambling Control Unit may issue one special raffle license per year to any 
19organization, department or class eligible to hold a raffle under subsection 1.  The 
20special raffle license entitles the licensee to hold one raffle in which the holder of a 
21winning chance receives something of value worth more than $10,000 but not more 
22than $75,000.  A raffle licensed under this paragraph may be structured as a 
23progressive raffle that is divided into a maximum of 12 multiple drawings with 
24previous entries rolled into subsequent drawing pots and with the final drawing to be 
25held within 12 months of the first.  Drawings must be used to randomly select a 
26smaller group to be eligible for the final prize to be awarded after the final drawing.
27B.  The Gambling Control Unit may not issue a license under this subsection to hold 
28a raffle in which the holder of a winning chance receives a cash prize worth more 
29than $10,000.
30C.  All tickets sold pursuant to a special raffle license must be purchased from a 
31licensed distributor or licensed printer.  Tickets must be sequentially numbered and 
32have printed on their faces the following information: the name of the special raffle 
33licensee; a description of the prize or prizes; the price of the ticket; and the date, time 
34and place of the drawing.  Any organization, department or class listed in subsection 
351 that conducts a raffle under this section shall retain all unsold raffle tickets for 6 
36months after the raffle drawing and make those tickets available for inspection at the 
37request of the Gambling Control Unit.

18SUMMARY
19This bill repeals the current law governing raffles and enacts new law based on the 
20former Maine Revised Statutes, Title 17, section 1837, which was enacted by Public Law 
212009, chapter 487, Part A, section 2 and took effect on July 12, 2010.
