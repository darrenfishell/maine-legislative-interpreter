13. Measuring the effect of department initiatives to improve child and family

programming can contribute to improvements in child and family economic security, 
including increased ability to meet basic needs, improved educational levels and 
increased incomes. Beginning January 15, 2021, and annually thereafter, the department

committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters

improving the economic security for children, families and individuals and make 
recommendations for addressing those obstacles, which may include improved 
coordination between agencies of State Government. The committee's review must 
include the opportunity for public comment on the department's presentation and the 
committee may introduce any legislation that it considers necessary to address barriers 
faced by the department in improving economic security for children, families and 
individuals in this State.'

29 SUMMARY 
30 This amendment makes the following changes to the bill. 
31 1. It removes the requirement for the Department of Health and Human Services to 
32 establish annual improvement targets to ensure access to public assistance programs.

2. It clarifies that sources of information to compile data measures of child and 
2 family economic security are not limited to the data sources in the bill and specifies that 
3 data from other state agencies including the Department of Labor and the Department of 
4 Administrative and Financial Services, Bureau of Revenue Services must be compiled. 
5 3. It includes a data requirement that includes families earning below 50% of the 
6 federal poverty level at the time participation in the Temporary Assistance for Needy 
7 Families program was terminated and during the 2nd and 4th quarters after participation 
8 was terminated. 
9 4. It clarifies the language regarding the ratio of persons receiving food supplement 
10 assistance to those eligible to specify that the ratio applies to the number of persons 
11 overall, the number of persons 60 years of age and older, nonelderly persons with a 
12 disability and children under 18 years of age. 
13 5. It specifies that data collected regarding waiting times for calls to Department of 
14 Health and Human Services call centers are to a person and not to an interactive voice 
15 response system. 
16 6. It changes the requirements of the Department of Health and Human Services to 
17 measure and report on the effect of departmental initiatives to improve child and family 
18 economic security rather than establishing improvement targets with reporting 
19 requirements. 
20 7. It changes the amount of funding for navigators at educational institutions and 
21 programs from $500,000 to $250,000. 
22 The amendment also adds an appropriations and allocations section.
