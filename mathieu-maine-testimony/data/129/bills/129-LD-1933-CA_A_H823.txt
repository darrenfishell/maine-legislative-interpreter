12Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the 
13following:
14'Sec. 1.  7 MRSA §219-A is enacted to read:
15§219-A.  Reusable containers
16The department shall ensure that its rules, established in accordance with the 
17commissioner's rule-making authority in section 12, and guidelines neither preclude 
18business owners from allowing nor require business owners to allow consumers to supply 
19their own containers for the bulk purchase of certain food and nonfood items.  The 
20department shall provide technical assistance and education to business owners and 
21consumers regarding best practices for the use of reusable containers.
22Sec. 2.  Review and report.  The Department of Agriculture, Conservation and 
23Forestry shall review its rules and guidelines to ensure they are in compliance with the 
24Maine Revised Statutes, Title 7, section 219-A.  By February 1, 2021, the department 
25shall provide a report on its progress towards implementation of the requirements of Title 
267, section 219-A to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction 
27over innovation, development, economic advancement and business matters.' 
28SUMMARY
29This amendment replaces the bill, which is a concept draft.  The amendment requires 
30the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to ensure that its rules and 
31guidelines neither preclude business owners from allowing nor require business owners to 
32allow consumers to supply their own containers for the bulk purchase of certain food and 
33nonfood items and to provide technical assistance and education regarding best practices.  
34The amendment requires the department to report on its progress by February 1, 2021 to Date: (Filing No. H-          )
