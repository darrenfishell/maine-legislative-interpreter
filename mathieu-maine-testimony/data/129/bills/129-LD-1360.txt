6years of age with income at or below 138% of the federal poverty level;
72.  Expand hospital presumptive eligibility to include eligibility for individuals 
8receiving acute care in a hospital who no longer require acute hospital care as certified by 
9a physician but still require medical care that can be provided by a certified home health 
10agency, long-term home health care program, hospice facility or residential health care 
11facility.  A qualified individual must reasonably appear to meet all of the criteria, 
12financial and nonfinancial, pending the completion of the full eligibility determination;
133.  Provide 20 days for hospitals to submit a copy of a completed MaineCare hospital 
14presumptive eligibility card to the Department of Health and Human Services;
154.  Require a qualified hospital to offer to assist individuals determined to be 
16presumptively eligible with completing and submitting full MaineCare application forms; 
17and
185.  Require the Department of Health and Human Services to amend the performance 
19standards for qualified hospitals to require that 85% of presumptive eligibility 
20determinations made by hospitals will be found eligible for full MaineCare coverage.  
21Hospitals that do not meet these standards may be subject to presumptive eligibility 
22training facilitated by the Department of Health and Human Services or lose their 
23qualified entity status.
24SUMMARY
25This resolve directs the Department of Health and Human Services to amend the 
26department's rule Chapter 332:  MaineCare Eligibility Manual, Part 18:  Presumptive 
27Eligibility Determined by Hospitals regarding expanding hospital presumptive eligibility, 
28hospital presumptive eligibility cards, assisting individuals with MaineCare application 
29forms and performance standards for qualified hospitals to require that 85% of 
30presumptive eligibility determinations made will be found eligible for full MaineCare 
31coverage.
