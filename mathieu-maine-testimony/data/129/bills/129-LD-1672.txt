11SUMMARY
12This bill makes changes to the law governing the admissibility of health care records 
13as evidence in court. It specifies that records, including itemized bills, kept by health care 
14practitioners, health care entities, health care providers, pharmacists and pharmacies may 
15be admissible in court as evidence of (1) the fair and reasonable charge for such services 
16or the necessity of services or treatments; (2) the diagnosis provided by the medical 
17entity; (3) the prognosis provided by the medical entity; (4) the opinion provided by the 
18medical entity regarding the proximate cause of the condition diagnosed by the medical 
19entity; and (5) the opinion provided by the medical entity regarding any disability or 
20incapacity proximately resulting from the condition diagnosed by the medical entity.
