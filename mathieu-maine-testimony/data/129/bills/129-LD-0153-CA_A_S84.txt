17 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, amends the bill to 
18 provide that the State is not establishing a mandate on school administrative units. It 
19 provides that a school must test water used for drinking or culinary purposes for lead to 
20 the extent the Department of Health and Human Services provides the necessary 
21 resources to the school so that the school is not required to expand or modify its activities 
22 so as to necessitate additional expenditures from local revenue. It directs the department 
23 to adopt rules regarding testing and guidance to reduce exposure to lead but provides that 
24 the department may not require a school to expand or modify its activities so as to 
25 necessitate additional expenditures from local revenue in implementing the rules. It 
26 further provides that the department may provide resources to schools within its existing 
27 resources or identify alternative means to achieve the purposes of the legislation. It 
28 requires the Department of Health and Human Services to report annually beginning 
29 January 1, 2021 on lead testing in schools.

This bill requires the Department of Human Services (DHHS) to adopt rnles for schools related to water lead, 
including but not limited to, establishing water lead levels; testing protocols, including the frequency of testing; 
abatement or mitigation methods; procedures for guidance to reduce exposure; and public notification. Schools are 
required to test water used for d1inking or culinary purposes only to the extent that the DHHS provides the necessary 
resources to ensure that schools do not incur additional expenditures from local revenue. The DHHS has indicated it 
can provide water testing kits and related laboratory analyses for the schools within its currently budgeted resources. 
For any additional costs arising from discovery oflead in water, potentially including abatement and mitigation, the 
bill requires that DHHS must, to the maximum extent possible, provide resources to schools or seek to identify 
alternative means to achleve the bill's purposes. No additional funding is provided in the bill at this time.
