14§302.  Maine Aeronautical Advisory Board
151.  Board established. 
16A.    The Maine Aeronautical Advisory Board, established by Title 5, section 12004-
17I, subsection 81, and in this section called "the board," shall be is a board within the 
18Department of Transportation.
192.  Membership. 
20A.    The membership of the board consists of at least 7 voting members: one person 
21from an airport association in the State appointed by the association's board of 
22directors; one person from a pilot's association in the State appointed by the 
23association's board of directors; and the remaining members appointed by the 
24Commissioner of Transportation. All members shall serve a term of office of 2 years 
25consistent with the state fiscal year. Vacancies in membership must be filled in the 
26same manner as the original appointment. The commissioner serves as secretary of 
27the board. Terms must be staggered so that approximately half of the board is 
28renewed each year.  The commissioner shall endeavor to appoint a balance of public 
29and private sector members holding diverse knowledge and perspectives of the 
30aeronautics industry.  Vacancies may be filled to serve out the remainder of a term if 
31a member resigns or is terminated for cause or a vacancy is created in any other 
32manner.
33B.  The commissioner or the commissioner's designee shall serve as a nonvoting 
34secretary and clerk of the board and be responsible for board records and filings.  All 
35other members are voting members.
36C.  The board shall annually elect a chair and vice-chair from among its membership 
37to serve a one-year term.  If a vacancy occurs in the office of chair or vice-chair, the 
38board shall endeavor to fill that office for the remainder of the term.
39D.  Members are not entitled to compensation.

22§4209-A.  Public Transit Advisory Council
231.  Council established.  The Public Transit Advisory Council, referred to in this 
24section as "the council," is established in accordance with Title 5, section 12004-I,

29term and long-term fiscal, operating and capital investments, and the integration of transit

20SUMMARY
21This bill changes the membership of the Maine Aeronautical Advisory Board and 
22provides for staggered terms.  It provides that the Commissioner of Transportation serves 
23as a nonvoting secretary and clerk of the board and authorizes the board to adopt bylaws.
24The bill authorizes the Commissioner of Transportation to designate as voting 
25members of the Public Transit Advisory Council those persons the commissioner is 
26required to invite to participate in council meetings.  It provides for staggered terms, 
27requires the council to meet at least once per year and authorizes the council to adopt 
28bylaws.  It also changes the reporting requirements for the council.
