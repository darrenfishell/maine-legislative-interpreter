23SUMMARY
24This bill amends the Act To Implement the Maine Indian Claims Settlement by:
251. Transferring jurisdiction over violations of a tribal ordinance from the State to the 
26Passamaquoddy Tribe and the Penobscot Nation over a person who is not a member of 
27either tribe or nation in accord with and to the extent authorized by federal law;
282. Increasing the level of certain criminal offenses from a maximum period of 
29imprisonment of one year and a maximum amount of $5,000 to a maximum period of 
30imprisonment of 3 years and a maximum amount of $15,000 over which the Penobscot 
31Nation has the right to exercise exclusive jurisdiction as authorized by the federal Tribal 
32Law and Order Act of 2010; and
333.  Clarifying that the Penobscot Nation has concurrent jurisdiction with the State 
34over criminal offenses as authorized by the federal Violence Against Women 
35Reauthorization Act of 2013.
