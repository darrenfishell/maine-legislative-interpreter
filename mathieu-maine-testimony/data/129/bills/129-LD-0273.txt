3ยง3210-G.  Purchase of renewable energy
4A transmission and distribution utility shall, in accordance with this section, at the 
5request of the owner of a renewable resource, purchase the electricity generated by that 
6renewable resource at a price per kilowatt-hour that is 50% of the average cost per 
7kilowatt-hour to generate electricity using a fossil fuel in this State.  For the purposes of 
8this section, "renewable resource" has the same meaning as in section 3210, subsection 2, 
9paragraph C, except that "renewable resource" does not include hydroelectric generators.
101.  Standards and procedures.  The commission by rule or order shall:
11A.  Establish reasonable terms and conditions governing the purchase of electricity 
12under this section;
13B.  Annually determine the average cost per kilowatt-hour to generate electricity 
14using a fossil fuel in this State; and
15C.  Require an investor-owned transmission and distribution utility to resell 
16electricity purchased under this section.
172.  Cost recovery.  Reasonable costs incurred by a transmission and distribution 
18utility under this section are just and reasonable expenses for rate-making purposes.
193.  Rules.  The commission may adopt rules to implement this section. Rules adopted 
20pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined by Title 5, chapter 375, 
21subchapter 2-A.
22SUMMARY
23This bill requires a transmission and distribution utility, at the request of the owner of 
24a renewable resource, to purchase the electricity generated by that renewable resource at a 
25price per kilowatt-hour that is 50% of the average cost per kilowatt-hour to generate 
26electricity using a fossil fuel in this State.
