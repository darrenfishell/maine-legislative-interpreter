2Sec. 1.  5 MRSA §17859, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2011, c. 420, Pt. L, §1, is 
3further amended to read:
41.  Restoration to service.  Any state employee or teacher who has reached normal 
5retirement age and who retires after September 1, 2011 may be restored to service for up 
6to 5 years.  The decision to hire a retired state employee or retired teacher under this 
7section is at the discretion of the appointing authority.  The retired state employee or 
8retired teacher must have had a bona fide termination of employment in accordance with 
9state and federal laws and rules, may not return to employment after retirement with the 
10same employer for at least 30 calendar days after the termination of employment and may 
11not return to employment before the effective date of the person's retirement.
12Sec. 2.  5 MRSA §17859, sub-§1-A, ¶A, as enacted by PL 2013, c. 486, Pt. A, 
13§1, is amended to read:
14A.  In one-year contracts, which may be nonconsecutive. The maximum time that a 
15classroom-based employee may be restored to service with an individual school 
16administrative unit pursuant to this paragraph is 5 years;
17Sec. 3.  5 MRSA §17859, sub-§1-A, ¶¶B and C, as enacted by PL 2013, c. 486, 
18Pt. A, §1, are repealed.
19Sec. 4.  5 MRSA §17859, sub-§2, ¶A, as amended by PL 2013, c. 486, Pt. A, §2, 
20is further amended to read:
21A.  The compensation of the retired state employee or retired teacher who returns to 
22service must be set at 75% of the compensation established for the position to be 
23filled, at a step determined by the appointing authority. The compensation of or the 
24retired classroom-based employee who returns to service as a classroom-based 
25employee pursuant to subsection 1-A, paragraph A must be set at 100% of the 
26compensation established for the position to be filled, at a step determined by the 
27school administrative unit, for up to the maximum 5-year period that a classroom-
28based employee may contract with an individual school administrative unit.
29Sec. 5.  5 MRSA §17859, sub-§5, as enacted by PL 2011, c. 380, Pt. MMM, §1, is 
30repealed.
31SUMMARY
32This bill eliminates the provisions in law that limit employment of a retired state 
33employee or teacher to 5 years and 75% of the compensation established for the position.
