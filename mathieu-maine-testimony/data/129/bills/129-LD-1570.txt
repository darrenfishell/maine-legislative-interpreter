9Sec. 2.  21-A MRSA §131 is enacted to read:
10§131.  Online registration
111.  Online registration.  A person who is qualified under section 111, subsections 1 
12to 3 to vote under this article and who possesses a valid driver's license or nondriver 
13identification card issued by the Secretary of State may submit a voter registration 
14application under section 152, subsection 1 on the Secretary of State's publicly accessible 
15website.
162.  Application.  A voter registration application submitted under this section must 
17contain:
18A.  The legal name of the voter, in one of the following combinations:
19(1)  First name and last name;
20(2)  First initial, middle name and last name; or
21(3)  First name, middle name or middle initial and last name;
22B.  Residence address, including street, street number, apartment number, town and 
23zip code;
24C.  Date of birth;
25D.  Most recent prior residence where registered to vote, including the municipality, 
26county and state, and the name under which previously registered, if changed;
27E.  Notification that failure to complete the entire application may prevent 
28registration;
29F.  Choice of political party if the applicant desires to enroll in a political party or an 
30indication that the applicant chose not to enroll in a party;
31G.  The unique identifying number on the applicant's driver's license or nondriver 
32identification card issued by the Secretary of State;
33H.  An affirmation by the applicant that the information provided in the application is 
34truthful and that the applicant is qualified to register in the State;
Page 2 - 129LR0195(01)-11I.  A place for the applicant to respond to the questions concerning the voter's 
2qualifications as required by the federal Help America Vote Act of 2002, Public Law 
3107-252; and
4J.  Any other information the Secretary of State determines necessary to establish the 
5identity of the applicant or to carry out the purposes of this article.
63.  Procedure.  An applicant may register to vote under this section no later than the 
77th day before election day as set forth in section 121-A.  Upon receipt of an application 
8under subsection 2, the Secretary of State shall verify that:
9A.  The applicant has a valid driver's license or nondriver identification card issued 
10by the Secretary of State and that the unique identifying number for the card provided 
11by the applicant matches the unique identifying number for the card in the Secretary 
12of State's records;
13B.  The date of birth provided by the applicant matches the date of birth of the 
14applicant in the Secretary of State's records; and
15C.  The applicant is a citizen of the State and that the information provided by the 
16applicant matches the information in the Secretary of State's records.
17After verifying the information under this subsection, the Secretary of State shall notify 
18the registrar of the voting district in which the applicant resides, revise the central voter 
19registration system and send confirmation of registration to the applicant either 
20electronically or by mail.  If the Secretary of State is unable to verify any information 
21required by this section, the Secretary of State shall reject the application as void and 
22send notification of and reason for the rejection to the applicant either electronically or by 
23mail within 5 days of the rejection.
244.  Alternative registration procedure for participants in Address 
25Confidentiality Program.  The Secretary of State may establish different criteria under 
26subsection 2, paragraphs A, B and D for an applicant who is certified by the Secretary of 
27State as a program participant in the Address Confidentiality Program in Title 5, section 
2890-B.
295.  Registration software.  The Secretary of State shall procure and use computer 
30software to administer the procedures in subsection 3 to provide immediate verification to 
31the extent possible.
326.  Rules.  The Secretary of State may adopt rules to carry out the purposes of this 
33section.  Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined 
34by Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
35SUMMARY
36This bill allows online application for voter registration by an eligible resident of the 
37State through the Secretary of State's publicly accessible website.
