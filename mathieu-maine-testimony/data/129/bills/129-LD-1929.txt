12Sec. 1.  Commission established.  Resolved:  That the Commission To Study 
13Fair, Equitable and Competitive Tax Policy for Maine's Working Families and Small 
14Businesses, referred to in this resolve as "the commission," is established.
15Sec. 2.  Commission membership.  Resolved:  That the commission consists of 
1611 members appointed as follows:
171.  Three members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including a 
18member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
192.  Three members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
20House, including a member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
21in the Legislature;
223.  One member representing the Office of the Governor, appointed by the Governor;
234.  One public member representing the interests of small businesses and one public 
24member representing the interests of working families, appointed by the President of the 
25Senate; and
265.  One public member representing the interests of small businesses and one public 
27member representing the interests of working families, appointed by the Speaker of the 
28House.
29Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate 
30chair and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
31commission.
32Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
33appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
34resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
35Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
36the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
37after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
Page 2 - 129LR2956(01)-11made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority 
2for the commission to meet and conduct its business.
3Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall study issues associated with 
4the impacts of tax policy on the State's working families and small businesses and shall 
5develop recommendations designed to ensure that the tax policy of the State is fair and 
6equitable, while ensuring the State remains competitive.  In examining the issues, the 
7commission shall consider measures designed to level the playing field for small 
8businesses and to aid entrepreneurs and the importance to working families and 
9businesses of adequately funding important government services, such as aid to local 
10services, education, infrastructure and affordable health care and propose measures that 
11ensure these services are adequately funded.  The commission shall also evaluate the 
12direct impact of any proposed tax changes on after-tax income by income decile.
13Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
14necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff 
15support is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
16Sec. 7.  Provision of information to commission.  The Department of 
17Administrative and Financial Services, Maine Revenue Services, shall provide to the 
18commission data consistent with the restrictions set forth in the Maine Revised Statutes, 
19Title 36, section 191 that is requested by the commission.
20Sec. 8.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than November 4, 2020, the 
21commission shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, 
22including suggested legislation, for presentation to the First Regular Session of the 130th 
23Legislature.
24Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 
25legislation takes effect when approved.
26SUMMARY
27This resolve establishes the Commission To Study Fair, Equitable and Competitive 
28Tax Policy for Maine's Working Families and Small Businesses and directs the 
29commission, no later than November 4, 2020, to submit a report, including suggested 
30legislation, for presentation to the First Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
