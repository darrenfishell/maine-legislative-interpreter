2Sec. 1.  7 MRSA §4019, sub-§2-A is enacted to read:
32-A.  Removal by other than authorized person.  After making reasonable efforts 
4to locate a motor vehicle's owner, a person other than an authorized person as defined in 
5subsection 1 may enter a motor vehicle to remove an animal if the person:
6A.  Notifies law enforcement or calls the E-9-1-1 system before entering the motor 
7vehicle;
8B.  Determines that the motor vehicle is locked and there is no other reasonable 
9means for exit and uses no more force than is reasonably necessary to enter the motor 
10vehicle and remove the animal;
11C.  Has a good faith belief, based upon known circumstances, that entry into the 
12motor vehicle is necessary because the animal's safety, health or well-being appears 
13to be in immediate danger from heat, cold or lack of adequate ventilation and the 
14conditions could reasonably be expected to cause extreme suffering or death; and
15D.  Remains with the animal in a safe location in reasonable proximity to the motor 
16vehicle until an authorized person as defined in subsection 1 arrives.
17Sec. 2.  7 MRSA §4019, sub-§3, as amended by PL 2011, c. 288, §1, is further 
18amended to read:
193.  Immunity.   An authorized person who removes an animal from a motor vehicle 
20pursuant to subsection 1 or a person who removes an animal from a motor vehicle 
21pursuant to subsection 2-A is immune from criminal or civil liability that might otherwise 
22result from the removal.
23SUMMARY
24This bill allows a person who is not a law enforcement officer, humane agent, animal 
25control officer, firefighter, first responder or security guard to enter a vehicle and remove 
26an animal if the animal's safety, health or well-being appears to be in immediate danger.  
27The bill provides that a person who removes an animal from a vehicle under these 
28conditions is immune from criminal or civil liability.
