3SUMMARY
4The majority of the Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs 
5voted to report out this committee bill to implement the recommendations of the work 
6group formed pursuant to Public Law 2019, chapter 403 to study the issue of 
7investigations of educator credential holders.  The bill does the following.
81.  It provides that a school entity must notify the Department of Education 
9immediately if a credential holder who is the subject of a covered investigation leaves the 
10school's employment upon the conclusion of the covered investigation and that if a 
11credential holder provides consent as part of that credential holder's application for 
12employment, the department must notify the school if that credential holder left 
13employment with a school entity upon completion of a covered investigation.
142.  It provides that the Department of Education must notify a school entity 
15immediately, rather than within 15 business days, of the department's initiating an 
16investigation into a holder of a credential who works for that school entity.
173.  It provides that the Department of Education must destroy copies of records and 
18reports related to a finding resulting in discipline, suspension or termination of a 
19credential holder if the finding resulting in that discipline, suspension or termination is 
20completely reversed upon appeal at the school entity level.
