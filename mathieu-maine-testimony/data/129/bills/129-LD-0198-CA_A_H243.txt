COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "/) .. to H.P. 161, L.D. 198, Bill, "An Act To 
Require That Nonmotorized Carriages Be Equipped with Reflectors and Lights" 
Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
'An Act To Require That Nonmotorized Carriages Be Equipped with Reflective 
Tape and Lights' 
Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the 
following: 
1Sec. 1. 29-A MRSA §1907, sub-§4, as enacted by PL 1993, c. 683, Pt. A, §2 and 
affected by Pt. B, § 5, is amended to read: 
4. Exceptions. This section does not apply to animal-drawn vehicles or unregistered 
farm tractors. 
Sec. 2. 29-A MRSA §1909-C is enacted to read: 
§1909-C. Animal-drawn vehicles 
An animal-drawn vehicle operated on a public way during nighttime must be 
equipped with the following: 
1. Light. An oil lantern or electric lights attached to the left side of the vehicle that 
displays a red light toward the rear of the vehicle and a white light toward the front of the 
vehicle: and 
2. Reflective tape. Grade DOT-C2 white reflective tape as described in 49 Code of 
Federal Regulations, Section 571.108, in effect on March 26, 2019, applied to the vehicle 
as follows: 
A. Seventy-two inches of reflective tape applied in segments that outline the rear 
frame of the vehicle;

6 SUMMARY 
7 This amendment expands the scope of the bill to apply to animal-drawn vehicles 
8 instead of just horse-drawn carriages. The amendment requires a light to be attached to 
9 the left side of the vehicle that displays a red light to the rear and white light to the front; 
10 specifies the placement and type ofreflective tape required on all sides of the vehicle; and 
11 removes the requirement for a reflective triangle to be placed on the rear of the vehicle. 
12 The amendment also exempts animal-drawn vehicles from the standard red rear reflector 
13 requirement.
