§1541-B. Net neutrality 
1. Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
following terms have the following meanings. 
A. 11Advanced communications technology infrastructure" has the same meaning as 
in Title 35-A, section 9202, subsection 1. 
B. 11Commit state funds11 means to enter into a contract, make a grant or otherwise 
commit any state funds, including but not limited to any act that would incur a 
financial obligation against the State Government subject to review under section 
1541, subsection 2. 
C. 11Internet service provider11 has the same meaning as in section 200-B, subsection 
1-A, paragraph A. 
D. 11Net neutral service11 means fixed or mobile Internet service that is provided 
without engaging in any of the following: 
(1) Blocking of lawful content, applications, services or devices; 
(2) Throttling; or 
(3) Paid prioritization. 
E. 11Paid prioritization11 means management of an Internet service provider's network 
to directly or indirectly favor some traffic over other traffic in exchange for payment. 
F. 11State entity11 means a department, agency or instrumentality of the State.

25 SUMMARY 
26 The amendment narrows the scope and application of the prohibition in the bill 
27 regarding committing state funds for payment to an Internet service provide1·. The 
28 amendment prohibits an agency, department or instrumentality of the State from 
29 committing state funds to an Internet service provider unless the Internet service provider 
30 agrees to provide net neutral service in the provision of Internet service directly to the 
31 state entity or the provision of service across advanced communications infrastructure 
32 constructed with the use of state funds. The amendment defines "net neutral service" as 
33 Internet service provided without engaging in any of the following: blocking of lawful 
34 content, applications, services or devices; throttling; 01· paid prioritization. The 
35 amendment removes the requirement in the bill that prohibits the commitment of state 
36 funds to an Internet service provider unless that provider agrees, in providing any service, 
37 to conform to the requirements of the Federal Communications Commission order, FCC 
3 8 15-24, known as the Open Internet Order.
