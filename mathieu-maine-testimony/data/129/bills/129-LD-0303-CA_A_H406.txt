26 SUMMARY 
27 This amendment, which is the unanimous report of the committee, strikes and 
28 replaces the bill and does the following. 
29 1. It makes the requirements of the bill apply only to recovery residences and not to 
30 other housing-based programs and expands the definition of "recovery residence." 
31 2. It changes the requirement for on-site storage of naloxone hydrochloride from at 
32 least one unit for every 3 residents of a housing-based program to at least 2 units for each 
33 floor of a recovery residence. 
34 3. It exempts from arrest or prosecution a person who in good faith administers 
35 naloxone hydrochloride to another person experiencing a dtug-related overdose. The 
36 person may not be arrested or prosecuted for a violation of laws prohibiting the unlawful 
37 possession of scheduled drugs, acquiring drugs by deception, the illegal possession of 
38 hypodermic apparatuses or the use of drug paraphernalia or a violation of probation if the 
39 grounds for arrest or prosecution are obtained as a result of the person's administering 
40 naloxone hydt·ochloride.
