2Sec. 1.  1 MRSA §601, first ¶, as amended by PL 2013, c. 368, Pt. YYYY, §1, is 
3further amended to read:
4To be qualified as a medium for the publication of legal notices, legal advertising and 
5other matter required by law to be published in a newspaper, a newspaper, unless 
6otherwise ordered by the court in the proceedings, must be printed in the English 
7language; must be entered as 2nd class postal matter in the United States mails; and must 
8have general circulation in the vicinity where the notice is required to be published.  Any 
9legal notice, legal advertising or other matter required by law to be published in a 
10newspaper must appear in all editions of that newspaper and must appear on any publicly 
11accessible website that the newspaper maintains in accordance with the requirements of 
12section 603.
13Sec. 2.  1 MRSA §602, as corrected by RR 1997, c. 2, §2, is amended to read:
14§602.  Additional media for publication of notices
15Notwithstanding section 601, all probate notices, notices of foreclosure, other legal 
16notices, legal advertising and other matter required by law to be published in a newspaper 
17that have been published in the Coastal Journal from the date of its first publication on 
18November 3, 1966 and in the Somerset Gazette from the date of its first publication on 
19April 16, 1990 to June 2, 1993, and that would have been valid but for the a former 
20provision of section 601 effective at any point from November 3, 1966 to the general 
21effective date for nonemergency laws passed during the First Regular Session of the 
22129th Legislature that requires required a newspaper carrying such notices to be entered 
23as 2nd class postal matter, are declared to be valid.
24SUMMARY
25This bill removes from the law governing publication of legal notices the requirement 
26that, in order for a newspaper to publish notices, it must be entered as 2nd class postal 
27matter in the United States mails.
