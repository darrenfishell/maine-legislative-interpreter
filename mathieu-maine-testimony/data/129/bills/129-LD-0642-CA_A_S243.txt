29 SUMMARY 
30 This amendment removes any preferences in the bill for American Sign Language. 
31 The amendment also adjusts reporting dates throughout the bill. 
32 The amendment also directs the Commissioner of Education no later than November 
33 1, 2019 to establish a departmental task force to recommend language developmental 
34 milestones from existing resources and current standards and to develop a parent resource 
35 portfolio to monitor and track the expressive and receptive language acquisition and 
36 developmental stages toward literacy in American Sign Language or English, or both, of 
37 children who are deaf and hard of hearing and sets criteria for the task force membership.

1 The bill directs the Department of Education to select tools and assessments for 
2 educators that can be used to assess the language and literacy development of children 
3 who are deaf and hard of hearing and to report to the joint standing committee of the 
4 Legislature having jurisdiction over education and cultural affairs. The amendment 
5 requires the department to submit an interim report no later than March 1, 2020 and a 
6 final report no later than January 1, 2021 on the parent resource portfolio developed by 
7 the task force and a procedure to disseminate the parent resource portfolio to parents, a 
8 procedure to disseminate the selected tools and assessments to educators and a procedure 
9 for educators to use when a child is not progressing in expressive and receptive language 
10 skills.
