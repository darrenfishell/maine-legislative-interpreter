SUMMARY  20 
This ame ndment replaces the bill, which is a concept draft.  This amendment is the 21 
majority report of the committee.  The amendment adds to the monthly report from the 22 
Department of Health and Human Services on child welfare services a report of the status 23 
of chil dren in the department 's custody whose parent s or primary caregivers are  24 
incarcerated in a jail or correctional facility and data regarding the status of children with 25 
whom the Office of Child and Family Services is working whose parent s or primary 26 
caregiv ers are incarcerated in a jail or correctional facility. The amendment adds a person 27 
with knowledge related to the incarceration of women to the board of visitors of each jail.  28 
The amendment requires the Department of Corrections standards for jails and c ounty and 29 
municipal detention facilities to include standards for the delivery of women 's health care 30 
services, for which compliance must be evaluated at least every 2 years and documented in 31 
the audit of the jail or facility.  The amendment establishes a board of visitors for women 's 32 
services to serve with regard to women who are incarcerated in Department of Corrections 33 
correctional and detention facilities.  The amendment adds to the rights of a person who is 34 
incarcerated in the custody of the Department  of Corrections opportunities and conditions 35 
for visitations with the child of the person  that parallel those opportunities and conditions 36 
for visitation for families receiving family reunification services from the Department of 37 
Health and Human Services.  The amendment requires that by February 1, 2021 and 38 
annually thereafter , the Department of Corrections must report to the joint standing 39 
committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over criminal justice and public safety 40 
matters on the number of juve niles in the custody of the department, whether as juvenile 41 
detainees or juvenile clients or under department supervision on probation, whose parents 42 
or primary caregivers are known to the department to be in the custody of the department 43 
or under the supe rvision of the department.  44
