18level statewide.  A Head Start program service provider that receives funds pursuant to 
19this Act shall use the funds to provide Head Start program services to applicants who 
20meet the eligibility standards under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 22, section 3736, 
21subsection 4, with preference given to children who are at risk or whose families are at 
22risk.  If a Head Start program service provider that receives funds pursuant to this Act has 
23a vacancy, the service provider shall seek referrals from the Office of Child and Family 
24Services.  For purposes of this section, "at risk" means affected by substance use disorder, 
25sexual or physical abuse, homelessness, a mental health disorder or another condition that 
26affects the emotional, mental or physical health of a child.

2This bill establishes eligibility standards for the Head Start program that allow 
3participation for a child up to 5 years of age who is or whose family is affected by 
4substance use disorder or whose family's income is at or below 185% of the federal

6Start program service providers proportionately in an amount based upon the percentage 
7of children up to 5 years of age who live at or below the federal poverty level in each 
8provider's service area compared to the percentage of children up to 5 years of age who 
9live at or below the federal poverty level statewide, with preference given to children who
