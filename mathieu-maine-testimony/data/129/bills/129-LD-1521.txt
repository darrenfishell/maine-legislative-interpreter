25 SUMMARY
26 This bill directs the Department of Education, in consultation with the Department of 
27 Labor, to develop an internship program for students who are 23 years of age or younger.  
28 The internship program developed by the department would authorize participants to 
29 receive compensation options, in combination with a variable hourly wage that is equal to 
30 or greater than the federal minimum wage, that may include academic credits, credentials 
31 of value and stackable credentials.  The Department of Education is directed to submit a 
32 report outlining the internship program developed, together with any necessary 
33 implementing legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural 
34 Affairs by December 4, 2019.
35 In addition, to facilitate participation in the Maine Apprenticeship Program 
36 established under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 26, section 3202, this bill directs the 
37 Department of Labor to calculate the amount of funding and the number of positions it 
38 would require in order to establish an apprenticeship coordinator at each career and 
39 technical education center in the State and report this information to the Joint Standing 
40 Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs by December 4, 2019.
