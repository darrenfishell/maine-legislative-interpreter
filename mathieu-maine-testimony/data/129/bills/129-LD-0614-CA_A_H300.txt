15 SUMMARY 
16 This amendment makes the following changes to the electric vehicle rebate program 
17 and the associated fund established in the bill: 
18 1. Adds plug-in hybrid electric vehicles to the definition of eligible electric vehicle; 
19 2. Removes the requirement that an eligible vehicle be a "new" vehicle; 
20 3. Limits the $50,000 cap on the manufacturer's suggested retail price to vehicles that 
21 have a gross vehicle weight rating of 10,000 pounds or less; 
22 4. Removes the program start date of July 1, 2020; 
23 5. Removes the fixed rebate amount of $2,500 and instead requires the Efficiency 
24 Maine Tmst to determine the rebate amount, which may vary by the size of the vehicle 
25 battery. It also permits the tmst to establish different rebate amounts to provide 
26 opportunities for participation in the program across different customer groups and 
27 geographic areas; 
28 6. Revises the eligibility criteria regarding registration of a vehicle in the State; 
29 7. Adds language to specify that the tmst may set limits on the number ofrebates per 
30 person or per vehicle; 
31 8. Removes the provision of the bill that provides a $500,000 General Fund 
32 appropriation to the Electric Vehicle Rebate Fund; and 
33 9. Establishes the Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastmcture Fund, administered by 
34 Efficiency Maine Tmst, to increase the availability and effectiveness of electric vehicle 
35 charging infrastmcture in the State.

Fiscal Note 
Current biennium cost increase -Efficiency Maine Trust 
Current biennium revenue increase -Efficiency Maine Trust 
This bill establishes an electric vehicle rebate program to be administered by the Efficiency Maine Trust (EMT) and 
creates two funds, the Electric Vehicle Fund and the Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure Fund. The program and 
associated funds will receive revenue from federal funds or awards, the interest generated by these funds and from 
other public or private sources. It is anticipated that the revenue derived from these sources will be sufficient to fund 
the program activities outlined in the bill and the administrative costs of the program for the EMT.
