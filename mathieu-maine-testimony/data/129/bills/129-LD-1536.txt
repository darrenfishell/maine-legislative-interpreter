19SUMMARY
20This bill:
211.  Provides that in the context of a road discontinuance proceeding the municipal 
22officers must proceed with a discontinuance without retaining a public easement if the 
23municipal officers verify that private easements that run with the title of the property 
24owners' land for the purpose of allowing travel along the way for all abutting property 
25owners and their lessees and guests have been filed with the registry of deeds.  If the 
26municipal officers are unable to verify those easements, they may discontinue the road 
27only if a public easement is retained and the public use for which the easement is retained 
28is specified.  If the municipality retains a public easement, the municipality must provide 
29sufficient public maintenance of the public easement to allow the specified use;
302.  Modifies the road abandonment law to provide that:
31A.  A presumption of abandonment of a way is fully rebutted and removed by 
32evidence that records maintained by the Department of Transportation indicate that a 
33municipality received funds under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 23, chapter 19, 
34subchapter 6 based on any mileage of the way during any portion of the 30 or more 
35consecutive years of apparent discontinuance;
36B.  A municipality may not intentionally cease to maintain a way as a means of 
37creating a presumption of abandonment and a presumption of abandonment is fully 
Page 3 - 129LR1364(01)-11rebutted and removed by evidence that a municipality intentionally ceased 
2maintenance as a means of creating a presumption of abandonment; and
3C.  A municipality may not assert a presumption of abandonment for any way that 
4was discontinued prior to September 3, 1965 that resulted in a public easement;
53.  Moves a provision of law relating to the removal of snow fences along public 
6highways from a section relating to mail routes to the section of law relating to snow 
7removal;
84.  Requires a town to maintain any town public right-of-way used as part of a United 
9States postal route in passable condition for postal service delivery.  All maintenance 
10must be in accordance with applicable United States Postal Service requirements.   If the 
11postal service route is seasonal, the right-of-way must be maintained for the applicable 
12season and, if the mail route is year-round, it must be maintained year-round; and
135.  Provides that a failure of a town to meet the snow removal and other maintenance 
14requirements of the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 23, section 3201 and section 3202, 
15subsection 1 is deemed a failure to maintain a way under Title 23, chapter 313 and the 
16actions and remedies specified in sections 3651 to 3655 of that chapter apply to such a 
17failure.
