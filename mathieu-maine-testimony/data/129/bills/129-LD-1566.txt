12SUMMARY
13This bill increases from 30 to 60 the number of tuition waivers for postsecondary 
14education allowed to be granted each academic year to persons who, at the time of their 
15graduation from high school or their successful completion of a general educational 
16development examination, were in the custody of the Department of Health and Human 
17Services and resided in foster care or subsidized adoptive care or were minor wards of a 
18subsidized permanency guardian.  This bill also removes the requirements that the tuition 
19waivers be apportioned to postsecondary institutions based upon the order in which the 
20waivers are made available.
