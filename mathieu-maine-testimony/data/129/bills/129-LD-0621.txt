25§1652.  State and political subdivision facilities and functions

2provisions of this section except that a food service establishment providing such services

10containers is not feasible and alternative goals are proposed.  The plan must include a 
11proposed capital plan for the acquisition of necessary equipment; and
12B.  The school or school administrative district has held a public hearing on the 
13proposal to use extruded polystyrene foam food service containers and the waste 
14reduction plan.
15The department may renew the waiver for 2-year periods if it finds that the school or 
16school administrative district has made reasonable progress toward implementing the 
17waste reduction plan.  The department, within available resources, may provide technical 
18and financial assistance to schools and school administrative districts to assist them with 
19meeting the goal of using durable containers.
203.  Meals on wheels.  A food service establishment funded in whole or in part, 
21directly or indirectly, by the Department of Health and Human Services to provide meals 
22at dispersed locations from central kitchen facilities is exempt.
23Sec. 5.  38 MRSA §1652-A is enacted to read:
24§1652-A.  Prohibition of extruded polystyrene foam food service containers
251.  Prohibition of extruded polystyrene foam food service containers.  Except as 
26provided in subsection 2 and except for food establishments providing food or beverages 
27under section 1652, a food establishment may not process, prepare, sell or provide food 
28or beverages in or on an extruded polystyrene foam food service container.
292.  Exemptions.  A food establishment may process, prepare, sell or provide food or 
30beverages in or on an extruded polystyrene foam food service container:
31A.  In an emergency for the immediate preservation of the public health or safety;
32B.  That a member of the public brings to the food establishment; and
33C.  For the retail sale of items that the food establishment receives wholesale in 
34packaging that contains extruded polystyrene foam.
35Sec. 6.  38 MRSA §1654, as enacted by PL 1987, c. 804, is repealed.
