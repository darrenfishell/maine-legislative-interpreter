41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Fixed route system" means a transit route that has a predetermined schedule of 
7stops and destinations.
8B.  "Public transportation" means a service operated for the purpose of transporting 
9members of the public as passengers from one destination to another.
102.  Automated announcement systems required.  Except as provided in subsection 
113, all state and municipal vehicles that operate on a fixed route system for the purpose of 
12public transportation must be equipped with an automated announcement system that 
13delivers audible messages to passengers. An automated announcement system must:
14A.  Deliver clear messages in the form of recorded, synthesized or digitized speech;
15B.  Deliver an audible message outside the vehicle at each stop communicating the 
16applicable transit route, destination of the vehicle and the current stop; and
17C.  Deliver an audible message inside the vehicle prior to each stop communicating 
18the applicable transit route, destination of the vehicle and the next stop.
193.  Vehicles exempt.  Vehicles that cannot reasonably be equipped with an automated 
20announcement system as determined by the Department of Transportation by rule are 
21exempt from the requirements of subsection 2.
22Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
23chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
24SUMMARY
25This bill requires that all state and municipal vehicles that operate on a fixed route 
26system for the purpose of public transportation must be equipped with an automated 
27announcement system that delivers audible messages to passengers regarding transit 
28route, current and next stop and destination of the vehicle.  The bill provides an 
29exemption for vehicles that cannot reasonably be equipped with an automated 
30announcement system.
