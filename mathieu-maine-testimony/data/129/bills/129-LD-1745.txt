9SUMMARY
10This bill makes changes to the laws governing shellfish sales and handling to ensure 
11compliance with the National Shellfish Sanitation Program in the protection of public 
12health and to create additional flexibility in shellfish sales.  The bill:
131.  Requires a person who holds an aquaculture license to tag that person's shellfish in 
14accordance with Department of Marine Resources rules;
152.  Expands the buying station permit to include other species of shellfish besides 
16soft-shelled clams, including mussels, quahogs and oysters; 
173.  Allows aquaculture license holders to sell shellfish to an enhanced retail certificate 
18holder in addition to a wholesale seafood license holder;
194.  Requires a person who holds a land-based aquaculture license to acquire an 
20aquaculture license to sell shellfish from the person's land-based aquaculture facility; and
215.  Clarifies that a shellfish sanitation certificate issued by a jurisdiction recognized 
22by the Interstate Shellfish Sanitation Conference allows a person to process, buy, sell, 
23ship, transport or shuck shellfish or whole scallops.
