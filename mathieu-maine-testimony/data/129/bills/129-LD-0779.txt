Page 1 - 129LR0519(01)-11Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
2Sec. 1.  17-A MRSA §208, sub-§1, ¶C, as amended by PL 2015, c. 358, §1, is 
3further amended to read:
4C.  Bodily injury to another under circumstances manifesting extreme indifference to 
5the value of human life. Such circumstances include, but are not limited to, the 
6number, location or nature of the injuries, the manner or method inflicted, the 
7observable physical condition of the victim or the use of strangulation.  For the 
8purpose of this paragraph, "strangulation" means the intentional impeding of the 
9breathing or circulation of the blood of another person by applying pressure on the 
10person's throat or neck.  Violation of this paragraph is a Class B crime.
11SUMMARY
12This bill removes the requirement that the act of strangulation has to be intentional in 
13order to be considered aggravated assault.
