5SUMMARY
6This bill amends the laws governing licensure for professional engineers and 
7certification for engineer-interns.  The bill:
81.  Changes the term "technology accreditation commission" to "engineering 
9technology education commission" to reflect current usage;
102.  Clarifies language regarding the use of professional engineers in public works 
11projects;
123.  Repeals the temporary licensing provisions for nonresidents;
134.  Clarifies and updates references to the building standards and eliminates 
14references to standards that have been superseded;
155.  Increases the maximum penalty for violations to $10,000;
166.  Clarifies that the State Board of Licensure for Professional Engineers may refuse 
17to renew a license for failure to pay a required fee or for failure to meet continuing 
18education requirements, which the bill renames professional development requirements;
197.  Allows the board to maintain its roster of active licensed professional engineers on 
20its publicly accessible website;
218.  Changes the term "certificate of record" to "active national council record" to 
22reflect current usage of the National Council of Examiners for Engineering and 
23Surveying;
249.  Clarifies and updates language regarding education requirements and 
25examinations;
2610.  Identifies the National Council of Examiners for Engineering and Surveying 
27engineering education standard as the standard used by the board when accepting 
28engineering and engineering technology credits from a program not approved by the 
29accreditation board;
3011.  Eliminates language regarding examinations on fundamental engineering 
31subjects and amends language on passing examination grades and the number of times an 
32examination may be taken by an applicant; and
3312.  Clarifies language regarding reinstatement of a license after revocation.
