2Sec. 3.  Appointments; convening of task force. Resolved:  That all 
3appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
4resolve. The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
5Council once all appointments have been completed. Within 15 days after appointment of 
6all members, the cochairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the task force, which 
7must be no later than 30 days following the appointment of all members. Subsequent 
8meetings are at the call of the cochairs or by decision of a majority of the members. The 
9task force may meet 10 times.
10Sec. 4.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the study conducted by the task force must 
11include, but is not limited to:
121.  A review of the population in need of home and community-based services, the 
13expected demand for services and approaches for determining eligibility;
142.  A review of the potential scope of services needed, including the type and 
15frequency of services, staff time and other resources required to provide those services 
16and the availability and qualifications of the workers who could provide those services;
173.  An examination of existing services provided through public sector and private 
18sector systems, gaps in the systems resulting in unmet needs and the costs to a typical 
19recipient expressed as a percentage of the income of typical recipients;
204. An analysis of the potential for innovative approaches to reimbursement for 
21services and a review of promising proposals and successful models from other 
22jurisdictions;
235.  A discussion with existing public and private home and community-based services 
24providers to analyze the potential for greater coordination and the options for expanding 
25services to meet projected needs;
266.  An analysis of the options for recruiting and retaining a workforce with the 
27requisite skills and with sufficient capacity to satisfy the needs of the population to be 
28served;
297.  An analysis of how federal Medicaid matching funding may provide opportunities 
30to achieve more sustainable delivery of home and community-based services in the 
31future;
328.  An analysis of how cost-sharing approaches can help maximize the scope and 
33efficacy of home and community-based services delivery available in the State; and
349.  An analysis of other policies and strategies that a majority of the task force 
35members agree to include in this study.
36Sec. 5.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human 
37Services and the Department of Labor shall provide the task force with staff assistance

3Sec. 6.  Interim report; public input. Resolved:  That the task force shall 
4publish an interim report by October 15, 2019 and seek and obtain input on the interim 
5report from a wide range of experts on home and community-based services policy, 
6workers, families and agencies around the State who are knowledgeable about home and 
7community-based services or affected by changes in cost or availability of home and 
8community-based services.
9Sec. 7.  Final report.  Resolved:  That the task force shall submit a report 
10containing its findings and recommendations, including any necessary implementing 
11legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services by December 
1215, 2019. The findings and recommendations submitted by the task force must relate to at 
13least the following proposals:
141.  The development of policies and strategies to increase the availability of home and 
15community-based services to seniors and persons with disabilities who have difficulty 
16with at least one activity of daily living;
172.  The establishment of an approach to workforce development that ensures that 
18sufficient personnel are trained and available to serve the home and community-based 
19services needs in the State at adequate compensation levels and allows providers to 
20establish stable and reliable employee work schedules;
213.  An approach to cost-sharing that marshals scarce resources while ensuring that 
22excessive cost is not a widespread obstacle to obtaining needed services; and
234.  The development of a procedure whereby the Department of Labor and the 
24Department of Health and Human Services will report annually to the joint standing 
25committees of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters 
26and labor matters on the subjects addressed by this task force.
27The Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services may submit a bill 
28related to this report to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
29Sec. 8.  Outside funding.  Resolved:  That the task force may seek outside 
30funding to support the work of the task force in accordance with the policy of the 
31Legislative Council with regard to outside funding.
32SUMMARY
33This resolve establishes the Task Force To Study Opportunities for Improving Home 
34and Community-based Services. The task force membership consists of Legislators, 
35representatives of entities knowledgeable about or involved in home and community-
36based services, one provider of such services, one recipient of such services, the 
37Commissioner of Health and Human Services, the Commissioner of Labor and one 
38member of the public. The task force's duties include examination and review of the 
39unmet need for home and community-based services, adequacy of the workforce

2community-based services and reimbursement arrangements in the home and community-
3based services sector. The task force is required to publish an interim report by October 
415, 2019 and then seek input from stakeholders around the State. The task force must 
5report its findings and recommendations, including any necessary implementing 
6legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services by December 
715, 2019.
