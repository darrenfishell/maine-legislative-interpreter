20Sec. 1.  22 MRSA §2428, sub-§13 is enacted to read:
2113.  Reorganization to for-profit status.  The reorganization of a registered 
22dispensary operating as a nonprofit mutual benefit corporation to a for-profit entity is 
23governed by this subsection.
24A.  A registered dispensary that is operating as a nonprofit mutual benefit corporation 
25may enter into any of the following transactions to reorganize the registered 
26dispensary to a for-profit entity:
27(1)  A mutual benefit corporation operating as a nonprofit registered dispensary 
28may merge with and into a business corporation formed pursuant to Title 13-C;
29(2)  A business corporation formed pursuant to the laws of this State may 
30purchase substantially all of the assets of a mutual benefit corporation operating 
31as a nonprofit registered dispensary; and
32(3)  Notwithstanding any provision of the law to the contrary in this Title, Title 
3313-B or Title 13-C, a mutual benefit corporation operating as a nonprofit 
34registered dispensary is entitled to convert into a domestic business corporation 
35by adopting a plan of entity conversion in accordance with Title 13-C, section 
36953 that is approved by a vote of 2/3 of the members of the board of directors of 
37the mutual benefit corporation at a meeting duly called for that purpose or by 
38unanimous written consent.  A plan of entity conversion adopted pursuant to this

2prescribed by the Secretary of State, must be executed and filed in the manner

6B.  Notwithstanding Title 13-B, section 718, and notwithstanding any provision to the 
7contrary in the articles of incorporation or the bylaws of a registered dispensary

11(1)  Approve a transaction set forth in paragraph A, subparagraph (1), (2) or (3);
12(2)  Issue any shares, membership interests or other securities, obligations, rights 
13to acquire interests or other securities, cash or other property; or
14(3)  Designate the directors or a business corporation in which the directors hold 
15interests as members of a nonprofit mutual benefit corporation that previously 
16had no members.
17C.  The patients of a registered dispensary that is operating as a nonprofit mutual 
18benefit corporation may not be deemed members entitled to vote under Title 13-B, 
19section 604, nor may such patients be deemed members for purposes of a merger, 
20purchase or conversion reorganization transaction pursuant to this subsection.
21D.  A registered dispensary that reorganizes to a for-profit entity pursuant to this 
22section, or the dispensary's successor in interest, shall pay to the Medical Use of 
23Marijuana Fund established under section 2430:
24(1)  An amount equal to 10% of the value of a sale or transfer of interest of the 
25reorganized registered dispensary, as determined by an independent appraisal at 
26the time of the sale or transfer of interest, when the sale or transfer of interest is 
27completed in the first year after the reorganization to a for-profit entity;
28(2)  An amount equal to 7.5% of the value of a sale or transfer of interest of the 
29reorganized registered dispensary, as determined by an independent appraisal at 
30the time of the sale or transfer of interest, when the sale or transfer of interest is 
31completed in the 2nd year after the reorganization to a for-profit entity;
32(3)  An amount equal to 5% of the value of a sale or transfer of interest of the 
33reorganized registered dispensary, as determined by an independent appraisal at 
34the time of the sale or transfer of interest, when the sale or transfer of interest is 
35completed in the 3rd year after the reorganization to a for-profit entity; and
36(4)  An amount equal to 2.5% of the value of a sale or transfer of interest of the 
37reorganized registered dispensary, as determined by an independent appraisal at 
38the time of the sale or transfer of interest, when the sale or transfer of interest is 
39completed in the 4th year after the reorganization to a for-profit entity.
40E.  A registered dispensary that reorganizes to a for-profit entity pursuant to this 
41section, or the dispensary's successor in interest, shall pay to the Medical Use of 
42Marijuana Fund established under section 2430 an amount that is not less than 2% of

32SUMMARY
33Public Law 2017, chapter 452 authorized registered dispensaries under the Maine 
34Medical Use of Marijuana Act operating as nonprofit entities to become for-profit 
35entities, but did not outline a pathway to follow for such a transaction.  This bill:
361.  Specifies the possible pathways of merger, purchase and conversion for such a 
37dispensary to become a for-profit entity;
Page 4 - 129LR1776(01)-112.  Provides that a registered dispensary operating as a nonprofit entity that 
2reorganizes as a for-profit entity retains its registration certificate;
33.  Requires that a registered dispensary that reorganizes as a for-profit entity pay to 
4the Medical Use of Marijuana Fund a percentage of the value of the sale or transfer of 
5interest; and
64.  Requires that a registered dispensary that reorganizes as a for-profit entity pay to 
7the Medical Use of Marijuana Fund 2% of gross sales for discounts to certain qualified 
8patients.
