COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A " to H.P. 118, L.D. 136, Bill, "An Act To 
Establish a Special Education Circuit Breaker Reimbursement Program" 
Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
'Resolve, To Require the Department of Education To Study and Report on the 
Special Education Funding Component of the School Funding Formula' 
Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and inserting the following: 
'Sec. 1. Report. Resolved: That the Department of Education shall study the 
special education funding component of the essential programs and services funding 
formula pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, chapter 606-B and report its 
findings to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
education matters no later than January 15, 2021. The report must include, but is not 
limited to, an examination of the special education budgetary hardship adjustment 
pursuant to Title 20-A, section 15689, subsection 15 and recommendations on how to 
make that adjustment more responsive to the needs of schools and an update on any 
review of the special education funding component that the department conducts in 
conjunction with the Maine Education Policy Research Institute. The joint standing 
committee may report out a bill relating to the subject matter of the report to the First 
Regular Session or Second Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.' 
Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or 
section number to read consecutively. 
SUMMARY 
This amendment, which strikes and replaces the bill with a resolve, requires the 
Department of Education to study and report to the joint standing committee of the 
Legislature having jurisdiction over education matters on the department's continued 
work with the Maine Education Policy Research Institute on the special education 
funding component of the school funding formula, including but not limited to an 
examination of the special education budgetary hardship adjustment. The joint standing
