Page 1 - 129LR0463(01)-11Sec. 1.  Maine Human Rights Commission pilot program to investigate 
2and report on incidents and complaints of harassment due to housing status, 
3lack of employment and other issues.  Resolved:  That the Maine Human Rights 
4Commission shall, within budgeted resources, implement a 2-year pilot program to 
5receive, review and investigate incidents and complaints of harassment due to a person's 
6lack of employment or housing status and other reports of interference with a person's 
7access to public accommodations.
8In carrying out the pilot program, the commission shall investigate and respond to 
9incidents and complaints of harassment as set out in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, 
10sections 4611 and 4612.  The commission may use any of its powers under Title 5, 
11section 4566 to carry out the pilot program.  The commission may limit the scope of the 
12pilot program in the interest of efficiency.
13The commission may, in order to carry out the pilot program, establish an advisory 
14board to document and evaluate incidents and complaints and to advise the commission 
15as to which should be acted on and possible solutions.
16The commission shall produce an interim report for submission to the Joint Standing 
17Committee on Judiciary by September 15, 2020.  The commission shall produce a final 
18report for submission to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
19jurisdiction over judiciary matters by September 15, 2021.  Either report may contain 
20recommendations on changes to the pilot program or for its continuation as well as 
21suggested legislation to carry out any of the recommendations.
22SUMMARY
23This resolve directs the Maine Human Rights Commission to create a 2-year pilot 
24program to receive, review and investigate incidents and complaints of harassment due to 
25a person's lack of employment or housing status and other reports of interference with a 
26person's access to public accommodations.  In carrying out the pilot program, the 
27commission must investigate and respond to incidents and complaints of harassment as 
28set out in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, sections 4611 and 4612.  The commission 
29is authorized to use any of its powers under Title 5, section 4566 to carry out the pilot 
30program and may limit its scope.  The commission is authorized to establish an advisory 
31board to document and evaluate complaints and to advise the commission as to which 
32incidents and complaints should be acted on and possible solutions.  The commission is 
33directed to produce an interim report for submission to the Joint Standing Committee on 
34Judiciary by September 15, 2020 and a final report to the joint standing committee of the 
35Legislature having jurisdiction over judiciary matters by September 15, 2021.  The 
36reports are authorized to contain recommendations on changes to the program or for its 
37continuation as well as proposed legislation to carry out any recommendations.
