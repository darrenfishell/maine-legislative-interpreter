3ยง1728.  Safety and security in health care facilities
41.  Definitions.  For the purposes of this section, unless the context otherwise 
5indicates, the following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Health care" means any care, treatment, service or procedure to maintain, 
7diagnose or otherwise affect an individual's physical or mental condition.
8B.  "Health care facility" means a facility, institution or entity licensed pursuant to 
9this Title that offers health care to persons in this State.
102.  Safety and security plan.  A health care facility shall, on an annual basis, adopt a 
11safety and security plan to protect the patients, visitors and employees of the health care 
12facility from aggressive and violent behavior.  The safety and security plan must include 
13a process for health care facilities to receive and record incidents and threats of violent 
14behavior occurring at or arising out of employment at the health care facility.  The safety 
15and security plan must prohibit a representative or employee of the health care facility 
16from interfering with a person making a report as provided in the plan.  The provisions of 
17this section do not apply to a hospital required to adopt a safety and security plan 
18pursuant to section 1832.
19SUMMARY
20Under current law, hospitals are required to annually adopt a safety and security plan 
21to protect patients, visitors and employees of the hospital from aggressive and violent 
22behavior.  This bill extends that requirement to all health care facilities.
