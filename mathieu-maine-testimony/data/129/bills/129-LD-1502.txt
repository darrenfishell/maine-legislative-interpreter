5ยง60-R.  Alternative path to licensure through apprenticeship
61.  Requirement.  Beginning January 1, 2020, a licensing authority shall grant a 
7license to an applicant who:
8A.  Successfully completes the 8th grade;
9B.  Completes an apprenticeship that:
10(1)  Is approved by the Department of Labor or federal Department of Labor or 
11otherwise permitted under state or federal law; and
12(2)  Is completed either at a school that is licensed by the State or by training with 
13a person licensed by the State who holds the license for which the applicant 
14applies; and
15C.  If an examination is required by the licensing authority, passes the required 
16examination.
172.  Examination.  If, in order to grant a license under this Act, a licensing authority 
18requires an applicant to pass an examination, the licensing authority shall establish a 
19passing score for an applicant for licensure under this Act that does not exceed the 
20passing score that is required of a person who applies for that license under another 
21chapter of this Title.
22A licensing authority may not require an applicant for a license under this Act to pass an 
23examination if the licensing authority does not require an applicant for that license under 
24another chapter of this Title to pass that examination.
253.  Rulemaking.  A licensing authority shall adopt rules necessary for the 
26implementation of this Act.  Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine 
27technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
28SUMMARY
29This bill establishes an alternative path to licensure through apprenticeship for certain 
30professions.  The bill requires that, beginning January 1, 2020, the appropriate licensing 
31authority grant to a person that has successfully completed 8th grade, passed any required 
32examinations and completed an approved apprenticeship a license to practice as:
331. An auctioneer;
342. A dietetic technician;
353. A veterinary technician;
364. A pharmacy technician;
