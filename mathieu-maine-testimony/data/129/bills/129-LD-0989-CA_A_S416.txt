10 Improve Maine's Tax Laws" 
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
12 'An Act To Improve Maine's Tax Laws by Providing a Property Tax Exemption for 
13 Central Labor Councils' 
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the 
15 following: 
16 'Sec. 1. 36 MRSA §652, sub-§1, ,rF, as amended by PL 2007, c. 627, §20, is 
17 further amended to read: 
18 F. The real estate and personal property owned and occupied or used solely for their 
19 own purposes by central labor councils, chambers of commerce or boards of trade in 
20 this State are exempt from taxation. For the purposes of this paragraph, "central labor 
21 council" means an association or network of labor unions designed to promote and 
22 protect the interests of their members. 
23 Further conditions to the right of exemption are that: 
24 (1) A director, trustee, officer or employee of any organization claiming 
25 exemption may not receive directly or indirectly any pecuniary profit from the 
26 operation of that organization, except as reasonable compensation for services in 
27 effecting its purposes or as a proper beneficiary of its purposes; 
28 (2) All profits derived from the operation of the organization and the proceeds 
29 from the sale of its property must be devoted exclusively to the purposes for 
30 which it is organized; and 
31 (3) The institution, organization or corporation claiming exemption. under this 
32 paragraph must file with the assessors upon their request a report for its 
33 preceding fiscal year in such detail as the assessors may reasonably require.'
