41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Correctional facility" has the same definition as in Title 34-A, section 1001, 
7subsection 6.
8B.  "Corrections officer" means an employee of the State or county whose place of 
9employment is a correctional facility or a county detention facility.
10C.  "Infectious disease" means a disease resulting from the presence and activity of a 
11pathogenic microbial agent, including but not limited to bacteria, viruses, fungi and 
12parasites.
13D.  "State mental health institute" has the same meaning as described in Title 22-A, 
14section 208.
152.  Compensation payable from date of incapacity.  Notwithstanding section 204, 
16compensation for incapacity to work is payable from the date of incapacity when 
17incapacity results in the course of employment from an assault by a person under the 
18employee's care or from an infectious disease transmitted to the employee by a person 
19under the employee's care, and that employee is:
20A.  A corrections officer;
21B.  An employee of a state mental health institute; or
22C.  An employee of the Department of Health and Human Services who provides 
23direct care to individuals through:
24(1)  Mental health and behavioral health services; or
25(2)  Developmental disability services.
26SUMMARY
27Under current law, compensation for incapacity to work is not payable for the first 7 
28days of incapacity, except that firefighters receive compensation from the date of 
29incapacity.  This bill provides that, when incapacity results in the course of employment 
30from an assault by a person under the employee's care or from an infectious disease 
31transmitted to the employee by a person under the employee's care, compensation is 
32payable from the date of incapacity for corrections officers, employees of state mental 
33health institutes and employees of the Department of Health and Human Services who 
34provide direct care to individuals through mental health and behavioral health services or 
35developmental disability services.
