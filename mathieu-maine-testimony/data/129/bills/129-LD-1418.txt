41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5following terms have the following meanings.
6A.  "Behavioral health needs" means a wide range of mental health disorders and 
7illnesses, substance use disorder, developmental disabilities and autism spectrum 
8disorder.
9B.  "Community service provider" has the same meaning as in Title 5, section 20003, 
10subsection 4.
11C.  "Extended stay" means a stay of a patient in a health care facility that is longer 
12than required to stabilize immediate threats to the life and safety of the patient for the 
13purpose of a longer term treatment of a health care issue.
14D.  "Hospital emergency department" means the department of a hospital responsible 
15for the provision of medical and surgical care to patients arriving at the hospital with 
16health conditions, including illnesses and trauma, requiring immediate care.
17E.  "Mental health disorders and illness" means certain mental and emotional 
18disorders including depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, 
19post-traumatic stress disorder, conduct disorder, attention deficit disorder and 
20attention deficit hyperactivity disorder and aggressive behavior, risk of harm to self 
21or others and difficulty with anger control.
22F.  "Substance use disorder" means a significant functional impairment of a person, 
23including a health problem or a disability, caused by the misuse of alcohol or another 
24drug as addressed by Title 5, chapter 521.
252.  Website.  The department shall maintain:
26A.  A daily updated statewide list on a publicly accessible website accessible by 
27hospital emergency departments that displays available placements to assist hospital 
28emergency departments to locate potential openings in mental health facilities or 
29programs and with community service providers for minors with behavioral health 
30needs for the purpose of referring the persons to the facilities or programs for 
31residential placement or care; and
32B.  A quarterly updated resource list for hospital emergency departments on a 
33publicly accessible website that includes mental health facilities or programs and 
34community service providers for minors with behavioral health needs with 
35descriptions of the admissions and exclusions criteria of the programs, facilities and 
36providers, including age, gender and populations served, such as individuals with 
37physical or developmental disabilities or autism or other behavioral health needs.

4SUMMARY
5This bill:
61. Defines "behavioral health needs" to mean a wide range of mental health disorders 
7and illnesses, substance use disorder, developmental disabilities and autism spectrum 
8disorder;
92. Directs the Department of Health and Human Services to take measures to address 
10the issue of extended stays in hospital emergency departments by minors with behavioral 
11health needs by:
12A.  Maintaining a daily updated online statewide list of available mental health 
13facility or program and community service provider placements for referral purposes 
14by hospital emergency departments;
15B.  Maintaining a quarterly updated online resource list of mental health programs or 
16facilities and community service providers that treat behavioral health needs; and
17C.  Collecting monthly data on and study the issue of extended stays of minors with 
18behavioral health needs in hospital emergency departments and annually submitting a 
19report with a summary of the study along with any recommendations to the joint 
20standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human 
21services matters;
223.  Directs the department to study the data and analysis on extended stays in hospital 
23emergency departments by minors with behavioral health needs and submit an annual 
24report to the Legislature that identifies:
25A.  Reasons for the extended stays;
26B.  Specific behavioral health needs treatment programs and the waiting list for 
27admission to each program; and
28C.  Funding mechanisms to provide short-term transitional assistance to minors with 
29behavioral health needs discharged from a hospital emergency department to 
30residential placements, partial hospitalizations or home-based programs; and
314.  Directs the department to study the feasibility of adding a child behavioral needs 
32advocate within the department to coordinate the department's activities with those of 
33various agencies and programs that provide behavioral health needs services to minors 
34and to submit a report to the Legislature by January 1, 2020.
