61.  Departmental collections; immediate payment to State Treasury.  A 
7department or agency of the State collecting or receiving public money, or money from 
8any source whatsoever, belonging to or for the use of the State, or for the use of any state 
9department or agency, shall pay the money immediately into the State Treasury, without 
10any deductions on account of salaries, fees, costs, charges, expenses, refunds, claims or 
11demands of any description whatsoever.  The Department of Agriculture, Conservation 
12and Forestry, Bureau of Parks and Lands may refund daily use and camping fees based on 
13the bureau's standard refund policies.  A department or agency may deposit such money 
14to the credit of the State upon communicating with the Treasurer of State and receiving 
15from the Treasurer of State instructions as to what state depository may be used for that 
16purpose, and in every such case the depositor shall send to the Treasurer of State a 
17statement of the deposits certified by the bank receiving it.  This section does not apply to 
18county or town officers.
192.  Certain payments not immediate.  Notwithstanding subsection 1, certain 
20payments due the State made through the use of automated procedures, electronic 
21processes and computer-driven technology must be deposited in the State Treasury within 
223 business days of the date of the transaction initiated by the obligor.  The Treasurer of 
23State and the State Controller may adopt rules to implement this subsection, including 
24rules outlining procedures for the use of automated procedures, electronic processes and 
25computer-driven technology for the collection of these payments pursuant to this 
26subsection.  Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection may not waive prohibitions against 
27deductions on account of salaries, fees, costs, charges, expenses, refunds, claims or 
28demands of any description whatsoever.  Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are 
29routine technical rules as defined by chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
30SUMMARY
31This bill requires the collection of certain payments due the State made through the 
32use of automated procedures, electronic processes and computer-driven technology to be 
33deposited in the State Treasury within 3 business days of the date of the transaction 
34initiated by the obligor.  It allows the Treasurer of State and the State Controller to adopt 
35routine technical rules outlining procedures for the use of these payment methods.  It 
36prohibits any rule adopted from waiving prohibitions against deductions on account of 
37salaries, fees, costs, charges, expenses, refunds, claims or demands of any description 
38whatsoever.
