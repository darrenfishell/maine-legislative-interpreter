21 SUMMARY 
22 This amendment expands the list of persons who may be served by a critical incident 
23 stress management team and changes the description of the employing entity from "a 
24 criminal justice agency" to a "public safety agency" and adds an organization involved in 
25 emergency care or response or a county jail or a correctional facility operated by the 
26 Department of Corrections. The amendment removes reference to cumulative exposure 
27 to stress and traumatic incidents· added by the bill. The amendment removes from the bill 
28 specific types of training, instead requiring training in accordance with national best 
29 practices and standards established by the Commissioner of Public Safety and providing 
30 routine technical rulemaking authority to the commissioner. The amendment removes the 
31 requirement that a critical incident stress management team have as a member a licensed 
32 mental health clinician. The amendment requires the critical incident stress management 
33 team to have a relationship with a licensed mental health clinician who is available for 
34 consultation with members of the team as needed and with the team at least once per 
35 year.
