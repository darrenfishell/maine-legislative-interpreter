41.     The commission shall be The Criminal Law Advisory Commission, referred to 
5in this chapter as "the commission," is composed of 9 members to be appointed by the 
6Attorney General. The members shall must be qualified by reason of their experience in 
7the prosecution or defense of criminal cases or by reason of their knowledge of the

11county or municipal law enforcement agency.  Members who are officers from a law 
12enforcement agency must have met the training requirements of Title 25, section 2804-C 
13or equivalent training in another state prior to appointment to the commission.
14Sec. 2.  Transition for appointment of Criminal Law Advisory 
15Commission members.  Notwithstanding the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 17-A, 
16section 1352, members of the Criminal Law Advisory Commission appointed by the 
17Attorney General serving on the effective date of this Act continue to serve until the 
18completion of their terms.  Their successors are appointed as provided in Title 17-A, 
19section 1352.
20SUMMARY
21This bill amends the membership of the Criminal Law Advisory Commission to 
22require that at least one member be a law enforcement officer from a state law 
23enforcement agency and at least one member be a law enforcement officer from a county 
24or municipal law enforcement agency.
