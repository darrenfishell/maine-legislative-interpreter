8SUMMARY
9This bill amends language contained in Public Law 2019, chapter 446 governing 
10contributions under the open enrollment provisions of the Retired County and Municipal 
11Law Enforcement Officers and Municipal Firefighters Health Insurance Program.  
12Currently, the law requires a person hired on or after October 1, 2019 to enroll no later 
13than 5 years after hire, conditioned on the enrollment and eligibility requirements of the 
14applicable health plan; this bill removes that condition.
15This bill also changes the percentage of gross wages for contributions from 1.5% to 
163% for the first 5 years and from 3% to 1.5% for additional years.  This bill removes the 
17requirement of 60 months of contributions by enrollees.  The bill also changes the State's 
18payment of certain premium subsidies from payment to the plan to payment directly to 
19enrollees.  The amendments to the open enrollment provisions are retroactive to 
20September 19, 2019, and the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, office 
21of employee health and benefits is required to ensure correct contribution amounts for 
22those who enrolled between September 19, 2019 and the effective date of this legislation.
