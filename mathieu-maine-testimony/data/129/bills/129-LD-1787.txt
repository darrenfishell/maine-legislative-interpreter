17SUMMARY
18This bill makes several changes to the State's fish and wildlife enforcement laws.  
19The bill:
201.  Makes it illegal for nonviolent juvenile offenders and persons convicted of 
21domestic violence to possess a firearm hunting license.  It also prohibits a person 
22convicted of a domestic violence offense from owning or possessing a crossbow, a 
23muzzleloader or archery or airbow equipment;
242.  Provides that a person is guilty of aggravated trafficking in a scheduled drug if the 
25person is convicted of trafficking while in an area open to fishing for only persons under 
2616 years of age or complimentary fishing license holders;
273.  Requires all edible meat from bear, deer and moose to be presented for registration 
28with evidence of sex;
294.  Clarifies that a person's hunting license will be revoked if the person is convicted 
30of night hunting while in possession of a thermal imaging device;
315.  Amends the definition of "owner" for the purpose of registration of a snowmobile, 
32watercraft and all-terrain vehicle;
336. Provides for penalties for bag limit and possession limit violations for individual 
34upland game species as established by the commissioner;
357.  Clarifies the time limit to register a bear, deer, moose or wild turkey;
368.  Clarifies that bear fat not attached to the meat can legally be used for personal or 
37commercial use without a hide dealer's license;
Page 11 - 129LR2355(01)-119.  Provides for restitution of expenses incurred as a result of scientific testing to 
2enhance investigation procedures;
310.  Clarifies that a person may not take or possess reptiles or amphibians from the 
4wild for export, sale or commercial purposes;
511.  Strengthens the prohibition of abuse of another person's property by removing 
6reference to certain stated activities such as hunting, fishing or trapping to allow the 
7Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife to enforce the law against persons who 
8abuse another person's property but who may not be involved in an activity such as 
9hunting, fishing or trapping; and
1012.  Replaces the prohibition on operating a motorboat that exceeds noise limits with 
11a prohibition on operating a marine engine that exceeds noise limits.
