11Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following:
12'An Act To Reform Compensation for Corrections Officers, Corrections Captains, 
13Correctional Lieutenants and Corrections Unit Managers in Maine' 
14Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and before the 
15emergency clause and inserting the following:
16'Sec. 1.  Adjustment of salary schedules for corrections officers.  
17Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, effective for the first pay period 
18commencing on or after July 1, 2020, the salary schedules for corrections officers must be 
19adjusted upward to provide increases in wages that will fully utilize over the fiscal year 
20the appropriation provided by this Act for that purpose. For purposes of this section, 
21"corrections officer" means an employee of the State whose place of employment is a 
22state correctional facility and who is responsible for the care, custody or direct 
23supervision of a person confined in a state correctional facility or committed to the Long 
24Creek Youth Development Center pursuant to an order of a court or as a result of an 
25arrest or detention. For purposes of this section, "state correctional facility" has the same 
26meaning as "correctional facility" as defined in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 34-A, 
27section 1001, subsection 6 and includes the Long Creek Youth Development Center.
28Sec. 2.  Adjustment of salary schedules for corrections captains, 
29correctional lieutenants and corrections unit managers.  Notwithstanding any 
30provision of law to the contrary, effective for the first pay period commencing on or after 
31July 1, 2020, the salary schedules for corrections captains, correctional lieutenants and 
32corrections unit managers must be adjusted upward to provide increases in wages that 
33will fully utilize over the fiscal year the appropriation provided by this Act for that 
34purpose.  For purposes of this section, "corrections captain," "correctional lieutenant" and 
35"corrections unit manager" are each defined to mean an employee of the State whose 
36place of employment is a state correctional facility, who supervises corrections officers 
37and who is responsible for the care, custody or direct supervision of a person confined in Date: (Filing No. H-          )

2This amendment is the majority report of the committee.  This amendment provides 
3funding for wage increases for corrections officers and funding for comparable wage 
4increases for corrections captains, correctional lieutenants and corrections unit managers 
5commencing with the first pay period that begins on or after July 1, 2020.

Fiscal Detail and Notes
The bill requires equal pay increases to correctional offi cers (and their equivalent position at Long Creek Youth 
Development Center) out of a total of $3,656,580. After dividing this total am ong the specified postions, the average 
increase in pay was 13%. The bill also requires wage increases  to those who supervise correctional officers, including 
corrections captains, correctional lieutenants, corrections  unit managers (and equivalent positions at Long Creek 
Youth Development Center) equal to the same percentage wage increase as given to correctional officers. The bill 
includes $4,085,590 in General Fund appropriations across corr ectional facilities in the Department of Corrections, 
with $3,656,580 appropriatied for correctional officers, and $429,010 for supervisory positions. Pay increases are 
retroactive to July 2020. Fiscal Note129th MAINE LEGISLATURE
