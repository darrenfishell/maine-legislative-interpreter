81.  At-risk person.  "At-risk person" means a person:
9A.  Who suffers from a mental or physical condition that may affect the person's 
10ability to communicate with a law enforcement officer or who may benefit from a 
11law enforcement officer's being aware of specific information and a contact person or 
12persons; and
13B.  Who voluntarily participates in the at-risk persons program and provides 
14information and a contact person or persons that may be helpful in maximizing the 
15safety of the at-risk person during an encounter with a law enforcement officer.
162.  At-risk persons program.  "At-risk persons program" means the program 
17developed and implemented pursuant to section 2242.
183.  At-risk persons system.  "At-risk persons system" means a database and required 
19interfaces to house and communicate voluntarily submitted information specific to an at-
20risk person.  The system must include interfaces with the State's telecommunications and 
21radio message switching system and must be accessible by law enforcement.
224.  Department.  "Department" means the Department of Public Safety.
235.  Law enforcement officer.  "Law enforcement officer" has the same meaning as in 
24section 3701, subsection 3.
256.  Legal guardian.  "Legal guardian" means a guardian appointed pursuant to Title 
2618-C, section 5-301 or 5-701.

281.  At-risk persons program; program elements.  The department shall develop 
29and implement the at-risk persons program using the at-risk persons system to assist law 
30enforcement agencies by creating access to information voluntarily submitted by an at-
31risk person or the legal guardian of the at-risk person.  The information that may be held 
32by the program is limited to information submitted to a law enforcement agency by the 
33at-risk person or that person's legal guardian.  The information may include, but is not 
34limited to, the following:

2at-risk person; and
3B.  Other information that may aid in maximizing the safety of the at-risk person 
4during an encounter with a law enforcement agency.
52.  Standards of procedure.  The at-risk persons program must include standards of 
6procedure for law enforcement agencies consistent with rules adopted by the department.  
7The standards must address processing the application of an at-risk person or that person's 
8legal guardian, including the determination of identity and confirmation of legal 
9guardianship, entering the information into the State's telecommunications and radio 
10message switching system and properly accessing the information.
113.  Rules.  The department may adopt routine technical rules pursuant to Title 5, 
12chapter 375, subchapter 2-A to carry out the purposes of this chapter.
13SUMMARY
14This bill creates the at-risk persons program to provide law enforcement officers with 
15the contact information for a person designated by an at-risk person or that person's legal 
16guardian.  The program also provides access to information that may aid in maximizing 
17the safety of the at-risk person during an encounter with a law enforcement officer.
