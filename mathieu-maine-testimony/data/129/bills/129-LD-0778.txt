12SUMMARY
13This bill establishes the Fund for Municipalities To Improve Pedestrian Safety as a 
14program account in the Highway Fund within the Department of Transportation.  The bill 
15provides that the fund must be used for pedestrian safety improvements, such as lights, 
16paint, signs, speed bumps and reconstruction of intersections.  The bill also provides that 
17a municipality or a group of municipalities may apply for funding from the fund.  Up to 
1880% of project costs for pedestrian safety improvements may be financed from the fund 
19with the remainder of the costs provided by the municipality or group of municipalities, 
20except that up to 100% of proposed project costs for pedestrian safety improvements may 
21be financed from the fund if the project is located within an area identified by the 
22Department of Transportation as a dangerous intersection based on pedestrian crash data.  
23The bill also requires the department to notify municipalities of the required biennial 
24report and pedestrian crash data.  Finally, the bill provides that the Fund for 
25Municipalities To Improve Pedestrian Safety receives revenue from uncommitted 
26balances in the Multimodal Transportation Fund and other funds from any public or 
27private source.
