24SUMMARY
25This bill accomplishes the following.
261.  It directs the Department of Health and Human Services to seek a waiver 
27beginning with federal fiscal year 2020, which begins October 1, 2019, and for each 
28federal fiscal year thereafter to allow individuals otherwise subject to a 3-month limit on 
29federal food supplement program benefits to continue to receive benefits if those 
30individuals reside in counties, labor market areas or other areas that qualify for a waiver 
31because of high unemployment or a lack of a sufficient number of jobs to provide 
32employment for those individuals.
332.  It requires the Department of Health and Human Services to calculate the amount 
34of the food stamp standard utility allowance using reliable data reflecting actual utility 
35costs in Maine.
