17 SUMMARY 
18 This amendment, which is the unanimous report of the committee, removes the 
19 sections of the bill increasing the lead poisoning prevention fee. It continues funding for 
20 5 limited-period Environmental Specialist III positions instead of making the positions 
21 permanent. It also amends the section that repeals the lead poisoning prevention fee 
22 when a period of 24 months has elapsed since the Department of Health and Human 
23 Services identified a child with an elevated blood lead level from a level of blood lead of 
24 10 micrograms per deciliter to 5 micrograms per deciliter. It adds an appropriations and 
25 allocations section.

The bill includes General Fund appropriations to the Department of Health and Human Services of $614,273 in fiscal 
year 2019-20 and $631,780 in fiscal year 2020-21 for 2 limited-period Environmental Specialist III positions, one 
Office Associate II position, one Public Health Nurse I position, one Chemist I position and one Chemist II position to

Healthy Maine allocations are also included to continue funding for 5 limited-period Enviromnental Specialist III 
positions and for the required inspections. Federal Block Grant Fund allocations are also included for the federal 
match on some of the required inspections.
