Page 1 - 129LR0073(01)-11Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services and judicial branch to 
2establish an intensive drug treatment court pilot project in the midcoast area 
3of the State. Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services, in 
4consultation with the judicial branch, shall establish an intensive drug treatment court 2-
5year pilot project in the midcoast area of the State, referred to in this resolve as "the pilot 
6project."  The pilot project must serve 10 participants who live in the midcoast area of the 
7State and meet the requirements for participation in the drug court programs established 
8in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 4, section 421.  The Department of Health and 
9Human Services shall provide case management and ancillary services that are currently 
10provided as conditions of participation in drug court programs as well as intensive 
11services to improve the likelihood of recovery.  As many of the following services as 
12necessary must be provided:
131.  Outpatient substance use disorder treatment services;
142.  Residential substance use disorder treatment services for 28 days;
153.  Medication management and medication-assisted treatment;
164.  Drug testing with a minimum of 2 random and observed tests per week;
175.  Safe, sober and stable housing in properties directly owned or rented by the 
18department;
196.  Case management services;
207.  Employment training;
218.  Transportation services;
229.  Incentives for use as rewards for altered behavior;
2310.  Forensic psychiatric evaluation;
2411.  Education including high school equivalency or community college classes;
2512.  Child care services;
2613.  Treatment services for the family members of the participants;
2714.  Medical and dental services;
2815.  Peer support; and
2916.  Public health nurse visits.
30Sec. 2.  Evaluation; professional development.  Resolved:  That the pilot 
31project established in section 1 must be operational no later than November 1, 2019 and 
32run for 2 years.  The pilot project must be evaluated by an independent organization with 
33experience in evaluation of specialty courts and substance use disorder.  Funding for the 
Page 2 - 129LR0073(01)-11pilot project must include funds for professional development for judges, case managers 
2and other service providers as well as the independent evaluation required in this section.
3Sec. 3.  Report.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services 
4shall provide an interim report to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human 
5Services no later than January 2, 2020 describing implementation of the pilot project.  
6The department shall submit a final report no later than January 2, 2022 to the joint 
7standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services 
8matters that includes details of the levels of services used by participants, outcome 
9measures including measures of recovery and the independent evaluation required in 
10section 2.  The committee may report out legislation regarding the report to the Second 
11Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.

16SUMMARY
17This resolve establishes an intensive drug treatment court 2-year pilot project in the 
18midcoast area of the State to be operational no later than November 1, 2019.  The pilot 
19project will serve 10 participants who meet the requirements for participation in drug 
20court programs.  The support services provided by the Department of Health and Human 
21Services under the pilot project are more intensive than those provided to current 
22participants in drug court programs.  The department is required to provide an interim 
23report on implementation and a final report that includes the results of an independent 
24evaluation of the project.GENERAL FUND 2019-20 2020-21
