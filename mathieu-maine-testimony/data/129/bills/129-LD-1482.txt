242-A.  Tracking.  A lot of wild blueberries harvested from a field may not be mixed or 
25commingled with wild blueberries harvested from another field. Each field from which 
26wild blueberries are harvested must have a unique identifying number, and a shipper or 
27processor must record for each lot of wild blueberries shipped, received, transported or

17SUMMARY
18This bill amends the laws regarding the wild Maine blueberry tax in the following 
19ways.
201.  It establishes a tax of 3/4¢ per pound for wild blueberries shipped from outside the 
21State for processing in the State.
222.  It requires detailed reporting of the wild blueberry transactions subject to the tax.
233.  It requires the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to conduct 
24periodic random inspections of wild blueberry shippers and processors so that each 
25shipper and processor is inspected at least once in a 3-year period.
264.  It requires the Wild Blueberry Commission of Maine to maintain and periodically 
27update a detailed survey of the wild blueberry industry acreage, assets and other industry 
28data.
295.  It requires the Wild Blueberry Commission of Maine to submit an itemized report 
30of its yearly revenue and expenditures to the Commissioner of Agriculture, Conservation 
31and Forestry and the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
32agriculture matters.
336.  It requires shippers and processors to maintain permanent records of the origin of 
34wild blueberries received by the shippers and processors.
357.  It prohibits the mixing or commingling of wild blueberries harvested from a field 
36with wild blueberries harvested from another field and requires each field from which 
37wild blueberries are harvested to have a unique identifying number and shippers and 
38processors to record for each lot of wild blueberries shipped, received, transported or 
39processed the unique identifying number of that lot.
Page 5 - 129LR1121(01)-118.  It changes the penalty for a 2nd violation of the record-keeping requirements 
2within a 5-year period from up to $10,000 to $10,000.
39.  It allows the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to audit a 
4shipper or processor at its own discretion.
