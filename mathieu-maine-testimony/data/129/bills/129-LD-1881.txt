14fee or penalty is below $10,000 and to the nearest multiple of $1,000 if the fee or penalty 
15is $10,000 or greater.
16SUMMARY
17This bill requires the Public Utilities Commission to report annually to the joint 
18standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over public utilities matters on 
19any utility-related fees and penalties that have not been adjusted in the previous 5 years 
20and to submit legislation to adjust each utility-related fee or penalty based on the 
21Consumer Price Index.  The commission is also required to submit legislation to the First 
22Regular Session of the 130th Legislature to adjust all fees and penalties paid by public 
23utilities based on the actuarially compounded Consumer Price Index for each fee or 
24penalty since enactment.
25The bill also requires the commission and the Public Advocate, beginning in 2021, to 
26annually review their expenses relating to all consumer-owned public utilities and 
27expenses relating to all investor-owned public utilities over the past year, determine the 
28average regulatory cost per customer per year for all consumer-owned public utilities and 
29all investor-owned public utilities in each utility industry and amend their rules or 
30introduce legislation to address a significant discrepancy in the average regulatory costs.
