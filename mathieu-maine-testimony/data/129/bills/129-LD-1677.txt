3ยง3036-B.  Reentry houses
41.  Reentry house defined.  For purposes of this section, "reentry house" means a 
5correctional program provided by the department through a written contract with one or 
6more private employers under which the employers provide and maintain housing for 
7specified prisoners, employ those prisoners and provide to those prisoners all meals, 
8laundry facilities and transportation to and from job sites.  A reentry house is not a 
9correctional facility.
102.  Contract.  The commissioner may enter into contracts for the establishment of 
11reentry houses for the purpose of providing housing and other assistance to prisoners 
12transferred to supervised community confinement under section 3036-A.
133.  Other assistance.  The department shall employ, or contract for, program staff to 
14provide other assistance to prisoners housed at a reentry house, including, but not limited 
15to, assistance with reentry planning.
164.  Transfer.  A prisoner may be transferred to supervised community confinement to 
17be housed in a reentry house only if the prisoner meets all of the eligibility requirements 
18of section 3036-A and the rules adopted by the commissioner pursuant to section 3036-A, 
19has successfully participated in a work release program at a department facility and has 
20the skills necessary to perform a job available from a private employer with which the 
21department has contracted.
225.  Supervision.  All of the provisions of section 3036-A and the rules adopted by the 
23commissioner pursuant to section 3036-A apply to a prisoner housed at a reentry house, 
24and supervision of the prisoner must be conducted by a probation officer in the same 
25manner as for any other prisoner transferred to supervised community confinement.
266.  Escape.  A prisoner who is transferred to supervised community confinement who 
27intentionally violates a requirement to reside at a reentry house or otherwise escapes is 
28guilty of escape under Title 17-A, section 755.
29SUMMARY
30This bill allows the Department of Corrections to contract with private employers for 
31reentry houses for the purpose of providing housing and other assistance to prisoners 
32transferred to supervised community confinement.
