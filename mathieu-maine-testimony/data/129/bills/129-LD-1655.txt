3Sec. 5.  22 MRSA §3173-J is enacted to read:
4§3173-J.  Social Determinants of Health Stakeholder Advisory Group
5The Social Determinants of Health Stakeholder Advisory Group, as established by 
6Title 5, section 12004-I, subsection 39-A and referred to in this section as "the advisory 
7group," is created within the department.
81.  Membership.  The advisory group consists of the commissioner or the 
9commissioner's designee and 5 other members appointed by the commissioner as follows:
10A.  A representative of an organization that provides home health care services as 
11defined in section 3172;
12B.  A representative of an organization that works to reduce food insecurity;
13C.  A representative of an organization that provides housing assistance;
14D.  A representative of an organization that promotes public health; and
15E.  A representative of an organization that supports the aging process.
162.  Terms; quorum.  The appointed members serve for 3-year terms and may be 
17reappointed.  Four members constitute a quorum.
183.  Meetings.  The advisory group shall hold at least one regular meeting and no 
19more than 4 meetings each year.
204.  Duties.  The advisory group shall:
21A.  Collaborate with providers of home health care services and other services 
22relating to the social determinants of health; and
23B.  Make recommendations to the department to increase collaboration and sharing of 
24resources among service providers to improve MaineCare members' health and 
25reduce unnecessary use of health care services.
26For the purposes of this section, "social determinants of health" means a person's 
27social, economic and physical environments that affect the person's health.

3The department shall, beginning in 2020 and at least every 2 years thereafter and

72.  Minimum rate.  A review conducted pursuant to subsection 1 may not result in a 
8reimbursement rate received by a provider that is lower than the rate in effect on

103.  Rulemaking.  The department shall adopt rules to establish reimbursement rates 
11under this section that take into account the costs of providing care and services in 
12conformity with applicable state and federal laws, rules and regulations; quality and 
13safety standards; and local competitive wage markets.  Rules adopted pursuant to this 
14subsection are major substantive rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
15This section applies to all funds, including federal funds, paid by any agency of the 
16State to a provider for care covered by the waiver under subsection 1.

20recommendations regarding in-person supervision requirements under rule Chapter 101: 
21MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter II, Sections 19, 40 and 96 and rule Chapter 5, 
22Office of Elder Services Policy Manual, Section 63 for all providers and regarding 
23meeting compliance requirements.  The joint standing committee may report out a bill 
24concerning the report to the Second Regular Session of the 129th Legislature.
25SUMMARY
26This bill:
271.  Establishes the Social Determinants of Health Stakeholder Advisory Group to 
28collaborate with providers of home health care services and other services relating to the 
29social determinants of health and make recommendations to the Department of Health 
30and Human Services;
312.  Provides for reimbursement for telehealth or telemonitoring private duty nursing, 
32home health services and personal care services for an adult MaineCare member with a 
33physical disability or an adult who is elderly who is receiving MaineCare services under a 
34waiver granted by the federal Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for 
35Medicare and Medicaid Services for home-based and community-based services or state-
36funded home-based and community-based support services.  The services must include: 
37A.  Services that support a member's ability to remain in the member's home, 
38including, but not limited to, telehealth and telemonitoring services that support a 
39member's safety, mobility or medication compliance, or that support virtual home 
40visits and clinical consultation; and 
41B.  Services of a pharmacist to provide medication evaluation or consultation to a 
42member;
