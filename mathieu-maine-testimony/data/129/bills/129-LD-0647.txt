4The Welcome Center Initiative, referred to in this section as "the initiative," is

71.  Welcome centers.  The initiative operates through a welcome center created 
8within the adult education program of participating school administrative units in 
9municipalities or regions in the State that have immigrant populations or that have 
10industries that are experiencing a shortage of trained workers.  A welcome center 
11educates and provides vocational training for foreign-trained workers, including 
12foreign-trained professionals, and matches those workers with employers in the State, 
13focusing on employers in industries that are experiencing a shortage of trained workers.
142.  Coordinator.  Each welcome center under subsection 1 must employ a full-time 
15coordinator.  The coordinator shall:
16A.  Administer the center;
17B.  Collaborate and work in conjunction with the department including the office to 
18carry out the purposes of this section, including developing a program or series of 
19programs designed to serve the needs of initiative participants; and
20C.  Secure additional funding from public and private sources, including donations, 
21grants or public-private partnerships, to provide ongoing funding of the welcome 
22center.
233.  Functions of a welcome center.  The functions of a welcome center under 
24subsection 1 include:
25A.  Providing services and training to prepare initiative participants for entry into 
26gainful employment in the State within 6 to 18 months of the beginning of services or 
27training;
28B.  Providing education and career planning case management;
29C.  Providing English language acquisition instruction;
30D.  Creating partnerships with employers in the State that may employ initiative 
31participants;
32E.  Identifying employment skills, academic skills and English language skills needed 
33for available positions with employers in the State in general or with employers in 
34paragraph D;
35F.  Collaborating with state and local organizations that provide employment and 
36workforce services and support;
37G.  Referring initiative participants to legal aid services; and

24.  Rules.  The department may adopt routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
3chapter 375, subchapter 2-A to carry out the purposes of this section, including the 
4placement of a welcome center under subsection 1.
5Sec. 2.  Department of Education to establish a welcome center in the City

10Sec. 3.  Vocation-specific English language acquisition and workforce 
11training program.  A vocation-specific English language acquisition and workforce 
12training program for immigrants in the State, referred to in this section as "the program," 
13is established.
141.  Program requirements.  The Department of Education's office of adult education 
15and family literacy, referred to in this section as "the office," shall administer a 2-year 
16program.  The program must prepare immigrants for identified workforce needs of 
17employers in the State to accelerate entry into livable wage employment and enhance 
18employer retention of the immigrants by combining vocation-specific English language 
19acquisition with training in the specific vocational areas required by the employers.  
20Training classes must be conducted at a participating employer's workplace pursuant to a 
21grant process under subsection 2.
222.  Training grants.  The office shall establish a statewide competitive grant process 
23to carry out the purposes of subsection 1.  The amount of a grant under this subsection is 
24determined by the office based upon the needs of the area to be served and the availability 
25of funding.  The office shall consider grants to cover all areas of the State with emphasis 
26on areas with large immigrant populations or where there is a need for skilled workers 
27that training under this section may provide.  A proposal for a grant under this subsection 
28must include:
29A.  Intensive vocation-specific English language and vocabulary classes;
30B.  Intensive workforce training classes that include soft skills and acquaint 
31immigrants with the culture of the workplace in the State.  For purposes of this 
32paragraph, "soft skills" means those basic skills necessary to obtain and maintain 
33employment, such as interviewing and communications skills;
34C.  Interviewing immigrant participants and identifying the education level, English 
35language ability, skill set, work experience, qualifications and credentials of each 
36immigrant participant;
37D.  Collaboration with participating employers to:
38(1)  Identify the employers' specific workforce needs;
39(2)  Identify skills needed for positions required by the employers;
Page 3 - 129LR0568(01)-11(3)  Receive input from the employers for the design of the training, including 
2information on required English language proficiency, workplace culture, on-site 
3space or other vocational training elements such as tools, manuals or site tours; 
4and
5(4)  Ensure that the employers provide the time and space to offer training classes 
6on-site at the employers' workplaces;
7E.  One or more training courses to prepare the immigrant participants as potential 
8workers to enter into and retain livable wage employment within 6 to 18 months of 
9the beginning of the training; and
10F.  Collection and reporting of data including outcomes of employment and job 
11retention.
12The office shall give special consideration to proposals for grants under this 
13subsection that provide incentives for immigrants to participate in the training, such as 
14agreements with employers to pay an employee the employee's hourly rate to attend the 
15training.
163.  Criteria for applicants.  An applicant for a training grant under subsection 2 
17must have demonstrated expertise and experience in the following:
18A.  Providing English language acquisition training, including the administration of 
19appropriate academic and vocational assessments and research-based instructional 
20approaches appropriate for English language learners;
21B.  Working with employers to develop relevant employee training; and
22C.  Working with partners in the state workforce and state agencies with expertise in 
23serving and preparing immigrants for employment through a case management 
24approach.
254.  Rules.  The office shall adopt routine technical rules as defined in the Maine 
26Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A to carry out the purposes of this 
27section.
285.  Report.  The office shall prepare a report on the training that includes:
29A.  The number and amount of grants awarded under this section;
30B.  The training and services provided by the grant recipients;
31C.  Aggregate demographic information about immigrant participants in the training, 
32participating employers, employment opportunities, employment placements and 
33retention by employers of immigrant participants placed under this section; and
34D.  An evaluation of programs and services most effective in carrying out the 
35purposes of this section.
36By November 4, 2021, the office shall submit the report along with any recommended 
37legislation to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
38education and cultural affairs.  The joint standing committee may report out a bill based 
39upon the report to the 130th Legislature.
Page 4 - 129LR0568(01)-11Sec. 4.  Local community planning support program.  The Department of 
2Education's office of adult education and family literacy, referred to in this section as "the 
3office," shall establish a local community planning support program, referred to in this

51.  Local community planning support program.  The office shall implement a 2-
6year program to assist communities within the State to manage new immigrant 
7populations that have settled in the communities or to attract immigrant populations to 
8address depopulation or workforce shortages in the communities.
92.  Program elements.  The office shall establish by rule the elements of the 
10program.  The program must provide a planning process for communities to provide 
11English language acquisition and training in vocational skills, identify employers or areas 
12that would benefit from immigrant residency or employment and cultivate community 
13support to integrate immigrants into the communities and local workforce.
143.  Funding.  The office shall consider applications from governmental entities 
15located in or containing communities that are affected by or wish to attract immigrant 
16populations and provide grants to those communities in a number and amount determined 
17by available funding.  An application for funding under this subsection may be made by 
18one governmental entity or a combination of 2 or more governmental entities and may 
19include a partnership by the governmental entity with a private or nonprofit organization.  
20For purposes of this subsection, "governmental entity" includes a county, municipality or 
21school administrative unit.
224.  Rules.  The office shall adopt routine technical rules as defined in the Maine 
23Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A to carry out the purposes of this 
24section.
255.  Report.  The office shall prepare a report on the program that includes every 
26governmental entity receiving a grant, the amount of the grant and an evaluation of the 
27outcomes and effectiveness of the grant on the target communities. By December 5, 2020, 
28the department shall submit the report along with any recommended legislation to the 
29joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over education and 
30cultural affairs.  The joint standing committee may report out a bill based upon the report 
31to the 130th Legislature.
32Sec. 5.  Department of Education to provide grants for English language 
33acquisition instruction for adult education programs.  The Department of 
34Education's office of adult education and family literacy shall develop and implement a 
35grant process to provide funding on a competitive basis to adult education programs in 
36communities experiencing an increase in immigrant populations or demand to reduce the 
37waiting list for English language acquisition classes and to increase the level of English 
38language acquisition instruction offered as needed by the communities applying for the 
39grants.  The department shall award grants under this section to eligible communities in a 
40number and amount determined by available funding.  The department shall adopt routine 
41technical rules as defined by the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 
422-A to carry out the purposes of this section.

30SUMMARY
31This bill creates various programs to provide education, services and training for the 
32State's workforce immigrant populations in the following ways:
331.  It establishes the Welcome Center Initiative to operate welcome centers in adult 
34education programs to provide education, services and training for foreign-trained 
35workers in municipalities or regions of the State that have immigrant populations or that 
36have industries that are experiencing a shortage of trained workers, patterned after the 
37New Mainers Resource Center operated by the City of Portland adult education program 
38through a pilot program created by the 126th Legislature.  The bill provides funds for
