3CHAPTER 318-A
4EXPIRED MARINE FLARES
5ยง2491.  Definitions
6As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 
7have the following meanings.
81.  Commissioner.  "Commissioner" means the Commissioner of Public Safety or the 
9commissioner's designee.
102.  Expired.  "Expired" means, with regard to a marine flare, beyond the 
11manufacturer-designated period of recommended use.
123.  Fire inspector.  "Fire inspector" means a person designated by the commissioner 
13to collect expired marine flares.
144.  Marine flare.  "Marine flare" means a device designed to produce a bright flame 
15for use as a signal or marker on the inland or coastal waters of the State or at sea.
16ยง2492.  Collection program
17The commissioner shall establish and oversee a program for the convenient and safe 
18collection of expired marine flares pursuant to this section.
191.  Site collection.  The commissioner shall coordinate the following site collection 
20operations.
21A.  The commissioner shall, in coordination with a statewide association of fire 
22chiefs, identify fire departments in the State that have the capacity and willingness to 
23accept expired marine flares from members of the public.  A participating fire 
24department may choose to designate the geographic area from which the fire 
25department will accept expired marine flares.  A participating fire department and the 
26commissioner shall coordinate for timely pickup of the collected expired marine 
27flares.
28B.  The commissioner shall coordinate with sponsors of annual community events 
29that choose to include expired marine flare collection as part of their events.
30C.  The commissioner shall coordinate with marinas, municipalities and groups that 
31organize events at which the public is invited to drop off expired marine flares for 
32timely collection.
332.  Short-term storage.  The commissioner shall provide guidance for short-term 
34storage to persons collecting expired marine flares under subsection 1 if the 
35commissioner determines that safety equipment or special procedures are required.
Page 2 - 129LR0006(01)-113.  Direct collection.  The commissioner shall designate fire inspectors to collect 
2expired marine flares in a timely way from individuals who fish commercially and 
3recreational boaters in the State who contact the Department of Public Safety to notify the 
4department of expired marine flares in need of disposal.
5ยง2493.  Disposal program
6The commissioner shall establish a program for the safe, nonpolluting disposal of all 
7types of expired marine flares pursuant to this section.
81.  Short-term storage.  Expired marine flares collected pursuant to section 2492 
9that require short-term storage must be stored according to standards established by the 
10Department of Public Safety.  
112.  Sorting.  Expired marine flares collected pursuant to section 2492 must be sorted 
12by type for proper incineration.
133.  Incineration.  Expired marine flares sorted pursuant to subsection 2 must be 
14incinerated at temperatures that are safe within Department of Environmental Protection 
15standards for preventing air pollution.
164.  Recycling.  To the extent practicable, material from incinerated expired marine 
17flares must be recycled.
18ยง2494.  Education program
19The commissioner shall establish an education program for the public and state 
20agencies regarding expired marine flares pursuant to this section.
211.  Education campaign.  The Department of Public Safety shall engage in an 
22education campaign to inform the public and personnel of the Department of Public 
23Safety, the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, the Department of 
24Inland Fisheries and Wildlife, the Department of Environmental Protection and the 
25Department of Marine Resources and other potentially interested entities about the 
26programs of collection and disposal of expired marine flares under this chapter, the 
27dangers expired marine flares pose to public health and the dangers expired marine flares 
28that are improperly disposed of pose to the environment and to safety of persons and 
29property.
302.  Department websites.  The Department of Public Safety shall maintain a publicly 
31accessible website supporting the education campaign under subsection 1 and serving as 
32an information resource regarding expired marine flares and their proper disposal 
33pursuant to this chapter.  The Department of Public Safety, the Department of 
34Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife, 
35the Department of Environmental Protection and the Department of Marine Resources 
36shall coordinate to update their publications and publicly accessible websites that are 
37suited for the purpose with information regarding the collection and disposal of expired 
38marine flares pursuant to this chapter.
Page 3 - 129LR0006(01)-113.  Printed materials.  Materials published by a state agency and intended for those 
2who fish commercially or for recreational boaters must, in printings that occur on or after 
3the effective date of this subsection, include information about environmental and safety 
4concerns related to improper disposal and storage of expired marine flares.  The programs 
5of collection and disposal of expired marine flares established pursuant to this chapter 
6must be described in these print materials, including contact information for the 
7Department of Public Safety's expired marine flare collection and disposal program.
84.  State agency notification.  The commissioner shall notify other state agencies or 
9entities as the commissioner determines appropriate about the expired marine flare 
10collection and disposal program under this chapter.
11SUMMARY
12This bill establishes within the Department of Public Safety programs for the 
13collection and disposal of expired marine flares and for education of the public and state 
14agency personnel regarding expired marine flares.
