161.  Develop and maintain a long-range strategic plan for the State's economy.  
17By December 31, 2020, the council shall develop a long-range strategic economic 
18improvement plan for a sustainable state economy in accordance with this subsection.  
19The plan must be sufficiently broad in scope to address relevant state, national and 
20international economic, financial, demographic, natural resources, educational, science 
21and technology and infrastructure factors.  The council shall update the plan by December 
2231, 2022 and every 4 years thereafter.  Plan updates may include, but are not limited to, 
23adjustments to objectives, action items, timelines or benchmarks in response to changing 
24economic conditions and market trends and taking into account current progress on 
25planned efforts.  The council shall complete a full review and revision of the plan by 
26December 31, 2024 and every 4 years thereafter.  Plan revisions may include, but are not 
27limited to, reconsideration and revision of the State's overall long-range economic 
28improvement strategy and related goals.
29A.  In developing and revising the plan, the council shall:
30(1)  Employ a continuous process with a 5-year to 10-year planning horizon;
31(2)  Include consideration of:
32(a)  The types of industries and jobs with significant growth potential in the 
33State;
34(b)  The State's evolving industrial base;
35(c)  The dynamic regional, national and international markets and the State's 
36comparative, competitive and absolute advantages, including, but not limited 
37to, the impact of tax policy, energy costs and regulation on competitiveness;
Page 2 - 129LR0632(01)-11(d)  Other relevant studies and evaluations in the private and public sectors 
2regarding the long-term economic growth of the State;
3(e)  The quantity, quality and demographic composition of the State's 
4workforce;
5(f)  Discrepancies in challenges and opportunities among different regions in 
6the State;
7(g)  Opportunities and challenges for small businesses; and
8(h)  Optimization of the return on investment in the State;
9(3)  Use a public and private partnership approach that is objective and 
10nonpartisan, with appointment of working groups and advisory committees as 
11necessary to represent stakeholders; and
12(4)  Use a transparent public process that allows for public input as appropriate.
13B.  The plan must consist of:
14(1)  Goals and objectives for the long-term economic improvement of the State 
15that support economic opportunity and a high quality of life for all people in the 
16State;
17(2)  Specific, quantifiable and measurable benchmarks against which progress 
18toward accomplishing the goals and objectives of the plan can be measured;
19(3)  Strategies and actions to accomplish the benchmarks based upon the best 
20practices in this State, other states and other countries; and
21(4)  Recommendations with respect to allocation of resources.
222.  Monitor progress.  The council shall regularly assess progress toward 
23achievement of the strategic plan goals, objectives and benchmarks and produce a written 
24progress report by December 31, 2022 and every 2 years thereafter.

40updated plans or revised plans submitted under paragraph A.

4SUMMARY
5This bill provides additional clarity, requirements and resources for the Maine 
6Economic Growth Council's efforts to fulfill its current statutory mandate to develop, 
7monitor and maintain a long-range strategic economic improvement plan for the State.  It 
8also includes provisions to support the State's achievement of the goals and objectives in 
9that plan by establishing requirements for the Governor, Legislature and agencies with 
10relevant programs and activities to consider the long-range strategic economic 
11improvement plan and provide information to the Maine Economic Growth Council at the 
12request of the council.
