2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, the COVID-19 pandemic has exacerbated homelessness for the most 
4 frequent users of high-cost services, such as psychiatric hospitals, emergency shelters, 
5 emergency rooms, police, jails and prisons; and
6Whereas, this legislation establishes the Frequent Users System Engagement 
7 Collaborative, which will develop a plan to provide stable housing and community services 
8 to 200 persons who are homeless or at risk of homelessness and who are frequent users of 
9 high-cost services; and
10Whereas, establishing this collaborative as soon as possible will help to put the stable 
11 housing and community services plan in place earlier; and
12Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
13 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
14 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
15 therefore, be it
16Sec. 1.  Frequent Users System Engagement Collaborative established.  
17Resolved:  That the Frequent Users System Engagement Collaborative, referred to in this 
18 resolve as "the collaborative," is established.
19Sec. 2.  Membership.  Resolved:  That the collaborative consists of the director of 
20 the Maine State Housing Authority or the director's designee, who shall serve as chair, and 
21 other members appointed by the director as follows:
22 1.  The Commissioner of Health and Human Services or the commissioner's designee;
23 2.  The Commissioner of Corrections or the commissioner's designee;
24 3.  One member representing the Statewide Homeless Council;
25 4.  One member representing the Maine Sheriffs' Association;
26 5.  One member representing providers of emergency health services; and
27 6.  Any other members with relevant expertise as determined by the director.
28Sec. 3.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the collaborative shall develop a plan to provide 
29 stable housing and community services to 200 persons who are homeless or at risk of 
30 homelessness who are the most frequent consumers of high-cost services, such as 
31 psychiatric hospitals, emergency shelters, emergency rooms, police, jails and prisons.
32Sec. 4.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than January 1, 2022, the collaborative 
33 shall submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services.  The 
34 report must include the collaborative's plan to provide stable housing and community 
35 services developed pursuant to section 3, including the cost savings that could be achieved 
36 by reductions in the use of high-cost services and any recommendations developed by the 
37 collaborative on providing stable housing and community services to persons consuming 
38 high-cost services.  The Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services may 
39 report out a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 130th Legislature related to the report.

3SUMMARY
4 This resolve establishes the Frequent Users System Engagement Collaborative in order 
5 to develop a plan to provide stable housing and community services to 200 persons who 
6 are homeless or at risk of homelessness who are the most frequent consumers of high-cost 
7 services, such as psychiatric hospitals, emergency shelters, emergency rooms, police, jails 
8 and prisons.  The collaborative must submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on 
9 Health and Human Services no later than January 1, 2022 on its plan and recommendations.  
10 The Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services is authorized to report out a 
11 bill to the Second Regular Session of the 130th Legislature related to the report.
