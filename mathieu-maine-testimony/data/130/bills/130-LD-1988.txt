11Sec. 1.  32 MRSA §86-A is enacted to read:
12§86-A.  Ambulance services are essential services
13 For purposes of eligibility for federal funding, emergency medical services provided 
14 by an ambulance service are essential services and as essential as services provided by a 
15 fire department or law enforcement agency.

19 Emergency Medical Services in the State, referred to in this section as "the commission," 
20 is established.

21Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation

23SUMMARY
24 This bill provides that for purposes of eligibility for federal funding, emergency 
25 medical services provided by an ambulance service are essential services. It also establishes 
26 the Blue Ribbon Commission To Study Emergency Medical Services in the State. The 
27 commission must examine and make recommendations on the support of and delivery of 
28 emergency medical services in the State and may look at all aspects of emergency medical 
29 services, including but not limited to workforce development, training, compensation, 
30 retention, costs, reimbursement rates, organization and local and state support. No later 
31 than December 7, 2022, the commission must submit a report that includes its findings and 
32 recommendations, including suggested legislation, to the joint standing committee of the 
33 Legislature having jurisdiction over public safety matters.24
