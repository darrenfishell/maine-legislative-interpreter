SUMMARY 
This amendment replaces the resolve. The amendment reestablishes the Commission 
To Develop a Paid Family and Medical Leave Benefits Program, which was established in 
Resolve 2021, chapter 122, and requires the commission to submit a report for presentation 
to the First Regular Session of the 131st Legislature no later than November 2, 2022. The 
amendment retains the provision in the resolve that provides that funds appropriated or 
allocated pursuant to Resolve 2021, chapter 122 do not lapse but must be carried forward 
into the next fiscal year to be used for the purpose for which the funds were provided. The 
amendment requires the appointing authorities, to the greatest extent practicable, to 
reappoint the persons they appointed to the former commission.

Legislative Cost/Study 
The additional costs to extend the C01mnission are projected to be $1,000 in fiscal year 2021-22 and $2,250 in fiscal 
year 2022-23. Resolve 2021, Chapter 122 provided $200,000 to the C01mnission to contract with an outside entity' 
conduct and complete an actuarial study. Section 2 of this resolve authorizes balances provided in Resolve 2021, 
Chapter 122 to be carried forward into fiscal year 2022-23 to be used for the same purpose. The estimated fiscal year 
2021-22 balance related to this study is expected to be $199,685 less amounts paid to the outside entity through June 
30, 2022.

Additional costs to the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, the Governor's Office, the Department 
of Labor and the Department of Health and Human Services to provide staffing assistance to the co1mnission is 
expected to be minor and can be absorbed within existing budgeted resources.
