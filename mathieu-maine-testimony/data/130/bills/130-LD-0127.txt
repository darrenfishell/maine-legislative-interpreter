12Sec. 1.  Commissioner of Education to establish pilot program to provide 
13professional development grants for computer science instruction. Resolved:  
14 That the Commissioner of Education shall establish a pilot program to award grants for the 
15 provision of professional development in computer science instruction for a period of 2 
16 years. The Commissioner of Education may accept donations from outside sources and 
17 state and federal funding to accomplish the purposes of this resolve.
18Sec. 2.  Application. Resolved:  That a local education agency may submit an 
19 application for grant funds under the pilot program pursuant to section 1. An application 
20 must address, but is not limited to, how the agency will:
21 1.  Engage new and existing teachers with minimal or no background in computer 
22 science instruction in professional development opportunities in computer science 
23 instruction;
24 2.  Use research-based or evidence-based best practices for high-quality professional 
25 development;
26 3.  Engage and support historically underrepresented student populations in computer 
27 science;
28 4.  Provide teachers with hands-on, inquiry-based practices; and
29 5.  Engage, involve or partner with outside organizations, including but not limited to 
30 the business community, nonprofit organizations and private entities.
31Sec. 3.  Use of funds. Resolved:  That a local education agency that receives a grant 
32 award during the pilot program under section 1 must use the funds only for high-quality, 
33 teacher-developed professional development for prekindergarten to grade 12 computer 
34 science pedagogy and content.
35Sec. 4.  Annual reports. Resolved:  That each local education agency that receives 
36 a grant under the pilot program under section 1 shall report annually for the duration of the 
37 grant to the Department of Education.
38Sec. 5.  Fund established. Resolved:  That the Professional Development Grant 
39 Pilot Program Fund is established as a nonlapsing fund under the Department of Education 
40 for the purpose of receiving funds from state, federal and other sources, including donations 
41 from private citizens, corporations and entities for the purposes of this resolve. The 
Page 2 - 130LR0557(01)42 department shall distribute any available funds in the fund to support the purposes 
43 described in this resolve. Upon distribution of all the funds, the fund is dissolved.
3Sec. 6.  Report to Legislature. Resolved:  That, no later than January 1, 2023, the 
4 Commissioner of Education shall submit a report regarding the pilot program pursuant to 
5 this resolve and make recommendations, including any suggested legislation, to the joint 
6 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over education and cultural 
7 affairs. The joint standing committee may report out legislation on the subject matter of the 
8 report to the First Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
9Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
10 takes effect when approved.
11SUMMARY
12 This resolve establishes a 2-year pilot program to provide grants for professional 
13 development in computer science instruction. 1
