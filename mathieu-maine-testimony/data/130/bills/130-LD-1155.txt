3ยง610.  Election transparency
41.  Transparency of election procedures.  The Secretary of State shall produce and 
5 publicly disseminate a guide to election procedures and revise the guide annually to reflect 
6 current law, rules and procedures regarding elections.  The guide must describe:
7 A.  Rules regarding polling places and the conduct of elections;
8 B.  Devices and software used to capture, interpret or tally votes and the capabilities 
9 and deployment of the devices and software;
10 C.  Duties of election officials on, before and after election day;
11 D.  Opportunities for citizens to monitor election procedures; and
12 E.  Other election laws, rules or procedures that the Secretary of State determines 
13 appropriate for the public interest.
142.  Election complaints.  The Secretary of State shall develop an election complaint 
15 process that any election official or member of the public can use to report excessive wait 
16 times, noncompliance with election procedures, obstacles or interference with voting or 
17 other concerns about the election process.  Instructions for filing a complaint under this 
18 subsection must be included in the guide to election procedures under subsection 1 and 
19 posted at all polling places.  The Secretary of State shall keep a record of the complaints 
20 received under this subsection and any actions taken in response to the complaints.
213.  Election report.  By December 15th in a year following a general election year, the 
22 Secretary of State shall submit a report to the Legislature that includes for all elections 
23 since the prior general election:
24 A.  A summary of the election complaints received under subsection 2;
25 B.  The number of voters removed from the central voter registration system since the 
26 previous report and the reason for their removal;
27 C.  A summary of any recounts conducted, including a description of any discrepancies 
28 in vote totals and explanations for discrepancies when available; and
29 D.  Recommendations for the remedy of any problems identified in the election process 
30 at the state or local level.
314.  Rules.  The Secretary of State may adopt routine technical rules as defined in Title 
32 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A to carry out the purposes of this section.

38Sec. 4.  Secretary of State to conduct pilot post-election audit.  The Secretary 
39 of State shall design and conduct a pilot post-election audit based on statistical principles 
40 designed to limit the risk that certified election returns may produce an incorrect outcome.  
Page 2 - 130LR1282(01)41 The pilot audit must be conducted following the general election scheduled for November 
42 2022.  The planning process for the pilot audit must include a study of best practices in 
43 post-election ballot audits, recounts and the intersection of audits with recounts and must 
44 include a review of security protocols related to hardware, software and paper ballots.  In 
45 conducting the pilot audit, the Secretary of State may consult with state and municipal 
46 election officials, election security advocates and other experts in the field of election audits 
47 and recounts.  By February 1, 2023, the Secretary of State shall submit a report on the pilot 
48 audit with any recommended legislation to the joint standing committee of the Legislature 
49 having jurisdiction over veterans and legal affairs.  The joint standing committee may 
50 report out a bill based upon the report to the First Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
11SUMMARY
12 This bill requires the Secretary of State to:
13 1.  Produce and publicly disseminate a guide to election procedures;
14 2.  Develop an election complaint process;
15 3.  Report to the Legislature on certain election issues after a general election; and
16 4.  Conduct a pilot post-election audit.1
