19SUMMARY
20 This bill amends the motor vehicle laws in the following ways.
21 1.  It amends various vehicle definitions.
22 2.  It adds a provision for the continued use by government entities of off-road vehicles.
23 3.  It allows for rulemaking for municipal agents similar to other rule-making authority.
24 4.  It moves statutory language from the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 29-A, section 
25 462 to the applicable dealer sections of law and the temporary registration plate section of 
26 law.
27 5.  It adds language to limit the number of active specialty license plates to no more 
28 than 15.
29 6.  It repeals the authority of the Secretary of State to issue placards for deaf, hard-of-
30 hearing and late-deafened persons.
31 7.  It changes the definition of "recycler" for consistency.
32 8.  It amends the law governing licenses issued to auction locations.
33 9.  It requires lienholders to register for a unique identifier to enable the bureau to 
34 maintain accurate records on lienholders.
35 10.  It amends statute to comply with the National Highway Traffic Safety 
36 Administration's final rule regarding odometer disclosures under 49 Code of Federal 
37 Regulation 580.17, the Federal Truth in Mileage Act.  Beginning January 1, 2021, vehicles 
38 with model year 2011 must have the odometer captured.  Vehicles model year 2010 and 
39 earlier remain exempt under the new rule.
40 11.  It amends dealer license fees.20
Page 14 - 130LR0471(01)1 12.  It amends the law regarding the number of plates issued to dealers.
2 13.  It requires dealers to comply with Title 10, chapter 217 as a condition of their 
3 license.
4 14.  It moves statutory references regarding the issuance of temporary registration 
5 plates and certificates.
6 15.  It provides clarity on which dealers may use service vehicle plates.
7 16.  It closes loopholes that allow individuals or businesses to engage in unlawful 
8 activities without being licensed as recyclers and more clearly defines the activity of a 
9 licensed recycler.
10 17.  It amends the law regarding mobile crushers to provide a penalty for violations.
11 18.  It clarifies that recycling of vehicles includes the actions of acquiring and selling 
12 vehicles or parts.
13 19.  It changes the record-keeping requirements of recyclers.
14 20.  It tightens the requirements for special restricted licenses by requiring applicants 
15 who are 15 years of age to hold a permit for 6 months before applying for a special restricted 
16 license.
17 21.  It allows the course completion certificate issued by motorcycle rider education 
18 schools to act as a 60-day temporary permit.
19 22.  It clarifies that the completion certificate for driver education instructors acts as a 
20 60-day temporary learner's permit.
21 23.  It adds requirements regarding background checks for motorcycle instructor 
22 licenses.
23 24.  It clarifies the definition of a Class A driver education school.
24 25.  It provides flexibility in what the Secretary of State may consider when approving 
25 a driver improvement program.
26 26.  It amends the law regarding when a person is eligible to install an ignition interlock 
27 device during the term of the person's suspension and when that person can remove the 
28 device without penalty.
