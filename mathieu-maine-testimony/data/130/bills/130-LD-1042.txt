65.  Building maintenance or repair.  Prior to any maintenance or repair of a state-
7 owned building by a person employed by a state agency, the bureau shall review the 
8 building's records in the database established and maintained pursuant to subsection 3 or

166.  Monitoring, abatement and remediation.  To promote a safe and healthy 
17 environment in state-owned buildings, the bureau's division of safety and environmental 
18 services shall:
19 A.  Provide statewide monitoring of state-owned buildings to continuously identify the 
20 presence of health hazards in state-owned buildings, including, but not limited to, 
21 asbestos, lead, black mold and other substances that may be harmful to human health;
22 B.  Conduct routine building inventories and testing as appropriate to discover and 
23 assess the presence of health hazards in state-owned buildings, including, but not 
24 limited to, asbestos, lead, black mold and other substances that may be harmful to 
25 human health;
26 C.  Identify any abatement, remediation, containment and maintenance necessary to 
27 address and prevent potential health hazards in state-owned buildings, including, but 
28 not limited to, asbestos, lead, black mold and other substances that may be harmful to 
29 human health; and
30 D.  Work with state departments and state agencies to develop safety protocols and 
31 train state employees on safety protocols.

3SUMMARY
4 This bill requires the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Bureau of 
5 General Services to inventory all state-owned buildings where state employees perform job 
6 duties to determine the presence of asbestos, lead, black mold and other substances that 
7 may be harmful to human health.  It requires property owners of buildings leased to the 
8 State to conduct a similar inventory and report the results to the bureau.  The bill requires 
9 the bureau to establish and maintain a database containing the results of the inventories and 
10 make the database readily accessible electronically to employee collective bargaining 
11 agents, employees working in state office buildings with asbestos, lead, black mold or other 
12 substances that may be harmful to human health, individuals who perform maintenance, 
13 repair or custodial services, firefighters, the Department of Labor, the Department of Health 
14 and Human Services, Maine Center for Disease Control and Prevention and members of 
15 the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over state and local 
16 government matters.  It requires the database to be consulted before maintenance of or 
17 repair to a state-owned building by a state employee and the provision of protective gear to 
18 the employee if appropriate.  The department may adopt rules to implement these 
19 requirements and is required to submit a biennial report to the joint standing committee 
20 regarding the conditions of the buildings.1
