39 in a primitive camping area in the State shall provide for every 12 trip participants at least 
40 one educational trip leader permitted pursuant to this section who is associated with the 
41 educational institution or a person who holds a guide license issued under this chapter.  For 
Page 3 - 130LR1184(01)42 purposes of this subsection, "educational institution" means a public school or private 
43 school, as those terms are defined in Title 20-A, section 1, or a postsecondary educational 
44 institution.
42.  Prohibition.  A person who does not hold a guide license issued under this chapter 
5 may not conduct overnight camping under subsection 1 without an educational trip leader 
6 permit issued under this section.  Each day a person violates this subsection, that person 
7 commits a Class E crime for which a minimum fine of $50 and an amount equal to twice 
8 the applicable license fee must be imposed.
93.  Application.  A person seeking an educational trip leader permit must submit an 
10 application on forms provided by the commissioner and pay the application fee.
114.  Qualifications.  To qualify for an educational trip leader permit, an applicant must:
12 A.  Be at least 18 years of age; 
13 B.  Show successful completion of an approved educational trip leader course created 
14 pursuant to a curriculum adopted pursuant to subsection 6 or complete an application 
15 provided by the commissioner outlining in detail the applicant's experience and training 
16 that qualifies that person to become an educational trip leader; and
17 C.  Meet any other requirements established by the commissioner.  The commissioner, 
18 in establishing requirements pursuant to this paragraph, shall take into consideration 
19 the advice of the Advisory Committee for Educational Trip Leader Permits pursuant to 
20 section 12864, subsection 2.
215.  Special waiver.  The commissioner, who shall take into consideration the 
22 recommendations of the Advisory Committee for Educational Trip Leader Permits under 
23 section 12864, subsection 2, may grant a waiver of the course requirement under subsection 
24 4, paragraph B on the basis of the applicant's experience after payment of the application 
25 fee under subsection 7.
266.  Curriculum.  The commissioner, who shall take into consideration the advice of 
27 the Advisory Committee for Educational Trip Leader Permits pursuant to section 12864, 
28 subsection 2, shall adopt an educational trip leader course curriculum and examinations.

30 following areas:
31 A.  Wilderness first aid;
32 B.  Water safety, including lifesaving techniques, as appropriate, and cardiopulmonary

34 C.  Group management and facilitation;
35 D.  Risk management;
36 E.  Wilderness traveling and camping;
37 F.  Camping, including practice in related activities; and
38 G.  The State's outdoor industry.

38SUMMARY
39 This bill creates a pathway for public and private elementary and secondary schools 
40 and postsecondary educational institutions to safely conduct overnight camping trips to 
41 primitive camping areas in the State for students and adults associated with those 39
40
41
Page 6 - 130LR1184(01)42 institutions by establishing an educational trip leader permit in the laws governing guides 
43 and youth camp trip leaders, using the youth camp trip leader permit as a model.  Schools 
44 currently conducting these trips as part of their programming have until October 1, 2022 to 
45 comply with the new permit process.  It requires camping trips to be led by persons who 
46 have obtained educational trip leader permits.  It also establishes the Advisory Committee 
47 for Educational Trip Leader Permits to provide advice to the Commissioner of Inland 
48 Fisheries and Wildlife regarding the permit and to regulate educational trip leader 
49 instructors.1
