112.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
12 following terms have the following meanings.
13 A.  "Commercial short-term rental purveyor" means a person who offers a short-term 
14 rental in a building owned by the person:
15 (1) Containing more than 2 short-term rental units owned or offered by the person 
16 and in which the person does not reside; or
17 (2) Containing more than 3 short-term rental units owned or offered by the person 
18 and in which the person may reside.
19 B.  "Noncommercial short-term rental purveyor" means a person who offers a short-
20 term rental that does not meet the criteria of paragraph A.
21 C.  "Owner of a short-term rental unit" means a person:
22 (1) Who owns or possesses a short-term rental unit; or
23 (2) Who owns more than 10% of shares or any other type of membership or 
24 ownership interest in a business entity that owns or possesses a short-term rental 
25 unit.
26 D.  "Short-term rental unit" means living quarters offered for rental through a transient 
27 rental platform as defined by Title 36, section 1752, subsection 20-C.
28Sec. 2.  Notice to municipalities.  Upon the effective date of this Act, the Maine 
29 State Housing Authority shall send electronically to all municipalities the provisions of this 
30 Act.
31SUMMARY
32 This bill defines terms to be used by municipalities when enacting ordinances regarding 
33 or regulating short-term rentals of living quarters offered through a transient rental 
34 platform, including definitions for "commercial short-term rental purveyor," 
35 "noncommercial short-term rental purveyor," "owner of a short-term rental unit" and 
36 "short-term rental unit."  This bill requires these definitions to be made publicly available 
37 on the State's website and on the Maine State Housing Authority's website and to be sent 
38 electronically to all municipalities in the State.32
