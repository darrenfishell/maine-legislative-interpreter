13 'Sec. 1. Fund established. The Atlantic Large Whale Take Reduction Plan Impact 
14 Fund, referred to in this Act as "the fund," is established as a nonlapsing fund within the 
15 Department of Marine Resources to mitigate negative financial impacts experienced by 
16 individuals and businesses involved in the State's fixed gear fishing industry resulting from 
17 the amendment of 50 Code of Federal Regulations, Sections 229.2, 229.32 and 697.21 
18 published in 86 Federal Register, 52011 to 52024 (2021). 
19 Sec. 2. Report. The Department of Marine Resources shall report on the status of 
20 the fund by January 15, 2023 to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
21 jurisdiction over marine resources matters. The report must include the amount of funds 
22 distributed to each recipient, the number of recipients and the results achieved or negative 
23 impact mitigated. The joint standing committee may report out a bill based on the report to 
24 the 131st Legislature in 2023. 
25 Sec. 3. Transfer. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, on or before 
26 June 30, 2022, the State Controller shall transfer $30,000,000 from the unappropriated 
27 surplus of the General Fund to the Department of Marine Resources, Atlantic Large Whale 
28 Take Reduction Plan Impact Fund program, Other Special Revenue Funds account. 
29 Sec. 4. Eligibility criteria. To be eligible to receive money from the fund, an 
30 individual must: 
31 1. Be a resident of the State; 
32 2. Have held a Class I, Class II or Class III lobster and crab fishing license pursuant to 
33 the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 12, section 6421 in each of the past 3 calendar years; and 
34 3. Have purchased at least 400 lobster trap tags during 2 of the past 3 calendar years.

Sec. 5. Fund distribution. The Department of Marine Resources shall distribute 
money from the fund to eligible individuals as follows: 
I. An eligible individual with an annual landed value of lobster from $10,000 to 
$25,000 during 2 of the past 3 calendar years is entitled to receive the base amount as 
determined by the department; 
2. An eligible individual with an annual landed value of lobster exceeding $25,000 
during 2 of the past 3 calendar years is entitled to receive 2 times the base amount as 
determined by the department; and 
3. An eligible individual with an annual landed value of lobster exceeding $25,000 
during 2 of the past 3 calendar years who holds both a state and federal lobster permit at 
the time of distribution is eligible to receive 3 times the base amount as determined by the 
department. 
An eligible individual is entitled to only one distribution. 
Sec. 6. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and 
allocations are made. 
MARINE RESOURCES, DEPARTMENT OF 
Atlantic Large Whale Take Reduction Plan Impact Fund N944 
Initiative: Provides one-time allocations for grants to individuals and businesses involved 
in the State's fixed gear fishing industry to mitigate negative financial impacts experienced 
as a result of the United States Department of Commerce, National Oceanic and 
Atmospheric Administration regulations resulting from the Atlantic Large Whale Take 
Reduction Plan. No more than $210,000 of the funds may be used by the Department of 
Marine Resources for administrative costs.

31 SUMMARY 
32 This amendment, which replaces the bill, moves the Atlantic Large Whale Take 
33 Reduction Plan Impact Fund into unallocated law to reflect the one-time allocation of 
34 money from the fund and adds eligibility criteria for receipt of funds and instructions on 
35 distribution of funds by the Department of Marine Resources. It also provides a minimum 
36 amount of funding to cover the department's administrative costs.

This bill establishes the Atlantic Large Whale Take Reduction Plan Impact Fund within the Department of Marine 
Resources (DMR) to mitigate negative financial impacts experienced by individuals and businesses involved in the 
State's fixed-gear fishing industry. The bill transfers $30,000,000 in fiscal year 2021-22 from the unappropriated 
surplus of the General Fund to the fund. The bill includes accompanying allocations of $29,999,500 in fiscal year 
2021-22 and $500 in fiscal year 2022-23 to allow disbursement of the funds to individuals and businesses who meet 
the eligibility criteria.
