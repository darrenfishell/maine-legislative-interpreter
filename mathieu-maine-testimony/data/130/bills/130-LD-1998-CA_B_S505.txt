18 §220-A. Farmers Drought Relief Grant Program 
19 1. Grant program established. The Farmers Drought Relief Grant Program, referred 
20 to in this section as "the program," is established in the department to assist farmers in the 
21 State to overcome the adverse effects of drought conditions by providing grants in 
22 accordance with this section. 
23 2. Eligibility. A farmer in the State may apply for a grant under the program if the 
24 farmer needs to establish a source for irrigation water or needs funding to adopt healthy 
25 soils best practices as defined in Title 12, section 351, subsection 5 to alleviate the risk of 
26 crop losses due to current or future threat of drought. The source for irrigation water must 
27 be sustainable, environmentally sound and affordable. 
28 3. Farmers Drought Relief Grant Program Fund. The Farmers Drought Relief 
29 Grant Program Fund is established as a nonlapsing fund to provide funding to achieve the 
30 purposes of the program. The fund consists of any funds received from private and public 
31 sources. The fund must be held separate and apart from all other money, funds and

ways to streamline permitting new sources of water for farmers. The Department

6 SUMMARY 
7 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, establishes the 
8 Farmers Drought Relief Grant Program to assist farmers in the State to overcome the 
9 adverse effects of drought conditions by providing grants, rather than a disaster relief grant 
10 program for potato farmers as in the bill. The amendment specifies that a farmer may apply 
11 for a grant if the farmer needs to establish a source for irrigation water or adopt healthy 
12 soils best practices to alleviate the risk of crop losses due to current or future threat of 
13 drought. 
14 The amendment also directs the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, 
15 Bureau of Agriculture, Food and Rural Resources to work with the Department of 
16 Environmental Protection and the Maine Land Use Planning Commission and other 
17 appropriate agencies to examine the permitting process for farmers seeking to secure 
18 permits for new sources of water for irrigation and to develop recommendations, including 
19 suggested legislation, to streamline the permitting process. The department is required to 
20 submit a report with findings and recommendations to the joint standing committee of the 
21 Legislature having jurisdiction over agriculture, conservation and forestry matters no later 
22 than January 1, 2023. The joint standing committee is authorized to submit a bill to the 
23 First Regular Session of the 131st Legislature relating to the subject matter of the report.

This bill creates the Farmers Drought Relief Grant Program and the Farmers Drought Relief Grant Program Fund 
(FDRGP) within the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to provide grants to farmers impacted by 
the adverse effects of drought conditions or to farmers seeking to establish sources of irrigation water to alleviate 
drought conditions. The bill also directs ACF to coordinate with the Land Use Planning Commission and the 
Department of Environmental Protection on strategies to streamline the irrigation permitting process and report these 
findings by January 1, 2023. The bill includes ongoing allocations of $500 to establish the FDRGP fund and allow for 
the acceptance of other funding from public and private sources. ACF has indicated that the scope and timeline of the 
reporting requirements regarding the irrigation permitting process will require additional contracted resources to 
complete. The bill includes a one-time appropriation of $75,000 in fiscal year 2022-23 for contracted services.
