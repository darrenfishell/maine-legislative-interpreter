2Sec. 1.  22 MRSA §51 is enacted to read:
3§51.  Exemption to written informed consent requirement for mental health services 
4and substance use disorder treatment during public health emergency
51. Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
6 following terms have the following meanings.
7 A. “Licensed facility” means a facility licensed under Title 5, section 20005, subsection 
8 6, paragraph B or Title 34-B, section 1203-A.
9 B. “Public health emergency” means a federal public health emergency declared 
10 pursuant to 42 United States Code, Section 247d or a state public health emergency 
11 declared pursuant to section 802 or Title 37-B, chapter 13, subchapter 2.
122. Written informed consent not required. The department may not require a 
13 licensed facility to obtain written informed consent from a person receiving mental health 
14 services or substance use disorder treatment from the licensed facility during a public health 
15 emergency.  The licensed facility may obtain consent through verbal, electronic or written 
16 means from a person receiving mental health services or substance use disorder treatment 
17 during a public health emergency.
183. Rulemaking. The department shall adopt rules to implement this section. Rules 
19 adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 
20 375, subchapter 2-A.
21Sec. 2. Department of Health and Human Services to amend certain 
22licensing rules.  No later than January 1, 2023, the Department of Health and Human 
23 Services shall amend its rules in 14-193 C.M.R. Chapter 6, Licensing of Mental Health 
24 Facilities, and 14-118 C.M.R. Chapter 5, Regulations for Licensing and Certifying of 
25 Substance Abuse Treatment Programs, to allow a facility licensed under the Maine Revised 
26 Statutes, Title 5, section 20005, subsection 6, paragraph B or Title 34-B, section 1203-A to 
27 obtain consent through verbal, electronic or written means from a person during a public 
28 health emergency in accordance with Title 22, section 51.  Rules adopted pursuant to this 
29 section are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A. 
30SUMMARY
31 This bill provides that the Department of Health and Human Services may not require 
32 a licensed mental health facility or licensed substance use disorder treatment facility to 
33 obtain written informed consent from a client during a federal or state public health 
34 emergency.  The licensed facility may instead obtain a client’s consent verbally, 
35 electronically or in writing.  The bill directs the Department of Health and Human Services 
36 to, no later than January 1, 2023, amend its rules in 14-193 C.M.R. Chapter 6, Licensing 
37 of Mental Health Facilities, and 14-118 C.M.R. Chapter 5, Regulations for Licensing and 
38 Certifying of Substance Abuse Treatment Programs, to meet the requirements of this 
39 legislation.31
