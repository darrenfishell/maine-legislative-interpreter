3CHAPTER 210-C
4MAINE DATA COLLECTION PROTECTION ACT
5§1350-C.  Short title
6 This chapter may be known and cited as "the Maine Data Collection Protection Act."
7§1350-D.  Definitions
8 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 
9 have the following meanings.
101.  Data collector.  "Data collector" means:
11 A.  A person that collects or attempts to collect data, directly or indirectly, from 
12 publicly maintained records and sells that data to 3rd parties for any purpose, including 
13 but not limited to the determination of an individual's eligibility for consumer credit, 
14 employment or residential housing;
15 B.  A person that collects data, directly or indirectly, from publicly maintained records 
16 and engages in a pattern and practice of using that data to determine an individual's 
17 eligibility for consumer credit, employment or residential housing; or
18 C. A person that acquires data from any person who meets the definition of "data 
19 collector" set forth in paragraph A.
20 A data collector as defined in paragraph A is considered a consumer reporting agency as 
21 defined in section 1308, subsection 3 and is subject to the provisions of the Fair Credit 
22 Reporting Act.
232.  Consumer.  "Consumer" means an individual whose publicly available data is 
24 collected by a data collector.
25 An individual who meets the definition of "consumer" for purposes of this Act is deemed 
26 to meet the definition of "consumer" in section 1308, subsection 2 and is entitled to the 
27 rights and protections of the Fair Credit Reporting Act.
28§1350-E.  Prohibited practices
29 A data collector may not aggregate, sell or use the following documents or the 
30 information obtained from the following documents for the purpose of determining a 
31 consumer's eligibility for consumer credit, employment or residential housing:
321.  No wrongdoing alleged.  Documents that are part of a court case or government 
33 action or investigation that does not allege wrongdoing by the consumer;
342.  Resolution by agreement of consumer.  Documents that are part of a court case 
35 or government action or investigation that is resolved by the agreement of the consumer;
Page 2 - 130LR1842(01)13.  Dismissed or resolved in favor of consumer.  Documents that are part of a court 
2 case or government action or investigation that is dismissed or resolved in favor of the 
3 consumer;
44.  Set aside or vacated.  Documents that are part of a court case or government action 
5 or investigation that is set aside or vacated;
65.  Judgment against consumer by default; exception.  Documents that are part of a 
7 court case or government action or investigation in which judgment is entered against the 
8 consumer by default unless the court has made a finding that the plaintiff has proven the 
9 alleged wrongdoing by the consumer;
106.  Older than 3 years.  Documents that are part of a civil court case or government 
11 action or investigation that is older than 3 years;
127.  Order.  Documents that are subject to an order of a court or government agency 
13 that prohibits the use of the documents or the information obtained from the documents to 
14 determine a consumer's eligibility for consumer credit, employment or residential housing; 
15 or
168.  COVID-19 public health emergency.  Documents that are part of a court case or 
17 government action or investigation that results in a judgment of eviction or a consumer 
18 debt during the period of the public health emergency resulting from coronavirus disease 
19 2019, or COVID-19, as declared by the Governor in the Proclamation of State of Civil 
20 Emergency to Further Protect Public Health, March 15, 2020, pursuant to Title 37-B, 
21 section 742 and as renewed and extended by subsequent proclamations.
22§1350-F.  License required
23 A person who meets the definition of "data collector" set forth in section 1350-D, 
24 subsection 1, paragraph A may not conduct business in this State without a valid license 
25 issued by the Superintendent of the Bureau of Consumer Credit Protection within the 
26 Department of Professional and Financial Regulation under the Maine Fair Debt Collection 
27 Practices Act.
28§1350-G.  Civil liability
291. Failure to comply with Act.  A data collector that fails to comply with any provision 
30 of this Act with respect to any consumer is liable to that person in an amount equal to the 
31 sum of:
32 A.  Actual damages sustained by the consumer as a result of such failure;
33 B.  In the case of any action by an individual consumer, such additional damages as the 
34 court may allow, not exceeding $2,000;
35 C.  In the case of a class action:
36 (1)  Such amount for each named plaintiff as may be recovered under paragraph 
37 A; and
38 (2)  Such amount as the court may allow for all other class members, without regard 
39 to a minimum individual recovery, not to exceed the lesser of $500,000 and 1% of 
40 the net worth of the data collector; and
Page 3 - 130LR1842(01)1 D.  In the case of any successful action to enforce liability set out in this subsection, 
2 the costs of the action, together with reasonable attorney's fees as determined by the 
3 court.  
42.  Considerations affecting liability.  In determining the amount of liability in any 
5 action brought under this chapter, the court shall consider, among other relevant factors:
6 A.  In any individual consumer action, the frequency and persistence of noncompliance 
7 by the data collector, the nature of that noncompliance and the extent to which the 
8 noncompliance was intentional; or
9 B.  In any class action, the frequency and persistence of noncompliance by the data 
10 collector, the nature of that noncompliance, the resources of the data collector, the 
11 number of persons adversely affected and the extent to which the data collector's 
12 noncompliance was intentional.
133.  Defense.  A data collector may not be held liable in any action brought under this 
14 chapter if the data collector shows, by a preponderance of the evidence, that the violation 
15 was not intentional and resulted from bona fide error, notwithstanding the maintenance of 
16 procedure reasonably adapted to avoid any such error.
174.  Action to enforce liability.  An action to enforce liability under this section must 
18 be brought within one year from the date on which the violation occurred or from the date 
19 on which the consumer was reasonably on notice of the violation, whichever is later.
205.  Bad faith, harassment.  On a finding by the court that an action under this section 
21 was brought in bad faith and for the purpose of harassment, the court may award the 
22 defendant reasonable attorney's fees and costs.
23SUMMARY
24 This bill creates the Maine Data Collection Protection Act, which prohibits data 
25 collectors from collecting and aggregating, selling or using specific types of public 
26 documents or information from those documents for the purpose of determining a 
27 consumer's eligibility for consumer credit, employment or residential housing.
