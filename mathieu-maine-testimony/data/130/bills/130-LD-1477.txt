14 A. The least restrictive treatment option upon discharge;
15 B. Be advised of realistic options when hospital care is no longer needed; and

17 committees that can help resolve patient questions;
18 3. Patient advocacy in discharge planning from health care facilities; 
19 4. The process for patients to dispute their discharge plan; and 
20 5. Standards of care, which must include that the patient advocate or discharge planning 
21 team provide:
22 A. Appointments and contact information for prescribed in-home rehabilitation

24 B. A copy of the list of barriers and explanations of any barriers preventing in-home 
25 rehabilitation services; and
26 C. In writing, the process for a patient or advocate to appeal decisions and overcome 
27 barriers in paragraph B.

33SUMMARY
34 This resolve directs the Department of Health and Human Services to collaborate with 
35 the long-term care ombudsman program to convene a stakeholder group to study and to 
36 identify and develop recommendations to resolve regulatory and administrative barriers 
37 that may result in a delay or denial for qualified persons to receive in-home rehabilitation 
38 services and to report and make recommendations to the Joint Standing Committee on 
39 Health and Human Services, which may report out legislation based upon the report to the 
40 Second Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.34
