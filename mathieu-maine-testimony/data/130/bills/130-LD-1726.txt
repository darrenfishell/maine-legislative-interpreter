2Sec. 1.  21-A MRSA §1014, sub-§3-B, as amended by PL 2007, c. 443, Pt. A, §9, 
3 is further amended to read:
43-B.  Newspapers.  A newspaper may not publish a communication described in 
5 subsections 1 to 2-A without including the disclosure required by this section.  For purposes 
6 of this subsection, "newspaper" includes any printed material intended for general 
7 circulation or to be read by the general public, including a version of the newspaper 
8 displayed on a website owned or operated by the newspaper.  "Newspaper" does not include 
9 a communication under subsection 5-A.  When necessary, a newspaper may seek the advice 
10 of the commission regarding whether or not the communication requires the disclosure.
11Sec. 2.  21-A MRSA §1014, sub-§5-A is enacted to read:
125-A.  Communication in the guise of news.  A communication in the guise of news 
13 that otherwise meets the criteria of subsection 1, 2 or 2-A or Internet and e-mail activities 
14 costing less than $100, as excluded by rule of the commission, published or paid for by an 
15 individual who is not required to register or file campaign finance reports with the 
16 commission and who is employed by a candidate, candidate's authorized campaign 
17 committee, party committee, political action committee or ballot question committee or an 
18 agent of a candidate, candidate's authorized campaign committee, party committee, 
19 political action committee or ballot question committee must disclose in an audio or video 
20 communication or at the top of the page of a printed, e-mail or Internet post communication 
21 "PAID FOR BY (insert name of employer)."  If the communication is a printed, e-mail or 
22 Internet post communication, the disclosure must be in capital letters and in at least 12-
23 point bold font.
