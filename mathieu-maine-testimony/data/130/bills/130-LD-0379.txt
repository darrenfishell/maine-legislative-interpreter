7§1374.  Maine State Cemetery Commission
8 The Maine State Cemetery Commission, referred to in this section as "the cemetery 
9 commission," is established by Title 5, section 12004-I, subsection 2-H as provided in this 
10 section.
111. Permanent members.  The cemetery commission has the following 8 permanent 
12 members:
13 A. Two members representing a statewide association dedicated to the preservation of 
14 neglected cemeteries, appointed by its board of directors;
15 B. Two members representing a statewide association of cemetery directors and 
16 supervisors, appointed by its board of directors;
17 C. One member representing a statewide association representing municipalities, 
18 appointed by its board of directors;
19 D. One member representing a statewide association of town clerks, appointed by its 
20 board of directors;
21 E. One member representing a statewide association of funeral directors, appointed by 
22 its board of directors; and
23 F. The Director of the Bureau of Maine Veterans' Services within the Department of 
24 Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management.
252. Optional members. The permanent members of the cemetery commission may 
26 invite, upon majority agreement, one member each from a state organization representing 
27 female descendants of veterans of the American Revolution, a state organization 
28 representing male descendants of veterans of the American Revolution and the American 
29 Legion or similar military veterans' group.
303. Duties. The cemetery commission may: 
31 A. Advise and educate municipalities and property owners about the existence of 
32 burying grounds and cemeteries and the laws applicable to burying grounds and 
33 cemeteries;
34 B. Advise and educate municipalities, property owners and owners of burying grounds 
35 and cemeteries on the proper care and preservation of graves, gravestones, walls and 
36 fences in and around burying grounds and cemeteries; and
37 C. Review statutes applicable to burying grounds and cemeteries and provide 
38 information to the legislature on recommended changes.  4
