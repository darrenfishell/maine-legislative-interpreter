2 Department of Education shall form a working group to explore and recommend changes

4 of:
5 1.  One or more representatives of the Department of Education;

10 5.  One representative from the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry's 
11 stakeholder group assembled pursuant to Resolve 2019, chapter 32 to end hunger in Maine 
12 by 2030.
13 In addition, the Department of Education shall invite the participation of a 
14 representative from the Maine School Nutrition Association, the Maine School 
15 Management Association, a nonprofit association addressing child hunger in the State, a 
16 nonprofit organization addressing overall child welfare in the State and the United States 
17 Department of Agriculture, Food and Nutrition Service, Northeast Regional Office.
18Sec. 2.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the working group under section 1 shall evaluate 
19 the methods by which schools collect family income data, including family income data 
20 that is collected by schools to secure funding for school nutrition programs but is also used 
21 for other programs.  The working group shall explore and recommend means of collecting 
22 family income data other than through school nutrition programs.
23Sec. 3.  Report.  Resolved:  That, by January 4, 2022, the Department of Education 
24 shall submit a report, including suggested legislation, on the working group's evaluation in 
25 section 2 to the Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs.  The 
26 committee is authorized to submit legislation related to the report to the Second Regular 
27 Session of the 130th Legislature.
28SUMMARY
29 This resolve directs the Department of Education to form a working group to explore 
30 and recommend changes to how schools collect family income data, including family 
31 income data that is collected by schools to secure funding for school nutrition programs but 
32 is also used for other programs.  The department is directed to submit a report by January 
33 4, 2022 to the Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs, which is 
34 authorized to submit legislation related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 
35 130th Legislature.
