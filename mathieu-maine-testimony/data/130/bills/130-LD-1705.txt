4§6992. Purple Star School
5 The department shall designate a school administrative unit as a Purple Star School if 
6 the school administrative unit applies and qualifies for the designation under this chapter.
7§6993. Program requirements
8 To qualify as a Purple Star School, a school administrative unit must:
91.  Military liaison.  Designate a staff member as a military liaison, whose duties 
10 include:
11 A.  Identifying military-connected students enrolled at the school administrative unit;
12 B.  Serving as the point of contact between the school administrative unit and military-
13 connected students and their families;
14 C.  Determining appropriate school administrative unit services available to military-
15 connected students; and
16 D.  Assisting in coordinating school administrative unit programs relevant to military-
17 connected students;
182.  Website.  Maintain on the school administrative unit's publicly accessible website 
19 an easily accessible web page that includes resources for military-connected students and 
20 their families, including information regarding:
21 A.  Relocation to, enrollment in and transferring records to the school administrative 
22 unit;
23 B.  Academic planning, course sequences and advanced classes available at the school 
24 administrative unit; and
25 C.  Counseling and other support services available for military-connected students 
26 enrolled in the school administrative unit;
273.  Transition program.  Maintain a transition program led by students, when 
28 appropriate, that assists military-connected students in transitioning into the school 
29 administrative unit;
304.  Professional development.  Offer professional development for staff members on 
31 issues related to military-connected students; and
325.  Initiatives.  Initiate one of the following:
33 A.  A resolution showing support for military-connected students and their families;
34 B.  Recognition of the month of the military child or military family month with 
35 relevant events hosted by the school administrative unit; or
36 C.  A partnership with a local military installation, as defined in section 20102, that 
37 provides opportunities for active duty military members to volunteer at the school 
38 administrative unit, speak at an assembly or host a field trip.

7Sec. 4.  20-A MRSA §10010-A is enacted to read:
8§10010-A.  Spouse or child of active duty member of United States Armed Forces
9 A spouse or child of an active duty member of the United States Armed Forces who is 
10 assigned to duty out of the State immediately following assignment to duty in the State is 
11 eligible for in-state tuition rates and fees at the University of Maine System, the Maine 
12 Community College System or the Maine Maritime Academy as long as the spouse or child 
13 resides continuously in the State while enrolled in the postsecondary institution.
14SUMMARY
15 This bill establishes the Purple Star Schools Program administered by the Department 
16 of Education to recognize school administrative units that provide strong services and 
17 support for military-connected students and their families.  
18 The bill also provides that:
19 1.  A dependent of a member of the United States Armed Forces who has received 
20 official military orders to relocate to this State is eligible to apply to a virtual public charter 
21 school;
22 2.  A military-connected student whose parent is transferred or is pending transfer to 
23 the State while on active military duty pursuant to an official military order is considered a 
24 resident of the school administrative unit in which the address identified by the parent is 
25 located.  The term "military-connected student" is defined in the bill; and
26 3.  A spouse or child of an active duty member of the United States Armed Forces who 
27 is assigned to duty out of the State immediately following assignment to duty in the State 
28 is eligible for in-state tuition rates and fees at the University of Maine System, the Maine 
29 Community College System or the Maine Maritime Academy as long as the spouse or child 
30 resides continuously in the State while enrolled in the postsecondary institution.15
