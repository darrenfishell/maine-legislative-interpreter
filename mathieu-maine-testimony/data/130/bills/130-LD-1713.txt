13§5219-YY.  Credit for paper manufacturing workforce training
141.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
15 following terms have the following meanings.
16 A.  "Certified applicant" means a qualified applicant that has received a certificate of 
17 approval from the commissioner pursuant to subsection 2.
18 B.  "Classified position" means a skilled job in manufacturing production, technical 
19 services or maintenance that is specifically assigned to a qualified employee. 
20 C.  "Commissioner" means the Commissioner of Economic and Community 
21 Development.
22 D.  "Employee based at a paper manufacturing facility" means an employee who 
23 performs more than 50% of the employee's employee-related activities for the 
24 employer at a paper manufacturing facility.
25 E.  "Full-time" means an average of at least 36 hours weekly during the period of 
26 employment.
27 F.  "Paper manufacturing facility" means a facility in the State at which paper products 
28 are manufactured, including facilities used in support of such paper manufacturing, that 
29 is not located within a low-income community.  As used in this paragraph, "low-
30 income community" has the same meaning as in 26 United States Code, Section 
31 45D(e)(1). 
32 G.  "Qualified applicant" means an applicant that, at the time an application for a 
33 certificate of approval is submitted, directly employs at least 400 qualified employees, 
34 at least 75% of whom earn at least 115% of the most recent annual per capita personal 
35 income in the county in which the qualified employee is employed.
36 H.  "Qualified employee" means a full-time employee based at a paper manufacturing 
37 facility:
38 (1)  For whom a retirement program subject to the federal Employee Retirement 
39 Income Security Act of 1974, 29 United States Code, Chapter 18 and group health 
40 insurance are provided; 
Page 2 - 130LR0521(01)1 (2)  Whose income derived from employment at a paper manufacturing facility, 
2 calculated on a calendar year basis, is greater than the most recent annual per capita 
3 personal income in the county in which the employee is employed; and
4 (3)  Who is not a salaried employee as described by Title 26, section 663, 
5 subsection 3, paragraph K. 
6 "Qualified employee" does not include an employee who is transferred, as determined 
7 by the commissioner, to a qualified applicant from an affiliated business, as defined in 
8 section 6753, subsection 1-A, located in the State.
9 I.  "Qualified workforce training wages" means wages paid by a certified applicant to 
10 a qualified employee hired on or after January 1, 2021 during:
11 (1)  The 6-week period following the qualified employee's date of hire; and
12 (2)  Time spent training a qualified employee for a promotion to a new classified 
13 position.
142.  Procedures for application; certificate of approval.  This subsection governs the 
15 procedures for applying for and obtaining a certificate of approval for the tax credit under 
16 this section.
17 A.  A qualified applicant may apply to the commissioner for a certificate of approval.
18 B.  An applicant shall submit to the commissioner information demonstrating that the 
19 applicant is a qualified applicant. 
20 C.  The commissioner, within 30 days of receipt of an application submitted pursuant 
21 to paragraphs A and B, shall determine whether the applicant is a qualified applicant 
22 and shall issue either a certificate of approval or a written denial indicating why the 
23 applicant is not a qualified applicant.  A certificate issued by the commissioner must 
24 specify a limit on the amount of qualified workforce training wages approved under 
25 the certificate.  The commissioner may not issue a certificate to a qualified applicant 
26 that approves qualified workforce training wages of more than $1,000,000 per year or 
27 $6,000,000 in total.
28 D.  A certified applicant shall obtain approval from the commissioner prior to the 
29 transfer of the certificate of approval to another person.  A certificate of approval may 
30 be transferred only if all or substantially all of the assets of the certified applicant are, 
31 or will be, transferred to that person or if 50% or more of the certified applicant's voting 
32 stock or analogous noncorporate equity interest is, or will be, acquired by that person.  
33 The commissioner shall approve the transfer of the certificate of approval only if the 
34 transfer meets the requirements of this paragraph and at least one of the following 
35 conditions is satisfied:
36 (1)  The transferee is a member of the certified applicant's unitary affiliated group, 
37 as defined in section 5102, subsection 1-B, at the time of the transfer; or
38 (2)  The commissioner finds that the transferee will, and has the capacity to, 
39 maintain operations of the paper manufacturing facility in a manner that meets the 
40 minimum qualifications for continued eligibility for credits under this section after 
41 the transfer occurs.

2 D, the transferee, from the date of the transfer, must be treated as the certified applicant 
3 and as eligible to claim any remaining credit under the certificate of approval that was 
4 not previously claimed by the transferor as long as the transferee meets the same 
5 eligibility requirements and conditions for the credit as applied to the transferor. 
6 F.  The commissioner shall revoke a certificate of approval if the certified applicant or 
7 transferee ceases operations of the paper manufacturing facility in the State or the 
8 certificate of approval is transferred to another person without approval from the 
9 commissioner pursuant to paragraph D.
103.  Refundable credit allowed.  A certified applicant is allowed a refundable credit as 
11 provided in this subsection.
12 A.  Subject to the limitations under paragraph B, for tax years beginning on or after 
13 January 1, 2021 and ending on or before December 31, 2025, a certified applicant is 
14 allowed a credit against the tax otherwise due under this Part for the taxable year in an 
15 amount equal to any qualified workforce training wages paid during the taxable year 
16 up to the limit set forth in the certified applicant's certificate of approval under 
17 subsection 2.
18 B.  Cumulative credits under this subsection for all certified applicants may not exceed 
19 $2,000,000 per year and $12,000,000 in total.
204.  Reporting required.  A certified applicant, the commissioner and the State Tax 
21 Assessor are required to make reports pursuant to this subsection.
22 A.  On or before March 1st of each year, a certified applicant shall file a report with 
23 the commissioner for the tax year ending during the immediately preceding calendar 
24 year, referred to in this paragraph as "the report year," containing the following 
25 information:
26 (1)  The number of qualified employees of the certified applicant on the last day of 
27 the report year; and
28 (2)  The amount of qualified workforce training wages paid during the report year.
29 B.  By April 1st of each year, the commissioner shall report to the joint standing 
30 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over taxation matters aggregate data 
31 on employment levels and qualified workforce training wages paid by certified 
32 applicants for each year for which a certified applicant claimed a credit under this 
33 section.  
34 C.  By April 1st of each year, the State Tax Assessor shall report to the joint standing 
35 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over taxation matters the revenue loss 
36 during the previous calendar year as a result of this section for each taxpayer claiming 
37 the credit.  
38 D.  Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the reports provided under 
39 this subsection are public records as defined in Title 1, section 402, subsection 3.

176.  Rules; fees.  The commissioner and the State Tax Assessor may jointly adopt rules

25SUMMARY
26 This bill creates a refundable Maine income tax credit for paper manufacturers 
27 providing workforce training to employees as long as certain employment and wage levels 
28 are met.  The income tax credit is equal to the amount of workforce training wages paid to 
29 certain employees each year and is subject to a limit of $2,000,000 per year among all 
30 eligible employers and $12,000,000 in total. The income tax credit is subject to ongoing 
31 legislative review.
32 This bill also requires employers receiving the income tax credit to file annual reports 
33 with the Commissioner of Economic and Community Development and requires the 
34 commissioner and State Tax Assessor to annually report to the joint standing committee of 
35 the Legislature having jurisdiction over taxation matters certain statistics and data related 
36 to the income tax credit.1
