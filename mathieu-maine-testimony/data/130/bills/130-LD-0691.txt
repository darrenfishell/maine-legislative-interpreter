3ยง219-A.  Fund To Address Food Insecurity and Provide Nutrition Incentives
41.  Fund established.  The Fund To Address Food Insecurity and Provide Nutrition 
5 Incentives, referred to in this section as "the fund," is established in the department to 
6 provide incentives to federal food and nutrition assistance program participants for the 
7 purchase of locally grown fruits and vegetables and to support outreach for and 
8 administration of programs that offer nutrition incentives to participants of federal food and 
9 nutrition assistance programs.  The fund is established to match contributions from private 
10 and public sources of up to $50,000 annually to further the purposes of this section.  The 
11 fund, to be accounted within the department, must be held separate and apart from all other 
12 money, funds and accounts.  Eligible investment earnings credited to the assets of the fund 
13 become part of the assets of the fund.  Any unexpended balances remaining in the fund at 
14 the end of any fiscal year do not lapse and must be carried forward to the next fiscal year. 
15 For purposes of this section, "local" means within the State.
162.  Fund recipients.  An organization based in the State that supports local food 
17 producers, local food production or low-income individuals in receiving food and nutrition 
18 assistance may receive proceeds from the fund upon application with the department.  The 
19 department shall prioritize an applicant that has a demonstrated history of incentivizing the 
20 use of federal food and nutrition assistance programs to purchase locally grown fruits and 
21 vegetables or that demonstrates the ability to leverage the proceeds to match or receive 
22 additional funds from local, state, federal or private sources.  The department shall 
23 periodically post a request for applications for eligible organizations to apply for fund 
24 proceeds.
253.  Report; audit.  The department shall require a periodic report from a recipient 
26 under subsection 2 detailing the use of fund proceeds and the federal food and nutrition 
27 assistance programs involved and to ensure that the funds are expended appropriately 
28 pursuant to this section.  The department may audit a recipient to carry out the purposes of 
29 this subsection.
304.  Rules.  The department may adopt routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
31 chapter 375, subchapter 2-A to carry out the purposes of the fund, including application 
32 criteria and procedures for recipients, disbursement of funds to recipients and for outreach 
33 and administration purposes and reporting and audit procedures for recipients.

3SUMMARY
4 This bill establishes the Fund To Address Food Insecurity and Provide Nutrition 
5 Incentives within the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to provide 
6 incentives to federal food and nutrition assistance program participants for the purchase of 
7 locally grown fruits and vegetables and to support outreach for and administration of 
8 programs that offer nutrition incentives to participants of federal food and nutrition 
9 assistance programs.  The fund will match contributions from private and public sources of 
10 up to $50,000 annually.  Fund recipients must be state-based organizations that support 
11 local food producers, local food production or low-income individuals in receiving food 
12 and nutrition assistance, with priority given to organizations that have a demonstrated 
13 history of incentivizing the use of federal food and nutrition assistance programs to 
14 purchase locally grown fruits and vegetables or that demonstrate the ability to leverage the 
15 proceeds to match or receive additional funds from local, state, federal or private sources.  
16 The bill provides reporting and audit requirements for recipients to ensure proper use of 
17 the funds and appropriates funds to capitalize the fund.1
