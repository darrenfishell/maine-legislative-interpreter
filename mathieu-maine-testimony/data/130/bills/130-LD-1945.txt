5§9601.  Definitions
6 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 
7 have the following meanings.
81. Affirmative written consent. "Affirmative written consent" means:
9 A. A specific, unambiguous and informed written consent given by an individual who 
10 is not under duress or undue influence at the time the consent is given; or
11 B. In the context of employment, a release signed by an employee as a condition of 
12 employment.
132. Biometric identifier.  "Biometric identifier" means information generated by 
14 measurements of an individual's unique biological characteristics, including imagery of the 
15 iris, retina, fingerprint, face or hand, that can be used to identify that individual.  "Biometric 
16 identifier" does not include:
17 A. A writing sample or written signature;
18 B. A photograph or video, except for measurable biological characteristics that can be 
19 generated or captured from a photograph or video;
20 C. A biological sample used for valid scientific testing or screening;
21 D.  Demographic information;
22 E. A tattoo description or a physical description, such as height, weight, hair color or 
23 eye color;
24 F.  A donated organ, tissue or other body part, blood or serum stored on behalf of a 
25 recipient or potential recipient of a living or cadaveric transplant and obtained or stored 
26 by a federally designated organ procurement organization;
27 G. Information captured from a patient in a health care setting or information collected, 
28 used or stored for health care treatment, payment or operations under the federal Health 
29 Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, Public Law 104-191; or
30 H. An x-ray, computed tomography, magnetic resonance imaging, positron emission 
31 tomography, mammography or other image or film of the human anatomy used to 
32 diagnose or treat an illness or other medical condition or to further validate scientific 
33 testing or screening.
343.  Personal information.  "Personal information" means information that identifies, 
35 relates to, describes, is reasonably capable of being associated with or could reasonably be 
36 linked, directly or indirectly, with a particular individual, household or electronic device.

55. Processor.  "Processor" means a private entity that collects, processes, stores or

7§9602.  Applicability
81. Processors. A processor is not required to comply with the requirements of this 
9 chapter with respect to the biometric identifiers that the processor stores, processes or 
10 otherwise uses under an agreement with another private entity with which the processor is 
11 not affiliated through common ownership.  A processor is required to comply with the 
12 requirements of this chapter with respect to the biometric identifiers that the processor 
13 collects, stores, processes or uses that are not covered by an agreement with another private 
14 entity with which the processor is not affiliated through common ownership.
152. General exceptions. This chapter does not apply to:
16 A.  The admission or discovery of a biometric identifier in any action of any kind in 
17 any court or before any government tribunal, board or agency;
18 B.  A requirement under the federal Health Insurance Portability and Accountability 
19 Act of 1996, Public Law 104-191 and applicable regulations; or
20 C.  A contractor, subcontractor or agent of a state agency or local unit of government 
21 when working for that state agency or local unit of government.
22§9603.  Biometric identifier policy requirements
231. Development of policy. Except as provided by subsection 3, a private entity in 
24 possession of biometric identifiers shall develop and make available to the public a written 
25 policy that establishes a retention schedule and guidelines for permanently destroying a 
26 biometric identifier of an individual on the earlier of:
27 A. The date on which the initial purpose for obtaining the biometric identifier has been 
28 satisfied; or
29 B. One year after the individual's last intentional interaction with the private entity in 
30 possession of the biometric identifier.
312. Adherence to policy. A private entity shall comply with the policy developed by 
32 the private entity under subsection 1, except for an action taken in response to a valid 
33 warrant or subpoena issued by a court of competent jurisdiction.
343. Exception.  A private entity is not required to make available to the public a written 
35 policy that:
36 A. Applies only to the employees of the private entity; and
37 B. Is used solely within the private entity for the operation of the private entity.
384.  Violation.  A violation of this section is a violation of the Maine Unfair Trade 
39 Practices Act.

16§9605.  Required disclosure
171. Disclosure required. On the request of an individual, a private entity that collects 
18 or possesses a biometric identifier shall disclose to that individual, free of charge, any 
19 biometric identifier associated with that individual and the information required by 
20 subsection 2. 
212. Required information. The information disclosed as required by subsection 1 must 
22 include all the relevant information for the period of 12 months before the biometric 
23 identifier was collected by or entered into the possession of the private entity, including:
24 A. The type of biometric identifier;
25 B. All personal information related to the biometric identifier;
26 C. The types of sources from which the private entity obtained the biometric identifier 
27 and personal information linked to the biometric identifier;
28 D. The use of the biometric identifier and personal information linked to the biometric 
29 identifier;
30 E. The type of 3rd party with which the private entity has shared the biometric 
31 identifier; and
32 F. The type of personal information linked to the biometric identifier that the private 
33 entity has disclosed to a 3rd party.
343.  Violation.  A violation of this section is a violation of the Maine Unfair Trade 
35 Practices Act.
36§9606.  Prohibitions
371. Collection or dissemination without consent. A private entity may not collect, 
38 purchase, receive through trade or otherwise obtain, use, disclose, transfer or otherwise 
39 disseminate an individual's biometric identifier unless the private entity first:
Page 4 - 130LR2477(01)1 A. Informs the individual in writing that a biometric identifier is being collected, 
2 purchased, received through trade or otherwise obtained, used, disclosed, transferred 
3 or otherwise disseminated;
4 B.  Informs the individual in writing of the specific purpose and length of time for 
5 which a biometric identifier is being collected, purchased, received through trade or 
6 otherwise obtained, used, disclosed, transferred or otherwise disseminated; and 
7 C.  Receives affirmative written consent from the individual.
8 This subsection does not apply to a disclosure of a biometric identifier if the disclosure 
9 completes a financial transaction requested or authorized by the individual, is required by 
10 state or federal law or municipal ordinance or is required pursuant to a valid warrant or 
11 subpoena issued by a court of competent jurisdiction.
122. Sale, lease or trade without consent. A private entity that is not acting as a 
13 processor and that collects a biometric identifier may not sell, lease, trade or otherwise 
14 profit from that biometric identifier unless the individual to whom the biometric identifier 
15 pertains has provided affirmative written consent for the sale, lease, trade or other activity.
163. Discrimination. A private entity may not:
17 A. Condition a sale of goods or the provision of a service on the collection, use, 
18 disclosure, transfer, sale or processing of a biometric identifier unless the use of a 
19 biometric identifier is strictly necessary to the sale of the goods or the provision of the 
20 service;
21 B. Charge a different price or rate for goods or the provision of a service to a customer 
22 that does not provide affirmative written consent to providing a biometric identifier; or
23 C. Provide a different quality of goods or a service to a customer that does not provide 
24 affirmative written consent to providing a biometric identifier.
254. Processors. A processor may not perform an action related to a biometric identifier 
26 unless:
27 A. The processor has received affirmative written consent from the individual to whom 
28 the biometric identifier pertains to perform the action; and
29 B. The action falls within the terms and conditions of an agreement between the 
30 processor and the private entity that owns or controls the biometric identifier.
315.  Violation.  A violation of this section is a violation of the Maine Unfair Trade 
32 Practices Act.

5SUMMARY
6 This bill provides for an individual's privacy regarding the collection and use of 
7 biometric identifiers of the individual and personal information connected to the biometric 
8 identifiers.  The bill requires a written release from an individual before a private entity 
9 may obtain or use biometric identifiers and requires the private entity to establish a policy 
10 for retention and destruction of the identifiers.  The bill provides for a private right of action 
11 for an aggrieved individual who has had biometric identifiers obtained or used in violation 
12 of the new provisions.  The bill also provides that violations of provisions related to 
13 biometric identifiers constitute violations of the Maine Unfair Trade Practices Act.6
