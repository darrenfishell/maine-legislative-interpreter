Page 1 - 130LR0848(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, ambulatory surgical centers provide cost-effective, high-quality health 
4 care; and
5Whereas, only one freestanding ambulatory surgical center in Maine deals with facial 
6 and oral surgery; and
7Whereas, the current MaineCare reimbursement rate is inadequate to cover such 
8 procedures; and
9Whereas, without an increase in the reimbursement rate, the center will stop accepting 
10 MaineCare patients; and
11Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
12 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
13 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
14 therefore, be it
15Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to increase MaineCare 
16rate for certain dental procedures performed by freestanding ambulatory 
17surgical centers.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services shall 
18 amend its reimbursement rate under the MaineCare program for dental code 41899 to 
19 $1,250 per procedure for freestanding ambulatory surgical centers performing procedures 
20 on the dentoalveolar structure.  Rules adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical 
21 rules as defined in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
22Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
23 takes effect when approved.
