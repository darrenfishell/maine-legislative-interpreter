14§13120-T.  Maine Workforce, Research, Development and Student Achievement

161.  Institute established.  The Maine Workforce, Research, Development and Student 
17 Achievement Institute, as established in Title 5, section 12004-I, subsection 6-I and referred 
18 to in this subchapter as "the institute," is established to collect and analyze information 
19 related to workforce training, research and development, student debt and economic 
20 advancement in the State, to make policy recommendations to the Legislature and to 
21 conduct targeted research for the Legislature.
222.  Steering committee.  The Maine Workforce, Research, Development and Student 
23 Achievement Institute Steering Committee, referred to in this section as "the steering 
24 committee," is established to advise the Legislature and the University of Maine System 
25 on all matters related to the institute.  With the exception of ex officio members, steering 
26 committee members serve for a term of 2 years as follows:
27 A.  The President of the Senate shall appoint:
28 (1)  One member from a private college in the State;
29 (2)  One member from the Maine Community College System;
30 (3)  One member from a regional economic development organization serving one 
31 or more of the following: Aroostook County, Franklin County, Oxford County, 
32 Piscataquis County, Somerset County and Washington County;
33 (4)  One member from a statewide organization responsible for business attraction 
34 and expansion;
35 (5)  One member involved in adult education;
36 (6)  One member involved in career and technical education;

22 pursuant to this subchapter must be maintained by the institute at the University of Maine 
23 and must be available for use by any interested group or individual.
24SUMMARY
25 This bill establishes the Maine Workforce, Research, Development and Student 
26 Achievement Institute and a related steering committee to advise the Legislature on matters 
27 related to workforce training, research and development, student debt and economic 
28 advancement in the State.  The bill describes the 18 required steering committee members 
29 and establishes that each member, other than ex officio members, serves a 2-year term and 
30 that the steering committee meets at least 4 times per year.25
