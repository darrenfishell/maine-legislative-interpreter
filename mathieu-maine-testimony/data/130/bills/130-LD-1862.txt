61.  A person who in good faith seeks medical assistance for or administers naloxone 
7 hydrochloride to another person experiencing a drug-related overdose, a person who is at 
8 the location where medical assistance was sought for or where naloxone hydrochloride was 
9 administered to another person experiencing a drug-related overdose or the person who is 
10 experiencing the drug-related overdose may not be arrested or prosecuted for a crime, 
11 unless the crime is a violent crime, or for a violation of probation as authorized by chapter 
12 67 or condition of release as authorized by Title 15, chapter 105-A, subchapter 5.
132.  For the purposes of this section, “violent crime” means an offense against the person 
14 as described in chapter 9; sexual exploitation of minors as described in chapter 12; 
15 kidnapping, criminal restraint and criminal forced labor as described in chapter 13; robbery 
16 as defined in section 651; sex trafficking as described in sections 852 and 853; and a 
17 sexually violent offense as defined in Title 34-A, section 11203, subsection 7.
18SUMMARY
19 This bill extends the State's Good Samaritan laws by exempting from arrest or 
20 prosecution for a nonviolent offense or for a violation of probation or condition of release 
21 a person at the location of a drug-related overdose for which medical assistance was sought 
22 or naloxone was administered.19
