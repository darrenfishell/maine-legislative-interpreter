5SUBCHAPTER 13
6PROHIBITED ACTS AND JUDICIAL ENFORCEMENT
7§880.  Prohibited acts
8 Notwithstanding any provision of this chapter to the contrary, an employer may not:
91.  Unlawful interference or denial of rights.  Interfere with, restrain or deny the 
10 exercise of or the attempt to exercise any right provided by this chapter, including those 
11 listed in subsection 2;
122.  Unlawful discrimination against exercise of rights.  Discharge, fine, suspend, 
13 expel, discipline or in any other manner discriminate against an employee for exercising 
14 any right provided by this chapter. Such rights include but are not limited to the right to 
15 request to exercise any rights within this chapter; the right to file a complaint with the 
16 Department of Labor or courts or to inform any person about an employer's alleged 
17 violation of any part of this chapter; the right to participate in an investigation, hearing or 
18 proceeding or to cooperate with or assist the Department of Labor in its investigations of 
19 alleged violations of any part of this chapter; and the right to inform any person of the 
20 person's potential rights under this chapter; and
213.  Unlawful discrimination against opposition.  Discharge, fine, suspend, expel, 
22 discipline or in any other manner discriminate against any employee for opposing any 
23 practice made unlawful by this chapter.
24§880-A.  Judicial enforcement
251.  Injunction and damages.  A civil action may be brought in the appropriate court 
26 by an employee against any employer to enforce this chapter. The court may enjoin any act 
27 or practice that violates or may violate this chapter and may order any other equitable relief 
28 that is necessary and appropriate to redress the violation or to enforce this chapter. The 
29 court may also:
30 A.  Award damages equal to the wages, salary, employment benefits or other 
31 compensation denied or lost to the employee by reason of the violation; or 
32 B.  Order the employer to pay liquidated damages of $100 to the employee for each 
33 day that the violation continued.
342.  Additional damages.  The court may also order the employer to pay an additional 
35 amount as liquidated damages equal to the amount awarded under subsection 1 if the 
36 employee proves to the satisfaction of the court that the employer's violation was willful.
Page 2 - 130LR0810(01)13.  Attorney's fees.  In any action brought pursuant to this section, in addition to any 
2 judgment awarded to the employee, the court shall award reasonable attorney's fees and 
3 other costs of the action to be paid by the employer.
44.  Other penalties preserved.  Nothing in this section may be construed to prohibit 
5 the imposition of penalties otherwise provided for in this chapter.
6§880-B.  Rules
7 The Department of Labor shall adopt rules to implement this subchapter.  Rules 
8 adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 
9 375, subchapter 2-A.
10Sec. 4.  Inconsistencies; report.  The Department of Labor shall review those parts 
11 of the Maine Revised Statutes governing prohibited acts and judicial enforcement under 
12 the laws governing employment practices to develop legislation to correct any 
13 inconsistencies in law that result from this Act.  By December 1, 2021, the Department of 
14 Labor shall submit the proposed legislation developed pursuant to this section to the Joint 
15 Standing Committee on Labor and Housing.  The joint standing committee may report out 
16 a bill relating to the proposed legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 130th 
17 Legislature.
18Sec. 5.  Effective date.  Those sections of this Act that repeal the Maine Revised 
19 Statutes, Title 26, sections 847 and 848 and enact Title 26, chapter 7, subchapter 13 take 
20 effect August 1, 2022.
21SUMMARY
22 This bill repeals the sections of law concerning prohibited acts and judicial 
23 enforcement under the laws governing family medical leave employment practices in the 
24 Maine Revised Statutes, Title 26, chapter 7 and enacts similar provisions that apply to all 
25 laws in the same chapter governing employment practices.22
