3CHAPTER 318-A
4EXPIRED MARINE FLARES
5§2491.  Definitions
6 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 
7 have the following meanings.
81.  Commissioner.  "Commissioner" means the Commissioner of Public Safety or the 
9 commissioner's designee.
102.  Expired.  "Expired" means, with regard to a marine flare, beyond the manufacturer-
11 designated period of recommended use.
123.  Fire inspector.  "Fire inspector" means a person designated by the commissioner 
13 to collect expired marine flares.
144.  Marine flare.  "Marine flare" means a device designed to produce a bright flame 
15 for use as a signal or marker on the inland or coastal waters of the State or at sea.
16§2492.  Collection program
17 The commissioner shall establish and oversee a program for the convenient and safe 
18 collection of expired marine flares pursuant to this section.
191.  Site collection.  The commissioner shall coordinate the following site collection 
20 operations.
21 A.  The commissioner shall, in coordination with a statewide association of fire chiefs, 
22 identify fire departments in the State that have the capacity and willingness to accept 
23 expired marine flares from members of the public.  A participating fire department may 
24 choose to designate the geographic area from which the fire department will accept 
25 expired marine flares.  A participating fire department and the commissioner shall 
26 coordinate for timely pickup of the collected expired marine flares.
27 B.  The commissioner shall coordinate with sponsors of annual community events that 
28 choose to include expired marine flare collection as part of their events.
29 C.  The commissioner shall coordinate with marinas, municipalities and groups that 
30 organize events at which the public is invited to drop off expired marine flares for 
31 timely collection.
322.  Short-term storage.  The commissioner shall provide guidance for short-term 
33 storage to persons collecting expired marine flares under subsection 1 if the commissioner 
34 determines that safety equipment or special procedures are required.
353.  Direct collection.  The commissioner shall designate fire inspectors to collect 
36 expired marine flares in a timely way from individuals who fish commercially and 
37 recreational boaters in the State who contact the Department of Public Safety to notify the 
38 department of expired marine flares in need of disposal.6

Page 2 - 130LR0682(01)1§2493.  Disposal program
2 The commissioner shall establish a program for the safe, nonpolluting disposal of all 
3 types of expired marine flares pursuant to this section.
41.  Short-term storage.  Expired marine flares collected pursuant to section 2492 that 
5 require short-term storage must be stored according to standards established by the 
6 Department of Public Safety.  
72.  Sorting.  Expired marine flares collected pursuant to section 2492 must be sorted 
8 by type for proper incineration.
93.  Incineration.  Expired marine flares sorted pursuant to subsection 2 must be 
10 incinerated at temperatures that are compliant with Department of Environmental 
11 Protection standards for preventing air pollution.
124.  Recycling.  To the extent practicable, material from incinerated expired marine 
13 flares must be recycled.
14§2494.  Education program
15 The commissioner shall establish an education program for the public and state 
16 agencies regarding expired marine flares pursuant to this section.
171.  Education campaign.  The Department of Public Safety shall engage in an 
18 education campaign to inform the public and personnel of the Department of Public Safety, 
19 the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, the Department of Inland 
20 Fisheries and Wildlife, the Department of Environmental Protection and the Department of 
21 Marine Resources and other potentially interested entities about the expired marine flare 
22 collection and disposal programs under this chapter, the dangers expired marine flares pose 
23 to public health and the dangers expired marine flares that are improperly disposed of pose 
24 to the environment and to the safety of persons and property.
252.  Department websites.  The Department of Public Safety shall maintain a publicly 
26 accessible website supporting the education campaign under subsection 1 and serving as 
27 an information resource regarding expired marine flares and their proper disposal pursuant 
28 to this chapter.  The Department of Public Safety, the Department of Agriculture, 
29 Conservation and Forestry, the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife, the 
30 Department of Environmental Protection and the Department of Marine Resources shall 
31 coordinate to update their publications and publicly accessible websites that are suited for 
32 the purpose with information regarding the collection and disposal of expired marine flares 
33 pursuant to this chapter.
343.  Printed materials.  Materials published by a state agency and intended for those 
35 who fish commercially or for recreational boaters must, in printings that occur on or after 
36 the effective date of this subsection, include information about environmental and safety 
37 concerns related to improper disposal and storage of expired marine flares.  The expired 
38 marine flare collection and disposal programs established pursuant to this chapter must be 
39 described in these print materials, including contact information for the Department of 
40 Public Safety's expired marine flare collection and disposal programs.
414.  State agency notification.  The commissioner shall notify other state agencies or 
42 entities as the commissioner determines appropriate about the expired marine flare 
43 collection and disposal programs under this chapter.2

2CHAPTER 727
3MARINE FLARE DISPOSAL FEE
4§5011.  Marine flare disposal fee imposed
5 A fee is imposed on the retail sale in this State of marine flares in the amount of 25¢ 
6 per flare for the purpose of offsetting the cost to the General Fund of paying for the marine 
7 flare disposal, education and collection programs pursuant to Title 25, chapter 318-A.
81.  Marine flare.  As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, 
9 "marine flare" means a device designed to produce a bright flame for use as a signal or 
10 marker on the inland or coastal waters of this State or at sea.
112.  Exemption.  Transactions that, under the laws of this State, are not subject to 
12 taxation in accordance with Part 3 are exempt from the fee imposed by this section.
133.  Administration.  The fee imposed by this section is administered as provided in 
14 chapter 7 and Part 3, with the fee imposed pursuant to this chapter to be considered as 
15 imposed under Part 3.  The revenue derived from the fee imposed by this chapter must be 
16 deposited in the General Fund.
174.  Effective date.  This chapter takes effect January 1, 2022.
18SUMMARY
19 This bill establishes within the Department of Public Safety programs for the collection 
20 and disposal of expired marine flares and for education of the public and state agency 
21 personnel regarding expired marine flares.  It establishes a marine flare disposal fee to 
22 offset the costs.19
