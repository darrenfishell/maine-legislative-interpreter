3ยง3174-FFF.  Reimbursement for donor breast milk
41.  Reimbursement.  The department shall provide reimbursement under the 
5 MaineCare program for pasteurized donor breast milk provided to an infant receiving 
6 benefits under this chapter if a physician or physician assistant licensed under Title 32, 
7 chapter 36 or 48 or an advanced practice registered nurse licensed under Title 32, chapter 
8 31 signs an order stating that such milk is medically necessary and:
9 A.  The infant is medically or physically unable to receive maternal breast milk or 
10 participate in breastfeeding; or
11 B.  The infant's mother is medically or physically unable to produce maternal breast 
12 milk in quantities sufficient for the infant.
132.  Rulemaking.  The department shall adopt rules to implement this section.  Rules 
14 adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined by Title 5, chapter 
15 375, subchapter 2-A.
16Sec. 2.  Medicaid state plan amendment or waiver.  The Department of Health 
17 and Human Services shall prepare and submit a Medicaid state plan amendment or waiver 
18 as necessary to the United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for 
19 Medicare and Medicaid Services no later than January 1, 2022 that provides or requests, as 
20 appropriate, Medicaid coverage for medically necessary donor breast milk for infants.
21SUMMARY
22 This bill requires that the Department of Health and Human Services provide 
23 reimbursement under the MaineCare program for pasteurized donor breast milk provided 
24 to an infant if a physician, physician assistant or advanced practice registered nurse signs 
25 an order stating that such milk is medically necessary and the infant is medically or 
26 physically unable to receive maternal breast milk or participate in breastfeeding or the 
27 infant's mother is medically or physically unable to produce maternal breast milk in 
28 quantities sufficient for the infant.
