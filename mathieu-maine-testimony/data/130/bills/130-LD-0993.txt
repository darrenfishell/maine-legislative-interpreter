161.  Required leave.  A school administrative unit shall grant a total of 80 hours of paid 
17 leave per academic year from work, except as described in subsection 4, for a public school 
18 employee affected by the illness caused by infection with the coronavirus SARS-CoV-2, 
19 referred to in this section as "COVID-19."
202.  Rate of pay.  Rate of pay for leave required under subsection 1 must be calculated 
21 as follows:
22 A.  In order to receive pay at 100% of the usual rate of pay a public school employee 
23 must:
24 (1)  Be subject to a federal, state or local quarantine order related to COVID-19;
25 (2)  Have been advised by a health care provider to self-quarantine for reasons 
26 related to COVID-19; or
27 (3)  Be experiencing symptoms of COVID-19 and be seeking a medical diagnosis; 
28 and
29 B.  In order to receive pay at 100% of the usual rate of pay, a public school employee 
30 who is not eligible under paragraph A must:
31 (1)  Be caring for an individual subject to a federal, state or local quarantine order 
32 related to COVID-19;
33 (2)  Be a parent or guardian providing care for a child whose school or place of 
34 child care is closed or unavailable due to COVID-19 precautions; or
35 (3)  Be experiencing other circumstances as determined in rule by the Department 
36 of Labor, in consultation with the Department of Health and Human Services.
373.  Length of employment.  Leave required under subsection 1 must be granted 
38 regardless of the length of employment of the employee.
394.  Part-time employees.  Part-time public school employees are entitled to 2 weeks 
40 of paid leave as required under this section based on the number of hours ordinarily worked

12SUMMARY
13 This bill requires school administrative units to provide a total of 80 hours of paid sick 
14 leave per academic year for public school employees affected by COVID-19.  The bill 
15 establishes criteria for rate of pay during the leave period and provides that part-time 
16 employees are eligible for leave based on the average number of hours worked.  The bill 
17 requires school administrative units to offer leave regardless of the length of employment 
18 of the employee.  It grants the Department of Education rule-making authority.1
