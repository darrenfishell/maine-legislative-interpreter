2Sec. 1.  12 MRSA §5018 is enacted to read:
3§5018.  Emergency Food Assistance Program Fund
41.  Fund established.  The Emergency Food Assistance Program Fund, referred to in 
5 this section as "the fund," is established as a nonlapsing fund to support the emergency 
6 food assistance program administered by the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and 
7 Forestry, referred to in this section as "the department."  Money in the fund must be 
8 expended as allocated by the Legislature for the purposes of the fund and may be invested 
9 as provided by law.  Interest on investments must be credited to the fund.
102.  Use of fund.  Amounts available in the fund must be used to support the emergency 
11 food assistance program administered by the department.
123.  Administration.  The department shall administer the fund and shall adopt rules as 
13 necessary to administer the fund.  Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine 
14 technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
154.  Income tax check-off funding.  Revenue collected from the income tax checkoff 
16 pursuant to Title 36, section 5293 must be credited to the fund.
175.  Other funds.  The fund may receive money from any source, including grants, gifts, 
18 bequests and donations.

39 Emergency Food Assistance Program Fund established in Title 12, section 5018.  A

62.  Contributions credited to Emergency Food Assistance Program Fund.  The 
7 State Tax Assessor shall determine annually the total amount contributed pursuant to 
8 subsection 1.  Prior to the beginning of the next year, the State Tax Assessor shall deduct 
9 the cost of administering the Emergency Food Assistance Program Fund checkoff and

12Sec. 4.  Application. This Act applies to tax years beginning on or after January 1, 
13 2022.
14SUMMARY
15 This bill requires the inclusion of a check-off provision on Maine income tax forms for 
16 taxpayers who wish to contribute to support the emergency food assistance program 
17 administered by the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry.  It also 
18 establishes the Emergency Food Assistance Program Fund, to which revenue collected 
19 from the income tax checkoff is credited.1
