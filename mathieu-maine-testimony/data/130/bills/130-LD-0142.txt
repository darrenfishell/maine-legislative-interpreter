29SUMMARY
30 This bill does the following.
31 1.  It requires the Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife to establish by rule 
32 an open season on hunting bear beginning no earlier than the 2nd Monday preceding 
33 September 1st and ending no later than November 30th annually.
34 2.  It clarifies that the commissioner may adopt rules to set the dates when dogs may 
35 be used to hunt black bear during any portion of the open bear hunting season.
36 3.  It provides that a person may not set bear bait earlier than 30 days before the first 
37 Monday preceding September 1st.
38 4.  It reduces the fee for a resident bear permit from $27 to $10.
39 5.  It requires the commissioner to adopt rules governing the number of bears that may 
40 be hunted and trapped in a season, which may not be more than one by trapping or 2 in 
41 total.
Page 4 - 130LR0099(01)1 6.  It establishes a daily bag limit of one bear by hunting.
2 7.  It provides that, beginning January 1, 2022, to obtain a bear trapping permit, a person 
3 must either have successfully completed a bear trapping education course or have 
4 previously held a valid Maine bear trapping permit in any year prior to 2022.
5 8.  It provides that a resident may hunt bear without a valid permit during the open 
6 firearm season on deer.
7 9.  It allows a person who holds a license authorizing hunting big game to obtain a bear 
8 trapping permit.
9 10.  It provides that a bear hunting permit is required for each bear when the bag limit 
10 on bear is more than one.
