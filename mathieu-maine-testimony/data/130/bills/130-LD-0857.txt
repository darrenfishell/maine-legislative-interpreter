2Sec. 1.  5 MRSA §13073-C is enacted to read:
3§13073-C.  Municipal Grant Fund
4 The Municipal Grant Fund is established as a nonlapsing fund within the Office of 
5 Community Development.  The Director of the Office of Community Development shall 
6 administer the Municipal Grant Fund, referred to in this section as "the fund."
71.  Fund purpose.  The purpose of the fund is to provide funding for municipalities 
8 for projects that further the goals of sustainable economic development as outlined by the 
9 Maine Economic Growth Council, established in Title 10, section 929-A and referred to in 
10 this section as "the council," in the council's annual "Measures of Growth" report, or 
11 successor report, and by the economic development strategy for the State as administered 
12 by the department.
132.  Application process.  The department shall adopt rules establishing an application 
14 process for municipalities for fund grants for the purposes set forth in this section.
153.  Competitive procedure.  Funds must be dispersed in accordance with a 
16 competitive, quality-based selection procedure as established and administered by the 
17 department.
184.  Preference in awards.  In awarding grants, the department shall give preference to 
19 those projects that further the goals of the council as outlined in the council's "Measures of 
20 Growth" report, or successor report, as well as goals related to the economic development 
21 strategy for the State as administered by the department.
225.  Maximum award.  A grantee may not be awarded a total amount in excess of 
23 $50,000 in a legislative biennium.
246.  Rules.  The department shall adopt rules necessary to carry out this section.  Rules 
25 adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical rules as defined in chapter 375, 
26 subchapter 2-A.

Page 2 - 130LR1810(01)1SUMMARY
2 This bill establishes a fund to provide competitive grants funding for cities and towns 
3 for projects that further the goals of sustainable economic development as outlined by the 
4 Maine Economic Growth Council in the council's annual "Measures of Growth" report and 
5 by the economic development strategy for the State as administered by the Department of 
6 Economic and Community Development.  The bill provides for the transfer of $250,000 in 
7 fiscal year 2021-22 from state-municipal revenue sharing to support the fund.2
