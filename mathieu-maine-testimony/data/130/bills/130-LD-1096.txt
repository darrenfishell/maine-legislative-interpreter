41.  Rules and orders; processes and procedures.  Notwithstanding any other 
5 provision of law, the Supreme Judicial Court may adopt rules and issue orders to permit or 
6 require the use of electronic forms, filings, records, e-mail and electronic signatures 
7 whenever paper forms, filings, records, written notice, postal mail and written signatures 
8 are required for judicial, legal or any other court-related process under the Maine Revised 
9 Statutes.
10 The Supreme Judicial Court, by rule, may determine any other processes or procedures 
11 appropriate to ensure adequate preservation, disposition, integrity, security, appropriate 
12 accessibility and confidentiality of the electronic records.  After the effective date of the 
13 rules as adopted or amended, all laws in conflict with the rules are of no further effect.
14SUMMARY
15 This bill clarifies the rule-making authority of the Supreme Judicial Court regarding 
16 rules concerning processes and procedures related to the filing of electronic records.  The 
17 bill adds a provision that nullifies laws that conflict with duly adopted rules.15
