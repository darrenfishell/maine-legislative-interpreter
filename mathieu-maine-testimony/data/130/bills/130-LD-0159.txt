7Sec. 3.  Effective date; certification. This Act does not take effect unless, within 
8 60 days of the adjournment of the First Regular Session of the 130th Legislature, the 
9 Secretary of State receives written certification by the Tribal Chief and Council of the 
10 Penobscot Nation and the Joint Tribal Council of the Passamaquoddy Tribe that the nation

12 Section 1725(e), copies of which must be submitted by the Secretary of State to the 
13 Secretary of the Senate, the Clerk of the House of Representatives and the Revisor of 
14 Statutes; except that in no event may this Act become effective until 90 days after the 
15 adjournment of the First Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
16SUMMARY
17 This bill extends all time limits for both the Passamaquoddy Tribe and the Penobscot 
18 Nation to add to their respective trust lands under the Act to Implement the Maine Indian 
19 Claims Settlement to January 31, 2030.
20 Because this bill amends the Act to Implement the Maine Indian Claims Settlement, 
21 this bill does not take effect unless the Joint Tribal Council of the Passamaquoddy Tribe 
22 and the Tribal Chief and Council of the Penobscot Nation agree to these changes and certify 
23 their agreement to the Secretary of State within 60 days of the adjournment of the First 
24 Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.1
