Page 1 - 130LR0890(01)1Sec. 1.  Convene working group. Resolved: That the Department of Corrections 
2 shall convene a working group, referred to in this resolve as "the working group," to review 
3 the use of the following terms in the Maine Revised Statutes and executive branch rules, 
4 forms, policies and publications: "convict," "offender," "inmate," "prisoner" and "drug 
5 user," collectively referred to in this resolve as "the terminology."
6Sec. 2.  Membership. Resolved: That the commissioners of the Department of 
7 Corrections, the Department of Public Safety, the Department of Agriculture, Conservation 
8 and Forestry and the Department of Health and Human Services shall each appoint at least 
9 one person as a member of the working group.  A member of the public with lived 
10 experience of incarceration, recovery or neurodivergence must be selected by the members 
11 and invited to assist the working group.
12Sec. 3.  Duties. Resolved: That the working group shall:
13 1. Identify in the Maine Revised Statutes and executive branch rules, forms, policies 
14 and publications each instance where the use of the terminology is stigmatizing;
15 2. Identify the terms to be used in place of the stigmatizing terminology; 
16 3. In selecting replacement terms, consider whether the following terms are appropriate 
17 replacements:
18 A. "Person who has committed a crime";
19 B. "Resident"; or
20 C. "Person who uses drugs";
21 4. Ensure that any proposed language changes are not in conflict with or prohibited by 
22 federal laws or regulations;
23 5. Ensure that any proposed language changes do not alter or render ambiguous the 
24 substantive meaning of any statute or any executive branch rules, forms, policies or 
25 publications;
26 6. Propose standards regarding the terminology to be used by the Revisor of Statutes 
27 when preparing legislation; and
28 7. Propose standards regarding the terminology to be used by executive branch 
29 agencies when drafting or amending rules, forms, policies and publications.
30Sec. 4. Report and recommendations. Resolved: That, by December 1, 2021, 
31 the Department of Corrections shall submit a written report to the Joint Standing 
32 Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety that includes the findings and 
33 recommendations of the working group, including any necessary implementing legislation. 
34Sec. 5.  Authority for legislation. Resolved: That, after receipt and review of the 
35 report and recommendations submitted pursuant to section 4, the Joint Standing Committee 
36 on Criminal Justice and Public Safety may submit legislation based on the report to the 
37 Second Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
38SUMMARY
39 This resolve requires the Department of Corrections to convene a working group to 
40 identify when the following terms are used in a stigmatizing manner in the Maine Revised 39
