18Sec. 2.  12 MRSA §10657-A is enacted to read:
19§10657-A.  Civil trespass with hunting dog
201.  Definitions.  For the purposes of this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, 
21 the following terms have the following meanings.
22 A.  "Dog handler" means a person involved in releasing a dog to pursue a wild animal, 
23 including but not limited to the owner of the dog and any person involved in 
24 transporting, loading, unloading, catching, restraining, releasing or tracking the dog.
25 B.  ''Turn a hunting dog loose" means to release or to be involved as a dog handler in 
26 the release of a dog to pursue a wild animal for the purposes of hunting the wild animal 
27 or in order to train the dog in hunting wild animals and includes but is not limited to 
28 the release of the dog to join other dogs already in pursuit of the wild animal.
292.  Prohibition.  While engaging in any activity regulated under this Part, a person may 
30 not turn a hunting dog loose in pursuit of a bear, coyote, bobcat, fox or raccoon:
31 A.  Onto the property of another if:
32 (1)  The landowner or the landowner's agent has personally communicated to a dog 
33 handler of the dog that the landowner does not want a hunting dog released onto 
34 that property; or
35 (2)  The property is posted in accordance with Title 17-A, section 402, subsection 
36 4 or in a manner reasonably likely to come to the attention of a person, unless a 
37 dog handler of the dog has express permission from the landowner or the 
38 landowner's agent to turn a hunting dog loose onto that property; or
39 B.  In a manner that results in the hunting dog entering the property of another, if a 
40 hunting dog has been previously found on that property and any dog handler of the 
41 hunting dog has been notified in writing by a law enforcement officer within the 
42 previous 365 days that the landowner does not permit hunting dogs on that property.

38SUMMARY
39 This bill, which is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Inland Fisheries 
40 and Wildlife pursuant to Resolve 2021, chapter 77, does the following.39
40
Page 4 - 130LR2600(01)1 1. It requires a person to obtain a permit to train a hunting dog on bear, fox or raccoon 
2 or hunt bear, coyote, bobcat, fox or raccoon with a dog. It exempts from the permit 
3 requirement a person with a junior hunting license and persons hunting or training with a 
4 guide. It provides for penalties for failure to have a permit and provides that permit fees are 
5 deposited into the Landowner Relations Fund.
6 2. It requires a person training a dog in pursuit of a bear, fox or raccoon to have a collar 
7 on the dog with a functioning global positioning system tracker and a collar that legibly 
8 provides the name, telephone number and address of the owner of that dog.
9 3. It requires a person hunting with a dog in pursuit of a bear, coyote, bobcat, fox or 
10 raccoon to have a collar on the dog that has a functioning global positioning system tracker 
11 and adds foxes and raccoons to the list of wild animals a person may not hunt with a dog 
12 unless the dog has a collar that legibly provides the name, telephone number and address 
13 of the owner of the dog.
14 4. It limits hunting foxes or raccoons with dogs to not more than 6 dogs. 
15 5. It prohibits certain types of trespass by a hunting dog. A person may not turn a 
16 hunting dog loose in pursuit of a bear, coyote, bobcat, fox or raccoon: 
17 A. Onto the property of another if the property is posted or if the landowner has notified 
18 the dog handler that hunting dogs are not allowed on the property; or
19 B. In a manner that results in the hunting dog entering the property of another, if a 
20 hunting dog has been previously found on that property and any dog handler of the 
21 hunting dog has been notified in writing by a law enforcement officer within the 
22 previous 365 days that the landowner does not permit hunting dogs on the property.
