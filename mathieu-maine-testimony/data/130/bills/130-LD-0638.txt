51.  Definition.  For the purposes of this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, 
6 "unofficial election results" means the number of votes cast in a ward or precinct as 
7 declared by the warden pursuant to section 695, subsection 3 in an election other than an 
8 election determined by ranked-choice voting with respect to each state or municipal 
9 question that appeared on a ballot in that ward or precinct and for each candidate, including 
10 each declared write-in candidate pursuant to section 722-A, for a federal, state, county or 
11 municipal office that appeared on the ballot in that ward or precinct.  For an election 
12 determined by ranked-choice voting, "unofficial election results" means the results of the 
13 first choice votes cast for each candidate, including each declared write-in candidate 
14 pursuant to section 722-A.
152.  Posting of unofficial election results.  As soon as practicable after declaring the 
16 results pursuant to section 695, subsection 3, the warden shall:
17 A. Post a paper copy of the unofficial election results at the voting place or at the 
18 municipal office in a secure place where the public may view that copy of the unofficial 
19 election results even when the voting place or municipal office is closed; and
20 B. Electronically transmit the unofficial election results to the Secretary of State in a 
21 format approved by the Secretary of State.
223.  Secretary of State duties.  The Secretary of State shall:
23 A.  Immediately post unofficial election results received pursuant to subsection 2, 
24 paragraph B on the Secretary of State's publicly accessible website;
25 B.  Upon receipt of an attested copy of an election return pursuant to section 711, 
26 subsection 3, correct any errors in the unofficial election results posted on the Secretary 
27 of State's publicly accessible website pursuant to paragraph A; and
28 C.  Develop and implement a statewide public awareness program about the 
29 availability of unofficial election results under this section.
304.  Acceptance of funds.  The Secretary of State may accept gifts, donations and grants 
31 from any private or public source, in addition to money appropriated or allocated by the 
32 State, for the purposes of implementing this section.
33SUMMARY
34 This bill requires, as soon as practicable after the election results are tabulated, the 
35 posting of unofficial election results for each municipality outside either the municipal 
36 office or each voting place in the municipality as well as on the Secretary of State's publicly 
37 accessible website.34
