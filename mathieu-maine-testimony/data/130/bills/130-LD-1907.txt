18§1809.  Commission To Review State-owned Lands and Waterways
191.  Established.  The Commission To Review State-owned Lands and Waterways, 
20 referred to in this section as "the commission," is established.
212.  Members.  The members of the commission are as follows:
22 A.  One member representing the bureau, appointed by the commissioner;
23 B.  One member representing the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife, 
24 appointed by the Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife;
25 C.  One member representing the Department of Marine Resources, appointed by the 
26 Commissioner of Marine Resources;
27 D.  One member representing the Passamaquoddy Tribe, appointed by the 
28 Passamaquoddy Tribe;
29 E.  One member representing the Penobscot Nation, appointed by the Penobscot 
30 Nation;
31 F.  One member representing the Houlton Band of Maliseet Indians, appointed by the 
32 Houlton Band of Maliseet Indians; and
33 G.  One member representing the Aroostook Band of Micmacs, appointed by the 
34 Aroostook Band of Micmacs.
353.  Duties.  The commission shall:
36 A.  Identify which state-owned lands or waterways have sacred, traditional or other 
37 significance to the Passamaquoddy Tribe, Penobscot Nation, Houlton Band of Maliseet 
38 Indians or Aroostook Band of Micmacs;15

2 lands and waterways to tribes and develop a process to return lands and waterways to 
3 the Passamaquoddy Tribe, Penobscot Nation, Houlton Band of Maliseet Indians and 
4 Aroostook Band of Micmacs.  The commission also shall identify resources to fund the 
5 cost to transfer these lands and waterways to the Passamaquoddy Tribe, Penobscot

7 C.  Make recommendations to the Legislature, including recommendations regarding 
8 additional members who should be added to the commission.
94.  Meetings.  The commission shall hold public meetings and hearings throughout the 
10 State and shall accept testimony and written comments from the public.  The commission 
11 shall invite the participation of stakeholders as determined by the commission and also 
12 invite the participation of large private landowners, land trusts and conservation 
13 organizations.
145.  Report.  The commission shall submit a report no later than November 15th of each 
15 year to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
16 conservation matters, the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction 
17 over inland fisheries and wildlife matters and the joint standing committee of the 
18 Legislature having jurisdiction over marine resources matters.  The joint standing 
19 committees may report out legislation related to the report of the commission.
20Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
21 takes effect when approved.
22SUMMARY
23 This bill establishes the Commission To Review State-owned Lands and Waterways 
24 to identify which state-owned lands or waterways have sacred, traditional or other 
25 significance to the Passamaquoddy Tribe, Penobscot Nation, Houlton Band of Maliseet 
26 Indians or Aroostook Band of Micmacs and develop a process to return lands and 
27 waterways to the Passamaquoddy Tribe, Penobscot Nation, Houlton Band of Maliseet 
28 Indians and Aroostook Band of Micmacs.  The commission must hold public meetings and 
29 hearings throughout the State and must accept testimony and written comments from the 
30 public.  The commission must invite the participation of stakeholders as determined by the 
31 commission and also invite the participation of large private landowners, land trusts and 
32 conservation organizations.  The commission must submit a report no later than November 
33 15th of each year to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
34 conservation matters, the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction 
35 over inland fisheries and wildlife matters and the joint standing committee of the 
36 Legislature having jurisdiction over marine resources matters.  The joint standing 
37 committees may report out legislation related to the report of the commission.23
