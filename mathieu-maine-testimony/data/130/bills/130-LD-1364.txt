4Sec. 2.  Commission membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
5 353, the commission consists of 16 members appointed as follows:
6 1.  Two members of the Senate, appointed by the President of the Senate;
7 2.  Four members of the House of Representatives, appointed by the Speaker of the 
8 House of Representatives;
9 3.  One member recommended by the State Fire Marshal, appointed jointly by the 
10 President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives;
11 4.  Two members of a statewide association of professional firefighters, appointed 
12 jointly by the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives;
13 5.  One member of a statewide association of builders and contractors, appointed jointly 
14 by the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives;
15 6.  One member of a statewide association of real estate agents, appointed jointly by 
16 the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives;
17 7.  Two members of a statewide association of insurance agents, appointed jointly by 
18 the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives;
19 8.  One member of a statewide association of fire sprinkler contractors, appointed 
20 jointly by the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives;
21 9.  One member of the Maine Community College System Board of Trustees, appointed 
22 jointly by the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives; and
23 10.  One member of a statewide association of water districts, appointed jointly by the 
24 President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives.
25Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named member from the Senate and the 
26 first-named member from the House of Representatives shall serve as cochairs of the 
27 commission.
28Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission; number of meetings. 
29Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, all appointments must be made no later 
30 than 30 days following the effective date of this resolve.  The appointing authorities shall 
31 notify the Executive Director of the Legislative Council once all appointments have been 
32 completed. After appointment of all members, the chairs shall call and convene the first 
33 meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more after the effective date of this resolve a 
34 majority of but not all appointments have been made, the chairs may request authority and 
35 the Legislative Council may grant authority for the commission to meet and conduct its 
36 business.  The commission may not meet more than 6 times.
37Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall study whether it is feasible 
38 and desirable to provide incentives for developers, builders and home buyers to install 
39 residential sprinklers and, if so, what form should the incentives take, who shall deliver the 
40 incentives and how should the incentives be advertised and delivered.
Page 2 - 130LR0861(01)1Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
2 necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff support 
3 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
4Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 1, 2021, the commission 
5 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
6 legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety.  The 
7 Joint Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety may submit legislation 
8 based upon the report to the Second Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
9SUMMARY
10 This resolve creates the Commission To Study and Recommend Incentives for 
11 Residential Fire Sprinkler Systems.  The commission has 16 members.  The commission is 
12 required to submit its report to the Joint Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and Public 
13 Safety no later than December 1, 2021.10
