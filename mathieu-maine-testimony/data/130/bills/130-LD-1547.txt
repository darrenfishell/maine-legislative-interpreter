7§5107-K.  Age-friendly State Steering Committee
81.  Steering committee established.  The Age-friendly State Steering Committee, as 
9 established in Title 5, section 12004-I, subsection 2-H and referred to in this section as "the 
10 steering committee," is established to promote intergovernmental coordination to improve 
11 opportunities for and access to social engagement, communication and information, 
12 employment, financial security, health care and health coverage, housing, recreation and 
13 transportation for older residents of the State.
142.  Membership.  The steering committee consists of the following 11 members:
15 A.  The commissioner or the commissioner's designee;
16 B.  The Commissioner of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry or the commissioner's 
17 designee;
18 C.  The Commissioner of Transportation or the commissioner's designee;
19 D.  The Commissioner of Labor or the commissioner's designee;
20 E.  The Commissioner of Economic and Community Development or the 
21 commissioner's designee;
22 F.  The Commissioner of Public Safety or the commissioner's designee;
23 G.  The Commissioner of Education or the commissioner's designee;
24 H.  A representative of the Governor;
25 I.  The director of the Maine State Housing Authority or the director's designee;
26 J.  A representative of the office for family independence within the department 
27 appointed by the commissioner; and
28 K.  A representative of the office of aging and disability services within the department 
29 appointed by the commissioner.
303.  Duties.  The steering committee shall:
31 A.  Promote coordinated intergovernmental collaboration in meeting the State's policy 
32 objectives related to older adults and aging in the State, including objectives designed 
33 to:
34 (1)  Promote respect, equity and social engagement so people of all ages can 
35 actively participate in their community;
36 (2)  Ensure access to information, community services, activities and programs;
37 (3)  Expand workforce opportunities, retirement education and financial 
38 protection;4
5
6
Page 2 - 130LR1594(01)1 (4)  Ensure affordable access to high-quality health coverage and health care and 
2 healthy aging and supportive services to maintain independence;
3 (5)  Ensure the availability of housing opportunities and home modification 
4 programs;
5 (6)  Promote natural resources management, outdoor spaces and recreation; and
6 (7)  Increase the availability of safe and affordable transportation options and 
7 alternatives to reduce social isolation, support independent living and allow adults 
8 to age in their communities; and
9 B.  Review and provide advice to the Governor, the Legislature and other officials of 
10 State Government on state efforts across state agencies directly related to older adults 
11 and aging in the State.
124.  Steering committee invitees.  The steering committee may invite members of a 
13 stakeholder advisory group that participated in the preparation of an age-friendly state plan 
14 to participate in steering committee meetings.
155.  Staff support.  The office of aging and disability services within the department 
16 shall provide primary staff support to the steering committee.  State agencies shall provide 
17 additional staff support, which may include persons working as interns, as requested by the 
18 steering committee.
196.  Funding.  The steering committee may accept funding received as contributions, 
20 grants or appropriations from private and public sources to accomplish the duties of the 
21 steering committee.
227.  Report.  By November 1, 2023 and every 2 years thereafter, the steering committee 
23 shall submit a report on the steering committee's activities to the joint standing committee 
24 of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters.
25SUMMARY
26 This bill establishes the Age-friendly State Steering Committee to promote 
27 intergovernmental coordination to improve opportunities for and access to social 
28 engagement, communication and information, employment, financial security, health care 
29 and health coverage, housing, recreation and transportation for older residents of the State.26
