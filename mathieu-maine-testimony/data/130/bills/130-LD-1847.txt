7 The following provisions apply to termination or disconnection of a public safety

91. Termination.  A public utility may not terminate or disconnect a public safety 
10 facility's utility service unless the public utility has:
11 A. Provided written notice to the governmental entity using the public safety facility 
12 of the public utility's intention to terminate or disconnect the facility's utility service at 
13 least 60 days before the termination or disconnection date;
14 B. Obtained from the commission written authorization to terminate or disconnect the 
15 public safety facility's utility service; and
16 C. Obtained from the Department of Public Safety written authorization to terminate 
17 or disconnect the public safety facility's utility service.
182. Rules. The commission may adopt rules to implement this section.  Rules adopted 
19 pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, 
20 subchapter 2-A.  In adopting rules pursuant to this subsection, the commission shall consult 
21 with the Department of Public Safety and a statewide organization representing municipal 
22 interests in the State.
23SUMMARY
24 This bill requires a public utility, before it terminates or disconnects a public safety 
25 facility's utility service, to provide 60 days' advance written notice to the public safety 
26 facility of its intent to terminate or disconnect utility service and to obtain written 
27 authorization from the Public Utilities Commission and the Department of Public Safety to 
28 terminate or disconnect the utility service.24
