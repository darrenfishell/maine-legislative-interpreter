3Sec. 1.  Maine Human Rights Commission pilot program to investigate and 
4report on incidents and complaints of harassment due to housing status, lack 
5of employment and other issues.  Resolved:  That the Maine Human Rights 
6 Commission shall, within budgeted resources, implement a 2-year 4-year pilot program to 
7 receive, review and investigate incidents and complaints of harassment due to a person's 
8 lack of employment or housing status and other reports of interference with a person's 
9 access to public accommodations.
10 In carrying out the pilot program, the commission shall investigate and respond to 
11 incidents and complaints of harassment as set out in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, 
12 sections 4611 and 4612.  The commission may use any of its powers under Title 5, section 
13 4566 to carry out the pilot program.  The commission may limit the scope of the pilot 
14 program in the interest of efficiency.
15 The commission shall produce an interim report for submission to the Joint Standing 
16 Committee on Judiciary by September 15, 2021 2022.  The commission shall produce a 
17 final report for submission to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
18 jurisdiction over judiciary matters by September 15, 2022 2023.  Either report may contain 
19 recommendations on changes to the pilot program or for its continuation as well as 
20 suggested legislation to carry out any of the recommendations.
21Sec. 2.  Retroactivity.  Resolved:  That this resolve is retroactive to June 16, 2020.
22SUMMARY
23 This resolve extends by 2 years the 2-year Maine Human Rights Commission pilot 
24 program to receive, review and investigate incidents and complaints of harassment due to 
25 a person's housing status and other reports of interference with a person's access to public 
26 accommodations and extends the deadlines for its reports.  The resolve is retroactive to 
27 June 16, 2020, the effective date of Resolve 2019, chapter 113.23
