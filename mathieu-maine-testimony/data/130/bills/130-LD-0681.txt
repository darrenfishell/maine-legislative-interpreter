2Sec. 1.  30-A MRSA §3801, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2013, c. 209, §1, is further

41.  Innkeeper.  "Innkeeper" means a person who keeps an inn, hotel or motel to provide 
5 lodging to travelers and others for compensation and who maintains the sleeping

8Sec. 2.  30-A MRSA §3803 is enacted to read:
9§3803.  Guests are not tenants; lodging facility owners are not landlords
101.  Not a landlord.  An innkeeper, lodging house keeper, boardinghouse keeper, 
11 rooming house keeper, restaurant owner or campground operator is not a landlord under 
12 the landlord and tenant laws in Title 14 but is subject to the provisions of this subchapter.
132.  Not a tenant.  A guest of an inn, hotel, motel, lodging house, boardinghouse, 
14 rooming house, restaurant or campground is not a tenant under the landlord and tenant laws 
15 in Title 14 but is subject to the provisions of this subchapter.
163.  License as proof.  A license issued by a licensing board under this chapter to an 
17 inn, hotel, motel, lodging house, boardinghouse, rooming house, restaurant or campground 
18 is considered conclusive proof of the facility's standing as such for purposes of determining 
19 whether a guest and lodging facility relationship exists for purposes of removal procedures 
20 under this subchapter and not a landlord and tenant relationship for purposes of forcible 
21 entry and detainer procedures under Title 14.
22Sec. 3.  30-A MRSA §3821, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2013, c. 209, §2, is further 
23 amended to read:
241.  Register of guests.  Every person conducting a hotel or lodging house shall have a 
25 register kept and maintained in the hotel or lodging house at all times.  The name of every 
26 guest or person renting a room or rooms in the hotel or lodging house must be written in 
27 the register.  The person renting the room or rooms, or someone under that person's 
28 direction, shall sign the register.  The proprietor of the hotel or lodging house, or the 
29 proprietor's agent, shall then write the number of each room assigned to each guest, together 
30 with the date that room is rented, opposite the name or names so registered.  A guest of a 
31 hotel or lodging house is not a tenant pursuant to the landlord and tenant laws as provided 
32 in Title 14.
33SUMMARY
34 This bill clarifies that lodging facilities and their guests do not have a landlord and 
35 tenant relationship and that removal of a guest is not subject to the forcible entry and 
36 detainer procedures under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 14.34
