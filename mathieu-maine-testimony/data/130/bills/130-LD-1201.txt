Page 1 - 130LR1612(01)1Sec. 1.  Fair chance housing examination. Resolved:  That the Maine State 
2 Housing Authority shall in collaboration with stakeholders examine fair chance housing 
3 policies considered or adopted in other jurisdictions. This examination must consider 
4 policies that may be adopted in the State to address housing issues for formerly incarcerated 
5 individuals and to expand housing opportunities for formerly incarcerated individuals 
6 without creating an excessive burden for housing providers. 
7 The authority shall invite stakeholders that include but are not limited to:
8 1.  Formerly incarcerated individuals;
9 2.  Housing providers that own, lease or manage residential housing units that:
10 A.  Are owned or subsidized by the Federal Government or the State; or
11 B.  Are financed in whole or part by government subsidies or tax credits; 
12 3.  Landlords or representatives of statewide organizations representing landowners 
13 and property managers;
14 4.  Representatives from a statewide organization that represents the interests of 
15 currently incarcerated or formerly incarcerated individuals;
16 5.  Representatives from a statewide civil legal aid and economic justice organization;
17 6.  Representatives of civil rights organizations whose primary mission is racial equity 
18 and justice; and
19 7.  Representatives of organizations that advocate for residents of the State in the 
20 following areas:
21 A.  Disability rights;
22 B.  Mental health;
23 C.  Substance use disorder and recovery; and
24 D.  Homelessness.
25Sec. 2. Report. Resolved:  That no later than February 15, 2022 the Maine State 
26 Housing Authority shall submit a report that includes findings and recommendations to the 
27 Joint Standing Committee on Labor and Housing.  The report must include a summary of 
28 any meetings that occur pursuant to section 1, a list of participants in the examination of 
29 fair chance housing policies, suggestions to increase the awareness among housing 
30 providers of the challenges faced by formerly incarcerated individuals in finding housing 
31 and any other relevant information. The report may include suggested legislation.  The 
32 Joint Standing Committee on Labor and Housing may report out legislation to the Second 
33 Regular Session of the 130th Legislature based on the report. 
34SUMMARY
35 This resolve directs the Maine State Housing Authority in collaboration with 
36 stakeholders to examine fair chance housing policies considered or adopted in other 
37 jurisdictions to evaluate options for the State to adopt that would address housing issues 
38 and expand housing opportunities for formerly incarcerated individuals without placing an 
39 excessive burden on housing providers.  It requires the authority to submit a report to the 
40 Joint Standing Committee on Labor and Housing that includes findings and 35
