Page 1 - 130LR1200(01)1Sec. 1.  Expanding workforce training options in Waldo County; 
2evaluation.  Resolved:  That the Maine Community College System shall evaluate the 
3 unmet need and capacity for expanding workforce training options in Waldo County with 
4 the goal of providing additional capacity for training in the most accessible platform 
5 possible in industries with identified employee shortages.  In conducting the evaluation, 
6 the Maine Community College System shall examine the following industries and trades:
7 1.  Nursing or other medical trades;
8 2.  Composites manufacturing;
9 3.  Carpentry;
10 4.  Electrical trades;
11 5.  Millwright trades; and
12 6.  Ventilation, air conditioning, refrigeration, plumbing and heating.
13 The Maine Community College System shall use its established methods and practices 
14 in assessing the need for expanded workforce training options in Waldo County.
15Sec. 2.  Strategies for expanding workforce training options.  Resolved:  
16 That, if the Maine Community College System, while conducting the study in section 1, 
17 finds that a need for workforce training options exists in any of the industries and trades 
18 examined, the Maine Community College System shall assess the best way to increase 
19 capacity and opportunity.  Potential strategies for the consideration include but are not 
20 limited to:
21 1.  Expanding access through Maine Quality Centers under the Maine Revised Statutes, 
22 Title 20-A, section 12727;
23 2.  Long-distance learning options; and
24 3.  Colocation or collaboration with other learning institutions.
25Sec. 3.  Report.  Resolved:  That, by December 1, 2021, the Maine Community 
26 College System shall submit a report based on its evaluation in section 1 identifying if and 
27 where needs exist and outlining the best options to meet those needs to the Joint Standing 
28 Committee on Innovation, Development, Economic Advancement and Business.  The 
29 report, including suggested legislation, must include but is not limited to:
30 1.  What unmet workforce training needs exist;
31 2.  What industries should be prioritized for expanded workforce training options;
32 3.  The best methods for expanding workforce training options;
33 4.  Costs and facility needs for expanding workforce training programming; and
34 5.  A timeline for implementation of expanded workforce training options.
35 The Joint Standing Committee on Innovation, Development, Economic Advancement 
36 and Business is authorized to submit legislation related to the report to the Second Regular 
37 Session of the 130th Legislature.
