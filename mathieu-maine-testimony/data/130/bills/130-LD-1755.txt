33SUMMARY
34 This bill makes the following changes to the laws governing the ombudsman program 
35 that provides ombudsman services to the children and families of the State regarding child 
36 welfare services provided by the Department of Health and Human Services.
37 1. It provides that the program is an independent program operated as a nonprofit 
38 organization to provide investigative, oversight and advocacy services on a statewide basis.
39 2. It requires the program to provide oversight of child welfare practice and policy to 
40 ensure the rights and safety of children and their families.
41 3. It specifies staffing for the program.34
35
36
Page 4 - 130LR2119(01)1 4. It provides that the Governor appoints the ombudsman, subject to review by the joint 
2 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services 
3 matters and to confirmation by the Legislature.
4 5. It provides that the ombudsman serves a 5-year term of office.
5 6. It requires the ombudsman to submit the ombudsman's budget recommendations, 
6 using a format prescribed by the State Budget Officer, as part of the unified current services 
7 budget legislation.
8 7. It provides full-time program employees access to health insurance benefits provided 
9 to state employees and retirement benefits provided to state employees.
10 8. It requires the program to provide services directly to individuals and families and 
11 authorizes the program to provide system-wide comment to the department and the joint 
12 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services 
13 matters.
14 9. It requires the department to inform the ombudsman of any statewide policy or 
15 practice changes in child welfare before they take effect.
16 10. It allows the ombudsman to participate in trainings, studies or policy development 
17 activities conducted by the department.
18 11. It requires the ombudsman to be available to provide information about child 
19 welfare issues on a statewide basis to individuals and entities outside the department.
20 12. It authorizes the ombudsman to disclose confidential information, records or case-
21 specific reports to a joint standing committee, joint select committee or oversight 
22 committee of the Legislature meeting in executive session.
23 13. It provides that the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction 
24 over government oversight matters along with the joint standing committee of the 
25 Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters, as in current law, 
26 shall review the operations of the ombudsman program and authorizes the committees to 
27 submit legislation.
28 This bill also includes the ombudsman as a member of the child death and serious 
29 injury review panel established by the Department of Health and Human Services and 
30 requires that the panel, beginning January 1, 2023 and every 2 years thereafter, submit a 
31 report on the panel’s recommendations to the joint standing committee having jurisdiction 
32 over health and human services matters.
