3ยง3050.  Comprehensive substance use disorder treatment program
4 The commissioner shall maintain a comprehensive substance use disorder treatment 
5 program, referred to in this section as "the program," in all correctional facilities.  The 
6 program must include, but is not limited to, screening, assessment and treatment of persons 
7 residing in correctional facilities for substance use disorder, including alcohol use disorder.
81.  Screening, assessment and treatment.  The program must include screening, 
9 assessment and treatment, including, but not limited to, screening during the intake process, 
10 medically managed withdrawal, medication-assisted treatment, individual and group 
11 counseling and other behavioral treatment options.  Medication-assisted treatment must use 
12 medications approved or authorized by the United States Food and Drug Administration 
13 for the treatment of substance use disorder, including alcohol use disorder, including at 
14 least one of each formulation of all United States Food and Drug Administration-approved 
15 medication-assisted treatments for those disorders.
162.  Training and technical assistance.  The program must provide initial and ongoing 
17 training and technical assistance for correctional facility staff and health care practitioners 
18 in each correctional facility.
193.  Program coordination.  The program must include coordination with community-
20 based treatment and recovery organizations to facilitate supportive reentry and continuity 
21 of care after release.
224.  Report.  The department shall provide an annual report regarding the program to 
23 the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over criminal justice and 
24 public safety matters by January 15th.
25SUMMARY
26 This bill is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and 
27 Public Safety pursuant to Resolve 2021, chapter 113 and is supported by a majority of the 
28 committee.  The bill directs the Commissioner of Corrections to maintain a comprehensive 
29 substance use disorder treatment program in all state correctional facilities.
