11§26003.  Prohibitions concerning sharing and use of information; compliance with 
12federal law and law enforcement
131.  Restricting other government entity from sending or receiving citizenship or 
14immigration status information.  A government entity may not prohibit or in any way 
15 restrict any other government entity from sending to or receiving from the Department of 
16 Homeland Security information regarding the lawful or unlawful citizenship or 
17 immigration status of any individual.
182.  Restrictions concerning immigration status information.  With respect to 
19 information regarding the lawful or unlawful immigration status of an individual, a 
20 government entity may not prohibit or in any way restrict:
21 A.  Sending such information to or requesting or receiving such information from the 
22 Department of Homeland Security;
23 B.  Maintaining such information;
24 C.  Exchanging such information with another government entity or a federal agency;
25 D.  Using such information to determine eligibility for a public benefit, service or 
26 license provided by federal law or a law of the State or its political subdivisions;
27 E.  Using such information to verify a claim of residence or domicile if a determination 
28 of residence or domicile is required under federal law or a law of the State or its 
29 political subdivisions or under a judicial order issued pursuant to a civil or criminal 
30 proceeding in the State; or
31 F.  Using such information to confirm the identity of a person who is detained by a law 
32 enforcement agency.
333.  Compliance with and support for federal immigration laws.  A government 
34 entity shall fully comply with and to the full extent permitted by law support the 
35 enforcement of federal immigration law.
364.  Enforcement of federal immigration laws.  A government entity may not limit or 
37 restrict the enforcement of federal immigration law, including, but not limited to, limiting 
38 or restricting a government entity from complying with an immigration detainer, limiting 
39 or restricting a government entity from providing a federal immigration official access to 
40 an inmate for an interview, limiting or restricting a government entity from initiating an 
41 immigration status investigation or limiting or restricting a government entity from 
42 providing a federal immigration official with the incarceration status or release date of an 
43 inmate in custody of a government entity.13

Page 3 - 130LR1768(01)1§26004.  Complaint process; appeals
21.  Resident complaints and Legislator requests submitted to Attorney General.  
3 A resident of the State may submit a complaint to the Attorney General asserting a violation 
4 of section 26003 by a government entity.  The complaint must be in writing and in a form 
5 and manner as prescribed by the Attorney General. A member of the Legislature may 
6 request that the Attorney General investigate and issue an opinion as to whether a 
7 government entity has violated section 26003.
82.  Attorney General investigation.  After receiving a complaint or request under 
9 subsection 1, the Attorney General shall investigate and determine whether a violation of 
10 section 26003 has occurred. If after the investigation the Attorney General determines that 
11 a violation of section 26003 has occurred, the Attorney General shall issue an opinion that 
12 a violation has occurred.  The Attorney General shall make the opinion available to the 
13 public.
143.  Review of Attorney General findings.  If the Attorney General issues an opinion 
15 pursuant to subsection 2 that a government entity has violated section 26003, the 
16 government entity has 30 days from the date the opinion was issued to contest the finding 
17 in Superior Court.
18 A.  If the Superior Court affirms the Attorney General's findings, the court shall 
19 immediately enjoin the policy or practice in violation of section 26003.  A government 
20 entity that fails to comply with an injunction is subject to a fine of $500 for each day 
21 the policy or practice of the government entity remains in effect.
22 B.  If the Superior Court overturns the Attorney General's findings, the Attorney 
23 General shall immediately certify that the government entity is in compliance with 
24 section 26003.
25§26005.  Duty to report
26 A state government entity official, local government entity official, law enforcement 
27 agency official or person holding public office shall report a violation of section 26003 by 
28 submitting a complaint to the Attorney General pursuant to section 26004, subsection 1.  A 
29 person who reports a violation under this section is protected under the Whistleblowers' 
30 Protection Act.
31§26006.  Implementation
32 This chapter must be implemented in a manner consistent with federal laws regulating 
33 immigration, protecting the civil rights of all persons and respecting the privileges and 
34 immunities of citizens of the United States.  In complying with the requirements of this 
35 chapter, a government entity may not consider an individual's race, color or national origin, 
36 except to the extent permitted by the United States Constitution and the Constitution of 
37 Maine.
38SUMMARY
39 This bill establishes prohibitions concerning restricting the sharing and use of 
40 immigration and citizenship information.  It prohibits restricting the enforcement of federal 
41 immigration law.  It establishes a complaint process and a duty to report.2
