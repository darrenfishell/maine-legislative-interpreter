11 The state registrar shall enter into a long-term nonexclusive contract with a private entity

14 intentions to marry, domestic partnerships and death, including fetal death, in exchange for 
15 allowing the private entity to also provide that index to its subscribers and customers.  The 
16 online index must include the names of persons appearing on the certificate or record, the 
17 municipality in which the certificate or record was recorded, the date of the event and the 
18 certificate number for the certificate or record number for the record.  The online index 
19 must cover in its entirety the time period specified under this subsection.
20SUMMARY
21 This bill provides that indices to certificates and records of marriage, intentions to 
22 marry, domestic partnerships and death, including fetal death, from 1892 to the present at 
23 the municipal and state levels are open to the public without restriction, and that indices to 
24 certificates and records of birth at the municipal and state levels are open to the public after 
25 a period of 75 years from the date of the birth.  The bill also requires the state registrar to 
26 enter into a long-term nonexclusive contract with a private entity experienced in 
27 maintaining genealogical research databases to create, maintain and update at no direct cost 
28 to the State an online index to certificates and records of birth, marriage, intentions to 
29 marry, domestic partnerships and death, including fetal death, in exchange for allowing the 
30 private entity to also provide that index to its subscribers and customers.21
