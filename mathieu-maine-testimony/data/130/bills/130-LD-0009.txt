142.  Power-to-fuel pilot program; criteria.  The commission shall establish a power-
15 to-fuel pilot program in accordance with this section.  Between January 1, 2022 and 
16 December 31, 2027, the commission shall approve up to 2 power-to-fuel projects under the 
17 program.  In implementing a power-to-fuel pilot program, the commission shall:
18 A.  Limit the production capacity of a power-to-fuel project to 10 megawatts;
19 B.  Prioritize power-to-fuel projects with lower greenhouse gas emissions; and
20 C.  Require that no thermal renewable energy credits may be bought or sold in relation 
21 to fuel produced by a power-to-fuel project.
223.  Exemptions for sales of electricity to a power-to-fuel project.  Sales of electricity 
23 to a power-to-fuel project under the power-to-fuel pilot program are exempt from:
24 A.  Distribution charges regulated by the commission, including but not limited to 
25 volumetric, demand and standby charges;
26 B.  Charges associated with the procurement of energy efficiency resources by 
27 transmission and distribution utilities ordered under section 10110, subsection 4-A; and
28 C.  Renewable portfolio standards requirements under section 3210, subsections 3-A, 
29 3-B and 3-C.
30 The exemptions provided under this subsection begin on the date of operation of a power-
31 to-fuel project and end 15 years after that date, except that the commission may approve 
32 continuation of the exemptions beyond 15 years on the basis of project need.

3SUMMARY
4 This bill requires the Public Utilities Commission to establish and oversee a power-to-
5 fuel pilot program.  The commission is required to approve up to 2 power-to-fuel projects 
6 between January 1, 2022 and December 31, 2027, each up to 10 megawatts in production 
7 capacity, that convert renewable energy to hydrogen gas, methane gas or other fuel. Under 
8 the pilot program, sales of renewable energy to a power-to-fuel project are exempt from 
9 distribution charges, charges associated with the procurement of energy efficiency 
10 resources and renewable portfolio standards requirements for a period of 15 years from the 
11 date of operation of the power-to-fuel project. The bill requires the commission to submit 
12 a report on the pilot program by November 1, 2023 to the joint standing committee of the 
13 Legislature having jurisdiction over energy and utility matters.1
