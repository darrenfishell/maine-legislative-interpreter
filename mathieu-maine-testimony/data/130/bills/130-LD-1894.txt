13Sec. 2.  35-A MRSA §9219 is enacted to read:
14§9219.  Internet access expansion
151. Internet Access Expansion Investment Fund. The Internet Access Expansion 
16 Investment Fund, referred to in this section as “the fund,” is established to expand and 
17 improve Internet access in this State. The fund may receive appropriations made by the 
18 Legislature and may also receive grant funds for which states, counties or municipalities 
19 are eligible. Money received for a county or municipality must be held by the fund and 
20 administered on the county's or municipality's behalf.
21 A. The fund must support projects to:
22 (1) Expand Internet access to parts of the State that do not have access to speeds of 
23 at least 10 megabits per second of download speed and at least one megabit per 
24 second of upload speed to achieve service that meets those thresholds; and 
25 (2) Increase Internet speeds to at least 25 megabits per second of download speed 
26 and at least 3 megabits per second of upload speed for parts of the State that do not 
27 have access to those speeds.
28 B. The fund must give priority to projects that assist state or local ownership of 
29 broadband infrastructure, with at least 50% of funded projects annually meeting that 
30 criterion, and to projects that:
31 (1) Serve areas with demonstrated local project support, including from local 
32 government;
33 (2) Increase Internet access or speeds for hospitals, educational institutions, 
34 libraries or government offices;
35 (3) Demonstrate technical capacity to achieve project goals on a proposed 
36 timetable, preferably within 5 years; and
37 (4) Achieve cost-efficiency by providing the most service improvements for the 
38 least cost, including through leveraging funding sources in addition to the fund.
39 C. The authority shall provide technical assistance to project applicants to plan, prepare 
40 and submit applications to the fund.

11SUMMARY
12 This bill establishes the Internet Access Expansion Investment Fund to expand and 
13 improve Internet access in this State. The fund must give priority to projects that assist state 
14 or local ownership of broadband infrastructure, with at least 50% of funded projects 
15 annually meeting this criterion. The bill allows an electric cooperative or utility, including 
16 a municipal utility, to own, lease, construct, maintain and operate broadband or other 
17 Internet access systems on the electric cooperative's or utility's electric or other utility 
18 delivery system and to provide broadband or other Internet access services to the public on 
19 the electric cooperative's or utility's electric or other utility delivery system.12
