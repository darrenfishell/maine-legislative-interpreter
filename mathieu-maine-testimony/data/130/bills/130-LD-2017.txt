Page 1 - 130LR2659(01)1Sec. 1.  Governor's Energy Office; monitor and report. Resolved: That the 
2 Governor's Energy Office shall monitor efforts undertaken in other states to improve 
3 energy use data standards and to implement multiple-use online energy data platforms that 
4 will provide natural gas and electric utility customers with access to information about their 
5 energy usage and will allow for the aggregation of and removal of personally identifiable 
6 information from community-level energy data. The office shall submit a report, on or 
7 before November 2, 2022, to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
8 jurisdiction over utilities and energy matters including information regarding other states' 
9 efforts and recommendations for how similar efforts could be undertaken in the State. The 
10 committee may report out related legislation to the 131st Legislature in 2023.
11Sec. 2.  Public Utilities Commission; commission evaluation of statewide, 
12multiple-use online energy data platform. Resolved: That the Public Utilities 
13 Commission shall issue a request to transmission and distribution utilities in the State for 
14 the following information:  
15 1.  Whether the utility has the present ability to map specific meters to specific 
16 buildings and, if the utility does not have the ability, a description of the process necessary 
17 to achieve this ability, including the costs to the utility's ratepayers; and
18 2.  Whether the utility has the present ability to export energy data in the Green Button 
19 Alliance format and support the Green Button Connect My Data standard and the energy 
20 services provider interface of the North American Energy Standards Board, and, if the 
21 utility does not have the present ability to export energy data in the Green Button Alliance 
22 format and support the Green Button Connect My Data standard and the energy services 
23 provider interface of the North American Energy Standards Board, a description of the 
24 process necessary to achieve this ability, including the costs to the utility's ratepayers.
25Sec. 3.  Report. Resolved: That, by November 2, 2022, the Public Utilities 
26 Commission shall submit a report to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
27 jurisdiction over utilities and energy matters that includes the information gathered under 
28 section 2. The committee may report out related legislation to the 131st Legislature in 2023.
29SUMMARY
30 This resolve directs the Governor's Energy Office to monitor efforts undertaken in 
31 other states to improve energy use data standards and to implement multiple-use online 
32 energy data platforms. On or before November 2, 2022, the office must submit information 
33 regarding other states' efforts and recommendations to the joint standing committee of the 
34 Legislature having jurisdiction over utilities and energy matters. The committee may report 
35 out related legislation to the 131st Legislature in 2023. The resolve also requires the Public 
36 Utilities Commission to issue a request for information to transmission and distribution 
37 utilities in the State and to report the results of the request to the committee. The committee 
38 may report out related legislation to the 131st Legislature in 2023.30
