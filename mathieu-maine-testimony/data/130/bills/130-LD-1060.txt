30SUMMARY
31 This bill makes the following changes, effective January 1, 2023, to the laws governing 
32 the allocation of revenue from slot machines and table games operated by licensed casinos 
33 in the State.
34 1.  It repeals the provision of law requiring that 1% of gross slot machine income from 
35 the Bangor casino be deposited in the General Fund for the administrative expenses of the 
36 Department of Public Safety, Gambling Control Board.
37 2. It repeals the provision of law requiring that 4% of net slot machine income from 
38 the Oxford County casino be distributed to the tribal governments of the Penobscot Nation 
39 and the Passamaquoddy Tribe.
40 3. It requires each licensed casino to collect 5% of net slot machine income and 5% of 
41 net table game income for distribution to the tribal governments of the federally recognized 
42 Indian tribes in the State in the proportions annually established by the Indian Casino 31
32
33
Page 7 - 130LR1887(01)43 Revenue Distribution Committee established in the bill.  If a federally recognized Indian 
44 tribe owns or operates a licensed slot machine facility or casino in the State, this 5% of net 
45 slot machine income and 5% of net table game income must be deposited in the General 
46 Fund instead of being distributed to tribal governments.
5 4. It requires each licensed casino to collect a specific percentage of net slot machine 
6 income and of net table game income for deposit in the General Fund so that a total of 50% 
7 of net slot machine income and of net table game income is collected by the casino and 
8 distributed to the funds, programs and governmental entities identified in the Maine 
9 Revised Statutes, Title 36, section 1036 or shared with the host municipality under a 
10 revenue-sharing agreement required by section 1011, subsection 4.
11 The bill also creates the Indian Casino Revenue Distribution Committee, which is a 
12 17-member committee composed of the 13 members of the Maine Indian Tribal-State 
13 Commission as well as one member appointed by the Penobscot Nation, one member 
14 appointed by the Passamaquoddy Tribe, one member appointed by the Houlton Band of 
15 Maliseet Indians and one member appointed by the Aroostook Band of Micmacs.  This 
16 committee must, beginning January 1, 2023 submit an annual report to the Treasurer of 
17 State and the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over slot 
18 machines matters setting forth the proportion of net slot machine income and net table game 
19 income that should be distributed to each of the federally recognized Indian tribes in the 
20 State in the subsequent year.1
