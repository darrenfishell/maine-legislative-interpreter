3SUMMARY
4 This bill makes the following changes to the fish and wildlife laws.
5 1.  It corrects cross-references.
6 2.  It corrects a conflict created by Public Law 2017, chapters 427 and 458, which 
7 affected the same provision of law.  It provides that no more than 8% of moose hunting 
8 permits may be issued to nonresidents and no more than 2% may be issued to hunting 
9 outfitters.
10 3.  It removes an obsolete provision related to road safety management areas.
11 4.  It eliminates the requirement that moose hunters complete a questionnaire.
12 5.  It provides that a person who has lawfully killed and registered a wild turkey may 
13 sell the plumage, wing bones, beard and lower legs of that animal.
14 6.  It provides that a person may not sell any live wildlife, subject to certain exceptions.
15 7.  It removes an obsolete provision related to a nonresident junior fishing license.
16 8.  It provides that a person licensed to deal in live smelts or baitfish may not receive, 
17 possess for resale, sell or offer to sell gift baitfish or gift smelts.
18 9.  It adds the Pleasant Bay Wildlife Management Area in Washington County to the 
19 list of wildlife management areas and adds 2 towns to the Plymouth Bog Wildlife 
20 Management Area.
21 10.  It exempts all foreign watercraft from the requirement that the owner obtain a 
22 certificate of number from the Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife.
23 11.  It makes requirements for taillights for snowmobiles and all-terrain vehicles 
24 consistent.
25 12.  It changes a reference in the laws governing all-terrain vehicle registration from 
26 June 31st to June 30th.
27 13.  It changes the language of the provision governing the registration of an all-terrain 
28 vehicle by a member of the Armed Forces of the United States who is permanently 
29 stationed in this State to make it more consistent with a similar provision for the registration 
30 of a snowmobile.  It provides that registration fees for the registration of such an all-terrain 
31 vehicle must be allocated as if the person registering the all-terrain vehicle was a resident 
32 of the municipality where the member is stationed.
33 14.  It removes incorrect cross-references to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 29-A in 
34 the laws governing wearing helmets while on an all-terrain vehicle.
35 15.  It makes consistent the provisions prohibiting the operation of all-terrain vehicles 
36 and snowmobiles too close to certain buildings.4
