Page 1 - 130LR1743(01)1Sec. 1.  Department of Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management to 
2study veterans' benefits. Resolved:  That the Department of Defense, Veterans and 
3 Emergency Management shall conduct a study of federal and state benefits available to 
4 veterans in the State and the efficacy of the benefits in addressing deficiencies in 
5 comprehensively and fairly addressing issues experienced by the veterans, including 
6 whether:
7 1. Elderly veterans are receiving proper care and benefits commensurate with their 
8 advanced age and health care and living accommodation needs;
9 2. The provision of benefits based upon factors such as length and type of service, 
10 whether the veteran incurred a service-related disability, the level of a service-related 
11 disability, the income of the veteran and other factors leaves gaps in the provision of 
12 benefits to veterans who have a need for the benefits;
13 3.  Health care, dental care and mental health care benefits adequately address the 
14 issues experienced by veterans; and
15 4. Mental health care benefits can be augmented or enhanced to adequately address the 
16 large number of suicides by veterans.
17Sec. 2.  Report. Resolved: That the Department of Defense, Veterans and 
18 Emergency Management shall report its findings under section 1, including proposed 
19 legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs by January 1, 
20 2022.  The joint standing committee may report out a bill on the subject of the report to the 
21 Second Regular Session of the 130th Legislature.
22SUMMARY
23 This resolve requires the Department of Defense, Veterans and Emergency 
24 Management to study the provision of federal and state benefits available to veterans in the 
25 State and the efficacy of the benefits in addressing deficiencies in comprehensively and 
26 fairly addressing issues experienced by the veterans and by January 1, 2022 report its 
27 findings, including proposed legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Veterans and 
28 Legal Affairs, which may report out a bill on the subject of the report to the Second Regular 
29 Session of the 130th Legislature.23
