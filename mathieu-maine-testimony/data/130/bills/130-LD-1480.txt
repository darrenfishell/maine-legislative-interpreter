2Sec. 1.  5 MRSA §200-K, sub-§8 is enacted to read:
38.  Attorney General's investigation.  The Attorney General shall complete an 
4 investigation of and submit to the panel the findings regarding the use of deadly force 
5 pursuant to section 200-A within 180 days of receiving notice of the use of deadly force by 
6 a law enforcement officer.  If the Attorney General is unable to complete the investigation 
7 and submit the findings within 180 days, the Attorney General shall notify the panel prior 
8 to the expiration of the 180-day period.  The Attorney General's notice to the panel under 
9 this subsection must provide a summary of the investigation up to the date of the notice, 
10 identify the reason for the delay and provide an anticipated conclusion date for the 
11 investigation and findings, which may not exceed 270 days from receiving notice of the 
12 use of deadly force.
13Sec. 2.  16 MRSA §803, sub-§7, as enacted by PL 2013, c. 267, Pt. A, §3, is

157.  Intelligence and investigative record information.  "Intelligence and investigative

17 direction of a criminal justice agency or kept in the custody of a criminal justice agency

20 "Intelligence and investigative record information" includes information of record

22 prepared or collected by a criminal justice agency or other agency.  "Intelligence and 
23 investigative record information" does not include criminal history record information as 
24 defined in section 703, subsection 3 and does not include , a video recording of the use of 
25 force by a law enforcement officer or information of record collected or kept while 
26 performing the administration of juvenile justice.
27SUMMARY
28 This bill requires the Attorney General to complete an investigation of and submit the 
29 findings regarding the use of deadly force by a law enforcement officer to the Attorney 
30 General's Deadly Force Review Panel within 180 days of receiving notice of the use of 
31 deadly force. The Attorney General is required to provide notice to the panel if the 
32 investigation and findings are not complete within 180 days and provide a summary of the 
33 investigation up to the date of the notice, identify the reason for the delay and provide an 
34 anticipated conclusion date for the investigation and findings, which may not exceed 270 
35 days from receiving notice of the use of deadly force.  The bill also excludes from the 
36 definition of "intelligence and investigative record information", which is confidential 
37 under the law, a video recording of the use of force by a law enforcement officer.28
