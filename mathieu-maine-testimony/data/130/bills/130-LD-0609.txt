12Sec. 1.  Commission established.  Resolved:  That the Commission To Increase 
13 Housing Opportunities in Maine by Studying Zoning and Land Use Restrictions, referred 
14 to in this resolve as "the commission," is established.
15Sec. 2.  Commission membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
16 353, the commission consists of 15 members appointed as follows:
17 1.  Two members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including a 
18 member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
19 2.  Two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
20 House, including a member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
21 in the Legislature;
22 3.  The Director of the Maine State Housing Authority, or the director's designee;
23 4.  One member representing the Office of the Governor, appointed by the Governor;
24 5.  Four public members, one representing a statewide municipal association, one 
25 representing a statewide organization that advocates for affordable housing, one 
26 representing statewide agricultural interests and one who is in the building trades, 
27 appointed by the President of the Senate; and
28 6.  Five public members, one representing a regional planning association or a 
29 statewide organization that advocates for smart growth policies and projects, one 
30 representing the real estate industry, one who is a residential developer, one representing 
31 an organization that advocates for low-income or middle-income renters or homeowners 
32 and one representing a local or statewide organization promoting civil rights that has racial 
33 justice or racial equity as its primary mission, appointed by the Speaker of the House.
34Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
35 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
36 commission.
37Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
38 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
39 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
40 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
41 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
Page 2 - 130LR0384(01)42 after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
43 made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for 
44 the commission to meet and conduct its business.
4Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission is authorized to meet 6 times and 
5 shall:
6 1.  Review data on housing shortages in the State for low-income and middle-income 
7 households;
8 2.  Review state laws that affect the local regulation of housing;
9 3.  Review efforts in other states and municipalities to address housing shortages 
10 through changes to zoning and land use restrictions;
11 4.  Consider measures that would encourage increased housing options in the State, 
12 including but not limited to municipal incentives, state mandates, eliminating or limiting 
13 single-family-only zones and allowing greater housing density near transit, jobs, schools 
14 or neighborhood centers; and
15 5.  Review and consider the historical role of race and racism in zoning policies and 
16 the best measures to ensure that state and municipal zoning laws do not serve as barriers to 
17 racial equality.
18Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
19 necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff support 
20 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
21Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, no later than 
22 November 3, 2021, the commission shall submit a report that includes its findings and 
23 recommendations, including suggested legislation, for presentation to the Joint Standing 
24 Committee on Labor and Housing.
25Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
26 takes effect when approved.
27SUMMARY
28 This resolve establishes the Commission To Increase Housing Opportunities in Maine 
29 by Studying Zoning and Land Use Restrictions, which is a 15-member commission directed 
30 to review data on housing shortages in the State for low-income and middle-income 
31 households, state laws that affect the local regulation of housing and efforts in other states 
32 and municipalities to address housing shortages; to consider measures that would 
33 encourage increased housing options in the State; and to review and consider the historical 
34 role of race and racism in zoning policies and the best measures to ensure that state and 
35 municipal zoning laws do not serve as barriers to racial equality.  The commission must, 
36 no later than November 3, 2021, submit a report, including suggested legislation, for 
37 presentation to the Joint Standing Committee on Labor and Housing.1
