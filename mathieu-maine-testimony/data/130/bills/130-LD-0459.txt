2Sec. 1.  32 MRSA §4204, as repealed and replaced by PL 2013, c. 264, §14, is

4§4204.  Penalties
51.  Penalty.  A person who fails to be licensed as required by this chapter, violates the 
6 sterilization, sanitation or safety standards adopted by the Department of Health and 
7 Human Services Professional and Financial Regulation under section 4251 or performs 
8 tattooing on a minor commits a civil violation for which a fine of not less than $500 nor 
9 more than $1,000 may be adjudged for each violation.
102.  Enforcement.  A person who fails to pay a penalty imposed pursuant to this chapter:
11 A.  May be referred to the Attorney General for appropriate enforcement action; and
12 B.  In addition to all fines and penalties imposed pursuant to this chapter, is liable for 
13 any interest, costs and fees incurred by the Department of Health and Human Services  
14 Professional and Financial Regulation, including attorney's fees.

16SUBCHAPTER 2
17DEPARTMENT OF HUMAN SERVICES  LICENSING AUTHORITY
18Sec. 3.  32 MRSA §4251, as amended by PL 1975, c. 293, §4 and PL 2003, c. 689, 
19 Pt. B, §6, is further amended to read:
20§4251.  Rules and regulations
21 The Department of Health and Human Services Professional and Financial Regulation 
22 is authorized and empowered to make necessary rules and regulations governing the 
23 application of tattoos upon the bodies of human beings.
24Sec. 4.  32 MRSA §4252, as amended by PL 2013, c. 264, §15, is further amended 
25 to read:
26§4252.  Issuance of licenses
27 The Department of Health and Human Services Professional and Financial Regulation 
28 may license persons to practice the art of tattooing. Such licenses are issued for a term of 
29 one year and may be renewed annually.  The fee for an initial license or a renewal license 
30 may not exceed $250.  All fees collected by the department pursuant to this section must 
31 be deposited in a special revenue account dedicated to a health inspection program.
32Sec. 5.  32 MRSA §4301, as amended by PL 1975, c. 293, §4 and PL 2003, c. 689, 
33 Pt. B, §6, is further amended to read:
34§4301.  License required
35 No person shall place a tattoo upon the body of another human being without first 
36 obtaining a license from the Department of Health and Human Services  Professional and 
37 Financial Regulation.
Page 2 - 130LR1823(01)1SUMMARY
2 This bill changes the licensing authority for tattoo artists from the Department of Health 
3 and Human Services to the Department of Professional and Financial Regulation.
