3 Intellectual or Developmental Disabilities, referred to in this resolve as "the task force," is 
4 established to identify barriers inhibiting adults 18 years of age to 40 years of age with

6 recommend possible solutions.

8Sec. 3.  Chair; meetings. Resolved:  That the Commissioner of Education shall 
9 appoint a chair of the task force and convene the initial meeting of the task force no later 
10 than 30 days following the effective date of this resolve. The task force shall hold a 
11 minimum of 4 meetings.
12Sec. 4.  Duties. Resolved:  That the task force shall study the barriers to efficient 
13 and effective services for young adults with intellectual or developmental disabilities after 
14 high school and make recommendations on targeted reforms to improve the efficiency and 
15 effectiveness of these services, including but not limited to:
16 1.  The coordination of services provided by different agencies; and
17 2.  The benefits, assessments, expansion and availability of continuing educational 
18 opportunities for young adults with disabilities after high school.
19 The task force shall also identify the function and efficacy of current federal and state 
20 mandates pertaining to young adults with intellectual or developmental disabilities and 
21 develop recommendations to enhance the coordination of programs to ensure the most 
22 efficient and effective provision of essential services, including continuing purposeful 
23 individualized education, development of self-determination and personal advocacy skills, 
24 job training, housing, development of independent living skills including management of 
25 personal health and finances, transportation, recreation and development of social 
26 functioning.  The task force shall analyze current data and retrospective data for the past 6 
27 years and provide the measurable success rate of programs providing these essential 
28 services, with consideration of the preferences, strengths and interests of individual young 
29 adults with intellectual or development disabilities and parental concerns.
30Sec. 5.  Staff assistance. Resolved:  That the Department of Education shall provide 
31 the task force with staff assistance from existing resources.
32Sec. 6.  Outside funding. Resolved:  That the task force may seek outside funding 
33 to support the work of the task force.
34Sec. 7.  Final report. Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, no later than 
35 one year after the initial convening of the task force under section 3, the task force shall 
36 submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations for presentation to the Joint 
37 Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs, the Joint Standing Committee on 
38 Health and Human Services and the Joint Standing Committee on Labor and Housing.

40 This resolve establishes the Task Force To Study the Coordination of Services and 
41 Expansion of Educational Programs for Young Adults with Intellectual or Developmental 
42 Disabilities to identify barriers inhibiting adults 18 years of age to 40 years of age with 40

44 recommend possible solutions.  The membership of the task force consists of Legislators 
45 who serve on the joint standing committees of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
46 education and cultural affairs, health and human services matters and labor and housing 
47 matters, the Commissioner of Education, the Commissioner of Health and Human Services, 
48 the Commissioner of Labor, members of organizations or associations knowledgeable 
49 about services for young adults with disabilities after high school, parents or guardians or 
50 direct care providers of a young adult with a disability and young adults with disabilities 
51 who are eligible to receive services from the State.  The Commissioner of Education 
52 convenes the task force, which must hold a minimum of 4 meetings and submit a report to 
53 the Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs, the Joint Standing 
54 Committee on Health and Human Services and the Joint Standing Committee on Labor and 
55 Housing with recommendations on targeted reforms to improve the efficiency and 
56 effectiveness of services provided by different agencies and continuing educational 
57 opportunities for young adults with intellectual or developmental disabilities after high 
58 school.  The task force must also analyze current data and retrospective data for the past 6 
59 years regarding the function and efficacy of current federal and state mandates pertaining 
60 to young adults with intellectual or developmental disabilities and provide 
61 recommendations to enhance the coordination of programs to ensure the most efficient and 
62 effective provision of essential services.1
