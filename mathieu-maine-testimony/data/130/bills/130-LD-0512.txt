142.  Assignment to counties.  The department shall assign at least one intensive case 
15 manager to work in each county.  An intensive case manager must be based in a county or 
16 regional jail or, in the absence of a county or regional jail, in the county sheriff's office.
173.  Intensive case manager duties.  The duties of intensive case managers include but 
18 are not limited to:
19 A.  Overseeing all persons detained by a law enforcement officer who are evaluated 
20 for a condition, including:
21 (1)  Connecting a person with a condition to services provided at the county or 
22 regional jail;
23 (2)  Developing and implementing a reentry-focused treatment plan for a person 
24 with a condition to be implemented upon the person's release and ensuring 
25 adequate monitoring of the person's compliance with the treatment plan for at least 
26 60 days after the person reenters the community; and
27 (3)  For a person with a condition who is incarcerated for a Class D or Class E 
28 crime or is otherwise detained by a law enforcement officer, developing a diversion 
29 plan;
30 B.  Identifying any person with a condition detained by a law enforcement officer who 
31 may be eligible for services through the MaineCare program, completing an application 
32 for MaineCare coverage with that person and confirming activation of MaineCare 
33 benefits or the continuation of MaineCare benefits pursuant to Title 22, section 
34 3174-CC prior to that person's release from the county or regional jail and during the 
35 implementation of any diversion plan or reentry-focused treatment plan; and
36 C.  Working with the court system to ensure that all persons detained by a law 
37 enforcement officer who are identified as having a condition are provided due process 
38 and a speedy trial.

3SUMMARY
4 This bill directs the Department of Health and Human Services to create additional 
5 intensive case manager positions so that counties that do not have a county jail or regional 
6 jail will have an intensive case manager.  An intensive case manager oversees persons who 
7 are detained by a law enforcement officer and who have intellectual disabilities or mental 
8 health conditions or have misused substances and connects them to the services for which 
9 they qualify, works with the court system to ensure that they receive due process and speedy 
10 trials and assists persons who qualify for the MaineCare program to apply for and receive 
11 MaineCare benefits and services while being detained or incarcerated, including during the 
12 implementation of diversion and reentry plans.1
