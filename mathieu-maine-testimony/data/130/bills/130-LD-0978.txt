6§5219-YY.  Access to justice credit
71.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
8 following terms have the following meanings.
9 A.  "Court" means the Supreme Judicial Court or its designee.
10 B.  "Eligible attorney" means a person eligible to practice law in the State under Title 
11 4, chapter 17 who, after January 1, 2022:
12 (1)  Agrees to practice law in a private practice setting in an underserved area for 
13 at least 5 years by joining an existing legal practice, establishing a new legal 
14 practice or purchasing an existing legal practice;
15 (2)  Is rostered by the Maine Commission on Indigent Legal Services to accept 
16 court appointments to represent clients in an underserved area;
17 (3)  Agrees to perform pro bono legal services in an underserved area; and
18 (4)  Is certified by the court under subsection 3 to be eligible for the credit under 
19 this section.
20 C.  "Underserved area" means an area in the State that is determined by the court to be 
21 an area where there is insufficient access to legal services.  When identifying 
22 underserved areas, the court shall take into consideration the ratio of the number of 
23 attorneys to the population.
242.  Credit.  For tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2022, an eligible attorney is 
25 allowed a credit for each taxable year, not to exceed $6,000, against the taxes due under 
26 this Part.  The credit may be claimed in the first year that the eligible attorney meets the 
27 conditions of eligibility for at least 6 months and in each of the 4 subsequent years.
283.  Eligibility limitation; certification.  The court may certify up to 5 eligible attorneys 
29 in each year from 2022 through 2027. Additional attorneys may not be certified after 2027.  
30 The court shall annually, at year-end, verify that certified attorneys continue to be eligible 
31 for the credit under this section and shall decertify any attorney who ceases to meet the 
32 conditions of eligibility. The court shall notify the bureau whenever an attorney is certified 
33 or decertified. A decertified attorney ceases to be eligible for the credit under this section 
34 beginning with the tax year during which the attorney is decertified.
354.  Rules.  The court shall adopt rules to implement this section.
365.  Report; review.  By February 15, 2027, the court shall submit to the joint standing 
37 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over taxation matters a report that 
38 identifies the number of eligible attorneys claiming the credit under this section each year 
39 in which the credit is available and identifies the underserved areas where those attorneys 
40 practice. The committee shall review the report and determine the effectiveness of the credit 
Page 2 - 130LR0649(01)41 in expanding legal services to underserved areas.  The committee may submit legislation 
42 to the First Regular Session of the 133rd Legislature related to the report.
3SUMMARY
4 This bill provides an income tax credit for 5 years for attorneys who practice law in the 
5 State and agree to practice for at least 5 years in an underserved area of the State as 
6 determined by the Supreme Judicial Court.  Eligibility for the credit is open from 2022 
7 through 2027.  The court may certify up to 5 eligible attorneys each year.  The joint standing 
8 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over taxation matters is directed to review 
9 the effectiveness of the credit and may submit legislation to extend or revise it.1
