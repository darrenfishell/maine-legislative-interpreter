310.  Companion animals.  In the disposition of property pursuant to subsection 1, the 
4 court, with respect to a companion animal, shall consider the well-being of the companion 
5 animal and all relevant factors, including, but not limited to:
6 A.  The basic daily needs of the companion animal;
7 B.  The amount of time each party has spent with the companion animal during the 
8 marriage tending to the companion animal's nutritional, grooming, physical and 
9 medical needs;
10 C.  The ability of a party to continue to own, financially support and provide adequate 
11 care for the companion animal;
12 D.  The emotional attachment of a party to the companion animal;
13 E.  The emotional attachment of any child in the household to the companion animal 
14 and the benefit to the child of the companion animal's remaining in the primary 
15 residence of the child;
16 F.  Any domestic violence between the parties or in the household of the parties; and
17 G.  Any history of animal abuse or other unsafe conditions for the companion animal.
18 For the purposes of this subsection, "companion animal" means an animal kept primarily 
19 for companionship rather than as a working animal, service animal or farm animal kept for 
20 profit.
21SUMMARY
22 This bill provides that in a proceeding for legal separation or divorce, in the disposition 
23 of property a court must consider the well-being of a companion animal and all relevant 
24 factors and specifies a number of factors that the court must consider.22
