30SUMMARY
31 This bill requires a candidate or a political action committee seeking to influence the 
32 election of a candidate for municipal office of a town or city with a population of 50,000 
33 or more to submit registrations and campaign finance reports with the Commission on 
34 Governmental Ethics and Election Practices instead of with the municipal clerk, and it 
35 requires a town or city with a population of less than 50,000 to post campaign finance 
36 reports on the municipality's publicly accessible website within 24 hours after the deadline 
37 for filing campaign finance reports.  This bill also makes the commission responsible for 
38 oversight of the filing of registrations and campaign finance reports relating to municipal 
39 referenda campaigns in a town or city with a population of 50,000 or more.  The bill also 
40 makes it a requirement for the municipal clerk to refer a potential violation to the 
41 commission.  Current law makes it permissive.1
