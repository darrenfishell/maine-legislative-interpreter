91.  Definitions.  As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
10 following terms have the following meanings.
11 A.  "Authority" means the Finance Authority of Maine.
12 B.  "First responder" has the same meaning as in Title 22, section 832-A, subsection 1, 
13 paragraph E.
14 C.  "Fund" means the Educational Opportunity Grant Program for Family Members of 
15 Deceased Veterans, First Responders and Health Care Workers Fund under section 
16 12942.
17 D.  "Program" means the Educational Opportunity Grant Program for Family Members 
18 of Deceased Veterans, First Responders and Health Care Workers under subsection 2.
19 E.  "Public postsecondary educational institution" means the University of Maine 
20 System, the Maine Community College System or the Maine Maritime Academy.
212.  Program.  The Educational Opportunity Grant Program for Family Members of 
22 Deceased Veterans, First Responders and Health Care Workers is established within and 
23 administered by the authority.  The program provides a grant to an eligible person to cover 
24 the cost of tuition, room and board, books and other required educational materials and 
25 other fees and expenses related to attending a public postsecondary educational institution 
26 typically paid for by financial aid. 
273.  Eligibility.  A person is eligible to receive a grant under the program if:
28 A.  The person is a child or spouse of a veteran, first responder or health care worker 
29 who during the course of employment as a military service member, first responder or 
30 health care worker dies or contracts an illness or condition that leads to the death of the 
31 veteran, first responder or health care worker;
32 B.  The person or the deceased veteran, first responder or health care worker under 
33 paragraph A is a resident of the State at the time of the death or contraction of the 
34 illness or condition leading to the death of the veteran, first responder or health care 
35 worker; and
36 C.  The person is accepted to a course of study in a public postsecondary educational 
37 institution.

8ยง12942. Educational Opportunity Grant Program for Family Members of Deceased 
9Veterans, First Responders and Health Care Workers Fund
10 The Educational Opportunity Grant Program for Family Members of Deceased 
11 Veterans, First Responders and Health Care Workers Fund is established within and 
12 administered by the authority to fund grants under and administer the program.  The fund 
13 is funded by appropriations, allocations or grants or donations by public or private sources, 
14 including a resource for postsecondary student financial aid that is related to a service or 
15 profession of the deceased parent or spouse under section 12941, subsection 3, paragraph 
16 A.  The authority may engage in the solicitation of a grant or donation to be deposited into 
17 the fund. The authority shall deposit with the Treasurer of State to the credit of the fund 
18 money in the fund not currently needed by the authority to carry out the purposes of the 
19 fund, which may be invested as provided by law.  Interest earned on investment of money 
20 under this section must be credited to the fund. Balances in the fund do not lapse but must 
21 be carried over to the next fiscal year.  The authority may use principal of the fund to pay 
22 for the authority's reasonable expenses in administering the fund.
23SUMMARY
24 This bill creates the Educational Opportunity Grant Program for Family Members of 
25 Deceased Veterans, First Responders and Health Care Workers, administered by the 
26 Finance Authority of Maine, to provide postsecondary education grants to eligible children 
27 and spouses of veterans, first responders and health care workers who die or contract a 
28 condition or illness that leads to their death.  The program is funded by a fund that may 
29 accept appropriations, allocations, grants or donations from public and private sources, 
30 including financial aid resources, and the authority may solicit private donations for the 
31 fund.24
