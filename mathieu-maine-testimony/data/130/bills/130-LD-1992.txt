13Sec. 8. Report.  The Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife, in conjunction with 
14 adopting rules governing electronic registration of turkey pursuant to the Maine Revised 
15 Statutes, Title 12, section 12301-B, shall examine laws governing the tagging and 
16 registration of harvested animals to determine if there are laws that should be updated or 
17 revised to facilitate an electronic tagging system for turkey.  The department shall submit 
18 a report of its findings and recommendations, together with any suggested legislation, to 
19 the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over inland fisheries and 
20 wildlife matters by November 2, 2022. The committee may report out a bill related to 
21 electronic tagging to the First Regular Session of the 131st Legislature. 
22SUMMARY
23 This bill, which is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Inland Fisheries 
24 and Wildlife pursuant to Public Law 2021, chapter 121, does the following. 
25 1.  It directs the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife to adopt rules allowing 
26 for the electronic registration of turkey beginning in 2023.
27 2. It requires the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife to report to the joint 
28 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over inland fisheries and wildlife 
29 matters by November 2, 2022 on additional statutory changes needed in order to facilitate 
30 the electronic tagging of turkey.  It authorizes the committee to report out a bill related to 
31 electronic tagging to the First Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.1
