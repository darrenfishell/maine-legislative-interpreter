Page 1 - 130LR0895(01)1Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to study information 
2relating to human trafficking. Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human 
3 Services shall research and study data regarding sex trafficking of minors and adults in the 
4 State and create a human trafficking needs assessment patterned on the human trafficking 
5 needs assessment produced in 2015 by Hornby Zeller Associates, Inc. for the Maine 
6 Coalition Against Sexual Assault. The department shall conduct the study and draft the 
7 assessment with the assistance of law enforcement agencies, judicial resources and 
8 nonprofit and other organizations with an interest in preventing sex trafficking.  The 
9 department may use its own funding or seek other funding to carry out the purposes of this 
10 resolve.
11Sec. 2.  Information to be studied. Resolved:  That, in developing the assessment 
12 under section 1, the Department of Health and Human Services shall include the following 
13 information in its research and study:
14 1. A profile of victims of sex trafficking, including:
15 A.  Race;
16 B.  Gender;
17 C.  Age range;
18 D.  Rural or urban living situation;
19 E.  History of sexual abuse or domestic violence;
20 F.  Absence of a supportive caregiver; and
21 G.  Drug use;
22 2. Data on sex trafficking in the State, including:
23 A.  The prevalence of sex trafficking and number of cases annually;
24 B.  The prevalence of labor trafficking;
25 C.  The percentage of law enforcement officers who have been involved in a sex 
26 trafficking case in the previous year;
27 D.  The percentage of law enforcement officers who are not familiar with an 
28 organization in the State addressing human trafficking; and
29 E.  The percentage of law enforcement officers who believe that the officersâ€™ respective 
30 departments are prepared to address sex trafficking cases involving minors;
31 3. Data on the reentry of victims and prevention of sex trafficking, including:
32 A.  The services that are being received and by whom;
33 B.  The percentage of victims and survivors receiving services;
34 C.  The prevalence of family reunification;
35 D.  The rate of homelessness;
36 E.   Access to health insurance and health care; and
37 F.  The rate of incarceration of traffickers and victims; and
38 4. Other benchmarks that the department determines necessary.
Page 2 - 130LR0895(01)1Sec. 3.  Report.  Resolved:  That, by December 7, 2022, the Department of Health 
2 and Human Services shall report its human trafficking needs assessment under section 1 
3 and recommendations to aid the State in combating human trafficking with regard to 
4 prevention and reentry, including suggested legislation, to the joint standing committee of 
5 the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters.  The joint 
6 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services 
7 matters may report out legislation based upon the report to the First Regular Session of the 
8 131st Legislature.
9SUMMARY
10 This resolve directs the Department of Health and Human Services to conduct an 
11 assessment of human trafficking in the State patterned on an assessment conducted in 2015 
12 and to provide the assessment and recommendations to the Legislature on how to combat 
13 human trafficking with regard to prevention and reentry.10
