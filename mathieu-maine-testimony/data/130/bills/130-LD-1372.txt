18§1984.  Noise abatement
19 The authority shall install devices to collect data measuring noise levels at certain 
20 points adjacent to the turnpike corridor. Data collected under this section must be:
211.  Residential neighborhoods and public health facilities. Collected where a 
22 residential neighborhood of 10 or more domicile units per quarter mile or a public health 
23 care facility exists:
24 A.  Within 1,500 feet of the roadway on the turnpike, not including entrance and exit 
25 ramps where the posted speed limit does not exceed 45 miles per hour; or
26 B.  Within 1,500 feet of a public rest area managed by the authority; and
272.  Data specifications. Collected at various distances up to 1,500 feet and must 
28 distinguish noise levels at different times of day and night, on different days of the week 
29 and at different times of the year.
30 The authority shall provide an annual report summarizing recent data and multiyear 
31 comparisons of data collected under this section to the joint standing committee of the 
32 Legislature having jurisdiction over transportation matters by December 1, 2022 and each 
33 year thereafter.
34Sec. 5.  Turnpike noise abatement.  No later than February 2, 2023, the Maine 
35 Turnpike Authority shall submit to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
36 jurisdiction over transportation matters a plan for a noise abatement program including 
37 procedures, noise thresholds and noise mitigation options for routes and public rest areas 
38 managed by the authority, in accordance with United States Department of Transportation, 
39 Federal Highway Administration regulations relating to noise abatement on existing 
40 highways. The plan developed by the authority must include, but is not limited to, indicated 
41 noise thresholds along the turnpike based on data collected pursuant to Maine Revised 
Page 2 - 130LR1416(01)42 Statutes, Title 23, section 1984 and proposed legislation to establish criteria by major 
43 substantive rules for assessing the reasonableness and feasibility of noise abatement 
44 measures.
4SUMMARY
5 This bill requires the Maine Turnpike Authority to install devices to collect data 
6 measuring noise levels along routes managed by the authority, sets specific criteria for the 
7 data that must be collected and requires the authority to report this data to the joint standing 
8 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over transportation matters no later than 
9 December 1, 2022. This bill also requires the authority to submit to the joint standing 
10 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over transportation matters a plan to 
11 develop a noise abatement program in accordance with United States Department of 
12 Transportation, Federal Highway Administration regulations and sets specific criteria for 
13 the plan developed by the authority.1
