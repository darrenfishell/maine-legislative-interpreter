4§11209.  Discharge of firearm, bow or crossbow near dwelling or building
51.  Prohibition.  A person may not:
6 A.  Unless a relevant municipal ordinance provides otherwise and except as provided 
7 in subsections 3 and 4 and sections 12401 and 12402, discharge a firearm, including a 
8 muzzle-loading firearm, bow or crossbow or cause a projectile to pass as a result of 
9 that discharge within 100 yards of a building or residential dwelling without the 
10 permission of the owner or, in the owner's absence, of an adult occupant of that building 
11 or dwelling authorized to act on behalf of the owner; or
12 B.  Possess a wild animal or wild bird taken in violation of this subsection, except as 
13 otherwise provided in this Part.
14 This subsection may not be construed to prohibit a person from killing or taking a wild 
15 animal in accordance with sections 12401 and 12402.
16 This subsection does not apply to a person discharging a bow in an expanded archery deer 
17 zone during an expanded archery deer hunting season created by the commissioner by rule.
18 For purposes of this section, "building" means any residential, commercial, retail, 
19 educational, religious or farm structure that is designed to be occupied by people or 
20 domesticated animals or is being used to shelter machines or harvested crops.
21 For purposes of this section, "projectile" means a bullet, pellet, shot, shell, ball, bolt or 
22 other object propelled or launched from a firearm, bow or crossbow.
232.  Penalty.  A person who violates subsection 1 commits a Class E crime.
243.  Target practice; crossbow.  Unless otherwise prohibited, a landowner or the 
25 landowner's invitee may discharge a bow or crossbow for recreational target practice 
26 purposes on the landowner's property within 100 yards of a building or residential dwelling 
27 without the permission of the owner of that building or dwelling.  Nothing in this subsection 
28 authorizes a landowner or the landowner's invitee to cause a projectile discharged from a 
29 bow or crossbow to enter property owned by another person.
304.  Sport shooting ranges.  Unless otherwise prohibited, a person may discharge a 
31 firearm on a sport shooting range as defined in Title 30‑A, section 3011, subsection 1 that 
32 is within 100 yards of a building if the sport shooting range was established and in regular 
33 operation prior to the erection of the building.
34SUMMARY
35 This bill prohibits the discharge of a bow within 100 yards of a building or residential 
36 dwelling without the permission of the owner, but allows for recreational target practice.  
37 It also provides an exception for discharging a bow in an expanded archery deer zone 
38 during an expanded archery deer hunting season created by the Commissioner of Inland 
39 Fisheries and Wildlife by rule.35
