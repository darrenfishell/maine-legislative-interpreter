6 SUMMARY 
7 This amendment removes from the bill references to the State's hydrofluorocarbon use 
8 restrictions under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 38, section 1613. It also removes a 
9 related provision reallocating that statute to correct a numbering problem. 
10 The amendment adds to the bill a new term, "acceptable refrigeration or air 
11 conditioning products or equipment," which is defined to mean refrigeration or air 
12 conditioning products or equipment that uses a refrigerant listed by the United States 
13 Environmental Protection Agency in regulations adopted pursuant to the federal Clean Air 
14 Act as acceptable, acceptable subject to use conditions or acceptable subject to narrowed 
15 use limits and is installed in accordance with any applicable conditions or limitations 
16 imposed by those regulations. 
17 The amendment replaces the substance of the bill and provides that a municipality may 
18 not through its adopted building code prohibit the installation or use of such products or 
19 equipment.
