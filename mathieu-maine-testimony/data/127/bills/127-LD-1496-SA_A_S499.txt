The bill creates 3 new peer centers in different parts of the State to coordinate and run peer support programs to help 
persons in recovery from drug addiction. This amendment reduces the bill's General Fund appropriation to the 
Department of Health and Human Services from $600,000 to $400,000 in fiscal year 2016-17 only by moving one of 
the peer centers to fiscal year 2017-18.
