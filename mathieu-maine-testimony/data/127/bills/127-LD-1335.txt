SUMMARY  34 
This bill makes the following changes to the election laws.  35 
1.  It adds a restriction that a registrar may not serve during an election when an 36 
immediate family me mber of the registrar is a candidate for federal office, and clarifies 37 
the language regarding compensation.  The restriction previously applied only for 38 
candidates for state, local or county offices.  39 
 
 Page 10 - 127LR0576(01) -1 
 2.  It removes the provision regarding the use of a supp lemental incoming voting list.  1 
3.  It updates the list of the outside agencies that must provide voter registration 2 
pursuant to the National Voter Registration Act of 1993.  3 
4.  It provides that a primary election for State Senator, State Representative or  4 
county office will only be held when there is more than one candidate who has filed a 5 
candidate petition and consent; when there is at least one candidate who has filed a 6 
candidate petition and consent and at least one candidate who has qualified as a wri te-in 7 
candidate; or when there is no candidate who has filed a candidate petition and consent 8 
but there is at least one candidate who has qualified as a write -in candidate.  Otherwise, a 9 
party candidate is nominated to the general election ballot based on acceptance of a 10 
primary petition.  11 
5.  It provides that the written acceptance for a replacement candidate must include a 12 
place for the registrar of the candidate's municipality of residence to certify the 13 
candidate's registration and enrollment status.  14 
6.  It changes the deadline for a candidate to withdraw from 60 days to 75 days before 15 
a primary or general election and allows for a replacement candidate.  It also specifies 16 
that the candidate may withdraw only for good cause and must include that reason in  the 17 
written notice of withdrawal filed with the Secretary of State.  18 
7.  It changes the deadline from 60 days to 75 days before the general election for a 19 
nonparty candidate for Vice President to withdraw and allow for a replacement candidate.  20 
8.  It provi des that if a candidate or nominee for a federal or gubernatorial office 21 
withdraws less than 75 days before any election, the Secretary of State is not required to 22 
produce new ballots.  23 
9.  It clarifies that a candidate may withdraw after the withdrawal dea dline if the 24 
candidate is incapacitated by a catastrophic condition or injury and allows for a 25 
replacement candidate.  Current law provides for a candidate's withdrawal only for 26 
incapacitation due to a catastrophic illness.  27 
10.  It adds the restriction tha t a municipal clerk may not serve as the supervisor of an 28 
election when a member of the municipal clerk's immediate family is a candidate for 29
