 SUMMARY  1 
This bill requires a court to sentence a person convicted of violating a protective 2 
order or court -approved consent agreement, currently a Clas s D crime, to a minimum 3 
term of imprisonment of 30 days.  Subsequent convictions of violating a protective order 4 
or court -approved consent agreement are subject to enhanced minimum sentences.  5 
This bill also requires a court to sentence a person convicted o f violating a protective 6 
order through conduct that is reckless and that creates a substantial risk of death or 7 
serious bodily injury to the plaintiff named in the protective order or who assaults the 8 
plaintiff named in the protective order, currently a Cl ass C crime, to a minimum term of 9 
imprisonment of 2 years; subsequent convictions are subject to enhanced minimum 10 
sentences.  11 
This bill also creates the Class C crime of false claim, which occurs when a person 12 
during a proceeding for a protection from abuse  petition makes a false claim of abuse or 13 
neglect or abandonment of a child or alleges sexual exploitation of a minor, sex 14 
trafficking, aggravated sex trafficking or patronizing prostitution of a minor or person 15 
with a mental disability and that claim or a llegation is made for the purpose of gaining an 16 
advantage in a divorce proceeding.  17
