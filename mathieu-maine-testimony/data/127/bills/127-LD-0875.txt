 PART B  1 
Sec. B -1.  Transportation debt policy for capital planning purposes.  Due 2 
to the capital -intensive nature of transportation investment and the long lead times 3 
necessary to plan, permit, design and deliver transportation projects, in the absence of 4 
other specific gui dance, the Department of Transportation shall publish its work plans in 5 
the 10 -year period from 2016 to 2026 based upon the assumption that General Fund 6 
general obligation bonding in an amount averaging $95,000,000 per year will be provided 7 
for highway and  bridge and other multimodal purposes. This section and any planning 8 
undertaken pursuant to this section cannot and may not bind future Legislatures and does 9 
not authorize the issuance of any debt. Debt may be issued only in accordance with 10 
applicable auth orizing law. The department shall adjust its work plans to reflect actual 11 
bonding levels.  12 
SUMMARY  13 
The funds provided by this bond issue, in the amount of $190,000,000, will be used 14 
for reconstruction and rehabilitation of highways and bridges and for facil ities or 15 
equipment related to ports, harbors, marine transportation, aviation, freight and passenger 16 
railroads, transit and bicycle and pedestrian facilities, matching an estimated 17 
$290,000,000 in federal and other funds.  18
