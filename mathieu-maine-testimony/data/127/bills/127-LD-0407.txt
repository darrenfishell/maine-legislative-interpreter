SUMMARY  22 
This bill establishes the Maine Buy America Act and requires that all contracts for 23 
the construction, reconstruction, alteration, repair, improvement or maintena nce of a 24 
public building or public work made by a state agency, board, commission or institution 25 
contain a provision that the manufactured goods, including iron and steel, used or 26 
supplied in the performance of the contract or any subcontract under the con tract must be 27 
manufactured in the United States.  This requirement does not apply to municipalities or 28 
school administrative units.  29 
The bill requires that, in the case of a manufactured good other than an iron or steel 30 
product, all of the manufacturing pro cesses take place in the United States and the origin 31 
of the manufactured good's components or subcomponents meet a minimum level of 32 
domestic content as established by rule.  33 
Under the Act, a public agency may apply to the Governor or the Governor's designe e 34 
for a waiver of the requirement if the executive head of the public agency finds that the 35 
application of the requirement would be inconsistent with the public interest, that the 36 
necessary manufactured goods are not produced in the United States in suffic ient and 37 
reasonably available quantities and of a satisfactory quality or that inclusion of 38
