SUMMARY  4 
This bill amends the laws governing the enforcement of the marine resources laws in 5 
the following ways.  6 
1.  It specifies that notices of penalties and hearings are deemed received 3 days after 7 
they are mailed.  8 
2.  It authorizes the Commissioner of Marine Resources to deny the renewal of a 9 
license for an elver harvester or elver dealer who has not paid a pecuniary gain fine 10 
assessed to that harvester or dealer for buying or selling elvers in excess of that 11 
harvester's or dealer's quota.  12 
3.  It amends the consent to inspection pr ovision to allow covert electronic 13 
surveillance by the Bureau of Marine Patrol, including allowing the bureau to place 14 
electronic surveillance equipment on lobster vessels for the purpose of determining if a 15 
lobster and crab fishing license holder is fishi ng over the trap limit.  16 
4.  It imposes a time limit of up to 60 days for an administrative hearing on a license 17 
suspension to be held, in order to prevent an individual from continually delaying a 18 
license suspension.  19 
5.  It changes the penalty for scrubbin g egged lobsters from a one -year license 20 
suspension to license revocation.  21 
6.  It specifies that notice of failure to comply with monthly reporting requirements 22 
must be by mail or by serving the notice in hand and not by e -mail or telephone.  23
