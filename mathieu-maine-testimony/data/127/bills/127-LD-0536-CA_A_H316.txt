COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A" to H.P. 360, L.D. 536, Bill, "An Act To 
Amend Provisions Regarding the Appointment of Members of the Maine Charter School 
Commission" 
Amend the bill in section I in paragraph A by striking out all of subparagraph (1) 
(page 1, lines 7 to 12 in L.D.) and inserting the following: 
'(1) Three members are appointed by the state board and must be members of the 
state board, and those 3 members, shall nominate the other 4 members ,,,,he must 
be approved by a majority vote of the state beard for appointment to the 
commission. Each nomination by' the state board is subject to review by the joint 
standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over education matters 
and to confirmation by the Senate.' 
Amend the bill by stliking out all of section 2 (page 2, lines 18 to 23 in L.D.) and 
inserting the following; 
'Sec. 2. Transition.' Notwithstanding the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, 
section 2405, subsection 8, members of the Maine Charter School Commission serving 
on the effective date of this Act continue to serve until the completion of their tenns. 
Their successors are nominated and appointed as provided in Title 20-A, section 2405, 
subsection 8, paragraph A.' 
SUMMARY 
This amendment is the majolity report of the committee. The amendment makes 
changes to the provisions in the bill that apply to the appointment of 4 of the 7 members 
of the Maine Charter School Commission. Instead of'having the Governor appoint the 4 
commission members nominated by the 3 members of the State Board of Education 
appointed to the commission by the state board, the amendment provides that the 4 
commission members are nominated by those 3 members of the state board appointed to 
the commission and are subject to review by the joint standing committee of the 
Legislature having jurisdiction over education matters and to confinnalion by the Senate,
