26 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, replaces the concept 
27 draft to establish the Summer Success Program Fund, a dedicated fund to be directed and 
28 administered by the Commissioner of Education and held by the Treasurer of State, to 
29 encourage the facilitation of high-quality summer success programs in school 
30 administrative units throughout the State. The amendment accomplishes the following. 
31 1. It provides that money paid into the fund may include General Fund 
32 appropriations, as well as grants, gifts and other money from any unit of federal, state or 
33 local government or from any person, firm, partnership or corporation for deposit to the 
34 fund and money received from a social impact bond. It requires that school 
35 administrative units ensure that grants provided from the fund for expanding access to 
36 summer success programs supplement and not supplant federal funding.

Fiscal Detail and Notes 
This bill includes General Fund appropriations of $76,729 in fiscal year 2015-16 and $99,394 in fiscal year 2016-17 
to a newly established Summer Success Program within the Department of Education for one Education Specialist III 
position and related all other costs to implement and administer the Maine Summer Success Program. 
This bill also includes Other Special Revenue Funds base allocations of $500 per year beginning in fiscal year 2015-
16 in the event that funds are received from other sources such as grants, gifts or other monies from State, federal or 
local sources, from any person, firm, partnership or corporation and from social impact bonds. 
The Department of Education is required to provide funding to cover 90% of the costs of summer success programs in 
eligible school administrative units beginning in fiscal year 2016-17 through a grant application process. If sufficient 
funding is not available to support this requirement tlu'ough the alternative sources of funding listed in the bill, 
additional General Fund appropriations will be required.
