Sec. 2.  7 MRSA §416 is enacted to read:  12 
§416.  Purchasing food from known sources  13 
1.  License not required for sale of farm food produ cts.  An agricultural producer 14 
is exempt from licensing requirements under Title 22, chapter 551 for sales of farm food 15 
products when the sales:  16 
A.  Are made directly to an informed end consumer; and  17 
B.  Occur at a farmers' market, on the farm where the fa rm food product originated or 18 
at the home of the informed end consumer.  19 
2.  License not required for sale or offering of homemade food.  A home kitchen 20 
producer is exempt from licensing requirements under Title 22, chapter 551 for sales or 21 
the offering of homemade food:  22 
A.  Directly to an informed end consumer; and  23 
B.  Occurring at a farmers' market, at the home of the home kitchen producer or at a 24 
traditional community social event.  25 
3.  Food exempt from inspection.  Farm food products and homemade food 26 
produced and sold in this State or prepared and served in this State are not subject to 27 
inspection by the department under section 446 or Title 22, section 2162 or 2164 when:  28 
A.  The transaction is between an agricultural producer or a home kitchen producer 29 
and an informed end consumer; or  30 
B.  The farm food product or homemade food is sold or served at a traditional 31 
community social event or an agritourism event.  32 
Farm food products and homemade food must be sold in packages from a private home 33 
kitchen, farm st and or farmers' market with a label on each package containing the 34 
following information:  the name, address, including zip code, and phone number of the 35 
producer; the name of the food product; the ingredients of the food product in descending 36

This bill facilitates direct sales between Maine farmers and consumers.  It allows 17 
persons preparing food in their own homes to sell directly to consumers or to offer 18
