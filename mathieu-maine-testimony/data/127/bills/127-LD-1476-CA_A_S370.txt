5 SUMMARY 
6 This amendment replaces the bill. The bill allows a carbon monoxide detector 
7 installed in certain buildings to be powered by a battery other than a nonreplaceable 10-
8 year battery if the detector uses low-power radio frequency wireless communication, uses 
9 multiple sensors, has low-frequency audible notification capability or is connected to a 
10 control panel. The amendment clarifies that a carbon monoxide detector that is powered 
11 by replaceable batteries is allowed if the carbon monoxide detector uses a low-power 
12 radio frequency wireless communication signal, uses multiple sensors, has low-frequency 
13 audible notification capability or is connected to a control panel.
