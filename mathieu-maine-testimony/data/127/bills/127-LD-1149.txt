 Sec. 1.  Maine Center for Disease Control and Prevention to report.  1 
Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services, Maine Center for 2 
Disease Control and Prevention shall report by October 1, 2015 to the Joint Standing 3 
Committee on Health and Human Services on state -led efforts to achieve goals identified 4 
in its document "Healthy Maine 2020" pertaining to reproductive health, including 5 
strategies to reduce preterm births, increase the proportion of live births that are the result 6 
of an intended  pregnancy, reduce the rate of infant death, increase the proportion of 7 
pregnant women who receive early and adequate prenatal care and increase abstinence 8 
from alcohol, cigarettes and illicit drugs among pregnant women.  The report must 9 
include a descript ion of the activities and efforts led by the Maine Center for Disease 10 
Control and Prevention and other state -operated programs that seek to improve the health 11 
and welfare of citizens of the State, including efforts to increase high school graduation 12 
rates.   Where an effort has failed or is failing to meet a "Healthy Maine 2020" goal, the 13 
report must include an explanation why the effort has not or is not meeting the goal and 14 
include evidence -based strategies or recommendations on how state programs can meet  15 
the goal; and be it further  16

SUMMARY  21 
This resolve directs the Department of Health and Human Services, Maine Center for 22 
Disease Control and Prevention to report by October 1, 2015 to the Joint Standing 23 
Committee on Health and Human Services on state-led efforts to achieve goals identified 24 
in its document "Healthy Maine 2020" pertaining to reproductive health.  The report must 25 
include an explanation of failed or failing efforts to meet a goal and evidence -based 26 
strategies or recommendations on ho w state programs can meet the goal and a description 27 
of the State's efforts to improve the health and welfare of its citizens, including efforts to 28 
increase high school graduation rates.  The joint standing committee is authorized to 29 
report out a bill base d on the report to the Second Regular Session of the 127th 30 
Legislature.  31
