15 SUMMARY 
16 This amendment implements additional recommendations of the Commission To 
17 Study the Public Reserved Lands Management Fund that were not included in the bill. 
18 These recommendations include: 
19 1. Directing the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to conduct a 
20 detailed forest inventory of the State's public reserved lands and public nonreserved lands 
21 by March 15, 2017 and every 5 years thereafter; 
22 2. Establishing additional guidelines that must be followed by the Department of 
23 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry before it may adopt any rule that would make 
24 changes to the annual allowable harvesting level for public reserved lands; 
25 3. Directing the Bureau of Parks and Lands within the Department of Agriculture, 
26 Conservation and Forestry to identify areas where the State does not currently have 
27 deeded access to the State's public reserved lands. This amendment also requires the 
28 bureau to direct regional foresters in the bureau who are familiar with the physical 
29 landscape and landowners to develop goals and priorities regarding increased access to 
30 the State's public reserved lands and requires the bureau to submit their findings to the 
31 joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over public reserved lands 
32 matters by March 1, 2018; 
33 4. Directing the Bureau of Parks and Lands within the Department of Agriculture, 
34 Conservation and Forestry to develop a statewide priority list of recreational 
35 infrastructure projects for the State's public reserved lands and a statewide priority list of 
36 projects under the federal Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 for the State's public 
37 reserved lands. The priority lists must be presented to the joint standing committee of the 
38 Legislature having jurisdiction over public reserved lands matters by March 1, 2018. 
39 After receipt of the lists, the joint standing committee is required to hold a meeting for the 
40 purpose of obtaining public input related to the lists; and

The general operating expenses of this study are projected to be $1,500 in fiscal year 2016-17. The Legislature's 
budget includes $10,000 in in fiscal year 2016-17 for legislative studies. Whether this amount is sufficient to fund all 
studies will depend on the number of studies authorized by the Legislative Council and the Legislature. The 
additional costs of providing staffing assistance to the study during the interim can be absorbed utilizing existing 
budgeted staff resources. 
Fiscal Detail and Notes 
The bill requires the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry (ACF) to conduct a detailed forest 
inventory of the State's public reserved lands and public nonreserved lands, provide educational grants to certain 
educational programs related to logging and to develop a statewide priority list of projects under the federal 
Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 for the State's public reserved lands. This bill includes Other Special 
Revenue Funds allocations to the Public Reserved Lands Management Fund within ACF in fiscal year 2016-17 of 
$65,000 for the forest inventory, $300,000 for educational grants, $50,000 for conducting recreational infrastructure 
projects related to the federal Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 and $3,250 for other related costs. The Public 
Reserved Lands Management Fund has sufficient resources to cover these expenditures.
