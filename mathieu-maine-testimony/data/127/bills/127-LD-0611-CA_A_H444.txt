SUMMARY 
This amendment is the minority report of the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary. 
This amendment replaces the bill and changes the title, It amends the laws governing 
hospitals to require every hospital licensed in this State to hold at least annually a public 
meeting to discuss issues relating to the operation of the hospital and concerns of the 
community with respect to the delivery of services at the hospital. 
The chief executive officer of the hospital, the chair of the hospital board of trustees 
and at least 51 % of the members of the hospital board of trustees must attend the meeting 
and be available to respond to questions from members of the public. 
The meeting must be open to members of the public and organized so as. to provide 
the community served by the hospital with information about the operation of the hospital 
and to provide an opportunity for members of the public to ask questions and raise issues 
of concern to them. 
The hospital is directed to provide public notice of the meeting by posting notice at 
the hospital and publishing notice in the newspaper and on the hospital's publicly 
accessible website. The hospital is required to provide notice to the Department of 
Health and Human Services, and the department is directed to post the notice on the 
department's publicly accessible website.
