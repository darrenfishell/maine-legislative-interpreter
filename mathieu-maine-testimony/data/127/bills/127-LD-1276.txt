SUMMARY  22 
This bill:  23 
1.  Directs the Department of Education to terminate the State's membership in the 24 
Smarter Balanced Assessment Consortium and adopt a method of education assessment 25 
that complies with federal law but does not collect or disseminate personal data and 26 
attributes of students, such as attitudes, values, motivations, stereotypes or feelings;  27 
2. Requires that a state assessment of student achievement be developed with direct 28 
input from teachers, parents and school boards and specifically address the needs of 29 
students and citizens of the State;  30 
3. Requires that personally identifying data of a student derived from a state 31 
assessment of student achievement be disseminated only w ith the express written 32 
permission of each of the student's parents or guardians;  33 
4. Requires that the State's participation in or entrance into an agreement with an 34 
organization, consortium or association in establishing or implementing standards and a 35 
state assessment of student performance be done through major substantive rules; and  36 
5.  Prohibits a state assessment of student achievement from being aligned with the 37 
so-called common core state standards initiated and adopted by several states.  38
