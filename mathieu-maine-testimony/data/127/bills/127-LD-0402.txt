Sec. 1.  Rule adoption.  Resolved:  That the Department of Labor shall amend its 19 
rule regarding proof of ownership of logging equipment and recruitment by employers 20 
employing foreign laborers to operate logging equipment in conformance with the 21 
provisionally adopted major substantive rule submitted to the 126th Legislature for 22 
review, except that the rule must incorporate the following changes that were authorized 23 
by the 126th Legislature:  24 
1.  The portion of the rule designated in the provisionally a dopted rule as Section 25 
VI(A) must be amended to clarify that each foreign laborer may use only one type of 26 
equipment for an employer; and  27 
2.  The portion of the rule designated in the provisionally adopted rule as Section III 28 
must be amended to provide tha t a foreign laborer may not own any equipment used in 29 
the course of the employment, directly or indirectly, except as to equipment for which 30 
there is a prevailing rate established by the United States Department of Labor.  The 31 
penalty for violation of this  requirement must be a fine of not less than $5,000 and not 32 
more than $25,000 assessed against the employer of that foreign laborer and collected by 33 
the Commissioner of Labor.  Additionally, the rule must state that, upon conviction of a 34 
violation of this requirement, the Commissioner of Labor may prohibit the employer from 35 
employing foreign laborers in the State for 2 years.  36 
The department is not required to hold hearings or undertake further proceedings 37 
prior to adoption of the rule in accordance with thi s section.  38 
The department shall implement the rule using existing resources; and be it further  39
