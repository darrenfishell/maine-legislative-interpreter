 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 1 
become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and  2 
Whereas, the 90 -day period may not terminate until after the beginning of the next 3 
fiscal year; and  4 
Whereas, certain obligations and expenses incident to the operation of state 5 
departments and institutions will become due and payable immediately; and  6 
Whereas, costs for providing public education have increased at an alarming rate 7 
despite declining stude nt populations and without significant improvement in student 8 
outcomes, requiring immediate attention to address the underlying condition and its 9 
consequences; and  10 
Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 11 
the mean ing of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 12 
immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 13 
therefore,  14 
Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:  15

SUMMARY  11 
Part A of the bill directs the Commissioner of Education or the commissioner's 12 
designee to convene, no later than May 1, 2016, a commission to reform public education 13 
funding and improve stud ent performance in the State. The Commissioner of Education is 14 
directed to submit by January 10, 2017 and January 10, 2018 to the Governor and the 15 
joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over education matters a 16 
report of the commis sion that includes findings and recommendations for action to reform 17 
public education funding and improve student performance in Maine. Notwithstanding 18 
Joint Rule 353, the commissioner is authorized to submit to the Legislature a bill to 19 
implement the comm ission's recommendations.  20 
Part B requires the State Controller to transfer $15,000,000 in fiscal year 2016 -17 21 
from the General Fund unappropriated surplus to the General Purpose Aid for Local 22 
Schools program, General Fund account within the Department of E ducation.  23 
Part C establishes the total cost of education from kindergarten to grade 12 for fiscal 24 
year 2016 -17, the state contribution and the annual target state share percentage. In 25 
addition, Part C accomplishes the following.  26 
1.  Under current law, the Commissioner of Education may report and pay to the 27 
Treasurer of State to be credited to the National Board Certification Salary Supplement 28 
Fund, Other Special Revenue Funds account within the Department of Education no more 29 
than $335,000 in fiscal year 20 14-15 from fees for the initial issuance of and renewal of 30 
teacher, education specialist and administrator certificates. This Part extends that 31 
limitation to each fiscal year beyond fiscal year 2014 -15. 32 
2.  This Part changes from fiscal year 2016 -17 to fis cal year 2017 -18 when the State 33 
must begin increasing the state share percentage of the funding for the cost of essential 34 
programs and services by at least one percentage point per year over the percentage of the 35 
previous year until the state share percent age of the total cost of funding public education 36 
from kindergarten to grade 12 reaches 55%.  37 
3.  It adds, beginning in fiscal year 2016 -17, charter schools to the list of essential 38 
programs and services components to be reviewed.  39 
 
 Page 12 - 127LR2742(01) -1 
 4.  It changes the deadli ne for the commissioner to make annual recommendations 1

Preliminary Fiscal Impact Statement
Legislative Cost/Study
Additional costs to the Department of Education to provide staff support to the commission can be absorbed within 
existing budgeted resources.  Additional costs to the University of Maine System, the Maine Community College 
System and the State Board of Education to serve on the commission can be absorbed within existing budgeted 
resources.Providing for a one-time transfer of $15,000,000 from the unappropriated surplus of the General Fund to the General 
Purpose Aid for Local Schools program, General Fund account within the Department of Education no later than June 
30, 2017 will decrease the funds available in the General Fund for other purposes. The State Budget Officer, upon 
approval of the Governor, is authorized to allot these funds. The general operating expenses of this study are projected to be $1,000 in fiscal year 2015-16 and $3,000 in fiscal 
year 2016-17. The Legislature's budget includes $10,000 in fiscal year 2015-16 and $10,000 in fiscal year 2016-17 for 
legislative studies.  Whether the amounts are sufficient to fund all studies will depend on the number of studies 
authorized by the Legislative Council and the Legislature.
