2.  Program established.   The Vaccine Consumer Protection Program is established 15 
within the department.  The Vaccine Consumer Protection Office is established within the 16 
department to carry out the purposes of the program.  17 
3.  Services.   Under the program, the office must provide information about vaccine 18 
injuries and immunizations to health care providers and the public.  19 
The office shall establish and implement procedures to:  20 
A.  Promote public awareness of the Vaccine Injury Ta ble through development and 21 
implementation of an educational outreach program that provides, at a minimum, the 22 
information contained in the Vaccine Injury Table to any individual in the State who 23 
requests information related to vaccine injuries or immuniza tions;  24 
B.  Educate health care providers on the contents of the Vaccine Injury Table to help 25 
providers better screen for and diagnose adverse events caused by vaccines;  26 
C.  Require health care providers to use vaccine package inserts that describe the 27 
warn ings, precautions and adverse reactions potentially caused by a particular 28 
vaccine during the screening and diagnosing of an illness, disability, injury or adverse 29 
condition caused by vaccines;  30 
D.  Write and publicize a Maine Vaccine Information Statement.   The statement must 31 
include evidence -based information about vaccines and their potentially adverse side 32 
effects, inform patients about their right to opt out of vaccine requirements based on 33 
religious and philosophical grounds and offer a step -by-step ex planation about how to 34 
pursue legal recourse if an individual suspects a vaccine injury has occurred.  When 35 
drafting the statement, the department shall consult with nationally recognized 36 
vaccine safety advocates and the families of Maine children who have  been injured 37 
by vaccines.  38
