§3331.  Annual adjustment of tax rates  5 
1.  Generally.   Beginning July 1, 2015, the excise tax imposed upo n internal 6 
combustion engine fuel pursuant to section 2903, subsection 1 and the excise tax imposed 7 
upon distillates pursuant to section 3203, subsection 1 -B are subject to a rate of 8 
adjustment pursuant to this section. On or about February 15th of each ye ar, the State Tax 9 
Assessor shall calculate the adjusted rates by multiplying the rates in effect on the 10 
calculation date by a fuel economy index as computed in subsection 2. The adjusted rates 11 
must then be rounded to the nearest 1/10 of a cent and become e ffective on the first day 12 
of July immediately following the calculation. The assessor shall publish the adjusted fuel 13 
tax rates and shall provide all necessary forms and reports to suppliers, distributors and 14 
retail dealers.  15 
2.  Method of calculation; fuel  economy index defined.   Except as provided in 16 
subsection 3, the fuel economy index is based on increases to federal fuel efficiency 17 
standards as set out in this subsection.  18 
A.  Starting with fiscal year 2016 and every fiscal year thereafter, the fuel econ omy 19 
index is the percentage change in the corporate average fuel economy standard 20 
established by the federal Department of Transportation, National Highway Traffic 21 
Safety Administration, averaged for all classes of passenger cars and light trucks, 22 
between the model year of the current fiscal year and the model year corresponding 23 
with the upcoming fiscal year.  24 
B.  The fuel economy index for fiscal year 2016 is 1.046.  25 
3.  Maximum.   The fuel economy index established in subsection 2 may not exceed 26 
the Chained Consumer Price Index as defined in section 5402, subsection 1 for the 27 
calendar year ending on the December 31st immediately preceding the calculation date, 28 
divided by the Chained Consumer Price Index for the previous calendar year.  29 
4.  Exclusion.   This sec tion does not apply to internal combustion engine fuel 30 
purchased or used for the purpose of propelling jet or turbojet engine aircraft.  31 
5.  Legislative review.   Starting in 2021 and in each odd -numbered year thereafter, 32 
the Department of Transportation sha ll submit a bill by the cloture date established for 33 
departments and agencies that suspends any adjustment in fuel tax rates in the upcoming 34 
biennium resulting from the operation of this section.  35
