SUMMARY  14 
This bill implements the recommendations of the Commission To Study the Public 15 
Reserved Lands Management Fund.  16 
The committee has not taken a position on the substance of the commission's plan or 17 
this bill and by  reporting this bill out the committee is not suggesting and does not intend 18 
to suggest that it agrees or disagrees with any aspect of the commission's plan or this bill.  19 
The committee is reporting the bill out for the sole purpose of turning the commissi on's 20 
proposal into a printed bill that can be referred to the committee for an appropriate public 21 
hearing and subsequent processing in the normal course.  The committee is taking this 22 
action to ensure clarity and transparency in the legislative review of t he commission's 23 
proposal.  24 
Part A prohibits any expenditures from the Public Reserved Lands Management Fund 25 
other than for the general operations of the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and 26 
Forestry, Bureau of Parks and Lands with respect to manageme nt of public reserved 27 
lands, unless the fund has at least $2,500,000 at the start of the fiscal year.  28 
Additionally, it gives the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 29 
jurisdiction over public reserved lands matters the authority to approve all  expenditures 30 
from the Public Reserved Lands Management Fund.  This authority was repealed by 31 
Public Law 2013, chapter 368, Part LLLL, section 2.  32 
It requires the bureau's annual report dealing with public reserved lands to include a 33 
breakdown of growth and  yield in any regional public reserved lands units, identifying 34 
any harvesting that occurred during the preceding fiscal year in individual management 35 
units where yield exceeds annual growth.  36
