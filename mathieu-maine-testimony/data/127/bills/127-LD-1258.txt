SUMMARY  8 
This bill amends the Maine Medical Use of Marijuana Act in the following ways.  9 
1.  It clarifies that a primary caregiver may cultivate up to 6 marijuana plants for each 10 
of up to 5 qualifying patients.  11 
2.  It allows a primary caregiver to employ or contract with more than one person to 12 
assist with the duties required of that primary caregive r.  The Department of Health and 13 
Human Services is required to adopt rules regarding the licensing of these assistants.  14 
3.  It allows the Department of Health and Human Services to make onsite 15 
assessments of registered primary caregivers who cultivate mari juana for 3 or more 16 
registered patients at a time to ensure compliance.  17 
4.  It allows a registered primary caregiver to transfer excess prepared marijuana to a 18 
qualifying patient for reasonable compensation.  19 
5.  It provides that a primary caregiver or regi stered dispensary that receives 20 
compensation from a qualifying patient for the costs associated with cultivating 21 
marijuana for that qualifying patient or assisting that qualifying patient is required to 22 
register as a seller with the State Tax Assessor and collect and remit sales tax.  Under the 23 
current law, marijuana is specifically excluded from the sales tax exemption for 24 
medicines.  25
