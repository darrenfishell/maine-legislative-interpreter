SUMMARY  35 
This bill protects the social media privacy of employees and applicants for 36 
employment.  37

 The bill prohibits an employer from requiring or coercing an employee or applicant to 1 
disclose, or requesting that an employee or applicant disclose, the password or any other 2 
means for accessing a personal social media account.  It also prohibits an empl oyer from 3 
requiring or coercing an employee or applicant to access, or requesting that an employee 4 
or applicant access, a personal social media account in the presence of the employer or an 5 
agent of the employer.  It also prohibits an employer from requiri ng or coercing an 6 
employee or applicant to disclose any personal social media account information, except 7 
when the employer reasonably believes it to be relevant to an investigation of allegations 8 
of employee misconduct or a workplace -related violation of applicable laws, rules or 9 
regulations but only when not otherwise prohibited by law.  It that situation, the employer 10 
may access the information and use it solely to the extent necessary for purposes of that 11 
investigation or a related proceeding.  12 
The bill prohibits an employer from requiring or causing an employee or applicant to 13 
add anyone, including the employer or the agent of the employer, to the employee's or 14 
applicant's list of contacts associated with a personal social media account.  It also 15 
prohibi ts an employer from requiring or causing an employee or applicant to alter, or 16 
requesting that employee or applicant alter, settings that affect a 3rd party's ability to 17 
view the contents of a personal social media account.  18 
The bill prohibits an employer f rom taking adverse action against an employee or 19 
applicant who refuses to take part in actions that the employer is prohibited from taking.  20 
It allows a court in a civil action to award a prevailing plaintiff declaratory relief, 21 
damages and reasonable atto rney's fees and costs and injunctive relief against an 22 
employer or the agent of the employer.  23
