SUMMARY  8 
This bill directs Jobs for Maine's Graduates, a state -authorized nonprofit corporation, 9 
to provide mentoring and counseling services, course guidance and graduation planning, 10 
peer support services and financial guidance to up to 200 students and at up to 4 11 
postsecondary institutions in the State for the purpose o f significantly increasing the 12 
percentage of eligible students who obtain a postsecondary degree.  Eligible students 13 
must have previously been enrolled in a Jobs for Maine's Graduates high school program, 14 
been in or currently be in foster care or have earn ed a high school equivalency diploma 15 
within the 5 years prior to obtaining these services.  16
