§471.  Definitions  22 
As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 23

1.  Amusement ride.  "Amusement ride" means a device or combination of devices 25

course or within a defined area for the primary purpose of amusement or entertainment.  27 
"Amusement ride" does not include nonmechanized playground equipment or a coin - 28 
operated ride that is manually, mechanically or electrically operated, is customarily 29 
placed in a public location and does not normally require the supervision o r services of an 30 
operator.  31 
2.  Amusement show.  "Amusement show" means a fixed or traveling show, whether 32 
held indoors or outdoors, for which admission is charged and that is designed to provide 33 
amusement to members of the public, except that "amusement sh ow" does not include a 34 
circus.  "Amusement show" includes but is not limited to a carnival, thrill show, ice show 35

SUMMARY  3 
The bill does the following.  4 
Part A restores the inspection and certificate requirements for boilers located in 5 
structures used by schools or colleges for the purpose of housing classrooms, 6 
gymnasiums, audit oriums or dormitories.  These provisions were discontinued by Public 7 
Law 2013, chapter 595, Part U, section 11.  8 
Part B allows qualified private sector organizations or individuals to inspect 9 
amusement rides to ensure they are safe for the public.  The bill  assigns oversight 10 
responsibilities over the certification of qualified amusement ride inspectors to the Office 11 
of the State Fire Marshal.  The bill also corrects cross -references.  12
