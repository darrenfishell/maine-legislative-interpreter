SUMMARY  10 
This bill makes the following changes to the laws governing fish  and wildlife.  11 
1.  It eliminates the disabled hunter, trapper and angler advisory committee and a 12 
reference to that committee.  13 
2.  It provides that a person must hold a valid archery hunting license to hunt with a 14 
hand -held bow and arrow during any open se ason on a bird or animal and that a person 15 
must hold a valid big game archery license or big game hunting license and a valid 16 
crossbow hunting license to hunt with a crossbow during any open season on a bird or 17 
animal.  18 
3.  It enacts in the statutes certain  requirements regulating the use of crossbows and 19 
eliminates the maximum draw weight for crossbows of 200 pounds.  20 
4.  It amends the supervisory requirements for apprentice hunter licenses and 21 
apprentice trapper licenses.  It specifies that the holder of an  apprentice hunter license 22 
must hunt in the presence of a person who is 18 years of age or older and who holds a 23 
valid adult hunting license and specifies that the holder of an apprentice trapper license 24 
must trap in the presence of a person that has held a valid adult trapper license for the 25 
prior 3 years.  26 
5.  It specifies that a nonresident junior hunting license includes all permits, stamps 27 
and other permissions needed to hunt at no additional cost.  28 
6.  It corrects cross -references to statutory provision s that authorize the taking of 29 
wildlife that attack domestic animals, destroy property or cause damage to crops or 30 
orchards.  31 
7.  It eliminates language imposing a 5% surcharge on agents who fail to pay the 32 
amount owed the State for watercraft registration,  snowmobile registration and all -terrain 33 
vehicle registration.  34 
8.  It repeals the provision of current law that prohibits a person from operating an 35 
all-terrain vehicle on a private road after being forbidden to do so by the owner or a 36 
municipal official.  37
