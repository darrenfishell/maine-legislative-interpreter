6 SUMMARY 
7 This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment does the following. 
8 1. It requires carbon monoxide detectors in all fraternity houses, sorority houses and 
9 dormitories affiliated with educational facilities, children's homes, shelters and residential 
10 care facilities licensed by the Department of Health and Human Services and eating and 
II lodging places and lodging places. 
12 2. It requires carbon monoxide detectors in all educational facilities, bllt it exempts 
13 donuitories and other buildings of educational facilities that meet National Fire 
14 Protection Association standards. 
15 3. It defines "educational facility" to mean public and private postsecondary 
16 institutions and child care facilities. 
17 4. It specifies which types of power sources for carbon monoxide detectors may be 
18 used in which buildings. 
19 5. It provides a schedule for public and private postsecondary institutions to achieve 
20 full compliance by January 1, 2019. 
21 6. It includes a transition provision for carbon monoxide detectors in use on the 
22 effective date of this legislation that have nonconforming power sources. 
23 7. It provides an effective date of January 1, 2016 .
