SUMMARY  30 
This bill requires a retailer to assess a 5¢ fee for a single -use disposable plastic or 31 
kraft paper bag distributed to a customer at the point of retail sale.  The retailer retains 1¢ 32 
from each 5¢ fee for administrative costs and m ay elect to retain an additional 1¢ from 33 
each 5¢ fee to provide a carryout program that credits a customer no less than 5¢ for each 34 
bag provided by the customer at the point of retail sale for packaging purchases.  The 35 
revenues from the fee, less the porti on retained by the retailer for administrative costs 36 
and, if applicable, the carryout program, are credited to the Plastics and Paper Recycling 37 
Fund administered by the Department of Environmental Protection for the purpose of 38 
promoting recycling efforts i n the State related to plastics and paper.  A retailer must 39 
provide reusable bags for purchase by customers.  This bill authorizes the Department of 40 
Environmental Protection to enforce these laws and impose penalties for violations.  This 41 
bill also directs  that, by January 1, 2016, the Department of Environmental Protection 42 
must conduct a public information campaign relating to plastics and paper and their 43
