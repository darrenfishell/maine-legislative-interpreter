SUMMARY  24 
The purpose of this bill is to clarify the laws governing the employment of minors 25 
and to conform the State’s laws to federal law, thus expanding work opportunities for 26 
minors.  27 
1.  It clarifies that minors who are younger than 14 years of age may be employed 28 
only in the planting, cultivating or harvesting of field crops, or other agricultural 29 
employment th at does not place them in direct contact with hazardous machinery or 30 
hazardous substances.  31 
2.  It conforms to federal law the hours that minors who are 16 and 17 years of age 32 
may work, by extending the permissible time until which such a minor may work on a 33 
day preceding a school day.  34 
3.  It eliminates the prohibition on minors under 16 years of age working more than 6 35 
consecutive days.  36

 4.  Eliminates the prohibition on minors under 18 years of age working more than 50 1 
hours per week when school is not in s ession.  2 
5.  It specifies that the restrictions on the hours of employment of minors does not 3 
apply to:  4 
A.  Agricultural employment that does not require direct contact with hazardous 5 
machinery or hazardous substances;  6 
B.  Employment or training as a theatr ical actor or a film actor;  7 
C.  The taking or catching of lobsters, fish or other marine organisms, as long as the 8 
work does not require direct contact with hazardous machinery or hazardous 9 
substances; and  10 
D.  At a business that is solely owned by the mino r’s parent, as long as the work does 11 
not require direct contact with hazardous machinery or hazardous substances.  12 
6.  It specifies that superintendents of school administrative units issue work permits 13 
to minors and copy the Department of Labor, Bureau of  Labor Standards; the bureau 14 
develops the blank permits, furnishes them to the superintendents and serves as a resource 15 
for superintendents regarding federal and state law.  16 
7.  It maintains the authority of the bureau to revoke work permits if state or fed eral 17 
law has been violated.  18 
8.  It repeals obsolete language dealing with triplicate permits and a master permit 19 
system.  20
