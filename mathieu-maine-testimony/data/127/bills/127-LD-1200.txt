SUMMARY  20 
This bill creates a civil cause of action for intentional interference with business 21 
operations.  "Business operations" is defined to mean an activity engaged in with the 22 
object of gain, benefi t or advantage, either direct or indirect, by a private entity.  The bill 23 
provides that:  24 
1.  An individual is liable to a private entity for damages if the individual commits an 25 
act that hinders, impairs or obstructs or attempts to hinder, impair or obstru ct the 26 
performance of business operations by the private entity;  27 
2.  An organization that promotes, advocates for or directs an individual to engage in 28 
the acts described in the bill is liable to the private entity in the same manner as the 29 
individual comm itting the act;  30 
3.  The private entity may recover damages by filing an action in court and may be 31 
awarded actual damages, a civil penalty and costs and fees, including reasonable 32 
attorney's fees; and  33 
4.  A person who knowingly uses the provisions in this bill to demand or extract 34 
money from an individual or organization that is not legally obligated to pay damages 35 
commits a Class E crime.  36
