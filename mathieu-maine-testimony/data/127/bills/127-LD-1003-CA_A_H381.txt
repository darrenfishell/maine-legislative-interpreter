SUMMARY 
This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, replaces the bill. It 
amends the Maine Human Rights Act to expand the protection from employment 
discrimination to cover the use of a particular drug, device or medical service related to 
the reproductiv~ health decisions of employees, the dependents of employees and 
applicants for employment. It also prohibits an employer, employment agency or labor 
organization from obtaining or inquiring about infonnation about reproductive health 
decisions of an employee, an employee's dependent or an applicant for employment.
