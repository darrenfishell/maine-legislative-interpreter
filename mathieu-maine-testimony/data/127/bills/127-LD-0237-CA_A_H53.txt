The bill establishes a Consumer Advisor position within the Office of the Public Advocate (OPA) to advise members 
of the using and consuming public regarding participation in proceedings beforethe Public Utilities Commission. The 
bill includes an Other Special Revenue Funds allocation in fiscal years 2015-16 and 2016-17 of $111,749 and 
$109,858, respectively, for the position plus support costs. The I?osition was previously established by Financial Order 
as a Limited Period position and is set to end on June 30, 2015. The OPA has indicated that it already has sufficient 
resources available to fund the allocation. 
The Governor's Proposed Budget for the 2016-2017 Biennium also includes $111,749 in fiscal year 2015-16 and
