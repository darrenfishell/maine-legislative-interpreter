§13201.  Definitions  9

§13203.   Invest in Maine Capital Fund  10 
1.  Invest in Maine Capital Fund.  The Invest in Maine Capital Fund is established 11 
as a nonlapsing revolving loan and equity fund administered by the authority to support 12 
the capital needs of major business development projec ts.  The fund consists of sums that 13 
are appropriated by the Legislature or transferred to the fund from time to time by the 14 
State Controller, interest earned from the investment of fund balances, state bond issues, 15 
state employees' pension funds, instituti onal endowments, seed capital investment tax 16 
credits, income from subsection 13204, subsection 4 and other funds from any public or 17 
private source received for use for any of the purposes for which the fund has been 18 
established.  Disbursements from the fun d must be made pursuant to section 13204.  19 
§13204.  Loan and equity support for major business development projects  20 
1.  Loan  and equity support.  The authority shall recommend to the Finance 21 
Authority of Maine disbursements from the fund or shall provide o ther financial support 22 
to a qualifying applicant to support a major business development project pursuant to this 23 
section. Any financial support provided by this section must be memorialized in a written 24 
contract as determined by the authority.  25 
2.  Criteri a to qualify for financial support.  The authority shall provide financial 26 
support to an applicant to support a major business development project based upon the 27 
following criteria:  28 
A.  The creditworthiness of the applicant, considering factors such as the  applicant's 29 
historical financial performance, management ability, plan to market the applicant's 30 
product or service and whether the applicant meets or exceeds industry average 31 
financial performance ratios commonly accepted in determining creditworthiness in 32 
that industry;  33 
B.  The sufficiency of collateral pledged by the applicant;  34 
C.  The sufficiency of projected revenues from the project or other sources to repay 35 
the financial obligation and meet the requirements of subsection 3 for the term of the 36 
obliga tion; 37 
D.  The level that financial support from the authority enhances the employment and 38 
wage benefits projected to be created by the major business development project;  39

applicant's inability to obtain financing from another source.  5 
3.  Financial support.  The authority may provide the following financial support to 6

SUMMARY  27 
This bill creates the Invest in Maine Capital Authority with bonding authority to 28 
provide loans or bond funding to major business development projects, which are def ined 29 
as development projects that have a projected cost of at least $50,000,000 or are projected 30 
to result in the creation or retention of at least 250 full -time employment positions that 31 
pay at least 125% of the state annual average weekly wage.  32
