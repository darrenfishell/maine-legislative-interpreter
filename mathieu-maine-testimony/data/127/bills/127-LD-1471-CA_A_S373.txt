17 'Sec. 1. Finance Authority of Maine to select entity to provide food 
18 harvested in the State to residents with food insecurity and to administer 
19 funds. Resolved: That the Finance Authority of Maine, through the request for 
20 proposal process, shall select and contract with an appropriate statewide entity to 
21 purchase, process, store and transport fresh and fresh frozen fruits and vegetables and 
22 seafood harvested in the State in order to increase access to fresh and fresh frozen fruit 
23 and vegetables and seafood to residents of the State with food insecurity. When selecting 
24 the entity, the authority shall consult with and accept assistance from experts in the areas 
25 of agriculture, food security and public health. The entity selected must be qualified in 
26 the safe handling of food products by adhering to all local, state and federal food safety 
27 regulations, by participating in an annual 3rd-party food safety audit performed by a 
28 nationally certified auditor and having experience in the distribution of food to low-
29 income populations. The authority shall administer the funds allocated for the purposes 
30 stated in this section until they are finally disbursed and may retain a portion of those 
31 funds for the reasonable costs of administering this section; and be it further 
32 Sec. 2. Report. Resolved: That the Finance Authority of Maine, as a condition 
33 of the award of the contract pursuant to section 1, shall require the entity selected to 
34 submit a report by December 1st of 2016, 2017 and 2018 to the joint standing committees 
35 of the Legislature having jurisdiction over agriculture, conservation and forestry matters 
36 and over health and human services matters. Each report must contain information 
37 regarding the entity's progress on meeting the purposes of section 1, including how funds

This amendment makes changes to the process by which the Finance Authority of 
Maine selects an entity to implement the program in the bill by requiring the authority to 
consult with relevant experts and clarifying the food safety qualifications the selected 
entity must possess. The amendment specifies that fresh and fresh frozen fruits and 
vegetables harvested in Maine must be provided through the program. 
23 The amendment clarifies that the program will be funded through a transfer of 
24 $3,000,000 from the Fund for a Healthy Maine on a one-time basis and the authority is 
25 directed to administer the funds allocated for the program only until they are finally 
26 disbursed. The amendment requires the selected entity to submit a report to both the joint 
27 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over agriculture, conservation 
28 and forestry matters and the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
29 jurisdiction over health and human services matters by December 1st of 2016, 2017 and 
30 2018.

This bill includes a one-time Fund for a Healthy Maine allocation of $3,000,000 in fiscal year 2016-17 to the Finance 
Authority of Maine to create and administer the Maine Harvested Food Products for Residents with Food Insecurity 
program. The unallocated balance in the Fund for a Healthy Maine is sufficient to cover this additional allocation. 
The Finance Authority of Maine has indicated that it will be able to implement and administer the program within the 
provisions established in this legislation.
