SUMMARY  20 
This bill does the following:  21 
1.  Raises to Class C the crime of sexual abuse of a minor who is either 14 or 15 years 22 
of age;  23 
2.  Raises to Class C the crim e of visual sexual aggression against a child under 14 24 
years of age;  25 
3.  Raises to Class C the crime of sexual misconduct with a child and changes the age 26 
of the victim to include children 14 years of age or younger.  Current law applies only to 27 
children u nder 14 years of age;  28 
4.  Raises to Class C the crime of solicitation of a child to commit a prohibited act 29 
and changes the age of the victim to include children 14 years of age or younger.  Current 30 
law applies only to children under 14 years of age;  31 
5.  E nacts a prohibition on traveling into, out of or within this State or attempting to 32 
travel for the purposes of illegal sexual acts involving a minor. The bill also prohibits 33 
transporting a minor, attempting to transport a minor or soliciting or enticing a minor to 34 
travel into, out of or within the State for illegal sexual purposes.  Illegal sexual purposes 35 
include engaging in a sexual act with a minor, prohibited under the Maine Revised 36 
Statutes, Title 17 -A, chapter 11; engaging in sexual exploitation of a minor, prohibited 37 
under Title 17 -A, chapter 12; or engaging in sex trafficking or prostitution, prohibited 38 
under Title 17 -A, chapter 35.  The bill makes illegal traveling a Class B crime;  39
