9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A" to S.P. 651, L.D. 1613, Bill, "An Act To 
10 Exempt from Sales Tax Meals Sold by Nonprofit Organizations" 
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
12 'An Act To Exclude from Sales Tax Certain Sales by Civic, Religious and Fraternal 
13 Organizations' 
14 Amend the bill by striking out all of sections 1 to 3 and inserting the following: 
15 'Sec. 1. 36 MRSA §1752, sub-§1-D, as amended by PL 2005, c. 218, §12, is 
16 further amended to read: 
17 1-D. Casual sale. "Casual sale" means an isolated transaction in which tangible 
18 personal property or a taxable service is sold other than in the ordinary course of repeated 
19 and successive transactions of like character by the person making the sale. "Casual sale" 
20 includes transactions at a bazaar, fair, rummage sale, meal, picnic or similar event by a 
21 civic, religious or fraternal organization that is aot a registereEl retailer. The sale by a 
22 registered retailer of tangible personal property that that retailer has used in the course of 
23 the retailer's business is not a casual sale if that property is of like character to that sold by 
24 the retailer in the ordinary course of repeated and successive transactions. "Casual sale" 
25 does not include any transaction in which a retailer sells tangible personal property or a 
26 taxable service on behalf of the owner of that property or the provider of that service.' 
27 Amend the bill by relettering or renumbering any nonconsecutive Part letter or 
28 section number to read consecutively. 
29 SUMMARY 
30 This amendment includes within the definition of "casual sale," which is a sale that is 
31 not subject to sales tax, a transaction in connection with a meal provided by a civic, 
32 religious or fraternal organization. The amendment also removes the provision that 
33 prohibits such an organization from being exempt if it is a registered retailer.
