SUMMARY  4 
This bill includes the final recommendations of the Commission on Independent 5 
Living and Disability and does the following.  6 
1.  Part A replaces the requirement in current law for biennial plans for regional 7 
transit with a requirement for quinquen nial plans, which is consistent with federal 8 
requirements.  It eliminates the Interagency Transportation Coordinating Committee and 9 
replaces it with a new public transit advisory council.  It also specifies the role of the 10 
council and requires reporting ev ery 2 years.  It requires the Department of Health and 11 
Human Services to convene a work group to develop a statewide transportation voucher 12 
program for persons with disabilities.  13 
2.  Part B adds new transition planning requirements for students with disabi lities to 14 
include team meetings that must begin at 14 years of age with community partners, 15 
community service providers, the students and their families, the division of vocational 16 
rehabilitation within the Department of Labor, Bureau of Rehabilitation Ser vices and the 17 
agency that is designated by the Governor to serve as the protection and advocacy agency 18 
for persons with disabilities.  It requires that the transition planning include independent 19 
living assessments for the students.  For students who recei ve services from the 20 
Department of Health and Human Services, Office of Child and Family Services, it 21 
requires the school administrative unit to work in consultation with the division of 22 
vocational rehabilitation within the Department of Labor, Bureau of R ehabilitation 23 
Services to include postsecondary preparation strategies for the students during transition 24 
planning.  25 
3.  Part C requires the Statewide Independent Living Council to provide an annual 26 
report to the Legislature on the State's strategic plannin g efforts to increase opportunities 27 
for persons with disabilities to live independently within the community.  It also requires 28 
the Commissioner of Labor to provide an annual report to the Legislature on the State's 29 
efforts to improve vocational rehabilita tion outcomes and reduce the length of time it 30 
takes the department to enter into an individualized plan of employment with individuals 31 
eligible to receive rehabilitation services.  32 
4.  Part D amends the Maine Human Rights Act to require an on -site inspecti on by a 33 
representative of the Office of the State Fire Marshal to ensure that new public buildings 34 
and certain buildings to which the public has access are constructed in compliance with 35 
the Maine Human Rights Act.  It also requires the Technical Building Codes and 36 
Standards Board to adopt the most recent federal Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 37 
accessibility guidelines as published by the International Code Council. It also authorizes 38 
the agency that is designated by the Governor to serve as the pro tection and advocacy 39 
agency for persons with disabilities in Maine to bring a civil action in Superior Court for 40 
violations of the Maine Human Rights Act regarding public accommodations and allows 41 
the agency to receive reasonable attorney's fees and costs.  42
