SUMMARY  19 
This bill amends  the education statutes related to regional collaboration between 20 
school administrative units to deliver certain administrative, instructional and 21 
noninstructional functions.  The bill facilitates the transformation of the public education 22 
system in the St ate by providing grant funding from the Fund for the Efficient Delivery of 23 
Educational Services to support regional collaborative agreements between 2 or more 24 
school administrative units and, whenever possible, with local and county governments 25 
and State G overnment or with any public institution of higher education or nonprofit 26 
corporation to achieve efficiencies and reduce costs in the delivery of collaborative 27 
programs and support services related to:  28 
1.  Standards that guide curriculum and instruction an d promote student advancement 29 
and graduation based on student demonstration of proficiency in meeting educational 30 
standards pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20 -A, section 4722 -A; and  31 
2.  Professional development standards that foster access to  professional 32 
development, training and support for teachers and principals and expand opportunities 33 
for professional growth for teachers and principals pursuant to Title 20 -A, chapter 508.  34 
The bill also requires the Department of Education to provisionall y adopt 35 
amendments to rules established for the Fund for the Efficient Delivery of Educational 36 
Services in order to align the awarding of grant funds to school administrative units with 37 
the provisions amended by this bill beginning with the 2016 -2017 schoo l year.  38
