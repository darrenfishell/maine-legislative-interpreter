19 'Sec. 1. Veterans transportation study to determine need. Resolved: That 
20 the Department of Transportation, in conjunction with the Department of Defense, 
21 Veterans and Emergency Management, the Department of Health and Human Services 
22 and the Department of Labor, shall conduct a study, using available federal funds, to 
23 determine the need for locally available transportation services that convey veterans to 
24 and from employment or employment-related services, medical appointments, mental 
25 health services, social services and community activities; and be it further 
26 Sec. 2. Study purpose. Resolved: That the purpose of the study is to inform the 
27 development of a pilot project to be established in a location served by a regional 
28 transportation network to provide transportation for veterans. The goal of the study and 
29 the pilot project is to improve access to essential programs and services for veterans, 
30 including, but not limited to, employment or employment-related services, medical 
31 appointments, mental health services, social services and community activities, and to 
32 inform potential future development of a long-term transportation policy designed to 
33 enable the delivery of cost-effective, sustainable and veteran-focused transportation 
34 services to meet the current and future needs of veterans in the State; and be it further 
35 Sec. 3. Participants. Resolved: That the Department of Transportation shall 
36 invite the participation of various stakeholders in the study and in the development of the

23 This amendment replaces the resolve and requires the Department of Transportation, 
24 in conjunction with the Department of Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management, 
25 the Department of Health and Human Services and the Department of Labor, to conduct a 
26 study, using available federal funds, to determine the need for locally available 
27 transportation services that convey veterans to and from employment or employment-
28 related services, medical appointments, mental health services, social services and 
29 community activities. The purpose of the study is to inform the development of a pilot 
30 project to provide transportation for veterans in order to improve access to essential 
31 programs and services for veterans and to inform potential future development of a long-
32 term transportation policy designed to enable the delivery of cost-effective, sustainable 
33 and veteran-focused transportation services to meet the current and future needs of 
34 veterans in the State. 
35 This amendment also provides $40,000 in one-time funding for the study.
