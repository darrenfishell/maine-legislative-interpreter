§480 -VV.  Beach Management Fund  6 
The Beach Management Fund, referred to in this section as "the fund," is created 7 
within the department as a nonlapsing dedicated fund for carrying out the purposes of this 8 
articl e.  Funds deposited in the fund may be disbursed only for purposes specified in this 9 
section and for reimbursement of administrative costs as specified in Title 36, section 10 
4912.  Any funds appropriated or allocated to the fund must be deposited in the fun d, and 11 
the fund may accept grants, bequests, gifts or contributions from any person.  12 
1.  Technical assistance and information.  The department may disburse funds from 13 
the fund to develop coordinated programs for technical assistance to municipalities and 14 
homeowners and to make available information to municipalities and homeowners 15 
relating to beach management, including guidance on the creation of local or municipal 16 
beach management plans.  17 
2.  Municipal projects.  The department may disburse funds from the fund to 18 
municipalities to provide assistance in the development and implementation of local 19 
beach management, preservation and restoration projects.  Disbursements from the fund 20 
to municipalities must be consistent with the priority list established under section 21 
480-UU.  The department may not disburse funds from the fund to a municipality unless 22 
the municipality has in place a written beach management plan, approved by the 23 
commissioner, that accounts for the protection and preservation of any species desi gnated 24 
as endangered or threatened pursuant to Title 12, section 12803 whose habitat is located 25 
wholly or partly within the beach area.  The department may not disburse funds from the 26 
fund to a municipality unless the municipality demonstrates that any pri vately owned 27 
portion of the beach included in the proposed project has in place all easements necessary 28 
to provide public access to that beach.  29 
3.  Administrative costs.  The department may use the fund to reimburse the 30

SUMMARY  27 
This bill implements and provides a funding mechanism for an integrated coastal 28 
beach management program.  It imposes a $1 per day fee, effective October 1, 2015, on 29 
the rental, between May 1st and October 31st, of living quarters in any hotel, rooming 30 
house o r tourist or trailer camp, to be deposited into the Beach Management Fund 31 
established by the bill.  This bill also establishes the Beach Advisory Group, chaired by 32 
the Commissioner of Environmental Protection and composed of 8 additional members 33 
from state  agencies and the public, which meets at least twice per year and advises the 34 
Department of Environmental Protection on matters related to the management, 35 
preservation and restoration of beaches in the State.  The advisory group must also 36 
deliver an annual  report to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 37 
jurisdiction over natural resources matters, and the committee may report out a bill 38 
related to the report.  This bill directs and provides criteria for the department to develop 39 
and maintai n a priority list ranking all beaches within the State based on suitability for 40 
beach nourishment, dune restoration, land acquisition or other enhancement techniques.  41 
This bill stipulates that resources in the Beach Management Fund may be used to develop 42 
coordinated programs for technical assistance to municipalities and homeowners; to 43
