Sec. J-1. Increasing reimbursement for support services for adults with 
intellectual disabilities or autistic disorder. By July 1, 2016, the Department of 
Health and Human Services shall amend its rules in rule Chapter 101: MaineCare

SUMMARY 
Part J requires the Department of Health and Human Services to amend its rules to 
increase the combined annual limit for reimbursement for home support, community 
support, work support-individual or work support-group, assistive technology and career 
planning to $47,550. The department is required to determine the number of hours of 
specific support services on the basis of the increased combined annual limit. 
Part K directs the Department of Health and Human Services to amend its rules to 
provide for increases in the personal needs allowances of residents in nursing facilities 
and residential care facilities. The rules are designated as routine technical rules. 
This amendment provides funding for the initiatives in this amendment by reducing 
the amount transferred to the Maine Budget Stabilization Fund by $4,505,567 to 
$39,994,433.

Part J of this amendment includes a General Fund appropriation of $3,004,750 in fiscal year 2016-17 for the services 
provided under Section 29: Home and Community Benefits for Members with Intellectual Disabilities or Autistic 
Disorder. Part J also includes Federal Expenditures Fund allocations required for the FMAP match. 
Part K of this amendment includes a General Fund appropriation of$1,500,817 in fiscal year 2016-17 to amend 
Department of Health and Human Services rules to provide for increases in the personal needs allowances of residents 
in residential care facilities from $70 to $90 and for residents in nursing facilities from $40 to $50. Part K also 
includes Federal Expenditures Fund allocations required for the FMAP match. 
It also reduces the amount transferred from the unappropriated surplus of the General Fund to the Maine Budget 
Stabilization Fund by $4,505,567 in fiscal year 2015-16 to pay for the new General Fund appropriations in fiscal year 
2016-17.
