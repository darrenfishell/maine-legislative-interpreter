1.  Definitions.   As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, t he 5 
following terms have the following meanings.  6

C.  "Commu nity" means a municipality with a population of at least 1,200 people, as 11

E.  "Fund" means the Municipal Gigabit Broadband Network Access Fund 17

F.  "Regional partnership" means 2 or more municipalities that do not, on their own, 19 
meet the requirements of paragraph C and have joined together with one or more 20 
contiguous municipalities in the region to achieve the population requirements of 21 
paragraph C.  22 
2.  Fund established.   The Municipal Gigabit Broadband Network Access Fund is 23 
established as a nonlapsing, revolving fund administered by the department for the 24 
purposes of supporting the activities and projects of the department under this section.  25 
All mo ney in the fund must be continuously applied by the department to carry out this 26 
section.  The fund consists of money appropriated to it by the Legislature from the 27 
General Fund, as well as any gifts, contributions, grants or bequests received from 28 
federal , private or other sources.  29 
3.  Purpose of the fund.   The fund is established to address the need in the State for 30 
access to ultra high -speed broadband infrastructure that will enhance the State's 31 
competitiveness in national and international economies.  T he fund must be used to 32 
provide grants to communities, regional partnerships and municipalities to support 33 
public -private partnerships to support a municipal gigabit fiber -optic broadband network 34 
in their regions with the following goals:  35 
A.  Provide high -speed broadband access to attract, create and grow the State's 36 
economy and market the products and services of businesses in the State in national 37 
and international markets with ultra high -speed symmetric connectivity and address 38 
challenges in geography;  39

 B.  Provide expanded health care services by facilitating access to telemedicine, as 1 
defined in Title 24 -A, section 4316, subsection 1, and state and local services for 2 
senior citizens and persons with disabilities;  3 
C.  Expand educational opportunities for students across the State through virtual and 4 
distance learning;  5 
D.  Facilitate broader access for the public to services provided by municipal and 6

4.  Implementation grant s; maximum awards.   Beginning January 1, 2016, the 15 
department is authorized to award implementation grants as follows.  16 
A.  The department shall award a minimum of 50 implementation grants to achieve 17 
the goals described in subsection 3. The department may a ward additional 18 
implementation grants if funding is available.  19 
B.  An implementation grant to an applicant may not exceed $200,000 for each 20 
eligible project selected for funding.  21 
C.  The department shall award at least 25 implementation grants to economica lly 22 
disadvantaged communities based on criteria established by the department.  23 
D.  An implementation grant may be awarded only to an applicant that has 24 
demonstrated to the satisfaction of the department that it has participated in a 25 
planning grant process as described in subsections 5 and 6.  26 
E.  Municipalities selected for funding must be required to provide a 25% cash 27 
match.  28

completion of the planning process necessary to  achieve the goals of this section, the 30 
department may award up to 50 planning grants of up to $20,000 for community 31

cash match.  The department shall establish appl ication requirements for planning grants 33 
for community and regional applicants that require an applicant to demonstrate to the 34 
satisfaction of the department participation with public and private institutions and local 35 
businesses in the development of the grant process.  Municipal applicants must provide 36

high-speed broadband access to fulfill the economic goals of the municipal ity; 39 
B.  A written commitment to nondiscriminatory open access to the broadband 40 
infrastructure by all parties involved in the grant;  41

Sec. A -2.  Department of Economic and Community Development to 27 
establish timeline and standards for grants under the Municipal Gigabit 28 
Broadband Network Access Fund.   By November 1, 2015, the Department of 29 
Economic and Community Development shall establish standards for implementation of 30 
the Municipal Gigabit Broadband Network Access Fund established in the Maine 31 
Revised Statutes, Title 35 -A, section 9217, including a process to identify how awards for 32 
certification as a broadband -ready community can be made  under the fund, a process for 33 
providing technical assistance to applicants that are interested in applying for grants 34 
under the fund, a process for providing for the installation of the broadband network, a 35 
process for providing workshops in 6 regions of the State to assist applicants with the 36 
certification and application process under the fund and a process for providing applicants 37 
with assistance in the selection of a consultant, when necessary, to achieve the goals of 38 
the fund and for requesting propos als for management of the broadband network once a 39 
grant from the fund has been made.  40

 SUMMARY  1 
This bill establishes the Municipal Gigabit Broadband Network Access Fund within 2 
the Department of Economic and Community Development  to increase access across the 3 
State to ultra high -speed broadband infrastructure that will enhance the State's 4 
competitiveness in national and international economies.  The bill establishes funding for 5 
the department to award planning and implementation g rants to communities and 6 
municipalities in order to support public -private partnerships that will establish a 7 
municipal gigabit fiber -optic broadband network in their regions.  8
