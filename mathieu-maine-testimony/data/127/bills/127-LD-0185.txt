1.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context oth erwise indicates, the 4 
following terms have the following meanings.  5 
A.  "Mobile telephone" means a device used to access wireless telephone service.  6 
B.  "Handheld mobile telephone" means a mobile telephone with which a user 7 
engages in a telephone call using  at least one hand.  8 
C.  "Hands -free mobile telephone" means a mobile telephone that has an internal 9 
feature or function, or that is equipped with an attachment or addition, whether or not 10 
permanently part of the mobile telephone, by which a user may activa te and 11 
deactivate the phone and talk and listen without the use of either hand.  12 
D.  "Using" means manipulating, talking into or otherwise interacting with a mobile 13 
telephone but does not include passively listening to music or other recorded sounds 14 
emanati ng from a mobile telephone.  15 
2.  Prohibition.  A person may not operate a motor vehicle while using a handheld 16 
mobile telephone.  A person may not operate a motor vehicle while using a hands -free 17 
mobile telephone unless the features of the mobile telephone that allow hands -free 18 
operation are being used.  19 
3.  Penalty.  The following penalties apply to violations of this section.  20 
A.  A person who violates this section commits a traffic infraction for which a fine of 21 
not less than $50 may be adjudged.  22 
B.  A pers on who violates this section after having been previously adjudicated as 23 
violating this section commits a traffic infraction for which a fine of not less than 24 
$250 may be adjudged.  25

 SUMMARY  1 
This bill prohibits the use of a handheld mobile telephone w hile operating a motor 2 
vehicle.  The bill clarifies that law enforcement officers, corrections officers, firefighters, 3 
drivers of authorized emergency vehicles, holders of commercial driver's licenses, 4 
physicians, municipal public works personnel, Maine Tu rnpike Authority personnel and 5 
state transportation personnel, including all employees and contractors of the Department 6 
of Transportation, may use handheld mobile telephones while driving within the scope of 7 
their employment.  The bill makes the offense a  traffic infraction.  This bill does not 8 
affect the current prohibition against text messaging while operating a motor vehicle.  9
