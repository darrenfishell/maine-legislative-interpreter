COMMITTEE AMENDMENT ·A " to H.P. 854, L.D. 1254, Bill, "An Act To 
Implement and Fund an Integrated Beach Management Program" 
Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
'Resolve, To Further Study the Implementation and Funding of an Integrated Beach 
Management Program' 
Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and before the summary and 
inserting the following: 
'Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and 
Whereas, this legislation must take effect before the expiration of the 90-day period 
in order to provide the working group created through this legislation sufficient time prior 
to the reporting deadline of January 31, 2017 to compile data on and develop 
recommendations for the implementation and funding of an integrated beach management 
program; and 
Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
therefore, be it 
Sec. 1. Beach working group. Resolved: That the Commissioner of 
Environmental Protection and the Commissioner of Agriculture, Conservation and 
Forestry shall convene a working group to review the report titled "Protecting Maine's 
Beaches for the Future: A Proposal to Create an Integrated Beach Management Program," 
dated February 2006, prepared by the Beach Stakeholder's Group and submitted to the 
Joint Standing Committee on Natural Resources during the Second Regular Session of 
the 122nd Legislature, update the data and findings contained in that report and develop 
recommendations regarding the implementation and funding of an integrated beach 
management program and comprehensive beach nourishment policy that establishes 
priority areas and evaluates public and private funding sources, implementation time

16 SUMMARY 
17 This amendment replaces the bill with a resolve directing the Commissioner of 
18 Environmental Protection and the Commissioner of Agriculture, Conservation and 
19 Forestry to convene a working group to review the report titled "Protecting Maine's 
20 Beaches for the Future: A Proposal to Create an Integrated Beach Management Program," 
21 dated February 2006, prepared by the Beach Stakeholder's Group and submitted to the 
22 Joint Standing Committee on Natural Resources during the Second Regular Session of 
23 the 122nd Legislature, update the data and findings contained in that report and develop 
24 recommendations regarding the implementation and funding of an integrated beach 
25 management program. The Commissioner of Environmental Protection is directed, by 
26 January 31, 2017, to submit a report to the joint standing committee of the Legislature 
27 having jurisdiction over environment and natural resources matters detailing the findings 
28 and recommendations of the working group, and the committee is authorized to report out 
29 a bill relating to the report to the First Regular Session of the 128th Legislature. The 
30 amendment also adds an emergency preamble and emergency clause.
