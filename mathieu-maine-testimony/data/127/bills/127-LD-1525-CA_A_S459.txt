4 Sec. 3. 5 MRSA §1815-A is enacted to read: 
5 §1815-A. Procurement Review Board 
6 1. Board established. The Procurement Review Board, referred to in this section as 
7 "the board," is established pursuant to section 12004-G, subsection 29-E to cany out the 
8 duties of the board as provided in this section. 
9 2. Membership; vacancy; compensation. The board consists of 7 members as 
10 follows: 
11 A. Five members who have business or professional experience in the area of 
12 procurement, appointed by the Governor and subject to review by the joint standing 
13 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over state and local government 
14 matters and to confirmation by the Senate; 
15 B. The Attorney General or the Attorney General's designee, who serves as a 
16 nonvoting member; and 
17 C. The Treasurer of State or the treasurer's designee, who serves as a nonvoting 
18 member. 
19 A member of the Legislature may not be appointed to or serve on the board. 
20 In the case of a vacancy, the Governor may nominate an individual to fill the vacant 
21 position. If the Governor does not nominate an individual to fill a vacant position on the 
22 board within 30 days of the vacancy, the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the 
23 House of Representatives shall jointly appoint an individual to fill the vacancy. A 
24 member appointed by the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House of 
25 Representatives is not subject to confirmation by the Legislature. 
26 Members described in paragraph A are entitled to reimbursement for direct expenses of 
27 attendance at meetings of the board. 
28 3. Terms. Members of the board appointed by the Governor serve 4-year terms. 
29 Board members may serve 2 consecutive terms. 
30 4. Chair. The board shall elect annually a chair from among its members to serve 
31 for one year. 
32 5. Powers and duties. The board has the following powers and duties: 
33 A. To review, comment on and approve all requests for proposals, bids, contract 
34 renewals and amendments to, extensions of and other changes to existing contracts 
35 for the procurement of supplies, professional or artistic services, construction and real 
36 property and capital improvement leases procured by the State valued at $1,000,000 
3 7 or more; and 
38 B. To review, upon a majority vote of its voting members, requests for proposals, 
39 bids, contract renewals and amendments to, extensions of and other changes to an 
40 existing contract valued at less than $1,000,000.

1 6. Staff support. Upon a majority vote of its voting members, and to the extent 
2 funds are available, the board may employ an executive director and staff support. 
3 7. Meetings. The board shall meet at least twice each month. The board shall meet 
4 in person. Each meeting of the board must be open to the public. The board shall keep 
5 records and minutes of its activities under this section and meetings. These records and 
6 minutes must be made easily accessible to the public and be provided expeditiously upon 
7 request. 
8 8. Interests of members. That a member of the board is employed by or holds an 
9 interest in an entity doing business with or attempting to do business with the State does 
10 not preclude the entity from doing business with or attempting to do business with the 
11 State. 
12 9. Board approval required. State departments and agencies shall submit all 
13 requests for proposals, bids, contract renewals and amendments to, extensions of and 
14 other changes to existing contracts to the board. A proposal, bid, contract renewal or 
15 amendment to, extension of or other change to an existing contract valued at $1,000,000 
16 or more must be approved by the board before proceeding. If a proposal, bid, contract 
17 renewal or amendment to, extension of or other change to an existing contract is valued at 
18 less than $1,000,000 and if the board takes no action to initiate a review within 30 days of 
19 receiving the proposal, bid, contract renewal or amendment to, extension of or other 
20 change to an existing contract, the affected department or agency may proceed with the 
21 proposal, bid, contract renewal or amendment to, extension of or other change to an 
22 existing contract without the review, comment or approval of the board. The State 
23 Purchasing Agent, any other position that is authorized to make procurements and each 
24 state department or agency shall cooperate with the board, provide information to the 
25 board and respond to the board in the board's reviews, studies and hearings. 
26 10. Legal advice from Attorney General required for expenditures of $3,000,000 
27 or more. Prior to seeking approval from the board, state departments and agencies shall 
28 request and obtain legal advice from the Attorney General if an expenditure of 
29 $3,000,000 or more is reasonably expected as a result of a request for proposals or bids or 
30 a contract renewal or amendment to, extension of or other change to an existing contract 
31 under this section. 
32 11. Legal advice from Attorney General required in specific instances. The 
33 board shall request and obtain legal advice from the Attorney General on a proposed 
34 contract if the board determines: 
35 A. The contract may expose the State to substantial risk in the event of 
36 nonperformance; or 
37 B. The contract could reasonably be expected to incur costs to the State in excess of 
38 $3,000,000 over the full term of the proposed contract, exclusive of extensions or 
39 amendments. 
40 12. Conditions for board approval of a contract. The board shall approve the 
41 award of a contract pursuant to subsection 9 when the board determines: 
42 A. The service to be performed under the contract cannot be economically provided 
43 by State Government;

22 SUMMARY 
23 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, makes the following 
24 changes to the bill. 
25 1. It changes the title of the bill. 
26 2. It amends the bill to clarify that the law's application must be consistent with all 
27 applicable trade obligations and not only with the State's international trade obligations. 
28 3. It amends the bill to include the University of Maine System, the Maine 
29 Community College System and the Maine Maritime Academy in the definition of 
30 "public agency." 
31 4. It establishes the 7-member Procurement Review Board, which must review 
32 proposals, bids, contract renewals and amendments to, extensions of and changes to 
33 existing contracts valued over $1,000,000 and, upon a majority vote of the board, may 
34 review proposals, bids, contract renewals and amendments to, extensions of and changes 
35 to existing contracts valued under $1,000,000. The Attorney General and Treasurer of 
36 State serve as nonvoting members of the board. 
37 5. It requires the Procurement Review Board to meet at least twice per month in 
38 person and make records and minutes of meetings accessible to the public.
