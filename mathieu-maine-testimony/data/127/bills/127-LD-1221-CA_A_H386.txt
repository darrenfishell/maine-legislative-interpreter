12 SUMMARY 
13 This amendment is the majority report of the committee. This amendment removes 
14 section 2 of the bill, which changes the process for the appointment of the Director of the 
15 Efficiency Maine Trust. This amendment also removes section 4 of the bill, which 
16 clarifies that the procurement cap is to apply to the total retail electricity supply and 
17 transmission and distribution sales in the State. Lastly, it specifies that Efficiency Maine 
18 Trust fimds provided to fund the Maine Energy Office may only be used for office 
19 activities that support or are reasonably related to programs or activities of the Efficiency 
20 Maine Trust. 
21 This amendment adds an appropriations and allocations section.

The bill renames the Governor's Energy Office as the Maine Energy Office (MEO). It allows for the MEO to receive 
up to $300,000 in total from the Efficiency Maine Trust (EMT) for MEO activities that suppOli or reasonably relate to 
programs or activities of the EMT. This fiscal note assumes the MEO will be able to justify transfer ofthe full 
$300,000 over time. No estimate is made of how long it will take to receive the full amount. This bill also establishes 
a Deputy Commissioner position to be appointed by the Governor. The bill includes General Fund appropriations to 
the MEO of $90,483 in fiscal year 2015-16 and $92,340 in fiscal year 2016-17 for the establishment of this Deputy 
Commissioner position and related costs.
