§3501.  Definitions  5 
As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following ter ms 6 
have the following meanings.  7 
1.  Employer.  "Employer" means a person, firm, association, corporation, public 8 
employer, public school employer, public college, public university, public institution or 9 
public education agency.  10 
2.  Labor organization.  "Labor organization" means an organization, agency or 11 
employee representation committee or union that exists for the purpose, in whole or in 12 
part, of negotiating or bargaining with employers on behalf of employees concerning 13 
wages, rates of pay, hours of wor k, other conditions of employment or other forms of 14 
compensation.  15 
§3502.  Right to refrain  16 
Notwithstanding any law to the contrary, a person may not be required, as a condition 17 
of employment or continuation of employment, to:  18 
1.  Member.  Become or remain a member of a labor organization;  19 
2.  Dues.  Pay any dues, fees, assessments or other similar charges, however 20 
denominated, of any kind or amount to a labor organization; or  21 
3.  Payment to 3rd party.  Pay to a charity or other 3rd party, in lieu of payment s 22 
specified in subsection 2, any amount equivalent to or a pro rata portion of dues, fees, 23 
assessments or other charges required of members of a labor organization.  24 
§3503.  Agreements in violation  25 
An agreement, understanding or practice, written or oral, i mplied or expressed, 26 
between a labor organization and an employer that violates a provision of this chapter is 27 
unlawful, void and without any legal effect.  28 
§3504.  Penalty  29 
A person who directly or indirectly violates any provision of this chapter commits a  30 
Class D crime.  31

 §3505.  Injunctive relief  1 
A person injured as a result of another person's violation or threatened violation of 2 
the provisions of this chapter is entitled to injunctive relief against the person violating or 3 
threatening to violate this chap ter. 4 
§3506.  Damages  5 
A person injured as a result of a violation or threatened violation of the provisions of 6 
this chapter may recover all damages, including court costs and reasonable attorney's 7 
fees, resulting from the violation or threatened violation. A recovery of damages under 8 
this section is independent of and in addition to the penalty provided in section 3504.  9 
§3507.  Duty to investigate and enforce  10 
The Attorney General shall investigate complaints of a violation or threatened 11 
violation of this cha pter and shall prosecute any person violating any of its provisions. 12 
The Attorney General shall use all means available to ensure effective enforcement of 13 
this chapter.  14 
§3508.  Jurisdiction  15 
The Superior Court has jurisdiction to hear and decide prosecution s of all offenses 16 
under this chapter.  17 
§3509.  Exceptions  18 
This chapter does not apply to the following:  19 
1.  Federal Railway Labor Act.  Employers and employees covered by the federal 20 
Railway Labor Act, 45 United States Code, Chapter 8;  21 
2.  Federal employers  and employees.  Federal employers and employees;  22 
3.  Federal enclaves.  Employers and employees on federal enclaves;  23 
4.  Federal preemption or conflict.  Federal law when the provisions of this chapter 24 
conflict with or are otherwise preempted by that fede ral law; or  25 
5.  Existing contract.  An employment contract entered into before the effective date 26 
of this chapter during the term of that contract.  27 
§3510.  Application  28 
This chapter applies to a contract entered into, renewed or extended on or after the 29 
effective date of this chapter.  30 
SUMMARY  31 
This bill prohibits a person from being required to join a labor organization or pay 32 
any labor organization dues or fees as a condition of employment or continuation of 33
