5 SUMMARY 
6 This amendment removes the interfund advance of funds for one day at the end of 
7 fiscal year 2015-16. 
8 This amendment strikes Part J of the committee amendment and instead indexes the 
9 Maine exclusion amount for decedents dying on or after January 1, 2016 to the federal 
10 exclusion amount. It also amends the section of the estate tax law that applies to the tax 
11 on resident estates to apply the tax rates to each $3,000,000 increment above the yearly 
12 exclusion amount. 
13 This amendment increases the homestead property tax exemption by $5,000 to 
14 $15,000 for property tax years beginning on April 1, 2016 and by $10,000 to $20,000 for 
15 property tax years beginning on or after April 1, 2017. This amendment also increases 
16 state reimbursement to 50% for the additional exemption amount for property tax years 
17 beginning April 1, 2016 and to 75% for property tax years beginning on or after April 1, 
18 2017. 
19 This amendment provides that for fiscal years 2015-16, 2016-17, 2017-18 and 2018-
20 19 the amount transferred for state-municipal revenue sharing is 2% of revenue from the 
21 income tax, the sales tax and a portion of the service provider tax. 
22 This amendment makes the following changes to the individual income tax. 
23 1. This amendment repeals the provision of law requiring the Department of 
24 Administrative and Financial Services, Maine Revenue Services to provide for the 
25 assignment of income tax refunds on income tax returns to the Maine College Savings 
26 Program administered by the Finance Authority of Maine. 
27 2. This amendment reduces the individual income tax rates and establishes new tax 
28 bracket amounts for tax years beginning after 2015. The proposed rate structure for tax 
29 years beginning after December 31, 2015 consists of 5.8%,6.75% and 7.15% taxable 
30 income brackets. 
31 3. This amendment repeals the income subtraction modifications for long-term care 
32 premiums paid and for contributions of up to $250 per beneficiary to 529 college tuition 
33 plans for tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2016. 
34 4. This amendment eliminates the jobs and investment tax credit, the credit for 
35 employer-assisted day care, the credit for employer-provided long-term care benefits, the 
36 high-technology investment tax credit, the credit for dependent health benefits, the 
37 quality child care investment credit and the credit for biofuel commercial production but 
38 retains the carry-forward of unused credit amounts for tax years beginning after 
39 December 31,2015.
