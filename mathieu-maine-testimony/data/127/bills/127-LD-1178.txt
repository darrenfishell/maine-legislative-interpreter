§2431.  Purpose  8 
The purpose of this chapter is to create a unified organizational structure, under the 9 
auspices of a state agency, to provide for the advancement of the maple industry in this 10 
State in the public interest and for the public good.  U nder this organizational structure, 11 
all elements of the maple industry in this State must be represented and work together 12 
under the leadership of a unified, public board to solve the problems facing the industry.  13 
§2432.  Maine Maple Promotion Board  14 
1.  Establishment.  The Maine Maple Promotion Board, as established by Title 5, 15 
section 12004 -H, subsection 15 and referred to in this chapter as "the board," is a body 16 
corporate and politic and an incorporated public instrumentality of the State and the 17 
exercis e of powers conferred by this chapter is determined to be the performance of 18 
essential government functions.  For the purposes of the budget, accounts and control, 19 
purchasing or other provisions of Title 5, Part 4, the board may not be construed to be a 20 
state agency.  The board consists of 7 members who must be elected in accordance with 21 
the procedures set forth in this chapter and such additional procedures as the board may 22 
prescribe by rulemaking.  23 
2.  Composition of the board; voting.  The board consists of the following 7 24 
members:  25 
A.  One current officer from a statewide association of maple producers in this State;  26 
B.  Two producers of maple sugar products with more than 25,000 taps;  27 
C.  One producer of maple sugar products with between 5,001 and 25,000 taps; 28 
D.  One producer of maple sugar products with between 1,501 and 5,000 taps; and  29 
E.  Two producers of maple sugar products with between 50 and 1,500 taps.  30 
Subject to such staggered terms as the board may provide by rule, board members serve 31 
3-year ter ms, except that the board member from a statewide association of maple 32 
producers in this State serves only a one -year term and that a board member may 33 
continue to serve until a successor is duly elected.  Board members may not serve more 34 
than 3 consecutive  terms.  Every board member has one vote, except that the 2 board 35 Maine Maple Promotion Board   Expenses Only  7 MRSA §2432

3.  Board officers and committees.  The board shall annually elect officers, 3

4.  Board meetings.  A regular annual meeting of the board must be held on a date 8 
determined by the board.  Other meetings, of which there must be at least 6 per year, may 9 
be held upon the call of the chair or of a majority of the board or by v ote of the board.  A 10

5.  Staff.  The board shall appoint an executive director who is the board's chief 15 
administrative officer and who serves at the pleasure of the board .  The executive director 16 
shall employ such additional staff as the board directs, and the staff serves at the pleasure 17 
of the executive director.  Staff of the board is not subject to the Civil Service Law.  The 18 
salary paid to the executive director and o ther staff of the board must be fixed by the 19 
board.  The board may delegate to its staff the power to execute the board's policies and 20 
programs, subject to regular oversight by the board.  21 
6.  Compensation.  Board members may be compensated and reimbursed f or 22 
expenses such as travel, meals, lodging and other necessary costs in accordance with such 23

§2433.  Powers and duties of the Maine Maple Promotion Board  25 
To carry out the purposes of this chapter, the board has the fo llowing powers and 26 
duties.  27 
1.  Bylaws.  The board may adopt bylaws to govern its functions.  28 
2.  Programs.  The board may make studies; undertake research, development and 29 
investment in infrastructure, marketing and promotional programs; publish and 30 
dissemi nate information, including, but not limited to, information related to consumer 31 
education and quality assurance; and implement other programs in furtherance of its 32 
legislative purposes, provided that the programs undertaken by the board are designed to 33 
benefit the maple industry in this State or segments of the industry, but may not be 34 
designed to benefit exclusively any one person or entity involved in the industry.  35 
3.  Funding; accounts.  In addition to the money received by the board pursuant to 36 
section  2434, the board may receive and expend funds from any source, public or private, 37 
that it considers necessary to carry out its legislative purposes.  The board shall establish 38 
an account, known as the maple promotion account, to receive and expend funds fo r 39 
carrying out the board's responsibilities.  40

 4.  Books and records; confidentiality.  The board shall keep books, records and 1

§2434.  Tap fees  12 
1.  Tap fees.  Producers of maple sugar products licensed in th is State pursuant to 13 
Title 7, chapter 101 shall pay an annual tap fee.  Fees paid pursuant to this section must 14 
be collected directly by the board and deposited into the maple promotion account 15 
established in section 2433, subsection 3.  16 
The following fee s chedule is established:  17 
A.  For producers with 1 to 250 taps, the fee is $20;  18 
B.  For producers with 251 to 1,000 taps, the fee is $40;  19 
C.  For producers with 1,001 to 5,000 taps, the fee is $80;  20 
D.  For producers with 5,001 to 20,000 taps, the fee is $200 ; and  21 
E.  For producers with more than 20,000 taps, the fee is $0.01 per tap.  22 
§2435.  Money received  23 
Money received by the board, including all fees collected under section 2434, must 24 
be used for the activities of the board authorized under this chapter.  25 
SUMMARY  26 
This bill establishes the Maine Maple Promotion Board and creates its basic structure, 27 
core functions and duties.  28
