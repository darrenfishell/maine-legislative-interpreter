This bill is a concept draft pursuant to Joint Rule 208.  4 
This bill proposes to require the State to advance implementation of proficiency - 5 
based diplomas and a standards -based system of education by using strategic planning, 6 
monitoring and economies of scale to accelerate implementation of the diplomas and 7 
system at the local level.  The bill proposes to meet these goals by:  8 
1.  Requiring the Department of Education to develo p a 5 -year plan and budget to 9 
outline strategic supports for the implementation of proficiency -based diplomas under the 10 
Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20 -A, section 4722 -A and submit the plan and budget to the 11 
Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultu ral Affairs by January 31, 2016;  12 
2.  Requiring the Department of Education annually to collect and report progress 13 
towards the implementation of proficiency -based diplomas in public schools, public 14 
charter schools and private schools under the Maine Revise d Statutes, Title 20 -A, section 15 
2951, subsection 5, including the number of students graduating with proficiency -based 16 
diplomas and the number of students awarded proficiency in each of the content areas of 17 
the system of learning results established under Title 20 -A, section 6209 and the guiding 18 
principles set forth in department rules governing implementation of the system of 19 
learning results established pursuant to Title 20 -A, section 6209;  20 
3.  Maintaining school administrative unit per -pupil subsidies at  the current rate;  21 
4.  Providing competitive grants to regional district partnerships and nonprofit 22 
organizations to address statewide priorities and involve the State's business and higher 23 
education communities in delivering consistent messages about stan dards and 24 
proficiency, create a statewide research and development process to solve complex 25 
implementation problems and provide on -site consultation in curriculum, instruction, 26 
assessment and organizational change, increase local knowledge in key implement ation 27 
areas and build local support systems and services; and  28 
5.  Requiring the Department of Education to contract with an external organization 29 
through a request for proposals process to develop, administer and track the competitive 30 
grant process and rep ort annually to the joint standing committee of the Legislature 31 
having jurisdiction over education and cultural affairs, the Commissioner of Education 32 
and the public on the progress of the implementation of a standards -based system of 33 
education and emergin g needs as the needs arise.  34
