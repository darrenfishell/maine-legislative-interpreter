SUMMARY  26 
This bill permits the Depart ment of Public Safety, Gambling Control Board, 27 
beginning January 1, 2016, to issue a license to a charitable nonprofit organization that is 28 
a veterans' organization that is tax -exempt under the United States Internal Revenue Code 29 
of 1986 to operate up to 3  slot machines on premises that have been owned, rented or 30 
leased by the organization for at least 2 consecutive years, that serve as its primary 31 
administrative operations headquarters and that are located in a municipality that has, by 32 
referendum of the v oters, approved the operation of slot machines in that municipality. 33 
The charitable nonprofit veterans' organization must be able to demonstrate that it has a 34 
cash reserve of $1,000 for each machine the organization intends to operate.  35 
A charitable nonprof it veterans' organization that wishes to apply prior to January 1, 36 
2016 may file a declaration of intent to apply with the Gambling Control Board. An 37 
application must include a refundable $2,500 deposit. The initial application fee for a slot 38 
machine opera tor license is $500, and the annual renewal fee is $175.  39 
 
 Page 8 - 127LR0298(01) -1 
 A slot machine operated by a charitable nonprofit veterans' organization is subject to 1 
the same central site monitoring that applies to casinos and slot machine facilities at 2 
harness racing tracks. T he total number of slot machines allowed to be operated by 3 
charitable nonprofit veterans' organizations statewide between January 1, 2016 and 4 
December 31, 2016 is 80; beginning January 1, 2017 the number increases to 150.  5 
The bill provides that 10% of the net slot machine income from a charitable nonprofit 6 
veterans' organization is required to be deposited directly with the Gambling Control 7 
Board for administrative expenses; 8% goes directly to the General Fund; 10% goes to 8 
the host municipality; and 2% is dedicated to gambling addiction prevention and 9 
treatment. A charitable nonprofit veterans’ organization that is licensed to operate slot 10 
machines is required to establish a separate account, from which the board may withdraw 11 
funds to distribute the net rev enue percentages. The remaining revenue generated from 12 
the slot machines must be used to support the charitable purposes of the veterans' 13 
organization.  14
