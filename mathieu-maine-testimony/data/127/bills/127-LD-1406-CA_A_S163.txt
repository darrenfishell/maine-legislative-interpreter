lO SUMMARY 
11 This amendment makes the following changes to the recommendations proposed by 
12 the Commission To Study College Affordability and College Completion for 
13 consideration by the Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs. 
14 1. It strikes the proposal to increase the minimum Maine State Grant Program awards 
15 from $1,000 to $1,500. 
16 2. It changes the date for the initial report required from the Finance Authority of 
17 Maine for the annual Maine State Grant Program awards made to eligible students, 
18 including the possible tiered grants awarded, from January 15, 2016 to September 1, 2016 
19 and also amends the annual report date to September 1 st. 
20 3. It changes the date for the college attainment and degree completion goal reports 
21 required from the University of Maine System, the Maine Community College System 
22 and the Maine Maritime Academy from July 1,2015 to January 1, 2016. 
23 4. It changes the date for the reports on strategic planning and funding required to 
24 implement college completion strategies required from the University of Maine System, 
25 the Maine Community College System and the Maine Maritime Academy from July 1, 
26 2015 to January 1,2016. 
27 5. It strikes the proposal to require the Finance Authority of Maine to amend the 
28 Maine State Grant Program rules related to awarding tiered grants to eligible students. 
29 6. It strikes the appropriations to the Finance Authority of Maine for tiered grant 
30 awards and to the Maine Community College System for adding College Navigator 
31 positions and expanding student work study opportunities and replaces them with an 
32 allocation to fund the one-time costs of the legislative per diem and expenses of the Task 
33 Force To Study College Attainment and Degree Completion Goals.

Fiscal Detail and Notes $3,750 $0 $0 $0 
The bill includes an Other Special Revenue Funds allocation to the Legislature of $3,750 in fiscal year 2015-16 for the 
one-time costs of the per diem for 7 Legislators and expenses of the Task Force to Study College Attainment and 
Degree Completion Goals. The bill requires the task force seek outside funding to fully fund all costs of the task force 
and prohibits the task force from meeting or incurring expenses if sufficient funding has not been received within 30 
days of the effective date of the bill. 
Additional costs to the Finance Authority of Maine, the University of Maine System, the Maine Community College 
System, Maine Maritime Academy, the Department of Education, the Department of Economic and Community 
Development and the Department of Labor to implement the requirements of this legislation can be absorbed within 
existing budgeted resources.
