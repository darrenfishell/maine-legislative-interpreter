SUMMARY  23 
This bill requires a retailer to assess a 5¢ fee for a single -use plastic disposable bag 24 
distributed to a customer at the point of retail sale.  The retail er retains 1¢ from each 5¢ 25 
fee for administrative costs and may elect to retain an additional 1¢ from each 5¢ fee to 26 
provide a carryout program that credits a customer no less than 5¢ for each bag provided 27 
by the customer at the point of retail sale for pa ckaging purchases.  The revenues from 28 
the fee, less the portion retained by the retailer for administrative costs and, if applicable, 29 
the carryout program, are credited to the Plastics Recycling Fund administered by the 30 
Department of Environmental Protecti on for the purpose of promoting recycling efforts in 31 
the State related to plastics.  A retailer may provide recyclable paper products at the point 32 
of sale and must provide reusable bags for purchase by a customer.  This bill authorizes 33 
the Department of En vironmental Protection to enforce these laws and impose penalties 34 
for violations.  This bill also directs that, by January 1, 2016, the Department of 35 
Environmental Protection must conduct a public information campaign relating to plastics 36 
and their impact on the environment and must implement an outreach campaign aimed at 37 
providing reusable bags to residents of the State, especially to elderly and low -income 38 
households.  39
