Sec. A -6.  Use of bonds.  Bond proceeds may be used for a program to award 2 
grants for investment in economic revitalization, environmental enhancement and 3 
community betterment projects along waterfronts of  the State in a fashion that preserves 4 
and enhances economic activity, environmental protection and the quality of life for 5 
residents of the State, except that use of the proceeds is subject to the following 6 
conditions and requirements.  7 
1.  Any municipalit y with either ocean or river waterfront and any nonprofit 8 
corporation organized in whole or in part for the purposes of economic, environmental 9 
and community development of waterfront in the State may apply to the Department of 10 
Economic and Community Devel opment for funding from this grant program.  11 
2.  For a project to be eligible to receive grant funding, the project must have as its 12 
purpose:  13 
A.  The development or restoration of public parks, walkways and other public spaces 14 
along waterfront corridors sit uated either in downtown or developed waterfront 15 
locations or between and connecting 2 or more such locations;  16 
B.  Rehabilitation of degraded or dilapidated shorelines in downtown waterfront 17 
locations in a manner designed to encourage subsequent private de velopment on or 18 
near those shorelines; or  19 
C.  The restoration of native fisheries and aquatic ecosystems.  20 
3.  A proposed project must demonstrate support from a broad range of public and 21 
private entities and the local community.  22 
4.  Eligible applications m ust include a commitment of one dollar in matching funds 23 
from other public or private sources for every dollar in bond funds.  24

SUMMARY  28 
The funds prov ided by this bond issue, in the amount of $10,000,000, will be used to 29 
fund a grant program to invest in projects that contribute to economic activity, 30 
environmental protection and community development along the State's waterfronts.  It 31 
also enacts a gran t program for waterfront development, which is modeled after the 32 
Riverfront Community Development Program.  33
