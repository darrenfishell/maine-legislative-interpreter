§11401.  Esta blishment  16 
The Sex Offender Management and Risk Assessment Advisory Commission, referred 17 
to in this chapter as "the commission," established by Title 5, section 12004 -I, subsection 18 
4-C, is created for the purpose of conducting a continuing study of methods that may be 19 
used to predict the risk of recidivism by a sex offender and to develop a method that may 20 
be used for such purposes. For purposes of this chapter, "sex offender" has the same 21 
meaning as "offender" in section 11273, subsection 10.  For purposes of this chapter, 22 
"registrant" has the same meaning as in section 11273, subsection 11.  23 
§11402.  Membership; terms; vacancies  24 
1.  Composition; qualifications.  The commission is composed of 7 members, 25 
appointed by the Attorney General. The members may be qu alified by reason of their 26 
expertise in sex offender matters, including but not limited to risk assessment methods, 27 
corrections, sex offender law and the prosecution or defense of sex offender crimes.  28 
2.  Terms.  Members of the commission serve for a term of 2 years and may be 29 
reappointed. Members continue to serve until their replacements are designated.  30 
3.  Vacancy.  In the event of the death or resignation of a member, the Attorney 31 
General shall appoint a member to complete the unexpired term.  32

 SUMMARY  1 
This bill moves the law establishing the Sex Offender Risk Assessment Advisory 2 
Commission from the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 17 -A to Title 34 -A and changes the 3 
name of the commission to the Sex Offender Management and Risk Assessment 4 
Advisory Commission. The bill also makes changes to the provisions of law regarding the 5 
commission's powers and duties by requiring the commission to study and offer 6 
amendments to the s ex offender registration and notification laws and by allowing the 7 
commission to accept funding.  8
