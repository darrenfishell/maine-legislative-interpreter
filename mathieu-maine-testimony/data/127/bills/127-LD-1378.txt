§11.  General obligation bonds  3 
The Governor shall authorize the issuance of a general obligation bond that has been 4 
ratified by the legal voters of the St ate unless:  5 
1.  Debt service greater.  The Treasurer of State determines that the debt service on 6 
the bond would be greater than the amount budgeted;  7 
2.  Adverse impact on credit rating.  The Treasurer of State determines that the 8 
issuance of the bonds wil l adversely affect the credit rating of the State;  9 
3.  Advantageous interest rate.  The Treasurer of State determines that a delay in 10 
the issuance of the bonds will likely result in a more financially advantageous interest 11 
rate; 12 
4.  Project not proceeding.   The Governor receives notification that a project for 13 
which the bond was authorized is not going forward and the funding to be provided by 14 
the bond is not required; or  15 
5.  Alternative funding available.  The Governor receives notification that 16 
alternativ e funding sources are available to implement, within a comparable time frame, a 17 
project and the purposes for which the bond was authorized and ratified.  18

SUMMARY  23 
This bill makes the following changes to the laws governing the issuance of bonds:  24 
1.  It requires the Governor to issue a general obligation bond that has been ratified 25 
by the legal voters of the State unless one of 5 specific conditions exist, and it requires the 26 
Governor to provide certain information upon delaying or forgoing issuance of a bond;  27 
2.  It eliminates the requirement that registered bonds bear the facsimile signature of 28 
the Governor; and  29 
3.  It requires the Treasurer of State to issue bonds described in Public Law 2009, 30 
chapter 414, Part E, section 6, as amended by Public Law 2009, chapter 645, Part J, 31 
section 3, and Public Law 2011, chapter 696, section 6 and ratified by the voters of the 32 
State in statewide elections held in November 2010 and November  2012, respectively.  33
