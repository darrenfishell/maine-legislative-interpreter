SUMMARY  20 
This bill establishes a scholarship granting organization credit to provide a Maine 21 
income tax credit to individuals and corporations who donate to scholarship granting 22 
organizations beginning in the 2016 tax year. Scholarship granting organizations are 23 
defined as organizations that provide educational scholarships, beginning in the 2016 - 24 
2017 school year, to public or private school students in kindergarten to grade 12 who  are 25 
from households in which the household incomes are equal to or below 250% of the 26 
income guidelines used to qualify for federal Department of Health and Human Services 27 
programs.  28 
The amount of the credit is the amount of voluntary cash contributions mad e to a 29 
scholarship granting organization, up to a maximum of $1,000 annually of the tax owed. 30 
Scholarship granting organizations that receive such contributions are subject to reporting 31 
requirements. The credit is not available for an educational scholarsh ip provided to a 32 
student who attends a school that employs a paid staff or board member who is a member 33 
or an immediate family member of a member of the scholarship granting organization.  34
