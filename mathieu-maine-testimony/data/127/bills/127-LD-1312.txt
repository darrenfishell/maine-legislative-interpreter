1.  Purpose.  In the interest of the protection of the lives, health and welfare of 4 
citizens of this State and  the safe and successful operation of public health facilities, this 5 
section expands the licensing authority of the department's division of licensing and 6 
regulatory services to regulate and license surgical abortion facilities.  7 
2.  Definitions.  As used i n this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 8 
following terms have the following meanings.  9 
A.  "Abortion" means the intentional interruption of a pregnancy by the application of 10 
external agents, whether chemical or physical, or the ingestion of chemical agents 11 
with an intention other than to produce a live birth or remove a dead fetus, regardless 12 
of the length of gestation.  13 
B.  "Division" means the department's division of licensing and regulatory services.  14 
C.  "Person" means an individual per son, partnership, association or corporation, 15 
including state, county or local governmental units.  16 
D.  "Physician" means a person licensed under Title 32, chapter 36 or 48 to practice 17 
medicine in this State as an osteopathic or allopathic physician.  18 
E.  "Regular service" means a procedure that is performed on site on a routine basis.  19 
F.  "Surgical abortion facility" means an outpatient facility that is not otherwise 20 
licensed by the division that provides surgical termination of pregnancy as a regular 21 
servic e or that uses physical external agents to terminate pregnancies in accordance 22 
with chapter 263 -B.  Premises operated primarily as a physician practice are not 23 
surgical abortion facilities.  24 
3.  License required.  The following provisions govern the licensi ng of surgical 25 
abortion facilities.  26 
A.  A person may not establish or operate a surgical abortion facility without 27 
obtaining a license issued by the division.  28 
B.  A license issued under this section is valid for 24 months from the date of issue, 29 
unless sus pended or revoked or otherwise conditioned pursuant to section 1817.  30 
C.  A license issued under this section is not assignable or transferable.  31 
D.  A person seeking a license to operate a surgical abortion facility shall file an 32 
application and pay initial  licensing and renewal fees in accordance with rules 33 
adopted pursuant to this subsection.  34 
E.  The department shall adopt rules, which are major substantive rules as described 35 
in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2 -A, identifying the conditions under which a  36 
surgical abortion facility license may be granted, conditioned or denied.  These rules 37 
must include licensing fees.  38

SUMMARY  12 
This bill requires outpatient surgical abortion facilities to be licensed by the 13 
Department of Health and Hu man Services.  The department must adopt rules governing 14 
licensing of these facilities and may adopt rules establishing reasonable operational and 15
