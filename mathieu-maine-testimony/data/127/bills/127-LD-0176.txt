 §903 -E.  Residency restrictions pertaining to circulators of petitions  1 
A person who is not a resident of the State may not act as a circulator collecting 2 
signatures on a petition for the direct initiative of legislation or a people's veto  3 
referendum and may not handle a petition in any manner.  A person who is not a resident 4 
of the State may provide another person information about a petition.  5 
A person who violates this section commits a Class E crime.  6 
SUMMARY  7 
This bill prohibits persons w ho are not residents of the State from collecting 8 
signatures on a petition for the direct initiative of legislation or a people's veto 9 
referendum and from handling such a petition in any manner.  The bill permits persons 10 
who are not residents to provide ot hers with information about a petition.  The bill 11 
requires a person employed by a petition organization to register with the Commission on 12 
Governmental Ethics and Election Practices and to disclose to the commission 13 
information regarding the person's place  of residence, employment history, compensation, 14 
number of signatures gathered in a month and petitions circulated and to wear an 15 
identification badge when collecting signatures.  The bill requires a petition organization 16 
to post a $2,000 bond on a circula tor receiving over $2,500 in compensation.  The bill 17 
makes a violation of any of these provisions a Class E crime.  18
