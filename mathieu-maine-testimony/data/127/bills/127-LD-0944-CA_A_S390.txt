10 Recovery of Emergency Response Costs Related to an OUI Offense" 
11 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enaQting clause and before the 
12 summary and inserting the following: 
13 'Sec. 1. 29-A MR.SA §2411, sub-§9 is enacted to read: 
14 9. Recovery of emergency response costs. In addition to any other charges, fines, 
15 penalties, liabilities and :financial obligations associated with a conviction of OUI under 
16 this section or otherwise arising from operating under the influence of intoxicants, a 
17 motor vehicle operator convicted of OUI is strictly liable for and subject to a court-
18 ordered payment plan for the reimbursement of up to $1,000 for the cost of emergency 
19 response services including but not limited to all costs reasonably calculated by the 
20 emergency response agency for the mechanical extraction of persons from vehicles; the 
21 removal, containment and remediation of hazardous materials; the application of foam or 
22 other fire retardants; traffic redirection labor; and the provision of medical services at or 
23 near the scene of the accident.' 
24 SUMMARY 
25 This amendment is the minority report of the committee and replaces the bill. The 
26 amendment makes a person who is convicted of operating a motor vehicle while under 
27 the influence of alcohol or drugs strictly liable for the costs of specified emergency 
28 response services, up to $1,000, under a court-ordered payment plan. The bill makes 
29 such a person, when causing an incident requiring an emergency response, liable for the 
30 costs of that response up to $2,500.
