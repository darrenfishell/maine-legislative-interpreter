 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 1 
become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and  2 
Whereas, a legislative study by a study commission needs sufficient time for the 3 
commission to prop erly do its work; and  4

to properly do its work before reporting to the Legislature; and  6 
Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 7

therefore, be it  10

Sec. 6.  Duties. Resolved:   That the commission shall:  18 
1.  Review t he services provided by the State to veterans for gaps, duplications and 19 
inefficiencies;  20 
2.  Study improving the State’s engagement and communication with veterans of the 21 
conflicts in Afghanistan and Iraq;  22 
3.  Study how the State can provide health care an d mental health services to the 23 
State's veterans when there is a clear gap in federal services;  24 
4.  Study how to better align community, state and federal services and resources for 25 
homeless veterans;  26 
5.  Study how the State can develop and implement a cam paign and marketing 27 
strategy to communicate with veterans and military retirees and encourage them to attend 28 
state colleges and to live and work in the State;  29 
6.  Make recommendations to the Commissioner of Defense, Veterans and 30 
Emergency Management concer ning practices and rules regarding services and programs 31 
for veterans and the development of a 5 -year work plan; and  32 
7.  Make recommendations to the Joint Standing Committee on Veterans and Legal 33 
Affairs concerning changes to services and programs for vete rans; and be it further  34

SUMMARY  8 
This resolve establishes the Commission To Strengthen and Align the Services 9 
Provided to Maine's Veterans, which must report by December 2, 2015 with its findings 10 
and recommendations and suggested legislat ion to the Joint Standing Committee on 11 
Veterans and Legal Affairs.  12
