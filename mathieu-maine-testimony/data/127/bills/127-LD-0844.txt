SUMMARY  17 
This bill replaces the requirement in current law for biennial plans for regional transit 18 
with a requirement for quinquennial plans, which is consistent with federal requirements.  19 
The bill eliminates the Interagency Transportation Coordinating Committee and replaces 20 
it with a new Public Transit Advisory Council.  The council is required to assist state 21 
agencies and the Legislatu re on issues related to public transit services.  22 
The bill provides that the council must include, at a minimum, the Commissioner of 23 
Transportation, the Commissioner of Health and Human Services, the Commissioner of 24 
Labor and the Commissioner of Economic an d Community Development or their 25 
designees.  The other members are appointed by the Commissioner of Transportation.  26 
The commissioner is required to invite members from the joint standing committee of the 27 
Legislature having jurisdiction over transportation  matters representing different political 28 
parties and at least one representative of the Northern New England Passenger Rail 29 
Authority to participate in council meetings.  30 
The council is required to report to the Governor and the joint standing committees o f 31 
the Legislature having jurisdiction over transportation matters and health and human 32 
services matters by March 1st of each odd -numbered year.  33
