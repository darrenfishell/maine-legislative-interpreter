SUMMARY  18 
The purpose of this bill is to increase the number of students pursuing graduate and 19 
undergraduate degrees in the fields of science, computer science, technology, engineering 20 
and mathematics.  This bill creates the Ma ine Science, Technology, Engineering and 21 
Mathematics Loan Program administered by the Finance Authority of Maine.  Under the 22 
bill, loans with varying interest rates will be provided to participating students depending 23 
on their future residency and employme nt: loans bearing an interest rate of 0% annually 24 
will be available to students who remain in or return to the State and work in the fields of 25 
science, computer science, technology, engineering and mathematics upon graduation; 26 
loans bearing an interest rat e of 5% annually will be available to students who remain in 27 
or return to the State upon graduation but are not employed in the fields of science, 28 
computer science, technology, engineering and mathematics; and loans bearing an 29 
interest rate of 8% annually will be available for students who live and work outside the 30 
State upon graduation.  The bill also authorizes the Commissioner of Education to grant 31 
funds to the program's Maine Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics Loan 32 
Fund if they become avai lable.  33
