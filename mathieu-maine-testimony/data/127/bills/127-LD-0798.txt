 SUMMARY  1 
This bill, which is contingent on approval by the voters of the State at referendum, 2 
does the following.  3 
1. It expands medical coverage under the MaineCare program to adults who q ualify 4 
under federal law with incomes up to 133% of the nonfarm income official poverty line, 5 
with the 5% federal income adjustment for family size.  It repeals the expansion of 6 
medical coverage under the MaineCare program December 31, 2020, and it provide s for 7 
repeal of the expansion prior to 2020 under certain circumstances.  8 
2. It requires the Commissioner of Health and Human Services to provide certain 9 
information to the Secretary of the United States Department of Health and Human 10 
Services to enable the  secretary to make the determination as to the eligibility of the State 11 
to obtain an enhanced Federal Medical Assistance Percentage for services for MaineCare 12 
members eligible as childless adults. It provides that until the secretary confirms that the 13 
State will get the enhanced reimbursement rate as set forth in the federal Patient 14 
Protection and Affordable Care Act, for the childless adult population in MaineCare, 15 
including persons who were members under that eligibility grouping on December 1, 16 
2009, the expansion of medical coverage under the MaineCare program will not take 17 
effect.  It requires the commissioner, upon receiving confirmation from the United States 18 
Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services, 19 
to notify  the President of the Senate, the Speaker of the House of Representatives and the 20 
Revisor of Statutes and to provide them with a copy of the written confirmation.  21 
3. It requires the Office of Fiscal and Program Review to contract with a private, 22 
nonpartisa n research organization to evaluate the impact of the MaineCare expansion. It 23 
requires a report to the Legislature on the amount of General Fund savings resulting from 24 
the MaineCare expansion.  25 
4. It amends current law on copayments in the MaineCare program . It directs the 26 
Department of Health and Human Services to increase copayments for adults with 27 
income above 100% of the nonfarm income official poverty line to the maximum 28 
allowable under federal law and to increase nominal copayments by the annual 29 
percen tage increase in the medical care component of the Consumer Price Index for All 30 
Urban Consumers.  It directs the department to increase MaineCare copayments for 31 
services provided in a hospital emergency room when the services are not emergency 32 
services.  I t requires the department to track aggregate copayments in compliance with 33 
federal law.  34
