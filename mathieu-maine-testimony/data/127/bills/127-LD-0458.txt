SUMMARY  17 
This bill requires every law enforcement officer in the State to use an electronic 18 
Violation Summons and Complaint form if requested by the person who has been served 19 
a traffic summons.  The Chief Judge of the District Court is required to approve for use 20 
an electronic Violation Summons and Compl aint. 21 
The bill also requires both the paper and electronic forms for a traffic summons for a 22 
traffic infraction, referred to as the Violation Summons and Complaint, to include the 23 
regular mailing address and the e -mail address of the person who has been se rved the 24 
traffic summons.  The bill also requires that the clerk of the District Court Violations 25 
Bureau enter the paper form of a Violation Summons and Complaint into the violations 26 
bureau's case management system within 2 business days of the law enforce ment officer's 27 
filing of the original of the Violation Summons and Complaint.  28 
The bill also requires that the Chief Judge of the District Court provide a report to the 29 
joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over transportation matt ers 30 
and the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over judiciary 31 
matters no later than January 15, 2017 regarding implementation of an electronic case 32 
management, data storage and filing system for the judicial branch.  The bill p rovides that 33 
the report must summarize efficiencies realized by electronic processing of traffic 34 
summonses within the District Court Violations Bureau.  The bill also requires the Chief 35 
Judge of the District Court and the Commissioner of Public Safety to f acilitate the 36 
roadside credit card payment of traffic summonses for traffic infractions and to develop 37 
and initiate a campaign to educate the public about electronic Violations Summonses and 38 
Complaints and roadside credit card payment.  39
