9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A "to S.P. 141, L.D. 373, Billl, "An Act To Allow lOa Moose Permit To Be Transferred to a Family Member" 
11 Amend the bill in section 1 by striking out all of subsection 15 (page 1, lines 3 to 17 
12 in L.D.) and inserting the following: 
13 '15. Moose permit transfer; family members. The commissioner, in cases 
14 involving exceptional extenuating circumstances as determined by the commissioner, 
15 may authorize a person who holds a valid moose permit to transfer that permit to a family 
16 member who meets the eligibility and permit requirements under this section and who is 
17 not otherwise prohibited from holding the moose permit. A transferor and a transferee 
18 are subject to the elimination of any accumulated points under subsection 8 and the 
19 3-year ineligibility period under subsection 5. For purposes of this subsection, "family 
20 member" means the, transferor's spouse, child, stepchild, grandchild, parent, grandparent, 
21 stepparent, brother, sister, half-sister, half-brother or adopted child. 
22 The commissioner shall adopt rules to implement this subsection and may establish a 
23 transfer fee to recover administrative costs associated with transferring moose permits. 
24 Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 
25 chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.' 
26 SUMMARY 
27 This amendment replaces the bill. It limits the allowed transfer of a moose permit to 
28 a family member to cases determined by the Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and 
29 Wildlife to involve exceptional extenuating circumstances. It adds to the definition of 
30 "family member" in the bill the transferor's stepchild and grandchild.
