2.  Procedures.  Except as provided in subsections 3 and 4, the following procedures 26 
are used to  determine the winner in an election for an office elected by ranked -choice 27

continuing candidate must be counted. Each continuing ballot counts as one vote for its 29 
highe st-ranked continuing candidate for that round. Exhausted ballots are not counted for 30

SUMMARY  13 
This initiated bill provides ranked -choice voting for the offices of United States  14 
Senator, United States Representative to Congress, Governor, State Senator and State 15 
Representative for elections held on or after January 1, 2018. Ranked -choice voting is a 16 
method of casting and tabulating votes in which voters rank candidates in order o f 17 
preference, tabulation proceeds in rounds in which last -place candidates are defeated and 18 
the candidate with the most votes in the final round is elected.  19
