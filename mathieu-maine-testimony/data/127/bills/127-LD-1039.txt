SUMMARY  4 
The bill makes the following chan ges to the Polygraph Examiners Act:  5 
1.  It defines "polygraph examination;"  6 
2.  It specifically authorizes a polygraph examiner to disclose information acquired 7 
from a polygraph examination to a member of a criminal justice agency if the polygraph 8 
examinat ion is conducted in the course of a criminal investigation; another licensed 9 
polygraph examiner in a professional consultation; an employee or intern working with 10 
the polygraph examiner; or as otherwise provided by law;  11 
3.  It provides that statements or d isclosures voluntarily made by an examinee that are 12 
adverse to the examinee's interests may be used against the examinee in the course of a 13 
criminal investigation or prosecution, to the fullest extent permitted by law;  14 
4.  It establishes fees for initial a nd renewal polygraph examiner licenses, and for 15 
intern polygraph examiner licenses;  16 
5.  It extends the term of a renewal polygraph examiner license from 2 to 4 years; and  17 
6.  It more specifically outlines the qualifications a person must have in order to b e 18 
eligible to obtain a polygraph examiner license.  19
