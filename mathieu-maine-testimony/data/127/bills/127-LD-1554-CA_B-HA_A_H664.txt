12 SUMMARY 
13 This amendment repeals 2 conflicting versions of the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 
14 17-A, section 1107-A, subsection 1, paragraph B, which was amended by Public Law 
15 2015, chapter 308, section 2 and chapter 346, section 6. The amendment resolves the 
16 conflicts. 
17 Under this amendment, as long as a person does not have one or more prior 
18 convictions for furnishing or trafficking scheduled drugs or operation of a 
19 methamphetamine laboratory, possession of the following is a Class D crime: 
20 1. Heroin in the amount of 200 milligrams or less; 
21 2. Cocaine in the amount of 2 grams or less; 
22 3. Cocaine in the form of cocaine base in the amount 2 grams or less; 
23 4. Oxycodone in the amount of 200 milligrams or less; 
24 5. Hydrocodone in the amount of 200 milligrams or less; 
25 6. Hydromorphone in the amount of 200 milligrams or less; 
26 7. Methamphetamine in the amount of 200 milligrams or less; or 
27 8. Fentanyl powder in the amount of 200 milligrams or less. 
28 For a person who has one or more convictions for violating the laws governing 
29 furnishing or trafficking scheduled drugs or operating a methamphetamine laboratory, 
30 possession of the above-listed scheduled drugs is a Class C crime.
