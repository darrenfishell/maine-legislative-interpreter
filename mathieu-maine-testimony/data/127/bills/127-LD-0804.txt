 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:  1 
Sec. 1.  Establishment.  The Maine Veterans Coordinating Committee is 2 
established as a nonprofit, nonpartisan and nonsectarian organization for the purpose of 3 
serving the State's congressional d elegation, the Legislature, local governments and the 4 
general public as a clearinghouse of information and suggested legislation regarding 5 
veterans' issues at local, state and federal levels.  6 
Sec. 2.  Definitions.  As used in this Act, the following terms have the following 7 
meanings unless otherwise indicated.  8 
1.  "Committee" means the Maine Veterans Coordinating Committee.  9 
2.  "Member organization" means any veterans' organization, including any auxiliary 10 
unit or society of or trust or foundation for a vet erans' organization, in the State qualifying 11 
as a tax -exempt organization under Section 501(c)(19) of the United States Internal 12 
Revenue Code of 1986, as amended.  13 
Sec. 3.  Composition.  The committee is composed of:  14 
1.  Five delegates from each member orga nization participating in the committee.  15 
Each member organization shall appoint an alternate for each delegate;  16 
2.  The Director of the Bureau of Veterans' Services within the Department of 17 
Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management or a designee; and  18 
3.  The administrator of the Maine Veterans' Home or a designee.  19 
The committee may invite the participation of the Center Director at VA Maine 20 
Healthcare System within the United States Department of Veterans Affairs.  21 
The commander, president or senior office r of a member organization who is present 22 
may appoint an acting delegate to replace an absent delegate if an alternate is not present 23 
at any meeting of the committee.  24 
Sec. 4.  Duties.  The committee shall:  25 
1.  Adopt bylaws to govern its doings, including b ylaws concerning member 26 
organization dues and voting;  27 
2.  Appoint officers, including a chair, a first vice -chair, a 2nd vice -chair, a secretary, 28 
a treasurer, a chaplain, a judge advocate and directors, and appoint members to standing 29 
committees, which mus t include an executive board and a budget committee.  Officers 30 
and members of standing committees serve 2 -year terms and must be appointed in 31 
October on even -numbered years;  32 
3.  Meet monthly from October through June; and  33 
4.  Serve as a clearinghouse of in formation and suggested legislation regarding 34 
veterans' issues at local, state and federal levels.  35
