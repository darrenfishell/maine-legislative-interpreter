15 Amend the bill by striking out all of section 2 and inserting the following: 
16 'Sec. 2. Department of Health and Human Services; standard admission 
17 contract; rulemaking. The Department of Health and Human Services shall amend its 
18 rules Chapter 110: Regulations Governing the Licensing and Functioning of Skilled 
19 Nursing Facilities and Nursing Facilities and Chapter 113: Regulations Governing the 
20 Licensing and Functioning of Assisted Housing Programs to modify the standard 
21 admission contract for residents of nursing homes and assisted housing programs to 
22 provide enhanced notice of an agent's obligations relating to a resident's eligibility for 
23 MaineCare benefits and to the agent's filing an application for those benefits on the 
24 resident's behalf. The amendments to the rules must describe a nursing home's or assisted 
25 housing program's legal remedies if an agent fails to fulfill the agent's obligations with 
26 respect to a nursing home or assisted housing program resident's eligibility and 
27 application for MaineCare benefits. Modifications to the standard admission contract in 
28 accordance with this section must specify in substantially similar language that, by 
29 signing the standard admission contract, the resident of a nursing home or assisted 
30 housing program, the resident's agent and the nursing home or assisted housing program 
31 acknowledge the following obligations: 
32 1. The nursing home or assisted housing program must explore whether the resident 
33 is financially eligible for MaineCare benefits and, if the resident is financially eligible, 
34 must refer the resident or the resident's agent to the appropriate regional office of the 
35 office for family independence within the Department of Health and Human Services for 
36 the purpose of filing an application for MaineCare benefits;

the department or its authorized agent when the nursing home or assisted housing 
program has reason to believe that the resident is financially eligible for MaineCare

6 3. The nursing home or assisted housing program must remind the resident or the 
7 resident's agent on a quarterly basis of the availability of MaineCare benefits when the 
8 nursing home or assisted housing program has reason to believe that the resident is 
9 financially eligible for such benefits. The nursing home or assisted housing program 
10 must document each quarterly reminder provided to the resident or the resident's agent; 
11 4. The nursing home or assisted housing program must assist the resident or the 
12 resident's agent in completing and filing an application for MaineCare benefits; 
13 5. The resident or the resident's agent must make timely payment to the nursing 
14 home or assisted housing program for the resident's care from the resident's resources that 
15 are available for that purpose; 
16 6. The resident or the resident's agent must notify the nursing home 'or assisted 
17 housing program when the resident or the resident's agent has reason to believe that the 
18 resident is financially eligible for MaineCare benefits and must timely apply for 
19 MaineCare benefits and provide to the department all information necessary to support 
20 the resident's application; and 
21 7. As an alternative to their obligation to file an application for MaineCare benefits, 
22 the resident or the resident's agent must authorize the nursing home or assisted housing 
23 program to file an application for MaineCare benefits on behalf of the resident and timely 
24 provide all necessary financial information to the nursing home or assisted housing 
25 program or provide the nursing home or assisted housing program with all authorizations 
26 necessary to secure this financial information, so that the nursing home or assisted 
27 housing program may complete the application process on behalf of the resident. 
28 The standard admission contract must provide that, if the resident's agent fails in the 
29 agent's obligation to ensure that the nursing home or assisted housing program is timely 
30 paid for the costs of the resident's monthly care or fails to perform any of the agent's 
31 obligations described in the amended rule or otherwise required by law or rule, in 
32 addition to any other legal remedy the nursing home or assisted housing program may 
33 possess, the nursing home or assisted housing program may pursue a legal action against 
34 the agent in Probate Court or District Court for review of the agent's performance. In 
35 addition to any other relief that the nursing home or assisted housing program may 
36 request from the court under such action, it must also include a request for attorney's fees 
37 and costs. 
38 Rules adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical rules as defined in the 
39 Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.'
