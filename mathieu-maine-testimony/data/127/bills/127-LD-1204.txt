 SUMMARY  1 
This bill establishes th e Recycling Grants and Low -interest Loan Program and the 2 
Maine Recycling Fund under the Department of Environmental Protection to assist 3 
municipalities and persons engaged in the business of collecting, transporting, processing 4 
or marketing recyclable mate rials and to fund activities designed to enhance the State's 5 
recycling rate and the diversion of recyclable materials that would otherwise be disposed 6 
of in a landfill or processed at a waste -to-energy facility.  The bill requires the fund to be 7 
used only to fund the program and to provide assistance to municipal recycling efforts.  8 
The bill establishes the Recycling Public Advisory Council to assist the department in 9 
the administration of the program.  The bill directs the council, in cooperation and 10 
consul tation with the department, to initiate and conduct public outreach and education 11 
programs on the purposes and value of source -separated recycling, or recycling by 12 
separation at the point of generation, and sustainable materials management in order to 13 
maxi mize the recovery of all recyclable materials and yard waste generated by 14 
commercial and residential sectors.  15 
This bill phases out beverage containers 32 ounces and greater in capacity from the 16 
bottle redemption laws beginning December 1, 2016 and ends the  liability of redemption 17 
centers and dealers to consumers for deposits on January 1, 2017 and the liability of 18 
manufacturers and distributors to redemption centers for deposits and handling fees on 19 
February 1, 2018.  The bill requires manufacturers and dis tributors of bottles 32 ounces or 20 
greater in capacity to pay a fee, beginning December 1, 2016 and ending December 1, 21 
2022, at the rate of $0.005 per beverage container delivered for sale or distribution during 22 
any month in order to provide revenue to the fund.  23
