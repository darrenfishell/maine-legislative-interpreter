17 SUMMARY 
18 This amendment strikes and replaces the bill to delay until the 2017-2018 school year 
19 the requirement that school administrative units fully implement the performance 
20 evaluation and professional growth system established pursuant to the Maine Revised 
21 Statutes, Title 20-A, chapter 508 to measure educator effectiveness. The amendment 
22 provides that, during the 2016-2017 school year, each unit must operate and apply a pilot 
23 project to all of the schools and applicable staff in the unit. The amendment delays the 
24 use of statewide student assessment data as part of the performance evaluation and 
25 professional growth system to measure educator effectiveness. The amendment also 
26 delays until the 2017-2018 school year the use of statewide assessment data of student 
27 academic achievement as part of a system to evaluate or rate the performance of public 
28 schools in the State that is similar to or different from the school performance grading 
29 system developed by the Department of Education and introduced on May 1, 2013.

Pilot Project expanded -School Administrative Units 
Requiring those local school administrative units (SAU's) that operated a pilot project related to the development and 
implemention of a performance evaluation and professional growth system for educators in school year 2015-2016 in 
one or more of the schools in the unit to operate the pilot project in all of the schools in the unit if the SAU chooses to 
operate the pilot project for a second year will not have a fiscal impact to the State or the Department of Education.
