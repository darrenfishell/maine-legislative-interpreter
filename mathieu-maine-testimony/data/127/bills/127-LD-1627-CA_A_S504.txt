SUMMARY 
2 This amendment strikes and replaces the bill, which was reported out by the Joint 
3 Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs for consideration by the 
4 committee and which includes certain recommendations proposed in the report submitted 
5 by the Maine Proficiency Education Council established pursuant to Resolve 2015, 
6 chapter 41, section 3. The amendment proposes the following initiatives related to 
7 certain recommendations included in the report submitted by the Maine Proficiency 
8 Education Council. 
9 I. Like the bill, it adds a provision to the laws governing accreditation standards that 
IO requires schools to demonstrate evidence of sufficient capacity through multiple 
11 pathways for students to reach proficiency in each of the content areas and the guiding 
12 principles of the system of learning results established under the Maine Revised Statutes, 
13 Title 20-A, section 6209. 
14 2. It refines a school's requirements regarding a student's demonstration of 
15 proficiency in order to be awarded a proficiency-based diploma that indicates the student 
16 has graduated from a secondary school that is subject to the system of learning results. 
17 3. It specifies a phase-in period for the implementation of the secondary school 
18 diploma requirements for students from the 2020-2021 school year to the 2024-2025 
19 school year. 
20 4. It strikes the proposal in the bill related to certifying a student's highest standard of 
21 proficiency in each content area of the system of learning results. 
22 5. It strikes the proposal in the bill related to certifying a student's demonstration of 
23 measures of college and career readiness. 
24 6. It amends the exception provision related to awarding a diploma to a secondary 
25 school student who is a child with a disability. 
26 7. It amends the exception provision related to awarding a diploma to a secondary 
27 school student who is enrolled in a career and technical education program by specifying 
28 a phase-in period for implementation of the exception provision for students enrolled in 
29 career and technical education programs from the 2020-2021 school year to the 2023-
30 2024 school year. 
31 8. Like the bill, it extends the provision of annual transition grants from the 
32 Department of Education through the 2020-2021 school year to provide funding to school 
33 administrative units to implement the proficiency-based graduation requirements and it 
34 allows the Commissioner of Education to include annual transition grant funding for 
35 school administrative units to meet the proficiency-based reporting and credentials 
36 requirements pursuant to Title 20-A, section 6209, subsection 3-A. 
3 7 9. It simplifies the language in the bill regarding certification of college and career 
38 readiness by removing the criteria of problem-solving, collaboration, critical thinking and 
39 communication. 
40 10. It directs the Commissioner of Education to develop rules for proficiency-based 
41 diploma standards under Title 20-A, section 4722-A to:

The Department of Education estimates the total cost to build a system to collect and aggregate data on the 
certification of content area proficiency reported by school administrative units to be $100,000. The department 
anticipates needing $75,000 in General Fund support in fiscal year 2017-18 and an additional $25,000 in General 
Fund support in fiscal year 2018-19 for contracted staff to build the system. This estimate is based on the assumption 
by the department that 7 5% of the work will be completed in fiscal year 2017-18 and 25% will be completed in fiscal 
year2018-19. 
Additional costs to the Department of Education associated with the rulemaking process can be absorbed within 
existing budgeted resources.
