 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 1 
become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and  2 
Whereas, Maine ranks first in New England in food insecurity; and  3 
Whereas, Maine has failed to use up to $30,000,000 per year of federal funding for 4 
student meals; and  5 
Whereas, the Task Force To End Student Hunger in Maine has compiled a 5 -year 6 
plan to end student hunger, including the convening of a working group to review the 7 
options available under s tate and federal law and address any barriers to ending student 8 
hunger; and  9 
Whereas, in order to ensure that the working group has adequate time to prepare its 10 
report, it is critical that the working group be convened by March 1, 2015; and  11 
Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 12 
the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 13 
immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 14 
therefore, be it  15 
Sec. 1.  Community Eligibility Provision working group.  Resolved:  That, 16 
by March 1, 2015, the Department of Education shall convene a working group to review 17 
the opportunities and challenges of the Community Eligibility Provision in the United 18 
States Depa rtment of Agriculture, study best practices related to the Community 19 
Eligibility Provision for school administrative units, address barriers to the use of the 20 
Community Eligibility Provision and make recommendations to the Department of 21 
Education and the D epartment of Health and Human Services.  The Department of 22 
Education shall, on behalf of the working group, issue a report to the Legislature and the 23 
Governor by April 30, 2015.  In the report, the working group shall specifically review 24 
options for reconc iling the differing requirements for the use of the Community 25 
Eligibility Provision and for reimbursement under Title I of the Elementary and 26 
Secondary Education Act, Public Law 89 -10 and for essential programs and services 27 
funding and shall recommend any needed legislation, rule changes or alternative solutions 28 
to address these issues.  29 
Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this 30
