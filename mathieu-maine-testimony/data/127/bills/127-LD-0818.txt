SUMMARY  28 
This bill amends the Maine Emergency Medical Services Act of 1982.  29 
Spec ifically, the bill:  30 
1.  Changes the term "first responder" to "emergency medical responder" to align the 31 
law with the Emergency Medical Services rules and the national scope of practice levels;  32 
2.  Modifies the composition of the Medical Direction and Prac tices Board to include 33 
an at -large member, a pharmacist and the statewide assistant emergency medical services 34

 medical director and to specify that the representative of the Maine Chapter of the 1 
American College of Emergency Medicine Physicians must be an emergency physician;  2 
3.  Removes a requirement that the Emergency Medical Services' Board or its staff 3 
must request the Attorney General to file a complaint in District Court to commence 4 
either full or emergency proceedings to suspend a license for more th an a year or to 5 
revoke a license when the board or its staff concludes to so suspend or revoke the license 6 
is in order;  7 
4.  Requires applicants and licensees to notify the Emergency Medical Services' 8 
Board regarding name and address changes, criminal convi ctions, actions taken by 9 
another regulatory agency against any of their occupational and professional licenses and 10 
material changes to their conditions and qualifications set forth in their original 11 
applications for licensure; and  12 
5.  Repeals the law estab lishing the Epinephrine Training Fund.  13
