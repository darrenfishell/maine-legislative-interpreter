SUMMARY  12 
This bill proposes to improve teacher quality in the State by amending the current 13 
statutes related to programs involving teacher preparation, support systems for 14 
provisional teachers, teacher certification, te acher salaries and college loans for students 15 
enrolled in a program of study leading to certification as a teacher.  The bill includes the 16 
following provisions.  17 
1.  It expands the student teaching experience and specifics an academic achievement 18 
level nece ssary for applicants to qualify for a provisional teaching certificate.  19 
2.  It strengthens the support systems required to provide assistance for provisional 20 
teachers during each year of their probationary period.  21 
3.  It directs the Commissioner of Educati on to pay salary supplements to classroom 22 
teachers who are involved in the operation of a school administrative unit's support 23 
system approved in accordance with the provisions of the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 24 
20-A, section 13015.  25 
4.  It provides that an individual who has not completed a master's degree program 26 
and who is issued a teacher certificate in accordance with Title 20 -A, chapter 502 on or 27 
after July 1, 2015 may not receive a renewed certificate or a new teaching certificate 28 
unless the individ ual completes a master's degree program within 5 years of being 29 
employed as a teacher in the State.  30 
5.  It increases the minimum salaries for certified teachers to $40,000 beginning in 31 
the 2016 -2017 school year and includes state funding for the incrementa l costs of meeting 32 
this minimum as part of the school funding formula.  33 
6.  It requires the Department of Education to increase the qualifying scores for the 34 
standardized qualifying examinations for applicants for initial teacher certification in its 35 
Rule C hapter 13:  Qualifying Examinations for Teachers, Educational Specialists and 36 
Administrators no later than July 1, 2016.  37 GENERAL FUND  2015 -16 2016 -17
