2.  Program created.  The Maine Paid Family Leave Insurance Program is created 13 
within the Department of Labor.  The program provides wage -replacement benefits to 14 
eligible persons who are on family medical leave from employment.  15 
3.  Eligibility.  To be eligible for benefits, a person must be employed and must have 16 
had earnings from employ ment and made contributions to the program in at least 12 of 17 
the prior 52 weeks.  An employee shall file a written notice with the employee's employer 18 
at least 30 days prior to commencement of the family medical leave.  Failure by the 19 
employee to provide t he written notice may result in delay or reduction in the benefits, 20 
except in the event the time of the leave is unforeseeable or the time of the leave changes 21 
due to unforeseeable circumstances.  22 
4.  Benefits provided.  An eligible employee under this subc hapter receives a weekly 23 
benefit of 2/3 of that person's average weekly wage in the 2 quarters in the preceding 52 24 
weeks in which the person's wage was highest.  The maximum amount of the weekly 25 
benefit is 100% of the state average weekly wage.  Benefits a re provided for up to 6 26 
weeks in any 12 -month period.  Benefits must be reduced by workers' compensation and 27 
sick pay. Benefits may not be reduced by other disability benefits, pension payments or 28 
other earnings.  The waiting period before benefits begin i s 7 days, except that a benefit 29 
payment for the first 7 days must be made retroactively if the family medical leave lasts 3 30 
weeks or more.  31 
5.  Fund created.  The Paid Family Leave Insurance Fund is created to collect 32 
contributions and fund benefits under t his section.  The fund may be used to pay 33 
administrative costs of the program and to pay benefits.  The fund does not lapse but must 34 
be carried forward to implement this subchapter.  35

SUMMARY  28 
This bill creates the Maine Paid Family Leave Insurance Program to provide wage - 29 
replacement benefits to persons who qualify for family medical leave.  The program is 30 
funded by em ployee contributions and provides 2/3 of the employee's average weekly 31 
wage for up to 6 weeks.  Employee contributions are collected on a sliding scale based on 32 
wages.  33
