 SUMMARY  1 
This bill establishes the Ocean Acidification Council to identify, study, prevent , 2 
remediate and mitigate the direct and indirect effects of coastal and ocean acidification on 3 
species that are commercially harvested and grown in the State's coastal and ocean 4 
environments.  5 
It provides for 16 council members, including 2 members of the S enate, 3 members 6 
of the House of Representatives, 2 representatives of an environmental or community 7 
group, 3 persons who fish commercially, including at least one aquaculturist, 3 scientists 8 
and the Commissioner of Marine Resources, the Commissioner of En vironmental 9 
Protection and the Commissioner of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry or those 10 
commissioners' designees.  11 
The powers and duties of the council include, but are not limited to, the following:  12 
1.  Reviewing, analyzing and studying the existing  scientific literature and data on 13 
coastal and ocean acidification and how it has directly or indirectly affected or may 14 
potentially affect commercially harvested and grown species along the coast of the State;  15 
2.  Identifying and monitoring the factors co ntributing to coastal and ocean 16 
acidification and identifying methods to mitigate acidification;  17 
3.  Working to implement the recommendations contained in the December 2014 18 
report of the Commission To Study the Effects of Coastal and Ocean Acidification an d Its 19 
Existing and Potential Effects on Species That Are Commercially Harvested and Grown 20 
Along the Maine Coast established by Resolve 2013, chapter 110;  21 
4.  Advising state agencies, the Legislature, Maine's congressional delegation, the 22 
Governor and feder al entities on matters of coastal and ocean acidification;  23 
5.  Assisting the Legislature and the Governor with pending legislation related to 24 
coastal and ocean acidification including giving testimony at a public hearing on 25 
legislation before a joint stand ing committee of the Legislature;  26 
6.  Identifying and promoting economic development opportunities afforded by ocean 27 
acidification through development and commercialization of new technologies and 28 
businesses;  29 
7.  Recommending or submitting legislation to t he Legislature relating to coastal and 30 
ocean acidification matters; and  31 
8.  Holding public hearings to receive testimony and recommendations from 32 
members of the public and qualified experts on matters related to coastal and ocean 33 
acidification.  34 
This bill a lso requires the council to submit an annual report to the Legislature and 35 
authorizes the council to accept funding from outside sources and contains a provision 36 
repealing the laws establishing the council December 31, 2018.  37
