SUMMARY  22 
This bill extends the jurisdiction of the District Court to be concurrent with the courts 23 
of probate over matters concerning custody or other parental rights of a child under the 24 
Maine Revised Statutes, Title 18 -A, including, but not li mited to, adoption, termination of 25 
parental rights, change of name and guardianship of a minor.  The District Court has 26 
exclusive, continuing jurisdiction over a matter concerning custody or other parental 27 
rights of a child if an interim or final order con cerning the child was entered in the 28 
District Court and remains in effect, proceedings seeking such an order are pending in the 29 
District Court or a matter has been removed to the District Court from the Probate Court.  30 
This bill provides that, in any matter  concerning custody or other parental rights of a 31 
child, the judge of the District Court or the probate judge who is presiding require all 32 
parties to disclose whether they have knowledge of any interim or final order then in 33 
effect concerning custody or ot her parental rights of the minor child, any proceeding 34 
seeking such an order or other related actions currently filed or pending before any court 35 
of this or another state.  If the proceeding is in a Probate Court and the judge determines 36 
that the District Court has exclusive, continuing jurisdiction, the judge of probate must 37 
transfer the case to the District Court.  38
