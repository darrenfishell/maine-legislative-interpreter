9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "U" to S.P. 671, L.D. 1646, Bill, "An Act To 
10 Prevent Opiate Abuse by Strengthening the Controlled Substances Prescription 
11 Monitoring Program" 
12 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
13 'An Act To Clarify the Authority for Partial Dispensing of Opioid Prescriptions' 
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and before the 
15 summary and inserting the following: 
16 'Sec. 1. 32 MRSA §13702-A, sub-§20-A is enacted to read: 
17 20-A. Opioid medication. "Opioid medication" means a controlled substance 
18 containing an opioid included in schedule II of 21 United States Code, Section 812 or 21 
19 Code of Federal Regulations, Part 1308. 
20 Sec. 2. 32 MRSA §13786-B is enacted to read: 
21 §13786-B. Partial dispensing of prescription for opioid medication 
22 1. Partial dispensing authorized. Notwithstanding any law or rule to the contrary, 
23 a pharmacist may partially dispense a prescription for an opioid medication in a lesser 
24 quantity than the recommended full quantity indicated on the prescription if requested by 
25 the patient for whom the prescription is written. The remaining quantity of the 
26 prescription in excess of the recommended full quantity is void and may not be dispensed 
27 without a new prescription. 
28 2. Notice to practitioner. If a pharmacist partially dispenses a prescription for an 
29 opioid medication as permitted under this section, the pharmacist or the pharmacist's 
30 designee shall, within a reasonable time following the partial dispensing but not more 
31 than 7 days, notify the practitioner of the quantity of the opioid medication actually 
32 dispensed. The notice may be conveyed by a notation on the patient's electronic health 
33 record or by electronic transmission, by facsimile or by telephone to the practitioner.'
