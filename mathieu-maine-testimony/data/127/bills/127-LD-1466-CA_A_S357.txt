9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT " A " to S.P. 564, L.D. 1466, Bill, "An Act To 
10 Exempt Certain Liquor Sales Representatives from Licensing Requirements" 
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
12 'An Act Regarding Sales Representatives Employed by Licensed Wholesalers' 
13 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and before the 
14 summary and inserting the following: 
15 'Sec. 1. 28-A MR.SA §1401, sub-§7, as enacted by PL 1987, c. 45, Pt. A, §4, is 
16 amended to read: 
17 7. Warehouses and sales representatives. A wholesale licensee shall maintain a 
18 warehouse or warehouses within the State and employ one or more sales representatives, 
19 licensed under chapter 59, for the purpose of soliciting orders. For the purposes of this 
20 subsection. "sales representative" means an employee of a wholesale licensee whose 
21 primary duty is soliciting orders from or making sales to retail licensees. 
22 Sec. 2. 28-A MR.SA §1502, first ,r, as amended by PL 1997, c. 373, §135, is 
23 further amended to read: 
24 The sales Sales representatives, including those described in section 1401. subsection 
25 L shall apply to the bureau for a license disclosing the person, firm or corporation 
26 represented.' 
27 SUMMARY 
28 This amendment replaces the bill and changes the title. The amendment provides a 
29 definition of "sales representative" as it applies to licensed wholesalers. It clarifies that 
30 the provision in current law that requires liquor sales representatives to be licensed by the 
31 Department of Administrative and Financial Services, Bureau of Alcoholic Beverages 
32 and Lottery Operations applies to sales representatives employed by licensed wholesalers.
