SUMMARY 
2 The bill lowers the eligibility age for a junior hunting license from 10 to 8 years of 
3 age; this amendment instead removes the minimum age requirement for a junior hunting 
4 license. This amendment also provides that the adult supervisor of the junior hunter must 
5 hold or have held a Maine hunting license or have successfully completed a hunter safety 
6 course. It also increases the number of times a person may hold an apprentice hunter 
7 license from twice to 5 times before becoming ineligible to purchase the license. This 
8 amendment provides an effective date of January 1, 2016.

The bill removes the requirement that somebody be at least 10 years old to be eligible for a junior hunting license and 
allows those under 16 years old to obtain this license. This will increase General Fund revenue collected through the 
Department ofInland Fisheries & Wildlife (IF&W) by an estimated $27,875 annually beginning in fiscal year 2016-
17. IF&W expects to increase Federal Expenditures Fund revenue by $25,445 annually for federal grants for the 
expected increase in licenses issued beginning in fiscal year 2016-17.
