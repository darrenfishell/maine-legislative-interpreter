 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:  1 
Sec. 1.  36 MRSA §1752, sub -§8-D is enacted to read:  2 
8-D.  Prescription.  "Prescription" means an order, formula or recipe issued in any 3 
form of oral, written or electronic means or other means of transmission by a health care 4 
practitioner licensed under Title 32.  5 
Sec. 2.  36 MRSA §1752, sub -§9-F is enacted to read:  6 
9-F.  Prosthetic or orthotic device.  "Prosthetic or orthotic device" means a 7 
replacement, corrective or supportive device, includin g repair and replacement parts for 8 
such device, worn on, in or next to the body to:  9 
A.  Artificially replace a missing portion of the body;  10 
B.  Prevent or correct physical deformity or malfunction; or  11 
C.  Support a weak or deformed portion of the body.  12 
Sec. 3.  36 MRSA §1760, sub -§5-A, as amended by PL 2009, c. 434, §24, is 13 
further amended to read:  14 
5-A.  Prosthetic or orthotic devices.  Sale of prosthetic aids, hearing aids or 15 
eyeglasses and artificial devices designed for the use of a particular individual to correct 16 
or alleviate physical incapacity;  or orthotic devices sold by prescription  and sale of 17 
crutches and wheelchairs for the use of sick, injured or disabled persons and not for 18 
rental.  19 
Sec. 4.  Effective date.  This Act takes effect October 1, 2015.  20 
SUMMARY  21 
This bill creates definitions for "prosthetic or orthotic device" and "prescription."  It 22 
clarifies the sales tax exemption for prosthetic devices and exempts orthotic devices sold 23 
by prescription.  24
