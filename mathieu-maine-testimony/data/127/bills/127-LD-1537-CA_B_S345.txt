9 Sec. B-1. Law enforcement and county jail initiatives regarding 
10 treatment, recovery and support services. The Commissioner of Public Safety, 
11 after receiving advice from the Maine Sheriffs' Association and the Maine Chiefs of 
12 Police Association, shall administer grants to local law enforcement agencies and county 
13 jails located in geographically diverse communities throughout the State to fund projects 
14 designed solely to facilitate pathways to community-based treatment, recovery and 
15 support services. Grant applications must include statements of purpose and measurable 
16 goals for the projects and use for the funds. Grant recipients shall report to the 
17 Commissioner of Public Safety annually on the anniversary date of the grant award 
18 regarding the status of the projects, a description of how the funds were spent, the results 
19 of the projects and use of the funds and any recommendations for modification of the 
20 projects, including any available information concerning their effectiveness in reducing 
21 drug use and recidivism. The Commissioner of Public Safety shall provide a report 
22 summarizing the results of the grant program and providing recommendations as to its

11 'Sec. C-1. Detoxification center. The Department of Health and Human Services 
12 shall provide funding to a substance abuse treatment entity to develop and operate a 
13 detoxification center with at least 10 beds that provides a social detoxification program in 
14 an organized residential nonmedical setting delivered by appropriately trained staff that 
15 provide safe 24-hour monitoring, observation and support in a supervised environment 
16 for a client to achieve initial recovery from the effects of alcohol or another drug. 
17 1. The substance abuse treatment entity must: 
18 A. Be located in a northern or eastern area of the State with high rates of opioid use 
19 and accessible to related services and supports; 
20 B. Specialize in treating substance abuse and mental health disorders; and 
21 C. Have an established history of providing substance abuse treatment and running 
22 residential programs in the region. 
23 2. At least 40% of the occupancy in the detoxification center established under this 
24 section must be made available to individuals who do not have MaineCare coverage or 
25 health insurance coverage for detoxification treatment. 
26 3. No later than June 30, 2016 the department shall begin distributing the funds 
27 appropriated in section 5.

29 periodically before the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction

8 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, makes the following 
9 changes to the bill. 
10 1. In Part A, it: 
11 A. Adds the Joint Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety to the 
12 entities to which periodic reports must be made by the Commissioner of Public 
13 Safety; 
14 B. Adds funding provided by financial order 03451F16 to the unencumbered 
15 balances that may not be transferred; 
16 C. Adds funding provided by financial order 03451F16 to the unencumbered 
17 balances that may not lapse and restricts the nonlapsing year to fiscal year 2015-16 
18 only; 
19 D. Eliminates the appropriation for 10 investigative agents in fiscal year 2015-16 and 
20 reduces the appropriation in fiscal year 2016-17 from $1,600,000 to $1,230,000; and 
21 E. Adds a section to transfer $1,230,000 from the Gambling Control Board 
22 administrative expenses, Other Special Revenue Funds account to the unappropriated 
23 surplus of the General Fund on or before the close of fiscal year 2016-17. 
24 2. In Part B, it: 
25 A. Requires the Department of Public Safety, rather than the Attorney General, to 
26 administer the grants to local law enforcement agencies; 
27 B. Requires consultation with the Maine Sheriffs' Association and the Maine Chiefs 
28 of Police Association and requires enhanced reporting; and 
29 C. Limits to fiscal year 2015-16 the period for which funds provided by this Part 
30 may not lapse. 
31 3. In Part C, it: 
32 A. Changes the requirement for a new 10-bed detoxification center to a requirement 
33 for a new detoxification center with at least 10 beds; 
34 B. Limits to fiscal year 2015-16 the period for which funds provided by this Part 
35 may not lapse; and 
36 C. Changes the requirement that the center be in Bangor to a requirement that the 
3 7 center be in a northern or eastern area of the State with high rates of opioid use and 
38 accessible to related services and supports.

4. In Part C and Part D, it changes the reporting requirements so that the 
Commissioner of Health and Human Services reports periodically to the Joint Standing 
Committee on Health and Human Services rather than the Joint Standing Committee on 
Appropriations and Financial Affairs. 
5. In Part D, it: 
A. Limits to fiscal year 2015-16 the period for which funds provided by this Part 
may not lapse; 
B. Removes the reference to the Maine Association of Substance Abuse Programs 
and directs the Department of Health and Human Services to provide funding within 
60 days after the effective date of the Part to an organization with expertise and 
experience in substance abuse prevention, treatment and peer recovery services to 
provide substance abuse services statewide; 
C. Adds to the services to be provided a requirement that coordination services 
include links to career services and a requirement that the organization develop and 
maintain a directory of providers and resources that is publicly available; and 
D. Requires that although funding may be sole-sourced for fiscal years 2015-16 and 
2016-17, future awards must be awarded on a competitive basis. It requires the 
Department of Health and Human Services to issue a request for proposals by 
January 1, 2017 that is competitively bid through the regular process pursuant to the 
Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, chapter 155 for ongoing funding awards beginning 
no later than July 1, 2017. 
6. In Part E, it: 
A. Limits to fiscal year 2015-16 the period for which funds provided by this Part 
may not lapse; and 
B. Adds individual, group and intensive outpatient treatment to the substance abuse 
outpatient services for the uninsured for which a funding increase is provided. 
7. In Part F, it changes the source of the transfer to the unapporpriated surplus of the 
General Fund from the funds received pursuant to the court order in State of Maine v. 
McGraw-Hill Companies, Inc. and Standard & Poor's Financial Services, LLC to the 
Fund for a Healthy Maine. 
8. It adds Part G, which transfers funds from Personal Services to All Other in the 
Judicial Department, Courts -Supreme, Superior and District program, General Fund 
account in fiscal year 2015-16 to provide funding to support increased criminal dockets, 
including an increase in criminal jury trials.

Fiscal Detail and Notes 
Part A of this bill includes a General Fund appropriation of $1,230,000 in fiscal year 2016-17 to the Department of Public Safety 
to fund 10 investigative agents. Part A includes a one-time transfer of$1,230,000 in fiscal year 2016-17 to the unappropriated 
surplus of the General Fund from the Gambling Control Board administrative expenses, Other Special Revenue Funds account. 
Part B of this bill includes a General Fund appropriation of $50,000 in fiscal years 2015-16 and 2016-17 for the Department of 
Public Safety to provide funding to local law enforcement entities. 
Part C, D and E of this bill includes General Fund appropriations to The Department of Health and Human Services of $675,000 
in fiscal year 2015-16 and $1,725,000 in fiscal year 2016-17. This funding will be used to provide multiple services, including 
funding for the development and operation of a detoxification center, ongoing funding for annual grants for the establishment and 
expansion of peer support recovery centers and the coordination and provision of substance abuse prevention and education in 
schools and communities, and ongoing funding to increase substance abuse residential treatment and outpatient services for the 
uninsured. 
Part F of this bill provides one-time transfers of $725,000 in fiscal year 2015-16 and $1, 775,000 in fiscal year 2016-17 to the 
unappropriated surplus of the General Fund from the Fund for a Healthy Maine. 
Part G of this bill includes a General Fund line category transfer from Personal Services to All Other of $240,000 in fiscal year 
2015-16 for the Judicial Department.
