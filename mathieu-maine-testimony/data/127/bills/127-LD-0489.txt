CHAPTER 43  33 
RIGHT TO REFRAIN FRO M JOINING A UNION  34 
§3501.  Definitions  35 
As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 36 
have the following meanings.  37

 1.  Employer.  "Employer" m eans a person, firm, association, corporation, public 1 
employer, public school employer, public college, public university, public institution or 2 
public education agency.  3 
2.  Labor organization.  "Labor organization" means an organization, agency or 4 
employe e representation committee or union that exists for the purpose, in whole or in 5 
part, of negotiating or bargaining with employers on behalf of employees concerning 6 
wages, rates of pay, hours of work, other conditions of employment or other forms of 7 
compens ation.  8 
§3502.  Right to refrain  9 
Notwithstanding any law to the contrary, a person may not be required, as a condition 10 
of employment or continuation of employment, to:  11 
1.  Member.  Become or remain a member of a labor organization;  12 
2.  Dues.  Pay any dues, fees, assessments or other similar charges, however 13 
denominated, of any kind or amount to a labor organization; or  14 
3.  Payment to 3rd party.  Pay to a charity or other 3rd party, in lieu of payments 15 
specified in subsection 2, any amount equivalent to or a pro rata portion of dues, fees, 16 
assessments or other charges required of members of a labor organization.  17 
§3503.  Agreements in violation  18 
An agreement, understanding or practice, written or oral, implied or expressed, 19 
between a labor organization and an em ployer that violates a provision of this chapter is 20 
unlawful, void and of no legal effect.  21 
§3504.  Penalty  22 
A person who directly or indirectly violates any provision of this chapter commits a 23 
Class D crime.  24 
§3505.  Injunctive relief  25 
A person injured as a r esult of another person's violation or threatened violation of a 26 
provision of this chapter is entitled to injunctive relief against the person violating or 27 
threatening to violate this chapter.  28 
§3506.  Damages  29 
A person injured as a result of a violation or threatened violation of a provision of 30 
this chapter may recover all damages, including court costs and reasonable attorney's 31 
fees, resulting from the violation or threatened violation. A recovery of damages under 32 
this section is independent of and in addit ion to the penalty provided in section 3504.  33

 §3507.  Duty to investigate and enforce  1 
The Attorney General shall investigate complaints of a violation or threatened 2 
violation of this chapter and shall prosecute any person violating any of its provisions. 3 
The Attorney General shall use all means available to ensure effective enforcement of 4 
this chapter.  5 
§3508.  Jurisdiction  6 
The Superior Court has jurisdiction to hear and decide prosecutions of all offenses 7 
under this chapter.  8 
§3509.  Exceptions  9 
This chapter d oes not apply to the following:  10 
1.  Federal Railway Labor Act.  Employers and employees covered by the federal 11 
Railway Labor Act, 45 United States Code, Chapter 8;  12 
2.  Federal employers and employees.  Federal employers and employees;  13 
3.  Federal enclaves.   Employers and employees on federal enclaves;  14 
4.  Federal preemption or conflict.  Federal law when the provisions of this chapter 15 
conflict with or are otherwise preempted by that federal law; or  16 
5.  Existing contract.  An employment contract entered into  before the effective date 17 
of this chapter during the term of that contract.  18 
§3510.  Application  19 
This chapter applies to a contract entered into, renewed or extended on or after the 20 
effective date of this chapter.  21 
SUMMARY  22 
This bill prohibits a person, incl uding a public employee, an employee of the 23 
University of Maine System, the Maine Maritime Academy or the Maine Community 24 
College System and an employee of the judicial branch, from being required to join a 25 
labor organization or pay any labor organization dues or fees as a condition of 26 
employment or continuation of employment, notwithstanding any state law to the 27 
contrary.  A violation is a Class D crime and is also subject to civil damages and 28 
injunctive relief.  The Attorney General is responsible for enf orcement and is required to 29 
prosecute all violations.  30
