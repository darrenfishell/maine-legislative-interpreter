SUMMARY  26 
This bill allows the Public Utilities Commission to contract for the sto rage and 27 
distribution of liquefied natural gas to provide a source of natural gas during peak winter 28 
months. This bill defines a "liquefied natural gas contract" and provides that, prior to 29 
entering into a contract, the Public Utilities Commission must ens ure that a liquefied 30 
natural gas facility will be located in a rural, low -income community within the State, a 31 
study has been conducted by an independent 3rd party showing the construction of the 32 
facility will result in a minimum of 200 direct and indirect  jobs, exclusive of jobs directly 33 
linked to the actual construction of the facility and, if feasible, the facility will be 34 
constructed by a qualified construction firm based in the State.  35 
This bill also requires that before the Public Utilities Commission executes or directs 36 
the execution of an LNG contract, the Governor must approve in writing the contract.  37
