SUMMARY  6 
This bill requires the Director of the Bureau of Forestry within the Department of 7 
Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to appoint no fewer than 16 Forest Ranger IIIs, 8 
subject to the Civil Service Law, who are each to be armed with a firearm and are 9 
selected from forest rangers employed by the bureau.  A Forest Ranger III is required to 10 
meet the sa me training, certification and evaluation requirements as a full -time law 11 
enforcement officer as established by the department and Board of Trustees of the Maine 12 
Criminal Justice Academy.  A Forest Ranger III has the same powers and duties as a 13 
sheriff or sheriff's deputy to enforce criminal and civil laws, with an emphasis on forestry, 14 
natural resource and wildfire protection laws.  15 
Additionally, the director is required to appoint the state supervisor and no fewer than 16 
32 Forest Ranger IIs, subject to the Civil Service Law, who serve in a role similar to that 17 
of current forest rangers and who must be provided ballistic vests and electronic control 18 
devices for their safety while performing their duties.  19 
This bill changes the duties of forest rangers to inclu de investigating, planning and 20 
implementing measures regarding forest health issues, including the control of invasive 21 
forest insect species, and providing support to the units within the bureau that are 22 
responsible for forest health and insect disease con trol.  It also changes the duties of 23 
forest rangers to include providing assistance in disasters and emergencies, including 24 
search and rescue operations.  It removes the prohibition of the purchase of firearms, 25 
ballistic vests and other firearms -related eq uipment by the Department of Conservation.  26
