 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 1 
become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and  2

SUMMARY  7 
The purpose of this bill is to address  drug addiction by increasing the number of drug 8 
enforcement agents and by increasing the availability of prevention, treatment and 9 
recovery programs.  10 
1.  Part A provides ongoing funding for 10 investigative agents in the Department of 11 
Public Safety, Maine  Drug Enforcement Agency.  12 
2.  Part B directs the Department of the Attorney General to administer grants to local 13 
law enforcement agencies and county jails located in geographically diverse communities 14 
throughout the State to fund projects designed solely to facilitate pathways to community - 15 
based treatment, recovery and support services.  16 
3.  Part C directs the Department of Health and Human Services to provide grants to a 17 
substance abuse treatment entity to develop and operate a 10 -bed social detoxification  18 
center located in the greater Bangor area.  19 
4.  Part D provides ongoing funding for annual grants to the Maine Association of 20 
Substance Abuse Programs for the establishment and expansion of peer support recovery 21 
centers and the coordination and provision o f substance abuse prevention and education 22 
in schools and communities.  23 
5.  Part E provides funding to increase substance abuse residential treatment and 24 
substance abuse outpatient services for the uninsured.  25 
6.  Part F transfers $725,000 in fiscal year 201 5-16 and $1,775,000 in fiscal year 26 
2016 -17 from the funds received pursuant to the court order in State of Maine v. 27 
McGraw -Hill Companies, Inc. and Standard & Poor's Financial Services, LLC , Kennebec 28 
County Superior Court Docket No. BCD -CV-14-49, to the un appropriated surplus of the 29 
General Fund.  30
