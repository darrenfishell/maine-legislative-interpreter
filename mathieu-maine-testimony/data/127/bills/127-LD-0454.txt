ยง6006.  Student information privacy; school service providers  3 
This section may be known and cited as "the Student Information Privacy Act."  4 
1.  Defin itions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 5 
following terms having the following meanings.  6 
A.  "Mobile application" means a software application designed to be downloaded to 7 
and installed on a portable computing device.  8 
B.  "School service" means a website, mobile application or online service that:  9 
(1)  Is designed and marketed for use in elementary or secondary schools in the 10 
State;  11 
(2)  Is used at the direction of teachers or other employees of an elementary or 12 
secondary  school; and  13 
(3)  Collects, maintains or uses student personal information.  14 
"School service" does not include a website, mobile application or online service that 15 
is designed and marketed for use by individuals or entities generally, even if it is also 16 
marketed to elementary or secondary schools in the State.  17 
C.  "School service provider" means an entity that operates a school service.  18 
D.  "Student" means a student of an elementary or secondary school in the State.  19 
E.  "Student personal information" means i nformation collected through a school 20 
service that identifies an individual student or that is linked to information that 21 
identifies an individual student.  22 
2.  Transparency.  A school service provider may collect, use and share student 23 
personal information  only in accordance with this section.  24 
A.  A school service provider shall provide to schools and teachers that use the 25 
provider's school service clear and easy -to-understand information about the types of 26 
student personal information it collects and about  how it uses and shares student 27 
personal information.  28 
B.  A school service provider shall provide to schools and teachers that use the 29 
provider's school service prominent notice before making material changes to its 30 
privacy policies.  31 
C.  A school service p rovider shall facilitate access to and correction of a student's 32 
personal information by a student or student's parent or guardian either directly or 33 
through the student's school or teacher.  34 
D.  When a school service is offered to a school or teacher, a sc hool service provider 35 
may provide information required by paragraphs A and B to the school or teacher.  36

 E.  A school service provider may collect, use and share student personal information 1 
only for purposes authorized by the student's school or teacher or with the consent of 2 
the student or the student's parent or guardian.  3 
F.  A school service provider may not sell student personal information.  4 
G.  A school service provider may not use or share student personal information for 5 
purposes of targeting advertis ements to students based on students' online behaviors.  6 
H.  A school service provider may not use student personal information to create a 7 
personal profile of a student other than for supporting purposes authorized by the 8 
student's school or teacher or wit h the consent of the student or the student's parent or 9 
guardian.  10 
I.  A school service provider must obtain consent before using student personal 11 
information in a manner that is inconsistent with the provider's privacy policy for the 12 
applicable school serv ice in effect at the time of collection of the information.  If the 13 
student's personal information was collected directly from a student, the school 14 
service provider must obtain consent from the student or the student's parent or 15 
guardian.  In all other ca ses, the school service provider may obtain consent from the 16 
school or teacher.  17 
J.  A school service provider shall maintain a comprehensive information security 18 
program that is reasonably designed to protect the security, privacy, confidentiality 19 
and inte grity of student personal information.  The information security program 20 
must use appropriate administrative, technological and physical safeguards.  21 
K.  A school service provider may not knowingly retain student personal information 22 
beyond the time period authorized by the school or teacher, unless the school service 23 
provider has obtained consent of the student or the student's parent or guardian.  24 
L.  A school service provider shall obligate any 3rd parties involved on the provider's 25 
behalf in the providing  of school services to meet the requirements of this subsection.  26 
M.  Before permitting a successor entity to access student personal information, a 27 
school service provider shall ensure that the successor entity will abide by all privacy 28 
and security commit ments related to previously collected student personal 29 
information.  30 
3.  Construction.  Nothing in this Act may be construed to:  31 
A.  Prohibit the use of student personal information for purposes of customized 32 
education; or  33 
B.  Authorize the dissemination of  information in violation of section 6001.  34 
4.  Rulemaking.  The commissioner may adopt rules to implement this section.  35 
Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, 36 
chapter 375, subchapter 2 -A. 37
