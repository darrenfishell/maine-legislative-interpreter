SUMMARY  5 
This bill provides that, effective January 1, 2016, a person may not package or label a 6 
disposable cleaning or personal hygiene product for distribution or sale in the State in a 7 
package or with a label that states the product is flushable and safe for sewer a nd septic 8 
systems unless the package or label states in a highly visible manner that the product is 9 
flushable and safe for sewer and septic systems; the person makes available to the 10 
Department of Environmental Protection and maintains written documentatio n of testing 11 
substantiating that the product meets the acceptance criteria for flushability as published 12 
in the Guidance Document for Assessing the Flushability of Nonwoven Consumer 13 
Products, published by the Association of the Nonwoven Fabrics Industry on  June 1, 14 
2013; and this testing has been performed by a laboratory that is capable of performing 15 
and qualified to perform the testing.  The bill also provides that a person may not package 16 
or label a disposable cleaning or personal hygiene product for dist ribution or sale in the 17 
State that is not flushable or safe for sewer and septic systems unless the package or label 18 
on the product states in a highly visible manner that the product is not flushable or safe 19 
for sewer or septic systems.  These requirements  do not apply to a wholesaler or retailer 20 
that distributes or sells but does not package or label disposable cleaning or personal 21 
hygiene products or to liquid, gel or powder cleaning products or septic treatment 22 
products.  The bill directs the department to adopt rules to implement these provisions.  23
