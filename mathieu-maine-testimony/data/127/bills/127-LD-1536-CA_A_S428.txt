14 Amend the bill by striking out all of section 1 and inserting the following: 
15 'Sec. 1. 25 MRSA c. 411 is enacted to read: 
16 CHAPTER 411 
17 BALLISTIC VESTS 
18 §3851. Provision of ballistic vests for law enforcement 
19 1. Definitions. As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
20 following terms have the following meanings. 
21 A. "Ballistic vest" means a form of personal armor worn on the torso that prevents or 
22 restricts penetration by a projectile discharged by a firearm as defined in Title 17-A, 
23 section 2, subsection 12-A. 
24 B. "Law enforcement officer" means an active: 
25 (1) State police officer: 
26 (2) Game warden or other law enforcement officer employed by the Department 
27 of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife or any bureau or division of the department: 
28 (3) Employee of the Office of the State Fire Marshal who has law enforcement 
29 powers pursuant to section 2396, subsection 7; 
30 (4) Judicial marshal;

marine patrol officer appointed by the Commissioner of Marine Resources; 
(6) Forest ranger employed by the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and

19 "Law enforcement officer" does not include a federal law enforcement officer or 
20 federal attorney prosecuting for the State. 
21 2. Provision of ballistic vests for law enforcement officers. A law enforcement 
22 officer employed by the State must be provided a well-fitting, contemporary ballistic vest. 
23 3. Provision of ballistic vests for dogs engaged in law enforcement. If the State 
24 uses a dog for the pumose of aiding in law enforcement, the dog must be provided a well-
25 fitting, contemporary ballistic vest. 
26 4. Funding. The State may accept public and private funds to implement the 
27 purposes of this chapter.
