§431.  Determination and date of primary; voter eligibility  17 
1.  Determination of primary.  Whenever the state committee of a party certifies 18 
that there is a contest among candidates for nomination as the presidential candidate of 19 
the party and that the committee has voted to conduct a presidential primary election, the 20 
State shall hold a presidential primary election.  21 
2.  Date of primary.    A pre sidential primary election held pursuant to subsection 1 22 
may not be held earlier than January 1st of the year in which the presidential election is 23 
held.  The date of the presidential primary election must be chosen in the following 24 
manner.  25 
A.  If certific ation is made pursuant to subsection 1 for only one party and that party 26 
chooses a date for the presidential primary election, the State shall hold the election 27 
on that date.  The party shall deliver to the Secretary of State notification of the 28 
chosen dat e by December 1st of the year prior to the presidential election year.  29 
B.  If certification is made pursuant to subsection 1 for more than one party and those 30 
parties agree by November 1st of the year prior to the presidential election year to 31 
one date, th e State shall hold the presidential primary election on that agreed -upon 32 
date.  33

§435.  Selection of delegates  13 
1.  Selection of delegates.  If a party chooses to participate in a presidential primary 14 
election under this subchapter, delegates to national presidential nominating conventions 15 
may be selected by the party meeting in convention under subchapter 1, article 3 at any 16 
time after the presidential primary election.  Selection of delegates must be in accordance 17 
with any reasonable procedures established at the state party convention.  18 
2.  Allocation of delegates.  In the absence of any procedures established by state or 19 
national party rules, the allocation of delegates must be as set out in this subsection.  20 
A.  Delegates to the national convention must be allocated in proportion with the 21 
candidate votes and the uncommitted votes cast in the presidential primary election of 22 
the party.  23 
(1)  A candidate receiving 10% or more of the total votes for candidates or 24 
uncommitted is allocated a share of delegates that approaches, as closely as 25 
possible , the respective share of the total vote.  26 
(2)  The percentages of votes attributable to candidates receiving less than 10% 27 
of the total vote must be added to the total percentage of uncommitted votes for 28 
the purpose of allocating delegates.  29 
B.  A delegate elected as an uncommitted delegate may support any presidential 30 
candidate at any time and may change support for this candidate in the delegate's sole 31 
discretion.  32 
C.  A delegate elected for a particular presidential candidate according to the 33 
proportional allocation specified by paragraph A shall vote for that candidate on the 34 
first ballot at the national presidential nominating convention, unless the state 35 
committee of the party affirmatively votes to follow another procedure or, prior to the 36 
filing of the  certification under section 431, subsection 1, the candidate for whom a 37 
particular delegate is elected specifically withdraws, as verified by the chair of the 38 
national party, from consideration for the presidential nomination at any time before 39 
the first ballot at the national presidential nominating convention.  40

 SUMMARY  1 
This bill amends the presidential nomination process in the State, to be in place for 2 
the 2020 presidential election.  The bill p rovides that, whenever the state committee of a 3 
party certifies that there is a contest among candidates for nomination as the presidential 4 
candidate of the party and that the committee has voted to conduct a presidential primary 5 
election, the State shall hold a presidential primary election.  Under the bill, only voters 6 
who are enrolled in the party may vote in that party's presidential primary election.  7 
Under the bill, delegates to the national convention must be allocated in proportion with 8 
the candidat e votes and the uncommitted votes cast in the presidential primary election of 9 
the party.  10
