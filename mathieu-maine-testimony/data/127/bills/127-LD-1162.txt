 §2660 -T.  Uniform testing recommendation; specified contaminants  1 
The department shall develop a uniform recommendation for the testing of specific 2 
contaminants that should be periodically t ested for in residential private drinking water 3 
wells.  The uniform recommendation must specify contaminants that should be included 4 
in the periodic testing, including but not limited to arsenic, uranium, manganese, fluoride 5 
and radon, unless the departmen t determines that testing for a contaminant listed in this 6 
section is not necessary based on previous test results or credible scientific evidence.  A 7 
person who recommends or provides testing of or provides education or advertisements 8 
related to testing o f a residential private drinking water well shall clearly identify the 9 
uniform testing recommendation developed by the department pursuant to this section.  10 
§2660 -U.  Fees  11 
The department shall establish a fee schedule in accordance with this section.  12 
1.  Water testing.  A person who tests water samples shall pay a fee not to exceed $5 13 
to the department for each test of a water sample from a residential private drinking water 14 
well for one or more of the contaminants specified in section 2660 -T.  The fees coll ected 15 
must be credited to the Private Well Safe Drinking Water Fund established in section 16 
2660 -X and used for the purpose of increasing testing of residential private drinking 17 
water wells.  If more than one test is conducted of a water sample from the sam e 18 
residential private drinking water well, the department shall waive payment of a fee 19 
established under this subsection for a one -year period.  20 
2.  Water treatment.  A person who sells and installs water treatment equipment to 21 
reduce the level of contamina nts specified in section 2660 -T in residential private 22 
drinking water wells shall pay a fee not to exceed 3% of the total cost of each installation.  23 
The fees collected must be credited to a separate account in a water treatment fund 24 
established by the Mai ne State Housing Authority pursuant to Title 30 -A, section 4741, 25 
subsection 19.  The fees may be used for the sole purpose of providing persons of low 26 
income with affordable water treatment for residential private drinking water wells that 27 
have exceeded he alth guidance levels for one or more of the contaminants specified 28 
pursuant to section 2660 -T. 29 
Fees established under this section are in addition to fees under section 2602 -A.  The 30 
department shall grant a waiver of the fee established under subsection 1 to a person who 31 
qualifies for a waiver of water testing fees pursuant to section 2602 -A. 32

SUMMARY  30 
This bill improves testing for and treatment of contaminants in residential private 31 
drinking water wells that are otherwise exem pt from state and federal safe drinking water 32 
laws.  33 
The bill requires the Department of Health and Human Services to develop a uniform 34 
testing recommendation for testing of residential private drinking water wells.  35 
The bill requires testing when residentia l private drinking water wells are 36 
constructed.  37 
 
 Page 5 - 127LR1294(01) -1 
 The bill establishes the Private Well Safe Drinking Water Fund to support 1 
educational outreach and to improve testing rates of residential private drinking water 2 
wells.  The fund is funded by fees on the test ing of residential private drinking water 3 
wells.  4 
The bill establishes a fund within the Maine State Housing Authority funded by fees 5 
on the installation of water treatment equipment for the purpose of providing persons of 6 
low income with affordable water t reatment.  7 
The bill requires the department to conduct educational outreach regarding the 8 
potential health effects of contaminants and the need for testing and treatment of 9 
residential private drinking water wells.  10
