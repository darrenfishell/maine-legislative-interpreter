SUMMARY  31 
This bill amends the Maine Administrative Procedure Act as follows:  32 
1.  It repeals a requirement that rules be approved for form and legality by the 33 
Attorney General and adds a requirement that rules be submitted to the Attorney General 34 
for advice as to form and legality;  35 
 
 Page 4 - 127LR1898(01) -1 
 2.  It changes a statutory provision, which previously provided that the Attorney 1 
General may not approve a rule if it is reasonably expected to result in a t aking of private 2 
property except under certain conditions, to provide that an agency may not adopt a rule 3 
if it is reasonably expected to result in such a taking;  4 
3.  It changes notice requirements, which previously provided that notice and copies 5 
of propo sed rules be provided by mail unless the requestor specified they be provided 6 
electronically, to provide that notice and copies of proposed rules be provided 7 
electronically unless the requestor specifies they be provided by mail;  8 
4.  It allows for electron ic submission of certain rule -making information;  9 
5.  It enacts a provision that allows an agency to choose to incorporate by reference 10 
subsequent amendments to a code, standard, rule or regulation; and  11 
6.  This bill also corrects cross -references.  12
