CHAPTER 43 3 
CALL CENTER RETENTION 4 
§3501.  Definitions 5 
As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 6 
have the following meanings. 7 
1.  Agency.  "Agency" means an executive branch agency of the State. 8 
2.  Call center.  "Call center" means a business enterprise that employs, for the 9 
purpose of customer service or back office operations: 10 
A.  Fifty or more employees, excluding part-time employees; or 11 
B.  Fifty or more employees who in the aggregate work at least 1,500 hours per week, 12 
excluding overtime hours. 13 
3.  Commissioner.  "Commissioner" means the Commissioner of Labor. 14 
4.  Employer.  "Employer" means a person, partnership, firm, association or 15 
corporation, public or private, that operates a call center. 16 
5.  Part-time employee.  "Part-time employee" means an employee who works an 17 
average of less than 20 hours per week or who has been employed for less than 6 months 18 
during the 12 months preceding the date on which notice is required pursuant to section 19 
3502, subsection 1. 20 
§3502.  Notice requirement; list of employers 21 
1.  Notice requirement.  An employer that intends to relocate a call center, or one or 22 
more facilities or operating units within a call center handling at least 30% of the call 23 
center's total call volume for the previous 12 months, from the State to a foreign country 24 
shall notify the commissioner at least 60 days before the relocation. 25 
2.  List.  The commissioner shall semiannually compile a list of all employers that 26 
have relocated a call center, or one or more facilities or operating units within a call 27 
center as described in subsection 1, since the compilation of any previous list.  The 28 
commissioner shall distribute the list to all agencies under the jurisdiction of the 29 
commissioner. 30 
3.  Penalty.  An employer that fails to notify the commissioner of the relocation of a 31 
call center at least 60 days before the relocation may be assessed a fine of $350 for each 32 
day the employer fails to notify the commissioner, except that the commissioner may 33 
reduce the amount for just cause shown. 34

 §3503.  Grants; guaranteed loans; tax benefits 1 
1.  Ineligibility.  Except as provided in subsection 3, and notwithstanding any other 2 
provision of law, an employer that appears on a list described in section 3502, subsection 3 
2 is ineligible for any direct or indirect state grant, state guaranteed loan or state tax 4 
benefit for 2 years after the date the list that the employer appears on is compiled. 5 
2.  Remit remaining amount.  Notwithstanding any other provision of law, an 6 
employer that appears on a list described in section 3502, subsection 2 shall remit the 7 
unamortized value of any state grant, state guaranteed loan or state tax benefit the 8 
employer previously received to the commissioner. 9 
3.  Exceptions.  The commissioner, in consultation with the appropriate agency, may 10 
waive the eligibility restriction under subsection 1 if the employer applying for a state 11 
grant, state guaranteed loan or state tax benefit demonstrates that the lack of the grant, 12 
loan or benefit would result in substantial job loss in the State or harm the environment. 13 
§3504.  In-state procurement 14 
An agency head shall ensure that all call center work contracted for by the agency is 15 
performed by contractors or their agents or subcontractors located entirely within the 16 
State. 17 
§3505.  State benefits for workers 18 
This chapter may not be construed to permit withholding or denial of payments, 19 
compensation or benefits under any other state law, including state unemployment 20 
compensation, disability payments and worker retraining and readjustment funds, from or 21 
to employees employed by employers that relocate to a foreign country. 22 
Sec. 2.  Contract with State.  An employer that on the effective date of this Act is 23 
under contract with the State to perform call center work and performs that work outside 24 
the State has 2 years to comply with the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 26, chapter 43, 25 
except that if the employer adds employees to perform call center work under the 26 
contract, the new employees must immediately be employed within the State. 27 
Sec. 3.  Effective date.  This Act takes effect 180 days after adjournment of the 28 
First Regular Session of the 127th Legislature. 29 
SUMMARY 30 
This bill requires the Commissioner of Labor to create a list of employers that have 31 
relocated a call center, or a facility or operating unit handling at least 30% of the call 32 
volume within a call center, from the State to a foreign country.  An employer appearing 33 
on the list is ineligible for a state grant, loan or tax benefit for 2 years.  The bill also 34 
requires the employer to pay back the unamortized value of a state grant, loan or tax 35 
benefit previously issued to the employer.  The bill requires that call center work for 36 
executive branch agencies of the State be performed in the State.  If the employer fails to 37
