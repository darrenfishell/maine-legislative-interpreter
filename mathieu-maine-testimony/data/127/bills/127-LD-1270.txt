SUMMARY  21 
This bill enacts a process for patient -directed care at the end of life for Maine 22 
residents who  are adults who are terminally ill and who have been determined to have a 23 
limited life expectancy.  The bill provides that such a patient has a right to information 24 
and includes requirements for patient and physician action and documentation in the 25 
patient 's medical records of the steps taken.  The bill authorizes a physician to prescribe a 26 
medication that the patient may self -administer for the purpose of hastening the patient's 27 
death.  The bill provides protections for the physician, the patient's health care facility and 28 
health care providers.  The bill protects the patient's life insurance and the health care 29 
providers' medical professional liability insurance.  The bill protects the patient's right to 30 
palliative care.  The bill requires rulemaking by th e Department of Health and Human 31 
Services to provide for safe disposal of medications that are prescribed for end -of-life 32 
care and that are not used by the patient.  The bill specifically states that nothing in the 33 
provisions of the bill may be construed t o authorize a physician or other person to end a 34 
patient's life by lethal injection, mercy killing or active euthanasia. The bill specifically 35 
states that the provisions of the bill may not be construed to conflict with Section 1553 of 36 
the federal Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act, as amended by the federal Health 37 
Care and Education Reconciliation Act of 2010.  38
