COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A " to H.P. 252, L.D. 365, Bill, "An Act To 
Provide a Tax Reduction for Modifications To Make a Home More Accessible for a 
Person with a Disability" 
Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and inserting the 
following: 
'Sec. 1. 36 MRS A §5219-NN is enacted to read: 
§5219-NN. Credit for certain homestead modifications 
1. Credit allowed. A person with federal adjusted gross income not exceeding 
$55,000 who makes qualified expenditures for the purpose of making all or any portion 
of an existing homestead, as defined in section 5219-II, subsection 1, paragraph C, 
accessible to an individual with a disability or physical hardship who resides or will 
reside in the homestead is allowed a credit against the tax otherwise imposed under this 
Part in an amount equal to the applicable percentage of the qualified expenditures or 
$9,000, whichever is less. 
2. Qualified expenditures. An individual claiming a credit under this section must 
demonstrate to the Maine State Housing Authority that the homestead modifications for 
which the expenditures were incurred comply with applicable building standards 
governing home accessibility in the jurisdiction where the homestead is located and are 
consistent with standards adopted by the authority. The authority may adopt rules 
consistent with this section to identify the types of homestead modifications that will 
enable accessibility for individuals with disabilities or physical hardships. Rules adopted 
under this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, 
subchapter 2-A. 
3. Certification. The Maine State Housing Authority shall certify to the State Tax 
Assessor the total qualified expenditures made by an individual seeking to claim a credit 
under this section. The authority may contract with a public or private entity to make the 
certification required under this subseCtion.

taxable year in which the qualified expenditures were incurred, Any unused portion of

4 years,

means: 
A. For taxpayers with a federal adjusted gross income of $0 to $25,000, lOO%; 
B. For taxpayers with a federal adjusted gross income over $25,000 but not over 
$30,000, 90%; 
C. For taxpayers with a federal adjusted gross income over $30,000 but not over 
$35,000, 80%; 
D, For taxpayers with a federal adjusted gross income over $35,000 but not over 
$40,000,70%; 
E, For taxpayers with a federal adjusted gross income over $40,000 but not over

F. For taxpayers with a federal adjusted gross income over $45,000 but not over

6. Annual limit on credits. Credits approved under this section may not exceed 
$1,000,000 for any calendar year. If the $1,000,000 annual limitation is reached, any 
additional applications for a credit in that year must be held and given priority in 
consideration in the following calendar year.

This legislation creates a tax credit for expenses incurred for certain home modifications to make a homestead 
accessible to an individual with a disability or physical hardship and would result in a reduction in General Fund 
revenue of $71 ,250 in fiscal year 2016-17 and a reduction in Local Government Fund revenue of $3,750 in fiscal year 
2016-17. The bill includes a one-time General Fund appropriation to the Department of Administrative and Financial 
Services of $22,000 in fiscal year 2016-17 for computer programming charges. 
This bill also includes General Fund appropriations of $50,000 per year beginning in fiscal year 2015-16 to a newly 
created Home Modification Certification program within the Maine State Housing Authority for the cost of making 
the required certification.
