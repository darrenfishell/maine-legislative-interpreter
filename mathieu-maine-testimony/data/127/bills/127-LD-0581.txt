 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 1 
become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and  2 
Whereas, many municipalities in the State have areas within their corporate limits 3 
that can be clas sified as underutilized and have a significantly lower property value; and  4

Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:  12 
Sec. 1.  30-A MRSA §5222, sub -§1-B is enacted to read:  13 
1-B.  Blighted area.  "Blighted area" means an area within the corporate  limits of a 14 
municipality that has been designated by the municipality's comprehensive plan as being 15 
underutilized and within which property values on an equal acreage basis are at least 75% 16 
below the surrounding areas due to the condition of the propertie s located in that area.  17 
Sec. 2.  30-A MRSA §5222, sub -§3-A is enacted to read:  18 
3-A.  Credit enhancement agreement.  "Credit enhancement agreement" means an 19 
agreement to rebate a portion of a tax increment to a property owner within a 20 
development district.  21 
Sec. 3.  30-A MRSA §5224 -A is enacted to read:  22 
§5224 -A.  Use of credit enhancement agreements  23 
1.  Use of credit enhancement agreements.  Credit enhancement agreements may 24 
be used to assist businesses locating in blighted areas when the number of persons 25 
employed full -time in any such business increases by at least 50% and the increased 26 
employment results from positions that are created by the business in the State.  Rebates 27 
may not be paid pursuant to a credit enhancement agreement until the business certif ies to 28 
the municipality that the 50% increase has occurred.  Rebates may not be paid 29 
retroactively.  30 
2.  Businesses without nonmanagement employees.  If a business locating in a 31 
blighted area has no nonmanagement employees, a rebate may not be paid pursuant  to a 32 
credit enhancement agreement until the business creates at least 10 full -time positions per 33 
$1,000,000 of the increased assessed value.  The business may not count for the purposes 34 
of certification under subsection 1 any employees moved from another employment 35 
location of the same business that is located within 50 miles of the development district in 36 
order to qualify for a rebate.  37

SUMMARY  16 
This bill defines the te rms "blighted area" and "credit enhancement agreement" in the 17 
municipal development district laws and provides a mechanism for the use of credit 18 
enhancement agreements in blighted areas.  It also requires greater advance notice of 19 
public hearings on develo pment districts and broadens the range of parties entitled to 20 
receive direct notice of such hearings.  21
