replaced statue and its relocation to the State, must be funded entirely by private 25 
donations.  26

 SUMMARY  1 
This bill is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Educatio n and Cultural 2 
Affairs pursuant to Resolve 2015, chapter 51, section 2.  As required by the resolve, the 3 
Maine Arts Commission, the Maine Historic Preservation Commission and the Maine 4 
State Museum Commission submitted to the committee their study and reco mmendations 5 
regarding the replacement of statues provided by the State to the National Statuary Hall 6 
Collection in the United States Capitol.  This bill reflects the recommendations of the 7 
study.  8 
The committee has not taken a position on the substance of t he study plan or this bill, 9 
and by reporting this bill out the committee is not suggesting and does not intend to 10 
suggest that it agrees or disagrees with any aspect of the study plan or this bill.  The 11 
committee is reporting the bill out for the sole purp ose of turning the study plan into a 12 
printed bill that can be referred to the committee for an appropriate public hearing and 13 
subsequent processing in the normal course.  The committee is taking this action to 14 
ensure clarity and transparency in the legisla tive review of the commissions' proposal.  15 
This bill provides that the Maine Arts Commission, the Maine Historic Preservation 16 
Commission and the Maine State Museum Commission are responsible for the placement 17 
and replacement of statues representing Maine in  the National Statuary Hall Collection in 18 
the United States Capitol.  A statue must meet all applicable federal requirements; 19 
replacement of a statue must be funded from private sources; and a replaced statue must 20 
be returned to Maine, where it must be dis played in public view.  The bill requires a 21 
statue placed in the National Statuary Hall Collection to represent Maine as demonstrated 22 
by the results of a survey of public opinion.  This bill also directs the Maine Arts 23 
Commission, the Maine Historic Preser vation Commission and the Maine State Museum 24 
Commission to replace the statue of Hannibal Hamlin currently in the National Statuary 25 
Hall Collection with a statue of Margaret Chase Smith by March 15, 2020.  26
