SUMMARY  8 
This bill assists Maine residents to obtain highly skilled, high -demand, livable -wage 9 
jobs by creating a new jobs training program and improving and funding existing jobs 10 
training programs.  11 
1.  Part A establishes the Put ME to Work Program in th e Maine Quality Centers to 12 
facilitate the establishment of job training programs at postsecondary institutions of 13 
higher education in this State by working with private businesses and postsecondary 14 
institutions of higher education to provide training to pr epare workers for jobs in high - 15 
demand fields.  In order to participate in the program, a business or group of businesses is 16 
required to provide at least 50% of the cost of the program, which may be through in -kind 17 
contributions.  Part A provides $250,000 f or needs -based tuition assistance, scholarships 18 
and grants for students in the program and $650,000 to match funding or in -kind 19 
contributions by businesses participating in the program.  20 
Part A also provides $100,000 to the Industry Partnership Assistance C ollaborative to 21 
fund a position and associated costs within the collaborative.  22 
2.  Part B allows employers to make voluntary donations to the Competitive Skills 23 
Scholarship Fund.  These donations would be in addition to the required employer 24 
contributions and would be used for the Competitive Skills Scholarship Program.  25
