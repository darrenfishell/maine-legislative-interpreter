SUMMARY  24 
This bill is presented pursuant to Joint Order 2016, S.P. 630, which authorizes the 25 
Joint Standing Committee on Appropriations and Financial Affairs to report  out a bill 26 
regarding tax conformity including funding.  It provides conformity with the United 27 
States Internal Revenue Code of 1986 but only for tax years beginning in 2015.  28 
Part A of this bill:  29 
1.  Decouples the Maine individual and corporate income taxe s from the federal 30 
bonus depreciation deductions for taxable years beginning on or after January 1, 2015.  In 31 
addition, it provides a Maine capital investment credit for taxable years beginning in 2015 32 
with respect to depreciable property placed in service  in Maine.  The credit is equal to 9% 33 
of the amount of the net increase in depreciation attributable to the depreciation deduction 34 
claimed by the taxpayer under the Code, Section 168(k) with respect to property placed in 35 
service in the State during the tax able year for taxable corporations; for individuals the 36 
credit is 8% of such amount for tax years beginning in 2015;  37 
 
 Page 11 - 127LR2662(01) -1 
 2.  Decouples Maine’s individual and corporate income tax from the Code, Section 1 
179 expensing deductions for tax years beginning on or aft er January 1, 2016.  It enacts 2 
addition and subtraction modifications to reverse any increase allowed at the federal level 3 
for the first year the property is placed in service, and then to allow depreciation 4 
deductions for the remainder of the asset’s life ; 5 
3.  Decouples Maine’s individual income tax from the teacher expense deduction 6 
under the Code, Section 62(a)(2)(D) by requiring an addition modification in the amount 7 
of the federal deduction;  8 
4.  Disallows for Maine itemized deduction purposes the feder al deduction for 9 
mortgage insurance premiums treated as qualified residence interest; and  10 
5.  For taxable years beginning after 2017, decouples the Maine earned income tax 11 
credit from the increased federal 45% earned income tax credit rate for taxpayers wi th 3 12 
or more qualifying children and from the higher phase -out thresholds for married 13 
individuals filing joint returns.  14 
Part B transfers $9,535,933 from the Tax Relief Fund for Maine Residents to the 15 
unappropriated surplus of the General Fund by the end of  fiscal year 2015 -16. 16 
Part C deappropriates funds no longer needed for debt service costs.  17 
Part D requires the State Controller to transfer the first $153,447 of unexpended 18 
Personal Services savings that would otherwise lapse to the General Fund Salary Pl an 19 
program to the General Fund unappropriated surplus at the close of fiscal year 2015 -16.  20 
This Part also allows the State Controller to transfer funding from the General Fund 21 
Salary Plan program to the General Fund unappropriated surplus in the event tha t the full 22 
$153,447 of Personal Services savings in this Part is not achieved.  23 
Part E requires the State Controller to transfer the first $1,034,880 of unexpended 24 
Personal Services savings that would otherwise lapse to the General Fund Salary Plan 25 
program to the General Fund unappropriated surplus at the close of fiscal year 2016 -17.  26 
This Part also allows the State Controller to transfer funding from the General Fund 27

Fiscal Detail and Notes127th MAINE LEGISLATURE
An Act To Provide for Tax Conformity and Funding Methods
Fiscal Note for Original Bill
Committee: Appropriations and Financial Affairs
Fiscal Note Required: Yes
Parts D and E lapses $153,447 in fiscal year 2015-16 and $1,034,880 in fiscal year 2016-17 from Personal Services 
appropriations that would otherwise lapse to the Salary Plan account in the Department of Administrative and Financial Services.Part B of the bill transfers $9,535,933 from the Tax Relief Fund for Maine Residents to the unappropriated surplus of 
the General Fund in fiscal year 2015-16.
Part C deappropriates $6,113,120 from the Office of the Treasurer of State in fiscal year 2015-16 to reflect savings in 
debt service.This legislation has no net General Fund cost in fiscal year 2015-16 and fiscal year 2016-17. It proposes to conform 
to certain federal tax laws for tax year 2015 only and would result in a loss of General Fund revenue of $15,802,500 in fiscal year 2015-16 and $1,034,880 in fiscal year 2016-17. It would also result in a reduction in transfers to the Local Government Fund of $322,500 in fiscal year 2015-16 and $21,120 in fiscal year 2016-17.Fiscal Note
