2.  Program established.  The Farmland Restoration Program is established within 11 
and is administered by the department.  The program provides approved applicants with 12 
assistance in improving lands for increasing food and fiber producti on agriculture, 13 
improving soil quality and strengthening soil conservation and watershed protection.  14 
The program provides payments of up to 50% of the cost or $20,000, whichever is less, 15 
of an approved restoration project under subsection 5.  For projects involving state -owned 16 
or municipality -owned farmland under long -term agricultural leases of 5 years or more, 17 
the total program payment may be up to 90% of the restoration costs, up to $20,000.  18 
3.  Participation; application; review panel.  Individuals must  submit an 19 
application to the department to be eligible for participation in the program, pursuant to 20 
procedures developed by the department.  Applications must include a detailed 21 
conservation plan as described under subsection 4.  An approved application is required 22 
prior to funding approval.  23 
The department shall establish a review panel to evaluate and approve applications for 24 
participation in the program based on the 3 equal objectives of increasing food and fiber 25 
production agriculture, improving soil q uality and strengthening soil conservation and 26 
strengthening watershed protection.  The panel may further evaluate and approve 27 
applications and proposed conservation plans based upon other lesser criteria developed 28 
by the department.  The panel must notify  all applicants of their application status in a 29 
timely manner.  30 
4.  Conservation plan required.  An application for the program must include a 31 
detailed conservation plan.  An applicant that chooses not to prepare a plan in 32 
consultation with the United Stat es Department of Agriculture, Natural Resources 33 
Conservation Service must provide a plan developed in consultation with the applicant's 34 
local soil and water conservation district in order to be considered for funding under the 35 
program.   The conservation p lan must at a minimum comply with the latest best 36 
management practices.  In scoring plans, the panel will award higher scoring to 37

This bill establishes the Farmland Restoration Program, establishes a framework for 6 
the program, establishes the Farmland Restoration Fund and gives the Department of 7 
Agriculture, Conservatio n and Forestry authority to adopt rules to implement the 8
