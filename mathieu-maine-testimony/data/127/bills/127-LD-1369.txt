SUMMARY  20 
This bill repeals the section of law that provides for an importation permit for wildlife 21 
and exotic species and amends the section of law regardin g permits for the possession of 22 
wildlife in captivity to include importation.  It provides for an application fee and amends 23 
the permit fees.  This bill also requires the Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife 24 
to grant permits to introduce, import, transport, receive or possess fish or gametes and to 25 
maintain a list of unregulated fish and wildlife species that is available to the public.  The 26 
Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife is also required to amend its rules to 27 
maintain updated inspecti on provisions for applicants attempting to acquire a permit to 28 
possess or introduce, import and possess fish or wildlife in captivity, to maintain a fee 29 
structure to establish fees for inspection provisions for regulated species, to provide a 30 
process that allows authorized independent contractors to meet with permit applicants to 31 
educate applicants on minimum standard facility requirements and to inspect current 32 
facilities to recommend approval or denial of a permit and to charge a responsible party 33 
for the  cost incurred to remove or euthanize unpermitted regulated fish or wildlife 34 
species.  The bill also adds educational purposes to the purposes for which the 35 
commissioner may issue permits to hunt, trap, possess, band and transport wild animals 36 
and wild bir ds; current law provides that such permits may be issued for scientific 37 
purposes.  38
