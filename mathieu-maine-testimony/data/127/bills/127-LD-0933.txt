SUMMARY  19 
This bill implements the recommendations of the Task Force To End Student Hunger 20 
in Maine as follows.  21 
1.  The bill establishes the Commission  To End Student Hunger, an ongoing 22 
commission of 17 appointed members, which is directed to work to implement a 5 -year 23 
plan to end student hunger, is authorized to conduct a statewide summit of state leaders to 24 
end student hunger and is required to advise 4 hunger coordinators who will assist in 25 
implementing the 5 -year plan.  26 
2.  The bill provides that the full costs of school nutrition are subsidizable costs in the 27 
state education funding formula.  28 
3.  The bill directs the Department of Education and the Dep artment of Health and 29 
Human Services to collaborate to reduce student hunger. The bill requires the 2 30 
departments to meet quarterly to collaborate on child hunger and nutrition programs.  The 31 
bill requires the 2 departments to provide quarterly reports to the joint standing 32 
committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over education matters and to the 33 
Commission To End Student Hunger.  The bill requires the 2 departments to expand 34 
access to departmental data and to provide each school administrative uni t and school 35 
with analyses of its existing child nutrition programs and their funding and federal 36 
funding not being used.  The bill requires child nutrition data to be publicly available on 37 
the websites of the Department of Education and the Department of Health and Human 38 
Services.  The bill requires the Department of Education to establish a grant program to 39 
assist schools and school administrative units in ending student hunger.  40

 4.  The bill requires the Department of Education to convene a working group by 1 
March 1, 2015 to review the opportunities and challenges of the community eligibility 2 
provision administered by the United States Department of Agriculture and study best 3 
practices and barriers related to the community eligibility provision and make 4 
recommendations to the Department of Education and the Department of Health and 5 
Human Services.  The bill requires the Department of Education to submit a report to the 6 
Legislature and the Governor by April 30, 2015.  7 
5.  The bill requires the Department of Ed ucation and the Department of Health and 8 
Human Services to encourage the congressional delegation of the State to make 9
