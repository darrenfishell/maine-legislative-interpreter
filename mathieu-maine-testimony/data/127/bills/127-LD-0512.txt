SUMMARY  20 
This bill implements the following Criminal Law Advisory  Commission 21 
recommendations respecting the Maine Criminal Code.  22 
1.  The bill elevates to Class A crimes in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 17 -A, 23 
section 208 those forms of bodily injury that result in serious permanent physical damage 24 
to a victim, while retaining as Class B crimes other forms of bodily injury.  25 
2.  The bill amends the definition of the term "public way" in Title 17 -A, section 505 26 
to cover public ways, including sidewalks, over which the public has a right to pass by 27 
foot and vehicle.  28 
3.  T he bill amends Title 17 -A, section 554 regarding endangering the welfare of a 29 
child by removing the requirement that the child victim be under 16 years of age and by 30 
adding the designation of the class of each crime to the appropriate paragraph.  31 
4.  The bi ll amends Title 17 -A, section 1201 to specify that when a court sentences a 32 
person to a term of imprisonment for a sex offense followed by a period of supervised 33 
release, as authorized by Title 17 -A, chapter 50, that person is not eligible for the 34 
impositi on of a sentence alternative that includes a period of probation.  35 
5.  The bill repeals Title 17 -A, section 1206, subsection 7 -B regarding probation 36 
revocation hearings.  37
