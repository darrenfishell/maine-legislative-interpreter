 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 1 
become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and  2 
Whereas, the economic cost of obesity in the United States has been calculated to 3 
amount to as much  as $190,000,000,000 annually; and  4 
Whereas, the National Institute for Children's Health Quality reports that almost 5 
30% of children in the State are either overweight or obese; and  6 
Whereas, the Department of Health and Human Services and the task force 7 
proposed by this resolve must begin their work as soon as possible in order to recommend 8 
legislation to establish and support obesity prevention and treatment programs; and  9 
Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 10 
the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 11 
immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 12 
therefore, be it  13 
Sec. 1.  Commissioner of Health and Human Services to convene task 14 
force.  Resolved:  That the Commissioner of Health and Human Services shall convene 15 
a task force to study the epidemic of childhood obesity in Maine.  In addition to the 16 
commissioner's designees from the Department of Health and Human Services and 17 
representat ives from the Maine Center for Disease Control and Prevention, the 18 
commissioner shall invite the participation of interested stakeholders to serve as members 19 
of the task force, including but not limited to families that have been affected by obesity, 20 
repre sentatives of the Maine Chapter of the American Academy of Pediatrics and 21 
representatives of the Maine Academy of Family Physicians; and be it further  22 
Sec. 2.  Task force; duties.  Resolved:  That the task force convened under 23 
section 1 shall evaluate the epidemic of childhood obesity in the State.  The evaluation 24 
conducted by the task force must include, but is not limited to:  25 
1.  Delineating the scope of childhood obesity in the State;  26 
2.  Identifying groups that may be at particularly high risk for obesi ty; 27 
3.  Assessing the availability of prevention and treatment programs; and  28 
4.  A review of strategies proposed or adopted in other states and jurisdictions; and be 29 
it further  30 
Sec. 3.  Task force; report.  Resolved:  That the Commissioner of Health and 31 
Human Services shall report the findings and conclusions of the task force, including any 32 
proposed legislation to address the task force's recommendations, to the Joint Standing 33 
Committee on Health and Human Services by December 6, 2017.  The joint standing 34 
committee is authorized to submit a bill related to the report to the Second Regular 35 
Session of the 128th Legislature.  36
