2.  Program created.   The Maine Paid Family Leave Insurance Program is created 13 
within the Depart ment of Labor.  The program provides wage -replacement benefits to 14 
eligible persons who are on family medical leave from employment.  15 
3.  Eligibility.   To be eligible for benefits under the program, a person must be 16 
employed and must have had earnings from e mployment and made contributions to the 17 
program in at least 12 of the prior 52 weeks.  The person must file a written notice with 18 
the person's employer at least 30 days prior to commencement of the family medical 19 
leave.  Failure by the person to file the w ritten notice may result in delay or reduction in 20 
the benefits, except in the event the time of the leave is unforeseeable or changes due to 21 
unforeseeable circumstances.  22 
4.  Benefits provided.   An eligible person under this subchapter receives a weekly 23 
benefit of 2/3 of that person's average weekly wage in the 2 quarters in the preceding 52 24 
weeks in which the person's wage was highest, except that the maximum amount of the 25 
weekly benefit is 100% of the state average weekly wage.  Benefits are provided for u p to 26 
6 weeks in any 12 -month period.  Benefits must be reduced by any available workers' 27 
compensation benefits and sick pay. Benefits may not be reduced by other disability 28 
benefits, pension payments or other earnings.  The waiting period before benefits b egin is 29 
7 days, except that a benefit payment for the first 7 days must be made retroactively if the 30 
family medical leave lasts 3 weeks or more.  31 
5.  Fund created.   The Paid Family Leave Insurance Fund is created to collect 32 
contributions and fund benefits u nder this subchapter.  The fund may be used to pay 33 
administrative costs of the program and to pay benefits.  The fund does not lapse but must 34 
be carried forward to implement this subchapter.  35

SUMMARY  29 
This bill creates the Maine Paid Family Leave Insurance Program to provide wage - 30 
replacement bene fits to persons who qualify for family medical leave.  The program is 31 
funded by employee contributions and provides 2/3 of a person's average weekly wage or 32 
100% of the state average weekly wage, whichever is lower, for up to 6 weeks in any 12 - 33 
month period .  Employee contributions are collected on a sliding scale based on wages.  34
