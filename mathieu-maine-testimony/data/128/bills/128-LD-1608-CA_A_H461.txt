3-A. Credit history inquiries; prohibition. The State may not seek to obtain or 
inquire about the credit history of a prospective employee or require that a prospective 
employee provide a credit history as a condition of state employment, except when: 
A. The position of employment involves a fiduciary responsibility to the State, 
including, but not limited to, the authority to manage accounts, issue payments, 
collect debts, transfer money or enter into contracts, when the amount of money or 
value of assets involved is $10,000 or more; 
B. The position of employment is a law enforcement position; or 
C. The State is otherwise required by federal law to obtain a credit hist01y. 
For the purposes of this subsection, "state employment" means employment in the 
legislative, executive or judicial branch of State Government. State employment does not 
include employment in a quasi-independent state entity or public instrumentality of the 
State or in a school administrative unit, municipality, county or other political subdivision 
of the State.' 
SUMMARY 
This amendment is the majority repott of the committee. The amendment provides 3 
exceptions from the provision in the bill that prohibits the State from seeking to obtain or 
inquire about the credit history of a prospective employee or require that a prospective 
employee provide a credit history as a condition of employment when: the position 
involves a :fiduciary responsibility to the State, the position is a law enforcement position 
or the State is otherwise required by federal law to obtain a credit hist01y. The
