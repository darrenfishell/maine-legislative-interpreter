SUMMARY  16 
This bill requires a law enforcement agency that commits a prisoner to the sheriff's 17 
charge prior to arraignment on a nonviolent Class D or Class E crime to reimburse th e 18 
sheriff for each day that the prisoner is held by the sheriff prior to arraignment and to pay 19 
a one -time surcharge of $50 for holding the prisoner. The bill defines "nonviolent Class D 20 
or Class E crime" to mean a Class D or Class E crime other than a vio lation of the Maine 21 
Revised Statutes, Title 17 -A, chapter 9, 11, 12 or 13; a violation of Title 17 -A, section 22 
506-B; a violation of Title 17 -A, section 1002 or 1004; or a crime involving domestic 23 
violence as defined in Title 15, section 1003, subsection 3 -A.  The bill requires that the 24 
per diem rate for the prisoner is the same per diem rate that is established by the 25 
Department of Corrections for the purposes of funding county jail operations.   26
