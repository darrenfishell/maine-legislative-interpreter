18 SUMMARY 
19 This amendment does the following: 
20 1. It clarifies the scope of the bill to ensure that local food ordinances must relate to 
21 direct producer-to-consumer transactions involving food or food products intended for 
22 human consumption. It also provides definitions of "direct producer-to-consumer 
23 transaction" and "food or food products"; 
24 2. It clarifies that the state laws in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 7 and Title 22 
25 that regulate direct producer-to-consumer transactions involving food or food products 
26 are the state laws whose application such local ordinances may affect; and 
27 3. It makes other clarifying amendments to the bill and adds a provision authorizing 
28 the Joint Standing Committee on Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to report out a 
29 bill to the Second Regular Session of the 128th Legislature relating to the food 
30 sovereignty law.

Fiscal Note 
Clarifying language avoids federal action 
Fiscal Detail and Notes 
This bill provides clarifying language that will help the State avoid any potential fiscal impacts, resulting from 
current law, of federal termination of the State's implementation of the meat and poultry inspection program and 
transference of such inspection to the USDA-Food Safety and Inspection Service.
