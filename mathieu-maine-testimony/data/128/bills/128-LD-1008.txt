§25001.  Commission established  15 
The Permanent Commi ssion on the Status of Racial and Ethnic Populations 16 
established by section 12004 -I, subsection 74 -J and referred to in this chapter as "the 17 
commission," is an independent commission.  The commission shall promote, carry out 18 
and coordinate programs designe d to improve opportunities for racial and ethnic 19 
populations in the State.  20 
§25002.  Membership  21 
The commission consists of 17 members, including 7 appointed by the Governor, 5 22 
appointed by the President of the Senate and 5 appointed by the Speaker of the Ho use of 23 
Representatives.  In making these appointments, the Governor, the President of the 24 
Senate and the Speaker of the House of Representatives shall consider and appoint 25 
residents of the State who have a knowledge of problems facing racial and ethnic 26 
populations in the State, who have experience in advocacy relating to racial and ethnic 27 
populations' issues and who provide leadership in programs or activities that improve 28 
opportunities for racial and ethnic populations.  The members of the commission must be 29 
chosen from throughout the State, and the majority of members must be members of 30 
racial and ethnic populations.  A member of the Legislature may not be appointed to the 31 
commission.  The Governor shall appoint one member representing each of the 32 
followin g:  the elderly, low -income people, persons with disabilities, youth, victims of 33 
domestic violence and federally recognized Indian tribes in the State.  34 Racial and Ethnic

 §25003.  Term of office  1 
Members of the commission are appointed for 2 -year terms.  A member may serve 2 
multiple terms.  3 
Members of the commission may serve after the expiration of their terms until their 4 
successors have taken office.  The Governor, the President of the Senate and the Speaker 5 
of the House may terminate the membership of their respective appoint ees for good 6 
cause.  The reason for the termination must be communicated in writing to a member 7 
whose membership is terminated.  The membership of any member of the commission 8 
must be terminated if the member is absent from 3 consecutive meetings without 9 
communicating good cause to the chair of the commission.  10 
§25004.  Vacancies  11 
A member appointed to fill a vacancy occurring prior to the expiration of the term for 12 
which the member's predecessor was appointed serves only for the remainder of that term 13 
and mu st be appointed by the same appointing authority.  Any vacancy on the 14 
commission does not affect its powers.  15 
§25005.  Chair  16 
The Governor shall select a chair from among the members of the commission.  The 17 
chair is authorized to appoint subcommittees.  18 
§2500 6.  Powers and duties  19 
The commission shall advise and consult with the Governor and the Legislature 20 
about, and assist them in improving, opportunities for racial and ethnic populations in the 21 
State by:  22 
1.  Research.   Carrying out research programs necessar y to determine the status of 23 
racial and ethnic populations in the State;  24 
2.  Activities.   Promoting and coordinating activities on state and local levels 25 
designed to meet the problems of racial and ethnic populations in the State;  26 
3.  Advocate.   Serving as  an advocate for racial and ethnic populations in making 27 
recommendations on proposed budgetary, legislative and policy actions to the Governor, 28 
the Legislature and other officials of the State and the Federal Government with respect 29 
to state and federal po licies, programs and other activities affecting or relating to racial 30 
and ethnic populations in the State;  31 
4.  Information.   Informing the public about the presence or absence of opportunities 32 
for racial and ethnic populations in the State;  33 
5.  Meetings.   Conducting public hearings, conferences, workshops and other such 34 
meetings to obtain information about, discuss and publicize the needs of and solutions to 35 
the problems of racial and ethnic populations; and  36
