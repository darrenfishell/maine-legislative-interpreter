SUMMARY  33 
This bill amends the laws governing the cultivation, possession and use of medical 34 
marijuana.  It:  35 
1.  Amends definitions to add terms and to expand upon or provide clarity for 36 
existing terms;  37 
2.  Amend s the qualifying condition of intractable pain to include pain that a medical 38 
provider determines is not managed effectively by prescription narcotics and allows a 39 
medical provider the discretion to issue a written certification for any medical condition 40 
that the physician believes may be alleviated by the patient's using marijuana for medical 41 
use.  It also requires consultation with a minimum of 3 medical professionals, one of 42 
 
 Page 27 - 128LR1833(01) -1 
 whom may be selected by the petitioner, prior to accepting or denying a petition  to add a 1 
debilitating medical condition as a qualifying condition;  2 
3.  Replaces the limit of 2 1/2 ounces of marijuana that may be dispensed to a 3 
qualifying patient who is a Maine resident during a 15 -day period with a limit of no more 4 
than 2 pounds in on e transfer;  5 
4.  Allows a qualifying patient who is cultivating marijuana to furnish seeds and 6 
plants to another qualifying patient;  7 
5.  Permits a qualifying patient to designate more than one primary caregiver to assist 8 
the patient; the additional primary caregivers may not cultivate marijuana for the patient;  9 
6.  Prohibits a visiting qualifying patient, who is not a resident of Maine, from 10 
cultivating marijuana;  11 
7.  Permits a primary caregiver designated to cultivate marijuana to furnish seeds and 12 
plants t o an authorized person;  13 
8.  Increases the number of employees that a registered cultivating primary caregiver 14 
may employ. A primary caregiver designated to cultivate can employ one person for each 15 
registry identification card the caregiver is issued;  16 
9.  P ermits a primary caregiver designated to cultivate marijuana to dispose of 17 
marijuana by transferring the marijuana to a designated primary caregiver; current law 18 
allows the transfer to a dispensary;  19 
10.  Allows for certain authorized transfers of marijuana  by a primary caregiver 20 
designated to cultivate marijuana for reasonable compensation;  21

members of the primary caregiver’s household or family to not register with the 23 
departme nt; 24

to 6 mature marijuana plants per registry identification card.  The maximum number of 26 
plants allowed for cultivation is the same as in current law;  27 
13.  Removes the l imit of 2 1/2 ounces of prepared marijuana and establishes the 28 
allowable amount of harvested marijuana to be up to 8 pounds that may be possessed by a 29 
patient or authorized person on behalf of a patient.  It also establishes the allowable 30 
amount of marijua na to be up to 8 pounds per registry identification card for a designated 31 
primary caregiver required to register, no more than 8 pounds per patient, up to 2 patients, 32
