SUMMARY  6 
This bill allows the Adjutant General to provide to or accept military forces from 7 
other states in support of federally funded National Guard missions.  It provides the 8 
Adjutant General the authority to appoint a provost mar shal to oversee security at 9 
National Guard military facilities and certain real property.  It allows the Military Bureau 10 
to rent its readiness centers and other real property in addition to armories and allows the 11 
Adjutant General to waive fees for certain  rentals.  It changes the description of a 12 
stepchild who is eligible to receive certain educational benefits.  It prohibits a veteran 13 
service officer from knowingly presenting or prosecuting a fraudulent or frivolous claim.  14
