COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "B" to H.P. 916, L.D. 1322, Bill, "An Act 
Regarding Mental Health First Aid Training for Corrections Personnel" 
Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and before the 
summary and inserting the following: 
'Sec. 1. Report. The Maine Criminal Justice Academy shall report to the joint 
standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over criminal justice and public 
safety matters by January 1, 2018, and every 4 months thereafter until January 1, 2022, 
on mental health first aid training that -has been provided to corrections officers. The 
report must provide data on the hours of mental health first aid training provided to 
corrections officers across the State by the Maine Criminal Justice Academy as basic 
training or ongoing training and by the law enforcement agencies that employ the 
corrections officers as part of ongoing training provided as a requirement of employment.' 
SUMMARY 
This amendment is the minority report of the committee. The amendment requires 
that the Maine Criminal Justice Academy periodically report on the hours of mental 
health first aid training provided to corrections officers across the State by the Maine 
Criminal Justice Academy as basic training and as ongoing training and by the law 
enforcement agencies that employ the corrections officers as part of ongoing training 
provided as a requirement of employment.
