ยง1826 -P.  Definitions  5 
As used in this subchapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following 6 
terms have the following meanings.  7 
1.  Bureau.  "Bureau" means the Bureau of General Services within the Department 8 
of Administrative and Financial Services.  9 
2.  Class A apprenticeship program.  "Class A apprenticeship program" means an 10 
apprenticeship prog ram that is registered with and approved by the United States 11 
Department of Labor or a state apprenticeship agency as defined in Title 26, section 3201, 12 
subsection 22 and has graduated apprentices to journeyman status for at least 3 of the past 13 
5 years.  14 
3.  Construction project.  "Construction project" or "project" means a publicly 15 
funded project to build, construct or complete major alteration or repair of buildings or 16 
public works that is funded with state funds in excess of $50,000 or receives other 17 
financial assistance from the State with a value in excess of $50,000.  18 
4.  Contractor.  "Contractor" means a developer, construction manager, prime 19 
contractor, subcontractor or any other entity that performs work on a construction project 20 
or submits a bid to p erform such work.  21 
5.  Craft worker. "Craft worker" is a person who possesses experience and 22 
proficiency in a trade involved in building construction.  23 
6.  Prime contractor.  "Prime contractor" is a contractor that has entered into a 24 
contract with the State for a construction project and that is responsible for completion of 25 
the project even if that prime contractor employs one or more subcontractors to carry out 26 
portions of the project.  27

SUMMARY  32 
The purpose of this bill is to ensure that the work on public construction and 33 
maintenanc e contracts is performed by responsible, qualified firms that maintain the 34 
capacity, expertise, personnel and other qualifications and resources necessary to 35 
successfully perform public contracts in a timely, reliable and cost -effective manner.  36 
This bill establishes responsible contractor requirements for publicly funded construction 37

 projects that receive state funds in excess of $50,000 or other financial assistance from 1 
the State with a value in excess of $50,000.  The bill outlines a responsible contrac tor 2 
certification process to be administered by the Department of Administrative and 3 
Financial Services, Bureau of General Services.  4
