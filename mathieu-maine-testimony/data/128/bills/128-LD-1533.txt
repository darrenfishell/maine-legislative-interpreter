SUMMARY  30 
This bill:  31 
1.  Replaces the term "golf club" with "golf course" to eliminate the confusion in 32 
Title 28 -A between golf clubs and other types of clubs;  33 
2.  Eliminates a threshold amount for a single retail purchase of liqu or; 34 
3.  Clarifies which type of establishments on the same premises as an off -premises 35 
retailer must serve full course meals or food;  36 
 
 Page 8 - 128LR0718(01) -1 
 4.  Removes the requirement that a business prior to being considered for an agency 1 
liquor store license must have sold ma lt liquor and wine for one year and have been 2 
violation free for that same period;  3 
5.  Amends language that establishes a minimum inventory value of spirits based on 4 
the population threshold established in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 28 -A, section 5 
461; 6 
6.  Removes language providing an exception for businesses with more than 5 7 
locations in the State that are sold to a company that has sold malt liquor or wine in 8 
another state;  9 
7.  Eliminates the fee for an application for a relocation of an agency liq uor store 10 
within the same municipality and adds a requirement that the application for relocation 11 
include proof that all existing agency liquor stores in the same municipality were notified 12 
of the relocation prior to approval by that municipality;  13 
8.  Repe als the requirement that the Department of Administrative and Financial 14 
Services, Bureau of Alcoholic Beverages and Lottery Operations hold a public hearing on 15 
the relocation application.  The only approval needed is from the municipality on the 16 
relocation  request by the agency liquor store;  17 
9.  Adds railroads to the list of public service corporation licensees in order to align 18 
with the definition of "public service corporation";  19 
10.  Clarifies that identification issued by a federally recognized Indian tr ibe in the 20 
State is an acceptable form of identification for the purposes for purchasing liquor;  21 
11.  Amends the provision of law requiring that any malt liquor served from a faucet, 22 
spigot or dispensing apparatus must be identified by trade name to includ e all types of 23 
liquor;  24 
12.  Reduces the number of gallons that constitute a keg;  25 
13.  Establishes that mobile service bars used on golf courses may serve all types of 26 
liquor; and  27 
14.  Corrects provisions of law to refer to the director of the bureau instea d of the 28 
Commissioner of Administrative and Financial Services. These changes were 29 
inadvertently left out of Public Law 2013, chapter 368, Part V and Public Law 2013, 30 
chapter 476, Part A.  The bill also provides that the director or the director's designee  may 31 
approve an alcohol server education course that meets specific criteria.  32
