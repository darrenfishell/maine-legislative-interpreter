SUMMARY 
This amendment changes the agency responsible for the administration of the Maine 
Medical Use of Marijuana Act program from the Department of Administrative and 
Financial Services to the Department of Health and Human Services. This amendment 
also allows naturopathic doctors to provide written certifications for the medical use of 
marijuana. 
This amendment also corrects cross-references and terms in the Maine Medical Use 
of Marijuana Act to conform to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 28-B, Adult Use 
Marijuana, as enacted in Public Law 2017, chapter 409, including providing for the 
change in the terms 11pdmary caregiver" and "registered primary caregiver" to "caregiver" 
and "registered caregiver, 11 respectively.

This amendment would repeal sections of P .L. 2017, Chapter 409 that transferred the administration and enforcement 
of the Maine Medical Use of Marijuana Act from the Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS) to the 
Department of Administrative and Financial Services (OAFS). The amendment includes net deallocations to OAFS 
of $1,085,931 and 6.25 positions beginning in fiscal year 2018-19 for the transfer from the Medical Use of Marijuana 
Fund within OAFS back to the Medical Use of Marijuana Fund within DHHS. 
The amendment includes net allocations to DHHS of $1,056,295 beginning in fiscal 2018-19 to repeal the transfer of 
the Medical Use of Marijuana Fund within the Department of Health and Human Services to the Medical Use of 
Marijuana Fund within the Department of Administrative and Financial Services. Additionally, $140,751 is 
deappropriated from the Maternal and Child Health Block Grant Match program in DHHS and $29,636 is deallocated 
to the Maine Center for Disease Control and Prevention to eliminate the creation of 1.2 Public Service Cool'dinator II 
positions and 0.5 Office Assistant II positions, since the positions they were created to replace will no longer be 
moving with the Medical Use of Marijuana Fund to OAFS.
