§9401.  Definitions  26 
As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 27 
have the following meanings.  28 
1.  Broadband service.  "Broadband service" means service of high -speed Internet 29 
access capability in excess of 144 kilobits per second both to and from a computer or 30 
network.  31 
2.  Capital costs.  "Capital costs" means all costs of providing a service that are 32 
capitalized in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles.  33

 3.  Direct costs.  "Direct costs" means those ex penses of a municipality that are 1 
directly attributable to providing broadband service and would be eliminated if broadband 2 
service was not provided by the municipality.  3 
4.  Feasibility consultant.  "Feasibility consultant" means a person with expertise in  4 
the processes and economics of providing broadband service.  5 
5.  Full-cost accounting.  "Full -cost accounting" means the accounting of all costs 6 
incurred by a municipality in providing broadband service, including all capital costs, 7 
direct costs and indire ct costs.  8 
6.  Indirect costs.  "Indirect costs" means any costs identified with 2 or more services 9 
or other functions that are not directly identified with a single service or function.  10 
"Indirect costs" includes the costs of administration, accounting, pe rsonnel, purchasing, 11 
legal support and other staff or departmental support.  12 
7.  Private provider.  "Private provider" means a private entity that provides 13 
broadband service.  14 
8.  Provide.  "Provide" means, as it relates to a municipality and broadband servi ce, 15 
to provide or offer broadband service directly or indirectly, including through an 16 
authority or instrumentality acting on behalf of the municipality or for the benefit of the 17 
municipality, through a partnership or joint venture or by contract, resale o r otherwise.  18 
9.  Subscriber.  "Subscriber" means a person that lawfully receives broadband 19 
service from another person that provides broadband service.  20 
§9402.  Scope of chapter  21 
Nothing in this chapter applies to a municipality purchasing, leasing, construc ting or 22 
equipping facilities that are designed to provide services within the municipality and that 23 
the municipality uses for internal municipal government purposes or, by written contract, 24 
leases, sells capacity in or grants other similar rights to a priv ate provider to use the 25 
facilities in connection with the private provider's offering broadband service.  26 
§9403.  Municipal limitations on providing broadband service  27 
Except as provided in this chapter, a municipality may not provide broadband service 28 
to a subscriber or, for the purpose of providing broadband service to a subscriber, 29 
purchase, lease, construct, maintain or operate a facility that is designed to provide 30 
broadband service.  A municipality may not offer to provide or provide broadband service 31 
to a subscriber that does not reside within the geographic boundaries of the municipality.  32 
§9404.  Antitrust immunity  33 
Notwithstanding Title 10, section 1104, subsection 4, a municipality that provides 34 
broadband service is not exempt from the recovery of dam ages, interest on damages, 35 
costs and attorney's fees for antitrust violations in providing broadband service.  A 36 
municipality that provides broadband service is subject to applicable antitrust liabilities 37

SUMMARY  6 
This bill creates requirements that a municipality must meet in order to provide 7 
broadband service to its residents.  It requires the completion of a feasibility study and 8 
requires the municipality to hold a referendum to decide whether the municipality may 9 
offer broadband service.  It includes general operation limitations, including tho se related 10 
to the establishment of rates, the use of subsidization and the use of eminent domain.  It 11 
requires the adoption of ordinances relating to service quality and enforcement by a 12 
municipality that elects to provide broadband service.  It also remov es antitrust liability 13 
protections from a municipality as those protections relate to the municipality's offering 14 
broadband service.  15 
The bill also requires that in order to receive any grants from the ConnectME 16 
Authority, the municipality must show evidenc e of compliance with the requirements of 17 
the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 35 -A, chapter 94.  18
