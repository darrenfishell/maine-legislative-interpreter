25 SUMMARY 
26 This amendment, which is the minority report, strikes the bill and replaces it with a 
27 resolve. The amendment requires the Secretary of State to request a waiver of the 
28 requirements of the federal REAL ID Act of 2005 from the United States Department of 
29 Homeland Security pending the results of federal legislation. The amendment requires 
30 the Secretary of State to establish a process to provide a passport card or passport book to 
3 1 residents of Maine who do not have the required photographic identification to access 
32 federal or federally controlled facilities or to board commercial aircraft and demonstrate 
33 significant financial hardship, making the fee for a passport card or passport book 
34 cost-prohibitive. The amendment also requires the Secretary of State to administer an 
35 educational campaign about the requirements of the federal REAL ID Act of 2005 and 
36 the availability of the United States Department of State passport cards and passport 
3 7 books as an option to access federal facilities and commercial aircraft and the opportunity 
38 for Maine residents who require financial assistance to apply for a state-funded passport 
39 card or passport book. The funds for these purposes are in an amount approximately
