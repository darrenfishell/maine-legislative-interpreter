4 1.  Definition.  For the purposes of this section,  "consumer-directed attendant 
5 services" means  services that are reimbursable under the MaineCare  program  and provide 
6 assistance with the activities of daily living in the home or community for a MaineCare 
7 member who directly employs  and trains the attendant,  supervises the provision of 
8 reimbursable services  and,  if necessary, terminates the  services of  the attendant.
9 2.  Relatives or legal  guardians as attendants.  A MaineCare member approved  for 
10 consumer-directed attendant  services  under the MaineCare program may employ  a family  
11 member, including  a spouse,  parent, stepparent, adult child  and other legally responsible  
12 relative, or the legal guardian of the member as an attendant to be reimbursed by the 
13 MaineCare program.
14 3.  Rulemaking.  The department shall  adopt rules to implement this section.  Rules 
15 adopted pursuant  to this subsection are routine  technical rules  as defined in Title 5, 
16 chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
17 Sec. 2.  Waiver  request.   No later than January 1, 2019,  the Department of Health 
18 and Human  Services shall submit a waiver  request to the United States  Department of 
19 Health and Human Services,  Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services that requests  
20 approval for Medicaid reimbursement for employment of a relative or legal guardian 
21 providing consumer-directed attendant services  to a MaineCare member receiving those 
22 services under rule Chapter 101:  MaineCare  Benefits  Manual, Chapter II, Section 12.  
23 The department shall  take all reasonable and necessary  steps to seek approval of the 
24 waiver.
25 Sec. 3.  Rulemaking.   Within 180 days of receiving  approval for coverage  by the 
26 United States  Department  of Health  and Human  Services, Centers  for Medicare  and 
27 Medicaid Services pursuant to a waiver  request in accordance with this Act, the 
28 Department of Health  and Human Services  shall adopt rules to implement the Maine  
29 Revised Statutes,  Title 22, section 3174-AAA.  Rules adopted  pursuant to this section  are 
30 routine technical rules  as defined  in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
31 Sec. 4.  Contingent effective date.   That section of this Act that enacts the Maine 
32 Revised Statutes, Title 22, section  3174-AAA  takes effect only if the United States 
33 Department of Health and Human Services, Centers  for Medicare and Medicaid  Services  
34 approves a waiver request submitted in accordance with this Act.  Upon approval  of the 
35 waiver request, the Commissioner of Health and Human Services shall  notify the 
36 Secretary of State, the Secretary  of the Senate, the Clerk  of the House of Representatives

2 This bill allows a MaineCare member  approved for consumer-directed attendant 
3 services under the MaineCare program to hire any family member, including  a spouse, or 
4 a legal  guardian to provide those  services in the home or community.  It requires  the 
5 Department of Health and Human Services to submit a waiver  request to the United  
6 States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for Medicare and Medicaid  
7 Services no later than January 1, 2019.  The department is required to adopt  rules within
