24 SUMMARY 
25 This amendment is the minority report of the committee. The amendment clarifies 
26 that the alternative therapies to be evaluated must include physical therapy, acupuncture 
27 services, chiropractic services and other services provided by licensed complementary 
28 health care providers. The amendment also changes the date the report is required to be 
29 submitted and the reference to the appropriate session of the Legislature.

Fiscal Detail and Notes 
This bill requires the Bureau ofinsurance to convene a stakeholders group to identify the best practices for evidence­
based alternative therapy for the treatment of opioid and heroin addiction. The bureau has indicated it has no expertise 
in this area and would most likely hire a qualified consultant. No estimate of the cost involved is made at this time.
