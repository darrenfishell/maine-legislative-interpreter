Sec. 1.  9-A MRSA §2 -311 is enacted to read:  2 
§2-311.  Small loan requirements  3 
A lender providing a small loan must comply with the following requirements. For 4 
purposes of this section, "small lo an" means a loan in an amount of $2,000 or less.  5 
1.  The term for a small loan must be at least 50 days for a loan amount of $1,000 or 6 
less and at least 6 months for a loan amount greater than $1,000 and less than or equal to 7 
$2,000.  8 
2.  The interval betwe en payments for a small loan may be agreed upon by the 9 
parties, except that intervals between payments must be substantially equal with 10 
reasonable variance for the first payment and nonbusiness days.  11 
3.  Installment payments for a small loan must be substa ntially equal and fully 12 
amortizing over the contracted term.  For closed -end credit, the contracted term may not 13 
exceed 24 months.  For a line of credit, the minimum payments due must be substantially 14 
equal and fully amortizing based on a period not to exc eed 12 months.  For any new 15 
draws under the line of credit, a lender shall recalculate installment payments for a period 16 
not to exceed 12 months.  17 
4.  The amount of a borrower's payments coming due within a calendar month with 18 
respect to a small loan may no t exceed 10% of the borrower's gross monthly income as 19 
evidenced by the borrower's pay stub or other equivalent proof of income.  20 
5.  A borrower may cancel a small loan and avoid the payment of finance charges by 21 
returning the amount financed to the lender providing the small loan by the end of the 22 
next business day after the origination of the small loan.  23 
6.  A lender providing a small loan may not take personal or real property as 24 
collateral from the borrower as security for payment of the small loan.  25 
7.  A lender providing a small loan may not charge any fees related to insurance in 26 
connection with the origination of the small loan.  27

Sec. 4.  9-A MRSA §2 -403 is enacted to read:  14 
§2-403.  Finance charge for small loans  15 
A supervised lend er offering a small loan in compliance with the requirements under 16 
section 2 -311 may charge the following fees in addition to the interest rate allowed under 17 
sections 2 -401 and 2 -402: 18 
1.  An origination fee not to exceed 10% of the principal amount. The or igination fee 19 
may be considered a prepaid finance charge and is not subject to refund upon prepayment 20 
in full; and  21 
2.  A monthly maintenance fee not to exceed 15% of the average principal loan 22 
amount outstanding for that month.  The monthly maintenance fee  is earned and may be 23 
charged for each month the loan is outstanding.  24 
SUMMARY  25 
This bill amends the Maine Consumer Credit Code.  It enacts requirements for loans 26 
of $2,000 or less and it authorizes consumer loan lenders and open -end credit creditors to 27 
charge origination fees and monthly maintenance fees.  28
