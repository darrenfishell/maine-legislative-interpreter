6 SUMMARY 
7 This amendment: 
8 1. Changes the date in the bill that a detailed forest inventory of the State's public 
9 reserved lands and nonreserved public lands must be conducted by from March 15, 2018 
10 to March 15, 2021 and keeps the requirement that an inventory must be done every 5 
11 years thereafter; 
12 2. Reenacts a provision of law regarding expenditures from the Public Reserved 
13 Lands Management Fund in the statute governing revenue from public reserved lands that 
14 states that money may not be expended without allocation by the Legislature. The 
15 allocations must be approved by the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
16 jurisdiction over appropriations and financial affairs; 
17 3. Adds a list and description of roads built and roads maintained in the preceding 
18 fiscal year and a list and description of roads to be built and roads to be maintained in the 
19 succeeding fiscal year to the items that must be included in the annual report dealing with 
20 public reserved lands submitted by the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and 
21 Forestry, Bureau of Parks and Lands; 
22 4. Adds forestry to the programs eligible to receive funding under the educational 
23 grant program established in the bill; 
24 5. Adds career and technical education programs to the types of programs that are 
25 eligible to apply for an educational grant related to logging and forestry; 
26 6. Removes language specifying that educational grants are only one-time grants, 
27 specifies that those grants may be given to an approved eligible educational program if 
28 funds are available and provides that the total distributions from the Public Reserved 
29 Lands Management Fund under the grant program may not exceed $300,000 in the state 
30 budget biennium; and 
31 7. Requires the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
32 public reserved lands matters, after public input, to rank the statewide priority list of 
33 recreational infrastructure projects for the State's public reserved lands and the statewide 
34 priority list of projects under the federal Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 for the 
35 State's public reserved lands submitted by the Depaitment of Agriculture, Conservation 
36 and Forestry, Bureau of Parks and Lands and to make recommendations, if funding is 
37 available, to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
38 appropriations and financial affairs for funding projects.

The bill requires the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry (ACF) to conduct a detailed forest 
inventory of the State's public reserved lands and public nonreserved lands by March 15, 2021 and every 5 years 
thereafter, to provide educational grants to certain educational programs related to logging and to develop a statewide 
priority list of projects under the federal Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 for the State's public reserved lands. 
In fiscal year 2017-18 the bill allocates Other Special Revenue Funds to the Public Reserved Lands Management Fund 
within ACF of $150,000 for educational grants, $50,000 for conducting recreational infrastructure projects related to 
the federal Ame1icans with Disabilities Act of 1990 and $3,250 for other related costs. In fiscal year 2018-19 the bill 
allocates Other Special Revenue Funds to the Public Reserved Lands Management Fund within ACF of $65,000 for 
the forest invent01y and $150,000 for educational grants. The Public Reserved Lands Management Fund has sufficient 
resources to cover these expenditures.
