The foll owing provisions govern the rate modification applicable to small business 15

1.  Rate review.  The superintendent shall develop and implement a process of 17 
heightened scrutiny review for rates applicable to small business employers.  This review 18 
must include, at a minimum, a comparison of the risk to insurers applicable to this class 19 
of employers and the workers' compensation insurance costs to such employers.  The rate 20 
review required by this subsection must be conducted periodically, but no less t han 21 
annually.  22 
2.  Modification factor; rate adjustment.  Based on the information gathered 23 
through the rate review conducted pursuant to subsection 1, and taking into account the 24 
standards in section 2382, the superintendent shall develop a modification fa ctor 25 
applicable to small business employers for the purpose of adjusting rates applicable to 26 
such employers downward to better match the relative risk to insurers to the relative cost 27 
burden of workers' compensation insurance costs to such employers.  The superintendent 28 
shall ensure that all rates of insurers applicable to a small business employer include the 29 
appropriate modification factor determined according to this section.  30 
3.  Annual report.  The superintendent and the Workers' Compensation Board shal l 31 
review claims applicable to small business employers and compare the claims data to the 32

SUMMARY  8 
This bill charges the Superintendent of Insurance with developing a modification 9 
factor to reduce the workers' compensation insurance rates for employers with fewer than 10 
50 employees based on an asse ssment of the relative risk to the insurer and the relative 11 
cost burden of the insurance on such small business employers.  The small business 12 
employer modification factor applies to workers' compensation insurance policies issued 13 
or renewed on or after Ja nuary 1, 2018.  14
