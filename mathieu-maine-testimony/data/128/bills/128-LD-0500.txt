SUMMARY  32 
This bill provides for the formation of transportation corridor districts within the 33 
current law relating to transit districts and regional transportation corporations.  The bill 34 
provides t hat a municipality may, by itself or in cooperation with one or more other 35 
municipalities, form a transportation corridor district for the purposes of providing an 36 
environment to fund public transportation and accessibility needs, including passenger 37 
rail, ferry, bus, bicycle and pedestrian facilities and routes, and promoting economic 38 
development at transportation station areas and in downtown areas.  39 
The bill requires a municipality or group of municipalities to select the borders of the 40 
transportation cor ridor district.  The bill requires that the formation of a transportation 41

The bill authorizes a district's board of directors, with approval from all municipalities in  2

participating in the district, may borrow money temporarily and issue its negotiable  notes 5 
for that money and issue securities of the district.  6
