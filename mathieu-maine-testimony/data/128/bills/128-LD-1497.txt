SUMMARY  14 
This bill replaces the Hooked on Fishing Not on Drugs program with youth and 15 
family outdoor recreational programs and activities to encourage hunting and fishing 16 
activities as well as shooting sports .  It amends language regarding certain licenses to 17 
provide that certain permits are included in the licenses.  It allows the spouse and children 18 
of a member of the Armed Forces of the United States on active duty who is permanently 19 
stationed outside of th e State to purchase trapping licenses at a reduced rate.  It simplifies 20 
language regarding a person 70 years of age or older hunting with a crossbow.  It repeals 21 
a provision allowing a person under 16 years of age holding a valid junior hunting license 22 
to obtain a muzzle -loading permit from the Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and 23 
Wildlife.  It adds a wild turkey hunting permit to the nonresident small game 24 
apprenticeship hunter license.  It repeals law establishing a youth bear hunting day.  It 25 
changes the  law regarding unlawful possession of wild turkeys to reflect the increase of 26 
the bag limit from one to 2.  It prohibits placing bait for wild turkeys and hunting over 27 
that bait for 30 days prior to the spring wild turkey hunting season.  It adds firearms using 28 
a caliber cartridge smaller than the .22 caliber cartridge to the exceptions from the law 29 
prohibiting hunting with any automatic firearm.  It amends the provision of a residents - 30 
only day in the law regarding open and closed seasons for deer to accoun t for an 31 
exception for certain nonresident landowners.  It repeals a provision making nonresident 32 
aliens ineligible to purchase a trapping license, as they are eligible to purchase a license 33 
to trap beaver when their state or province of residency allows r esidents of the State to 34 
trap beaver in that state or province and removes language requiring clerks or agents 35 
appointed by the Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife to charge $2 for each 36 
trapping license issued.  It raises the fee for a resident a nd nonresident apprentice trapper 37 
license by $1, consistent with changes to fees in Public Law 2015, chapter 245.  It makes 38 
it a Class E crime for a licensee to receive, possess for resale, sell or offer to sell gift 39 
baitfish or gift smelts.  It amends the  law describing how the Open Water and Ice Fishing 40 
Regulations are distributed.  It updates the name of an endangered species of bird.  It 41 
changes the expiration date of a taxidermy license so that licenses expire 3 years from 42 
 
 Page 10 - 128LR0688(01) -1 
 their date of issuance.  It c orrects a cross -reference.  It allows ATVs, including 2 -wheel 1 
off-road motorcycles, without headlights or taillights to be operated between sunrise and 2 
sunset.  3
