1.  Definitions.   As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 4 
following terms have the fol lowing meanings.  5 
A.  "Coal tar" means a viscous substance obtained by the destructive distillation of 6

milligrams per kilogram.  "Coal tar" includes, but is not limited to, r efined coal tar, 8 
high temperature coal tar and coal tar pitch.  9 
B.  "Coal tar sealant product" means a surface -applied sealant product that contains 10 
coal tar or coal tar pitch volatiles.  11 
C.  "Polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons" means a group of compounds that  are 12 
by-products of incomplete combustion that includes several carcinogens and, when in 13 
high quantities, are characterized as toxic hazardous waste.  14 
2.  Sale of coal tar sealant products prohibited.   Beginning October 1, 2019, a 15 
person may not sell at who lesale or retail a coal tar sealant product that is labeled as 16 
containing coal tar.  17 
3.  Application of coal tar sealant products prohibited.   Beginning October 1, 18 
2020, a person may not apply a coal tar sealant product on a driveway or parking area.  19 
4.  Exemptions.   A person may request an exemption from the prohibitions in 20 
subsections 2 and 3 by submitting a written request to the commissioner.  The request 21 
must include the reason an exemption is needed.  The commissioner may exempt a 22 
person from the prohi bitions in subsections 2 and 3 if the commissioner determines that  23 
the person is researching the effects of a coal tar sealant product on the environment or 24 
the person is developing an alternative technology and the use of a coal tar sealant 25 
product is re quired for research or development.  26 
5.  Penalties.   A person who violates the provisions of this section is subject to 27 
penalties under section 349.  28 
SUMMARY  29 
This bill prohibits the sale of coal tar sealant products beginning October 1, 2019 and 30 
prohibits th e application of coal tar sealant products on driveways or parking areas 31 
beginning October 1, 2020.  The Commissioner of Environmental Protection may grant 32 
an exemption from the prohibitions for research or development purposes.  33
