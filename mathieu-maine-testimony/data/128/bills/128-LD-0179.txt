 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:  1 
Sec. 1.  25 MRSA §3801, sub -§§1 and 2, as enacted by PL 1997, c. 426, §1, are 2 
amended to read:  3 
1.  Creating police standoff.   A person commits the civil violation  is guilty  of 4 
creating a police standoff if that person:  5 
A.  Is in fact barricaded as a result of the person's own actions;  6 
B.  Is or claims to be armed with a dangerous weapon; and 7 
C.  Is instructed by a law enforcement agency, either personally, electronically or in 8 
writing, to leave t he barricaded structure; and  surrender.  9 
D.  Fails in fact to leave the barricaded structure within 1/2 hour of receiving the 10 
instruction from a law enforcement agency.  11 
2.  Class E crime.   Creating a police standoff is a civil violation  Class E crime .  The 12 
court may order a person who creates a police standoff to make restitution to each agency 13 
that responded to the standoff.  Restitution must equal the direct costs incurred in 14 
responding to the standoff or $500, whichever is greater.  If any portion of the restitution 15 
remains after each agency has been paid its costs, the remainder must be divided equally 16 
among the agencies.  17 
SUMMARY  18 
This bill changes the offense of creating a police standoff from a civil violation to a 19 
Class E crime.  This bill also removes from the provision governing police standoffs the 20 
ability of a person to avoid the offense of creating a police standoff by surrendering 21 
within 1/2 hour of receiving police instruction to do so.  22
