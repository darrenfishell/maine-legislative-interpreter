16 'Sec. 1. 17-A MRSA §1107-A, sub-§6 is enacted to read: 
17 6. It is an affirmative defense to prosecution under this section that the evidence of 
18 possession was obtained as a result of the person: 
19 A. Seeking, in good faith. medical assistance for someone experiencing a drug-
20 related overdose; or 
21 B. Seeking or obtaining medical assistance for a drug-related overdose the person is 
22 experiencing. 
23 The assertion of an affirmative defense under this subsection is not grounds for 
24 suppression of evidence with respect to any crime.' 
25 SUMMARY 
26 This amendment is the majority report of the committee. The amendment replaces 
27 the bill. The amendment provides an affirmative defense in a prosecution for unlawful 
28 possession of scheduled drugs that the evidence of possession was obtained as a result of 
29 the person seeking, in good faith, medical assistance for someone experiencing a drug-
30 related overdose or seeking or obtaining medical assistance for a drug-related overdose 
31 the person is experiencing. Unlike the bill, the amendment provides that the assertion of 
32 this affmnative defense is not grounds for suppression of evidence with respect to any 
33 crime.
