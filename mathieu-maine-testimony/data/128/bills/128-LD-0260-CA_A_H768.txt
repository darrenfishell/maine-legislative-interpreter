11 SUMMARY 
12 This amendment does the following. 
13 1. It specifies that the Commissioner of the Maine Energy Office instead of the 
14 Director of the Govemor's Energy Office serves on the Efficiency Maine Trust Board. 
15 2. It specifies that the deputy commissioner serves at the pleasure of the 
16 commissioner and not of the Governor as in the bill. 
17 3. It removes the $300,000 annual funding for the Maine Energy Office from the 
18 Efficiency Maine Trust, but includes from current law that the office may receive funds 
19 from the Efficiency Maine Trust for office activities that reasonably relate to programs or 
20 activities of the Efficiency Maine Trust. 
21 4. It removes unnecessary language regarding energy infrastructure corridors. 
22 5. It adds an appropriations and allocations section. 
23 6. It adds an effective date of January 1, 2019.

This bill establishes the Maine Energy Office (MEO) and transfers the staff, powers, duties and functions of the 
Governor's Energy Office (GEO) to it on Januaiy 1, 2019. The GEO is eliminated and the bill includes Federal 
Expenditures Fund and Other Special Revenue Funds deallocations to the GEO and corresponding allocations to the 
MEO. The bill also includes General Fund appropriations to the IVIEO of $75,000 in fiscal year 2018-19 for activities 
relating to energy resources, planning and development. The Maine Energy Resources Development Program is 
established with a $500 allocation to promote energy research and demonstration activities as future funding allows. 
The MEO may receive funds from the Efficiency Maine Trust (EMT) for office activities that are reasonably related 
to programs or activities of the EMT.
