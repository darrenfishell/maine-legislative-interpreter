SUMMARY  5 
This bill does the following.  6 
1. It gives the Public Utilities C ommission authority to direct investor -owned 7 
transmission and distribution utilities to enter into long -term contracts for regional 8 
procurement of capacity resources, energy or renewable energy credits.  9 
2. It requires the Public Utilities Commission to iss ue an annual report on the 10 
procurement of capacity resources, energy and renewable energy credits in the preceding 11 
12 months with a plan for the succeeding 12 months that is consistent with the State’s 12 
goals for greenhouse gas reduction.  13 
3. It requires cer tain rules adopted under the expedited permitting of grid -scale wind 14 
energy development provisions to be major substantive rules.  15 
4.  It allows a property owner to request that the owner's property be included as an 16 
expedited permitting area for grid -scale  wind energy development.  17 
5.  It requires rules in determining the effects of grid -scale wind energy development 18 
on scenic character and related existing uses to be major substantive rules.  19 
6.  It directs the Department of Environmental Protection to conve ne a stakeholder 20 
group regarding the effect of expedited wind energy on scenic character and existing 21 
uses, which may issue a report of its findings, including recommended legislation, to the 22 
Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and Technology.  23
