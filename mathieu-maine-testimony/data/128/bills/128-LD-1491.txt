SUMMARY  36 
This bill amends the laws regarding the sale and distribution of marijuana for adult 37 
use and medical purposes as follows.  38 
 
 Page 10 - 128LR1291(01) -1 
 1.  It transfers the state licensing authority from the Departme nt of Agriculture, 1 
Conservation and Forestry to the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, 2 
Bureau of Alcoholic Beverages and Lottery Operations.  3 
2.  It allows for the limited sale of marijuana for adult use while the bureau begins the 4 
rule-making process and issuing licenses for retail marijuana establishments pursuant to 5 
those rules by allowing dispensaries registered under the Maine Medical Use of 6 
Marijuana Act to sell limited marijuana retail products to persons 21 years of age or older 7 
in accordance with certain conditions. These early sales provisions are repealed 8 
December 31, 2018 unless the bureau has not, as of December 31, 2018, begun issuing 9 
licenses for retail marijuana establishments, in which case the dispensaries can continue 10 
to sell limited marijuana retail products until the bureau begins, through final agency 11 
action, to issue licenses for retail marijuana establishments. It allows registered 12 
dispensaries to purchase marijuana and marijuana products from registered primary 13 
caregi vers for purposes of distribution to persons 21 years of age and older.  It imposes a 14 
tax of 10% of the sale price of limited marijuana retail products.  It allocates 10% of the 15 
tax revenue to the municipality where the tax -paying dispensary is located, 45 % of the 16 
tax revenue to the Department of Health and Human Services to fund regulatory 17 
oversight and enforcement of sales of limited marijuana retail products as well as efforts 18 
by the department to deter use of marijuana by persons under 21 years of age a nd the 19 
remaining 45% to the bureau to fund administration, regulatory development and 20 
enforcement of the Marijuana Legalization Act.  It also imposes state sales tax on limited 21 
marijuana retail products.  22 
3.  It imposes additional financial qualifications o n applicants for retail marijuana 23 
establishments and retail marijuana social clubs to ensure future regulatory compliance.  24 
4.  It imposes annual financial audit responsibilities on all license holders.  25 
5.  It removes the requirement that a dispensary under  the Maine Medical Use of 26 
Marijuana Act be incorporated under the Maine Nonprofit Corporation Act and operated 27 
on a not -for-profit basis and exempts such reorganizations from the requirement to obtain 28 
court approval or provide notice.  29 
6.  It imposes Maine state residency requirements on all applicants.  It also allows for 30 
the transfer of a retail marijuana establishment or social club license to a person or entity 31 
that does not meet the residency requirement and allows for investment in a retail 32 
marijuana e stablishment or social club license by a person or entity that does not meet the 33 
residency requirement once the retail marijuana establishment or social club licensee has 34 
been operational for 2 years.  35 
7.  It allows for an entity or individual with a minori ty ownership interest in a retail 36 
marijuana testing facility to also have an ownership interest in a retail marijuana 37 
establishment or retail marijuana social club to encourage investment in retail marijuana 38 
testing facilities.  It prohibits testing facili ties from testing retail marijuana and retail 39 
marijuana products from or intended to be sold at a medical dispensary, by a caregiver or 40 
at a retail marijuana establishment or social club in which the retail marijuana testing 41 
facility licensee has any finan cial interest.  42
