 §1367.  Tastings at farmers' markets  1

may conduct taste -testing events at farmers' markets.  For purposes of this section, 4

2.  Conditions on taste -testing activities.  The following conditions apply to 6 
taste-testing activi ties allowed under this section.  7 
A.  An individual at a taste -testing activity may not be charged a fee.  8 
B.  An individual at a taste -testing activity may not be served a taste -testing sample 9 
of more that 4 ounces of malt liquor, 1 1/2 ounces of wine or 1/ 2 ounce of spirits.  An 10 
individual is limited to 6 samples per day per manufacturer licensed under section 11 
1355 -A. 12 
C.  Malt liquor, wine or spirits for taste testing may not be poured in advance and 13 
made available for individuals participation in the taste  testing to serve themselves.  14 
D.  An individual at a taste -testing activity who is visibly intoxicated may not be 15 
served malt liquor, wine or spirits.  16 
E.  Taste -testing activities must be conducted within the hours of retail sales 17 
established in this Title . 18 
F.  Taste -testing activities are not allowed in any municipality where on -premises and 19 
off-premises sales are not allowed pursuant to chapter 5.  20 
G.  A licensee under section 1355 -A conducting a taste -testing activity at a farmers' 21 
market must keep a reco rd of when and where the activity took place.  A licensee is 22 
limited to 6 taste -testing events per calendar year at farmers' markets.  23 
SUMMARY  24 
This bill allows the sale of wine, spirits and malt liquor manufactured in Maine for 25 
off-premises consumption at t aste-testing events and the sale of spirits manufactured in 26 
Maine at farmers' markets.  Under current law, the sale of wine and malt liquor at 27 
farmers' markets is allowed.  The bill also allows taste testings to be conducted at farmers' 28 
markets.  29
