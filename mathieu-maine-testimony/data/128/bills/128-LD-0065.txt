Sec. 5.  26 MRSA  c. 43 is enacted to read:  3 
CHAPTER 43  4 
RIGHT TO REFRAIN FRO M JOINING A UNION  5 
§3501.  Definitions  6 
As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 7 
have the following meanings.  8 
1.  Employer.  "Employer" means a person, firm , association, corporation, public 9 
employer, public school employer, public college, public university, public institution or 10 
public education agency.  11 
2.  Labor organization.  "Labor organization" means an organization, agency or 12 
employee representation co mmittee or union that exists for the purpose, in whole or in 13 
part, of negotiating or bargaining with employers on behalf of employees concerning 14 
wages, rates of pay, hours of work, other conditions of employment or other forms of 15 
compensation.  16 
§3502.  Righ t to refrain  17 
Notwithstanding any law to the contrary, a person may not be required, as a condition 18 
of employment or continuation of employment, to:  19 
1.  Member.  Become or remain a member of a labor organization;  20 
2.  Dues.  Pay any dues, fees, assessments o r other similar charges, however 21 
denominated, of any kind or amount to a labor organization; or  22 
3.  Payment to 3rd party.  Pay to a charity or other 3rd party, in lieu of payments 23 
specified in subsection 2, any amount equivalent to or a pro rata portion of  dues, fees, 24 
assessments or other charges required of members of a labor organization.  25 
§3503.  Agreements in violation  26 
An agreement, understanding or practice, written or oral, implied or expressed, 27 
between a labor organization and an employer that violate s a provision of this chapter is 28 
unlawful, void and of no legal effect.  29 
§3504.  Penalty  30 
A person who directly or indirectly violates any provision of this chapter commits a 31 
Class D crime.  32

 §3505.  Injunctive relief  1 
A person injured as a result of another pe rson's violation or threatened violation of a 2 
provision of this chapter is entitled to injunctive relief against the person violating or 3 
threatening to violate this chapter.  4 
§3506.  Damages  5 
A person injured as a result of a violation or threatened violatio n of a provision of 6 
this chapter may recover all damages, including court costs and reasonable attorney's 7 
fees, resulting from the violation or threatened violation. A recovery of damages under 8 
this section is independent of and in addition to the penalty provided in section 3504.  9 
§3507.  Duty to investigate and enforce  10 
The Attorney General shall investigate complaints of a violation or threatened 11 
violation of this chapter and shall prosecute any person violating any of its provisions. 12 
The Attorney General shall use all means available to ensure effective enforcement of 13 
this chapter.  14 
§3508.  Jurisdiction  15 
The Superior Court has jurisdiction to hear and decide prosecutions of all offenses 16 
under this chapter.  17 
§3509.  Exceptions  18 
This chapter does not apply to th e following:  19 
1.  Federal Railway Labor Act.  Employers and employees covered by the federal 20 
Railway Labor Act, 45 United States Code, Chapter 8;  21 
2.  Federal employers and employees.  Federal employers and employees;  22 
3.  Federal enclaves.  Employers and emp loyees on federal enclaves; or  23 
4.  Federal preemption or conflict.  Federal law when the provisions of this chapter 24 
conflict with or are otherwise preempted by that federal law.  25 
SUMMARY  26 
This bill prohibits a person, either in the public or private sector, from being required 27 
to join a labor organization or pay any labor organization dues or fees as a condition of 28 
employment or continuation of employment, notwithstanding any state law to the 29 
contrary.  A violation is a Class D crime and is also subject to ci vil damages and 30 
injunctive relief.  The Attorney General is responsible for enforcement and is required to 31 
prosecute all violations.  32
