33 annuity owner or a retained asset account holder.

3. Required procedures. As pmi of its record-keeping procedures, an insurer shall

A. Common nicknames, initials used in lieu of a first or middle name and use of a

4. Disclosure of personal information. To the extent permitted by law, an insurer

5. No fees. An insurer or its service provider may not charge any beneficiary or 
other authorized representative for any fees or costs associated with a death master file

6. Payment of benefits. The benefits from a policy, a contract or a retained asset 
account, plus any applicable accrued contractual interest, is payable to a designated 
beneficiaty or annuity owner. In the event a beneficiaty or owner cannot be found after 
good faith efforts to contact the beneficiary or owner pursuant to this section have been 
carried out and documented, the insurer shall dispose of any unclaimed money according 
to Title 33, chapter 41.' 
SUMMARY 
This amendment replaces the bill. The amendment requires life insurers to implement 
specific procedures for confirming the death of a policyholder and identifying 
beneficiaries or other persons entitled to payment of life insurance policies.
