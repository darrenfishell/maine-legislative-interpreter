5 Sec. 2.  36 MRSA  §5219-RR is enacted  to read:
6 §5219-RR.  Access to  justice credit
7 1.  Definitions.   As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
8 following terms  have the following  meanings.
9 A.  "Board" means the Board of Overseers of the Bar.
10 B.  "Eligible  attorney"  means a person eligible to practice law in the State under Title 
11 4, chapter 17 who, after January  1, 2019:
12 (1)  First begins the private practice of law in the State by joining an existing 
13 legal practice in an underserved area or by establishing  a new legal  practice or 
14 purchasing an existing  legal  practice  in an underserved area;
15 (2)  Agrees to practice  full time for at least 5 years  in an underserved area; and
16 (3)  Is certified by the board under subsection  3 to be eligible for the credit  under  
17 this section.
18 C.  "Underserved area" means an area in the State that is determined by the board to 
19 be an area  where there is insufficient access  to legal services.
20 2.  Credit.   An eligible  attorney is allowed a credit for each taxable year, not to 
21 exceed $12,000, against  the taxes due under this Part.  The credit may be claimed in the 
22 first year that the eligible attorney meets the conditions of eligibility for at least 6 months 
23 and in each of the  4 subsequent years.
24 3.  Eligibility limitation;  certification.  The board may certify up to 5 eligible  
25 attorneys in each year in 2019  through 2024.  Additional  attorneys  may not be certified 
26 after 2024.  The board shall monitor certified attorneys to ensure that they continue to be 
27 eligible for the credit under this section and shall decertify any attorney  who ceases to 
28 meet the conditions of eligibility.  The board  shall  notify the bureau whenever an attorney 
29 is certified or decertified.  A decertified attorney  ceases to be eligible  for the credit under 
30 this section  beginning with the tax year  during which the attorney  is decertified.
31 4.  Report; review.  By February 15, 2024, the State Tax Assessor shall submit to the 
32 joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over taxation matters a 
33 report that identifies the number of eligible attorneys claiming  the credit under this 
34 section each year in which  the credit is available and identifies the underserved areas 
35 where those  attorneys practice.  The committee  shall review the report and determine the 
36 effectiveness of the credit in expanding legal services to underserved areas.  The 
37 committee may submit legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature 
38 related to the report.

2 This bill provides an income tax credit  for 5 years  for attorneys who begin the 
3 practice of law in the State  and agree to practice for at least 5 years in an underserved 
4 area of the State as determined by the Board  of Overseers  of the Bar.  Eligibility for the 
5 credit is open from 2019 through 2024.  The board may certify up to 5 eligible  attorneys  
6 each year.   The joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 
7 taxation matters  is directed to review  the effectiveness of the credit and may submit 
8 legislation to extend or  revise it.
