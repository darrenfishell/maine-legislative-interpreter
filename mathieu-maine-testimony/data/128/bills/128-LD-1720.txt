19 2.  Fishing  and record-keeping requirements.  The holder of the Class I, Class II or 
20 Class III lobster and crab fishing  license must be present on the vessel with the individual 
21 authorized to fish under  this section  for at least half of the hours the individual spends 
22 fishing under  the authority  of that license.  The logbook required  under  section 6445 must

25 on the vessel during those hours.  The holder of the license on which the temporary 
26 terminal illness  medical  allowance  is based  shall submit the logbook to the commissioner  
27 before the allowance may be renewed or prior to December 31st of the year in which  the 
28 allowance was issued, whichever is earlier.
29 3.  Exclusivity.  The issuance  of a temporary terminal illness medical allowance  
30 under this section does not affect a license holder's eligibility for a temporary medical 
31 allowance under section  6450.
32 4.  Term; renewal.  A temporary terminal illness medical allowance  may not exceed 
33 one year.  The commissioner may renew a temporary terminal illness medical allowance  
34 up to 2 times upon a request in writing from  the holder  of the Class I, Class  II or Class III 
35 lobster and crab fishing license on which the temporary terminal illness medical 
36 allowance is  based.
37 SUMMARY
38 This bill extends the maximum duration of a temporary medical allowance for lobster 
39 and crab  fishing  license holders from one year to 2 years.
40 The bill also creates a temporary terminal illness medical allowance that allows an 
41 individual to fish under  the authority of the license  of a Class  I, Class  II or Class III 
Page 3 - 128LR2554(01)-11 lobster and crab fishing license holder  when the individual is a spouse or child of the 
2 license holder and has completed the lobster apprentice program  and the license holder 
3 has been diagnosed with a terminal illness and harvested a minimum  of 1,000 pounds of 
4 lobsters within  one year prior to the request for the temporary  terminal illness medical 
5 allowance. The bill requires the license holder to be present  on the vessel for at least half 
6 of the hours  during which  the individual is fishing under the authority of the license on 
7 which the temporary terminal  illness medical allowance  is based. The temporary  terminal 
8 illness medical allowance  may not exceed  one year in duration and may be renewed up to 
9 2 times.
