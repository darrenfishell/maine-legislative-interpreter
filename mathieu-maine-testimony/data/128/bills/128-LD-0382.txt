Sec. 1.  2 MRSA §11 is enacted to read:  2 
§11.  General obligation bonds  3 
The Governor shall authorize the issuance of a general obligation bond that has been 4 
ratified by the legal voters of the St ate unless:  5 
1.  Debt service greater.   The Treasurer of State determines that the debt service on 6 
the bond would be greater than the amount budgeted;  7 
2.  Adverse impact on credit rating.   The Treasurer of State determines that the 8 
issuance of the bonds wil l adversely affect the credit rating of the State;  9 
3.  Advantageous interest rate.   The Treasurer of State determines that a delay in 10 
the issuance of the bonds will likely result in a more financially advantageous interest 11 
rate; 12 
4.  Project not proceeding.   The Governor receives notification that a project for 13 
which the bond was authorized is not going forward and the funding to be provided by 14 
the bond is not required; or  15 
5.  Alternative funding available.   The Governor receives notification that 16 
alternativ e funding sources are available to implement, within a comparable time frame, a 17 
project and the purposes for which the bond was authorized and ratified.  18 
Upon the occurrence of any of the circumstances set out in subsections 1 to 5, the 19 
Governor shall immed iately notify the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the 20 
House of Representatives and the majority and minority leaders of the Senate and the 21 
House of Representatives of the specific bond delayed or otherwise not issued, the extent 22 
of the delay of the issuance of the bond and the effect of the delay or nonissuance on the 23 
project and purposes of the bond.  24

SUMMARY  13 
This bill ma kes the following changes to the laws governing the issuance of bonds:  14 
1.  It requires the Governor to issue a general obligation bond that has been ratified 15 
by the legal voters of the State unless one of 5 specific conditions exists, and it requires 16 
the G overnor to provide certain information upon delaying or forgoing issuance of a 17 
bond;  18 
2.  It eliminates the requirement that registered bonds bear the facsimile signature of 19 
the Governor; and  20 
3.  It provides that the bill applies to all general obligation b onds ratified by the voters 21 
but as yet unissued as well as all future general obligation bonds ratified by the voters.  22
