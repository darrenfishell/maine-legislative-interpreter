17 The bill authorizes a person with specific military training and experience to take the 
18 examination for licensure as a licensed practical nurse. This amendment requires such a 
19 person to complete a practical nursing preparation program approved by the State Board 
20 of Nursing and designed specifically for applicants with military training and experience 
21 prior to taking the licensing examination. The program may include a combination of 
22 apprenticeship and traditional educational experiences. The board is directed to adopt 
23 provisional, major substantive rules establishing the requirements for approval of the 
24 program by March 1, 2019 and is directed to invite the participation of stakeholders in the 
25 rule-making process. 
26 The amendment implements a recommendation of the Task Force on Maine's 21st 
27 Century Economy and Workforce by providing $650,000 in ongoing funding to support 
28 the hiring of additional nursing faculty and the acquisition of equipment and instructional 
29 space to expand nursing classes at Southern Maine Community College, Central Maine 
30 Community College and Eastern Maine Community College as well as $200,000 in one-
31 time funding to support the acquisition of a nursing simulator at Northern Maine 
32 Community College.

This bill includes General Funds appropriations totaling $850,000 in fiscal year 2018-19 to the Maine Community 
College System to support the hiring of additional nursing faculty and the acquisition of equipment and instruction 
space to expand nursing classes. Of this amount, $600,000 per year is provided on an ongoing basis to the following: 
Southern Maine Community College ($200,000), Central Maine Community College ($200,000) and Eastern Maine 
Community College ($200,000). One-time funding of $250,000 in fiscal year 2018-19 is provided to Northern Maine 
Community College to support the acquisition of a nursing simulator. 
Additional costs to the State Board of Nursing, affiliated with the Department of Professional and Financial 
Regulation, to implement the requirements of this legislation can be absorbed within existing budgeted resources. 
Additional dedicated revenue received from licensing fees is expected to be minimal.
