4 §3028-A.  Disposal  of unidentified remains and abandoned human remains
5 Whenever unidentified human skeletal remains are recovered, the Chief  Medical 
6 Examiner may store the remains, release  them to an educational institution,  inter them  in 
7 an appropriate resting place  or have them cremated.  Ashes of remains  cremated  may be 
8 disposed of in any appropriate  manner.   Human skeletal  remains uncovered in a cared-for 
9 cemetery or  known to be  Indian remains are  excluded from the operation of this section.
10 The Chief Medical Examiner may assume responsibility for the disposal of human  
11 remains that are the subject of a medical examiner case if no one takes custody and 
12 control of the human  remains  for a period of 30 days after the Chief Medical Examiner 
13 has both completed an autopsy or necessary examination of the human remains and made 
14 reasonable inquiry under section 3028.  The Chief Medical Examiner  shall file or cause to 
15 be filed a certificate of abandonment in the municipality where the human  remains  were  
16 recovered that indicates the means  of disposal.
17 Payment of expenses  incurred by the Chief Medical  Examiner  pursuant to this section 
18 must be  made pursuant to section 3028-D, subsection 2.

Page 2 - 128LR2760(01)-11 SUMMARY
2 This bill authorizes the Chief Medical Examiner to assume responsibility for the 
3 disposal of abandoned human remains that are the subject of a medical examiner case if 
4 no one takes  custody  and control  of them 30 days after an autopsy or necessary 
5 examination has taken place.  It also directs  the Office of Chief  Medical Examiner to 
6 charge $100 per year,  per case  for forensic  preservation  of body fragments and fluids.
