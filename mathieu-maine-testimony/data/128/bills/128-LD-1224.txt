SUMMARY  20 
This bill directs the Public Utilities Commission to develop clear standards and 21 
protectio ns to be imposed on investor -owned transmission and distribution utilities to 22 
prevent them from providing preferential treatment to their generation affiliates in Maine.  23 
This bill requires the commission to adopt major substantive rules to protect 24 
ratepaye rs and establish standards of conduct that govern the relationship between an 25 
investor -owned transmission and distribution utility and a generation affiliate.  It requires 26 
that standards of conduct adopted by commission rule ensure at a minimum that a 27 
gene ration affiliate is not given preference over nonaffiliated competitive generators; 28 
costs of the generation affiliate are not recovered from ratepayers; employees of an 29 
investor -owned transmission and distribution utility are physically separate from and n ot 30 
shared with those of a generation affiliate; and the accounts and records of an investor - 31 
owned transmission and distribution utility and a generation affiliate are separate.  32 
This bill defines "generation affiliate" as an affiliated interest that owns, h as a 33 
financial interest in or controls generation or generation -related assets.  34 
This bill defines "financial interest" as any voting or nonvoting equity interest, 35 
partnership interest, whether limited or general, trust interest or joint venture in an entit y 36 
and specifies that a financial interest is not created between an investor -owned 37 
transmission and distribution utility and a generation affiliate solely by virtue of their 38 
relationship with a common parent company.  39
