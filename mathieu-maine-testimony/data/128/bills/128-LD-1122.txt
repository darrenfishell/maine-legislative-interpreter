SUMMARY  19 
The bill amends the laws governing the financing of political campaigns and the 20 
Commission on Governmental Ethics and Election Practices by:  21 
1.  Clarifying that the definition of "election" covers both candidate and ballot 22 
question elections;  23 
2.  Requiring the commission to submit an annual statistical report of campaign 24 
finance activity to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 25 
legal affairs;  26 
3.  Amending the requirement for state party commi ttees to provide names, mailing 27 
addresses and e -mail addresses of the chair and treasurer for municipal, district and 28 
county party committees to the commission;  29 
4.  Eliminating the requirement for unopposed primary election candidates to report 30 
contributio ns and expenditures of $1,000 or more within 24 hours during the last 13 days 31 
before the primary election;  32 
5.  Requiring candidates to disclose the addresses of payees and creditors in their 33 
campaign finance reports;  34 
6.  Permitting candidates to spend surp lus campaign contributions on expenses 35 
associated with a recount of an election;  36 
7.  Clarifying the reporting deadlines for state party committees, political action 37 
committees and ballot question committees.  Under the bill, quarterly campaign finance 38 
 
 Page 10 - 128LR0352(01) -1 
 reports are due every year; committees must file preelection and post -election reports on 1 
the 11th day before and the 42nd day after an election for both the primary and general 2 
elections during a candidate election year; and for elections held in other years,  3 
committees must file the preelection and post -election reports only if they received 4 
contributions or made expenditures for the purpose of influencing the election;  5 
8.  Clarifying that municipal, district and county party committees are not required to 6 
report large contributions and expenditures within 24 hours during the last 13 days before 7 
a primary election;  8 
9.  Increasing the potential penalty for an individual's failure to register as a candidate 9 
with the commission from $10 to $100;  10 
10.  Defining "ca ucus political action committee" to mean a committee designated by 11 
a party leader in the Legislature to promote the election of the nominees of their political 12 
party to the House or Senate;  13 
11.  Eliminating the requirement for candidates to report money or der fees paid by 14 
the candidate with personal funds when seeking Maine Clean Election Act funding; and  15 
12.  Prohibiting candidates and others from signing the name of a contributor on a 16 
receipt and acknowledgment form submitted to the commission, except whe n a 17 
contributor's immediate family member, domestic partner or live -in caregiver signs on 18 
behalf of the contributor due to a physical impairment or disability.  19
