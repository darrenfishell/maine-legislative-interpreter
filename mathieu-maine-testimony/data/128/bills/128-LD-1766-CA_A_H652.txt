SUMMARY 
2 This amendment, which is the unanimous report of the committee, requires that the 
3 funds in the Harness Racing Promotional Fund be used solely for the marketing and 
4 promotion of harness racing and requires the State Harness Racing Commission to invite 
5 input on the marketing and promotion of harness racing in this State from a statewide 
6 association of harness horsemen, a statewide association of Standardbred breeders, a 
7 statewide association of agricultural fairs and persons who are members of organizations 
8 representing the interests of commercial harness racing tracks and off-track betting 
9 facilities. It requires the State Harness Racing Commission, in its annual report, to 
10 include an account of the commission's operations and actions regarding the promotion of 
11 harness racing, a summary of income and expenses of the Harness Racing Promotional 
12 Fund, including any receipts and disbursements, and an assessment of the economic 
13 condition of the harness racing industry in this State. The amendment also changes the 
14 date of the annual report from April 1st in 2018 to February 15th beginning in 2019. It 
15 also adds an appropriations and allocations section.

This bill repeals the Harness Racing Promotional Board (board) and moves its duties and responsibilities to the 
existing State Harness Racing Commission ( commission), which is within the Department of Agriculture, 
Conservation and Forestry. The Harness Racing Promotional Fund, currently administered by the board, will be 
transferred to the commission. Annual revenue of $50,000 from commissions on live racing will now accrue to the 
commission starting in fiscal year 2018-19 and the bill includes an ongoing allocation of $50,000 beginning in fiscal 
year 2018-19.
