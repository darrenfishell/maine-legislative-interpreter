(1)  Comply with all applicable federal environmental laws and standards, 22

(2)  Require that all mining areas be left in or returned to a geologically stable 25

remediation and closure;  29

(5)  Require that all remediation costs related to a mining area be paid by a 34 
permittee and not by the State;  35

SUMMARY  36 
This bill establishes the Mining Advisory Panel, the purpose of which is to design 37 
and submit to the Legislature recommendations regarding the establishment of a statutory 38 
and regulato ry framework for metallic mineral mining in the State.  The panel consists of 39 
between 3 and 7 members, some of whom must have expertise in the mining of 40 
volcanogenic massive sulfide deposits.  On or before December 31, 2019, the panel must 41 
submit a report to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over 42
