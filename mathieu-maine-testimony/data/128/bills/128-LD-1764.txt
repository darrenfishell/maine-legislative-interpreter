§13063 -S.  Bureau established  11 
The Bureau of the Special Advocate, referred to in this article as "the bureau," is 12 
established within the department to assist in resolving regulatory enforcement actions 13 
affecting small businesses that, if taken, are likely to result in significant economic 14 
hardship and to advocate for small business interests in other regulatory mat ters. 15 
§13063 -T.  Definitions  16 
As used in this article, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 17 
have the following meanings.  18

2.  Agency enforcement action.  "Age ncy enforcement action" means an 20 
enforcement action initiated by an agency against a small business.  21 
3.  Complaint.  "Complaint" means a request to the special advocate for assistance 22 
under section 13063 -V. 23 
4.  Regulatory impact notice.  "Regulatory impact  notice" means a written notice 24 
from the commissioner to the Governor as provided in section 13063 -X. 25

§13063 -V.  Small business requests for assistance  8 
A small business may file a complaint requesting the assistance of the special 9 
advocate i n any agency enforcement action initiated against that small business.  The 10 
special advocate may provide assistance to the small business in accordance with section 11 
13063 -W, subsection 2.  The special advocate shall encourage small businesses to request 12 
the assistance of the special advocate as early in the regulatory proceeding as possible.  13 
Before providing any assistance, the special advocate shall provide a written disclaimer to 14 
the small business stating that the special advocate is not acting as an at torney 15 
representing the small business, that no attorney -client relationship is established and that 16 
no attorney -client privilege can be asserted by the small business as a result of the 17 
assistance provided by the special advocate under this article.  18 
§1306 3-W.  Power and duties of the special advocate  19 
1.  General advocacy.  The special advocate may advocate generally on behalf of 20 
small business interests by commenting on rules proposed under chapter 375, testifying 21 
on legislation affecting the interests of small businesses, consulting with agencies having 22 
enforcement authority over business matters and promoting the services provided by the 23 
special advocate.  24 
2.  Advocate on behalf of an aggrieved small business.  Upon receipt of a 25 
complaint requesting assist ance under section 13063 -V, the special advocate may:  26 
A.  Consult with the small business that filed the complaint and with the staff in the 27 
agency that initiated the agency enforcement action to determine the facts of the case;  28 
B.  After reviewing the com plaint and discussing the complaint with the small 29 
business and the agency that initiated the agency enforcement action, determine 30 
whether, in the opinion of the special advocate, the complaint arises from an agency 31 
enforcement action that is likely to res ult in a significant economic hardship to the 32 
small business;  33 
C.  If the special advocate determines that an agency enforcement action is likely to 34 
result in a significant economic hardship to the small business, seek to resolve the 35 
complaint through consu ltation with the agency that initiated the agency enforcement 36 
action and the small business and through participation in related regulatory 37 
proceedings in a manner allowed by applicable laws; and  38

§13063 -X.  Regulatory impact notice  10 
At the recommendation of the special advocate, the commissioner may issue a 11 
regulatory impact notice to the Governor i nforming the Governor that an agency has 12 
initiated an agency enforcement action that is likely to result in significant economic 13 
hardship to a small business when an alternative means of enforcement was possible, and 14 
asking that the Governor take action, a s appropriate and in a manner consistent with all 15 
applicable laws, to address the small business issues raised by that agency enforcement 16 
action.  The regulatory impact notice may include, but is not limited to, a description of 17 
the role of the special adv ocate in attempting to resolve the issue with the agency, a 18 
description of how the agency enforcement action will affect the interests of the small 19 
business and a description of how an alternative enforcement action, when permitted by 20 
law, would relieve th e small business of the significant economic hardship expected to 21 
result from the agency enforcement action.  The commissioner shall provide a copy of the 22 
regulatory impact notice to the agency that initiated the agency enforcement action, the 23 
small busine ss that made the complaint and the joint standing committee of the 24 
Legislature having jurisdiction over the agency.  25 
§13063 -Y.  Regulatory Fairness Board  26 
The Regulatory Fairness Board, as established in §12004 -I, section 2 -G and referred 27 
to in this section as "the board," is established within the department to hear testimony 28 
and to report to the Legislature and the Governor at least annually on regulatory and 29 
statutory changes necessary to enhance the State's business climate.  30 
1.  Membership.  The board con sists of the commissioner or the commissioner's 31 
designee, who shall serve as the chair of the board, and 4 public members who are 32 
owners, operators or officers of businesses operating in different regions of the State, 33 
appointed as follows:  34 
A.  One public member appointed by the President of the Senate;  35 
B.  One public member appointed by the Speaker of the House; and  36 
C.  Two public members appointed by the Governor, one of whom represents a 37 
business with fewer than 50 employees and one of whom represents a business with 38 
fewer than 20 employees.  39 
The commissioner shall inform the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 40 
jurisdiction over business matters in writing upon the appointment of each member.  41
