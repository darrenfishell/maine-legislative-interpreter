Sec. 6.  21-A MRSA §1060 -A is enacted to read:  3 
§1060 -A.  Reporting by major contributors  4 
Any person, other than an individual, that makes cash contributions to a political 5 
action committee, party committee or ballot question committee aggregating in excess of 6 
$100,000 within a calendar year is considered a major contributor to the recipient 7 
committee and shall file reports as set out in this section.  The requirement to file a report 8 
as a major contributor does  not apply to a political action committee, party committee or 9 
ballot question committee registered with the commission or a municipality.  10 
1.  Notice to major contributor.  Within 5 days of receiving more than $100,000 in 11 
the aggregate within a calendar ye ar from a major contributor, the recipient committee 12 
shall provide written notice to the major contributor of the reporting requirement under 13 
this section and shall submit a copy of the notice to the commission.  If the contribution 14 
that exceeds the $100,0 00 aggregate amount is received during the last 13 days before an 15 
election, the recipient committee shall provide written notice of the reporting requirement 16 
to the major contributor within 24 hours of receipt and submit a copy of the notice to the 17 
commiss ion.  The commission shall prepare a sample written notice for this purpose.  18 
2.  Required reports.  A major contributor shall file a report containing the 19 
information required in subsection 3 on or before the filing deadline under section 1059, 20 
subsection 2 for the recipient committee's October quarterly or 11 -day preelection 21 
campaign finance report, whichever occurs first after the major contributor receives 22 
notice of the reporting requirement.  If a major contributor makes a contribution of more 23 
than $100 ,000 during the 13 days before an election, the major contributor shall file a 24 
report within 2 business days of receiving notice from the recipient committee or the 25 
commission.  The commission shall prescribe and prepare forms for these reports and 26 
may req uire major contributors to file reports electronically.  27 
3.  Content.  In the reports required under subsection 2, a major contributor shall 28 
provide:  29 
A.  The name of and relevant contact information for the major contributor and the 30 
name of a responsible of ficer of the major contributor;  31 
B.  The form of organization and purpose of the major contributor;  32 
C.  The amounts and dates of each contribution from the major contributor to the 33 
recipient committee during the calendar year;  34 
D.  A certification that the m ajor contributor has not received contributions, in whole 35 
or in part, for the purpose of initiating or influencing a campaign in this State, or if 36 
the major contributor has received such contributions, the date, source and amount of 37 
any such contributions;  38

4.  Noncompliance.  The commission may assess a civil penalty against a major 13 
contributor that does not file a timely report required under this section.  The preliminary 14 
penalty is 10% of the total contributions required to be reported, up to a maximum of 15 
$50,000.  Within 2 weeks of receiving notice of the preliminary penalty from the 16 
commission, the major contributor may request that the penalty be waived in full or in 17 
part.  In considering a request for a waiver, the commission shall consider, among other 18 
things, any lack of notice to the major contributor of the reporting requirement, the 19 
number of days t hat the report was filed late and the amount of the contributions required 20 
to be reported.  The commission may assess a civil penalty in the same amount against a 21 
recipient committee that has not provided written notice of the reporting requirements to 22 
the major contributor, using the same procedures as set out in this subsection for penalties 23 
against the major contributor.  24 
SUMMARY  25 
This bill amends the law governing campaign financing and reporting by:  26 
1.  Setting more specific criteria for the reporting of  contributions by an organization 27 
that has received funds for multiple purposes and has formed a political action 28 
committee; and  29 
2.  Requiring an organization that has contributed more than $100,000 to a political 30 
action committee, party committee or ballo t question committee in this State to file a one - 31 
time report with the Commission on Governmental Ethics and Election Practices 32 
disclosing information about the organization, its top 5 sources of funding and a 33 
certification as to whether the organization ha s received money to influence elections in 34 
this State.  35
