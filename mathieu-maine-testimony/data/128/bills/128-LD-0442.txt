SUMMARY  7 
This bill provides an income tax credit for certain eligible expenditures incur red by a 8 
family caregiver for the care and support of an eligible family member 18 years of age or 9 
older.  Eligible expenditures include the improvement of or alteration to the caregiver's 10 
primary residence to permit the eligible family member to remain mo bile, safe and 11 
independent in the home and community; the caregiver's purchase or lease of equipment 12 
necessary to assist the eligible family member in carrying out one or more activities of 13 
daily living; and costs incurred to assist the caregiver to provid e care to an eligible family 14 
member, such as expenditures related to hiring a home care aide, respite care, adult day 15 
care and transportation and for technology to assist the family caregiver to care for the 16 
eligible family member.  17 
To be eligible for the t ax credit, a family caregiver claiming the credit must have a 18 
federal adjusted gross income of less than $75,000 if the family caregiver is filing as a 19 
single individual or as a married person filing separately and $150,000 if the family 20 
caregiver is filin g as a head of household or is filing a married joint return.  21 
An eligible family member must require assistance with at least one activity of daily 22 
living, as certified by a qualified licensed health care practitioner, and qualify as a 23 
dependent of, spouse  of, registered domestic partner of, parent of or other relation by 24 
blood or marriage to the family caregiver or a registered domestic partner of the family 25 
caregiver.  26 
The amount of the credit is equal to the eligible expenditures incurred by the eligible 27 
caregiver during the taxable year up to a maximum of $2,500.  28
