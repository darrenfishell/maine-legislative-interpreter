 Sec. 1.  Joint study of effect of ticks on the deer and moose population.  1 
Resolved:  That the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife and the University of 2 
Maine jointly shall study the effects of ticks and ticks infected with disease, including 3 
Lyme disease, on the deer population and the effects of winter ticks on the moose 4 
population.  The department shall submit a report to the joint standing committee of the 5 
Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services matters regarding the 6 
findi ngs of the joint study and any necessary implementing legislation by December 8, 7 
2019; and be it further  8

SUMMARY  13 
This resolve, for the purpose of reducing the effects o f Lyme disease, does the 14 
following:  15 
1.  It requires the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife and the University of 16 
Maine to undertake a joint study into the effects of ticks and disease -infected ticks on the 17 
deer population and the effects of winter  ticks on the moose population; and  18 
2.  It provides funding to the Department of Health and Human Services, Maine 19 
Center for Disease Control and Prevention for the Lyme and Vector -Borne Disease 20 
Laboratory in the Maine Medical Center Research Institute and the University of Maine 21 
for research and development to combat Lyme disease.  22
