SUMMARY  14 
This bill proposes to improve teacher quality in the State by am ending the current 15 
statutes related to programs involving teacher preparation, support systems for 16 
provisional teachers, teacher certification and teacher salaries and funding college loans 17 
for students enrolled in a program of study leading to certificati on as a teacher.  18 
The bill expands the student teaching experience and academic achievement 19 
requirements for a provisional teacher certificate. It specifies the number of hours of 20 
practicum an applicant must complete, clarifies the time frame during which t he student 21 
teaching experience must occur and specifies that the grade point average requirement 22 
applies only to an applicant's teacher preparation course work. The bill enacts a grade 23 
point average requirement with respect to applicants recommended by edu cational 24 
institutions offering teacher preparation programs.  25 
The bill strengthens the support systems required to provide assistance for provisional 26 
teachers during each year of their probationary period and requires the Commissioner of 27 
Education to pay sa lary supplements to teachers who are involved in the operation of 28 
such systems.  29 
The bill increases the minimum salary for certified teachers to $40,000 beginning 30 
with the 2018 -2019 school year and includes in the school funding formula state funding 31 
for th e incremental costs of meeting this minimum beginning in fiscal year 2018 -19. 32 
The bill requires the State Board of Education to amend its rule Chapter 114: 33 
Purpose, Standards and Procedures for the Review and Approval of Preparation Programs 34 
for Education Personnel to articulate and outline clear standards for a memorandum of 35 
understanding between student teachers, teacher preparation programs and participating 36 
school administrative units.  37 GENERAL FUND  2017 -18 2018 -19
