'§2660-T. Uniform testing recommendation; specified contaminants and properties 
The department shall develop a uniform recommendation for the testing for specific 
contaminants or properties for which residential private drinking water wells should 
periodically be tested. The uniform recommendation must specify contaminants or 
properties that should be included in the periodic testing, including but not limited to 
arsenic, bacteria, nitrates, nitrites, chloride, hardness, copper, iron, pH, sodium, lead, 
uranium, manganese, fluoride and radon, unless the department determines that testing 
for a contaminant or property listed in this section is not necessary based on previous test 
results or credible scientific evidence. The department or an entity that provides testing 
of or provides education or advertisements related to testing of a residential private 
drinking water well shall include the uniform recommendation developed by the 
department pursuant to this section in its written materials related to testing of a 
residential private drinking water well.'

23 This amendment is the minority report of the committee and makes the following 
24 changes to the bill. It removes the requirement for private laboratories that test 
25 residential private drinking water wells to submit the test results to the Department of 
26 Health and Human Services. It adds additional enumerated contaminants or properties to 
27 be included in the uniform testing recommendation. It requires the department to update 
28 its education and outreach materials as needed and to recommend water testing through a 
29 state-certified laboratory. The amendment removes the requirement for landlords to 
30 conduct water tests. It provides that the allocations from the Private Well Safe Drinking 
31 Water Fund may be used to defray costs resulting from the department's waiver of fees 
32 for water supply testing upon a showing of indigency. It requires that the advisory 
33 committee for the Private Well Safe Drinking Water Fund include representatives from at 
34 least 2 state-certified laboratories. It requires that the Maine Water Well Commission 
3 5 distribute educational materials to landowners when a residential private drinking water 
36 well is drilled or deepened.

The bill includes Other Special Revenue Funds allocations to the Department of Health and Human Services of 
$51,660 in fiscal year 2017-18 and $68,880 in fiscal year 2018-19 to provide additional water testing and update the 
education and outreach materials and to conduct educational outreach on the importance of testing for arsenic and 
other contaminants. The Other Special Revenue Funds revenue will be collected through a fee, not to exceed $10, 
from individuals or entities ordering a water test sample from a residential drinking water well.
