 SUMMARY  1 
This initiated bill allows slot machines or a cas ino in York County.  The initiated bill 2 
authorizes the Department of Public Safety, Gambling Control Board to accept 3 
applications for a license to operate slot machines or a casino from an entity that owned 4 
in 2003 at least 51% of an entity licensed to ope rate a commercial track in Penobscot 5 
County that conducted harness horse racing with pari -mutuel wagering on more than 25 6 
days in 2002 if certain criteria are met.  The operation of the slot machines or casino must 7 
be approved by the voters or the municipa l officers of the municipality in which the slot 8 
machines or casino will be located.  The slot machines must be located and operated in a 9 
slot machine facility or the slot machines and table games must be located and operated 10 
in a casino.  11 
Current law provi des that a casino operator license or slot machine operator license 12 
may not be issued to operate any casino or slot machine facility within 100 miles of a 13 
licensed casino or slot machine facility.  This initiated bill exempts a slot machine 14 
operator or a c asino operator licensed under the provisions of this initiated bill from the 15 
100-mile restriction.  16 
This initiated bill raises the limit on the number of slot machines allowed to be 17 
registered in the State from 3,000 to 4,500.  The operation of the slot mac hines or casino 18 
is subject to regulation by the Gambling Control Board.  The applicant for a slot machine 19 
operator license or casino operator license must pay the $5,000,000 license fee set out in 20 
the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 8, section 1018, subsecti on 1-A. 21 
The slot machine or casino operator must collect and distribute 1% of gross slot 22 
machine income to the Treasurer of State for deposit in the General Fund for the 23 
administrative expenses of the Gambling Control Board.  24 
The slot machine or casino oper ator must collect and distribute 39% of the net slot 25 
machine income to the Gambling Control Board for distribution by the board as follows:  26 
1.  One percent must be deposited to the General Fund for administrative expenses of 27 
the board except for a certain amount that must be transferred annually to the Gambling 28

2.  Ten percent must be credited to a fund to supplement harness racing purses;  30 
3.  Three percent must be credited to the Sire Stakes Fund;  31

6.  Two percent m ust be forwarded to the University of Maine System Scholarship 36

board of trustees to fund its scholarship program;  38
