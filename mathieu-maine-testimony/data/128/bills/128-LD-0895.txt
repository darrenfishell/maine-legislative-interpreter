SUMMARY  23 
This bill establishes the Maine Buy America Act and requires that all contracts for 24 
the construction, reconstruction, alteration, repair, improvement or maintenance of a 25 
public building or public work made by a state agency, board, commission or institution 26 
contain a provision that the manufactured goods, including iron and steel, used or 27 
supplied in the performance of the contract or any subcontract to the contract must be 28 
manufactured in the United States.  This requirement does not apply to counties, 29 
municipalities or school administrative units.  30 
The bill requires that, in the case of a manufactured good other than an iron or steel 31 
product, all of the manufacturing processes take place in the United States and the origin 32 
of the manufactured good's components or subcomponents meet a minimum level of  33 
domestic content as established by rule.  34 
Under the Act, a public agency may apply to the Governor or the Governor's designee 35 
for a waiver of the requirement if the executive head of the public agency finds that the 36 
application of the requirement would be inconsistent with the public interest, that the 37 
necessary manufactured goods are not produced in the United States in sufficient and 38
