SUMMARY  22 
Under current law, in order to award to a student a diploma indicating graduation 23 
from secondary school, a school must certify that the student has demonstrated 24 
proficiency in each of the guiding principles set forth in rules adopted  by the Department 25 
of Education.  In accordance with rules adopted by the department, the guiding principles 26 
are measured using rubrics that assess and grade a student's proficiencies in being a clear 27 
and effective communicator, a self -directed and lifelon g learner, a creative and practical 28 
problem solver, a responsible and involved citizen and an integrative and informed 29 
thinker.  This bill repeals this requirement.  30
