SUMMARY  26 
This bill establishes the Fund for Substance Use Disorder Prevention, Treatment and 27 
Recovery and funds it by requiring 10% of the tax revenue from the retail sale of 28 
marij uana and marijuana products to be deposited in the fund.  The fund is used for 29 
substance use disorder prevention, recovery and treatment programs administered by the 30 
Department of Health and Human Services and the Maine Center for Disease Control and 31 
Preve ntion.  This bill specifies that the additional funding for existing programs for 32 
substance use disorder, treatment and recovery is intended to supplement, not supplant, 33 
funding appropriated for those purposes.  34
