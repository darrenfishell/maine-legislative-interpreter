appointing authority that made th e original appointment. An appointed member may not 37 
serve more than 2 terms. A member may designate an alternate to serve on a temporary 38 
basis. A member who is a Legislator serves a 2 -year term coterminous with the elected 39 
term.  Except for a member who is  a Legislator, a member may continue to serve after 40 
expiration of the member's term until a successor is appointed.  41

4.  Duties.   The advisory committee shall:  4 
A.  Advise the Governor and Legislature regarding the interests of individuals and 5 
employers with respect to an exchange;  6 
B.  Serve as a liaison between  an exchange and individuals and small businesses 7 
enrolled in the exchange;  8 
C.  Evaluate the implementation and operation of an exchange with respect to the 9 
following:  10 
(1)  Whether the State should transition from a federally facilitated exchange 11 
model to a state -based exchange or partnership model;  12 
(2)  The essential health benefits benchmark plan designated in this State under 13 
the federal Affordable Care Act, including whether the State should change its 14 
designation;  15 
(3)  The impact of federal and state l aws, rules and regulations governing the 16 
health insurance rating for tobacco use and coverage for wellness programs and 17 
smoking cessation programs on accessibility and affordability of health 18 
insurance;  19 
(4)  The consumer outreach and enrollment conducted b y the exchange and 20 
whether the navigator program is effective and whether navigators or other 21 
persons providing assistance to consumers are in compliance with any federal or 22 
state certification and training requirements;  23 
(5)  The coordination between the s tate Medicaid program and the exchange;  24 
(6)  Whether health insurance coverage through the exchange is affordable for 25 
individuals and small businesses, including whether subsidies for individuals are 26 
adequate;  27 
(7)  Whether the exchange is effective in prov iding access to health insurance 28 
coverage for small businesses;  29 
(8)  The implementation of rebates under the federal Affordable Care Act and 30 
section 4319;  31 
(9)  The coordination of plan management activities between the bureau and the 32 
exchange, including th e certification of qualified health plans and rate review;  33 
(10)  The potential for establishing a basic health program or seeking a Medicaid 34 
state plan amendment or state innovation waiver to provide alternative health 35 
coverage programs for individuals;  36 
(11)  Whether changes should be considered in federal law or regulations to 37 
address dental health coverage available through the marketplace, including, but 38 
not limited to, premiums and out -of-pocket costs;  39

5.  Quorum.   A quorum is a majority of the members of the advisory committee.  21 
6.  Meetings .  The advisory committee shall meet at least 4 times a year at regular 22 
intervals and may meet at other times at the call of the chairs. Meetings of the advisory 23 
committee are public proceedings as provided by Title 1, chapter 13, subchapter 1.  24 
7.  Records .  Except for information designated as confidential under federal or state 25 
law, information obtained by the advisory committee is a public record as provided by 26 
Title 1, chapter 13, subchapter 1.  27

operation of the advisory committee.  29
