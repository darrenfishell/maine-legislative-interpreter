SUMMARY  22 
The purpose of this bill is to clarify the laws governing the employment of minors 23 
and to conform the State’s laws to federal law, thus expanding work opportunities for 24 
minors.  25 
1.  It clarifies that minors who are younger than 14 years of age may be employed 26 
only in the planting, cultivating or harvesting of field crops, or other agricultural 27 
employment that does not p lace them in direct contact with hazardous machinery or 28 
hazardous substances.  29 
2.  It conforms to federal law the hours that minors who are 16 and 17 years of age 30 
may work, by extending the permissible time until which such a minor may work on a 31 
day precedi ng a school day.  32 
3.  It eliminates the prohibition on minors under 16 years of age working more than 6 33 
consecutive days.  34 
4.  Eliminates the prohibition on minors under 18 years of age working more than 50 35 
hours per week when school is not in session.  36 
5.  It specifies that the restrictions on the hours of employment of minors does not 37 
apply to:  38 
 
 Page 7 - 128LR1435(01) -1 
 A.  Agricultural employment that does not require direct contact with hazardous 1 
machinery or hazardous substances;  2 
B.  Employment or training as a theatrical actor or  a film actor;  3 
C.  The taking or catching of lobsters, fish or other marine organisms, as long as the 4 
work does not require direct contact with hazardous machinery or hazardous 5 
substances; and  6 
D.  At a business that is solely owned by the minor’s parent, a s long as the work does 7 
not require direct contact with hazardous machinery or hazardous substances.  8 
6.  It allows the parent or legal guardian of a minor under 16 years of age to sign a 9 
work permit for the minor and removes school superintendents from th e work permit 10 
process.  It requires copies of the minor's signed permit and proof of age to be kept at the 11 
minor's place of employment.  It authorizes the Department of Labor, Bureau of Labor 12 
Standards to revoke a work permit.  13 
7.  It maintains the authorit y of the bureau to revoke work permits if state or federal 14 
law has been violated.  15 
8.  It repeals obsolete language dealing with triplicate permits and a master permit 16 
system.  17
