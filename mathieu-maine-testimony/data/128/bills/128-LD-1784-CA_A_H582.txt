COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A" to H.P. 1229, L.D. 1784, Bill, "An Act To 
Update the Laws Governing the Department of Environmental Protection's Rule-making 
Authority Concerning Underground Oil Storage Facilities To Align with Federal 
Regulations" 
Amend the bill by inserting after the title and before the enacting clause the 
following: 
'Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and 
Whereas, in October 2015, the United States Environmental Protection Agency 
completed revisions to the federal regulations concerning technical standards and 
corrective action requirements for owners and operators of underground oil storage tanks; 
and 
Whereas, states that have been delegated or authorized to administer federal 
programs must adopt regulations that are no less stringent than applicable federal 
requirements within 3 years from the date that any revisions to those federal requirements 
are adopted; and 
Whereas, the State has been delegated or authorized to ,administer the federal 
regulations governing underground oil storage tanks since July 1992; and 
Whereas, the State must adopt several revisions to its statutory provisions and 
corresponding rules regarding underground oil storage facilities no later than October 
2018 in order to maintain its delegated status; and 
Wherea~, in the judgment of the L6gislature, these facts create an emergency within 
the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
immediately hecessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
therefore, 1
