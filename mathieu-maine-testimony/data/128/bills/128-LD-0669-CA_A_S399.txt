5 SUMMARY 
6 This amendment strikes the bill, which is a concept draft, and replaces it with a 
7 number of changes to the Competitive Skills Scholarship Program administered by the 
8 Department of Labor, including the following. 
9 1. It requires that the Department of Labor's outreach efforts regarding the program 
10 be tailored to focus on unemployed and underemployed workers, veterans, immigrants, 
11 recipients of benefits under the statewide food supplement program, low-skilled 
12 manufacturing workers and students enrolled in postsecondary education. 
13 2. It directs the Department of Labor to collaborate with other entities to improve 
14 program outreach to target demographics, including, at a minimum, collaboration with 
15 the Maine Community College System. 
16 3. It makes changes to the criteria used to determine approved education and training 
17 under the program, including consideration of employer input, changing demographics 
18 and traditional industries in the State in which innovations and new technologies are 
19 creating a demand for skilled workers. 
20 4. It requires that a program participant be given information about the family 
21 development account program under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 10, chapter 110, 
22 subchapter 4-A and the allowable uses of such an account. 
23 5. It adds a requirement that a participant be offered comprehensive career planning 
24 services prior to the program's development of an individual career plan with the 
25 participant and permits the Department of Labor to partner with other entities to provide 
26 this service. It directs the department to include in this partnership, at a minimum, the 
27 Maine Community College System with respect to participants enrolled or seeking 
28 enrollment in a training or education course provided by the Maine Community College 
29 System. It also directs the Maine Community College System to assist the Department of 
30 Labor in program management and oversight for such participants. 
31 6. It repeals the January 1, 2020 repeal provision on the eligibility for the program of 
32 a full-time student at a public secondary school enrolled in a career and technical 
33 education program at a career and technical education center or a career and technical 
34 education region.
