COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A" to H.P. 198, L.D. 265, Bill, "An Act 
Concerning Screening of Newborns for Lysosomal Storage Disorders" 
Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
'An Act Concerning Screening of Newborns for Krabbe Disease' 
Amend the bill in section 1 in paragraph D-1 in the last line (page 1, line 4 in L.D.) 
by striking out the following: "lysosomal storage disorders" and inserting the following: 
'Krabbe disease' 
Amend the bill in section 2 in the last 2 lines (page 1, lines 9 and 10 in L.D.) by 
striking out the following: "disorders known as Krabbe, Pompe, Gaucher, Fabry and 
Niemann-Pick diseases" and insetting the following: 'disorder known as Krabbe disease' 
Amend the bill by adding after section 2 the following: 
'Sec. 3. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and 
allocations are made. 
HEALTH AND HUMAN SERVICES, DEPARTMENT OF (FORMERLY DHS) 
Maine Center for Disease Control and Prevention 0143 
Initiative: Provides allocations to add to the newborn screening program the lysosomal 
storage disorder known as Krabbe disease.

The bill includes allocations to the Department of Health and Human Services of $67, 170 in fiscal year 2017-18 and 
$134,340 in fiscal year 2018-19 to add to the newborn screening program the lysosomal storage disorder known as 
Krabbe disease. Current newborn screening fees would be increased from $110 to $120 per baby in order to cover 
these additional costs.
