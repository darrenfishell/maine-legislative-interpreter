Page 1 - 128LR2941(01)-11 Sec. 1.  Statewide plan for computer science.  Resolved:   That the 
2 Department of Education shall lead the effort  to expand computer  science education in 
3 the State's  elementary  and secondary schools through  collaboration with the Computer 
4 Science for Maine Coalition, the Science, Technology,  Engineering and Mathematics 
5 Council and other supportive partners.  The department shall  create a statewide plan for 
6 computer science  education  to communicate and implement  the department's vision of 
7 and goals for kindergarten to grade 12 computer science education in the State.  As part 
8 of the plan, the department  shall assign a staff member beginning in the 2018-2019 
9 school year to serve as the kindergarten to grade 12 computer science liaison to support 
10 the implementation of computer  science education  across the State's  schools.  Priorities of 
11 the plan must  include:
12 1.  Assigning a staff member with dedicated time to support computer  science 
13 teachers in the State;
14 2.  Advocating for continued growth of computer  science education in the State 
15 through supporting  efforts to integrate  computer  science  education standards  into 
16 classroom instruction and graduation  requirements; and
17 3.  Advocating for the development of multiple pathways for preservice  aspiring  and 
18 in-service professional teachers  to become computer  science  teachers.
19 SUMMARY
20 This resolve  is reported out by the Joint Standing Committee on Education and 
21 Cultural Affairs pursuant to Resolve 2017, chapter  21, section 4.  This resolve  includes 
22 certain recommendations proposed in the report submitted by the computer science 
23 education task  force for  consideration  by the  128th Legislature.
24 The joint standing committee has not taken  a position on the substance  of the report  
25 or this resolve; and by submitting this resolve the committee is not suggesting and does 
26 not intend  to suggest that it agrees or disagrees with any aspect  of the recommendations 
27 and the necessary  changes to the Maine  Revised Statutes,  Title  20-A  included  in the 
28 report or this resolve.  The joint standing  committee is submitting the resolve  for the sole 
29 purpose of turning certain proposals in the report into a printed resolve that can be 
30 referred to the committee for an appropriate public hearing and subsequent processing in 
31 the normal course of business.  The joint standing committee is taking this action  to 
32 ensure clarity and transparency in the legislative review  of the proposals included  in the 
33 report submitted by the computer  science education task  force.
