2.  Testimony.  A person who provides testimony to a legislative committee may not:  24 
A.  Purposely provide false testimony regarding a material fact pertaining to that 25 
person's testimony;  26 
B.  Purposely falsify any testimony; or  27 
C.  Purposely omit or conceal a ma terial fact related to the testimony.  28 
3.  Oath.  Notwithstanding any law to the contrary, any member of a legislative 29 
committee may require that the chair of the committee place a person providing 30 
testimony under oath.  31 
4.  Penalty.  A person who violates s ubsection 2 commits a Class E crime.  If that 32 
person violates this section while under oath pursuant to subsection 3, that person 33 
commits a Class D crime.  34

SUMMARY  5 
This bill prohibits a person such a s a lobbyist, state employee, executive branch 6 
official or member of the public from purposely providing, either in person or through 7 
another means, false testimony, either orally, in writing or by other means, to a legislative 8 
committee or purposely omitt ing or concealing a material fact related to testimony 9 
provided to a legislative committee.  This bill allows any member of a legislative 10 
committee to require the chair of that committee to place a person testifying before that 11 
committee under oath.  12 
A pers on who purposely provides false testimony to a legislative committee or who 13 
omits or conceals a material fact commits a Class E crime.  If the person was under oath 14 
at the time, the person commits a Class D crime.  If the person is a lobbyist or lobbyist 15 
associate, the person may be suspended from lobbying before the Legislature for up to 2 16 
years.  17
