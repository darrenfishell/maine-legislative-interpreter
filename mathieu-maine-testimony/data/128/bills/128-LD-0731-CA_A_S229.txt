19 SUMMARY 
20 This amendment replaces the bill. It imposes the duty to act in good faith and with 
21 fair dealing and consistently with specified standards of care on any person who services 
22 a consumer credit transaction that is made to finance or refinance the acquisition of real 
23 estate or the initial construction of a dwelling or that is secured by a first-lien mortgage 
24 on real estate. This amendment specifies that the duties and standards of care may not be 
25 waived or modified. 
26 This amendment allows an aggrieved consumer to recover all damages or an amount 
27 determined by the comi not less than $250 nor more than $2,000. Under current law, an 
28 aggrieved consumer may recover no more than $1,000. The amendment provides that the 
29 comi may, in addition to awarding actual damages, award a homeowner or borrower 
30 under the Maine Consumer Credit Code statutory damages not to exceed $15,000 if the 
3 1 creditor, assignee or servicer engaged in a pattern or practice of violating the duties and 
32 standards of care.
