SUMMARY  13 
The purpose of this bill is to ensure that Maine is in compliance with 23 United States 14 
Code, Section 159(a)(3)(A), a federal law that requires the withholding of federal funds 15 
unless a state provides for a mandatory 6 -month revocation or suspension of the driver's 16 
license of a person convicted of violating the federal Contro lled Substances Act of 1970, 17 
21 United States Code, Section 801 et seq. or a drug offense.  18 
This bill:  19 
1.  Requires a court to suspend for a minimum of 6 months the driver's license of a 20 
person convicted of a crime specified in the Maine Revised Statutes, T itle 17 -A, chapter 21 
45, except for section 1116, which prohibits trafficking or furnishing imitation scheduled 22 
drugs;  23 
2.  If a person who is convicted of a crime specified in Title 17 -A, chapter 45, except 24 
for section 1116, does not have a driver's license,  or the person's driver's license is already 25 
suspended, requires the court to delay the issuance or reinstatement of the license of that 26 
person for at least 6 months after the person applies for issuance or reinstatement of a 27 
driver's license;  28 
3.  Requires  the Secretary of State, on receipt of an attested copy of a court record of 29 
a suspension of the driver's license of a person convicted of violating the federal 30 
Controlled Substances Act of 1970, 21 United States Code, Section 801 et seq. or of a 31 
crime spe cified in Title 17 -A, chapter 45, except for section 1116, to immediately record 32 
the suspension and send written notice of the suspension to the person whose license has 33 
been suspended. If that person does not have a driver's license or that person's drive r's 34 
license is already suspended, the Secretary of State is required to delay the issuance or 35 
reinstatement of that person's license for the period specified by the court; and  36 
4.  Increases the minimum period of suspension for a person convicted of operati ng 37 
under the influence of intoxicants, including scheduled drugs, who has no prior 38 
convictions for operating under the influence within a 10 -year period, from 150 days to 39 
180 days.  40
