SUMMARY  3 
This bill is a concept draft pursuant to Joint Rule 208.  4 
This bill proposes to address veteran homelessness in this State in a comprehensive 5 
and collaborative manner by imp lementing strategies recommended in the "Strategic Plan 6 
to Prevent and End Veteran Homelessness" prepared by the Department of Defense, 7 
Veterans and Emergency Management, Bureau of Maine Veterans' Services.  8 
1.  The bill would create a subcommittee of the S tatewide Homeless Council, referred 9 
to in this bill as "the subcommittee," to share information among all organizations 10 
working to prevent and address veteran homelessness.  11 
A.  The subcommittee consists of the following members:  12 
(1)  The members of the Mai ne Homeless Veterans Action Committee;  13 
(2)  A representative of the Bureau of Maine Veterans' Services, who is the chair;  14 
(3)  A representative of the United States Department of Veterans Affairs, who is 15 
the vice -chair;  16 
(4)  A veteran who advocates on beha lf of homeless veterans;  17 
(5)  The justice action coordinator from the United States Department of Veterans 18 
Affairs;  19 
(6)  A representative of a community action program;  20 
(7)  A representative of the Veterans Justice Outreach Program of the United 21 
States Dep artment of Veterans Affairs;  22 
(8)  A representative of the Maine Department of Labor;  23 
(9)  A representative of the Maine Apartment Owners and Managers Association;  24 
(10)  An employment specialist from the United States Department of Veterans 25 
Affairs;  26 
(11)  A  representative of Shelter Plus Care or the Bridging Rental Assistance 27 
Program at the Maine Department of Health and Human Services;  28 
(12)  A member of the Statewide Homeless Council; and  29 
(13)  Other representatives of organizations that provide services t o homeless 30 
veterans.  31 
B.  The following are the duties of the subcommittee:  32 
(1)  Manage the list by name of homeless veterans through regular review and 33 
updating and conferencing to coordinate the deployment of resources;  34

2.  The bill would encourage the execution of memoranda of understanding between 7 
state agencies that provide services to veterans, including the Bureau of Maine Veterans' 8 
Services, Department of Health and Human Services and Department of L abor, to foster 9 
the improvement of communication between these agencies and the improvement of the 10 
sharing of data among providers of services to veterans.  11 
3.  The bill would provide funding for the establishment of an Assistant Coordinator 12 
of Homeless Vet erans Services in the Bureau of Maine Veterans' Services.  This position:  13 
A.  Reports to, staffs and coordinates the activities of the subcommittee;  14 
B.  Works with agencies and organizations to coordinate service delivery, data 15 
collection and  data analysi s and reporting; and  16 
C.  Administers the Landlord Assistance Fund, to be established in the bill.  17 
4.  The bill would provide funding for enhancements to technology, including the 18 
development of uniform reporting standards and improved data collection.  19 
5.  The bill would provide grants for a statewide system of veteran homelessness 20 
prevention.  Grants must be approved by the subcommittee and administered by the 21 
Bureau of Maine Veterans' Services.  22 
6.  The bill would provide training on best practices for refe rring a homeless veteran 23 
to law enforcement, hospital staff or helpline staff accessed by dialing 2 -1-1.  The 24 
subcommittee would administer this training.  25 
7.  The bill would add capacity to the 2 -1-1 system to help homeless veterans and 26 
encourage people to  use the 2 -1-1 system to report a veteran in distress.  27 
8.  The bill would seek to find long -term solutions to reduce barriers to veterans 28 
securing housing, including, but not limited to, substance abuse, mental illness, felony 29 
charges, lack of a driver's l icense, physical disability, lack of case management and 30 
unemployment.  31 
9.  The bill would increase the number of permanent supportive housing units.  32 
10.  The bill would require the subcommittee to establish a pilot project to determine 33 
best practices for a  coordinated system of entry using a standard assessment process by 34 
all providers in order to provide a consistent level of services to homeless veterans 35 
regardless of which provider first serves a veteran.  36
