SUMMARY  18 
This bill:  19 
1.  Moves the planning functions of the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife 20 
from the Division of Planning, which no longer exists, to the Bureau of Resource 21 
Management;  22 
2.  Allows a person to capture and possess reptiles, amphibians and invertebrates 23 
native to this State and not listed by the department as threatened, endangered or of 24 
special concern without a permit, within certain possession limits;  25 
3.  Clarifies the purp ose for each type of captive wildlife permit issued by the 26 
department and the issuance of 3 -day hold game bird propagation permits and one -year 27 
game bird propagation permits and establishes fees for those permits;  28 
4.  Clarifies that the provision of law re garding release of wildlife into the wild 29 
applies to birds, mammals, reptiles, amphibians and invertebrates;  30 
5.  Eliminates the requirement to band imported pheasants; and  31 
6.  Gives the Commissioner of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife the authority to open a 32 
town or a designated geographical area to the taking of antlerless deer within a wildlife 33 
management district that does not allow the taking of antlerless deer.  34
