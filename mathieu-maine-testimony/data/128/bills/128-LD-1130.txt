§9951.  Traffic safety education defined  5 
As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, "traf fic safety 6 
education" means education designed to provide instruction regarding traffic safety laws 7 
and rules, including best practices for users of public and private ways, including motor 8 
vehicle operators, pedestrians, bicyclists and other vulnerable us ers, as that term is 9 
defined in Title 29 -A, section 101, subsection 91 -A. 10 
§9952.  Traffic safety education required  11 
A school administrative unit shall provide at least one hour of age -appropriate traffic 12 
safety education annually to students in grade 2 and  above.  The traffic safety education 13 
must meet the minimum requirements specified in section 9953.  14 
§9953.  Traffic safety education program minimum requirements  15 
A traffic safety education program, referred to in this chapter as "the program," 16 
offered by a  school administrative unit must meet, at a minimum, the following 17 
requirements adjusted as appropriate for each grade.  18 
1.  Pedestrian instruction.  The program must provide instruction on traffic laws and 19 
rules applicable to pedestrians and safe pedestria n use of public and private ways, 20 
including:  21

B.  Travel on the shoulders of public ways, including direction of travel;  23 
C.  Entering, exiting and crossing public and private ways; and  24 
D.  Conspicuity.  25 
2.  Bicyclist inst ruction.  The program must provide instruction on traffic laws and 26

C.  Hand signals and use;  31

SUMMARY  18 
The purpose of this bill is to provide students in Maine with access to traffic safety 19 
education from an early age with the goal of teaching students safe practices and respect 20 
for all users of the road in or der to reduce the unacceptable number of pedestrian and 21 
bicyclist fatalities and make Maine's roadways safer and more enjoyable for all users.  22 
This bill requires all school administrative units to provide at least one hour annually 23 
of age -appropriate traff ic safety education to students in grades 2 to 12.  The traffic safety 24 
education program must provide, at a minimum, instruction on the use of public and 25 
private ways by pedestrians, bicyclists and motor vehicle operators and on the laws and 26 
rules regardin g that use.  A school administrative unit may contract with a 3rd party to 27 
provide the education.  28
