SUMMARY  14 
This bill amen ds the laws regarding penalties for violating consent agreements and 15 
protective orders in cases of domestic violence.  16 
This bill requires a court to sentence a person upon a 2nd conviction of violating a 17 
protective order or court -approved consent agreement,  which is a Class D crime, to a 18 
definite term of imprisonment of not less than 14 days and a definite term of probation of 19 
not less than one year unless the court makes findings on the record of the court's reasons 20 
for not ordering such definite terms.  Th e bill makes a 3rd or subsequent conviction of 21 
violating a protective order or court -approved consent agreement a Class C crime and 22 
requires a court to sentence the person to a definite term of imprisonment of not less than 23 
60 days and a definite term of p robation of not less than 2 years unless the court makes 24 
findings on the record of the court's reasons for not ordering such definite terms.  25 
The bill also requires a court to sentence a person upon a 2nd conviction of violating 26 
a protective order through c onduct that is reckless and that creates a substantial risk of 27 
death or serious bodily injury to the plaintiff named in the protective order or who 28 
assaults the plaintiff named in the protective order, which is a Class C crime, to a definite 29 
term of impris onment of not less than 14 days and a definite term of probation of not less 30 
than one year unless the court makes findings on the record of the court's reasons for not 31 
ordering such definite terms.  The bill requires a court to sentence a person upon a 3rd  or 32 
subsequent conviction to a definite term of imprisonment of not less than 60 days and a 33 
definite term of probation of not less than 2 years unless the court makes findings on the 34 
record of the court's reasons for not ordering such definite terms.  35 
The b ill also makes a person who commits a crime involving domestic violence 36 
ineligible for deferred disposition.  37
