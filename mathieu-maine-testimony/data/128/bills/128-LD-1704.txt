12 Sec. 1.  Downeast Correctional Facility  report.  The Department of 
13 Corrections shall submit a report concerning proposed  changes to the Downeast 
14 Correctional Facility  to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having  
15 jurisdiction over appropriations  and financial affairs  and the joint standing committee of 
16 the Legislature  having jurisdiction over criminal  justice and public safety matters no later 
17 than April 1, 2019.  The report  must include detailed plans  about  any proposed  changes 
18 concerning the prisoner population  at the Downeast Correctional Facility and the related 
19 impact on other correctional facilities. The Department of Administrative and Financial  
20 Services shall  assist  in the development  of the report including the impact  of any change 
21 to employee compensation and benefits, an economic  and community impact  analysis on 
22 any community  affected  by a shift in prisoner population and the proposed use of any 
23 surplus property generated by facility  closure.
24 Sec. 2.  Continued operation of Downeast Correctional Facility.   
25 Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the Downeast  Correctional Facility 
26 must remain open  and operational, except  that, after submission of the report required by 
27 section 1 of this Act, the facility  may be closed  if legislation approving  the closure  is 
28 enacted into law.

16 SUMMARY
17 This bill requires  the Department of Corrections to report concerning proposed  
18 changes to the Downeast Correctional Facility to the joint standing committee of the 
19 Legislature having jurisdiction over appropriations and financial affairs and the joint 
20 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction  over criminal justice and public 
21 safety matters  no later than April  1, 2019.  The report must  include  detailed plans about 
22 any proposed changes concerning the prisoner population at the Downeast Correctional 
23 Facility.  This bill also specifies the report must  include the related impact  on other 
24 correctional facilities, the impact  of any change to employee  compensation and benefits,  
25 an economic and community impact analysis on any community affected by a shift in 
26 prisoner population and the proposed use of any surplus property  generated by facility 
27 closure.
28 This bill requires the Downeast Correctional Facility  to remain open and operational 
29 prior to the submission of the report to the Legislature and permits  the closure of the 
30 facility only if  legislation  approving the closure is enacted  into law.
