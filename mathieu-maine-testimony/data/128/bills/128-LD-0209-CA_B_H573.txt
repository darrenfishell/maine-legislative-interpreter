SUMMARY 
This amendment replaces the bill, which is a concept draft. The amendment 
increases the amount of time a temporary sign may be placed in the public right-of-way 
from a maximum of 6 weeks per calendar year to a maximum of 12 weeks per calendar 
year. The amendment clarifies that temporary signs within the public right-of-way must 
include or be marked with the name and address of the individual, entity or organization 
that placed the sign within the public right-of-way and the date the sign was erected 
within the public right-of-way.
