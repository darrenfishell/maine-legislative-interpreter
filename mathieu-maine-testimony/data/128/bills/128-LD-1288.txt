SUMMARY  5 
This bill allows that proficiency in a computer programming language may substitute 6 
for prof iciency in a world language to meet the parameters of secondary school essential 7 
instruction and graduation requirements.  This bill also appropriates $1,000,000 per year 8 
of the 2018 -2019 biennium to the Department of Education to issue a request for 9 
propo sals to purchase a student information system that tracks student proficiency, grades 10 
and other student information, that links with the Department of Education student 11 
information system and that can be given to school administrative units at no cost.  12
