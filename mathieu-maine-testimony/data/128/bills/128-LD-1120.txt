 Sec. 1.  Maine Commission on Domestic and Sexual Abuse to study 1 
economic abuse.  Resolved:  That the Maine Commission on Domestic and Sexual 2 
Abuse shall study, pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 19 -A, section 4013, 3 
subsection 3:  4 
1.  The effect o f economic abuse in the State, the enforcement of laws to prevent 5 
economic abuse and to provide relief to victims of economic abuse and the definition of 6 
economic abuse in Title 19 -A, section 4002, subsection 3 -B.  For purposes of this resolve, 7 
"economic a buse" means the exercise between domestic or dating partners of control over 8 
access to economic resources, including finances, documents or property, that reduces the 9 
capacity of the victim to meet basic needs or to be independent or self -sufficient and 10 
enforces the victim's dependence on the perpetrator;  11 
2.  The provision of training opportunities for public officials, including judicial 12 
officers, prosecutors and law enforcement officers, regarding economic abuse in the 13 
State;  14 
3.  The provision of a media campaign regarding economic abuse in the State, 15 
including information and instructions for victims of economic abuse directing them to 16 
seek appropriate remedies; and  17 
4.  The creation of or existing programs to provide legal remedies to victims of 18 
economic abuse, including reimbursement for attorney's fees.  19 
No later than February 7, 2018, the commission shall submit a report that includes its 20 
findings and recommendations, including suggested legislation, for presentation to the 21 
Second Regular Session of the 128th Legislature.  22 
SUMMARY  23 
This resolve directs the Maine Commission on Domestic and Sexual Abuse to study 24 
the effect of economic abuse and the enforcement of laws to prevent economic abuse and 25 
to provide relief to victims of economic abuse, to study the p rovision of training 26 
opportunities for public officials, to study the provision of a media campaign and to study 27 
the creation of programs to provide legal remedies, including the reimbursement of 28 
attorney's fees.  29
