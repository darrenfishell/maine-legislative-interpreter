Amend the bill by striking out all of section 1 and inserting the following: 
'Sec. I. 22 MR.SA §3174-AAA is enacted to read: 
§3174-AAA. Medical nutritional therapy; rules 
The department shall provide for the reimbursement under the MaineCare program 
for medical nutritional therapy services to treat obesity, unhealthy weight, hypertension, 
diabetes, prediabetes, disorders of lipid metabolism, HN infection, unintended weight 
loss in older adults. chronic kidney disease, gastrointestinal conditions, malnutrition and 
inappropriate eating habits. Medical nutritional therapy services may be provided by 
physicians licensed pursuant to Title 32, chapter 36 or 48 or dietitians licensed pursuant 
to Title 32, chapter 104 who practice in hospitals, outpatient settings or private offices. 
As used in this section, "medical nutritional therapy" means a therapeutic approach to 
treating medical conditions and their associated symptoms by use of counseling and a 
specifically tailored diet devised and monitored by a physician licensed pursuant to Title 
32, chapter 36 or 48 or a dietitian licensed pursuant to Title 32, chapter 104. 
The department shall adopt rules to implement this section. Rules adopted pursuant 
to this paragraph are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 
2-A. - .

11 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, provides funding for 
12 reimbursing medical nutritional therapy services from the Fund for a Healthy Maine for 
13 one year. It removes the requirement for MaineCare to reimburse for obesity treatment 
14 medication. It identifies the specific conditions for which medical nutritional therapy 
15 services are reimbursed and adds gastrointestinal conditions to the list. It removes 
16 dietitian nutritionists and clarifies that physicians and dietitians providing medical 
17 nutritional therapy services must be licensed by their professional licensing boards.
