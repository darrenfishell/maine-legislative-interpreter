9 SUMMARY
10 This bill is reported out by the Joint  Standing Committee on Judiciary to implement a 
11 recommendation in the report  of the Task Force To Address the Opioid Crisis in the 
12 State.  It provides  additional funding for the Judicial Department to establish a new drug 
13 court in the State for up to 30 participants or to expand by up to 30 the number of 
14 participants served by existing drug courts in the State pursuant to the Maine Revised  
15 Statutes, Title 4, section 421.  The bill also provides  funding for the case management 
16 and ancillary services provided to drug court participants by the office of substance  abuse  
17 and mental  health services within the Department of  Health  and Human Services.

Fiscal Detail and Notes
The bill includes General Fund appropriations to the Judicial Branch of $10,800 in fiscal year 2018-19 for active 
retired judges to cover other court time of full-time sitting judges assigned either to a new drug court in the State or to existing drug courts in the State that are expanded to allow for an increased number of participants up to a maximum of 30.The bill includes General Fund appropriations to the Department of Health and Human Services of $150,000 in fiscal 
year 2018-19 for case management and other ancillary services provided by the Office of Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services, for a maximum of 30 participants, for drug courts established by the Judicial Department.  These funds can be used either to provide services at a new drug court in the state or to provide services for an increased number of participants in existing drug courts in the State.Fiscal Note Required: Yes
