14 This amendment replaces the bill, which is a concept draft. This amendment 
15 establishes the Advisory Council on Senior Housing to study the unmet need for 
16 affordable housing units for elderly residents of the State as well as the financial burden 
1 7 of home modifications and repairs necessary to enable the State's elderly residents to 
18 remain in their homes. The council is directed to make recommendations to the director 
19 of the Maine State Housing Authority for the development by January 1, 2023 of a 
20 strategic housing plan enabling elderly residents of the State to live in affordable, safe 
21 housing as they age. The director is directed to report to the joint standing committee of 
22 the Legislature having jurisdiction over housing matters on the development or 
23 implementation of the strategic housing plan by March 15th of the first regular session of 
24 each Legislatme. The amendment also adds an appropriations and allocations section.

The bill includes ongoing General Fund appropriations of $1,000 per year to the Legislature beginning in fiscal year

Fiscal Detail and Notes 
This bill also includes ongoing General Fund appropriations of $45,200 per year beginning in fiscal year 2017-18 to 
the Advis01y Council on Senior Housing established within the Maine State Housing Authority to study the unmet 
need for affordable housing units for elderly residents in the State and the financial burden of home modifications and 
repairs necessary to allow elderly residents to remain in their homes. Of this amount, $2,000 per year is for general 
administrative costs and $43,200 is for one part-time position to provide staff support to the council.
