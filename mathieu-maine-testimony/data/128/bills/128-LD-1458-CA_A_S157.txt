16 'Sec. 1. 17-A MRSA §753, sub-§§1-C and 1-D are enacted to read: 
17 1-C. A person is guilty of hindering apprehension or prosecution if the person 
18 hinders the apprehension or prosecution of the other person for a violation of 
19 administrative release, probation, supervised release for sex offenders or parole by any 
20 means described in subsection 1-B, paragraphs A to F with the intent to hinder, prevent or 
21 delay discovery, apprehension, prosecution, revocation or punishment for the violation. 
22 The sentencing class for hindering apprehension or prosecution of the other person is one 
23 class less than the crime for which the other person was originally sentenced, except that 
24 if the crime for which the other person was originally sentenced is a Class E crime, 
25 hindering apprehension or prosecution is a Class E crime. 
26 1-D. A person is guilty of hindering apprehension or prosecution if the person 
27 hinders the apprehension or prosecution of the other person for a violation of deferred 
28 disposition by any means described in subsection 1-B, paragraphs A to F with the intent 
29 to hinder, prevent or delay discovery, apprehension, prosecution, termination of the 
30 period of deferment or punishment for the violation or crime to which the other person 
31 originally pied guilty. The sentencing class for hindering apprehension or prosecution of 
32 the other person is one class less than the crime for which the other person originally pied 
33 guilty, except that if the crime to which the other person originally pied guilty is a Class E 
34 crime, hindering apprehension or prosecution is a Class E crime.'

2 This amendment strikes the bill and creates the new crimes of hindering the 
3 apprehension or prosecution of a violator of either administrative release, probation, 
4 supervised release for sex offenders or parole or deferred disposition. It also clarifies the 
5 class of crime for hindering apprehension or prosecution when the underlying crime is a 
6 Class E crime.
