§1818.  Municipal local option sales tax  3 
1.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 4 
following terms have th e following meanings.  5 
A.  "Local option sales tax" means a sales tax imposed under this section.  6 
B.  "Participating municipality" means a municipality that has imposed a local option 7 
sales tax pursuant to this section.  8 
C.  "Sales tax base" means those item s subject to sales taxation under this Part.  9 
D.  "Single transaction limitation" means a dollar limit that a municipality imposing a 10 
local option sales tax pursuant to this section may place on the amount of tax the 11 
municipality collects from a single tran saction subject to the local option sales tax.  12 
2.  Authorization to impose local option sales tax.  A municipality by referendum 13 
conducted pursuant to subsection 8 may impose a local option sales tax on those items 14 
that are part of the sales tax base.  A m unicipality that adopts a local option sales tax may 15 
not alter the range of items subject to sales taxation under this Part. A municipality that 16 
adopts a local option sales tax also may adopt a single transaction limitation, but in no 17 
case may the single t ransaction limitation exceed $100.  18 
3.  Notify State Tax Assessor.  A municipality that imposes a local option sales tax 19 
shall notify the State Tax Assessor at least 90 days before the local option sales tax is 20 
effective.  21 
4.  Administration.  Retailers in a  participating municipality shall transfer the 22 
revenue from a local option sales tax at the time and in the manner provided in section 23 
1951 -A for the transfer of state sales tax revenue. The tax is subject to the same 24 
enforcement provisions, interest, pena lties and administrative actions as other taxes 25 
assessed under this Part.  26 
5.  Distribution of revenue.  Each month, the State Tax Assessor shall identify the 27 
amount of revenue attributable to each participating municipality under this section, 28 
subtract the  costs of administering this section and certify the net amount for that 29 
municipality to the Treasurer of State for distribution pursuant to this subsection.  From 30 
the net amount certified for a municipality under this subsection, the Treasurer of State 31 
shall make monthly payments as follows:  32 
A.  To the participating municipality, 50% of the net amount;  33 
B.  To the Local Government Fund established in Title 30 -A, section 5681, 25% of 34 
the net amount; and  35 
C.  To the Regional Economic Development Revolving Loan  Program established in 36 
Title 10, section 1026 -M, subsection 1, 25% of the net amount to be distributed 37

SUMMARY  33 
This bill allows a municipality to impose a local option sales tax if approved by 34 
referendum of the voters in that municipality.  The referendum question must identify the 35 
rate of the local option sales tax and th e purposes for which the revenue will be used. The 36 
local option sales tax would be collected and administered by the State in the same 37 
manner as the sales and use tax.  Revenue from the local option sales tax is distributed 38 
50% to the municipality, 25% to the Local Government Fund for state -municipal revenue 39 
sharing throughout the State and 25% to the Regional Economic Development Revolving 40
