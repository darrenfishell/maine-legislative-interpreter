SUMMARY  6 
This bill makes the following changes to the laws governing the Maine 7 
Microenterprise Initiative Fund.  8 
1.  It changes the name of the Maine Microenterprise Initiative Fund to t he Maine 9 
Small Business Initiative Fund.  10 
2.  It requires that a community -based organization have a demonstrated ability to 11 
leverage existing community -based resources and have a plan to promote collaboration 12 
and connection among entrepreneurs and communit y resources in order to be eligible to 13 
receive a grant from the fund.  14 
3.  It defines "small business" as a business located in the State that produces goods 15 
or provides services and has 20 or fewer full -time equivalent employees, including, but 16 
not limited  to, contractors and independent consultants, particularly those in trades and 17 
technology industries; family -owned small businesses whose founders are nearing 18 
retirement; small businesses undergoing change of ownership; and small businesses 19 
engaged in agri culture, forestry or fishing.  20 
4.  It authorizes the Department of Economic and Community Development to award 21 
a grant from the fund to a community -based organization to aid that organization in 22 
providing training to small businesses that show a high potent ial for growth, including 23 
training to provide them with the network, resources and motivation required to build a 24 
sustainable business of size and scale.  To be eligible for training, a small business with a 25 
high potential for growth must have been establi shed for at least 3 years and have annual 26 
revenue between $200,000 and $5,000,000 per year.  27 
5.  It requires the department, when making grants from the fund, to give priority to 28 
applications that target aid to areas with a higher percentage of small busine sses with 5 or 29 
fewer employees and low median household incomes.  30 
6.  It requires that an applicant for a grant to provide training to small businesses that 31 
show a high potential for growth must demonstrate a proven ability to increase the growth 32 
of small b usiness training participants.  33 
7.  It requires that technical assistance and training must address issues of regulatory 34 
compliance, personnel training and support, including wages, hours, benefits, health and 35 
wellness and environmental and safety issues, a nd other issues that affect performance, 36 
management and legal obligations of small business owners.  37
