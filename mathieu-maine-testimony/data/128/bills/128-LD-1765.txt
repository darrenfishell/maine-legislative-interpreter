4 §1651.  Highway  Fund established; purpose; revenue
5 To provide  funds for the construction of state,  state aid and town ways, for the 
6 maintenance of state and state aid highways, and interstate,  intrastate and international 
7 bridges, and for other items  of expenditure specified, there  is established a fund to be 
8 known as the General Highway Fund.  This fund shall  must include all fees received from 
9 the registration of motor vehicles and licensing of operators thereof ,; all fees accruing to 
10 the Treasurer of State  under Title  25, section 1502,; the receipts from  the tax on internal 
11 combustion engine fuels,; 10% of the receipts from  the sales and use tax imposed on 
12 transportation-related items  pursuant to Title 36, section 1966;  and all sums received  on 
13 account of the department for permits to open  highways, or from other  sources, the 
14 disposition of which  is not otherwise designated by law.  After payment from  said 
15 General the Highway Fund  of such sums for interest  and retirement as are necessary to 
16 meet the provisions  of bond issues for state highway and bridge construction, the 
17 remainder of said the fund shall  must be segregated,  apportioned  and expended as 
18 provided by the Legislature.
19 Sec. 2.  36 MRSA  §§1966 and 1967 are  enacted  to read:
20 §1966.  Transfer to Highway Fund
21 By the 20th day of each month,  the assessor shall  notify the State Controller and the 
22 Treasurer of State of the amount of revenue attributable to the tax collected under  this 
23 Part in the previous  month on sales of transportation- related  items reduced by the transfer 
24 to the Local Government Fund corresponding  to that tax revenue required  by Title  30-A, 
25 section 5681.  When notified by the assessor,  the State  Controller  shall transfer 10% of 
26 that amount to the Highway  Fund.  As used in this section,  "transportation-related items"  
27 means motor  vehicles and products for the repair  and maintenance of motor vehicles, 
28 including, but not  limited  to, tires, batteries  and motor oil.
29 The assessor shall adopt routine technical rules pursuant to Title 5, chapter 375, 
30 subchapter 2- A to implement this section.
31 §1967.  Transfer to Multimodal Transportation Fund
32 By the 20th day of each month,  the assessor shall  notify the State Controller and the 
33 Treasurer of State of the amount of revenue attributable to the tax collected under  this 
34 Part in the previous  month on sales of transportation- related  items reduced by the transfer 
35 to the Local Government Fund corresponding  to that tax revenue required  by Title  30-A, 
36 section 5681.  When notified by the assessor,  the State Controller shall transfer 2% of that 
37 amount to the Multimodal Transportation Fund established  in Title  23, section 4210-B.  
38 As used in this section, "transportation- related items" means motor vehicles and products  
39 for the repair and maintenance  of motor vehicles, including, but not limited to, tires, 
40 batteries and motor oil.

3 SUMMARY
4 This bill diverts 10% of the sales tax revenue on the sale of motor vehicles and motor  
5 vehicle-related items from  the General Fund  to the Highway Fund and 2% of that revenue 
6 from the General  Fund to the  Multimodal Transportation Fund.
