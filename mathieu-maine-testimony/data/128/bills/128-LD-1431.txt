SUMMARY  6 
This bill establishes the Substance Abuse Education, Prevention and Treatment Fund 7 
to provide funding for:  8 
1.  Programs for education regarding prevention of a nd support and treatment for 9 
substance abuse, including the prevention of marijuana use by minors;  10 
2.  Regulatory oversight of the retail marijuana industry; and  11 
3.  Increased costs to law enforcement agencies and the courts associated with the 12 
retail mari juana industry.  13 
The fund is administered by the Department of Health and Human Services and 14 
disbursed based on the recommendations of the Substance Abuse Services Commission.  15 
The fund is funded by an increase in the tax imposed on the retail sale of mariju ana 16 
and marijuana products from 10% to 15%, an increase on the tax imposed on medical 17 
marijuana from 5.5% to 15% and a wholesale tax imposed on the sale of marijuana and 18 
marijuana products for retail sale of 3%.  Five percent of the revenue from these taxe s is 19 
deposited in the Substance Abuse Education, Prevention and Treatment Fund; the balance 20 
is deposited in the General Fund.  21
