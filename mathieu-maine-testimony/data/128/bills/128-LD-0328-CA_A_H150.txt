The bill provides $25,000,000 in fiscal year 2017-18 and $0 in fiscal year 2018-19 to 
the Fund for the Efficient Delivery of Local and Regional Services. This amendment 
strikes the bill and instead shifts responsibility for the administration of the fund from the 
Department of Administrative and Financial Services to the Department of Economic and 
Community Development and changes the funding to $5,000,000 in fiscal year 2017-18 
and fiscal year 2018-19 only. The amendment also adds capital grants as a 3rd type of 
grant available from the fund; current law provides for planning grants and cooperative 
services grants.
