§8811.  Definitions  5 
As used in this subchapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the follo wing 6 
terms have the following meanings.  7 
1.  Program fund.  "Program fund" means the Summer Success Program Fund 8 
established in section 8812.  9 
2.  Social impact bond.  "Social impact bond" means a performance -based bond that 10 
operates over a fixed period of t ime and for which repayment to an investor is contingent 11 
upon a specific social outcome being achieved.  12 
3.  Summer success program.  "Summer success program" means a program offered 13 
by a public school that provides traditional and alternative teaching meth ods to ensure 14 
that every student has access to a high -quality extended school year program that helps 15 
the student improve the student's academic achievement and addresses the needs of the 16 
student to demonstrate content area proficiency based on the student 's grade level.  17 
§8812.  Summer Success Program Fund  18 
1.  Establishment.  The Summer Success Program Fund is established as a 19 
nonlapsing, dedicated fund to be directed and administered by the commissioner within 20 
the department and held by the Treasurer of St ate.  The State Controller shall set up and 21 
maintain an account for the money received from social impact bonds and other money 22 
paid into the program fund.  The Treasurer of State shall invest and reinvest the program 23 
fund for the benefit of summer success  programs.  24 
2.  Sources of money.  The following sources of money may be paid into the 25 
program fund:  26 
A.  Money appropriated from the General Fund;  27 
B.  Any grants, gifts and other money from the State and from any unit of federal, 28 
state or local government o r from any person, firm, partnership or corporation for 29 
deposit to the program fund;  30 
C.  All money received from a social impact bond; and  31 
D.  All interest, dividends or other pecuniary gains from investment of money in the 32 
program fund.  33 
3.  Social impact bond.   A social impact bond may involve the department or a 34 
school administrative unit partnering with a service provider or outside investor that 35

SUMMARY  27 
This bill establishes the Summer Success Program Fund, a dedicated fund to be 28 
directed and administered by the Commissioner of Education and held by the Treasurer 29 
of State, to encourage the facilitation of high -quality summer success programs in school 30 
administrative units throughout the State.  The bill accomplishes the following.  31 
1.  It provides that money paid into the fund may include General Fund 32 
appropriations, as well as grants, gi fts and other money from any unit of federal, state or 33 
local government or from any person, firm, partnership or corporation for deposit to the 34 
fund, money received from a social impact bond and interest, dividends and other 35 
pecuniary gains.  It requires t hat school administrative units ensure that grants provided 36 
from the fund for expanding access to summer success programs supplement and not 37 
supplant federal funding.  38 
2.  It requires the commissioner to administer the fund within the Department of 39 
Educatio n and to establish standards and approval for the allocation and use of fund 40

 money for summer success programs offered at elementary and secondary schools in the 1 
State.  It also allows the commissioner to include the following in the standards:  2 
A.  Guideli nes similar to the federal 21st Century Community Learning Centers 3 
program to close the achievement gap between high -performing and low -performing 4 
students;  5 
B.  Effective models of summer success programs that involve networking and 6 
partnerships with commu nity-based organizations that provide a range of high -quality 7 
services to support student learning and development; and  8 
C.  Implementation of the formative and summative assessment methods to measure 9 
student achievement in order to monitor the progress of students participating in 10 
summer success programs.  11 
3.  It provides that, beginning in fiscal year 2018 -19, the department is required to 12 
provide grant funding, through a grant application process, to cover 90% of the costs of 13
