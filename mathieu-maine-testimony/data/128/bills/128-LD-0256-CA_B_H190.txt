COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "B" to H.P. 189, L.D. 256, Bill, "An Act To 
Ensure Continued Availability of High-speed Broadband Internet at Maine's Schools and 
Libraries" 
Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
'Resolve, To Provide One-time Funding for the Maine School and Library Network' 
Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and before the summary and 
inserting the following: 
'Sec. 1. Stakeholder group. Resolved: That the Maine State Library shall 
convene a stakeholder group to examine the funding of the Maine School and Library 
Network, a service of Networkmaine within the University of Maine System, through the 
telecommunications education access fund and determine if there are feasible altematives 
to funding that do not involve surcharges or fees as currently assessed in accordance with 
the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 35-A, section 7104-B. Stakeholders must include at a 
minimum at least one representative of the Public Utilities Commission, the Office of the 
Public Advocate, voice network service providers providing service in the State and any 
other entities identified by the Public Utilities Commission that contribute to the 
telecommunications education access fund as described in Title 35-A, section 7104-B. 
The group shall submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and 
Technology that includes recommendations and any suggested legislation by January 15, 
2018. The committee may report out legislation based on the report; and be it further 
Sec. 2. Appropriations and allocations. Resolved: That the following 
appropriations and allocations are made. 
LIBRARY, MAINE STATE 
Maine School and Library Network N241 
Initiative: Provides one-time funds to support the Maine School and Library Network.

This amendment is the minority report of the committee, and it replaces the bill. It 
provides a one-time appropriation to the Maine State Library to support the Maine School 
and Library Network, a service ofNetworkmaine within the University of Maine System. 
It also directs the Maine State Library to convene a stakeholder group to look at 
alternatives to funding the Maine School and Library Network that do not involve 
surcharges or fees. This amendment requires the stakeholder group to submit a report of 
its findings to the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and Technology by 
January 15, 2018.
