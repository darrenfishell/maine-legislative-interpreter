SUMMARY  16 
This bill provides that if a court determines that an offender has violated unsecured 17 
preconviction bail and that the violation is not excused, the court  must enter an order of 18 
forfeiture of bail, which may not exceed the amount of the unsecured bail previously set.  19 
The bill authorizes an attorney for the State or a prosecutorial district to take action to 20 
collect the amount of forfeited bail and requires  the State Tax Assessor to withhold funds 21 
owed to a taxpayer with regard to whom a forfeiture order has been entered.  22 
This bill also allows the court to order the State Tax Assessor to withhold restitution 23 
owed by an offender from any tax refund owed to th at offender.  24
