COMMITTEE AMENDMENT" (3 "to H.P. 43, L.D. 56, Bill, "An Act To Include 
50 Milliliter and Smaller Liquor Bottles in the Laws Governing Returnable Containers" 
Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
'An Act To Reduce Roadside Litter by Increasing the Penalties for Littering' 
Amend the bill by striking out everything after the enacting clause and before the 
summary and inserting the following: 
'Sec. 1. 17 MRSA §2264-A, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2003, c. 452, Pt. I, §34 
and affected by Pt. X, §2, is further amended to read: 
1. Disposal of 15 pounds or less or 27 cubic feet or less of litter. A person who 
disposes of 15 pounds or less or 27 cubic feet or less of litter commits a civil violation for 
which a fine of not less than $-100 $500 and not more than~ $1,000 may be adjudged. 
Sec. 2. 17 MRSA §2264-A, sub-§1-A, as amended by PL 2011, c. 208, §4, is 
further amended to read: 
1-A. Disposal of 15 pounds or less or 27 cubic feet or less of litter; subsequent 
offenses. A person who violates subsection 1 after having previously violated subsection 
1 commits a civil violation for which a fine of not less than~ $1.000 and not more 
than~ $1.500 may be adjudged. 
Sec. 3. 17 MRSA §2264-A, sub-§2, 1A, as enacted by PL 2011, c. 208, §4, is 
amended to read: 
A. Shall impose a fine of not less than~ $1,000;' 
SUMMARY 
This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, replaces the bill, 
changes the title and increases the penalties for civil violations of the State's littering laws 
as follows:

1. For a person disposing of 15 pounds or less or 27 cubic feet or less of litter, the 
minimum fine is increased from $100 to $500 and the maximum fine is increased from 
$500 to $1,000. For subsequent violations, the minimum fine is increased from $500 to 
$1,000 and the maximum fine is increased from $1,000 to $1,500; and 
2. For a person disposing of more than 15 pounds or more than 27 cubic feet of litter, 
the minimum fine is increased from $500 to $1,000.
