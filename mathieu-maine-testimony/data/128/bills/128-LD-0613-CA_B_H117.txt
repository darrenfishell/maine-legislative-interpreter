SUMMARY 
This amendment, which is the minority report of the Joint Standing Committee on 
Labor, Commerce, Research and Economic Development, replaces the bill with language 
to clarify that the existing law that prohibits a person, corporation or other entity from 
denying goods or services to a person who refuses to provide a social security number 
also applies to the denial of benefits, including, but not limited to, employment.
