4 SUMMARY 
5 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, strikes the bill and 
6 replaces it with the following. 
7 1. It allows the Public Utilities Commission to fairly allocate the costs between 
8 ratepayers and shareholders of an investor-owned public utility when an audit of an 
9 investor-owned public utility contributes to a commission finding of imprudence that 
10 results in a cost disallowance. 
11 2. It directs the Public Utilities Commission to adopt major substantive rules 
12 regarding the testing of a transmission and distribution utility1s metering and billing 
13 systems by a transmission and distribution utility. 
14 3. It directs the Public Utilities Commission to consider whether the rules include 
15 provisions related to periodic, independent audits of an investor-owned transmission and 
16 distribution utility's metering and billing systems. 
17 4. It requires the Public Utilities Commission to submit a report to the Legislature 
18 that addresses whether enough is being done by investor-owned transmission and 
19 distribution utilities to strengthen and protect their systems, whether it is in the ratepayers1 
20 interest to require the utilities to do more to strengthen and protect their systems, and 
21 what can be done to improve public safety in storm events, especially in light of lessons 
22 learned from recent storms.
