Sec. 1.  21-A MRSA §610 is enacted to read:  2 
§610.  Election transparency  3 
1.  Transparency of election procedures.  The Secretary of State shall publish a 4 
guide to election procedures for public distribution and revise the guide annually to 5 
reflect current law, rules and procedures regarding elections.  The guide must describe:  6 
A.  Rules regarding polling places and the conduct of elections;  7 
B.  Devices and software used to capture, interpret or t ally votes and the capabilities 8 
and deployment of the devices and software;  9 
C.  Duties of election officials on, before and after election day;  10 
D.  Opportunities for citizens to monitor election procedures; and  11 
E.  Other election laws, rules or procedures that the Secretary of State determines 12 
appropriate for the public interest.  13 
2.  Duties of election officials.  For each election, an election official shall complete 14 
and return to the Secretary of State:  15 
A.  A completed checklist of election procedures for  the election official’s voting 16 
place; and  17 
B.  A ballot reconciliation form that accounts for ballots delivered, ballots voted, 18 
absentee ballots mailed and returned, spoiled ballots, unused ballots, challenged 19 
ballots and ballots unaccounted for.  20 
Each comp leted form required under this subsection must be signed by the municipal 21 
clerk and one other election official. The Secretary of State shall provide the forms 22 
required under this subsection and direct the manner in which the forms must be 23 
completed and re turned to the Secretary of State.  24 
3.  Election complaints.  The Secretary of State shall develop an election complaint 25 
process that an election official and the public can use to report excessive wait times, 26 
noncompliance with election procedures or other concerns about the election process.  27 
Instructions for filing a complaint under this subsection must be included in the guide to 28 
election procedures under subsection 1 and posted at all polling places.  The Secretary of 29 
State shall keep a record of and mak e public each complaint received under this 30 
subsection and any action taken in response to that complaint.  31 
4.  Maintenance of central voter registration system.  The Secretary of State shall 32 
routinely monitor the central voter registration system and, at l east once every 2 years, 33 
use the best means available to identify and resolve instances of a person registered in the 34 
system who is:  35 
A.  Registered in more than one municipality in the State;  36 
B.  Registered or has voted in another state;  37

3.  Develop an election complaint process;  33
