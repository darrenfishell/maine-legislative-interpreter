 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:  1 
Sec. 1.  22 MRSA §2354 is enacted to read:  2 
§2354.  Opioid Addiction Prevention and Rehabilitation Program  3 
1.  Program establishment.  The Opioid Addiction Prevention and Rehabilitation 4 
Program, referred to in this section as "the program," is established in the department to 5 
provide resources for opioid addiction prevention and rehabilitation services such as 6 
public awareness programs and education within public school systems; research on the 7 
analgesic and addictive properties of opioids and the causes of opioid abuse for persons 8 
dependent on opioids; and the contracting of public and private addiction treatment 9 
facilities that have been properly accredited as determined by the department by rule . 10 
2.  Fund establishment.  The Opioid Addiction Prevention and Rehabilitation 11 
Program Fund is established to implement the program.  The fund is funded from the tax 12 
on opioids collected pursuant to Title 36, section 4921 and from other funds accepted by 13 
the commissioner or allocated or appropriated by the Legislature.  All revenue deposited 14 
into the fund must be allocated by the department based on demonstrated need.  15 
3.  Rules.  The department shall adopt routine technical rules pursuant to Title 5, 16 
chapter  375, subchapter 2 -A to implement the provisions of this section.  17 
Sec. 2.  36 MRSA c. 723 is enacted to read:  18 
CHAPTER 723  19 
TAX ON OPIOIDS  20 
§4921.  Opioid tax  21 
1.  Definitions.  As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 22 
following ter ms have the following meanings.  23 
A.  "Active opioid ingredient" means the part of a product that is an opioid.  24 
B.  "Distribution" means the sale of opioids in the State.  25 
C.  "Manufacturer" means a person that makes opioids.  26 
D.  "Opiate" means the dried, con densed juice of a poppy, Papaver somniferum, that 27 
has a narcotic, soporific, analgesic and astringent effect.  28 
E.  "Opioid" means:  29 
(1)  An opiate;  30 
(2)  A synthetic or semisynthetic narcotic that has effects similar to natural opium 31 
alkaloids but is not deri ved from opium; or  32 
(3)  A derivative of an opioid described in subparagraph (1) or (2).  33 
F.  "Wholesaler" means a person that distributes opioids to a buyer whose intention is 34 
to resell the opioids.  35

 2.  Tax rate.  An excise tax is imposed upon the distribut ion of opioids by a person, 1 
including, but not limited to, a manufacturer or wholesaler, that makes the first sale of 2 
opioids in the State, if the distribution is for the purpose of resale, at the rate of 1¢ per 3 
milligram of active opioid ingredient.  4 
3.  Exemptions.  A person that the State is prohibited from taxing under the United 5 
States Constitution or laws of the United States or under the Constitution of Maine is 6 
exempt from the taxes imposed under this section.  7 
4.  Revenue.  All taxes, interest, penal ties and other amounts collected from the tax 8 
imposed under this section must be deposited into the Opioid Addiction Prevention and 9 
Rehabilitation Program Fund established by Title 22, section 2354.  10 
SUMMARY  11 
This bill establishes the Opioid Addiction Preven tion and Rehabilitation Program, to 12 
be funded by revenue generated by a tax imposed on the sale and distribution of products 13 
that contain opioids at the rate of 1¢ per milligram of active opioid ingredient.  14
