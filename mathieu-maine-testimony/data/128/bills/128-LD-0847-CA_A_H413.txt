§25002. Definitions 
For the purposes of this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates. the following 
terms have the following meanings. 
1. Absorptive capacity. "Absorptive capacity" means: 
A. The ability of a locality's social service and health care agencies to meet the needs 
of the locality's residents or anticipated residents: 
B. The availability of affordable housing. low-cost housing or both and the existence 
of waiting lists for such housing in the locality: 
C. The ability of the local school district to meet the needs of the student population 
or anticipated student population. including the refugee population or anticipated 
refugee population;

12 §25003. Withdrawal from refugee resettlement program 
13 State and local government agencies are not authorized to participate in the federal 
14 refugee resettlement program as created by the United States Congress pursuant to the 
15 Refugee Act of 1980. Public Law 96-212. 
16 §25004. Refugee resettlement liability 
17 A nongovernmental entity that provides refugee resettlement services in this State 
18 after Januruy 1. 2018 may be held liable for damages in a civil action in a court of 
19 competent jurisdiction if the entity resettles a refugee who subsequently commits an act 
20 of terrorism in this State and the entity knew or should have known that such an act of 
21 terrorism was likely or imminent. 
22 §25005. Eligibility for tax exemption 
23 To maintain eligibility for state or local tax exemptions, a nongovernmental entity 
24 that provides resettlement services in this State must annually certify: 
25 1. Quarterly meetings. That the entity has made an attempt to convene no less than 
26 quarterly meetings with appropriate state and local government officials of any locality 
27 that is a host or being considered as a host for refugee resettlement: 
28 2. Consideration of recommendations. That the entity has taken the 
29 recommendations of the state and local government officials into consideration in the 
30 planning of refugee resettlement within the State: and 
31 3. No action if no absorptive capacity indicated. That the entity has not taken any 
32 action to resettle refugees into a locality that has indicated that it does not have the 
33 absorptive capacity to accept refugees into its jurisdiction.

14 SUMMARY 
15 This amendment is the minority report of the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary. 
16 It replaces the bill, which is a concept draft. 
17 The amendment establishes the Responsible Refugee Resettlement Act. 
18 The Act prohibits the State and local governments from participating in the federal 
19 refugee resettlement program as created by the United States Congress pursuant to the 
20 Refugee Act of 1980, Public Law 96-212. 
21 Under the Act, any nongovernmental entity that provides refugee resettlement 
22 services after January 1, 2018 may be held liable in a civil action if it resettles a refugee 
23 who subsequently commits an act of terrorism in this State and the entity knew or should 
24 have known that the act of terrorism was likely or imminent. 
25 Under the Act, a nongovernmental entity that provides refugee resettlement services 
26 must certify that the following requirements are met in order to maintain eligibility for 
27 state or local tax exemptions: 
28 1. Attempt to convene at least quarterly meetings with state and local government 
29 officials of a locality that is a host or being considered as a host; 
30 2. Take into consideration recommendations of the state and local government 
31 officials; and 
32 3. Take no action to resettle refugees in any locality that has indicated it does not 
33 have the absorptive capacity, which is defined in the amendment, to accept new refugees. 
34 Under the Act, a nongovernmental entity that provides refugee resettlement services 
35 must submit an annual report to the Governor and the Legislature that includes specific 
36 information about the refugees, refugee demographics and plans for the following year.
