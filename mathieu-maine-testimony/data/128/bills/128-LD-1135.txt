 SUMMARY  1 
This bill makes the following changes to the Maine Medical Use of Marijuana Act:  2 
1.  The limit  on the number of qualifying patients a primary caregiver may assist is 3 
clarified to be for a period of one calendar month or more;  4 
2.  The definition of a collective is strengthened;  5 
3.  The penalties for participation in a collective are enhanced;  6 
4.  A level of local control is provided by allowing a municipality to limit the number 7 
of primary caregivers that may operate within that municipality and allowing for 8 
enactment of reasonable municipal regulations applicable to primary caregivers;  9 
5.  The confi dentiality provisions of primary caregivers are removed;  10 
6.  A primary caregiver and a registered dispensary are subject to fines for violations 11 
of the provisions of the Act or for failing to register as a primary caregiver or dispensary;  12 
7.  Fines prescri bed for violations of the Act are mandatory;  13 
8.  The Office of the Attorney General may seek an injunction to require a registered 14 
primary caregiver, a registered dispensary, a person who fails to register as a primary 15 
caregiver and who engages in conduct that is only authorized for a registered primary 16 
caregiver or a person or entity that fails to register as a dispensary and that engages in 17 
conduct that is only authorized for a registered dispensary to comply with the Act. The 18 
District Court may order the  registered primary caregiver, the registered dispensary or the 19 
person or entity to pay the costs of the investigation and the costs of suit, including 20 
attorney's fees;  21 
9.  The Office of the Attorney General may seek court action against a registered 22 
prima ry caregiver, a registered dispensary or a person or entity for violation of an 23 
injunction, including but not limited to imposition of a fine; and  24 
10.  The Department of Health and Human Services' burden of proof for a violation 25 
of the Act is a preponderan ce of the evidence.  26 
The bill also includes an appropriations and allocations section.  27
