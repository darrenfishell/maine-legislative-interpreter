§1826 -E.  Procurement Review Board  33 
1.  Board established.   The Procurement Review Board, referred to in this 34 
subchapter as "the board," i s established pursuant to section 12004 -G, subsection 32 -A. 35

 A.  The board consists of 7 members:  1 
(1)  The Attorney General or the Attorney General's designee;  2 
(2)  The Treasurer of State or the Treasurer of State's designee; and  3 
(3)  Five members nominated  by the Governor and confirmed by a majority vote 4 
of the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over state 5 
and local government matters and by a majority vote of the Senate.  Each 6 
member must have demonstrated sufficient business o r professional experience in 7 
the area of procurement to perform the functions of the board.  8 
The Attorney General and the Treasurer of State or their designees serve as nonvoting 9 
members of the board.  A member of the board may not be a member of the 10 
Legisl ature.  In the event that the Governor has not nominated an individual to fill a 11 
vacancy on the board within 30 days of the vacancy, the President of the Senate shall 12 
nominate an individual to fill the vacancy.  13 
B.  At the first meeting, the voting members of the board shall select a chair from 14 
among its members.  The chair serves for a one -year term.  15 
C.  A voting member of the board serves for a 4 -year term.  The Governor may 16 
nominate a voting member for a 2nd term.  17 
D.  A member of the board employed by or holding an interest in an entity doing 18 
business with or attempting to do business with the State does not by the member's 19 
service on the board preclude that entity from doing business with or attempting to do 20 
business with the State.  21 
E.  The board shall m eet at least twice per month.  Meetings of the board must be 22 
conducted in person and must be open to the public.  Written minutes of such 23 
meetings must be created and made available for public inspection and copying.  24 
F.  A majority of the voting members of  the board constitutes a quorum.  25 
G.  A member of the board receives no compensation but must be reimbursed for 26 
expenses reasonably incurred in the performance of the member's duties.  27 
2.  Authority and duties.   The board shall review, comment upon and appr ove or 28 
disapprove all requests for proposals; bids; and contract renewals, amendments, 29 
extensions or other changes to an existing contract for the procurement of supplies, 30 
professional or artistic services, construction and real property and capital improv ement 31 
leases procured by the State valued at $1,000,000 or more.  Upon a majority vote of its 32 
voting members, the board may review requests for proposals; bids; and contract 33 
renewals, amendments, extensions or other changes to an existing contract valued a t less 34 
than $1,000,000.  If the board takes no action to initiate the review of a request for 35 
proposal; bid; or contract renewal, amendment, extension or other change to an existing 36 
contract within 30 days of receiving it, a department or agency may procee d without the 37 
review, comment or approval of the board.  The board may review, study and hold public 38 
hearings concerning the implementation of its duties.  Each chief procurement officer, 39 
state purchasing officer, procurement compliance monitor and state d epartment or agency 40 
shall cooperate with the board, provide information to the board and be responsive to the 41 
board in the board's conduct of its reviews, studies and hearings.  42

4.  Contracts of $3,000,000 or more.   State departments or agencies that int end to 5

5.  Advice of Attorney General.   The board shall timely seek legal advice from the 13 
Attorney General regarding any proposed contract when the board determines the 14 
contract:  15 
A.  May expose the State to substantial risk in the event of nonperformance; or  16 
B.  Could reasonably be expected to incur costs to the State in excess of $3,000,000 17 
over the full term of the proposed contract, exclusive of extensions or amendments.   18 
Upon a majority vote of the voting members of the board, the board also may seek 19 
legal review by and consult with the Attorney General on any contractual matter 20 
subject to the board's approval.  21 
6.  Approval criteria.   The board shall approve the award of  a contract if the board is 22 
satisfied that:  23 
A.  The service to be performed under the contract cannot be economically provided 24 
by State Government;  25 
B.  The award of the contract is the most economical, effective and appropriate 26 
means of fulfilling a demons trated need;  27 
C.  The contract will not impair the ability of the department or agency to meet its 28 
statutory duties and responsibilities under other state laws; and  29 
D.  The contract does not diminish the impact of statewide or other budgetary cost - 30 
savings i nitiatives.  31 
Upon a majority vote of its voting members that the conditions under paragraphs A to D 32 
have not been satisfied, the board may reject a proposal; bid; or contract renewal, 33 
amendment, extension or change to an existing contract.  34

SUMMARY  24 
This bill establishes the Procurement Review Board, which is required to review and 25 
approve or disapprove all requests for proposals, bids, contract renewals, amendments, 26 
extensions or other changes to an existi ng contract for the State's procurement of supplies, 27 
professional or artistic services, construction and real property and capital improvement 28 
leases valued at $1,000,000 or more.  The board by majority vote may review 29 
procurements valued at less than $1,0 00,000.  30
