SUMMARY 
2 This amendment replaces the bill. Like the bill, it adds unlit fiber providers, 
3 telecommunications service providers and info1mation service providers to the list of 
4 entities that may be subject to an order for joint use of equipment. It defines the te1m 
5 "joint use entity" and requires that the interests of customers of a joint use entity be 
6 considered in any order or actions taken by the Public Utilities Commission. It requires 
7 that a joint use entity seeking access to the poles, ducts, conduits or rights-of-way owned 
8 or controlled by another joint use entity have the technical and financial capabilities to 
9 fulfill its obligations related to such joint use and that the Public Utilities Commission 
10 develop a process to ensure this through its rules. It requires that rules adopted by the 
11 Public Utilities Commission promote competition, further the state broadband policy and 
12 ensure safe, nondiscriminatory access on just and reasonable terms. It requires that the 
13 Public Utilities Commission amend its rules by January 15, 2018 to address the terms and 
14 conditions of joint use. It provides that the jurisdiction of the Public Utilities Commission 
15 over joint use entities not otherwise under its jurisdiction is only for limited purposes, and 
16 it makes changes to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 35-A, sections 2301 and 2501 for 
17 consistency with those provisions.
