SUMMARY  21 
This bill makes the following changes to the election laws.  22 
1.  It prohibits a voter from filing an application to change or withdraw party 23 
enrollment on the day of a primary election.  24 
2.  It clarifies that a municipality may not charge a rental fee or janitorial service fee 25 
to a municipal party committee for an available public building provided for the biennial 26 
municipal caucus.  27 
3.  It repeals the provision allowing a party to designate who can participate in the 28 
party's primary other than party members.  29 
4.  It removes the requirement for the st ate ballots to include a write -in space after the 30 
list of candidates for an office, unless there is a write -in candidate who has filed a 31 
declaration for that office.  32 
5.  It adds a requirement for a notary public or other person authorized to administer 33 
oaths to keep a log for the purpose of recording information about the direct initiative or 34 
people's veto petitions on which the notary public has administered the circulator's oath 35 
and clarifies that the notary public must sign the circulator's oath immediat ely after the 36 
circulator has signed the petition in the notary's presence.  37
