CHAPTER 35  13 
WELL WATER TESTING P ROGRAM  14 
§3201.  Definitions  15 
As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 16 
have the following meanings.  17 
1.  Basic pollutants.  "Basic pollutants" means contaminants known to be a threat to 18 
human health and to be present in potable wells in this State, as determined by the 19 
department by rule.  20 
2.  Contaminated area.  "Contaminated area" means an area in which there is a 21 
known environmental hazard or d ispersal of pollutants that may contaminate potable 22 
wells, as determined by the department by rule.  23 
3.  Potable well.  "Potable well" means a well that is a private water supply created 24 
or used to provide water for human consumption.   "Potable well" does not include a 25 
public water system as defined in Title 22, section 2601, subsection 8 or a roadside spring 26 
as defined in Title 22, section 2601, subsection 8 -A. 27 
§3202.  Testing required; schedule  28 
A potable well must be tested for basic pollutants in accorda nce with the following 29 
requirements.  30 
1.  New potable wells.  A person drilling or otherwise creating a new potable well 31 
after July 1, 2018 shall have the well tested for basic pollutants in accordance with rules 32 
adopted by the department.  33 
2.  Ongoing testi ng.  A person owning a potable well shall have the well tested for 34 
basic pollutants at least once every 5 years in accordance with rules adopted by the 35 
department.  36

SUMMARY  20 
This bill requires all private  wells used to provide water for human consumption to be 21 
tested for basic pollutants in accordance with rules adopted by the Department of 22 
Environmental Protection.  The wells are required to be tested at least once every 5 years, 23 
and all new wells created  after July 1, 2018 are required to be tested.  All fees are waived 24 
for testing a well within an area in which there is a known environmental hazard or 25 
dispersal of pollutants that may contaminate the well.  26 
The Department of Environmental Protection is req uired to determine the costs of 27 
implementing this legislation and, in consultation with the Public Utilities Commission 28 
and the Efficiency Maine Trust, identify appropriate funding sources to pay those costs.  29 
The department is required to submit its findi ngs and recommendations to the Joint 30 
Standing Committee on Appropriations and Financial Affairs by January 15, 2018.  31
