SUMMARY 
This amendment, which is the unanimous report of the committee, grants the Board 
of Osteopathic Li censure and the Board of Li censure in Medicine the authority to request 
state and national criminal history information, including fingerprint-based criminal 
history information, for physicians who request expedited licensure under the Interstate 
Medical Licensure Compact. The State may not participate in the Interstate Medical 
Licensure Compact unless the boards have the authority to obtain and review this

Minor cost increase -General Fund 
Minor cost increase -Highway Fund 
Minor cost increase -Other Special Revenue Funds 
Minor revenue increase -Other Special Revenue Funds 
Additional costs to the Board ofLicensure in Medicine and the Board of Osteopathic Licensure, affiliated with the 
Department of Professional and Financial Regulation, to participate in the Interstate Medical Li censure Compact can 
be absorbed within each board's existing budgeted resources. This legislation may result in increased dedicated 
revenue to each board from licensing and renewal fees. The amount of revenue to be received will depend on the fees 
established by the interstate commission and the number of physicians that apply for an expedited license each fiscal 
year. 
Additional costs to the Department of Public Safety associated with taking fingerprints and performing background 
checks can be absorbed within existing budgeted resources. Other Special Revenue Funds revenue will increase as 
the result of increased payments for background checks. No change in the budget is required.
