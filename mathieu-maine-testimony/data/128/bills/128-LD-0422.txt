SUMMARY  18 
This bill imposes a 2 -year moratorium, beginning November 1, 2017, on new 19 
contracts or agreements by a consumer -owned water utility, municipality, state agency or 20 
other governmental entity involving the extraction of more than 75,000 gallons of 21 
groundwater  during any week or more than 50,000 gallons of groundwater on any day.  22 
The bill also directs the Commissioner of Environmental Protection to convene a working 23 
group to develop the statutory and regulatory framework for the establishment of the 24 
Maine Wate r Trust, which must be designed to ensure a safe and plentiful drinking water 25 
supply to all residents of the State by regulating the use of groundwater for commercial 26 
purposes under laws that establish the absolute control and dominion of the State over al l 27 
groundwater supplies in the State.  The commissioner must, on or before January 15, 28 
2019, report the recommendations of the working group to the joint standing committee 29 
of the Legislature having jurisdiction over environment and natural resources matter s, 30 
which may report out a bill to implement those recommendations to the First Regular 31 
Session of the 129th Legislature.  32
