SUMMARY  12 
This bill makes a number of changes to the Competitive Skills Scholarship Program 13 
administered by the Department of Labor, in cluding the following.  14 
1.  It doubles the current employer contribution rate to the Competitive Skills 15 
Scholarship Fund.  16 
2.  It allocates an additional $3,000,000 on an ongoing basis from the Competitive 17 
Skills Scholarship Fund for the purpose of doubling the number of participants enrolled 18 
from the number of currently enrolled participants.  19 
3.  It dedicates 25% of the additional allocated funds per fiscal year for the use of 20 
program participants who are also participants in the federal supplemental nutriti on 21 
assistance program administered by the State pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, 22 
Title 22, section 3104.  23 
4.  It makes changes to the criteria used to determine approved education and training 24 
under the Competitive Skills Scholarship Program, includi ng consideration of employer 25 
input, changing demographics and traditional industries in the State in which innovations 26 
and new technologies are creating a demand for skilled workers.  27 
5.  It requires that a participant be given information about and assista nce in applying 28 
for the family development account program under Title 10, chapter 110, subchapter 4 -A. 29 
6.  It also adds a requirement that a participant be offered comprehensive career 30 
planning services prior to the program’s development of an individual career plan with 31 
the participant.  32
