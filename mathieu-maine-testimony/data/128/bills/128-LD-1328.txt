 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:  1 
Sec. 1.  23 MRSA §1353 is enacted to read:  2 
§1353.  Bicycle warning signs  3 
1.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 4 
following terms have the following  meanings.  5 
A.  "Bicycle warning sign" means a yellow retroreflective diamond -shaped sign that 6 
contains an image of a bicycle.  7 
B.  "Lane of substandard width" means a lane of a public way that the department 8 
determines is too narrow for a motor vehicle and a bicycle to safely travel side by 9 
side. 10 
C.  "Placard" means a yellow retroreflective rectangular sign that contains 11 
information for users of a public way and is used in conjunction with and placed 12 
underneath a bicycle warning sign.  13 
D.  "Public way" means a state highway or a state aid highway.  14 
2.  Bicycle warning signs.  The department shall place a bicycle warning sign on a 15 
public way in the following manner.  16 
A.  At the beginning of a public way in which all or a portion of the public way has a 17 
speed limi t over 35 miles per hour and a lane of substandard width, the department 18 
shall place on the same side of the public way as the lane of substandard width a 19 
bicycle warning sign with a placard that states "FULL LANE USE."  20 
B.  At the intersection between a pu blic way under paragraph A and a major public 21 
way or a collector and feeder route of the public way under paragraph A, the 22 
department shall place on the same side of the public way as the lane of substandard 23 
width a bicycle warning sign with a placard that  states "FULL LANE USE."  24 
3.  Municipalities.  A municipality shall place bicycle warning signs in accordance 25 
with subsection 2 on town ways within the municipality.  26 
4.  Rules.  The department may adopt routine technical rules as defined by Title 5, 27 
chapter  375, subchapter 2 -A to carry out the provisions of this section.  28

Sec. 4.  Department of Transportation to determine priority in sign 18 
placing.  The Department of Transportation shall determine the priority of the public 19

SUMMARY  24 
This bill requires the Department of Transportation and municipalities to place 25 
bicycle warning sig ns on public ways that have speed limits over 35 miles per hour and 26 
have lanes in which a motor vehicle and a bicycle cannot safely travel side by side. The 27 
bill also requires state income tax returns to include a voluntary checkoff of $5 or more to 28 
help p ay for the signs.  29
