§4981.  Lead abatement program  5 
1.  Program.  The Maine State Housing Authority shall establish and admin ister a 6 
program that provides assistance, including grants, for the abatement of lead paint 7 
hazards in residential housing, referred to in this subchapter as "the program." Program 8 
funds may be used to match federal funds available for lead abatement.  The  program is 9 
subject to the following conditions.  10 
A.  A grant may be made to a municipality that has administered a lead hazard 11 
program grant in the previous 5 years.  12 
B.  Program funds, provided as grants to municipalities, must be distributed on a 13 
basis pr oportional to the geographical distribution of children who have been 14 
determined to have lead poisoning, as defined in Title 22, section 1315, subsection 5 - 15 
C, to the extent reasonably practicable, for the most recent year such information is 16 
available.  17 
C.  The Maine State Housing Authority shall establish eligibility standards for 18 
participation in the program, including, but not limited to:  19 
(1)  Housing units or housing projects occupied by residents with incomes up to 20 
100% of the area median income as esta blished by the United States Department 21 
of Housing and Urban Development, whether the housing is owned by an 22 
individual or a for -profit or nonprofit entity, are eligible;  23 
(2)  Annual rent for a housing unit or housing project receiving project funds may 24

is eligible; and  29 
(4)  An owner of housing is eligible as long as the owner is not in arrears on any 30 
property taxes owed for that housing and has not been cited for more than 4 31 
violations of a building code or safety code on that housing within the prior 2 32 
years. 33

SUMMARY  29 
This bill establishes a residential housing lead abatement program, administered by 30 
the Maine State Housing Authority.  The purpose of the program is to provide grants to 31 
municipalities that have administered a lead abatement program and to housing units and 32 
housing projects th at meet specified eligibility standards.  Priority for lead abatement 33 
projects funded by the program is given to residences occupied by children who have 34 
tested positive for lead poisoning.  35 
The program is funded by a real estate transfer tax surtax of $0.3 0 per $500 of the 36 
value of property transferred by deed or property in which a controlling interest is 37 
transferred.  38
