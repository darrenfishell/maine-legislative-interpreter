 Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:  1 
Sec. 1.  36 MRSA §5219 -OO is enacted to read:  2 
§5219 -OO.  Credit for long -distance commuters  3 
1.  Definitions.   As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 4 
following terms  have the following meanings.  5 
A.  "Commute" means travel by means of a motor vehicle owned, leased or rented by 6 
an individual between the individual's primary residence and the individual's primary 7 
place of employment.  The distance traveled must be comput ed by using the most 8 
reasonable direct route of travel.  9 
B.  "Primary place of employment" means the location of an individual's employer to 10 
which the individual travels on average at least 3 days per week.  11 
C.  "Primary residence" means the place of residen ce of an individual occupied by the 12 
individual as a home.  13 
2.  Credit allowed.   An individual who has a round -trip commute of more than 50 14 
miles is allowed a credit against the taxes imposed by this Part in an amount equal to 25¢ 15 
per mile for miles commuted  during the tax year and the amount of tolls paid for the 16 
commute.  17 
3.  Limitation.   The credit allowed under this section does not apply to miles or tolls 18 
for which a deduction is claimed under the Code, Section 162 or for which the individual 19 
is compensat ed by the individual's employer.  The credit may not exceed $7,500 for miles 20 
commuted and $500 for tolls paid.  21 
SUMMARY  22 
This bill provides an income tax credit for an individual with a round -trip commute 23 
of at least 50 miles between the individual's primary  residence and primary place of 24 
employment.  The credit is equal to 25¢ per mile plus tolls.  The credit does not apply to 25 
miles or tolls for which the individual claims a deduction from federal income tax or if 26 
the individual is compensated by the employe r. 27
