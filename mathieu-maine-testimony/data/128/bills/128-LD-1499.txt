SUMMARY  3 
This bill is a concept draft pursuant to Joint Rule 208.  4 
This bill proposes to revise the laws regarding recreational marijuana sales and use 5 
to: 6 
1.  Change the state licen sing authority from the Department of Agriculture, 7 
Conservation and Forestry to the Department of Administrative and Financial Services, 8 
Bureau of Alcoholic Beverages and Lottery Operations;  9 
2.  Clarify the licensing preferences for medical marijuana dispe nsaries and 10 
caregivers under the Marijuana Legalization Act;  11 
3.  Establish a provisional license to allow marijuana dispensaries licensed under the 12 
Maine Medical Use of Marijuana Act to participate in the retail sale of marijuana prior to 13 
the establishment  of licenses for retail marijuana establishments;  14 
4.  Expand  protections for minors by modeling the laws regarding distribution, 15 
possession and use of marijuana by persons under 21 years of age on the laws prohibiting 16 
the distribution, possession and use of alcohol by persons under 21 years of age;  17 
5.  Establish a special marijuana tax at the rate of 10% on the retail sale of marijuana 18 
by marijuana dispensaries.  This tax is in addition to the sales tax currently imposed on 19 
medical marijuana and is only im posed until the retail sale of marijuana by other 20 
establishments is authorized by the bureau;  21 
6.  Modify the record -keeping, audit and other compliance requirements of a person 22 
licensed as a retail marijuana establishment or retail marijuana social club;  23 
7.  Authorize a nonprofit medical marijuana dispensary to reorganize as a for -profit 24 
entity; and  25 
8.  Increase the sales tax on retail marijuana and marijuana products from 10% to 26 
20%.  27
