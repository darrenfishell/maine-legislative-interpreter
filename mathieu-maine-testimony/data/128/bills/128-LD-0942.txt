Sec. 2.  26 MRSA §628 -A is enacted to read:  7 
§628 -A.  Job applicant compensation questions  8 
An employer may not inquire about the compensation history of a prospe ctive 9 
employee from the prospective employee or a current or former employer or require that 10 
a prospective employee's prior compensation history meet certain criteria, unless an offer 11 
of employment that includes all terms of compensation has been negotiate d and made to 12 
the prospective employee, after which the employer may inquire about or confirm the 13 
prospective employee's compensation history.  14 
This section may be enforced pursuant to section 626 -A.  The civil action provided 15 
pursuant to section 626 -A may be brought to enforce this section by or on behalf of one 16 
or more applicants for employment or by the Department of Labor on behalf of one or 17 
more applicants for employment, and the plaintiff or plaintiffs may also seek judgment 18 
for compensatory damages.  19 
SUMMARY  20 
This bill prohibits an employer from inquiring about a prospective employee's prior 21 
compensation history until after an offer of employment that includes all terms of 22 
compensation has been negotiated and made to the prospective employee.  The bill a lso 23 
prohibits an employer from requiring that a prospective employee's prior compensation 24 
history meet certain criteria.  An employer that violates this law is subject to a fine of not 25 
less than $100 and not more than $500 per violation and is also subject  to a civil right of 26 
action that may be brought by or on behalf of an affected prospective employee by the 27 
Department of Labor or the affected employee.  28
