SUMMARY  21 
This bill shortens the time requirement for the physica l examination of a child 22 
ordered into the custody of the Department of Health and Human Services from 10 days 23 
after the department's custody of the child commences to 3 days and allows a physician's 24 
assistant to conduct the examination.  It also requires, within 60 days after the 25 
department's custody of a child commences, that the child receives an appointment for a 26 
comprehensive medical, dental, educational and mental health assessment by a licensed 27 
pediatrician and a licensed child psychologist and that r eimbursement, including 28 
reimbursement for obtaining and reviewing relevant records, is provided under 29 
MaineCare for the assessment.  The department is directed to adopt routine technical 30
