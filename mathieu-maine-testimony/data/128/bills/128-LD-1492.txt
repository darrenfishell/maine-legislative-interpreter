§8613.  Welcome Center Initiative  29 
The Welcome Center Initiative, referred to in this section as "the initiative," is 30 
established and administered by the department's office of adult education and family 31 
literacy in collaboration with the Office of New Maine rs established in Title 5, section 32 
25001, referred to in this section as "the office."  33 
1.  Welcome centers.  The initiative operates through a welcome center created 34 
within the adult education program of participating school administrative units in 35 
municip alities or regions in the State that have immigrant populations or industries that 36 
are experiencing a shortage of trained workers.  A welcome center educates and provides 37 
vocational training for foreign -trained workers, including foreign -trained profession als, 38 
and matches those workers with employers in the State, focusing on employers in 39 
industries that are experiencing a shortage of trained workers.  40

 1.  Program requirements.  The Department of Education's office of adult education 1 
and family literacy, referred to in this section as "the office," shall administer the program 2 
in collaboration with the Office of New Mainers established in the Maine Revised 3 
Statutes, Title 5, section 25001.  The program must prepare immigrants for identified 4 
workforce needs of employers in the State to a ccelerate entry into livable wage 5 
employment for the immigrants and enhance employer retention of the immigrants by 6 
combining vocation -specific English -language acquisition with training in the specific 7 
vocational areas required by the employers.  Training  must be conducted pursuant to a 8 
grant process under subsection 2.  9 
2.  Training grants.  The office shall establish a statewide competitive grant process 10 
to carry out the purposes of subsection 1.  The amount of a grant under this subsection is 11 
determined by the office based upon the needs of the area to be served and the availability 12 
of funding.  The office shall consider grants to cover all areas of the State with emphasis 13 
on areas with large immigrant populations or where there is a need for skilled work ers 14 
that training under this section may provide.  A proposal for a grant under this subsection 15 
must include:  16 
A.  Intensive vocation -specific English -language and vocabulary classes;  17 
B.  Intensive workforce training classes that include soft skills and acq uaint 18 
immigrants with the culture of the workplace in the State.  For purposes of this 19 
paragraph, "soft skills" means those basic skills necessary to obtain and maintain 20 
employment, such as interviewing and communications skills;  21 
C.  Interviewing immigrant  participants and identifying the education level, 22 
English -language ability, skill set, work experience, qualifications and credentials of 23 
each immigrant participant;  24 
D.  Collaboration with participating employers to:  25 
(1)  Identify the employers' specific workforce needs;  26 
(2)  Identify skills needed for positions required by the employers; and  27 
(3)  Receive input from the employers for the design of the training, including 28 
information on required English -language proficiency, workplace culture, on -site 29 
space  or other vocational training elements such as tools, manuals or site tours;  30 
E.  One or more training courses to prepare the immigrant participants as potential 31 
workers to enter into and retain livable wage employment within 6 to 18 months of 32 
the beginning  of the training; and  33 
F.  Collection and reporting of data including outcomes of employment and job 34 
retention.  35 
The office shall give special consideration to proposals for grants under this 36 
subsection that provide incentives for immigrants to participate i n the training, such as 37 
agreements with employers to provide time and space for on -site training or to pay an 38 
employee the employee's hourly rate to attend the training.  39 
3.  Criteria for applicants.  An applicant for a training grant under subsection 2 40 
must have demonstrated expertise and experience in the following:  41
