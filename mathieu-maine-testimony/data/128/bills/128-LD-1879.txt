3 ยง12942.  Maine Mental  Health Providers Loan Repayment Program
4 1.  Establishment.  The Maine Mental  Health Providers Loan Repayment Program  is 
5 established to increase the number of mental health providers practicing in underserved 
6 areas of  the State.   The  authority shall administer the program and the  fund.
7 2.  Eligibility.  To be eligible to participate in the  program,  an applicant must:
8 A.  Be a mental health  provider;
9 B.  Have a bachelor's,  master's  or doctoral degree  related to the applicant's practice  
10 from a qualified institution of  higher  education;
11 C.  Possess an outstanding student loan  relating to the degree  under paragraph B;
12 D.  Practice in an underserved practice area as defined in rule, including but not 
13 limited to the practice  of mental health care or  social  work:
14 (1)  In a  public or private child welfare  or family service agency;
15 (2)  In a  public or private educational institution, kindergarten to grade 12;
16 (3)  In a  public or private hospital;
17 (4)  In a  public interest law service;
18 (5)  In a  public child care facility;
19 (6)  In a  public service for  individuals  with disabilities;
20 (7)  In a  public service for  the elderly;
21 (8)  In a  public service for  veterans;  or
22 (9) At an organization  exempt from taxation under the United States  Internal 
23 Revenue Code, Section 501(c)(3);
24 E.  Submit an application  to the authority, which must  include but is not limited to 
25 information concerning  academic performance,  awards and special honors and 
26 community involvement;  and
27 F.  Sign a statement  of intent to work  as a mental health provider in an underserved 
28 practice area in the State for a minimum  of 5 years after acceptance into the program 
29 in a form acceptable to the authority.
30 3.  Maximum amount;  participants.  The authority may annually accept up to 3 
31 participants from  the applications  submitted in accordance with subsection 2.  The 
32 maximum amount available  to each participant is $5,000 per year for a period  of up to 5 
33 years.

28 SUMMARY
29 This bill establishes and provides funding  for the Maine Mental Health Providers 
30 Loan Repayment Program to be administered by the Finance  Authority of Maine. Under 
31 the program, mental health providers may have portions  of their student  loans repaid 
32 annually for up to 5 years, as long as the participants meet certain criteria, including 
33 agreeing to practice  in an underserved practice area for at least 5 years after acceptance
