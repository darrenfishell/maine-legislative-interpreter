SUMMARY  28 
This bill amends the definition of "qualified legal interpretor" in the laws governing 29 
communication services f or deaf persons, hard -of-hearing persons and late -deafened 30 
persons in court to include in the definition a person who possesses qualifications, 31 
certifications or credentials to interpret in court proceedings as established by the 32 
Supreme Judicial Court.  T he bill prohibits discrimination on account of sexual 33 
orientation in jury selection.  The bill amends the requirements for record keeping for 34 
briefs filed in the Supreme Judicial Court.  It requires the preservation of briefs in all 35 
cases decided, permits the transfer of the briefs in electronic format and requires the clerk 36
