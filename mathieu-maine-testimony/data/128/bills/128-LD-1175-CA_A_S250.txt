17 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms

19 1. Family or household member. "Family or household member" has the same 
20 meaning as in Title 19-A, section 4002, subsection 4.

22 12-A.

24 written order issued by a court pursuant to this chapter prohibiting and enjoining a named 
25 person from purchasing, possessing, receiving or having in that person's custody or 
26 control any firearms. 
27 4. Law enforcement officer. "Law enforcement officer" has the same meaning as in 
28 Title 17-A, section 2, subsection 17. 
29 5. Restrained person. "Restrained person" means the person who is the subject of a 
30 gun violence restraining order.

§402. Petition, notice and hearing; evidence considered; evidentiary standard 
2 A law enforcement officer or a family or household member of a person may file a 
3 petition requesting that a court, after notice and a hearing, issue a gun violence restraining 
4 order enjoining the subject of the petition from purchasing, possessing, receiving or 
5 having in .the person's custody or control any firearms for a period of 6 months. · 
6 1. Petition. A petition for a gun violence restraining order must describe the 
7 number, types and locations of any firearms believed by the petitioner to be in the 
8 possession, custody or control of the person who is the subject of the petition. 
9 2. Hearing; evidence considered. In determining whether grounds for the issuance 
IO of a gun violence restraining order exist, the court shall hold a hearing to consider all 
11 evidence of the following: 
12 A. A recent threat or act of violence by the subject of the petition directed toward the 
13 subject of the petition or another person; 
14 B. A violation of a protection from abuse order issued pursuant to Title 19-A, 
15 chapter IO I that is in effect at the time the court is considering the petition; 
16 C. A recent violation of an unexpired protection from abuse order issued pursuant to 
17 Title 19-A, chapter 101; and 
18 D. A pattern of acts or threats of violence within the past 12 months, including, but 
19 not limited to, acts or threats of violence by the subject of the petition directed toward 
20 the subject of the petition or another person. 
21 For the purposes of this subsection and subsection 3, "recent" means within the 6 months 
22 prior to the date the petition is filed. 
23 3. Additional evidence of increased risk for violence. In addition to the evidence 
24 considered pursuant to subsection 2, in determining whether grounds for the issuance of a 
25 gun violence restraining order exist, the court may also consider any other evidence of an 
26 increased risk for violence, including, but not limited to: 
27 A. The unlawful and reckless use, display or brandishing of a firearm by the subject 
28 of the petition; 
29 B. The history of use, attempted use or threatened use of physical force by the 
30 subject of the petition against another person; 
31 C. Any prior arrest of the subject of the petition in this State or in another 
32 jurisdiction for any Class A, Class B or Class C crime; 
33 D. Any history of a violation by the subject of the petition of an emergency 
34 protection from abuse order issued pursuant to Title 19-A, chapter 101; 
35 E. Documentary evidence, including, but not limited to, police reports and records of 
36 convictions, of either recent criminal offenses by the subject of the petition that 
37 involve controlled substances or alcohol or ongoing abuse of controlled substances or 
38 alcohol by the subject of the petition; and 
39 F. Evidence ofrecent acquisition of firearms, ammunition or other deadly weapons.

4. Evidentiary standard; burden on petitioner. For the purposes of a hearing 
under this section, the petitioner has the burden of proving by clear and convincing 
evidence that: 
A. The subject of the petition poses a significant danger of personal injury to the 
subject of the petition or another person by having a firearm in the subject of the 
petition's possession, custody or control; and 
B. A gun violence restraining order is necessary to prevent personal injury to the 
subject of the petition or another person because less restrictive alternatives have 
been tried and found to be ineffective or because less restrictive alternatives are 
inadequate or inappropriate based on the circumstances of the subject of the petition. 
5. Additional evidentiary requirements if petitioner is law enforcement officer. 
If the petitioner is a law enforcement officer, the petitioner must prove the requirements 
of subsection 4 by clear and convincing evidence that includes the petitioner's personal 
knowledge or observations of the subject of the petition or the petitioner's personal 
knowledge or observations of the other person to whom the subject of the petition poses a 
significant danger of personal injury. 
6. Continuance. A hearing conducted pursuant to this section or pursuant to section 
403, subsection 3 or 4 may be continued upon a showing of good cause. Notwithstanding 
any provision of this chapter to the contrary, a gun violence restraining order issued 
pursuant to this chapter remains in effect during any period of continuance. 
§403. Issuance of order; order contents; termination; renewal 
1. Issuance. If, after conducting a hearing in accordance with section 402, the court 
finds by clear and convincing evidence that the requirements for a gun violence 
restraining order are met. the court shall issue an order prohibiting the subject of the 
petition from purchasing, possessing, receiving or having in that person's custody or 
control any firearms for a period of 6 months. A gun violence restraining order issued 
pursuant to this section expires 6 months from the date of issuance, except if terminated 
pursuant to subsection 3 or renewed pursuant to subsection 4. 
2. Order contents. A gun violence restraining order issued in accordance with 
subsection 1 must include the following: 
A. A statement of the grounds supporting the issuance of the order; 
B. The date and time at which the order expires; 
C. A statement regarding the required filing of the receipt resulting from the 
surrender of firearms pursuant to section 404, subsection 2, paragraph C; and 
D. The following statement: 
"To the restrained person: This order will last until the date and time noted above. If 
you have not done so already, you must surrender all firearms that you possess or 
have in your custody or control in accordance with the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 
15, section 404. While this order is in effect, you may not purchase, possess, receive 
or have in your custody or control any fireann. Pursuant to the Maine Revised 
Statutes, Title 15, section 403, subsection 3, you have the right to request one hearing

3. Termination of order. A restrained person may submit one written request at 
any time during the effective period of the order for a hearing to tenninate the order. 
A. At the time that the court issues an order under subsection I. the court shall 
inform the restrained person that the restrained person is entitled to one hearing to 
request a tennination of the order pursuant to this subsection and shall provide the 
restrained person with a form to request a termination hearing. 
B. Upon the written request of a restrained person for a hearing to terminate the 
order, the court shall conduct a hearing in accordance with section 402 to determine 
whether there continues to exist clear and convincing evidence that the evidentiary 
requirements in section 402, subsection 4 supporting the issuance of the order are 
satisfied. If the court finds after the hearing that the evidentiary requirements are no 
longer satisfied by clear and convincing evidence, the court shall terminate the order. 
4. Renewal of order. A law enforcement officer or a family or household member 
of a restrained person may file a petition requesting the renewal of a gun violence 
restraining order at any time within the 3 months before the order expires. 
A. The court may, after notice and a hearing in accordance with section 402, renew 
the order if the petitioner proves, by clear and convincing evidence, that the 
evidentiary requirements in section 402, subsection 4 supporting the issuance of the 
order continue to be satisfied. 
B. If pursuant to paragraph A the court detennines that the evidence supports 
renewal of the order, the court shall issue a gun violence restraining order that 
replaces the prior order and that meets the requirements of subsections 1 and 2. 
§404. Effect of order; service of order; surrender of firearms 
1. Effect of order; service of order. Upon issuance of a gun violence restraining 
order, the court shall order the restrained person to surrender to a local law enforcement 
agency all firearms in the restrained person's possession, custody or control. A restrained 
person may not purchase, possess, receive or have in that person's custody or control any 
firearms while the order is in effect. 
If the restrained person is not present in court at the time the order is issued or renewed. 
the order must be personally served on the restrained person by a law enforcement officer 
if the restrained person can reasonably be located. 
2. Surrender of firearms. A restrained person ordered to surrender all firearms 
pursuant to subsection I shall, upon request of a law enforcement officer and after service 
of the gun violence restraining order. immediately surrender to the officer all fireanns in 
the restrained person's possession, custody or control. 
A. A law enforcement officer who serves a gun violence restraining order on a 
restrained person that indicates that the restrained person possesses or has in the 
restrained person's custody or control any firearms shall request that the restrained 
person immediately surrender all firearms to the officer.

24 This amendment, which is the minority report of the committee, replaces the bill and 
25 creates a judicial process for the imposition of a gun violence restraining order whereby a 
26 court is authorized to order a person to surrender that person's firearms for a 6-month 
27 period of time when it has been proved by clear and convincing evidence that the person 
28 poses a danger of causing personal injury to that person or to another person. A person 
29 who possesses a firearm in violation of a gun violence restraining order commits a Class 
30 D crime, the sentence for which must include a prohibition on the possession of firearms 
31 for a 5-year period of time. The amendment also adds an appropriations and allocations 
32 section to provide funding to address anticipated new cases and required information 
33 technology updates within the Judicial Department and to provide funding to the 
34 Department of Public Safety for increased storage costs relating to retaining firearms 
35 under a gun violence restraining order process.
