13 'Sec. 1. 5 MRSA §20054 is enacted to read: 
14 §20054. Integrated treatment and recovery for families 
15 The department shall develop and fund housing-based programs employing evidence-
16 based strategies in a holistic approach to recovery for vulnerable families affected by 
17 substance abuse. The programs must treat mothers affected by substance abuse who have 
18 at least one child under 10 years of age when entering the program in an integrated family 
19 care model. The programs must provide to a mother in the program stable housing and 
20 comprehensive services that support recovery and unification with that mother's children. 
21 Comprehensive services provided include all of the following: care coordination, health 
22 care, child care, early childhood education, home supports, after-school programming, 
23 parenting education, treatment for mental health and substance abuse, postsecondary 
24 education, community-based transportation and employment supports. The programs 
25 must include coordinated data collection to assess long-term recovery outcomes, 
26 transition to employment and independence for mothers participating in the programs. 
27 Sec. 2. Department of Health and Human Services to issue requests for 
28 proposals. No later than January l, 2019, the Department of Health and Human 
29 Services shall issue requests for proposals to develop, pursuant to the Maine Revised 
30 Statutes, Title 5, section 20054, 2 housing-based programs for mothers affected by 
31 substance abuse who have children under 10 years of age. 
32 Sec. 3. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and 
33 allocations are made.

23 This amendment replaces the bill, which is a concept draft. It requires the 
24 Department of Health and Human Services to issue a request for proposals for 2 housing-
25 based programs for mothers affected by substance use disorder who have at least one 
26 child under 10 years of age when entering the program. The mothers in the programs 
27 must receive stable housing and comprehensive services that support recovery and 
28 unification with tlieir children. The services provided include care coordination, health 
29 care, child care, early childhood education, home supports, after-school programming, 
30 parenting education, treatment for mental health and substance use disorder, 
31 postsecondary education, community-based transportation and employment supports. 
32 The programs must include data collection to assess long-term recovery outcomes, 
33 transition to employment and independence. 
34 The amendment also adds an appropriations and allocations section for child care and 
35 contracted services in the integrated treatment and recovery for families program.
