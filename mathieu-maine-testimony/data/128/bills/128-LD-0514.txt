EXPUNGEMENT OF RECOR DS 4 
ยง2321.  Expungement of records of nonviolent crimes  5 
1.  Class E, D and C crimes.   A person convicted of a Class E, Class D or Class C 6 
crime may petition the court in which the conviction was recorded to expunge the record 7 
of the conviction after a period of 5 years from the completion of the sentence.  The court 8 
shall order all records of the conviction expunged if:  9 
A.  The defendant has not been convicted of a crime in this State or any other 10 
jurisdiction since the conviction subject to the petition and has no formal charging 11 
instrument for a crime pending in this State or any other jurisdiction; and  12 
B.  The crime is n ot a crime under Title 17 -A, chapter 11, 12, 25 or 35 or the crime 13 
involved violence, domestic violence or had as an element of the offense a victim 14 
who was 17 years of age or younger or a victim who was 65 years of age or older.  15 
2.  State Bureau of Identi fication.   Following receipt of a court order for 16 
expungement under subsection 1, the Department of Public Safety, State Bureau of 17 
Identification shall make the necessary arrangements with the identification division of 18 
the Federal Bureau of Investigation to have all references to the expunged crime deleted 19 
from the Federal Bureau of Investigation's identification record and any state materials 20 
returned to the contributing agency.  21 
SUMMARY  22 
This bill allows a person convicted of a Class E, Class D or Class C crime to petition 23 
the court where the person was convicted to expunge all records of the crime 5 years after 24 
the completion of the person's sentence.  Expungement is not available for persons who 25 
have subsequent convictions or pending criminal charges; for  crimes involving violence 26 
or sex offenses; or crimes that had as an element of the offense victims who were minors 27 
or are 65 years of age or older.  28
