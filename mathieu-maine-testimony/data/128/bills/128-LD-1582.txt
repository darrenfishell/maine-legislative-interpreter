SUMMARY  32 
This bill makes several changes to the State's fish and wildlife enforcement laws.  33 
The bill:  34 
1.  Makes  it legal for a person to use wireless, digital imaging technology as a means 35 
to comply with a trap tending requirement;  36 
2.  Makes it clear that hunting with thermal imaging equipment is illegal;  37 
3.  Creates a $20 penalty for each quart over the 2 -quart li mit on smelts;  38 
4. Amends terminology in the law governing apprentice hunter license restrictions;  39 
 
 Page 9 - 128LR0691(01) -1 
 5.  Makes it illegal to fraudulently obtain registrations in addition to licenses and 1 
permits that are provided by the department;  2 
6.  Makes failing to stop f or a law enforcement officer while operating a snowmobile 3 
or watercraft a Class D crime consistent with the provision applying to all -terrain 4 
vehicles;  5 
7.  Makes the airbow a legal hunting implement when used within certain parameters. 6 
It defines "airbow,"  "crossbow" and "hand -held bow";  7 
8.  Specifies that, in addition to a firearm, a person convicted of a domestic violence 8 
offense may not own, possess or have under that person's control a crossbow or 9 
muzzleloader or archery equipment;  10 
9.  Requires all edib le meat from bear, deer or moose to be presented for registration 11 
along with evidence of gender.  It allows these animals to be dismembered for ease of 12 
transportation;  13 
10.  Strengthens language within the prohibition of abuse of another person’s 14 
property b y removing reference to certain stated activities such as hunting, fishing or 15 
trapping to allow the Department of Inland Fisheries and Wildlife to enforce the law 16 
against persons who abuse another person’s property but who may not be involved in an 17 
activit y such as hunting, fishing or trapping;  18 
11.  Sets August 1st as the beginning date for bear baiting season; and  19 
12.  It replaces the requirement that a boat operator have a license from the 20 
department to carry passengers for hire with a requirement that th e operator successfully 21 
complete a boating safety course provided by a national association of boating law 22 
administrators approved by the commissioner.  23
