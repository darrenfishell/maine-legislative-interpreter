3.  Chairs.   The first member appointed to the commission under subsection 2, 5 
paragraph A is the Senate chair.  The first member appointed to the commission under 6 
subsection 2, paragraph B is the House chair.  7 
4.  Staff assistance.   Within the limits of its budget, the comm ission is authorized to 8 
contract with and employ staff members to assist the commission in carrying out its 9 
duties.  10 
5.  Quorum.   For purposes of holding a meeting, a quorum is a simple majority of all 11 
members appointed to the commission under subsection 2.   A quorum must be present to 12 
start a meeting or to vote but not to continue or adjourn a meeting.  13 
6.  Terms; vacancies.   Members of the commission serve for a term of 2 years and 14 
may be reappointed.  A vacancy must be filled in the same manner as the orig inal 15 
appointment.  Members may continue to serve until their replacements are designated.  A 16 
member may designate an alternate to serve on a temporary basis.  17 
7.  Consultation.   Whenever the commission considers it appropriate, the 18 
commission may seek the a dvice of experts in fields related to its duties.  19 
8.  Powers and duties.   The commission:  20 
A.  Shall meet at least 4 times annually.  The first meeting of the commission must be 21 
held no later than 45 days after the effective date of this section;  22 
B.  Shall review, compile and analyze scientific and other reports and publications 23 
relating to coastal and coastal watershed hazards, including, but not limited to, 24 
increased storm surges, extreme precipitation and other extreme weather events, 25 
projected sea level rise and increased river flooding and storm water runoff.  The 26 
commission shall make available on a publicly accessible website all publications and 27 
information reviewed or compiled for review by the commission;  28 
C.  Shall review United States Department of  Commerce, National Oceanic and 29 
Atmospheric Administration and other scientific agency projections of coastal storm 30 
inundation and flood risk to determine the appropriate information, data and property 31 
risks to be considered by the commission in developing  a unified plan and 32 
recommendations for action under paragraphs D and E;  33 
D.  Shall develop a unified plan to address and prepare for current and projected 34 
future risks posed by coastal and coastal watershed hazards identified by the 35 
commission; and  36 
E.  Sha ll make findings and develop recommended legislation, rules and other actions 37 
in accordance with the unified plan developed under paragraph D to address and 38 
prepare for coastal and coastal watershed hazards identified by the commission.  39

SUMMARY  21 
This bill establishes the Maine Coastal Risks and Hazards Commission, the purpose 22 
of which is to make findings and develop and submit to the Legislature recommendations 23 
regarding the ac tions to be taken by the State to address and prepare for coastal and 24 
coastal watershed hazards identified by the commission, including, but not limited to, 25 
increased storm surges, extreme precipitation and other extreme weather events, projected 26 
sea level  rise and increased river flooding and storm water runoff.  On or before 27 
November 1, 2019, and every 5 years thereafter, the commission must submit a report to 28 
the joint standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over environmental 29 
and natur al resources matters containing its findings and recommendations, including any 30 
draft legislation to address identified coastal and coastal watershed hazards.  31
