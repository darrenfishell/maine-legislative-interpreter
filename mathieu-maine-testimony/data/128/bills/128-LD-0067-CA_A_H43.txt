9 COMMITTEE AMENDMENT" "to H.P. 54, L.D. 67, Bill, "An Act To Improve 
10 Insurance Coverage for First Responders Answering a Call to Duty" 
11 Amend the bill by striking out the title and substituting the following: 
12 'Resolve, To Study Shortcomings in Workers' Compensation Insurance for 
13 Emergency First Responders' 
14 Amend the bill by striking out everything after the title and before the summary and 
15 inserting the following: 
16 'Sec. 1. Workers' Compensation Board to study shortcomings in workers' 
17 compensation insurance coverage for emergency first responders. Resolved: 
18 That the Workers' Compensation Board shall conduct a study to examine shortcomings in 
19 workers' compensation insurance coverage for emergency first responders and develop a 
20 proposed solution or solutions to ensure that emergency first responders receive workers' 
21 compensation insurance coverage that provides coverage from the time an emergency call 
22 is received until the time that the emergency first responders' duties have ended, 
23 otherwise known as tone-to-tone coverage. The study must include, but is not limited to, 
24 examining the following options: using an existing state insurance program to cover 
25 emergency first responders; establishing a state-run insurance program to provide more 
26 comprehensive insurance coverage for emergency first responders; and requiring an 
27 automatic administrative law judge review of any denied workers' compensation claims 
28 involving emergency first responders; and be it further 
29 Sec. 2. Report. Resolved: That the Workers' Compensation Board shall report 
30 its findings and recommendations to the Joint Standing Committee on Labor, Commerce, 
31 Research and Economic Development by December 15, 2017. The committee may report 
32 out a bill in response to this report in the Second Regular Session of the 128th 
33 Legislature; and be it further 
34 Sec. 3. Appropriations and allocations. Resolved: That the following 
35 appropriations and allocations are made.
