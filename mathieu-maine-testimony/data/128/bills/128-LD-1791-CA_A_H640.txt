18 SUMMARY 
19 This amendment, which is the majority report of the committee, establishes an 
20 executive committee of the Maine Lobster Marketing Collaborative to take certain 
21 actions on behalf of the collaborative and specifies which actions are prohibited. The 
22 amendment retains the provision in the bill that removes the October 1, 2018 repeal of the 
23 section of law that establishes the collaborative and instead changes the repeal date to 
24 October 1, 2021. The amendment continues through 2021 the surcharge amounts on 
25 various types of licenses to fund the collaborative that have been in place since 2016. The 
26 amendment directs the Commissioner of Marine Resources to investigate whether the 
27 surcharges assessed on a wholesale seafood license with lobster permits or a 
28 supplemental lobster transportation license may be amended to reflect the amount of 
29 lobster bought, sold, shipped or transported by the license holder or a class of license 
30 holders and to provide a report of recommendations to the joint standing committee of the 
31 Legislature having jurisdiction over marine resources matters by January 15, 2019.

Minor cost increase -Other Special Revenue Funds 
Any additional costs to the Department of Marine Resources (DMR) and the Maine Lobster Marketing Collaborative 
(MLMC) as a result of changes to the organizational structure of the MLMC, timeline for operations of the MLMC, 
continuation of license surcharges to fund the MLMC and requirements that DMR investigate and report on possible 
changes to certain license surcharges used to fund the MLMC are anticipated to be minor and can be absorbed within 
existing budgeted resources.
