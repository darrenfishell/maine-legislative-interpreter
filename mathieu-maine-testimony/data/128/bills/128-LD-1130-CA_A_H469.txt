COMMITTEE AMENDMENT "A" to H.P. 793, L.D. 1130, Bill, "An Act To 
Provide Traffic Safety Education in Schools" 
Amend the bill in section 1 by striking out all of §9952 (page 1, lines 11 to 14 in 
L.D.) and inserting the following: 
'§9952. Traffic safety education required 
1. Age-appropriate traffic safety education. A school administrative unit shall 
provide age-appropriate traffic safety education to students in accordance with this 
chapter. The age-appropriate traffic safety education must: 
A. Meet the minimum requirements specified in section 9953; 
B. Be at least one hour in length; and 
C. Be provided annually to at least 4 different grade levels from kindergarten to 
grade 12.' 
Amend the bill by inserting after section 1 the following: 
'Sec. 2. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and 
allocations are made. 
EDUCATION, DEPARTMENT OF 
Learning Systems Team Z081 
Initiative: Provides one-time funds for 90% of the cost to local school administrative 
units to implement and annually provide age-appropriate traffic safety education for at 
least 4 different grade levels between kindergaiten and grade 12.

This bill includes a one-time General Fund appropriation of $62,914 in fiscal year 2017-18 to the Leaming Systems 
program within the Department of Education for 90% of the cost to school administrative units to implement and 
annually provide age-appropriate traffic safety education to students in at least 4 different grade levels between

Fiscal Detail and Notes 
This fiscal note makes the following assumptions: 
1) Each kindergmten to grade 8 school will develop age-appropriate cmTiculum for traffic education safety for 
students in a grade lower than grade 4 
2) One-half of kindergarten to grade 8 schools will obtain age-appropriate curriculum for 2 grade levels between 
grade 4 and grade 8 that meets the requirements of this legislation at no cost and will devote one hour acclimating 
themselves to the curriculum. The remaining schools will have a presenter come into the school to present the traffic 
and safety education program at no cost to the school. 
3) One-half of the public high schools in the State will obtain age-appropriate curriculum for 1 grade level between 
9th and 12th grade that meets the requirements of this legislation at no cost and will devote one hour acclimating 
themselves to the curriculum. The remaining high schools will have a presenter come into the school to present the 
traffic and safety education program at no cost to the school.
