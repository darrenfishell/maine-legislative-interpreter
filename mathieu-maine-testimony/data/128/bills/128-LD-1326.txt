SUMMARY  22 
This bill:  23 
1.  Repeals the provision making possession of a hypodermic apparat us a crime;  24 
2.  Creates a medical assistance exemption from criminal liability, including arrest, 25 
prosecution or incarceration, for a person who seeks medical assistance for that person's 26 
self or another who experiences a drug overdose if the grounds for t he arrest, prosecution 27 
or incarceration were obtained as a result of the person's seeking medical assistance;  28 
3.  Removes Department of Health and Human Services rule -making authority over 29 
establishing community -based drug overdose prevention programs and adds statutory 30 
criteria for the establishment of the programs; and  31 
4.  Appropriates to the Department of Health and Human Services $75,000 for 32 
syringe exchange programs and $50,000 for naloxone hydrochloride distribution through 33 
community -based drug preven tion programs for each year of the current biennium.  34
