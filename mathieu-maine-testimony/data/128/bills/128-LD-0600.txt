 Sec. 1.  Grants for law enforcement drug -detecting dogs.  Resolved:  That 1 
the Commissioner of Public Safety shall establish a one -year grant program to assist law 2 
enforcement agencies to acquire and train drug -detecting dogs as provided in this section.  3 
1.  Definitions.   As used in this resolve, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 4 
following terms have the following meanings.  5 
A.  "Drug -detecting dog" or "dog" means a dog that is trained to detect the presence 6 
of drugs or other illegal substances and to alert the law enforcement officer who is the 7 
handler of the dog to the presence of the drugs or illegal substances.  8 
B.  "Grant program" means the program established under this section to provide 9 
assistance to law enforcement agencies as provided in sub section 2.  10 
C.  "Law enforcement agency" means the State Police, a sheriff's department or a 11 
municipal police department.  12 
D.  "Law enforcement officer" means any person who by virtue of public 13 
employment is vested by law with a duty to make arrests for crim es, whether that 14 
duty extends to all crimes or is limited to specific crimes.  15 
2.  Grant program.   The grant program may provide grants for one year to up to 10 16 
law enforcement agencies to acquire and train drug -detecting dogs.  The grant program 17 
may be use d by the law enforcement agency to pay for the cost of the dog; training for the 18 
dog, including but not limited to drug detection academy training; and drug detection 19 
training and certification for the law enforcement officer who is the handler of the dog,  20 
including but not limited to any overtime pay necessary for training for the officer.  The 21 
grant program may not be used by the law enforcement agency to pay for food or 22 
veterinary care for the dog or the cost of any certifications for the dog beyond the initial 23 
training.  24 
3.  Application form; criteria for awarding grants.   The Commissioner of Public 25 
Safety shall adopt rules to specify the application form for the grant program and the 26 
criteria for awarding grants.  The commissioner shall make applications  available on 27 
January 1, 2018 and grant awards by April 1, 2018.  Rules adopted pursuant to this 28 
subsection are routine technical rules as defined by the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, 29 
chapter 375, subchapter 2 -A. 30 
SUMMARY  31 
This resolve directs the Commiss ioner of Public Safety to establish a grant program 32 
for one year to assist law enforcement agencies to acquire and train drug -detecting dogs.  33

limited to drug detection academy training; and drug detection training and certification 36 
for the law enforcement officer who is the handler of the dog, including but not limited to 37 
any overtime pay  necessary for training for the officer.  The grant program may not pay 38 
for the cost of food or veterinary care for the dog or the cost of any certifications for the 39 
dog beyond the initial training.  The resolve directs the Commissioner of Public Safety to  40 
adopt routine technical rules to specify the application form for the grant program and the 41
