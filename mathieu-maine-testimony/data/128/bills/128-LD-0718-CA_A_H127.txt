Amend the bill in section 2 in §4320-K by striking out all of subsection 4 (page 3, 
lines 4 to 39 and page 4, lines 1 to 20 in L.D.) and inserting the following: 
14. Duties. The advisory committee shall: 
A. Study any federal health care legislation enacted by the United States Congress to 
make changes to the federal Affordable Care Act as defined in section 14 or 
otherwise determine the State's opportunities for health care reform; 
B. Determine the State's role in implementation of any federal health care legislation; 
C. Determine the impact of federal health care legislation on existing state law and 
programs that provide access to health care to residents of this State; 
D. Advise the Governor and the Legislature regarding the implementation of any 
federal health care legislation and the interests of residents of this State; 
E. Serve as a liaison between the State Government and the Federal Government 
with regard to federal health care legislation; 
F. Consider any issue related to the implementation of federal health care legislation 
agreed upon by the majority of the advisory committee; and 
G. Make recommendations for any changes in policy or law to improve access to or 
affordability of health care for residents of this State.'

Provides appropriations 
of $12,000 in each of fiscal years 2017-18 and 2018-19 to the Legislature for the operating 
and support costs of the Maine Health Care Reform Implementation Advisory Committee during the Legislative 
session and for the operating costs during the interim. Also provides an Other Special Revenue Funds allocation of 
$500 in the event the committee receives grants and other nongovernmental funds. As required in the bill, the funds 
provided to the committee are appropriated and allocated to a new account separate from all other funds of the 
Legislature. 
Fiscal Detail and Notes 
Any additional costs to the Department of Professional and Financial Regulation and the Department of Health and 
Human Services to implement the provisions of this bill are expected to be minor and can be absorbed within existing 
budgeted resources.
