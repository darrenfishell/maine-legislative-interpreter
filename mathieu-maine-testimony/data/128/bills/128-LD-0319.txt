Sec. 1.  Authorization of bonds.  The Treasurer of State is authorized, under the 6 
direction of the Governor, to issue bonds in the name and on behalf of the State in an 7

Sec. 2.  Records of b onds issued; Treasurer of State. The Treasurer of State 11 
shall ensure that an account of each bond is kept showing the number of the bond, the 12 
name of the successful bidder to whom sold, the amount received for the bond, the date of 13 
sale and the date when p ayable.  14 
Sec. 3.  Sale; how negotiated; proceeds appropriated.  The Treasurer of State 15 
may negotiate the sale of the bonds by direction of the Governor, but no bond may be 16 
loaned, pledged or hypothecated on behalf of the State.  The proceeds of the sale of the 17 
bonds, which must be held by the Treasurer of State and paid by the Treasurer of State 18 
upon warrants drawn by the State Controller, are appropriated solely for the purposes set 19 
forth in this Act.  Any unencumbered balances remaining at the completion o f the project 20 
in this Act lapse to the Office of the Treasurer of State to be used for the retirement of 21 
general obligation bonds.  22 
Sec. 4.  Interest and debt retirement.  The Treasurer of State shall pay interest 23 
due or accruing on any bonds issued under t his Act and all sums coming due for payment 24 
of bonds at maturity.  25 
Sec. 5.  Use of bonds.  Bond proceeds may be used for grants for investment in 26 
economic revitalization, environmental enhancement and community betterment projects 27 
along the clean and health y rivers of the State in a fashion that preserves and enhances 28 
economic activity, environmental protection and quality of life for the people of the State, 29 
except that use of the proceeds is subject to the following conditions and requirements.  30 
1.  Any mun icipality abutting a river and any nonprofit corporation organized in 31 
whole or in part for the purpose of waterfront economic development, environmental 32 
enhancement and community development in the State may apply to the Department of 33 
Economic and Communit y Development for funding from this grant program.  34 
2.  For a municipality -sponsored or nonprofit corporation -sponsored project to be 35 
eligible to receive grant funding, the project must have as its purpose:  36 
A.  The development or restoration of public parks , walkways and other public spaces 37 
along river corridors situated either in downtown, developed riverfront locations or 38 
between and connecting 2 or more such locations;  39

 Sec. 10.  Referendum for ratification; submission at election; form of 1 
question; effective date.  This Act must be submitted to the legal voters of the State at 2 
a statewide election held in the month of November following passage o f this Act.  The 3 
municipal officers of this State shall notify the inhabitants of their respective cities, towns 4 
and plantations to meet, in the manner prescribed by law for holding a statewide election, 5 
to vote on the acceptance or rejection of this Act b y voting on the following question:  6 
"Do you favor a $25,000,000 bond issue to fund a grant program 7 
administered by the Department of Economic and Community 8 
Development to invest in economic, environmental and community 9 
development projects along rivers in Maine and to be matched by at least 10 
$50,000,000 in private and public funds?"  11 
The legal voters of each city, town and plantation shall vote by ballot on this question 12 
and designate their choice by a cross or check mark placed within a corresponding square 13 
below the word "Yes" or "No."  The ballots must be received, sorted, counted and 14 
declared in open ward, town and plantation meetings and returns made to the Secretary of 15 
State in the same manner as votes for members of the Legislature.  The Governor shall 16 
review the returns.  If a majority of the legal votes are cast in favor of this Act, the 17 
Governor shall proclaim the result without delay and this Act becomes effective 30 days 18 
after the date of the proclamation.  19 
The Secretary of State shall prepare and fu rnish to each city, town and plantation all 20 
ballots, returns and copies of this Act necessary to carry out the purposes of this 21 
referendum.  22 
SUMMARY  23 
The funds provided by this bond issue, in the amount of $25,000,000, will be used to 24 
fund a grant program to  invest in projects along the State's rivers that contribute to 25 
economic, environmental and community development and revitalization, promote 26 
economic activity, protect the environment and enhance quality of life for Maine people.  27
