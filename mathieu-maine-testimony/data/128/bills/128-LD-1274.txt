 §7506.  Healthy Maine secondary payor; state health plan; subrogation rights  1 
1.  Secondary payor.  Healthy Ma ine serves as a secondary payor to any health 2 
insurance plan in which a beneficiary may be responsible for a beneficiary's health care 3 
expenses. The total of Healthy Maine's payment and all other payments may not exceed 4 
the amount that Healthy Maine would pay if it were the only payor.  5 
2.  State health plan.  Healthy Maine serves as a state health plan that pays for 6 
designated supplemental health care services for Medicare beneficiaries, except that 7 
Healthy Maine may not pay for services:  8 
A.  That are cover ed by Medicare Parts A, B and D;  9 
B.  That are covered by a Medicare Advantage plan that a beneficiary has with an 10 
entity other than Healthy Maine; or  11 
C.  That would have been paid by Medicare Parts B or D had the beneficiary 12 
purchased those optional Medica re coverages, unless:  13 
(1)  Healthy Maine has an agreement with the federal Department of Health and 14 
Human Services, Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services that requires it to 15 
pay for services that would have been paid under Medicare Parts B or D; or  16 
(2)  Healthy Maine offers a Medicare Advantage plan and the beneficiary 17 
voluntarily enrolls in that plan.  18 
3.  Rights of subrogation.  Healthy Maine has full rights of subrogation, ahead of the 19 
rights of a workers' compensation or other insurer or health care  plan, including the right 20 
to bring an independent lawsuit or to intervene in a lawsuit filed by a beneficiary, in order 21 
to recover health care costs from collateral sources for which the beneficiary has a right 22 
of action for compensation against the perso n or entity that caused the beneficiary's 23 
illness or injury. Healthy Maine may assert a lien against any proceeds recovered by the 24 
beneficiary.  25 
Healthy Maine may recover health care payments from any other collateral source, such 26 
as a health insurance plan , health benefit plan or other payor that is primary to Healthy 27 
Maine.  28

SUMMARY  16 
This bill es tablishes a single -payer health care system in the State, effective July 1, 17 
2020, that finances health care services for most Maine residents. The bill directs the 18 
Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services and the Joint Standing 19 
Committee on In surance and Financial Services to jointly submit during the 2017 20 
legislative interim legislation to fully implement the single -payer system.  21
