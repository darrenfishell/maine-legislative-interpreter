 Emergency preamble. Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 1 
become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and  2 
Whereas, the people of the State of Maine in November 2016 passed into law the 3 
Marijuana Legalizati on Act, which establishes a system of licensing for marijuana 4 
cultivation, testing, processing and retail sale to enable persons 21 years of age or older to 5

SUMMARY  11 
This bill amends the Marijuana Legalization Act with respect to the requirements for 12 
retail marijuana testing facilitie s and the testing of marijuana and marijuana products as 13 
follows.  14 
1.  It clarifies requirements for the testing of marijuana and marijuana products, 15 
including the mandatory testing of retail marijuana and retail marijuana products for 16 
homogeneity and canna binoid profiles.  17 
2.  It clarifies licensing and operational requirements for retail marijuana testing 18 
facilities, including requirements that such facilities:  19 
A.  Be certified by the Department of Health and Human Services, Maine Center for 20 
Disease Control  and Prevention, be accredited pursuant to standard ISO/IEC 17025 21 
of the International Organization for Standardization by a 3rd -party accrediting body, 22 
or be certified, registered or accredited by an organization approved by the 23 
Department of Agriculture,  Conservation and Forestry, and be licensed or approved 24 
by the municipality in which the facility is physically located.  It authorizes issuance 25 
of a provisional retail marijuana testing facility license to a testing facility that has 26 
applied for but not y et obtained certification, registration or accreditation from a 3rd - 27 
party accrediting body or an approved organization;  28 
B.  Meet all operational and technical requirements established by rule and follow 29 
testing protocols, standards and criteria established  by rule for testing of different 30 
forms of marijuana and marijuana products, determining batch size, sampling, testing 31 
validity and approval and disapproval;  32 
C.  Offer remediation and retesting of marijuana or a marijuana product that fails a 33 
test and disp ose of used, unused and waste marijuana and marijuana products; and  34 
D.  Maintain records of all business transactions and testing and report the results of 35 
certain tests to the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry.  36 DEPARTMENT TOTAL - ALL FUNDS  $84,722  $87,355

Fiscal Detail and Notes
The bill includes a General Fund appropriation to the Department of Health and Human Services of $84,722 in fiscal 
year 2017-18 and an Other Special Revenue Funds allocation of 87,355 in fiscal year 2018-19 for one Environmental Specialist III position to certify the licensed retail marijuana testing facilities. The bill also includes a General Fund appropriation to the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry of $91,032 in fiscal year 2017-18 and an Other Special Revenue Funds allocation of $92,301 in fiscal year 2018-19 for one Consumer Protection Inspector position to inspect licensed retail marijuana testing facilities for compliance. The Other Special Revenue Funds allocations will be funded by license fees authorized by the Marijuana Legalization Act, 7 MRSA, §2448, subsection 10. The General Fund appropriations are required because there will not be sufficient revenue from license fees in fiscal year 2017-18.Fiscal Note Required: Yes
