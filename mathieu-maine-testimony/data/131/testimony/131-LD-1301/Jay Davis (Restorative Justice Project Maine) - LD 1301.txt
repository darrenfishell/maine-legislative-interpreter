Restorative Justice Project Maine
LD 1301
26 March 2023
Senator Beebe-Center, Representative Salisbury and members of the Criminal Justice 
and Public Safety Committee. My name is Jay Davis, I live in Belfast and I write in 
support of LD 1301 on behalf of The Restorative Justice Project Maine.
RJP Maine believes that a priority of incarceration must be preparing an inmate for 
release in the best condition possible. Our work with the Maine Coastal Regional 
Re-entry Center in Belfast has given us important insights into best practices aimed at
achieving that. LD 1301 includes several initiatives that contribute to the low 
recidivism rate of those leaving MCRRC.
I believe the center employs a staff person who fits the description of a transition 
coordinator; so should every jail. Residents — that’s what they’re called at MCRRC 
— should be eligible to attend funerals of family members if they have demonstrated 
that they can do so safely and obediently. The re-entry center has a small lending 
library, as all jails should, and residents have access to learning programs as well.
Creation of a fund at the commissary for indigent residents is a noble idea that should 
be adopted throughout the system.,
Slowly, achingly sometimes, the corrections system is becoming more humane and 
effective. LD 1301 is a logical step in that direction, and RJPM urges your support.
Thank you.
Jay Davis
Belfast
For Restorative Justice Project Maine
