Amy Eshoo
Maine Climate Action Now
LD 1074
Senator Rotundo, Representative Sachs, and Honorable members of the Committee 
on Appropriations and Financial Affairs, my name is Amy Eshoo. I am a resident of 
North Yarmouth and Director for Maine Climate Action Now, a statewide coalition of
grassroots organizations who support transformative action in response to the climate 
crisis. We hold social, racial, and economic justice as critical to a just and equitable 
transition from a fossil fuel economy to a clean energy economy.
With the housing crisis exploding across the country and here in Maine we are 
concerned for all Mainers who may struggle for various reasons with secure housing. 
According to the Maine Association of Realtors, existing home sales in Maine in 
2022 dropped nearly 17.5%, but the median sales price rose 12% to $335,000. This 
puts purchasing a home out of reach for many in our state even if the stock were 
available.
Across the state case managers and rapid housing workers are struggling to secure 
housing for the number of clients that are getting referred to their services. The 
recidivism rate for our unhoused population and the rate at which social workers leave
the field is skyrocketing due to our lack of housing stock. Currently it would cost the 
state of Maine $150M annually to provide enough housing stock for everyone in 
need.
Maslow's hierarchy of needs1 shows that one of the most basic needs is shelter. 
Housing is our primary means of survival and our single most important asset to 
ensure we live healthy productive lives. The state of Maine has had a housing crisis 
for a long time with an increased number of people moving to our state for various 
reasons. The problem of securing affordable housing has become not only an issue for
Mainers experiencing chronic homelessness but also for our working class Mainers. 
Unless Mainers can meet their basic needs we will not be able to thrive, work 
together, and combat the climate crisis.
MCAN supports LD 1074 as the authorization of $100,000,000 in bonds designated 
for the Maine State Housing Authority to build new affordable housing for 
low-income households is crucial to providing homes for our state’s working families 
and those in need. Construction of new structures and adaptive reuse of existing 
structures for affordable housing will relieve the stress on Mainers without adequate 
housing and relieve some of the burden on the growing unhoused community.
Also of concern to us in our fight for climate justice is that newly constructed 
affordable housing, or the reuse of existing structures, be built or retrofitted to meet 
net zero greenhouse gas emissions in an effort to meet our needs to reduce resource 
consumption. We feel this should be added to the bill.
We urge you to vote OUGHT TO PASS for LD 1074. Thank you.
