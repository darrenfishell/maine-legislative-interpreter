John Cangelosi, PAEMST 2019, STEM Teacher, Bangor High School
 
In Support Of
 
L.D. 1291
An Act to Establish a Grant Program to Provide for Statewide and Equitable Access 
to Experiential Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics Education
 
April 11, 2023
 
Senator Curry, Representative Roberts, and distinguished members of the Committee 
on Innovation, Development, Economic Advancement and Business, we from Bangor
High School support L.D. 1291, “An Act to Establish a Grant Program to Provide for 
Statewide and Equitable Access to Experiential STEM Education.”
 
Bangor High School is the only public high school in Bangor, receiving students from
7 elementary and 2 middle schools, as well as from neighboring rural communities 
that do not have secondary schools. With a 9-12 enrollment of 1,151 students, it is the
largest high school in the region, and third largest in the State of Maine. Bangor High 
School is accredited by the New England Association of Schools and Colleges. The 
percentage of students receiving free and reduced lunch is 52%. The City of Bangor is
a rural service center with a population of approximately 32,000 people. It is home to 
government, housing, medical, retail, financial, transportation, communications, and 
entertainment services for a region of northern Maine that includes nearly 30,000 
square miles and only 664,000 people. This puts the City of Bangor in the center of 
the second-most rural Congressional District in the entire United States. The median 
household income for the metro- Bangor area in 2020 was $42,115. (Bangor High 
School Profile 2022- 2023). 
The STEM program at Bangor High School has been highly successful at the state, 
national, and international level and these successes depend on student participation 
in the Maine State Science Fair. Some notable awards include place winners at the 
Maine State Science Fair leading to participation at the International Science and 
Engineering Fair, Intel Science Talent Search ‘Global Good’ winner, nearly annual 
representation at the National Junior Science and Humanities Symposium, and 
multiple Stockholm Junior Water Prize National winners. The Bangor STEM 
Academy requires a two-year research component including summer internships. 
Presently there are 40 students enrolled in the program in grades 9 – 12. 
The University of Maine and The University of Southern Maine awarded full tuition 
scholarships through the 2023 Maine State Science Fair. Four of the scholarship 
winners were Bangor High School students who plan on attending The University of 
Maine. Scholarship Awards through the Maine State Science Fair motivate students to
pursue scientific research and continue their studies within the state.  
Activities within the MSSF experience also provide our students, and all Maine 
students, the chance to express themselves through creative ideas, making risky 
decisions with their experiments or designs, finding the lessons in “failures”, and 
practice working through set-backs, all culminating in advancing their knowledge 
about a subject or process.  We ask our students to think about how what they are 
doing during this process would look on a college resume.  They are doing something 
that all college admission staff and technical schools are looking for: learning how to 
solve problems.  Each of their projects have a uniqueness and outcome that is all their 
own.  They can demonstrate first-hand to others what they have learned about the 
process of science and engineering and apply that to future work and passions that 
they have developed.  It would be difficult to replace the entire MSSF experience with
anything similar in meaning, scale, or rewards. 
We at Bangor High School urge the members of the committee vote to invest in 
experiential and project-based STEM education facilitated through participation in the
Maine State Science Fair. 
Participation in the Maine State Science Fair is the goal of every student in the STEM
program at Bangor High School.  This participation provides unique opportunities to a
variety of students in several ways.  It is a long-term goal that requires the 
development of many life-long skills including planning, organization, commitment, 
perseverance, resourcefulness, and focus.  There are few activities that cover this 
range of attributes that our students learn in a supportive and team environment.  The 
MSSF also engages students in friendly competition where the rewards at the end are 
provided at both the personal and team level.
