Senator Beebe-Center, Representative Salisbury, and esteemed members of the Joint 
Standing Committee on Criminal Justice, 
My name is Dr. Tim Hughes, and I am writing in support of the concept of LD 1626, 
“An Act to Standardize Requirements Between Boards of Visitors for County Jails 
and Department of Corrections Correctional Facilities,” but with serious reservations 
regarding the bill as it is currently written.. 
I am a retired physician and a board member of the Restorative Justice Project Maine,
but I am writing to you as a member of the Knox and Waldo Counties Board of 
Visitors for close to the past three years. There are four of us who have willingly 
volunteered to take on this important obligation. We are two licensed clinical social 
workers, a retired criminal justice professor, and myself, a retired physician. Current 
law dictates seven members (or is it 14 members, since we are a combined Board for 
both Knox and Waldo Counties?), but we have been unable to find more than the four
of us. We are also supposed to visit each jail facility at least once per year but have 
been able to arrange to visit one facility only twice in three years and we have never 
visited the other facility, the stated reason being “understaffing” such that we cannot 
be accommodated.
The new bill mandates additional duties and responsibilities for the Board (with no 
mention of remuneration), including four visits per year to the the facility and an 
annual report to the Legislature for each County Board. It no longer allows counties to
combine boards and also mandates a specific knowledge, gender and profession for 
individual members.
My concern is that we in the Waldo and Knox Counties Board of Visitors already 
cannot find enough volunteers to fill the Board roster, and with added responsibilities 
and specifications, it will be completely impractical. 
There are various ways to solve this, but as it stands the bill appears to be 
unworkable.
Sincerely,
Tim Hughes MD
