Senator Chipman, Representative Williams, and Members of the Transportation 
Committee:
My name is Nathan Reimensnyder and I am a resident of New Gloucester. I am 
writing to testify in strong support of Rep. Bell’s proposed amendment to LD 209, 
“Resolve, to Direct the Department of Transportation to Implement the 
Recommendation of the Portland to Auburn Rail Use Advisory Council.”
This is an pivotal point in our time regarding the protection and preservation of access
to natural spaces in Maine. As more land is developed for private use, less is available
for those around it. Having this rail trail through my community would be an 
incredible resource for me, my family, and my community. 
New Gloucester is mostly rural with little to no available shoulder space and mostly 
45 MPH speed limits. This greatly limits our ability to enjoy riding bikes and running 
in our area and especially so when including our 6 year old. The proposed trail would 
provide a safe space for us to take family bike rides without having to worry about 
cars or trucks at all times. Growing up in the area, the Royal River was an amazing 
feature to our town. One incredible aspect of the proposed trail would be its proximity
to the river and the views and access it would provide. The views it would provide 
along this natural resource are some of the best in southern Maine, and access would 
allow for activities such as fishing and canoeing in the summertime and skating in the 
winter time. Overall, from a personal standpoint, my family and I would utilize this 
trail on a daily basis!
From a community standpoint, this trail would allow free access to natural spaces to 
those who aren't fortunate enough to have private access of their own. It would serve 
as a social hub, providing a physical space for our community to strengthen and grow.
Also, with the increasing popularity of outdoor activities like biking and running, this 
trail would be an attraction to visitors who would patronize our local businesses.
I firmly believe this is an incredible opportunity for our community and Maine as a 
whole!
Thank you for listening!
Nathan Reimensnyder
