24 April 2023
Senator Beebe-Center, Representative Salisbury and members of the Criminal Justice 
and Public Safety Committee. I’m Jay Davis, I live in Belfast and I write in support of
LD  433 on behalf of the Restorative Justice Project Maine, which is based in Belfast.
As you are well aware, bills to close Long Creek Development Center in South 
Portland , Maine’s only detention facility for youths, have been aired before your 
committee in recent years. The basic question involved is whether the Department of 
Corrections, which serves the punishment-minded legal system in Maine, is the right 
entity for helping craft better futures for adjudicated youths. 
We know that Commissioner Liberty has made impressive changes at DOC, including
hiring a Restorative Justice specialist and seriously reducing the population at Long 
Creek. We applaud those initiatives. We also know that the commissioner has begun a
process to identify community sites where youths can be housed, educated and trained
in a non-carceral manner.
Our work at Long Creek and with youths who are referred to us by the courts, JCCOs 
and others through a contract with DOC, leads us to support the closing of Long 
Creek post-haste and creation of a system for working with youths that emphasizes 
personal development, counseling and training. It is certainly possible to us that an 
agency other than DOC should at least be considered to operate those facilities.
Passage of LD 433 would be a transparent and forward-thinking step in that direction.
We would hope that the many Restorative Justice-practicing organizations across the 
state would be invited to work with the committee that will recommend the 
appropriate agency for improving the lives of adjudicated youths.
Please give an Ought to Pass recommendation to this important bill. Just considering 
a non-punitive, non-prison environment for youths is an indication that Maine “gets” 
the idea that youths who have been in trouble can find their way out, with help.
Thank you for your consideration.
Jay Davis
Belfast
Member, RJPM Legislative Committee
