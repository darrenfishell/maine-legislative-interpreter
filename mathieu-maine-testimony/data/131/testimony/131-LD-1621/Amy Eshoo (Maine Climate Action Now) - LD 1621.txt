Amy Eshoo
Maine Climate Action Now
LD 1621
Senator Brenner, Representative Gramlich and Honorable members of the Committee 
on Environment and Natural Resources, my name is Amy Eshoo. I am a resident of 
North Yarmouth and Director of Maine Climate Action Now (MCAN), a statewide 
coalition of grassroots organizations who support transformative action in response to 
the climate crisis. We hold social, racial, and economic justice as critical to the 
transition to zero carbon emissions by 2030. 
MCAN uses the lens of equity to determine our focus on steps towards a just 
transition. In doing so we have found that our work in Maine is hampered by the lack 
of a definition in statute of “environmental justice populations”. LD 1621 directs the 
Department of Environmental Protection to define “environmental justice 
populations”. While it is well recognized that there are populations that bear the brunt 
of the climate crisis in myriad ways we need a legal definition reflecting this and thus 
allowing fair and equitable considerations and engagement by the DEP as a start.
MCAN supports LD 1621. We see the necessity of this bill toward ensuring 
meaningful participation by environmental justice communities in Maine. We believe 
that everyone needs to be involved if we are to combat the climate crisis. The first 
step is for communities to have the opportunity to be heard; to know that their 
concerns will impact the decisions being made and for them to be able to see how 
their input is influencing the decision making. It is important that all Mainers are able 
to participate in protecting their lives, futures and communities from environmental 
burdens.
The appropriations and allocations outlined in LD 1621 for the DEP would allow for 
the facilitating of inclusion of environmental justice populations by funding translator 
services, travel expenses, appropriate venue rental fees; along with a Public Service 
Coordinator to ensure the outreach and participation of those communities often 
overlooked in our processes which deeply impact their lives.
LD 1621 offers procedural equity for environmental justice populations and frontline 
communities through enabling equitable participation in the Department of  
Environmental Protection’s decision-making processes. This is necessary for 
achieving authentic equity in Maine’s environmental protection. We urge you to vote 
OUGHT TO PASS for LD 1621. Thank you.
