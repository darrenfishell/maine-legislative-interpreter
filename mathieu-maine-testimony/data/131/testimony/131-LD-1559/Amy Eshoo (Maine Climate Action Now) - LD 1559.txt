Amy Eshoo
Maine Climate Action Now
LD 1559
Senator Chipman, Representative WIlliams, and Honorable Members of the 
Committee on Transportation, my name is Amy Eshoo. I am a resident of North 
Yarmouth and Director of Maine Climate Action Now, a statewide coalition of 
grassroots social justice and climate justice organizations who support transformative 
action in response to the climate crisis. 
LD 1559 addresses climate justice by advancing the climate goals of our state to 
reduce our greenhouse gas emissions, 54% of which comes from the transportation 
sector; but also by making transit more accessible, affordable and convenient for all 
Mainers. Public transit is part of the solution to reducing our emissions as well as 
bringing community benefits especially to rural Mainers. Being able to conveniently 
travel to jobs, health care, or family economically is as important as the transition 
away from fossil fuels. 
MCAN believes that now is the critical time to invest in solutions such as those in LD
1559. We acknowledge this is an important level of spending but one needed to 
combat the climate crisis and to do so in an equitable way. The one time appropriation
of $25M will help (among other things) to expand transit planning assistance for rural
communities. The one time appropriation of $13.6M will cover the expiring federal 
operating subsidies for transit which will ensure that it can continue to operate at its 
current level of service.
The bill’s call for amendments to the Sensible Transportation Policy Act (Title 23) to 
include clear commitments to public transportation and our state's climate goals is 
necessary to advance for the reasons stated above. And diversifying the Public Transit
Advisory Council (PTAC) membership by including labor, immigrant communities, 
and youth is bringing the voices of those most likely to benefit from increased public 
transit services to the decision making table. Transportation Justice is one thread of 
Climate Justice. LD 1559 promotes equity and justice with investments in our futures.
We urge you to vote OUGHT TO PASS for LD 1559. Thank you.
