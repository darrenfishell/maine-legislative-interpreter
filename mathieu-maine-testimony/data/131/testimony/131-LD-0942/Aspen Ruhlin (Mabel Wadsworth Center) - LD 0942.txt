LD 942 Public Testimony
An Act to Ensure the Collection of Complete Information by Adding a 3rd Option for Gender 
on State Forms
First, I would like to thank the chairs of this committee, Senator Carney and Representative 
Moonen, along with the rest of the Judiciary Committee for hearing my testimony today. I 
would also like to thank Representative Sheehan for presenting this bill and the cosponsors 
for cosponsoring. My name is Aspen Ruhlin, I use they/them pronouns, and I am speaking 
today both as a representative of Mabel Wadsworth Center and as an individual. For anyone 
who may not be familiar with our work, we are a feminist, not-for-profit, independent sexual 
and reproductive health center in Bangor. Among our array of services, we are well-known for
providing gender-affirming care to the trans community, and healthcare more generally to the 
entirety of the LGBTQ+ community. We have become known as a safe and competent 
healthcare provider for Mainers who too often find their care neglected. I am also trans and 
queer myself. Today, both I as an individual and Mabel Wadsworth Center are urging you to 
vote in support of LD 942.
In my work at Mabel Wadsworth Center, I have helped countless transgender patients
navigate name and gender marker changes. As laws have been updated since my time 
starting at the Center, I have seen barriers to making these changes lower and 
subsequently improve the wellbeing of our clients. The changes to updating the 
gender marker on birth certificates, both to remove outdated and archaic requirements
and to include an X marker, was a cause of celebration for many patients.
As a nonbinary transgender person myself, this has been a cause of celebration for 
me. Finally, I have the ability to be correctly identified on my legal documents as who
I am. All should be well, right? While the option of an X marker/third gender 
category was added because Maine anti-discrimination law demands it, many state 
forms do not have this option. This leads to two major issues: first, Mainers who have
updated their gender marker to X on legal documents have inconsistencies between 
those legal documents and other state forms. Second, forms that do not have a third 
gender option are in violation of existing state law.
These inconsistencies have led to issues for our clients, with confusion from state 
agencies like MaineCare because of the mismatch between legal documents and state 
forms. If a person with an X marker on all their legal documents is applying for 
MaineCare and they are required to pick between M and F, what are they supposed to 
do? This brings us to another issue our clients have faced because of this: harm from 
being misgendered or being forced to misgender themselves. It is already harmful 
enough to be misgendered, and that is made worse by having jumped through the 
many hoops to have documents updated only to still be misgendered.
This is something I have run into with my own insurance company. While I have 
private insurance and this bill is looking at state forms specifically, 
anti-discrimination laws still apply, and we know that laws regarding state forms will 
have a ripple effect. I deserve to be honored and recognized for who I am at all levels.
All transgender people deserve this, because all people deserve this. 
For the dignity and wellbeing of nonbinary trans people, we urge you to vote in support of LD 
942. To ensure compliance with existing Maine law, we urge you to vote in support of LD 942.
Thank you.
