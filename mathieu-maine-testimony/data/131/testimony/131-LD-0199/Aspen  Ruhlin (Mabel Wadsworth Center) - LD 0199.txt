LD 199 Public Testimony
An Act to Improve the Health of Maine Residents by Removing Exclusions to the MaineCare 
Program
First, I would like to thank the chairs of this committee, Senator Baldacci and Representative 
Meyer, along with the rest of the Health and Human Services Committee for hearing my 
testimony today. I would also like to thank Speaker Talbot Ross for presenting this bill and the
cosponsors for cosponsoring. My name is Aspen Ruhlin, and I am the Community 
Engagement Coordinator with Mabel Wadsworth Center in Bangor. We are an independent, 
not-for-profit, feminist health center that provides a wide range of services to our community, 
including prenatal care, abortion care, STI testing, gender-affirming hormone therapy, 
contraceptives, and primary care. Today, I am here on behalf of Mabel Wadsworth Center to 
ask that you vote in support of LD 199 to improve the health and healthcare access of all 
Maine residents.
As a part of our commitment to supporting our clients to overcome barriers to 
accessing healthcare, we created a Client Advocate position in 2019. Part of that role 
includes helping with insurance enrollment, including with MaineCare. I held that 
position for three years, and still currently oversee much of that work. Because of this,
I am uniquely situated to speak to the benefits to an individual’s wellbeing that having
access to healthcare can have. I have had the pleasure to watch the health of countless 
members of our community improve because of their newfound access to healthcare, 
particularly primary care and preventative services that keep patients out of our 
overburdened emergency departments. 
Unfortunately, there is one group of people in our community that has been kept from the 
human right to healthcare here in Maine: immigrants. Unjust and cruel restrictions on 
MaineCare have kept our newer neighbors from accessing healthcare that not only benefits 
the health of individuals, but of entire communities. These restrictions are not based in saving
money; CDC research shows that lack of access to healthcare reduces worker productivity 
and results in profit loss. Further, people just deserve access to healthcare. Yes, this is the 
smart thing to do in terms of finances and strengthening our work force, but more importantly 
it is the right thing to do.
In the Client Advocate role at Mabel Wadsworth Center, there was never anything 
more frustrating than working with a client who didn’t qualify for MaineCare because
they were an immigrant and who didn’t make enough money to get a plan through the
Marketplace. With these current barriers to accessing MaineCare, it is like we don’t 
expect immigrants to be people who can break a bone, get cancer, or have a stroke. It 
is like we don’t expect immigrants to be people. It is cruel.
Today, Mabel Wadsworth Center is urging you to vote in support of LD 199. We are not 
asking this simply because it makes economic sense. We believe that all Mainers deserve 
access to healthcare, and improving access to MaineCare is one important step to 
accomplish this. Thank you.
