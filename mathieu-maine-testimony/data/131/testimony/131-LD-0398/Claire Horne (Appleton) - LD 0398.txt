LD 398
Testimony in Support of LD 398 “An Act to Make Agricultural Workers and Other 
Related Workers Employees Under the Wage and Hour Laws”
April 17, 2023
Senator Tipping, Representative Roeder, and members of the Committee on Labor 
and Housing,
My name is Claire Horne and I’m a resident of Appleton, ME. I am writing today to 
encourage you to join me in support of LD 398: “An Act to Make Agricultural 
Workers and Other Related Workers Employees Under the Wage and Hour Laws”. 
The fact that agricultural workers are treated differently than workers in other 
industries is categorically unfair. This inequity has its roots in racist policies tracing 
all the way back to enslavement. Knowing this, it’s no surprise that 25% of 
farmworkers in Maine live in poverty, which is 4.5x more than other Maine workers. 
One of the reasons I love living in Maine is the prevalence of local farms, and the 
ability to have our food grown in nearby communities. I think that’s something many 
Mainers value. However, the state of Maine has fallen short in offering protections for
the farm workers who we depend on for local produce and goods. One way we can 
amend this inequity in our state is to update our laws to require that farm workers are 
given overtime pay, and paid a fair wage. I support LD 398 because I want to know 
that agricultural workers around the state are given the same rights as other workers in
the state by having the right to overtime pay. 
When we provide a way for the 13,440 hired farm workers in Maine to have access to
fair pay, this will be positive for the entire state. Since measures like this have been 
taken in other states like California, Colorado, Hawaii, Minnesota, Maryland, New 
York, Oregon, and Washington, we know that overtime pay ends up not being a 
considerable financial strain for farmers, and doesn’t increase retail prices 
considerably either. This bill helps to provide protections so that farm workers are not
taken advantage of, and can instead have a more fair standard of treatment at work.
Sincerely,
Claire Horne
