LD 714
20 April 2023
Dear Senator Beebe-Center, Representative Salisbury and other distinguished 
members of the Criminal Justice and Public Safety Committee,
Last year a bi-partisan group of legislators passed an expanded Good Samaritan law 
that severely restricted police from arresting those at the scene of a drug overdose to 
which emergency personnel, including officers, had been called. Testimony 
supporting the bill rightly claimed that numerous lives had been lost because people 
were disinclined to call police for fear of being arrested for being present at a drug 
scene.
The Good Samaritan law placed saving lives ahead of making arrests, which makes 
eminent good sense. Now, though, LD 714 has been introduced that would give 
police the right to arrest those at the scene who have illegal weapons or traffic and 
furnish illegal drugs, which would, of course, mean a lifesaving scene  becomes a 
police investigation. So much for the primacy of saving lives over making arrests. 
The Restorative Justice Project Maine, based in Belfast, strongly opposes LD 714. 
Maine police have plenty of weapons at their disposal without turning potential 
tragedy into law enforcement overkill.
Sincerely,
Jay Davis
Belfast
Member, RJPM Legislative Committee
