Aspen Ruhlin

Community Engagement Coordinator
Mabel Wadsworth Center
LD 535 An Act to Increase Access to Necessary Medical Care for Certain Minors
Public Testimony
I would like to begin by thanking the chairs Senator Carney and Representative 
Moonen along with the rest of the Judiciary Committee for hearing my testimony 
today. I would also like to thank Representative Sheehan for sponsoring this bill and 
the cosponsors for cosponsoring. My name is Aspen Ruhlin, and I am speaking to you
today as a representative of Mabel Wadsworth Center in Bangor. Our center provides 
a range of sexual and reproductive healthcare services, the most relevant of which in 
this hearing is gender-affirming hormone therapy and general trans-competent 
healthcare services. Additionally, I have an extensive employment history of working 
with youth, ranging from in-home behavioral health services to supervising a weekly 
youth group to leading workshops in school settings. Today, we are here to urge you 
to vote “ought to pass” on LD 535, An Act to Increase Access to Necessary Medical 
Care for Certain Minors.
I will be blunt and name the fact that there are violent attacks against transgender 
people in legislatures across this country happening now, often focused on trans 
youth. Maine has not been shielded from these attacks, but we have a few good things
going for us. Along with existing law that protects trans people and a legislature that 
seems to, largely, recognize our humanity, we have bills like LD 535 that seek to 
improve the lives of trans people.
Trans youth deserve bodily autonomy and the right to self-determination. Allowing 
access to gender-affirming hormone therapy for 16- and 17-year-olds is essential for 
that. At that age, our youth can get their driver’s license, access contraceptives, and 
apply for college. In an ideal world, all youth, including transgender youth, would 
have support from their parents in accessing the care they need. Unfortunately, we do 
not live in an ideal world. We live in a world where too many parents kick out their 
trans children that were brave enough to come out to them, a world where cable news 
stations get away with lying about the realities of gender-affirming care for youth, a 
world where too many people would rather a trans teen be dead than happy and 
affirmed for who they are.
I mentioned that at these ages, youth can already access contraceptives. Hormonal 
contraceptives. At 16, I began accessing hormonal birth control. As someone who 
accessed gender-affirming hormone therapy as an adult, I can say not just with 
professional perspective but personal perspective as well that there is no good reason 
to not allow trans youth to access gender-affirming care. The healthcare that the youth
of Maine are able to access should not be dependent on the politics or support of their 
parents.
Gender-affirming hormone therapy is not just life-saving care, but joyous care. 
Providers of gender-affirming care for minors are trained and need room to offer care 
when indicated. We trust our youth to know their needs and exercise their bodily 
autonomy. We recognize that Maine youth are people, not the property of their 
parents. We at Mabel Wadsworth Center urge you to vote “ought to pass” on LD 535.
Thank you for your time.
