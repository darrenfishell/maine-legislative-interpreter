My name is Jared Rolfe, and I am a 23-year-old resident of Maine, living with 
fibromyalgia. I am writing to express my strong support for LD 1686, which aims to 
eliminate unconstitutional restrictions on access to cannabis and recognize cannabis 
and hemp as food. This bill is crucial not only for my well-being but also for the 
well-being of my mother, who also relies on medical cannabis as a treatment option.
As a fibromyalgia patient, I have experienced chronic pain and a variety of other 
debilitating symptoms that significantly impact my quality of life. Conventional 
pharmaceutical treatments have not provided adequate relief, and in many cases, they 
come with undesirable side effects. Medical cannabis, on the other hand, has been a 
life-changing alternative that has allowed me to manage my symptoms effectively and
live a more fulfilling life.
LD 1686 removes the limitations on the amount of cannabis a qualifying patient may 
possess, cultivate, or harvest for personal medical use. As someone who cultivates 
cannabis for both my personal use and my mother's, these changes are vital. The 
current restrictions make it challenging for us to have a consistent and adequate 
supply of our medicine, and this bill would ensure we have access to the necessary 
amount of medical cannabis to manage our conditions effectively.
The bill also clarifies that hemp, cannabis, and the leaves, stems, flowers, and seeds of
a cannabis plant are considered food protected as a right under the Constitution of 
Maine, Article I, Section 25. This recognition not only helps destigmatize cannabis 
use but also acknowledges its nutritional benefits and its role in our holistic 
well-being.
By removing the tag requirement for cannabis plants under Title 28-B, chapter 3, this 
bill will also reduce the administrative burden on patients like myself and my mother. 
It will enable us to focus on our health and well-being rather than cumbersome 
regulatory processes.
In conclusion, I urge you to support LD 1686, as it is an essential step toward 
ensuring that individuals like my mother and me have access to the medical cannabis 
we rely on for our well-being. This bill will improve the lives of countless Maine 
residents who depend on medical cannabis to manage their health conditions.
Thank you for your time and consideration.
