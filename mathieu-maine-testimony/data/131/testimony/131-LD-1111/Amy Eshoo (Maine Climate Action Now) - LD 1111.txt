Amy Eshoo
Maine Climate Action Now
LD 1111
Senator Lawrence, Representative Zeigler and Honorable Members of the Committee 
on Energy, Utilities and Technology, my name is Amy Eshoo. I am a resident of 
North Yarmouth and Director for Maine Climate Action Now, a statewide coalition of
grassroots organizations who support transformative action in response to the climate 
crisis. Our members include youth, elder, faith, environmental, community, and 
health organizations. We hold social, racial, and economic justice as critical to a just 
transition. 
MCAN calls for equity and justice in the actions we take and policies we support. LD 
1111 is no exception. An abundance of drinking water is one of our state’s greatest 
assets and should be protected for all residents. In the face of increasing global 
drought and a future of increasing demand for clean drinking water, Maine should be 
more active and vigilant in protecting this great asset. Around the world investors and 
corporations are looking to increase their control of freshwater sources for private 
gain instead of public good. This will only lead to pitting those with access to water 
resources against those in need of this basic life necessity. We shouldn’t let this 
happen in our state.
Maine can no longer count on our water without practicing good stewardship as our 
climate rapidly changes and thus our water resources shift. MCAN is asking you for 
water protection for all - to avoid privatization of this life giving resource at the 
municipal level. 
We are currently without strong enough regulation to adequately protect our 
groundwater supplies. As an equity issue we believe in giving communities, which 
may bear the impacts of large-scale water exports, the opportunity to protect and 
steward their watersheds for themselves and for future generations. 
LD 1111 would offer such protection by calling for municipal approval, public 
meetings and public comments for contracts and agreements for the large scale 
extraction and transportation of water. LD 1111 relies on local democracy and local 
decision-making, where the impacts of these decisions are experienced. Calling for a 
3 year limit on a contract or agreement means accountability for both sides.
Maine Climate Action Now urges you to vote Ought To Pass on LD 1111 as a vote 
for equity and justice. Thank you.
