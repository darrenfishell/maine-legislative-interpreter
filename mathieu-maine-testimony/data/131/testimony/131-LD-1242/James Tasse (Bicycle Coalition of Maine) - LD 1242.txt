James Tasse
Bicycle Coalition of Maine
LD 1242
Testimony in Opposition to LD 1242 with Amendments
An Act to Eliminate Certain Motor Vehicle Inspections
Greetings Chairs Chipman and Williams and the distinguished members of the 
Committee on Transportation: 
My name is James Tassé and I am the Assistant Director of the Bicycle Coalition of 
Maine (BCM).  Our mission is to make Maine better and safer for bicycling and 
walking.  On behalf of our more than 20,000 members and supporters, I am 
submitting this testimony in OPPOSITION to LD 1242. The BCM believes that 
repealing the requirements to inspect motor vehicles and make sure that they are safe 
and road-worthy will make Maine’s public ways less safe for all users, including for 
walkers, bicycle riders, and drivers. For this reason, the BCM urges an “Ought Not to 
Pass” report from the committee.  
Motor vehicles of any age can, and often do, have mechanical problems. Brakes wear 
out, lights fail, steering components get loose, wipers get pitted, tires lose tread, 
electronics malfunction. These parts are all critical components of a motor vehicle 
that a driver uses to control speed, see where they are going, and guide the vehicle on 
the road safely. Maine’s inspection program ensures that every motor vehicle meets 
the minimum safe mechanical standards. 
For this reason, vehicle inspections are beneficial. Functioning brakes, good tires, and
working windshield wipers and lights are basic equipment every vehicle on the road 
needs to have to protect public safety. Inspections are also known to extend the life of 
a vehicle by alerting the owner of a problem, so that maintenance can occur before the
vehicle is damaged beyond the point of repair. And most importantly, a vehicle defect
found during an inspection and repaired could save a life. 
For these reasons, the BCM urges the committee to recommend an Ought Not to Pass 
report for LD 1242.
Thank you for your time, and for your service to the state.
James Tassé
Assistant Director
Bicycle Coalition of Maine
