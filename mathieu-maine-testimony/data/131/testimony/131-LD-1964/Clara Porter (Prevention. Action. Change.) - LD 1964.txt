Testimony In Support of LD 1964, An Act to Implement the Recommendations of the
Commission to Develop a Paid Family and Medical Leave Benefits Program
Clara Porter, Director
Prevention. Action. Change.
72 Rackleff Street
Portland, Maine
May 25, 2023 
Dear Senator Tipping, Representative Roeder, and honorable members of the Joint 
Standing Committee on Labor and Housing, 
My name is Clara Porter and I live in Portland. I am testifying in support of paid 
family and medical leave for Maine. 
Three times in my own life I needed and did not have access to paid family leave: 
twice when one of my children was in crisis and again when my spouse was ill and 
unable to care for himself. The emotional, mental, and economic toll of those periods 
persists to this day and with aging parents, there are more times to come.
I run a small business, Prevention. Action. Change. which is dedicated to preventing 
and responding to violence. Our nine teachers are all contract workers. None have 
access to paid family leave through this or their others jobs. None. This bill would 
make this benefit, this right, accessible and affordable to them, impacting every 
person in their lives. 
Passage of paid family leave in Maine will have a direct impact on myself, my 
employees, and the individuals and families with whom we work. Those most 
targeted for and impacted by violence will also benefit significantly from access to 
paid family leave. People experiencing intimate partner violence will have access to 
greater options for support and therefore independence. This bill will also address 
some of the barriers to care which are behind the significant disparities in life span 
and health of Black, Brown, and Indigenous Mainers.
I hope you will support LD 1964. Thank you for listening, I’m happy to answer any 
questions. 
Sincerely, 
Clara Porter
