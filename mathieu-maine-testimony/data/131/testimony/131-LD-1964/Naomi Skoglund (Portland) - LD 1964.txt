Naomi Skoglund
Portland
LD 1964
My name is Naomi Skoglund and I am a small business owner in Portland, Maine. I 
write today in support of  LD 1964, An Act to Implement the Recommendations of 
the Commission to Develop a Paid Family and Medical Leave Benefits Program.
I had my daughter when I was living in California and I was able to receive family 
leave benefits through the state’s short term disability fund, which I had contributed 
to as an employee. That financial support made a monumental difference in my ability
to care for my daughter and take the time I needed to recover from childbirth.
Here in Maine, I am a small business owner. I have had employees go on parental 
leave three times in the past 5 years and as much as I have wanted to support them 
financially, my business has not had the funds to provide maternity leave benefits. 
I offer employee benefits that include basic paid time off, but that would not cover 
very much if they needed surgery or had to care for a loved one for any reason.  I 
have seen employees forgo medical care because they couldn’t afford to miss work. 
This bill would strengthen my ability to support my employees and be competitive as 
an employer.
Thank you for hearing my concerns today. I ask you to support the passage of 
LD1964, An Act to Implement the Recommendations of the Commission to Develop 
a Paid Family and Medical Leave Benefits Program.
