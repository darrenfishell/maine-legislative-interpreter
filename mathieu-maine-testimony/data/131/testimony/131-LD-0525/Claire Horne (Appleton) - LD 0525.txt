LD 525
Testimony in Support of LD 525 “An Act to Protect Farm Workers by Allowing 
Them to Organize for the Purposes of Collective Bargaining”
April 17, 2023
Senator Tipping, Representative Roeder, and members of the Committee on Labor 
and Housing,
My name is Claire Horne and I’m a resident of Appleton, ME. I am writing today to 
encourage you to join me in support of LD 525: “An Act to Protect Farm Workers by 
Allowing Them to Organize for the Purposes of Collective Bargaining”. 
There are many ways that agriculture workers in Maine have less protections and 
rights than workers in other industries. The rights to organize a worker’s union and 
participate in collective bargaining are not protected for farm workers, and therefore 
are very dangerous for them to attempt. If farm workers could have the right to 
organize and join a union, they would be able to negotiate with their employers to 
make their jobs safer and more equitable. With protections from employer 
intimidation and fear of retaliation, farm workers could advocate for fair wages, 
reasonable schedules, safe working conditions, and humane housing accommodations.
Without explicit protection from the state, farm workers can be fired for advocating 
for better conditions and fair labor practices, which I think should be a human right 
for all workers regardless of industry. Giving workers the opportunity to organize 
leads to a healthier, happier, more productive workforce, and agriculture workers 
deserve this just as much as any other Maine worker. 
I believe all workers should have the right to collective bargaining. It is unfair and 
unjust that farm workers do not have the right to speak up and demand essentials like 
safe conditions and fair pay without risking their livelihood, housing, and physical 
safety. I hope you agree, and will support LD 525.
Sincerely,
Claire Horne
