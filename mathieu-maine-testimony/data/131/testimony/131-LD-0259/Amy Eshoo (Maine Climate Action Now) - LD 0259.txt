Senator Chipman, Representative WIlliams, and Honorable Members of the 
Committee on Transportation, my name is Amy Eshoo. I am a resident of North 
Yarmouth and Director for Maine Climate Action Now (MCAN), a statewide 
coalition of grassroots organizations who support transformative action in response to 
the climate crisis. We hold social, racial, and economic justice, and the equitable 
implementation of clean renewable energy, as critical to the transition to zero carbon 
emissions by 2030 and a more resilient Maine. 
We know that the transportation sector accounts for 54% of our state’s greenhouse 
gas emissions. In order to reduce these emissions in line with the goals the Governor 
has set, we need to not only transition to electrification of vehicles, but we need to 
reduce our dependence on cars. We need to increase our public transportation systems
and encourage widespread use of buses and rail. 
We have an over reliance on single occupancy vehicles (SOV) for commuting across 
the state. 82% of Mainers drive to work alone; meanwhile, SOVs are the highest 
emitting mode of transportation there is. We need to start prioritizing investments in 
our public transit infrastructure to reduce our carbon footprint. 
MCAN understands that the federal funding for transit operations runs out this year. 
The state is only proposing $1.1 million in annual subsidies, which is nearly not 
enough to maintain what we already have. This number has not changed in years, 
clearly not in line with the needs of the people and our environment as we combat 
climate change. 
We ask that you increase the budget to the amount the Maine Transit Association 
suggests to $6.8 million per year, instead of $1.1 million. This would allow us to 
reduce Maine’s large amount of greenhouse gas emissions by ensuring we still have 
affordable and reliable public transportation to serve all Mainers equitably. 
Maine Climate Action Now urges the Transportation Committee to increase public 
transit funding so we can maintain our existing level of service and expand it. Thank 
you for your consideration.
