RE: LD 1590 An Act to Require Disclosure of Campaign Funding Sources  
 
Chair Hickman, Chair Supica, and members of the Joint Standing Committee on Veterans and 
Legal Affairs: My name is David O’Brien and I am Policy Director for RepresentUs, the nation’s 
leading nonpartisan anti-corruption organization. RepresentUs is submitting testimony today in 
support of LD 1590. 
 
RepresentUs is a nonpartisan advocacy organization dedicated to the idea that the government 
should be representative of and accountable to the people. It advocates for policies to 
strengthen our democracy and provides activists with the resources and training to bring 
meaningful change.  
 
This bill would bring valuable and necessary transparency into the murky world of campaign 
spending. It would require the disclosure of donors and original sources of donations to party 
committees, PACs, and ballot question committees over $10,000 and for those entities to 
disclose their top three funders in certain campaign communications. 
 
Since the Supreme Court decided Citizens United v. Federal Elections Commission  in 2010, the 
amount of dark money spent on American elections has exploded. In the decade after Citizens 
United , $4.5 billion was spent by non-party independent groups, compared with just $750 million 
in the two decades prior.1 Americans' trust in government is already at near-historic lows,2 and 
the perception that anonymous wealthy donors have more power than voters can only 
undermine it further.  
 
This bill is a crucial step to strengthen transparency and accountability and will provide voters 
with valuable information about who is trying to influence their elections. This bill will put Maine
