James Tasse
Bicycle Coalition of Maine
LD 1587
Testimony in OPPOSITION to LD 1587
An Act to Amend the Motor Vehicle Inspection Law to Exempt 
Certain Motor Vehicles Less than 3 Years Old
My name is James Tassé and I am the Assistant Director of the Bicycle Coalition of 
Maine (BCM).  Our mission is to make Maine better and safer for bicycling and 
walking.  On behalf of our more than 20,000 members and supporters, I am 
submitting this testimony in OPPOSITION to LD 1587 “An Act to Amend the Motor 
Vehicle Inspection Law to Exempt Certain Motor Vehicles Less than 3 Years Old.” 
The BCM believes that repealing any requirement to inspect motor vehicles and make
sure that they are safe and road-worthy will make Maine’s public ways less safe for 
all users, including for walkers, bicycle riders, and drivers. For this reason, the BCM 
urges an “Ought Not to Pass” report from the committee.  
Many used vehicles less than three years of age come to Maine from other states to be
re-sold. In some cases, those vehicles have been through floods or other experiences 
that may compromise their mechanical integrity. They should be inspected before 
being permitted to use Maine roadways. 
Maine’s inspection program ensures that every motor vehicle—new or old— on the 
state’s roads meets the minimum safe mechanical standards. 
For these reasons, the BCM urges the committee to recommend an Ought Not to Pass 
report for LD 1587.
 
Thank you for your time and service to the people of Maine.
Sincerely,
James Tassé
Assistant Director
Bicycle Coalition of Maine
