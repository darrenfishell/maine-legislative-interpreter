To: Members of the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services
Testimony of: Henrietta Beaufait, Albion, Maine
Supporting: LD 536 -  An Act to Provide Natural Organic Reduction Facilities for 
Maine Residents for the Conversion of Human Remains to Soil
Sponsored by: Representative Doudera
________________________________________________
March 29, 2023
Dear Senator Baldacci, Representative Meyer, and members of the Health and Human
Services Committee,
My name is Henrietta Beaufait, a resident of Albion in Kennebec County. I am 70 
years old. I am testifying in support of LD 536 -  An Act to Provide Natural Organic 
Reduction Facilities for Maine Residents for the Conversion of Human Remains to 
Soil.
According to current Maine statute, human remains must be decently buried, 
entombed in a mausoleum, vault or tomb, or cremated. Entombment in a mausoleum 
requires toxic chemicals and a building footprint. Traditional burial involves the use 
of toxic chemicals and land on which to bury a casket. Even green burial, burial 
without embalming, requires land use for a long period of time. Both types of burial 
require the same soil type and drainage used for farming, further increasing pressure 
on our precious farmland. Cremation by heat requires a tremendous amount of fossil 
fuel. Cremation by alkaline hydrolysis requires safe discharge of wastewater.
Natural organic reduction (aka composting of human remains) does not involve use of
toxic chemicals, does not require any land use, and does not have any waste material. 
In fact the only by-product of natural organic reduction is compost which can be used 
to grow carbon-sequestering plants and pollinators for our bees. I have raised my 
children on an organic dairy farm and know first hand the benefits of composting and 
sustainable agriculture.
LD 536 proposes that Maine residents be given the option to utilize natural organic 
reduction by authorizing facilities to provide such services. Our survivors need access
to organized, well regulated human composting facilities. Please give us the 
opportunity to leave behind a truly meaningful life. I ask that your recommendation to
the Legislature would be to pass LD 536. 
Thank you for your time and consideration.
Respectfully,
Henrietta Beaufait, Albion, Maine
