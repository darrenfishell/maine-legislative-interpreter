Amy  Eshoo
Maine Climate Action Now
LD 1562
Senator Tipping, Representative Roeder, and Honorable members of the Committee 
on Labor and Housing, my name is Amy Eshoo. I am a resident of North Yarmouth 
and Director for Maine Climate Action Now, a statewide coalition of grassroots 
organizations who support transformative action in response to the climate and 
ecological emergency. We hold social, racial, and economic justice as critical to the 
transition to zero carbon emissions by 2030 and demand the equitable implementation
of clean renewable energy, ecological land-use practices, and bold community-led 
action for a more resilient Maine. 
Our coalition fully supported LD 99, An Act To Require the State To Divest Itself of 
Assets Invested in the Fossil Fuel Industry, in the 130th session. That bill was an 
important step for our state to take for our part combating the climate crisis facing us 
all. As the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change stated in their report last 
month a liveable future for all is possible, but only if we take urgent climate action 
can we avert irrevocable damage to the world. 
This bill would have us put our heads in the sand to the realities of the continued 
havoc and destruction brought on us by the fossil fuel industry. Maine Climate Action
Now strongly opposes LD 1562 as its restrictive definitions of key terms (fiduciary, 
material, pecuniary, etc…) are meant with the intent to undermine the ability of 
fiduciaries of the Maine Public Employees Retirement System to consider the context 
and meaning which come to bear on the full meaning of these terms. By prohibiting 
‘nonpecuniary’ factors as defined in this bill the fiduciary would be hampered to 
consider the ongoing destruction of the fossil fuel industry and the urgent necessity of
collective systemic change including divestment of MPERS from fossil fuel holdings.
We urge you to vote OUGHT NOT TO PASS. Thank you.
