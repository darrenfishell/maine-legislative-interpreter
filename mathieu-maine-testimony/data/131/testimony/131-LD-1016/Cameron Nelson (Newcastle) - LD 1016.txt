Dear Members of the State and Local Government Committee,
I am writing to share my personal story in support of LD 1016: An Act to Support 
College Student Participation as Policy Interns for State Legislators that would pay 
students to serve as policy interns for legislators during the legislative session. As 
someone who has benefited greatly from a policy internship under Representative 
Osher of Orono, I believe that this program can have a significant positive impact on 
both the interns and the legislative process.
I first learned about the opportunity to intern for a legislator through a close friend 
who was a political science major. He spoke highly of her own experiences working 
for a legislator and encouraged me to consider applying. As someone who had always
been interested in politics despite my enrollment in the Business program, I was 
immediately intrigued.
After doing some research, I contacted the Maine Business School internship 
coordinator to inquire about receiving course credit for my internship. With his help, I
was able to enroll and receive college credit for my internship under Rep. Osher.
During my time as an intern, I’ve had the opportunity to come to the State House and 
learn about the legislative process firsthand. I was amazed at the amount of work that 
went into making policy decisions that affect people's lives and was also able to 
attend committee hearings, meet with constituents, and provide research and analysis 
to support Rep. Osher’s legislative efforts.
My internship has had a profound impact on me as it has helped me to gain practical 
experience in government and public policy, while also deepening my understanding 
of the importance of civic engagement. It also had a positive effect on my college 
education, as I was able to apply what I had learned in the classroom to real-world 
situations and extracurricular activities.
I believe that paying students to serve as policy interns for legislators during the 
legislative session is good for Maine for several reasons. First, it provides young 
people with valuable opportunities to gain practical experience in government and 
public policy. This can help to prepare them for future careers in these fields, while 
also contributing to the development of a skilled and knowledgeable workforce in our 
state.
In addition, paying students for their work as policy interns can help to address issues 
of equity and access. Many students who are interested in government and public 
policy may not be able to afford to take an unpaid internship, and paying them for 
their work can help to ensure that this opportunity is available to a wider range of 
students.
Furthermore, having young people involved in the political process can bring new 
perspectives and ideas to the table. Students who are participating in an internship 
may be able to provide valuable insights and analysis that can help to inform the 
legislative process. This can help to ensure that policies are developed in a way that 
reflects the needs and interests of a diverse range of stakeholders.
Finally, paying students to serve as policy interns can help to improve the quality and 
effectiveness of the legislative process. By providing support to legislators and their 
staff, interns can help to ensure that policies are developed in a way that is 
well-informed and well-researched. This can help to improve the overall quality of 
legislation and ensure that policies are developed in a way that is evidence-based and 
effective.
In conclusion, I urge you to support legislation that would pay students to serve as 
policy interns for legislators during the legislative session. This program can provide 
valuable experience and opportunities for young people, while also improving the 
quality and effectiveness of the legislative process. As someone who has personally 
benefited from this program, I can attest to its value and importance. Thank you for 
considering this important issue.
