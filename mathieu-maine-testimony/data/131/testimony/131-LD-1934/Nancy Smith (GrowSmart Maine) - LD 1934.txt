Technical Assistance to Communities  in Maine  
Sec. 1.  Review of planning  grant s and technical assistance to communities  in Maine . 
Resolved:  That the Governor’s Office of Policy Innovation and the Future  shall review 
opportunities  and structural changes in state government organization and make 
recommendations that will result in the  improve d coordination and implementation of planning  
grants  and technical assistance  programs , and implementation and project funding  for Maine  
communities  and region al planning and development organizations . The review  shall include 
the following task s:  
1. Consider the m unicipal planning  grants , and technical assistance  needs , and 
implementation and project funding in  of communities , especially the smallest and 
most under resourced communities, particularly related to navigat ing complex 
challenges including, but not limited to land use planning, climate resilience  and 
energy issues , natural resource protection,  transportation,  housing  development , 
economic development , and infrastructure  and neighborhood ;master planning and 
development;  
2. Consider state programs and  inve stments that currently exist  to provide municipal 
planning  grants , and technical assistance , and implementation and project funding  to 
communities in Maine to help them address those needs , and identify gaps  in services 
and geographic coverage  and identify geographic areas of the state where there 
are gaps in funding ; 
3. Consider mechanisms to support municipalities and regional planning and 
development organizations  in competing for  ongoing competitive state and federal 
funding opportunities , and opportunities to streamline state approvals or processes to 
help prioritize projects that meet critical state and local goals . 
4. Recomm end the most effective organization al structure  for state funded programs  
oriented to supporting local government  to ensur e that municipalities  and regional 
planning and development organizations who support communities  have the support 
they need  in a coordinated, easy to access manner  with fewer points of entry ; and  
5. Recommend t he optimal organizational structure, staffing configurations, and budget 
implications  to improve  coordination and implementation  of planning  grants , and 
technical assistance , and implementation and project  funding  that respond s to state 
and local goals an d priorities, and help s to advance complex projects that require 
multiple sources of funding and the participation of multiple state agencies .
