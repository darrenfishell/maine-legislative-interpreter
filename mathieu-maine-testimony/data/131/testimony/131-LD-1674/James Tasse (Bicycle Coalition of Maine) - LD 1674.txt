James Tasse
Bicycle Coalition of Maine
LD 1674
Testimony in Support of LD 1674
An Act to Require and Encourage Safe and Interconnected Transportation 
Construction Projects
Greetings Chairs Chipman and Williams and the distinguished members of the 
Committee on Transportation: 
My name is James Tassé,  and I am the assistant director of the Bicycle Coalition of 
Maine (BCM).  Our mission is to make Maine better and safer for bicycling and 
walking.  On behalf of our more than 20,000 members and supporters, I am 
submitting this testimony in support of  LD 1674 “An Act to Require and Encourage 
Safe and Interconnected Transportation Construction Projects.” 
The Bicycle Coalition of Maine frequently hears complaints about how construction 
projects impede bicycle, pedestrian, and wheelchair access. Signs blocking bike lanes 
or reading “Sidewalk Closed” are routine at construction sites, and rarely is any 
provision made to maintain safe access for persons walking, bicycling or rolling in a 
wheelchair through them. This practice is common, even on state roads, although 
Title 23, §654 clearly states that
“Before a state or state-aid highway is closed due to construction, the department 
shall establish a practical detour route and properly sign the route at all intersections. 
The detour route shall be maintained in a condition adequate to serve traffic until the 
state or state-aid highway under construction is opened to traffic.”
“Traffic,” it should be noted, is defined in Maine law as including persons walking or 
riding bicycles.
LD 1674 would strengthen and clarify the requirement that connectivity must be 
maintained through construction sites for persons walking, riding bicycles, or rolling 
on wheelchairs, and that such connectivity shall conform to ADA requirements. 
BCM believes this bill would be strengthened with language that would impose fines 
on contractors for failure to provide safe connectivity during construction. 
The Bicycle Coalition of Maine strongly supports this bill, and urges an Ought to Pass
report. 
Thank you for your time and service to the people of Maine.
Sincerely,
James Tassé, Assistant Director
