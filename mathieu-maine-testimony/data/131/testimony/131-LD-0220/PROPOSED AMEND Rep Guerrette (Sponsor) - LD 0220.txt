 Amendment to LD 220 -  
An Act to Support Maine’s Potato Farmers and Processors  
Proposed by Representative Guerrette  
April 12 , 2023  
 
Amend the bill by striking the title and inserting the following:  
 
An Act to Support Maine’s Potato Farmers  
 
Amend the bill by striking everything after the enacting clause and inserting the following:  
 
Sec. 1. Appropriations and allocations. The following appropriations and allocations are 
made.  
 
FINANCE AUTHORITY OF MAINE  
 
Potato Marketing Improvement Fund  
 
Initiative: Provides one -time funding to be used for grants to potato farmers for the development 
of sustainable irrigation water sources that meet Department of Environmental Protection Chapter 
587 ru les relating to in -stream flows and lake and pond water levels . 
 
          2023-24 
           $3,000,000  
 
Total            $3,000,000  
 
Sec. 2.  Transfer from General Fund unappropriated surplus; grants to potato 
farmers for sustainable water sources.  Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, 
the State Controller shall transfer $ 3,000,000  from the unappropriated surplus of the General Fund 
to the Potato Marketing Improvement Fund established under Maine Revised Statutes Title 10, 
section 102 3-N to be used for grants to potato farmers for the development of sustainable irrigation 
water sources that meet the requirements of the Department of Environmental Protection Chapter 
587 rules relating to in -stream flows and lake and pond water levels .  A grant  award to an applicant 
for a project under this section may not be more $200,000  and may not exceed 75% of the total 
project cost.  
 
SUMMARY  
 
This amendment strikes and replaces the bill, which is a concept draft.  The amendment 
provides one -time funding of $3 million in SFY 2024 from the unappropriated surplus of General 
Fund to the Potato Marketing Improvement Fund established under Maine Revised Statutes Title 
10, section 1023 -N to be used for grants to potato farmers for the development of sustainable 
irrigation water sources that meet the requirements of the Department of Environmental Protection 
Chapter 587 rules relating to in -stream flows and lake and pond water levels.
