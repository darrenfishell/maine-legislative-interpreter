2curriculum, credential and career path for behavioral health professionals 
3serving children in the community.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and 
4 Human Services, Office of MaineCare Services and Office of Child and Family Services 
5 shall coordinate in the development of a course curriculum, credential and career path for 
6 behavioral health professionals.  The course curriculum must be offered through 
7 community college courses around the State or by a contracted agency with multiple sites 
8 around the State.  Individuals participating in the course curriculum must have a high 
9 school diploma or equivalent and a driver's license.  The course curriculum must lead to a 
10 credential that is sufficient to satisfy department licensing requirements for any behavioral 
11 health professional providing services to children in the community.  The department shall 
12 also coordinate the development of a career path for behavioral health professionals serving 
13 children in the community.  The career path must allow for individuals who have met the 
14 course curriculum requirements with a corresponding credential that is portable across 
15 behavioral health fields.
16Sec. 2. Incentives.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services 
17 shall explore options for providing incentives to individuals wishing to pursue the course 
18 curriculum developed pursuant to section 1, including but not limited to grants or free or 
19 reduced tuition.
20Sec. 3. Weekly supervision requirements.  Resolved:  That, no later than 
21 January 1, 2024, the Department of Health and Human Services shall require that all 
22 behavioral health professionals receive no less than one hour per week of supervision by a 
23 licensed clinician.  The supervision may be in a group or individual setting.
24Sec. 4. MaineCare rates for behavioral health professionals serving 
25children in the community.  Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human 
26 Services shall amend its rules in Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Section 28, 
27 Rehabilitative and Community Support Services for Children with Cognitive Impairments 
28 and Functional Limitations, and Section 65, Behavioral Health Services, to increase 
29 MaineCare rates for services provided by behavioral health professionals to children in the 
30 community.  Rate increases must apply only to those behavioral health professionals who 
31 have completed the course curriculum requirements and received the credential pursuant 
32 to section 1 to allow for a livable wage to be earned by the behavioral health professional.  
33 The department shall increase the rates to take effect as new behavioral health professionals 
34 complete the course curriculum and credential requirements.  Rules adopted pursuant to 
35 this section are routine technical rules as defined in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, 
36 chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
37SUMMARY
38 This resolve requires the Department of Health and Human Services, Office of 
39 MaineCare Services and Office of Child and Family Services to coordinate the 
40 development of a course curriculum, credential and career path for behavioral health 
41 professionals providing services to children in the community.  The course curriculum must 
42 be offered through community college courses around the State or by a contracted agency 
43 with multiple sites around the State.  The course curriculum must lead to a credential that 
44 is portable and sufficient to satisfy department licensing requirements for any behavioral 38
