Page 1 - 131LR1593(01)1Sec. 1. Commissioner of Education to establish pilot program to provide 
2grants to expand age eligibility for life skills and vocational programs. 
3Resolved: That the Commissioner of Education shall establish a pilot program to award 
4 grants to expand age eligibility for life skills and vocational programs in public schools for 
5 a period of 2 years. The Commissioner of Education may accept donations from outside 
6 sources and state and federal funding to accomplish the purposes of this resolve.
7Sec. 2. Application. Resolved: That a local education agency may submit an 
8 application for grant funds under the pilot program pursuant to section 1. The 
9 Commissioner of Education shall give priority to applicants that do not have career and 
10 technical education in middle schools and provide grants to schools that demonstrate the 
11 following goals:
12 1. Exposing students to vocations so that students can identify a vocational interest 
13 earlier in the students' academic careers;
14 2. Providing students with valuable life skills; and
15 3. Lowering the truancy rate and number of disciplinary infractions by increasing 
16 student engagement.
17Sec. 3. Use of funds. Resolved: That a local education agency that receives a grant 
18 award during the pilot program under section 1 may use the funds only for expanding age 
19 eligibility for life skills and vocational programs.
20Sec. 4. Evaluation; annual submissions. Resolved: That each local education 
21 agency that receives a grant under the pilot program under section 1 shall engage in 
22 evaluation activities, including interviews or surveys on the process and effect and 
23 outcomes of the pilot program, and annually for the duration of the grant submit to the 
24 Department of Education a report or other product that showcases learning achieved 
25 through the pilot program. The Department of Education annually shall compile the 
26 submissions and make them publicly available.
27Sec. 5. Fund established. Resolved: That the Life Skills and Vocational Program 
28 Grant Pilot Program Fund is established as a nonlapsing fund under the Department of 
29 Education for the purpose of receiving money from state, federal and other sources, 
30 including donations from private citizens, corporations and entities for the purposes of this 
31 resolve. The department shall distribute any available money in the fund to support the 
32 purposes described in this resolve. Upon distribution of all the money, the fund is dissolved.
33Sec. 6. Report to Legislature. Resolved: That, no later than December 4, 2024, 
34 the Commissioner of Education shall submit a report regarding the pilot program pursuant 
35 to this resolve and make recommendations, including any suggested legislation, to the joint 
36 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over education and cultural 
37 affairs. The joint standing committee may report out legislation on the subject matter of the 
38 report to the First Regular Session of the 132nd Legislature.
39Sec. 7.  Appropriations and allocations.  Resolved:  That the following 
40 appropriations and allocations are made.
