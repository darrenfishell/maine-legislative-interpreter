2Sec. 1.  28-A MRSA §1012, sub-§3, as repealed and replaced by PL 2021, c. 658, 
3 §157, is amended to read:
43.  Off-premises catering license.    A golf course, club licensed to sell spirits, wine 
5 and malt liquor or a licensed Class A restaurant, licensed Class A restaurant/lounge, 
6 licensed Class A lounge, licensed hotel, licensed bed and breakfast, licensed auditorium, 
7 licensed civic auditorium or licensed performing arts center may obtain a license to conduct 
8 off-premises catering of the same type or types of liquor that the establishment may sell 
9 pursuant to the establishment's underlying golf course, club, Class A restaurant, Class A 
10 restaurant/lounge, Class A lounge, hotel, bed and breakfast, auditorium, civic auditorium 
11 or performing arts center license as provided in section 1052.  The fee for an off-premises 
12 catering license is $10 per calendar day of the event or gathering.
13Sec. 2.  28-A MRSA §1052, sub-§1, as amended by PL 2021, c. 658, §167, is 
14 further amended to read:
151.  Off-premises catering license for sale of liquor.  A golf course, club licensed to 
16 sell spirits, wine and malt liquor or a licensed Class A restaurant, licensed Class A 
17 restaurant/lounge, licensed Class A lounge, licensed hotel, licensed bed and breakfast, 
18 licensed auditorium, licensed civic auditorium or licensed performing arts center may apply 
19 for an additional license to conduct off-premises catering at planned events or gatherings 
20 to be held at locations other than the licensee's premises under this section.
21Sec. 3.  28-A MRSA §1052, sub-§3-A, as enacted by PL 2021, c. 658, §167, is

233-A.  Type of liquor.  An off-premises catering licensee may sell at an event described

26 Class A lounge, hotel, bed and breakfast, auditorium, civic auditorium or performing arts 
27 center license.
28SUMMARY
29 This bill allows golf courses to obtain a license to conduct off-premises catering with 
30 the same type of liquor that the golf course is licensed to provide.29
