5Sec. 2.  Commission membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
6 353, the commission consists of 17 members:
7 1.  Seven members appointed by the President of the Senate as follows:
8 A.  Two members of the Senate, including one member of the party holding the largest 
9 number of seats in the Legislature and one member of the party holding the 2nd largest 
10 number of seats in the Legislature;
11 B.  Two members who are employed or volunteer in the field of emergency medical 
12 services, including one member who represents a community of 10,000 residents or 
13 more and one member who represents a community of fewer than 10,000 residents;
14 C.  One member who represents a statewide association of emergency medical services 
15 providers;
16 D.  One member who represents a private, for-profit ambulance service; and
17 E.  One member who represents a statewide association of municipalities;
18 2.  Eight members appointed by the Speaker of the House as follows:
19 A.  Four members of the House of Representatives, including 2 members of the party 
20 holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature and 2 members of the party 
21 holding the 2nd largest number of seats in the Legislature;
22 B.  One member who represents a tribal emergency medical service;
23 C.  One member who represents a volunteer emergency medical service;
24 D.  One member who represents a county government; and
25 E.  One member who represents a statewide association of hospitals;
26 3.  The Commissioner of Health and Human Services or the commissioner's designee; 
27 and
28 4.  The director of Maine Emergency Medical Services within the Department of Public 
29 Safety or the director's designee.
30Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
31 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
32 commission.
33Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That, 
34 notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, all appointments must be made no later than 15 days 
35 following the effective date of this Act. The appointing authorities shall notify the 
36 Executive Director of the Legislative Council once all appointments have been completed. 
37 Within 15 days after appointment of all members, the chairs shall call and convene the first 
38 meeting of the commission, which must be no later than 30 days following the appointment 
39 of all members.
Page 2 - 131LR0658(01)1Sec. 5.  Duties; meetings.  Resolved:  That the commission shall examine and 
2 make recommendations on the structure, support and delivery of emergency medical 
3 services in the State.  The commission shall maintain communication and coordinate with 
4 Maine Emergency Medical Services as defined in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 32, 
5 section 83, subsection 16-A so that Maine Emergency Medical Services is informed of the 
6 work of the commission and the commission is informed of the strategic planning work of 
7 Maine Emergency Medical Services.  The commission may look at all aspects of 
8 emergency medical services, including but not limited to workforce development, training, 
9 compensation, retention, costs, reimbursement rates, organization and local and state 
10 support. The commission is authorized to hold a maximum of 6 meetings.
11Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
12 necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff support 
13 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
14Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, no later than 
15 December 6, 2023, the commission shall submit a report that includes its findings and 
16 recommendations, including suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on 
17 Criminal Justice and Public Safety.
18SUMMARY
19 This resolve reestablishes the Blue Ribbon Commission to Study Emergency Medical 
20 Services in the State, which was previously established by the 130th Legislature in 2022.  
21 This 17-member commission is charged with examining and making recommendations on 
22 the structure, support and delivery of emergency medical services in the State.
23 The commission is directed to submit, no later than December 6, 2023, a report 
24 detailing its findings and recommendations, including any proposed legislation, to the Joint 
25 Standing Committee on Criminal Justice and Public Safety.19
