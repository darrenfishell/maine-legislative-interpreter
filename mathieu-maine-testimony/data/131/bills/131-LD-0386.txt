Page 1 - 131LR0464(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, fuel prices have increased significantly and Maine schools are experiencing 
4 increased costs for heating fuel and fuel associated with transportation; and
5Whereas, this legislation must be enacted before the expiration of the 90-day period 
6 to provide relief to schools during this school year; and
7Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
8 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
9 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
10 therefore,
11Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
12Sec. 1.  Fuel assistance.  Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the 
13 Department of Education shall provide funds to public elementary and secondary schools 
14 in the State to support the increased costs of heating fuel and fuel associated with 
15 transportation.  The amount of the funding provided to individual public elementary and 
16 secondary schools in the State must be equal to $100 per student based on the most recent 
17 enrollment totals.  A school shall apply the funds to its 2022-2023 budget for heating fuel 
18 costs and fuel costs associated with transportation.

34SUMMARY
35 This bill provides one-time funds to public elementary and secondary schools in the 
36 State for heating fuel costs and fuel costs associated with transportation during the 2022-
37 2023 school year.  The bill provides that the amount provided to individual public 
38 elementary and secondary schools in the State must be equal to $100 per student based on 
39 the most recent enrollment totals. A school must apply the funds to its 2022-2023 budget 
40 for heating fuel costs and fuel costs associated with transportation.28
