17SUMMARY
18 This bill creates a registration process for a communications entity that brokers 
19 numbering resources in the State. It defines "communications entity" to include all 
20 interconnected voice over Internet protocol service providers, local exchange carriers, 
21 voice service providers, wholesale competitive local exchange carriers or any other entity 
22 that obtains numbering resources from a North American organization responsible for 
23 neutrally administering numbering resources in a Maine numbering plan area. It allows the 
24 Public Utilities Commission to adopt routine technical rules relating to the registration 
25 process and allows the commission to include in its rules additional requirements for 
26 communications entities in order for the commission to manage the distribution of 
27 numbering resources in the State. It authorizes the commission to exercise any authority 
28 provided to the states by 47 Code of Federal Regulations, Part 52 in order to maximize 
29 numbering resources.
30 This bill also allows the commission to require a communications entity to pay into the 
31 State's universal service fund and telecommunications education access fund.18
