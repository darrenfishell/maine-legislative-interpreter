20SUMMARY
21 This bill creates a new category of pharmacy facility licensure for long-term care 
22 pharmacies. It defines terms regarding long-term care pharmacies for the Maine Pharmacy 
23 Act to mirror a proposed federal law. It increases the number of individuals on the Maine 
24 Board of Pharmacy from 7 to 8 members and requires that the additional member be a 
25 pharmacist working at a long-term care pharmacy at the time of appointment. It provides 
26 that a drug or pharmaceutical preparation may only be accepted back by a pharmacy to be 
27 dispensed again if the drug or preparation is packaged in an unbroken, sealed container.1
