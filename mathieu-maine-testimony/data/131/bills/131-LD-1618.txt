Page 1 - 131LR2356(01)1Sec. 1.  Department of Marine Resources to convene working group. 
2Resolved:  That the Department of Marine Resources shall convene a working group to 
3 develop a long-term plan to support the lobster industry. The department shall invite 
4 participation in the working group from at least the following: members of the lobster 
5 industry; local officials from towns with significant lobster industry activity; experts on the 
6 lobster industry and federal regulation of the lobster industry; and the Attorney General or 
7 the Attorney General's designee. The working group shall review and develop 
8 recommendations relating to the lobster industry, including, but not limited to, state and 
9 federal regulation, infrastructure needs, waterfront access, climate change concerns and 
10 marketing of the industry. By December 6, 2023, the department shall submit an interim 
11 report and recommendations, including any suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing 
12 Committee on Marine Resources. The committee may report out legislation related to the 
13 interim report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature. By November 6, 
14 2024, the department shall submit a final report and recommendations, including any 
15 suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Marine Resources. The joint 
16 standing committee having jurisdiction over marine resources matters may report out 
17 legislation related to the final report to the 132nd Legislature in 2025.
18SUMMARY
19 This resolve requires the Department of Marine Resources to convene a working group 
20 to develop a long-term plan to support the lobster industry. By December 6, 2023, the 
21 department must submit an interim report and recommendations to the Joint Standing 
22 Committee on Marine Resources. The committee may report out legislation related to the 
23 interim report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature. By November 6, 
24 2024, the department must submit a final report and recommendations, including any 
25 suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Marine Resources. The joint 
26 standing committee having jurisdiction over marine resources matters may report out 
27 legislation related to the final report to the 132nd Legislature in 2025.19
