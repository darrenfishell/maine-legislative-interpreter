4§1651.  Examination of jails; board of visitors
51.  Examination.  At the commencement of each session required by law, the county 
6 commissioners shall examine the jail in their county and take necessary precautions for the 
7 security of prisoners, for the prevention of infection and sickness and for the 
8 accommodations of the prisoners.
92.  Appointment.  The sheriff for each county shall appoint a board of 7 visitors for 
10 each correctional facility under the sheriff's supervision.
11 A.  Members of the boards of visitors serve for terms of one year  3 years.
12 B.  Members of the boards of visitors are eligible for reappointment at the expiration 
13 of their terms.  The boards of visitors must be representative of a broad range of 
14 professionals, family members and citizens interested in the well-being of prisoners, 
15 including representatives of advocacy groups for human and civil rights, medical and 
16 psychiatric professionals, persons who have served in corrections settings and other 
17 interested citizens.  One member of each board of visitors must be a person with 
18 knowledge of issues related to the incarceration of women.  One member of each board 
19 of visitors must be a woman who has been incarcerated in the State and who has prior 
20 child welfare experience with the Department of Health and Human Services, Office 
21 of Child and Family Services. One member of each board of visitors must be a medical 
22 or psychiatric professional.
23 C.  A member of the Legislature or a former or current employee of a sheriff's 
24 department may not serve on a board of visitors.
25 D.  The sheriffs of 2 or more counties, at their discretion, may appoint a joint board of 
26 visitors of  7 or more members.
273.  Powers.  Each board of visitors shall inspect the correctional facility to which it is 
28 assigned, subject to reasonable restrictions required by the sheriff to ensure the security of 
29 the jail, and make recommendations to the sheriff with respect to inmates who are mentally 
30 ill.
314.  Duties.  Boards of visitors have the following duties.
32 A.  Each board of visitors shall inspect the correctional facility to which it is assigned. 
33 Each board of visitors must be provided open access to all physical areas of the 
34 correctional facility, including access to areas housing prisoners. Each board of visitors 
35 must be provided the opportunity to speak to prisoners and to staff. Members of the 
36 board of visitors shall comply with all sheriff's department policies and procedures and 
37 facility security practices regarding access to the correctional facility, shall adhere to 
38 all federal and state laws regarding confidentiality and shall refer concerns or 
39 complaints regarding specific individuals to the jail administrator or advocate.
40 B.  Each board of visitors shall make recommendations to the sheriff regarding services 
41 or treatment for prisoners who have mental health challenges or are mentally ill.
Page 2 - 131LR0927(01)1 C.  Each board of visitors shall review the management of the correctional facility to 
2 which it is assigned to determine whether that management is consistent with the 
3 philosophy, mission and policy goals of the sheriff's department and facility. On or 
4 before the last day of the calendar year, each board of visitors shall prepare an annual 
5 report including its recommendations and shall provide copies of its report to the jail 
6 administrator, the county commissioners and the joint standing committee of the 
7 Legislature having jurisdiction over criminal justice and public safety matters. The 
8 sheriff shall provide copies with the sheriff's response to the reports to the joint standing 
9 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over criminal justice and public safety 
10 matters within one month of receiving the annual reports.
11 D.  Each board of visitors shall appear before the joint standing committee of the 
12 Legislature having jurisdiction over criminal justice and public safety matters upon 
13 request.
14 E.  Each board of visitors shall meet regularly and tour the correctional facility to which 
15 it is assigned at least 4 times a year. At each meeting, a board of visitors may request 
16 and must receive information from the jail administrator that the board determines will 
17 assist in the review of the management of the facility. To the extent that a board of 
18 visitors is not discussing matters made confidential by federal or state law, a meeting 
19 of the board is a public proceeding and must be conducted in accordance with Title 1, 
20 section 403. Boards of visitors may meet jointly.
21 F.  Each board of visitors shall share copies of that board's annual report with the other 
22 boards of visitors of that sheriff's department.
235.  Orientation and training.  A board of visitors must receive annual training, 
24 including:
25 A.  Facilities orientation, including a tour of the correctional facility to which the board 
26 of visitors is assigned, explanation of the facility command structure and receipt of the 
27 department's publication regarding detention and correctional standards for Maine 
28 counties and municipalities, facility prisoner handbooks, volunteer policies and board 
29 of visitors policies;
30 B.  Review of the federal Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 and guidance 
31 concerning domestic violence, substance use disorder including opioids, behavioral 
32 health and adverse childhood trauma;
33 C.  A list and explanation of available community resources;
34 D.  The legal requirements of this section; and
35 E.  Training on any other subject as determined by the sheriff or chair of the board of 
36 visitors.
376.  Public access.  A member of the public must have direct access to the board of 
38 visitors without being required to access the board through the sheriff's department or 
39 correctional facility administration. A prisoner handbook must provide the names and 
40 contact information for the membership of the board of visitors. A complaint made to a 
41 member of the board of visitors must be referred to the jail administrator or advocate and 
42 may not be directly acted upon by the member or the board.

2 with this section may be subject to withholding of state funding.

28SUMMARY
29 This bill amends and in part standardizes requirements and duties between the boards 
30 of visitors of county jails and Department of Corrections correctional facilities including 
31 the appointment, duties, orientation and training and access by the public to the boards of 
32 visitors.1
