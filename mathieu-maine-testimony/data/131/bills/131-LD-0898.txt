Page 1 - 131LR2381(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, foster parents serve an essential role in our society, and the State 
4 imminently needs more foster parents; and
5Whereas, current reimbursement rates are inadequate to encourage more individuals 
6 to serve as foster parents; and
7Whereas, increasing reimbursement rates as soon as possible will encourage more 
8 individuals to serve as foster parents; and
9Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
10 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
11 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
12 therefore, be it
13Sec. 1.  Department of Health and Human Services to increase foster care 
14reimbursement rates.  Resolved: That the Department of Health and Human Services 
15 shall amend its rule 10-148 C.M.R. Chapter 14, Section 4 to increase by 20% more than 
16 the reimbursement rates that were in effect January 1, 2023 the reimbursement rates for 
17 Level of Care A - Basic Care - Minimal Needs, Level of Care B - Basic Care - Mild Needs, 
18 Level of Care C - Treatment Care - Moderate Needs, Level of Care D - Treatment Care - 
19 High Needs, Level of Care D - Basic or Treatment Care - Exceptional Medical Care and 
20 Level of Care E - Treatment Care - Severe Needs.
21Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
22 takes effect when approved.
23SUMMARY
24 This resolve directs the Department of Health and Human Services to amend its rules 
25 to increase the reimbursement rates for foster parents for all foster care levels of care by 
26 20%.24
