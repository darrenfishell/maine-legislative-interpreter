11SUMMARY
12 This bill requires the Efficiency Maine Trust and the Maine State Housing Authority 
13 to develop an education program and conduct outreach to residents of manufactured 
14 housing, which includes mobile homes, to increase awareness of the energy efficiency 
15 programs administered by each entity.
16 This bill requires the Efficiency Maine Trust, in coordination with the Maine State 
17 Housing Authority, to develop a program to provide grants or loans to assist persons who 
18 qualify to purchase and install high-efficiency heat pump systems and weatherization 
19 products and services. The program must prioritize homeowners and renters with the 
20 highest energy burdens, who live in rural or underserved areas and who receive benefits 
21 under state or federal low-income assistance programs. The trust must provide education 
22 regarding program eligibility and assistance with program navigation to individuals who 
23 may qualify for the program. The trust must provide reports to the joint standing committee 
24 of the Legislature having jurisdiction over energy matters regarding the status of the 
25 program.1
