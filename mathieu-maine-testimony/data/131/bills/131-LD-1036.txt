43.  Authorized administration and dispensing of naloxone hydrochloride by law 
5enforcement officers, corrections officers and municipal firefighters.  A law 
6 enforcement agency as defined in Title 25, section 3701, subsection 1, a regional or county 
7 jail, a prison, a correctional facility as defined in Title 34‑A, section 1001, subsection 6 or 
8 a municipal fire department as defined in Title 30‑A, section 3151, subsection 1 is 
9 authorized to obtain a supply of naloxone hydrochloride to be administered or dispensed in 
10 accordance with this subsection.  A law enforcement officer as defined in Title 17‑A, 
11 section 2, subsection 17, in accordance with policies adopted by the law enforcement 
12 agency, a corrections officer, in accordance with policies adopted by the jail, prison or 
13 correctional facility, and a municipal firefighter as defined in Title 30‑A, section 3151, 
14 subsection 2, in accordance with policies adopted by the municipality, may administer or 
15 dispense intranasal naloxone hydrochloride as clinically indicated if the law enforcement 
16 officer, corrections officer or municipal firefighter has received medical training in 
17 accordance with protocols adopted by the Medical Direction and Practices Board 
18 established in Title 32, section 83, subsection 16‑B. A law enforcement officer, as defined 
19 in Title 17-A, section 2, subsection 17, in accordance with policies adopted by the law 
20 enforcement agency, shall carry naloxone hydrochloride at all times when on duty, obtain 
21 medical training in accordance with protocols adopted by the Medical Direction and 
22 Practices Board and administer or dispense intranasal naloxone hydrochloride as clinically 
23 indicated. The Medical Direction and Practices Board shall establish medical training 
24 protocols for law enforcement officers, corrections officers and municipal firefighters 
25 pursuant to this subsection.
26SUMMARY
27 The bill amends the law requiring the administration and dispensing of naloxone 
28 hydrochloride by law enforcement officers, corrections officers and municipal firefighters 
29 to further require law enforcement officers to obtain medical training in the dispensing of 
30 naloxone hydrochloride and to carry naloxone hydrochloride at all times when on duty.27
