4 The University of Maine shall maintain and provide cooperative extension education

8Sec. 2.  Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to create 
9exploratory committee.  The Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry 
10 shall create an exploratory committee to prioritize meat and poultry processing workforce 
11 training strategies in collaboration with the Department of Labor, the University of Maine 
12 Cooperative Extension and meat and poultry processors in the State. The strategies may 
13 include a correctional facility work-release program, the creation of a community college 
14 curriculum and certificate program in meat cutting, a train-the-trainer module or funding a 
15 mobile training and processing unit similar to units in other states. By December 6, 2023, 
16 the department shall report the results of the committee's findings, including any policy or 
17 funding recommendations, along with any recommended legislation, to the Joint Standing 
18 Committee on Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, which may report out legislation 
19 based upon the report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
20Sec. 3.  University of Maine to conduct study to assess feasibility of 
21processing and marketing meat and poultry products within State. The 
22 University of Maine shall conduct a study to assess the feasibility of processing and 
23 marketing meat and poultry products within the State. The study must provide an 
24 assessment identifying the financial, physical, regulatory, marketing and cultural barriers 
25 to increasing meat and poultry slaughter and processing in the State and develop important 
26 financial, regulatory, market and technical information reasonably useful in the decision 
27 making of investors and businesses seeking to establish new or expanding meat or poultry 
28 processing facilities in the State. The study must focus on the feasibility of operations 
29 producing meat or poultry products raised, slaughtered, processed and sold within the State 
30 and identify standards for producing halal, kosher and other religious and culturally 
31 important meat products. Upon conclusion of the study, the university shall:
32 1. Release a technical report of the study's findings conveying the feasibility 
33 assessment of meat and poultry processing in the State, including operational and 
34 marketing considerations;
35 2. Hold a public briefing regarding the study's findings and the technical report in 
36 subsection 1; and
37 3.  Produce:
38 A. Decision aids pertinent to meat and poultry processing facility operators and other 
39 meat and poultry marketing channel participants, including sample cost and revenue 
40 projections for a sample operation and sensitivity analysis.  For purposes of this 
41 paragraph, "sensitivity analysis" means the study of how an uncertainty in the output 
42 of a system can be predicted based upon the variables of the inputs to the system; and
Page 2 - 131LR0702(01)1 B. A series of fact sheets, bulletins or other publications useful to meat and poultry 
2 processors, investors and marketing channel participants summarizing areas of key 
3 findings of the study. 
4 For purposes of this subsection, "marketing channel" means the way a product or a service 
5 makes its way from the point of production to the point of consumption or use by the 
6 consumer.
