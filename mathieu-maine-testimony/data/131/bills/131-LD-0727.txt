2Sec. 1.  39-A MRSA §102, sub-§4, ¶E-1 is enacted to read:
3 E-1.  When an employee is employed as a first responder and is injured performing the 
4 duties of that employment and is also employed at an additional place of employment, 
5 the employee's average weekly wages are computed by combining the wages, earnings 
6 or salary received by the employee from each place of employment. For the purposes 
7 of this paragraph, "first responder" means a law enforcement officer as defined in Title 
8 17-A, section 2, subsection 17, a firefighter as defined in Title 17-A, section 752-E, 
9 subsection 2 or an emergency medical care provider as defined in Title 17-A, section 
10 752-C, subsection 2.
11SUMMARY
12 This bill amends the Maine Workers' Compensation Act of 1992 to provide that if an 
13 employee employed as a first responder is injured and is also employed at an additional 
14 place of employment, the employee's average weekly wages are computed by combining 
15 the wages, earnings or salary received by the employee from each place of employment.12
