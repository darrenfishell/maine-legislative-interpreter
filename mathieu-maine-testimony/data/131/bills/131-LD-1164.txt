Page 1 - 131LR0063(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, the provision allowing a student to apply for a Department of Education 
4 diploma if the student experienced a significant interruption in education as a result of the 
5 COVID‑19 pandemic and civil emergency is set to expire on September 1, 2023; and
6Whereas, this legislation is necessary to continue the effect of this provision without 
7 interruption; and
8Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
9 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
10 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
11 therefore,
12Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
13Sec. 1.  20-A MRSA §257-A, sub-§1-A, as enacted by PL 2021, c. 61, §1, is 
14 amended to read:
151-A.  Eligibility for students impacted by COVID-19 pandemic.  Notwithstanding 
16 the eligibility requirements in subsection 1, a student is eligible to apply for a Department 
17 of Education diploma if that student is a 4th year secondary school student and is unable to 
18 satisfy the requirements for a diploma from a school administrative unit because the student 
19 experienced a significant interruption to the student's education as a result of the COVID‑19 
20 pandemic and civil emergency during the student's secondary school education history.
21 This subsection is repealed on September 1, 2023.
22Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
23 takes effect when approved.
24SUMMARY
25 This bill continues the program to allow a student to obtain a Department of Education 
26 diploma due to significant interruption to the student's education as a result of the COVID-
27 19 pandemic, known as the "Maine Diploma" program.25
