3ยง20058.  Recovery residence rental assistance program
41. Recovery residence rental assistance program.  The recovery residence rental 
5 assistance program, referred to in this section as "the program," is established to provide 
6 assistance for rental fees for eligible individuals to reside in a certified recovery residence 
7 under section 20057. The department shall contract with a nonprofit organization in the 
8 State that manages ethical and safety standards and certifies recovery residences to 
9 administer the program. At least 50% of assistance provided under the program must be 
10 provided to individuals who are members of marginalized groups, including but not limited 
11 to Black, indigenous or other persons of color, persons with differences in mental or 
12 physical abilities and lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender persons. 
132. Eligibility. The program must provide assistance under subsection 1 for an amount 
14 equal to up to 3 months' rent in a certified recovery residence for an individual who:
15 A. Has been accepted to reside or resides in a certified recovery residence; and
16 B. Has an income that is equal to or less than the federal poverty level.
173. Recovery residence rental assistance fund.  The recovery residence rental 
18 assistance fund is established and administered by the department to support the program.  
19 Assistance under the program must be disbursed directly from the fund to the certified 
20 recovery residence for an eligible individual under subsection 2 approved for the program. 
21 The fund is funded by appropriations, allocations and other public and private sources.  The 
22 fund may be interest-bearing and interest accrued on money in the fund accrues to the fund.  
23 The fund does not lapse but carries over to the next fiscal year. 
244. Rules.  The department shall adopt rules to carry out the purposes of this section.  
25 Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules under chapter 375, 
26 subchapter 2-A.
27SUMMARY
28 This bill creates the recovery residence rental assistance program and fund to provide 
29 rental assistance for up to 3 months in a certified recovery residence for an individual 
30 accepted to reside in or who resides in a certified recovery residence and who has an income 
31 at or below the federal poverty level. The program is required to provide at least 50% of 
32 the assistance to individuals who are members of marginalized groups, including but not 
33 limited to Black, indigenous or other persons of color, persons with differences in mental 
34 or physical abilities and lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender persons.28
