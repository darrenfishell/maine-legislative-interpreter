72. Duties. The manager shall develop, train and maintain a statewide network of 
8 appropriate liaisons, referred to in this section as "the liaison network," to assist children 
9 experiencing disruption, including, but not limited to, homelessness or unstable residency, 
10 placement in foster care, hospitalization or placement in a correctional institution. The 
11 manager shall:
12 A. Develop, implement and coordinate strategies to ensure that such children and their 
13 families receive support, guidance and assistance in accessing and navigating 
14 educational, child welfare, mental health and other services and programs through a 
15 coordinated and seamless statewide support network; and 
16 B. Achieve the purposes of paragraph A through the liaison network, which must 
17 include a coordinated response team across all appropriate state agencies and local 
18 liaisons in regional state offices, school administrative units, juvenile correctional 
19 facilities, inpatient psychiatric hospitals, mental health crisis agencies and other 
20 appropriate entities that provide services useful and relevant to achieving the purposes 
21 of paragraph A. 
22 The manager shall work with all appropriate state agencies in carrying out the 
23 responsibilities of this subsection, including, but not limited to, the Department of Health 
24 and Human Services, the Department of Corrections and the Children's Cabinet.
253.  Support.  The department shall ensure that the manager has the support and staffing 
26 services necessary to achieve the purposes of this section and shall coordinate other 
27 activities and programs of the department relevant to the work of the manager so as to 
28 maximize the effectiveness of the manager in achieving the purposes of this section.
29SUMMARY
30 This bill establishes the Keeping Maine Children Connected Program Manager within 
31 the Department of Education to assist children in crisis or experiencing disruption by 
32 developing, implementing and coordinating strategies to ensure such children and their 
33 families receive support, guidance and assistance in accessing and navigating educational, 
34 child welfare, mental health and other services and programs through a coordinated and 
35 seamless statewide support network.  The manager is directed to establish, train and 
36 maintain a statewide network of persons able to achieve this purpose, including a 
37 coordinated response team across all relevant state agencies and local liaisons in regional 
38 state offices, school administrative units, juvenile correctional facilities, inpatient 
39 psychiatric hospitals, mental health crisis agencies and other appropriate entities.  
40 The department is directed to ensure that the manager has the necessary support and
