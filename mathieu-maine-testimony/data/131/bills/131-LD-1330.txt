23Sec. 1.  Determination of compliance with acquisition and operation 
24agreement.  Resolved:  That the Department of Administrative and Financial Services 
25 shall investigate and determine whether the benefits of the 2004 Agreement Regarding 
26 Solid Waste Disposal Facility Acquisition and Operation, referred to in this resolve as "the 
27 agreement," entered into by the State and the owner of the paper mill located in Old Town, 
28 referred to in this resolve as "the paper mill," regarding disposal capacity and tipping fees 
29 continues to properly accrue to the owner of the Old Town paper mill.  The department 
30 shall provide, in writing, the results of its investigation to the Joint Standing Committee on 
31 Environment and Natural Resources within 30 days of the effective date of this resolve.  
32Sec. 2.  Necessary action to ensure compliance with the acquisition and 
33operation agreement.  Resolved:  That if the Department of Administrative and 
34 Financial Services determines, following the investigation conducted pursuant to section 
35 1, that the benefits of the agreement are not being received by the owner of the paper mill 
36 on the terms originally contemplated by the agreement, the department shall take such 
37 action as is necessary to ensure the proper receipt of those benefits by the owner of the 
38 paper mill.  
39Sec. 3.  Report.  Resolved:  That, by January 15, 2024, the Department of 
40 Administrative and Financial Services shall submit a report to the Joint Standing 
41 Committee on Environment and Natural Resources on its findings and actions taken 
42 pursuant to this resolve.

2 In Resolve 2003, chapter 93, the State Planning Office was authorized to acquire and 
3 cause to be operated a solid waste disposal facility in the City of Old Town.  Pursuant to 
4 an agreement entered into with the paper mill located in Old Town, the State agreed to 
5 provide certain commitments and benefits to the owner of the paper mill in connection with 
6 the operation of the landfill either directly or through any operator authorized by the State 
7 to operate the landfill.
8 This resolve requires the Department of Administrative and Financial Services to 
9 investigate and determine whether the State is providing the owner of the paper mill in Old 
10 Town the benefits as required by the agreement.  If the department determines that the 
11 agreed-upon benefits are not being provided, the department is required to take necessary 
12 action to ensure the receipt of those benefits.  The resolve requires the department to report 
13 the results of its investigation to the Joint Standing Committee on Environment and Natural 
14 Resources within 30 days of the effective date of the resolve and to report again by January 
15 15, 2024 regarding any action the department needed to take to ensure receipt of the 
16 benefits by the owner of the paper mill.2
