12 Agency Home Support Service Providers described in rule Chapter 101: MaineCare

14 consists of officials from the Office of Aging and Disability Services and the department's

16 including one provider that supports individuals with extensive behavioral and medical 
17 needs. The Office of Aging and Disability Services shall appoint a chair. The chair of the 
18 working group shall provide written notice of meetings and copies of any minutes of 
19 meetings of the working group to the Commissioner of Health and Human Services or the 
20 commissioner's designee and to members of the Joint Standing Committee on Health and 
21 Human Services and the Office of Policy and Legal Analysis. No later than October 1, 
22 2024, the working group shall submit a report that includes its findings and 
23 recommendations to the Commissioner of Health and Human Services or the 
24 commissioner's designee. The department shall incorporate the daily rate payment system 
25 into the rate determinations required by section 1.
26Sec. 3. Rate and payment system implementation. Resolved:  That, no later 
27 than January 31, 2025, the Department of Health and Human Services shall implement the 
28 rates required to be determined under section 1 and the rate payment system developed 
29 under section 2.
30SUMMARY
31 This resolve directs the Department of Health and Human Services to conduct rate 
32 studies and rate determinations for home and community benefits and support services for 
33 members of the MaineCare program with intellectual disabilities or autism spectrum 
34 disorder. The Office of Aging and Disability Services within the department is directed to 
35 convene a working group to advise the department on the development of a daily rate 
36 payment system for agency home support service providers to be incorporated into the rate 
37 determinations. The department is required to implement the rates determined no later than 
38 January 31, 2025.31
