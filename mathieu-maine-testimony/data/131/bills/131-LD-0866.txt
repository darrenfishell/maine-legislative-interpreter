4 L.  Temporary If allowed by a municipal ordinance or a rule or ordinance adopted by 
5 a land use planning committee, temporary signs placed within the public right-of-way 
6 for a maximum of 12 weeks per calendar year, except that a temporary sign may not 
7 be placed within the public right-of-way for more than 6 weeks from January 1st to 
8 June 30th or for more than 6 weeks from July 1st to December 31st.  A temporary sign 
9 may not be placed within 30 feet of another temporary sign bearing the same or 
10 substantially the same message.  A temporary sign may not exceed 4 feet by 8 feet in 
11 size.  A sign under this paragraph must include or be marked with the name and address 
12 of the individual, entity or organization that placed the sign within the public right-of-
13 way and the date the sign was erected within the public right-of-way.
14SUMMARY
15 This bill replaces statewide requirements for the placement and duration of installation 
16 of temporary signs within the public right-of-way with a provision that imposes the 
17 requirements only if signs are allowed by an ordinance or rule adopted by a municipality 
18 or land use planning committee.15
