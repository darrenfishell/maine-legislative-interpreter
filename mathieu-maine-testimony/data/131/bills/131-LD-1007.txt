Page 1 - 131LR1387(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, employees of the State are issued computers, cellular telephones and other 
4 necessary electronic devices to carry out their duties in their employment by the State; and
5Whereas, certain software applications and programs such as TikTok represent a 
6 potential security risk as they may contain hidden and unknown capabilities to install 
7 spyware or otherwise compromise the integrity and security of official state 
8 communications and business that may be contained on state computers and devices; and
9Whereas, employees of the State may have currently installed or used TikTok or other 
10 concerning software applications and programs in the absence of a policy or official 
11 restrictions against doing so; and
12Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
13 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
14 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
15 therefore,
16Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
17Sec. 1.  5 MRSA ยง20-A, sub-ยง4 is enacted to read:
184.  Computers and other electronic devices.  A computer or other electronic device 
19 owned by the State may not be used to access, contain or download any program, 
20 application or electronic service prohibited by the Commissioner of Administrative and 
21 Financial Services, in accordance with rules adopted by the commissioner. For purposes of 
22 this section, "electronic device" has the same meaning as in Title 16, section 647, 
23 subsection 3. Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules under 
24 Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
25Sec. 2. Commissioner of Administrative and Financial Services to adopt 
26rules to prohibit the video hosting service TikTok from all state computers and 
27electronic devices.  Pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 5, section 20-A, 
28 subsection 4, the Commissioner of Administrative and Financial Services shall adopt rules 
29 to prohibit the use, presence or downloading of the video hosting service TikTok, owned 
30 by ByteDance Ltd., which is incorporated in the Cayman Islands, on any state computer or 
31 other electronic device.
32Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
33 takes effect when approved.
34SUMMARY
35 This bill authorizes the Commissioner of Administrative and Financial Services to 
36 prohibit any program, application or electronic service on a state-owned computer or other 
37 electronic device and directs the commissioner to prohibit the access, presence or 
38 downloading of the video hosting service TikTok on all state computers and electronic 
39 devices.35
