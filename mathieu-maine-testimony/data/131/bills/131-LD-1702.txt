22 people who have resettled in the State, such as asylum seekers, refugees and

26 D.  One member who is a student who has attended a public school in the State, selected

10Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
11 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the 
12 commission.
13Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
14 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
15 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
16 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
17 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
18 after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
19 made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for 
20 the commission to meet and conduct its business.
21Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall review the current barriers 
22 for people who have resettled in this State, such as asylum seekers, refugees and immigrants 
23 to access and successfully participate in public education programs and develop 
24 recommendations to improve the experience of people who have resettled in this State, 
25 such as asylum seekers, refugees and immigrants in public schools.  In undertaking its 
26 review, the commission shall provide the opportunity for input from affected communities, 
27 including, but not limited to, students who are asylum seekers, refugees or immigrants, 
28 their families and public school teachers and administrators.
29Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
30 necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff support 
31 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
32Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 6, 2023, the commission 
33 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
34 legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs and the joint 
35 standing committee may report out legislation related to the report to the Second Regular 
36 Session of the 131st Legislature.
37SUMMARY
38 This resolve establishes the Commission to Improve the Educational Experience of 
39 People Who Are Resettling in Maine to review the current barriers for people who have 
40 resettled in the State, such as asylum seekers, refugees and immigrants to access and 
41 successfully participate in public education programs.  By December 6, 2023, the 
42 commission must submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including 38
