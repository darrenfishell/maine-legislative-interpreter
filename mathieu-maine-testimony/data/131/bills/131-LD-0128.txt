2Sec. 1.  29-A MRSA §523, sub-§3, as amended by PL 2017, c. 43, §2 and PL 2019, 
3 c. 377, §6, is further amended to read:
43.  Special veterans registration plates.  The Secretary of State, on application and 
5 evidence of payment of the excise tax required by Title 36, section 1482 and the registration 
6 fee required by section 501 or by section 504, subsection 1 for a vehicle with a registered 
7 gross weight over 10,000 pounds, shall issue a registration certificate and a set of special 
8 veterans registration plates to be used in lieu of regular registration plates for a vehicle with 
9 a registered gross weight of not more than 26,000 pounds to any person who has served in 
10 the United States Armed Forces and who has been honorably discharged or to a person who 
11 has served in the United States Armed Forces for at least 3 years and continues to serve.  If 
12 a person who qualifies for a special veterans registration plate under this subsection is the 
13 primary driver of 3 4 vehicles, the Secretary of State may issue in accordance with this 
14 section a set of special veterans registration plates for each vehicle.
15 Each application must be accompanied by the applicant's Armed Forces Report of Transfer 
16 or Discharge, DD Form 214, certification from the United States Veterans Administration 
17 or the appropriate branch of the United States Armed Forces verifying the applicant's 
18 military service and honorable discharge, or a letter from the Department of Defense, 
19 Veterans and Emergency Management, Maine Bureau of Veterans' Services verifying 
20 active duty military service and length of service.
21 The Secretary of State shall recall a special veterans registration plate of a recipient who 
22 has been less than honorably discharged from the United States Armed Forces.
23 All surplus revenue collected for issuance of the special registration plates is retained by 
24 the Secretary of State to maintain and support this program.
25 The surviving spouse of a special veteran plate recipient issued plates in accordance with 
26 this subsection may retain and display the special veteran plates as long as the surviving 
27 spouse remains unmarried.  Upon remarriage, the surviving spouse may not use the special 
28 veteran plates on a motor vehicle, but may retain them as a keepsake.  Upon the death of 
29 the surviving spouse, the family may retain the special veteran plates, but may not use them 
30 on a motor vehicle.
31 The Secretary of State may issue a special disability registration plate for veterans in 
32 accordance with section 521, subsections 1, 5, 7 and 9.  The special disability registration 
33 plate for veterans must bear the International Symbol of Access.
34 The Secretary of State may issue a set of special veterans registration plates when the 
35 qualifying veteran is the primary driver of a company-owned vehicle if:
36 A.  The company is owned solely by a veteran who qualifies for a veteran plate under 
37 this section;
38 B.  The vehicle is leased by a veteran who qualifies for the veteran plate under this 
39 subsection; or
40 C.  The vehicle is leased by the employer of a veteran who qualifies for the veteran 
41 plate and the employer has assigned the vehicle exclusively to the veteran.  The 
42 employer must attest in writing that the veteran will have exclusive use of the vehicle 
43 and agrees to the display of the special veteran plate.

33-A.  Motorcycle plates; veterans.  In addition to any plate issued pursuant to

11 or Discharge, DD Form 214, certification from the United States Department of Veterans 
12 Affairs or the appropriate branch of the United States Armed Forces verifying the 
13 applicant's military service and honorable discharge, or a letter from the Department of 
14 Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management, Maine Bureau of Veterans' Services 
15 verifying active duty military service and length of service.
16 The Secretary of State shall recall a special veterans registration plate of a recipient who 
17 has been less than honorably discharged from the United States Armed Forces.
18 All surplus revenue collected for issuance of the special veterans registration plates is 
19 retained by the Secretary of State to maintain and support this program.
20 Upon request the Secretary of State shall issue special veterans registration plates for a 
21 motorcycle that are also vanity plates.  These plates are issued in accordance with this 
22 section and section 453.  Vanity plates issued under this subsection may not duplicate 
23 vanity plates issued in another class of plate.
24 The surviving spouse of a recipient of a special veterans registration plate issued in 
25 accordance with this subsection may retain and use the plate or plates as long as the 
26 surviving spouse remains unmarried.  Upon remarriage, the surviving spouse may not use 
27 the plate or plates, but may retain them.  Upon the death of the surviving spouse, the family 
28 may retain the plate or plates, but may not use them.
29 The Secretary of State may not issue special commemorative decals under subsection 5 or 
30 6 for use on special veterans registration plates for a motorcycle.
