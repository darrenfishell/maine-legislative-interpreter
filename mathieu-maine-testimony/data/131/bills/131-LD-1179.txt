222. Term.  A council member serves a 3-year term and continues serving until a 
23 successor is duly appointed and qualified. A member may not serve for more than 2 
24 consecutive terms. In the case of a vacancy, the commissioner shall promptly fill the 
25 vacancy.
263. Quorum.  A quorum is a majority of the members of the council.
274. Chairs and officers.  The council shall annually elect one of its members to serve 
28 as chair for a one-year term. The council may select other officers and designate their 
29 duties.
305. Meetings.  The council shall meet at least 4 times a year. It may also meet at other 
31 times at the call of the chair or the chair's designee or the commissioner or the 
32 commissioner's designee.
336. Purpose.  The council shall advise the commissioner on activities of the department 
34 that relate to marine recreational fishing and shall make recommendations to the 
35 commissioner concerning expenditures from the Marine Recreation Fishing Conservation 
36 and Management Fund for the purposes described in section 6032-A. The council may 
37 investigate problems affecting marine recreational fishing and make recommendations to 
38 the commissioner and the Marine Resources Advisory Council, established by Title 5, 
39 section 12004-G, subsection 27, concerning its investigations. The council may review 
40 current recreational fishing research programs and plans for research and submit to the 
41 commissioner and the Marine Resources Advisory Council annually its recommendations 
42 on those programs and plans.

7SUMMARY
8 This bill reestablishes the Marine Recreational Fishing Advisory Council.  The council 
9 was eliminated by Public Law 2009, chapter 369.  It also provides for the chair of the 
10 Marine Recreational Fishing Advisory Council to serve on the Marine Resources Advisory 
11 Council.  It also requires the Governor to appoint a member of a federally recognized Indian 
12 nation, tribe or band in the State.8
