2Sec. 1.  21-A MRSA §682, sub-§2, as amended by PL 2019, c. 371, §21, is further 
3 amended by amending the first blocked paragraph to read:
4 These limitations do not prohibit a candidate from attending the voting place and orally 
5 communicating with voters as long as the candidate does not attempt to influence their 
6 vote.  A candidate may not state the name of the office sought or request a person's vote , 
7 or no more than one representative of a candidate, from greeting a voter if the candidate or 
8 representative of the candidate does not state the name of the office that the candidate is 
9 seeking in that election year or wear any button, name tag, apparel or label or have or use 
10 any item or sign displaying the candidate's name or the name of the office the candidate is 
11 seeking or otherwise express support for or opposition to a party, a candidate or a ballot 
12 question.
13Sec. 2.  21-A MRSA §682, sub-§3, ¶A, as amended by PL 2009, c. 253, §27, is 
14 further amended to read:
15 A.  This subsection does not apply to advertising material on automobiles traveling to 
16 and from the voting place for the purposes of voting.  It does not prohibit a person who 
17 is at the polls solely for the purpose of voting from wearing clothing, a campaign button  
18 when the longest dimension of the button does not exceed 3 inches  or a hat that displays 
19 the name of a candidate or an advertising or campaign message if the message does not 
20 expressly advocate for the passage or defeat of a ballot question or election or defeat 
21 of a candidate for an office that is on the ballot for the election that day.
22SUMMARY
23 This bill places restrictions on the speech of candidates and representatives of 
24 candidates and buttons, clothing and other items at voting places.23
