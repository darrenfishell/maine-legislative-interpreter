13 responsible and efficient growth of the blue economy in Maine; and

18Sec. 1.  Task force established.  Resolved: That the Blue Economy Task Force to 
19 Support Maine’s Emergence as a Center for Blue Economy Innovation and Opportunity in 
20 the 21st Century, referred to in this resolve as "the task force," is established.
21Sec. 2.  Task force membership. Resolved: That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353,

9Sec. 3.  Chairs. Resolved: That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the Governor shall 
10 appoint 2 cochairs from among the task force’s members, one who is employed by a state 
11 agency and one who is a private citizen. The chairs shall set the schedule and agenda for 
12 task force meetings and preside at the meetings.
13Sec. 4.  Appointments. Resolved: That appointments to the task force must occur 
14 no later than August 31, 2023.
15Sec. 5.  Duties. Resolved: That the task force shall develop a strategy and 
16 recommend a specific action plan to accelerate the State's emergence as an important 
17 contributor to the sustainable use of the ocean's resources for economic health, improved 
18 livelihoods and jobs and ecosystem health, referred to in this resolve as "the blue economy." 
19 The strategy must identify the State's current and potential future opportunities and 
20 challenges in the blue economy. The action plan must identify and recommend solutions 
21 to leverage the State's competitive advantages and overcome potential technical, economic, 
22 regulatory and other challenges to expand the blue economy. In developing its strategy and 
23 recommending its action plan, the task force shall consider and make recommendations 
24 regarding the following:
25 1. The State's opportunity to expand the blue economy and attract blue economy 
26 investment to the State by:
27 A.  Identifying the components of the blue economy relevant to the State;
28 B.  Describing and quantifying the scale of the State's existing blue economy;
29 C.  Identifying the State's current and potential future competitive advantages in the 
30 blue economy; and
31 D.  Quantifying the State's blue economy growth opportunity by 2035 and 2050;
32 2. Constraints to the growth of the blue economy by:
33 A.  Reviewing relevant local, state and federal environmental and investment rules and 
34 regulations to identify regulatory constraints to the growth of the blue economy;
35 B.  Reviewing state agency structure, staff resources and administrative processes to 
36 identify agency constraints to the growth of the blue economy; and
37 C.  Developing recommendations to amend environmental and investment rules and 
38 regulations, and alter state agency structure, enhance staff resources and improve 
39 administrative processes to facilitate the growth of the blue economy; and
Page 3 - 131LR1268(01)1 3. Strategies to attract blue economy investment to the State and expand the blue 
2 economy in the State by:
3 A.  Considering the cumulative recommendations in the reports of SEAMaine, the 
4 Maine Aquaculture Roadmap and the Maine's Offshore Wind Roadmap to inform a 
5 unified vision for blue economy growth in Maine;
6 B.  Identifying the threats of climate change to the State's seafood economy and making 
7 recommendations to ameliorate the threats through modernization and technology 
8 improvements, decarbonization strategies and other means of diversification and 
9 adaptation to changing conditions;
10 C.  Identifying the State's current assets and areas of competitive advantage in the 
11 emerging blue economy and making recommendations to support existing and new 
12 businesses in those areas;
13 D.  Identifying the State's potential future competitive opportunities in the blue 
14 economy and making recommendations to attract investment to develop those 
15 opportunities;
16 E.  Identifying blue economy growth goals, such as revenues, jobs and metrics related 
17 to the growth goals, including intellectual property and multiplier effects for 2035 and 
18 2050;
19 F.  Identifying the State's institutional capacities to support the growth of the blue 
20 economy, opportunities to coordinate or integrate such capacities and needs and 
21 opportunities to invest in and strengthen those capacities;
22 G.  Identifying the State's working waterfront and industry support infrastructure 
23 capacities and evolving needs to accommodate the emerging blue economy;
24 H.  Identifying current and future workforce development needs to support the growth 
25 of the blue economy in the State; and
26 I.  Identifying current or future public and private investment vehicles, including state 
27 and federal funding and private investment, required to expand the State's blue 
28 economy.
29Sec. 6.  Staff assistance. Resolved: That the Office of Policy Innovation and the 
30 Future, the Department of Economic and Community Development, the Department of 
31 Environmental Protection, the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry and 
32 the Department of Marine Resources shall provide staff assistance to the task force.  At the 
33 request of the task force, other state agencies and state entities shall provide information 
34 and analysis to assist the task force in its deliberations and reporting.
35Sec. 7.  Report. Resolved: That the task force shall prepare and submit a written 
36 report to the Governor and the Legislature on its interim findings no later than December 
37 6, 2023.  The task force shall prepare and submit a written report of its final 
38 recommendations to the Governor, the President of the Senate and the Speaker of the House 
39 no later than November 6, 2024.
40Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
41 takes effect when approved.
