2 This bill makes the following changes to the State's cannabis laws.  It amends:
3 1.  The Cannabis Legalization Act to eliminate the tier 4 cultivation facility license, 
4 which authorizes a licensee to cultivate up to 20,000 square feet of plant canopy, and 
5 eliminate the option for a licensee to apply to increase its tier 4 plant canopy by 7,000 
6 square feet.  It also authorizes a tier 3 cultivation facility licensee to cultivate not more than 
7 7,000 square feet if cultivated indoors and not more than 21,000 square feet if cultivated 
8 outdoors.  It also changes from 30,000 to 28,000 square feet the maximum cultivation area 
9 for holders of multiple cultivation facility licenses.  It makes these changes effective 
10 October 15, 2024; and
11 2.  The Maine Medical Use of Cannabis Act to establish an upper limit for a dispensary 
12 to cultivate up to 7,000 square feet of mature plant canopy and to prohibit the Department 
13 of Administrative and Financial Services, office of cannabis policy from requiring on-site 
14 parking as a security measure for dispensaries.  It also authorizes a registered dispensary to 
15 cultivate up to 7,000 square feet if cultivated indoors and up to 21,000 square feet if 
16 cultivated outdoors.2
