5Sec. 2.  30-A MRSA §4852, sub-§4 is enacted to read:
64.  No-interest loans for new affordable housing.  The Maine State Housing 
7 Authority shall develop and administer within the Housing Opportunities for Maine 
8 Program a plan to provide no-interest loans to property owners to support the development 
9 of affordable housing.  The plan must include at least the following elements.
10 A. The plan must provide a loan of up to $50,000 to each eligible property owner to 
11 develop and offer for rent an affordable housing unit.  An eligible property owner may 
12 receive up to 4 loans for 4 such housing units.  A development may involve conversion 
13 of an existing unit or new construction, in accordance with standards established by the 
14 authority by rule. 
15 B.  Under the plan, in order to be eligible for a loan, a property owner shall sign an 
16 agreement acceptable to the authority that commits the property owner and any 
17 successor in interest to:
18 (1) Ensuring the development and its operation complies with all applicable local 
19 and state requirements and codes; 
20 (2) Offering the affordable housing units for a period of 25 years at rent that is 
21 equivalent to no more than 30% of the income of a household whose income is at 
22 or below 80% of area median income, as determined by the authority by rule; and
23 (3) Renting the affordable housing units in accordance with the fair housing 
24 requirements of the Maine Human Rights Act and on a renewable term basis.  In 
25 no case may a rental be for any renewable term that is less than 28 days.

5SUMMARY
6 This bill does the following:
7 1. It modifies the Maine State Housing Authority's Housing Opportunities for Maine 
8 Program to provide no-interest loans to property owners to support the development of 
9 affordable housing.  Loans of up to $50,000 are available to an eligible property owner to 
10 develop and offer for rent an affordable housing unit.  An eligible property owner may 
11 receive up to 4 loans for 4 such housing units.  A development may involve conversion of 
12 an existing unit or new construction, in accordance with standards established by the 
13 authority by rule. In order to receive a loan, a property owner must sign an agreement 
14 acceptable to the authority that commits the property owner and any successor in interest 
15 to: ensuring the development and its operation complies with all applicable local and state 
16 requirements and codes; offering the housing units for a period of 25 years at rent that is 
17 equivalent to no more than 30% of the income of a household whose income is at or below 
18 80% of area median income, as determined by the authority by rule; and renting the housing 
19 units in accordance with the fair housing requirements of the Maine Human Rights Act and 
20 on a renewable basis;
21 2. It modifies the distribution of the real estate transfer tax beginning in fiscal year 
22 2024-25.  It provides that 80% of the real estate transfer tax goes to the Maine State Housing 
23 Authority first to continue to meet the authority's obligations relating to bonds issued or 
24 planned to be issued by the authority under the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 30‑A, section 
25 4864 and 2nd for deposit in the Housing Opportunities for Maine Fund.  Ten percent goes 
26 to the General Fund and 10% remains with the registry of deeds; and
27 3. It modifies the property tax fairness income tax credit to increase the benefit amounts 
28 by 50% and to increase the income-related eligibly thresholds by 50%.1
