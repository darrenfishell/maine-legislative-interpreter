49.  Advisory organizations.  Obtain the services of educational advisory organizations.  
5 The Legislature recognizes the Maine School Management Association and the Maine 
6 School Boards Association as nonprofit advisory organizations and declares these 
7 associations to be instrumentalities of their member school administrative units, municipal 
8 and quasi-municipal corporations with their assets upon their dissolution to be delivered to 
9 the Treasurer of State to be held in custody for the municipalities of the State.  The 
10 Legislature recognizes the Maine Association for the Education of Young Children as a 
11 nonprofit advisory organization and declares this association to be an instrumentality of its 
12 member-licensed child care programs.  An educational advisory organization may receive 
13 federal and state grants or contributions for their activities with respect to the solution of 
14 local problems.
15 A municipality may provide health or remedial services to nonpublic school pupils as 
16 authorized by this section only if those services are available to pupils attending the public 
17 school serving the municipality.
18 Health and remedial services and instructional materials and equipment provided for the 
19 benefit of nonpublic school pupils under this section and the admission of pupils to the 
20 nonpublic schools must be provided without distinction as to race, creed, color, the national 
21 origin of the pupils or of their teachers.  No instructional materials or instructional 
22 equipment may be loaned to pupils in nonpublic schools or their parents unless similar 
23 instructional material or instructional equipment is available for pupils in a public school 
24 served by a municipality.
25SUMMARY
26 This bill provides that the Legislature recognizes the Maine Association for the 
27 Education of Young Children as a nonprofit advisory organization and declares this 
28 association to be an instrumentality of its member-licensed child care programs.  It also 
29 provides that an educational advisory organization may receive state grants or contributions 
30 for its activities with respect to the solution of local problems.26
