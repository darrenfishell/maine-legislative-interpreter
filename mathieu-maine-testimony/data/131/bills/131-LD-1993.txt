51.  Voluntary universal home visiting program.  The department, as permitted by 
6 the availability of funds, shall offer a voluntary universal home visiting program for new 
7 families with children from the prenatal stage of development through 5 years of age, 
8 regardless of family income level.  The home visiting program must incorporate the 
9 following principles:
10 A.  Healthy and strong parent-child attachment;
11 B.  Physical and behavioral health of the family;
12 C.  Reduced incidence of child abuse and neglect;
13 D.  Positive and creative learning environments for the child;
14 E.  Effective and positive parenting;
15 F.  Parental competencies and self-confidence;
16 G.  Reducing family isolation through community linkages;
17 H.  School readiness; and
18 I.  Family self-sufficiency.
19Sec. 2.  22 MRSA §3174-KKK is enacted to read:
20§3174-KKK.  Reimbursement for home visiting services
21 The department shall provide for the reimbursement under the MaineCare program for 
22 home visiting services under section 262 for MaineCare members.  The department shall 
23 adopt rules to implement this section.  In adopting rules under this section, the department 
24 shall consult with and consider the expertise of home visiting experts related to the rate of 
25 reimbursement.  Rules adopted pursuant to this section are routine technical rules as defined 
26 in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.  The department shall reevaluate reimbursement 
27 rates adopted pursuant to this section every 2 years.
28Sec. 3.  22 MRSA §3174-LLL is enacted to read:
29§3174-LLL.  Reimbursement for perinatal doula services
30 The department shall provide for the reimbursement under the MaineCare program for 
31 perinatal doula services.  Perinatal doula services must be provided within an existing 
32 program provided by the department, such as the voluntary universal home visiting 
33 program under section 262.  For the purposes of this section, "perinatal doula services" 
34 means regular physical, emotional or educational support to pregnant MaineCare members 
35 before, during and after childbirth for up to 12 months by a trained provider.  The 
36 department shall adopt rules to implement this section.  In adopting rules under this section, 
37 the department shall consult with and consider the expertise of doula experts, doula training 
38 providers and practicing doulas related to the rate of reimbursement.  Rules adopted 
39 pursuant to this section are routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, 
Page 2 - 131LR2389(01)40 subchapter 2-A.  The department shall reevaluate reimbursement rates adopted pursuant to 
41 this section every 2 years.
3Sec. 4.  22 MRSA §3174-MMM is enacted to read:
4§3174-MMM.  Reimbursement for services provided by certified professional 
5midwives
6 The department shall provide for the reimbursement under the MaineCare program for 
7 services provided by a certified professional midwife.  Services provided by certified 
8 professional midwives may be provided within the voluntary universal home visiting 
9 program under section 262.  For the purposes of this section, "certified professional 
10 midwife" has the same meaning as in Title 32, section 12501, subsection 4-B.  The 
11 department shall adopt rules to implement this section.  In adopting rules under this section, 
12 the department shall consult with and consider the expertise of certified professional 
13 midwives related to the rate of reimbursement.  Rules adopted pursuant to this section are 
14 routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.  The department 
15 shall reevaluate reimbursement rates adopted pursuant to this section every 2 years.
16Sec. 5.  Federal Medicaid waivers or state plan amendments.  No later than 6 
17 months after the effective date of this Act, the Department of Health and Human Services 
18 shall submit to the United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for 
19 Medicare and Medicaid Services any waivers or state plan amendments determined 
20 necessary in order to accomplish the purposes of this Act.
21SUMMARY
22 This bill requires the Department of Health and Human Services to:
23 1. Offer a voluntary universal home visiting program for new families with children; 
24 and
25 2. Provide for reimbursement under the MaineCare program for:
26 A. Home visiting services;
27 B. Perinatal doula services; and
28 C. Services provided by certified professional midwives.1
