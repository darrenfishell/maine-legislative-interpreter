24§8371.  Child Care Appeal Review Panel
25 The Child Care Appeal Review Panel, established by Title 5, section 12004-G, 
26 subsection 5-B, referred to in this chapter as "the review panel," shall review disputes 
27 between the department and a child care facility, as defined in section 8301-A, subsection 
28 1-A, paragraph B, a family child care provider as defined in section 8301-A, subsection 
29 1-A, paragraph C or a nursery school as defined in section 8301-A, subsection 1-A, 
30 paragraph D, collectively referred to in this chapter as "the facility," relating to compliance 
31 with licensing complaints and disputes.  The review panel may not review investigations 
32 of out-of-home child abuse and neglect.
331.  Membership.  The review panel has 10 voting members appointed by the Governor 
34 as follows:
35 A.  Three early childhood professionals;
36 B.  One pediatric health care professional;1
2
Page 5 - 131LR1057(01)1 C.  One parent of a child in a child care facility as defined in section 8301-A, subsection 
2 1-A, paragraph B or family child care provider as defined in section 8301-A, subsection 
3 1-A, paragraph C; and
4 D.  Five members representing a range of facilities from different geographic areas of 
5 the State.
6 The director of the department's Division of Licensing and Regulatory Services or the 
7 director's designee shall chair the review panel and is a nonvoting member.
82.  Legal counsel.  The Office of the Attorney General shall provide legal counsel for 
9 the review panel.
103.  Terms.  Voting members of the review panel are appointed for 5-year terms. A 
11 voting member of the review panel appointed to fill a vacancy occurring other than by 
12 expiration of a term is appointed only for the unexpired term.
134.  Duties.  The review panel shall review disputes between the department and a 
14 facility. The review panel shall, as it considers necessary, review records and conduct 
15 interviews or inspections, make a determination of fault and, if necessary, determine 
16 appropriate action to be taken by the department or the facility.  Determinations of the 
17 review panel subject to appeal in accordance with the Maine Administrative Procedure Act 
18 include the following:
19 A.  Revocation or suspension of a license to operate a facility;
20 B.  Denial of an application for a license to operate a facility;
21 C.  Conversion of a license from regular to conditional status;
22 D.  Resolution of a dispute between a facility and the department concerning 
23 compliance with rules; and
24 E.  Denials of alternative compliance requests.
255.  Timely review of disputes.  A facility that is aggrieved by a decision by the 
26 department shall file a request for the review panel to review the dispute within 30 days of 
27 receiving the decision by the department. The review panel shall schedule an initial meeting 
28 to review the dispute within 14 days of a request by a facility. A final decision on the 
29 dispute must be made no later than 30 days after the review panel has met to review the 
30 dispute.  Any review conducted by the review panel must be conducted in accordance with 
31 Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 4.
326.  Authority.  The department and the facility shall abide by the final decision by the 
33 review panel.

17SUMMARY
18 This bill amends the laws related to child care providers as follows. 
19 1.  It establishes the Child Care Appeal Review Panel to review and make 
20 determinations of disputes between the Department of Health and Human Services and a 
21 child care facility, family child care provider or nursery school relating to compliance with 
22 licensing or certification, complaints and disputes.
23 2.  It requires the department to establish payment rates for child care services that are 
24 at least equal to the 75th percentile of local market rates and requires that payment rates for 
25 child care services for children with special needs be higher than the 75th percentile.
26 3.  It establishes a license for a period of 5 years to a child care facility or family child 
27 care provider that has been continuously in operation for at least 5 years and has completed 
28 satisfactory inspections.
29 4.  It limits an inspection as a result of a complaint to the substance of the complaint.
30 5.  It authorizes conditional employment for up to 90 days for an individual working in 
31 a child care facility.
32 6.  It authorizes a recipient of a child care subsidy provided by the department to pay 
33 the difference between the amount of the subsidy and the amount charged by the child care 
34 provider.
35 7.  It amends the definitions of "family child care provider," "nursery school" and 
36 "small child care facility" to increase the minimum number of children from 3 to 5.
37 8.  It removes the authority of the department to publish certain information regarding 
38 an investigation on the department's publicly accessible website.
39 9.  It requires the department to develop a plan for a sliding scale of income and subsidy 
40 to eliminate the so-called welfare cliff.18
Page 10 - 131LR1057(01)1 10.  It requires the department to amend its rules for child care facilities and family 
2 child care providers in order to change staff-child ratios and change requirements relating 
3 to inspections and the licensing process, among other changes.
