5ยง620.  Actions intended to prevent exercise of protected rights
6 An employer that takes action intended to prevent or penalize a person from exercising 
7 rights protected under this chapter commits a civil violation for which a fine of not less 
8 than $500 nor more than $1,000 for each violation may be adjudged, in addition to any 
9 other remedy available under this chapter. The Department of Labor and the Attorney 
10 General may file a civil action to enforce this section.
11SUMMARY
12 This bill provides that an employer that takes action intended to prevent or penalize a 
13 person from exercising rights protected under the laws governing employment practices 
14 commits a civil violation for which a fine of not less than $500 nor more than $1,000 for 
15 each violation may be adjudged, in addition to any other remedy available under the laws 
16 governing employment practices. The Department of Labor and the Attorney General may 
17 file a civil action to enforce the provisions of the bill.12
