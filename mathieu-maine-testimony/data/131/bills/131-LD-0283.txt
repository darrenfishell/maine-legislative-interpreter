39SUMMARY
40 Part A makes changes to the property tax law. Part A:
41 1.  Allows disclosure of confidential information to the State Board of Property Tax 
42 Review; 1
2
3
40
Page 6 - 131LR0123(01)1 2.  Clarifies that a municipality's equalized tax rate of residential property is the 
2 equalized full value tax rate when calculating qualification for sudden and severe disruption 
3 assistance; 
4 3.  Removes the requirement that updates to the property tax assessment manual be 
5 made by the State Tax Assessor by rule; 
6 4.  Clarifies that the fixed-dollar parsonage and personal property exemptions for 
7 houses of religious worship are based on just value;
8 5.  Clarifies that only individuals qualify for certain property tax exemptions for 
9 veterans and removes gender-specific terms in those provisions; 
10 6.  Facilitates the repeal of the Circuitbreaker Program, which has expired, by moving 
11 definitions from that program to current provisions; 
12 7.  Clarifies that the income and asset limitations of the state property tax deferral 
13 program apply to all owners of the property; and
14 8.  Makes other technical and grammatical changes to improve the clarity of property 
15 tax provisions.
16 Part B makes changes to the income tax law. Part B:
17 1.  Clarifies that a petition for reconsideration related to a determination issued by 
18 Maine Revenue Services must be filed within the specified time period with Maine 
19 Revenue Services in order to be available for review in Superior Court or by the Maine 
20 Board of Tax Appeals; 
21 2.  Repeals the Maine income modifications related to the expired federal domestic 
22 production activities deduction; 
23 3.  Repeals the Maine income modification for the expired federal unemployment 
24 compensation deduction;
25 4.  Repeals the Maine income modification for the expired federal deferral of income 
26 from discharge of indebtedness; 
27 5.  Repeals the expired Maine income modification for insurance premiums for long-
28 term care insurance; 
29 6.  Repeals the expired Maine income modification for federal recovery amounts 
30 included in federal adjusted gross income;
31 7.  Clarifies Maine Revenue Services' rule-making authority regarding the Maine 
32 research expense income tax credit by correcting an erroneous reference to the federal 
33 Internal Revenue Code of 1986, removing unnecessary and confusing language and making 
34 the rule-making requirement discretionary; 
35 8.  Clarifies that the benefit base under the property tax fairness credit includes property 
36 tax payments, and not payments of rent constituting property taxes, under the State's 
37 property tax deferral program; and 
38 9.  Provides that the additional credit for permanently and totally disabled veterans 
39 under the property tax fairness credit is limited to the total taxes paid by the taxpayer and 
40 by the State on behalf of the taxpayer pursuant to the property tax deferral program, if any.
