61.  Definition.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, 
7 "individuals eligible for home care services" means:
8 A. Individuals who are elderly or disabled and whom the department determines are 
9 eligible for care provided in a nursing facility setting in accordance with the procedures 
10 described in section 3174-I, but for whom care can be appropriately provided in the 
11 individual's home or other residential community setting under a waiver granted by the 
12 United States Department of Health and Human Services, Centers for Medicare and 
13 Medicaid Services for home-based and community-based care and who choose to 
14 receive care in the individual's home or other residential community setting; or
15 B. Individuals whom the department determines are eligible for private duty nursing 
16 and personal care services provided in a residential setting or other setting where 
17 normal life activities take place.
182.  Waiting list established.  The department shall establish and maintain a waiting 
19 list of individuals eligible for home care services.
203.  Publicly available information.  The department shall publish on its publicly 
21 accessible website the following real-time data relating to the waiting list established 
22 pursuant to subsection 2, including the number of:
23 A. Individuals eligible for home care services who are fully served;
24 B. Individuals eligible for home care services who are partially served and the number 
25 of unstaffed hours for those individuals;
26 C. Individuals eligible for home care services who are waiting for services and the 
27 number of unstaffed hours for those individuals;
28 D. Days, on average, individuals eligible for home care services wait to receive 
29 services; and
30 E. Individuals eligible for home care services, broken down by county, who are fully 
31 served and partially served.
324.  Annual report.  Beginning February 1, 2024 and annually thereafter, the 
33 department shall submit to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
34 jurisdiction over health and human services matters a report on the waiting list of 
35 individuals eligible for home care services established in accordance with this section.  The 
36 report must include the department's plans to clear the waiting list and ensure that 
37 individuals eligible for home care services are receiving services for all the hours for which 
38 they are eligible.

15SUMMARY
16 This bill requires the Department of Health and Human Services to establish a waiting 
17 list of individuals eligible for home care services under rule Chapter 101: MaineCare 
18 Benefits Manual, Chapters II and III, Section 19, Home and Community Benefits for the 
19 Elderly and Adults with Disabilities, and Section 96, Private Duty Nursing and Personal 
20 Care Services, and to publish certain information about the waiting list on its publicly 
21 accessible website.  It requires the department to submit an annual report to the joint 
22 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over health and human services 
23 matters relating to the waiting list.
24 The bill requires the department to contract with a 3rd-party entity to conduct 
25 assessments for children who have not attained 21 years of age who the department 
26 determines are eligible for private duty nursing and personal care services provided in a 
27 residential setting or other setting where normal life activities take place.16
