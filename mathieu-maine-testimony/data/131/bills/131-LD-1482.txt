12ยง490-GG.  New open-pit mining moratorium
13 Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, a person may not engage in open-
14 pit mining in a quarry under this article if the person was not engaged in open-pit mining 
15 in that quarry on or prior to December 31, 2022.  For purposes of this section, "open-pit 
16 mining" means the process of excavating rock by use of surface pits or surface excavation.
17 This section is repealed January 1, 2027.
18Sec. 2. Department of Environmental Protection review of performance 
19standards for quarries. The Department of Environmental Protection shall review the

21 and develop recommendations to update the standards. The department shall consider 
22 public input during the review. By December 6, 2023, the department shall submit a report 
23 to the Joint Standing Committee on Environment and Natural Resources, which must 
24 include suggested legislation. The committee may submit legislation related to the report 
25 to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.

27Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
28 takes effect when approved.
29SUMMARY
30 This bill prohibits persons not engaged in open-pit mining on or prior to December 31, 
31 2022 from engaging in open-pit mining under the laws governing quarries.  The prohibition 
32 is retroactive to December 31, 2022 and is repealed January 1, 2027. The bill also requires 
33 the Department of Environmental Protection to review the statutory performance standards 
34 for quarries and submit recommended changes to the Joint Standing Committee on 
35 Environment and Natural Resources.30
