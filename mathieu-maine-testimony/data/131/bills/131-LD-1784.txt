Page 1 - 131LR1049(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, this legislation requires changes to certain MaineCare reimbursement rates 
4 and requires these changes to take effect July 1, 2023; and
5Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
6 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
7 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
8 therefore, be it
9Sec. 1. Department of Health and Human Services to rebase 
10reimbursement rates.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding any provision of law to the 
11 contrary, the Department of Health and Human Services shall rebase reimbursement rates, 
12 effective July 1, 2023, to reflect the increased costs experienced by nursing facilities, 
13 including, but not limited to, the cost of labor.
14Sec. 2. Department of Health and Human Services to establish enhanced 
15reimbursement rate. Resolved:  That the Department of Health and Human Services 
16 shall amend its rules in Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter III, Section 67, 
17 Principles of Reimbursement for Nursing Facilities, Section 22.3.3.4 to provide that, 
18 effective July 1, 2023, the upper limit on the base year case mix and regionally adjusted 
19 cost per day is the median multiplied by 130%.
20Sec. 3. Department of Health and Human Services to improve access to 
21nursing facilities for patients with special needs. Resolved:  That the Department 
22 of Health and Human Services shall work with nursing facilities to improve access to the 
23 facilities for patients with special needs, including, but not limited to, bariatric patients, 
24 patients in need of dialysis treatment, patients undergoing cancer treatment or long-term 
25 infusion therapy and patients with behavioral health needs.
26Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
27 takes effect when approved.
28SUMMARY
29 This resolve requires the Department of Health and Human Services to:
30 1. Rebase reimbursement rates, effective July 1, 2023, to reflect the increased costs 
31 experienced by nursing facilities;
32 2. Amend its rules in Chapter 101: MaineCare Benefits Manual, Chapter III, Section 
33 67, Principles of Reimbursement for Nursing Facilities to provide that, effective July 1, 
34 2023, the upper limit on the base year case mix and regionally adjusted cost per day is the 
35 median multiplied by 130%; and
36 3. Work with nursing facilities to improve access to the facilities for patients with 
37 certain special needs.29
