7Whereas, the federal Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 was passed over 32

12Whereas, Maine individuals with disabilities continue to face significant barriers in 
13 accessing Maine's state parks and historic sites; and
14Whereas, the first step to ensuring that Maine's state parks and historic sites are

17 to remediate those barriers; now, therefore, be it
18Sec. 1. Definitions.  Resolved: That, as used in this resolve, the following terms 
19 have the following meanings.
20 A. "Historic site" has the same meaning as in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 12, 
21 section 1801, subsection 5.
22 B. "Park" has the same meaning as in the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 12, section 
23 1801, subsection 7.
24Sec. 2. Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to conduct 
25accessibility study on parks and historic sites. Resolved: That the Department of 
26 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry shall conduct an accessibility study inspecting each 
27 park and historic site in the State to identify barriers to physical and program accessibility 
28 for individuals with disabilities.  In conducting the study, the department shall:
29 A. Engage individuals and entities with experience and expertise in conducting 
30 accessibility studies for developed outdoor areas;
31 B. Obtain input and guidance from individuals with a wide range of disabilities; and
32 C. Use the United States Department of Justice's 2010 ADA Standards for Accessible 
33 Design and the United States Access Board's published accessibility guidelines for 
34 developed outdoor areas.
35Sec. 3. Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to develop 
36remediation plan.  Resolved: That the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and 
37 Forestry shall develop a remediation plan based upon the accessibility study in section 2. 
38 In developing the remediation plan, the department shall obtain input and guidance in every 
39 stage of the development process from individuals with a wide range of disabilities. The 
40 remediation plan must:
41 A. List all of the barriers to physical and program accessibility identified by the 
42 accessibility study;
Page 2 - 131LR1259(01)1 B. Categorize the barriers listed in paragraph A and prioritize the barriers or categories 
2 of barriers for removal;
3 C. Provide a schedule with dates for the anticipated removal of each barrier or category 
4 of barriers; and
5 D. Include a reasonable estimate of the costs of removing each barrier or category of 
6 barriers.
7Sec. 4. Report. Resolved: That, by December 6, 2023, the Department of 
8 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry shall submit the plan required under section 3, 
9 along with any suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on Agriculture, 
10 Conservation and Forestry.  The Joint Standing Committee on Agriculture, Conservation 
11 and Forestry may submit legislation based upon the report to the Second Regular Session 
12 of the 131st Legislature. 
13SUMMARY
14 This resolve requires the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to 
15 conduct an accessibility study of every Maine state park and historic site to identify barriers 
16 to physical and program accessibility for individuals with disabilities and to develop a 
17 remediation plan to prioritize and remove the barriers. The department is required to submit 
18 the plan, with any recommended legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on 
19 Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry, which may submit legislation based upon the 
20 report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.14
