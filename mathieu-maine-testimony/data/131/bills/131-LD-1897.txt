3ยง710.  Island Road Maintenance Fund
41.  Fund established.  The Island Road Maintenance Fund, referred to in this section 
5 as "the fund," is established as a nonlapsing fund in the Department of Transportation.
62.  Use of funds.  Money deposited in the fund may be used by the Department of 
7 Transportation to provide grants to support public road maintenance for inhabited, 
8 unbridged island municipalities that are not served by the Maine State Ferry Service.
93.  Source of funds.  Money deposited in the fund includes, but is not limited to, 
10 revenue appropriated to the fund.  The fund may accept funds from other sources.
114.  Limit.  The balance in the fund may not exceed $5,000,000.
125.  Rules.  The Department of Transportation shall adopt routine technical rules as 
13 described in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A governing the making of grants from the 
14 fund to qualifying municipalities, with applications reviewed at least once annually.  The 
15 rules must provide that, when considering grant applications, the department must give 
16 preference to projects on islands that have a limited property tax base to support 
17 maintenance of public roads and projects that seek or have been granted funds from the 
18 Federal Government.

25SUMMARY
26 This bill establishes the Island Road Maintenance Fund in the Department of 
27 Transportation to support public road maintenance for inhabited, unbridged island 
28 municipalities that are not served by the Maine State Ferry Service.  In making grants from 
29 the fund, the department must give preference to projects on islands that have a limited 
30 property tax base to support maintenance of public roads and projects that seek or have 
31 been granted funds from the Federal Government.  The bill appropriates $2,000,000 to the 
32 fund.25
