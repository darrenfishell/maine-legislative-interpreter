2Sec. 1.  29-A MRSA §101, sub-§33-A is enacted to read:
333-A.  Mini-truck.  "Mini-truck" means a 4-wheeled motor vehicle that:
4 A.  Is able to attain a speed of at least 25 miles per hour but not more than 60 miles per 
5 hour;
6 B.  Does not exceed 2,000 pounds in unloaded weight;
7 C.  Is powered by a 2-cylinder, 3-cylinder or 4-cylinder gasoline or electric engine; and
8 D.  Meets or exceeds the motor vehicle inspection requirements of chapter 15, 
9 subchapter 1.
10 "Mini-truck" does not include a low-speed vehicle.
11Sec. 2.  29-A MRSA §501, sub-§14 is enacted to read:
1214.  Mini-trucks.  The Secretary of State may issue a registration for a mini-truck upon 
13 application and payment of the annual fee under subsection 1. The registrant must provide 
14 a certificate of title required by section 651, proof of financial responsibility required by 
15 section 1601 and evidence of payment of the excise tax required by Title 36, section 1482.

22Sec. 4.  29-A MRSA §2093 is enacted to read:
23§2093.  Operation of mini-trucks
24 A person operating a mini-truck on a public way shall comply with the provisions of 
25 this chapter as they apply to the operator of an automobile.  A person operating a mini-
26 truck must possess a valid Class A, Class B or Class C driver's license pursuant to section 
27 1252.
28SUMMARY
29 This bill permits mini-trucks to be operated on public ways.  The mini-truck, a 4-
30 wheeled motor vehicle with a maximum speed of 60 miles per hour and a maximum weight 
31 of 2,000 pounds, must be registered and meet the same inspection standards as other motor 
32 vehicles in order to be operated on a public way.29
