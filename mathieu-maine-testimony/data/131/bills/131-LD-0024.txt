2Sec. 1.  12 MRSA §9321, sub-§1-A is enacted to read:
31-A.  Prohibition during red flag warning.  The director may not issue a permit or

14Sec. 3.  12 MRSA §9321-B is enacted to read:
15§9321-B.  Definitions
16 As used in this article, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms have 
17 the following meanings.
181.  Recreational campfire.  "Recreational campfire" means an outdoor fire that is used 
19 for cooking, personal warmth, light or ceremonial or aesthetic purposes and that is not a 
20 part of debris disposal.  "Recreational campfire" includes a residential fire contained within 
21 an outdoor fireplace.  "Recreational campfire" does not include a portable lantern designed 
22 to emit light resulting from combustion or a stove that is used inside a structure or living 
23 accommodation.
242.  Red flag warning.  "Red flag warning" means a forecast warning from the United 
25 States Department of Commerce, National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, 
26 National Weather Service or its successor, in conjunction with the bureau, that informs the 
27 public, firefighters and land management agencies that conditions are ideal for wildland 
28 fire combustion and rapid spread.  A red flag warning indicates for a given area that the 
29 temperature is warm, the humidity is very low and strong winds are expected and that these 
30 factors produce an increased risk of fire danger.

9SUMMARY
10 This bill prohibits open burning, including recreational campfires, whether the open 
11 burning is permitted or not, during periods in which a red flag warning is in effect.  The 
12 bill limits the size of recreational campfires, including those that do not require a permit.10
