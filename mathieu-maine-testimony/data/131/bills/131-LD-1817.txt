4IMMERSIVE OUTDOOR EDUCATION
5ยง5301.  Definitions
6 As used in this chapter, unless the context otherwise indicates, the following terms 
7 have the following meanings.
81.  Department.  "Department" means the Department of Agriculture, Conservation 
9 and Forestry.
102.  Immersive outdoor education.  "Immersive outdoor education" means 
11 experiential, holistic and interdisciplinary education that uses the outdoor setting to engage 
12 students in educational opportunities about the outdoors and natural resources and 
13 industries and supports the health and wellness of students.
143.  Outdoor education program provider.  "Outdoor education program provider" 
15 means an organization that qualifies as a tax-exempt organization under 26 United States 
16 Code, Section 501(c)(3) that offers immersive outdoor education.
17ยง5302.  Outdoor School for All Maine Students Program
181.  Program established.  The University of Maine Cooperative Extension shall 
19 establish and administer the Outdoor School for All Maine Students Program, referred to 
20 in this chapter as "the program," to support immersive outdoor education and to assist 
21 school administrative units in partnering with outdoor education programs.
222.  Grant requirements.  The University of Maine Cooperative Extension shall 
23 provide grants to implement the program in order to assist school administrative units in 
24 partnering with outdoor education program providers. Grants under the program must be:
25 A.  Distributed to an outdoor education program provider that meets the requirements 
26 of section 5303;
27 B.  Used to serve publicly funded students enrolled in grade 4 to grade 8 in schools 
28 operated by tribal school committees or students enrolled in grade 4 to grade 8 in public 
29 schools in the State, as determined by the sending school administrative unit and the 
30 outdoor education program provider based on community needs and the curriculum;
31 C.  Provided to the same school grade each year to ensure that students in subsequent 
32 years have an opportunity to participate in the program; and
33 D.  If program funds are limited, prioritized for schools and communities that are 
34 historically underserved by outdoor education, including, but not limited to, Indian 
35 tribes and nations, migrant students, schools with high numbers of students who qualify 
36 for free and reduced-price lunches, rural and remote schools, students in alternative 
37 education programs, students of color, English language learner students and students 
38 receiving special education services.
Page 2 - 131LR1018(01)1ยง5303.  Outdoor education program provider requirements
2 This section governs the requirements for outdoor education program providers.
31.  Program curricula.  The program shall:
4 A.  Use the outdoors and local ecosystems as the primary context to support hands-on 
5 learning, including lessons, meals and on-site living;
6 B.  Engage students in safe and age-appropriate activities;
7 C.  Focus on the State's outdoor heritage, science, engineering, technology, 
8 mathematics, health, environment and natural resources and sustainability by including 
9 a strong academic component that supports overall academic achievement and career 
10 trajectories and connects science with other subjects, to support students' understanding 
11 of:
12 (1)  Conserving and developing marine and estuarine resources;
13 (2)  Conducting scientific research;
14 (3)  Promoting the State's coastal fishing industries;
15 (4)  Protecting and managing fish, game and other wildlife and their habitats, 
16 including restoring endangered species;
17 (5)  Promoting agriculture, conservation, forestry, land management and the well-
18 being of domestic animals;
19 (6)  Safely enjoying the outdoors; and
20 (7)  Promoting public health;
21 D.  Develop students' social and emotional skills, including teamwork, citizenship, self-
22 awareness and community responsibility;
23 E.  Support students' health and wellness by developing an appreciation for education 
24 and recreation in the outdoors;
25 F.  Encourage hands-on engagement, observation, discovery, intellectual curiosity and 
26 problem-solving; and
27 G.  Support students and teachers to combat the learning loss associated with the 
28 coronavirus pandemic.
292.  Program structure.  An outdoor education program provider shall:
30 A.  Obtain an educational trip leader permit under section 12863 or operate in 
31 accordance with the requirements of section 12863 for educational trip leaders;
32 B.  Provide programs for 3 to 5 days in duration including up to 4 overnights, or day 
33 programs when overnight programs are impractical due to health, cultural or capacity 
34 considerations;
35 C.  Pursue a sustainable funding model;
36 D.  Collaborate with schools and teachers to access the program;
37 E.  Pledge to provide equitable access to the program to all students;
38 F.  Train educators in educational strategies and field work;
Page 3 - 131LR1018(01)1 G.  Develop and participate in an evaluation program in accordance with section 5305; 
2 and
3 H.  Promote high levels of accessibility for students with disabilities.
4ยง5304.  Outdoor School for All Maine Students Fund
51.  Fund established.  The Outdoor School for All Maine Students Fund, referred to 
6 in this section as "the fund," is established under the department. The department shall 
7 direct funding to the University of Maine Cooperative Extension to administer the grant 
8 funds and administer the program in accordance with subsection 2. The fund is nonlapsing 
9 and may be invested in the same manner as permitted for the investment of other state 
10 funds.
112.  Use of fund.  Beginning in school year 2023-2024, the department shall determine 
12 the funding needed for the program and disburse funds to the University of Maine 
13 Cooperative Extension. The funding must be sufficient for students in all geographic 
14 regions of the State to participate in the program for a 2-night, 3-day experience.
153.  Sources of fund.  The State Controller shall credit to the fund:
16 A.  All money appropriated or allocated by the Legislature for inclusion in the fund;
17 B.  Money from any other source, whether public or private, designated for deposit into 
18 or credited to the fund; and
19 C.  Interest earned or other investment income on balances in the fund.
20 The department and the University of Maine Cooperative Extension must receive funds 
21 for staff and for administrative costs associated with administering the program and the 
22 fund.
23ยง5305.  Report required
24 The department, the University of Maine Cooperative Extension and an outdoor 
25 education program provider that meets the requirements of section 5303 shall develop an 
26 evaluation program and longitudinal study on current and future demographics of students 
27 participating in the program for the purpose of ensuring equitable access and to understand 
28 the effects of outdoor education on emotional, educational and career trajectories of 
29 students who have participated in the program.  Beginning December 1, 2023 and annually 
30 thereafter, the department and the University of Maine Cooperative Extension shall submit 
31 a report evaluating the program to the joint standing committees of the Legislature having 
32 jurisdiction over education matters, inland fisheries and wildlife matters and agriculture, 
33 conservation and forestry matters. The report must include an emphasis on the accessibility 
34 of the program from a geographic, economic, ethnic and disability focus and the program's 
35 effect on state workforce goals.

41 take into consideration geographic and demographic diversity in membership and ensure
