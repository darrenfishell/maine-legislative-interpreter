6Sec. 2.  5 MRSA §9062, sub-§2-A is enacted to read:
72-A.  Hearings involving Department of Health and Human Services.  The 
8 Attorney General shall appoint a presiding officer pursuant to this subsection for a hearing 
9 involving the Department of Health and Human Services.
10 A. The Attorney General shall keep a list of persons to serve as presiding officers. The 
11 list must include a statement of the qualifications of each listed person.
12 B. A presiding officer must be an attorney who has been admitted to the practice of law 
13 in at least one jurisdiction or an individual with knowledge of relevant state and federal 
14 health and human services law.
15 C. The Attorney General shall enter into a contract with a presiding officer to serve as 
16 an impartial presiding officer at a hearing under this subchapter.
17 D. A presiding officer may not be a person who:
18 (1) Is an employee of an agency; or
19 (2) Has a personal or professional interest that would conflict with the person's 
20 objectivity in the impartial due process hearing.
21 E. A presiding officer shall attend periodic training sessions concerning health and 
22 human services practices and law as determined appropriate by the Attorney General.
23 F.  A presiding officer may not have a client involved in a health and human services 
24 matter in the State.
25 G. A presiding officer may not preside over a hearing in which there is a party:
26 (1) Whom the presiding officer has represented in any matter within the 
27 immediately preceding 12-month period; or  
28 (2) By whom the presiding officer has been employed during the immediately 
29 preceding 3-year period.
30 A presiding officer under this subsection is not an employee of the State.
31SUMMARY
32 This bill requires that, in an adjudicatory proceeding regarding the Department of 
33 Health and Human Services, the Attorney General must appoint the presiding officer from 
34 a list of qualified persons, who:
35 1. Must be licensed attorneys or knowledgeable in health and human services law;
36 2. Must enter into a contract with the Attorney General;
37 3. May not be an employee of a state agency or have a personal or professional conflict 
38 that would prevent the person from being fair and impartial;32
