10Sec. 1.  Working group.  Resolved:  That the Department of the Secretary of State, 
11 Bureau of Motor Vehicles shall convene a working group to study potential solutions 
12 regarding the cost of and access to driver education for underserved populations and low-
13 income families.  
14Sec. 2.  Working group membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint 
15 Rule 353, the working group membership is as follows:
16 1.  One member of the Senate who serves on the Joint Standing Committee on 
17 Transportation, appointed by the President of the Senate;
18 2.  One member of the House of Representatives who serves on the Joint Standing 
19 Committee on Transportation, appointed by the Speaker of the House;
20 3.  One member representing the driver education school industry, appointed by the 
21 Secretary of State;
22 4.  One member representing a State Police traffic division, appointed by the Chief of 
23 the State Police; 
24 5.  One or more members representing affected groups, including, but not limited to, 
25 low-income families, immigrant or refugee communities, tribal communities and foster 
26 children, appointed by the Secretary of State; 
27 6.  An employee of the Bureau of Motor Vehicles who is responsible for the oversight 
28 of driver education, appointed by the Secretary of State;
29 7.  The Commissioner of Education or the commissioner's designee;
30 8.  The Commissioner of Health and Human Services or the commissioner's designee;
31 9.  The Deputy Secretary of State having oversight over the Bureau of Motor Vehicles 
32 or the deputy secretary's designee; and
33 10. The Secretary of State or the secretary's designee.  
34Sec. 3.  Compensation.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, members 
35 of the working group may not be compensated for their work on the working group, but 
36 legislative members may receive the legislative per diem.  
37Sec. 4.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the working group shall:
38 1.  Identify and document the current status of the availability of the State's driver 
39 education program;
Page 2 - 131LR0838(01)1 2.  Examine and identify potential methods of providing driver education at a lower 
2 cost for underserved populations and low-income families;
3 3.  Identify possible funding mechanisms to pay for part or all of driver education for 
4 low-income families; and
5 4.  Make recommendations based on the findings of the working group.  
6Sec. 5.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the 
7 Department of the Secretary of State, Bureau of Motor Vehicles shall provide necessary 
8 staffing services to the working group, and Legislative Council staff support is not 
9 authorized.  
10Sec. 6.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 6, 2023, the working 
11 group shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations for presentation 
12 to the Joint Standing Committee on Transportation.  The Joint Standing Committee on 
13 Transportation is authorized to submit legislation related to the report to the Second 
14 Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.  
15SUMMARY
16 This resolve directs the Department of the Secretary of State, Bureau of Motor Vehicles 
17 to convene a working group to study potential solutions regarding the cost of and access to 
18 driver education for underserved populations and low-income families.  The working group 
19 is required to submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations for 
20 presentation to the Joint Standing Committee on Transportation, which is authorized to 
21 submit legislation related to the report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st 
22 Legislature.16
