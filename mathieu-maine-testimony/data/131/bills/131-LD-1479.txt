2to liquefied propane gas. Resolved: That the Public Utilities Commission, referred to

4 to discuss matters relating to liquefied propane gas, including:
5 1.  The amendment to then-existing law in Public Law 2019, chapter 592, section 1;
6 2.  Amending the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 23, section 3360-A to provide for the

9 systems that have an underground tank or pipe; and
10 3.  The appropriateness of exempting certain liquefied propane gas distribution systems 
11 that have an underground tank or pipe from membership and participation in an 
12 underground facility damage prevention system.
13Sec. 2.  Stakeholder group.  Resolved: That members of the stakeholder group in 
14 section 1 must include:
15 1.  One or more representatives from the Maine Energy Marketers Association;
16 2.  One or more representatives from the Propane Gas Association of New England;
17 3.  One or more individuals who own or operate a liquefied propane gas distribution 
18 system of various sizes that has an underground tank or pipe; and
19 4.  Any other person that the commission determines necessary to ensure that all 
20 interests regarding the discussion in section 1 are represented fairly.  
21Sec. 3.  Report. Resolved: That, by December 6, 2023, the commission shall submit 
22 a report summarizing the discussion under section 1 and any recommended legislation to 
23 the Joint Standing Committee on Energy, Utilities and Technology, which may report out 
24 legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.  
25SUMMARY
26 This resolve directs the Public Utilities Commission to convene a stakeholder group to 
27 discuss the provisions in law requiring an underground facility damage prevention system, 
28 commonly called the "Dig Safe Law," regarding liquefied propane gas systems that have 
29 an underground tank or pipe, including recent amendments made to the law regarding 
30 liquefied propane gas systems and reducing the administrative burden on or possible 
31 exemption of certain liquefied propane gas systems from membership and participation in 
32 an underground facility damage prevention system. The resolve directs the commission to 
33 submit a report summarizing the stakeholder group's discussion and any recommended 
34 legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.26
