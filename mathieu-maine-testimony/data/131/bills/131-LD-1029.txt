31Sec. 3. Appointment terms.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the 
32 Legislators appointed to the commission pursuant to section 2 serve terms coincident with 
33 their legislative terms and are appointed every 2 years.  Legislators may be reappointed to 
34 the commission and may continue to serve until their replacements are designated.  Other 
35 appointed members are appointed for terms that expire December 31, 2026; a vacancy is 
36 filled in the same manner as the original appointment.
37Sec. 4. Appointments; chair; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That, 
38 notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, all appointments must be made no later than 30 days 
39 following the effective date of this resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the 
Page 2 - 131LR0486(01)40 Executive Director of the Legislative Council once all appointments have been completed.  
41 When appointment of all members is complete, the Executive Director of the Legislative 
42 Council shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission no later than December 
43 1, 2023.  The Secretary of State shall name 5 members to constitute an executive 
44 committee, which shall act as chair of the commission.  The Secretary of State, with advice 
45 from the commission, also may appoint honorary members and shall establish an advisory 
46 council composed of people who are especially interested in the celebration of the United 
47 States Semiquincentennial.
9Sec. 5. Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall develop and coordinate the 
10 commemoration of the 250th anniversary of the founding of the United States of America, 
11 referred to in this resolve as "the commemoration," through educational and historical 
12 events, activities and programs throughout the State.  All agencies of the State and political 
13 subdivisions of the State shall cooperate with the commission and may grant funds, 
14 property and services to the commission to carry out the purposes of this resolve.  To carry 
15 out the purposes of this resolve the commission may:
16 1.  Enter into contracts that have been approved by the Secretary of State and the 
17 Attorney General;
18 2.  Adopt an official flag, seal or other emblem for the commemoration, copyright an 
19 official flag, seal or emblem in the name of the State and, with the approval of the Secretary 
20 of State, lease or sell the title or right to use the official flag, seal or emblem upon terms 
21 determined by the commission.  Any revenue from the lease or sale of the title or right to 
22 use an official flag, seal or emblem under this subsection must be credited to the fund 
23 established in section 12; and
24 3. Accept donations of money, property or services and the cooperation of the Federal 
25 Government and state, civic, patriotic and historical groups and educational institutions.  
26 Any donations of money under this subsection must be credited to the fund established in 
27 section 12.
28Sec. 6. Proclamation.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the 
29 Governor shall issue a proclamation inviting the people of the State and of the United States 
30 to participate in and observe the commemoration.
31Sec. 7. Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the 
32 Secretary of State may employ administrative staff to serve the commission.
33Sec. 8. Expenses.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the executive 
34 committee established under section 4 shall draft a commission budget and may pay 
35 necessary expenses of the commission from the fund established under section 12.  The 
36 Secretary of State may request additional appropriations from the Legislature for necessary 
37 expenses of the commission.
38Sec. 9. Compensation.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, a member 
39 of the commission may receive reimbursement for necessary expenses related to the 
40 performance of the member's duties on the commission.
41Sec. 10. Meetings.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the 
42 commission may hold up to 5 meetings a year, one of which must be a public hearing.  The 1

3Sec. 11. Report.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the commission 
4 shall submit an annual report to the Governor and the Legislature on its activities.
5Sec. 12. Maine Semiquincentennial Fund.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding 
6 Joint Rule 353, the Maine Semiquincentennial Fund, referred to in this resolve as "the 
7 fund," is established.  The fund is administered by the Department of the Secretary of State 
8 on behalf of the commission. Balances in the fund may not lapse and must be carried 
9 forward and used for the purposes of this resolve.  The commission may accept and deposit 
10 in the fund money from appropriations and private and public sources.  The Treasurer of 
11 State shall invest unexpended money in the fund and all proceeds of these investments 
12 accrue to the fund. The fund terminates on December 31, 2027 and any unexpended balance 
13 in the fund on December 31, 2027 must be transferred to the General Fund.
14SUMMARY
15 This resolve establishes the Maine Semiquincentennial Commission to develop and 
16 coordinate the commemoration of the 250th anniversary of the founding of the United 
17 States of America through educational and historical events, activities and programs 
18 throughout the State.1
