19Sec. 1. Commission established.  Resolved:  That the Commission to Study the 
20 Need to Codify or Recodify Laws Regarding Noncondominium Community Associations, 
21 referred to in this resolve as "the commission," is established.
22Sec. 2. Commission membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
23 353, the commission consists of 10 members appointed as follows:
24 1.  Five members of the Maine State Bar Association, including 2 members with a 
25 substantial practice in condominium or community association law, appointed by the Chief 
26 Justice of the Supreme Judicial Court;
27 2.  One member representing the State's lending community, appointed by the chairs 
28 of the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary;
29 3.  One member representing the State's realtor community, appointed by the chairs of 
30 the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary;
31 4.  One member representing the real estate development community, appointed by the 
32 Governor; and
33 5.  Two members representing unit owners or homeowners in community associations 
34 in the State, appointed by the Governor. 
35 The commission and the terms of its members expire upon adjournment of the Second 
36 Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.  In the event of the death or resignation of a 
37 member, the appointing authority shall appoint a qualified person for the remainder of the 
38 term.
39Sec. 3. Chairs.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the first-named 
40 member from the Maine State Bar Association shall serve as chair of the commission.

10Sec. 5. Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall:
11 1.  Review data on existing condominium and noncondominium community 
12 associations in the State;
13 2.  Review state statutes that govern and case law that affects noncondominium 
14 community association developments in the State;
15 3.  Review efforts in other states to address problems with community associations; 
16 and
17 4.  Consider the adoption of the Uniform Common Interest Ownership Act and other 
18 laws to determine whether that act or other legislation may be suitable for adoption in this 
19 State.
20Sec. 6. Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the 
21 Legislative Council shall provide necessary staffing services to the commission, except that 
22 Legislative Council staff support is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or 
23 special session.
24Sec. 7. Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 6, 2023, the commission 
25 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
26 legislation, for presentation to the Joint Standing Committee on Judiciary.  The Joint 
27 Standing Committee on Judiciary may submit legislation related to the report to the Second 
28 Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
29Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
30 takes effect when approved.
31SUMMARY
32 This resolve establishes the Commission to Study the Need to Codify or Recodify Laws 
33 Regarding Noncondominium Community Associations.  The 10-member commission is 
34 directed to review data on existing condominium and noncondominium community 
35 associations in the State, review state statutes that govern and case law that affects 
36 noncondominium community association developments, review efforts in other states to 
37 address problems with community associations and consider the adoption of uniform and 
38 other laws.  The commission is required to submit a report to the Joint Standing Committee 
39 on Judiciary, which may submit legislation to the Second Regular Session of the 131st 
40 Legislature.32
