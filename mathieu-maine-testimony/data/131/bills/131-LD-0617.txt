20SUMMARY
21 This bill directs the State Board of Education to adopt rules to amend the credentialing 
22 of education personnel to create a pathway for a teacher to obtain an endorsement on a 
23 teaching certificate for a component of industrial arts with an experiential laboratory that 
24 includes, but is not limited to: automotive body repair, diagnostics and mechanics; welding; 
25 electrical; carpentry; and computer-aided design.  Rules must include at least the following 
26 qualifications for a teacher to be eligible for an endorsement:  a completed apprenticeship 
27 registered with a statewide or national apprenticeship and training organization; a 
28 completed 2-year degree or certificate from a technical institution accredited by a national 
29 association of career and technical schools or similar organization; completion of a 
30 minimum number of hours of paid applied employment or teaching in the endorsement area 
31 being sought; and completion of a minimum number of hours of experience learning the 
32 trade or craft for which the endorsement is sought.  21
