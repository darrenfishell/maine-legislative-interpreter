Page 1 - 131LR1957(01)1Sec. 1.  Task force established.  Resolved:  That the Task Force on Cannabis 
2 Hospitality Establishments, referred to in this resolve as "the task force," is established.
3Sec. 2.  Task force membership.  Resolved:  Notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, 
4 that the task force consists of 17 members appointed as follows:
5 1.  Four members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including 
6 members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
7 2.  Four members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
8 House, including members from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
9 in the Legislature;
10 3.  The following members appointed by the President of the Senate:
11 A. One supporter of the 2016 cannabis legalization ballot measure;
12 B. One expert on cannabis policy;
13 C. One expert on training for alcohol industry employees;
14 D. One member representing the interests of adult use cannabis consumers; and
15 E. One member representing the interests of municipal governments; and
16 4.  The following members appointed by the Speaker of the House:
17 A. One member representing the interests of adult use cannabis licensees;
18 B. One member representing the interests of the hospitality industry;
19 C. One member representing law enforcement; and
20 D.  One member representing public health.
21Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That the first-named Senate member is the Senate chair 
22 and the first-named House of Representatives member is the House chair of the task force.
23Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of task force.  Resolved:  That all 
24 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
25 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
26 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
27 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the task force.  If 30 days or more after 
28 the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been made, the 
29 chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for the task 
30 force to meet and conduct its business.
31Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the task force shall review how other states regulate 
32 cannabis hospitality establishments and draft recommendations for a bill to regulate 
33 cannabis hospitality establishments in this State. These recommendations must address 
34 issues including, but not limited to:
35 1.  Whether the State should issue a single cannabis hospitality establishment license 
36 or various license types for different business models;
37 2.  The qualifications for obtaining a cannabis hospitality establishment license and 
38 how applications will be reviewed;
Page 2 - 131LR1957(01)1 3.  Ensuring compliance with existing cannabis laws and regulations, including 
2 identification requirements, seed-to-sale tracking and product recalls; 
3 4.  Purchase and consumption limitations for patrons;
4 5.  How different methods of consumption are to be regulated;
5 6.  What other products or services, including food or entertainment, may be offered at 
6 cannabis hospitality establishments;
7 7.  Preventing the consumption of alcohol and tobacco at cannabis hospitality 
8 establishments;
9 8.  Preventing overconsumption and operating under the influence;
10 9.  Preventing disturbances to neighboring businesses or residences;
11 10.  Exemptions from the laws relating to clean indoor air;
12 11.  Training for cannabis hospitality establishment employees; and
13 12.  Local control, including whether municipalities need to opt in or opt out of 
14 allowing cannabis hospitality establishments and how municipalities may regulate them.
15Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
16 necessary staffing services to the task force, except that Legislative Council staff support 
17 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
18Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, no later than December 6, 2023, the task force 
19 shall submit a report that includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested 
20 legislation, for presentation to the Joint Standing Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs 
21 for the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
22SUMMARY
23 This resolve establishes the Task Force on Cannabis Hospitality Establishments.  The 
24 task force is directed to submit a report and any suggested legislation to the Joint Standing 
25 Committee on Veterans and Legal Affairs by December 6, 2023.23
