4 Notwithstanding the provisions of this chapter, Title 13, chapter 83 or Title 22, chapter

71. Municipal approval.  A nonprofit corporation must receive approval from the 
8 municipality in which it is located for the location and site and construction of a pyre to 
9 conduct open air cremation.  Fire trucks must have access to the location of the pyre.  A 
10 nonprofit corporation must receive authorization under Title 12, section 9321 for the day 
11 of an open air cremation by pyre.
122. Training required.  The board shall implement training for nonprofit corporations 
13 intending to conduct open air cremation by pyre.  The board must solicit training from an 
14 out-of-state organization that regularly conducts open air cremation by pyre until training 
15 may be conducted proficiently by a nonprofit corporation in this State.
163. Probation; number of cremations per month. A nonprofit corporation approved 
17 for open air cremation by pyre under this section must be under a probationary period for 
18 one year, during which time the number of cremations performed by the corporation may 
19 not exceed one per month.  After the probationary period, the number of cremations 
20 performed by the corporation may not exceed 6 per month.
214. Maximum number and required distance.  The maximum number of nonprofit 
22 corporations conducting open air cremation by pyre in the State may not exceed 6, and a 
23 nonprofit corporation conducting open air cremation by pyre must be at least 40 miles from 
24 any other nonprofit corporation conducting open air cremation by pyre.
255. Required size of land; number of cremations on site.  An open air cremation by 
26 pyre must be conducted on property owned or possessed by the nonprofit corporation that 
27 is at least 20 acres in size.  The property may have only one pyre.  Only one open air 
28 cremation may be conducted by the corporation at a time.
296. Disposition of ashes.  The ashes constituting the remains of a deceased person who 
30 is cremated under this section may be scattered on the property or otherwise disposed of 
31 pursuant to law.
32 The board shall adopt routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, 
33 subchapter 2-A to carry out the purposes of this section.
34SUMMARY
35 This bill allows a nonprofit corporation to conduct open air cremations by pyre if it
