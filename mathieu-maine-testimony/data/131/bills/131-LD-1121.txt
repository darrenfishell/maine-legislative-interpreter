10§3947.  Animal control officers
11 Each municipality shall appoint one or more animal control officers whose duties are 
12 enforcement of sections 3911, 3912, 3916, 3921, 3924, 3948, 3950, 3950‑A, 3952‑A, 4041 
13 and 4042 and Title 17, section 1023, responding to reports of animals suspected of having 
14 rabies in accordance with Title 22, sections 1313 and 1313‑A and any other duties to control 
15 animals as the municipality may require.  A municipality may appoint an employee of an 
16 animal shelter as an animal control officer as long as the person meets the qualifications 
17 and training requirements of this section.
18 A municipality may not appoint a person to the position of animal control officer who 
19 has been convicted of murder, a Class A or Class B offense or a violation of Title 17‑A, 
20 chapter 9, 11, 12 or 13 or has been convicted of a criminal violation under Title 17, chapter 
21 42 or has been adjudicated of a civil violation for cruelty to animals under chapter 739 or 
22 who has been convicted or adjudicated in any other state, provincial or federal court of a 
23 violation similar to those specified in this section.
24 Animal control officers must be certified in accordance with section 3906‑B, 
25 subsection 4.  Upon initial appointment, an animal control officer must complete basic 
26 training and be certified by the commissioner within 6 months of appointment.
27 An animal control officer must attend advanced training programs as described under 
28 section 3906‑B, subsection 4 to maintain certification.  An animal control officer must have 
29 a minimum of 8 hours of training each year.
30 Upon appointment of an animal control officer, municipal clerks shall notify the 
31 commissioner Commissioner of Public Safety of the name, address and telephone number 
32 of the animal control officer within 10 business days.  In the event the position is vacant 
33 upon termination or resignation of the animal control officer, the municipal clerk shall 
34 notify the commissioner Commissioner of Public Safety within 10 business days of the 
35 vacancy.
361.  Training and certification of animal control officers.  Upon initial appointment, 
37 an animal control officer must complete a basic training program and be certified by the 
38 Commissioner of Public Safety in accordance with this subsection within 6 months of 
39 appointment.  An animal control officer must attend an advanced training program to 
40 maintain certification.  An animal control officer must have a minimum of 8 hours of 
41 training each year.  The Commissioner of Public Safety shall develop both a basic and an 
42 advanced program to train animal control officers. The basic training program must include 
43 training in the following:
Page 2 - 131LR1972(01)1 A.  Investigation of complaints of cruelty to animals;
2 B.  Response to calls concerning animals suspected of having rabies; and
3 C.  Enforcement of dog licensing laws and rabies immunization laws.
4 The advanced training program must include, but is not limited to, training in animal cruelty 
5 with respect to hoarders of animals, animal cruelty with respect to domestic violence, new 
6 laws, case reviews and report writing.
7 The Commissioner of Public Safety shall certify all animal control officers who 
8 successfully complete training programs under this subsection.
9Sec. 4.  Transition provision.  All existing certifications of animal control officers 
10 by the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry that are valid on the effective 
11 date of this Act are transferred to the Department of Public Safety; animal control officers 
12 certified under the Department of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry continue to be 
13 certified under the Department of Public Safety until their certifications expire.
14SUMMARY
15 This bill provides that the Commissioner of Public Safety develop programs to train 
16 animal control officers rather than the Commissioner of Agriculture, Conservation and 
17 Forestry.  The bill also requires the Commissioner of Public Safety, rather than the 
18 Commissioner of Agriculture, Conservation and Forestry to certify all animal control 
19 officers who successfully complete the training programs.15
