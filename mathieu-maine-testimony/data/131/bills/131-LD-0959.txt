2Sec. 1.  38 MRSA §2147 is enacted to read:
3§2147.  Food waste at grocery stores
41.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5 following terms have the following meanings.
6 A.  “Composting facility” means a solid waste processing facility where organic 
7 materials are processed using a method of accelerated biological decomposition of 
8 organic material under controlled aerobic or anaerobic conditions.
9 B.  “Food waste” means food material produced from human or animal food 
10 production, preparation and consumption activities and that consists of, but is not 
11 limited to, fruits, vegetables and other vegetative material, grains and fish and animal 
12 products and by-products.  “Food waste” does not include hazardous waste, biomedical 
13 waste, sewage or septage.
14 C.  “Grocery store” means a store primarily engaged in the retail sale of canned food, 
15 dry goods, fresh fruits and vegetables, fresh meats, fish and poultry.
162.  Prohibition.  Beginning January 1, 2024, a grocery store may not dispose of or 
17 facilitate the disposal of food waste or surplus food acquired for retail sale by or generated 
18 at the grocery store at a waste-to-energy facility or landfill.  The grocery store shall ensure 
19 that such food waste or surplus food is:
20 A.  Donated to food banks, soup kitchens, shelters and other entities for feeding people;
21 B.  Diverted for use as animal feed; or
22 C.  Delivered to a composting facility for energy recovery through digestion, as input 
23 for other waste utilization technologies or for the creation of soil amendments through 
24 composting.
253.  Administration and enforcement; rules.  The department shall administer and 
26 enforce this section and may adopt rules as necessary to implement the provisions of this 
27 section.  Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are routine technical rules as defined in 
28 Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2-A.
29SUMMARY
30 This bill provides that, beginning January 1, 2024, a grocery store may not dispose of 
31 or facilitate the disposal of food waste or surplus food acquired for retail sale by or 
32 generated at the grocery store at a waste-to-energy facility or landfill.  Instead, the bill 
33 requires that a grocery store ensure that such food waste or surplus food is donated to food 
34 banks, soup kitchens, shelters and other entities for feeding people; diverted for use as 
35 animal feed; or delivered to a composting facility for energy recovery through digestion, 
36 as input for other waste utilization technologies or for the creation of soil amendments 
37 through composting.30
