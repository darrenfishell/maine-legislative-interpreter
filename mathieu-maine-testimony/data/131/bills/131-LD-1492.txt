41. Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
5 following terms have the following meanings.
6 A. "Auditor" means the auditor of the center pursuant to this section.
7 B. "Committee" means the joint standing committee of the Legislature having

92. Creation of position.  There is created within the Office of the Attorney General 
10 the position of auditor of the center. The auditor must be a person qualified by integrity and 
11 demonstrated ability in relevant fields. 
123. Appointment.  The Attorney General shall appoint the auditor without regard to 
13 political affiliation.
144. Powers and duties.  The auditor shall conduct systematic, ongoing oversight of the 
15 center to help bring transparency and accountability to its operations and to ensure that the 
16 center operates within the law and in a manner that preserves the privacy and liberty of all 
17 people in the State.  The auditor:
18 A.  Shall assist the committee in the oversight duties of the committee over the center;
19 B. Shall maintain a publicly accessible website that includes reports made to the 
20 committee pursuant to section 1801 and a means for the public to ask a question about 
21 the auditor or the center or submit a complaint about the auditor or center;
22 C. Shall keep confidential the identity of an officer that makes a good faith complaint 
23 against the center; and
24 D. May select the type and extent of reviews of center activities to conduct.
255. Reporting.  The auditor shall make the following reports to the committee:
26 A. An annual report no later than February 15th of each year regarding the oversight 
27 of the center;
28 B. An interim progress report no later than September 15th of each year, including 
29 progress made on previous goals, if applicable, and any obstacles the auditor has found 
30 in performing the requirements of this section; 
31 C. A report whenever the center or a state or local law enforcement agency refuses a 
32 data request or to make personnel available for auditor interviews, including the reason 
33 for the refusal, if known; and
34 D. Other reports as the auditor determines necessary.
35 The auditor shall redact from a report made under this subsection any information that is 
36 prohibited from disclosure, is sensitive and pertains to an ongoing investigation of criminal 
37 activity or would cause a specific and imminent threat to safety.
386. Limitations.  The auditor may not:
39 A. Require the center to take corrective action; or

37. Center cooperation.  The center shall make available documents and personnel as 
4 requested by the auditor.
58. Commissioner input.  The Commissioner of Public Safety may present a response 
6 to an issue reported by the auditor.

11Sec. 3.  Priority of tasks.  Notwithstanding the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 25, 
12 section 1802, subsection 4, paragraph D, the auditor of the Department of Public Safety, 
13 Maine Information and Analysis Center, a cooperative effort between the Department of 
14 Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management, Maine Emergency Management Agency 
15 and the State Police, in preparation for the auditor's first required report, shall review the 
16 center's record-keeping and information-sharing practices, particularly with respect to 
17 intelligence gathering.  The auditor shall assess the policies that the center uses to determine 
18 the quality or veracity of the information that the center gathers and whether those policies 
19 appropriately consider the privacy and liberty interests of all people in the State.  The 
20 auditor shall determine whether the center's practices comply with the requirements of 
21 federal regulations regarding criminal intelligence collection, retention and sharing, 
22 particularly 28 Code of Federal Regulations, Part 23.
23SUMMARY
24 This bill creates the position of auditor within the Office of the Attorney General for 
25 the purpose of conducting systematic, ongoing oversight of the Department of Public 
26 Safety, Maine Information and Analysis Center, a cooperative effort between the 
27 Department of Defense, Veterans and Emergency Management, Maine Emergency 
28 Management Agency and the State Police, to help bring transparency and accountability to 
29 its operations and to ensure that the center operates within the law and in a manner that 
30 preserves the privacy and liberty of all people in the State and directs the auditor to include 
31 certain reviews in the auditor's first required report.  The bill provides that information 
32 shared by the center with a private entity is a public record within the meaning of the 
33 Freedom of Access Act.24
