28Sec. 4.  22 MRSA §1912 is enacted to read:
29§1912.  Deceptive advertising; limited pregnancy services centers
301.  Dissemination prohibited.  A limited pregnancy services center may not, in 
31 connection with providing a medical or health counseling service related to pregnancy or 
32 pregnancy prevention, provide, disseminate or cause the dissemination of information, or 
33 provide only partial information, about pregnancy, pregnancy prevention or abortion that 
34 it knows or reasonably should know is deceptive or misleading. The prohibition in this 
35 subsection includes, but is not limited to, verbal statements and written statements in a 
36 newspaper, journal, pamphlet, publication or other written, recorded or electronic medium.
372.  Notice.  If the Attorney General has reason to believe that a limited pregnancy 
38 services center has violated subsection 1, the Attorney General shall provide written notice 
39 of the violation to the limited pregnancy services center.
Page 2 - 131LR0507(01)13.  Enforcement.  If a limited pregnancy services center that received written notice 
2 under subsection 2 fails to respond to the notice or comply with the requirements under 
3 subsection 1 within 10 days of receiving the written notice, the Attorney General may bring 
4 an action in the name of the State against the limited pregnancy services center to compel 
5 compliance with the requirements of subsection 1 by temporary or permanent injunction. 
6 In any action pursuant to which a temporary or permanent injunction is issued under this 
7 section, the court may, in addition to any other remedy within its authority, order the 
8 defendant to complete any combination of the following requirements:
9 A.  Disseminate, or pay for the dissemination of, information that corrects the 
10 information identified to be deceptive or misleading;
11 B.  Post, in locations determined by the court, a remedial notice that corrects the effects 
12 of the information identified to be deceptive or misleading; or
13 C.  Take any other action the court determines necessary to remedy adverse effects that 
14 the information identified to be deceptive or misleading had on a person who was 
15 seeking medical or health counseling services related to pregnancy or pregnancy 
16 prevention from the limited pregnancy services center.
17 Nothing in this section may be construed to prohibit the State or a political subdivision of 
18 the State from pursuing other administrative or legal action authorized by law including, 
19 but not limited to, any action that may be available under Title 5, chapter 10.
204.  Penalties.  In any action pursuant to which a temporary or permanent injunction is 
21 issued under this section, the court shall issue a civil penalty of not less than $50 and not 
22 more than $500 for each violation identified in the action. If the Attorney General prevails 
23 in an action under this section, the Attorney General is entitled to recover the reasonable 
24 costs and reasonable attorney's fees of pursuing that action.
255. Rulemaking. The Attorney General shall adopt rules necessary to implement this 
26 section. Rules adopted by the Attorney General must include, but are not limited to, the 
27 following:
28 A. The process through which a person can notify the Attorney General of a facility 
29 that the person believes has engaged in the dissemination of deceptive or misleading 
30 information prohibited under subsection 1;
31 B. The process and timeline that the Attorney General will follow in responding to a 
32 person who submitted notification under paragraph A; and
33 C. Examples of the dissemination of deceptive or misleading information provided by 
34 limited pregnancy services centers prohibited under subsection 1.
35 The processes required under paragraphs A and B, and the examples required under 
36 paragraph C, must be available to access on the Attorney General’s publicly accessible 
37 website.
