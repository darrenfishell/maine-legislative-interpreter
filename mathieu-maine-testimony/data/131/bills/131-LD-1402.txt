1417.  Tax-exempt property or economically disadvantaged student adjustment.  
15 Beginning in fiscal year 2024-25, and for each subsequent fiscal year, a school 
16 administrative unit may receive an adjustment if:
17 A.  At least 15% of the property within the school administrative unit is tax-exempt 
18 property that cannot be taxed to support public education in the school administrative 
19 unit; or
20 B.  At least 50% of the students within the school administrative unit are economically 
21 disadvantaged students. An adjustment under this paragraph must be in addition to any 
22 adjustment under subsection 11.
23 The Education Investment Account, referred to in this subsection as "the account," is 
24 established within the department to fund the adjustment under this subsection. After the 
25 close of the fiscal year ending June 30, 2023 and the close of each fiscal year thereafter, 
26 the State Controller shall transfer to the account the unencumbered balances remaining in 
27 the Department of Education, General Purpose Aid for Local Schools program, General 
28 Fund account that otherwise would be carried forward into the next fiscal year. To qualify 
29 for an adjustment under this subsection, a school administrative unit shall submit a plan to 
30 the commissioner for how the funds will be used to increase academic and social success 
31 of economically disadvantaged students. The amount of the adjustment must be based on 
32 a per-pupil rate calculated using the number of economically disadvantaged students at the 
33 school administrative unit and limited by the amount of funds in the account.
34Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
35 takes effect when approved.
36SUMMARY
37 This bill provides for an additional allocation of school funding for a school 
38 administrative unit in which at least 15% of the school administrative unit has tax-exempt 
39 property that cannot be levied to support public education in the school administrative unit 
40 or for a school administrative unit in which at least 50% of students are eligible for free or 
41 reduced-price meals. The adjustment is funded through any unencumbered balances that 
42 otherwise would be carried forward into the next fiscal year in the Department of 37
