14SUMMARY
15 This bill amends provisions of the Maine Criminal Code regarding drugs in the 
16 following ways.
17 1.  It adds to the definition of "traffick" possessing 2 grams or more of heroin or 90 or 
18 more individual bags, folds, packages, envelopes or containers of any kind containing 
19 heroin.
20 2. It adds to the definition of "furnish" possessing more than 200 milligrams but less 
21 than 2 grams of heroin or at least 45 but fewer than 90 individual bags, folds, packages, 
22 envelopes or containers of any kind containing heroin; and possessing more than 200 
23 milligrams but less than 2 grams of fentanyl powder or at least 45 but fewer than 90 
24 individual bags, folds, packages, envelopes or containers of any kind containing fentanyl 
25 powder.
26 3. It adds the possession of 4 grams or more of cocaine in the form of cocaine base to 
27 the inference under the Maine Rules of Evidence, Rule 303, which allows a court to permit 
28 a jury to infer guilt or a fact relevant to guilt based on a statutory presumption.
29 4. It adds to the crime of trafficking the trafficking of cocaine in the form of cocaine 
30 base in a quantity of 32 grams or more.
31 5. It adds to the crime of furnishing the furnishing of cocaine in the form of cocaine 
32 base in a quantity of 32 grams or more.
33 6. It adds 2 grams or more of cocaine in the form of cocaine base and removes the 
34 possession of 2 grams or more of heroin and 2 grams or more of fentanyl powder from the 
35 inference under the Maine Rules of Evidence, Rule 303, which allows a court to permit a 
36 jury to infer guilt or a fact relevant to guilt based on a statutory presumption.15
