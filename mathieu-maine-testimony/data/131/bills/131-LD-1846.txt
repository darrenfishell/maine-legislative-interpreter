24 The petitions must be signed in the same manner as are nonparty nomination petitions 
25 under section 354, subsections 3 and 4.  The circulator of a petition must sign the petition 
26 and verify by oath or affirmation before a notary public or other person authorized by law 
27 to administer oaths or affirmations the total number of signatures on the petition and that 
28 the circulator personally witnessed all of the signatures to the petition and that to the best 
29 of the circulator's knowledge and belief each signature is the signature of the person whose 
30 name it purports to be and that each signature authorized under section 153‑A was made 
31 by the authorized signer in the presence and at the direction of the voter. After 
32 administering the oath to the circulator, the notary public or other authorized person must 
33 sign the notarial certificate on the petition while in the presence of the circulator. After the 
34 petition is signed and verified in this manner, the petition must be submitted to the registrar 
35 for certification in accordance with the Constitution of Maine, Article IV, Part Third, 
36 Section 20.  If the petitions submitted to the registrar are not signed and verified in 
37 accordance with this paragraph, the registrar may not certify the petitions and is required 
38 only to return the petitions.  The clerk or registrar shall keep a log of petitions submitted to 
39 the municipal office for verification.  The log must contain the title of the petition, the name 
40 of the person submitting the petition, the date of submission, the number of petition forms 
41 submitted and the date and manner by which the petitions were returned.  The notary public 
42 or other authorized person who administered the oath to the circulator must keep a log of 
43 petitions for which that person administered the circulator's oath, listing the title of the 
44 petition, the name of the circulator taking the oath, the date of the oath and the number of 
45 petition forms signed and verified by the circulator that day.
