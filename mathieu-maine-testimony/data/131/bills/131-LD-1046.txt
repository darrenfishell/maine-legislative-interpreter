Page 2 - 131LR0831(01)11.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
2 following terms have the following meanings.  
3 A. "Authorized person" means a person having a relationship to the property that is 
4 unique and not shared by the general public. With respect to property owned by 
5 another, "authorized person" includes a tenant, custodian or night security guard. With 
6 respect to publicly owned property, "authorized person" includes police officers and 
7 other public employees charged with the responsibility of maintaining or protecting 
8 public property.
9 B. "Open container" means a container of liquor not having a cap, stopper or other 
10 cover in place.
11 C. "Public place" means:  
12 (1) A place owned or operated by a governmental entity to which the public or a 
13 substantial group has access, including but not limited to:  
14 (a) Public ways as defined in Title 17‑A, section 505, subsection 2;  
15 (b) Schools and government-owned custodial facilities;  
16 (c) The lobbies, hallways, lavatories, toilets and basement portions of 
17 apartment houses, hotels, public buildings and transportation terminals; and  
18 (d) Public beaches; and  
19 (2) Private ways and parking areas physically adjacent to public ways and designed 
20 primarily for vehicular traffic.
212.  Violation.  A person may not drink liquor in any public place within 200 feet of a 
22 notice posted conspicuously in the public place by the owner or an authorized person that 
23 forbids drinking in the public place or after being forbidden to do so personally by a law 
24 enforcement officer, unless the person has been given permission to do so by the owner or 
25 authorized person.  
263.  Evidence.  The possession of an open container of liquor in a public place is prima 
27 facie evidence of a violation of this section.
284.  Civil violation.  A person who violates this section commits a civil violation for 
29 which a fine of not more than $100 may be adjudged.
30SUMMARY
31 This bill changes public drinking from a crime to a civil violation for which a fine of 
32 not more than $100 may be adjudged.31
