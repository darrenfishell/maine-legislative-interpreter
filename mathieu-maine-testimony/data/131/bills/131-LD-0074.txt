221.  Clean-up and Response Fund Review Board.  The Clean-up and Response Fund

51-A.  Vacancies on review board.  Appointed members of the review board serve 
6 staggered 3-year terms. An appointed member may not serve more than 2 consecutive 3-
7 year terms. An appointed member continues to serve until that member has been 
8 reappointed or a successor has been appointed except that, if the member has not been 
9 reappointed or a successor has not been appointed one year after the member’s term 
10 expires, the member may no longer continue to serve.  A vacancy occurring other than by 
11 expiration of a term must be filled by appointment for the unexpired portion of the term.
122.  Powers and duties of review board.  The Clean-up and Response Fund Review 
13 Board has the following powers and duties:
14 A.  To hear appeals from insurance claims-related decisions of the commissioner and 
15 the State Fire Marshal under section 568‑A;
16 B.  To adopt rules in accordance with Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2 and guidelines 
17 necessary for the furtherance of the review board's duties and responsibilities under 
18 this subchapter;
19 D.  To monitor income and disbursements from the fund under section 551 and adjust 
20 fees pursuant to section 551, subsection 4, paragraph F, as required to avoid a shortfall 
21 in the fund;
22 E.  To, at such times and in such amounts as it determines necessary, and in consultation 
23 with the department, direct the transfer of funds from the Underground Oil Storage 
24 Replacement Fund to the fund;
25 F.  To review department priorities for disbursements from the fund and make 
26 recommendations to the commissioner on how the fund should be allocated;
27 G.  To review and comment on the State's marine oil spill contingency plan; and
28 H.  To review and monitor issues for oil spill prevention and response and recommend 
29 to the commissioner any regulatory changes that are appropriate.
302-A.  Meetings.  The Clean-up and Response Fund Review Board shall meet 4  2 times 
31 per year unless the review board votes not to hold an additional meeting or not to hold a 
32 meeting.  Action may not be taken unless a quorum is present.  A quorum is a majority of 
33 the seated members.
342-B.  Chair.  The review board shall annually choose a member to serve as chair of the 
35 review board every 2 years.
362-C.  Appeals to review board.  An applicant aggrieved by an insurance claims-related 
37 decision under section 568‑A, including but not limited to decisions on eligibility for 
38 coverage, eligibility of costs and waiver and amount of deductible, may appeal that decision 
39 to the Clean-up and Response Fund Review Board.  The appeals panel is composed of the 
40 member appointed under subsection 1, paragraph A‑1, the 2 members appointed under 
41 subsection 1, paragraph B, the member appointed under subsection 1, paragraph G and the 
42 member appointed under subsection 1, paragraph H.  The appeals panel shall hear and 
Page 4 - 131LR0097(01)43 decide the appeal.  Action may not be taken by the appeals panel unless a quorum is present.  
44 A quorum is a majority of the seated appeals panel members.  Except as provided in review 
45 board rules, the appeal must be filed within 30 days after the applicant receives the decision 
46 made under section 568‑A.  The appeals panel must hear an appeal at its next meeting 
47 following receipt of the appeal unless the appeal petition is received less than 30 days

132-D.  Report; adequacy of fund.  Beginning on April 15, 2015 and every other year 
14 thereafter, the Clean-up and Response Fund Review Board, with the cooperation of the 
15 commissioner, shall report to the joint standing committee of the Legislature having 
16 jurisdiction over natural resources matters on the department's and the review board's 
17 experience administering the fund, clean-up activities and 3rd-party damage claims.  The 
18 report must include an assessment of the adequacy of the fund to cover anticipated expenses 
19 and any recommendations for statutory change.   To carry out its responsibility under this 
20 subsection, the review board may order an independent audit of disbursements from the 
21 fund.
222-E.  Staff support.  The commissioner shall provide the Clean-up and Response Fund 
23 Review Board with staff support.
24SUMMARY
25 This bill shifts responsibility for appeals of certain insurance claims-related decisions 
26 from the Clean-up and Response Fund Review Board to the Board of Environmental 
27 Protection.
28 The bill also provides procedures for vacancies on the board, sets term limits for board 
29 members, reduces the number of required meetings and directs the board to choose a chair 
30 every 2 years.1
