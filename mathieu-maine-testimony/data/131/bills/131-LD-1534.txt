33Sec. 5.  Review of 2006 Rapid Response Plan.  The Interagency Task Force on 
34 Invasive Aquatic Plants and Nuisance Species, established by the Maine Revised Statutes, 
35 Title 5, section 12004-D, subsection 6, shall review the Department of Environmental 
36 Protection 2006 Rapid Response Plan For Invasive Aquatic Plants, Fish, and Other Fauna 
37 to further expedite the process for controlling invasive aquatic plant infestations, which 
38 may include ways to improve the surface use restriction process, identifying situations in 
39 which requiring inspections of boats launched and removed are advisable and a 
40 requirement to submit to an inspection by a trained inspector when launching or taking a 
41 boat out of an infested or at-risk lake.  The task force shall, no later than December 6, 2023, 
42 submit a report regarding its review, including any suggested legislation, to the Joint 
43 Standing Committee on Environment and Natural Resources.  The joint standing 
44 committee may report out a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.

2 This bill increases the fees for original or renewal certificates of number with 2 
3 validation stickers that are required for all resident motorboats and personal watercraft, and 
4 the fees for lake and river protection stickers for all motorboats and personal watercraft not 
5 registered in the State and seaplanes, operating on inland waters of the State.  Beginning 
6 January 1, 2024, the fees are increased by $10.
7 It also directs the Interagency Task Force on Invasive Aquatic Plants and Nuisance 
8 Species to review the Department of Environmental Protection 2006 Rapid Response Plan 
9 For Invasive Aquatic Plants, Fish, and Other Fauna to further expedite the process for 
10 controlling invasive aquatic plant infestations, which may include ways to improve the 
11 surface use restriction process, identifying situations in which requiring inspections of 
12 boats launched and removed are advisable and a requirement to submit to an inspection by 
13 a trained inspector when launching or taking a boat out of an infested or at-risk lake.  The 
14 task force is directed to submit, no later than December 6, 2023, a report regarding its 
15 review, including any suggested legislation, to the Joint Standing Committee on 
16 Environment and Natural Resources.  The joint standing committee is authorized to report 
17 out a bill to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.2
