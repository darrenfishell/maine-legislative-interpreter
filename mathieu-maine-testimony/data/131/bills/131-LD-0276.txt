5SUMMARY
6 For the purpose of protecting or restoring a great pond, current state law authorizes 
7 municipalities, subject to certain conditions, to appropriate funds to repair a private road, 
8 way or bridge to prevent storm water runoff pollution from reaching the great pond. This 
9 bill expands that law to authorize municipalities, subject to similar conditions, to 
10 appropriate funds to repair a private road, way or bridge to prevent storm water runoff 
11 pollution from reaching a threatened natural resource.
12 Under the bill, the term "threatened natural resource" is defined to mean a coastal bluff 
13 or a protected natural resource. Under the Natural Resources Protection Act, the term 
14 "protected natural resource" is defined to include coastal sand dune systems, coastal 
15 wetlands, significant wildlife habitat, fragile mountain areas, freshwater wetlands, 
16 community public water system primary protection areas, great ponds or rivers, streams or 
17 brooks.6
