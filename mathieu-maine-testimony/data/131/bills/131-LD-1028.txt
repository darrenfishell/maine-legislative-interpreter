32SUMMARY
33 This bill increases from 30 to 50 the number of tuition waivers for postsecondary 
34 education allowed to be granted each academic year to persons who, at the time of their 
35 graduation from high school or their successful completion of a general educational 
36 development examination, were in the custody of the Department of Health and Human 
37 Services and resided in foster care or subsidized adoptive care or were minor wards of a 
38 subsidized permanency guardian.  The bill also makes necessary changes to the provisions 
39 regarding how the tuition waivers are apportioned to postsecondary educational 
40 institutions.  The additional 20 waivers must be funded with General Fund appropriations.
