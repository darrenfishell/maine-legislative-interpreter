71. Membership.  The committee is composed of 13 members appointed at the

4SUMMARY
5 This bill does the following.
6 1.  It establishes the Joint Committee on Administrative Rules within the Legislature.  
7 The committee replaces the joint standing committees in reviewing the list, produced by 
8 the Secretary of State, of rules adopted in the previous calendar year; the Secretary of 
9 State’s report on rule-making activities in the previous calendar year; regulatory agendas 
10 submitted by agencies; provisionally adopted major substantive rules; and petitions for 
11 review of rules.
12 2.  It establishes the Legislative Economic Analysis Unit within the Legislature.  The 
13 unit is responsible for undertaking impact analysis of major substantive rules and other 
14 rules as directed.  The unit is also required, whenever it is unclear under applicable law 
15 whether a rule is a major substantive rule or a routine technical rule, to make a finding, on 
16 request, as to the appropriate classification of the rule. The unit is further directed to 
17 produce an impact analysis of the State’s acceptance and use of any federal or other grants, 
18 at the request of the President of the Senate, Speaker of the House or either chair of or a 
19 ranking member of any legislative committee with subject matter jurisdiction over the 
20 grant.  The unit is responsible for any other work assigned to it jointly by the President of 
21 the Senate and the Speaker of the House or by the Legislature.1
