2 Force, referred to in this resolve as "the task force," is established.
3Sec. 2.  Task force membership. Resolved: That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, 
4 the task force consists of 10 members as follows:
5 1. The Executive Director of the Office of Affordable Health Care or the executive 
6 director's designee; 
7 2. The director of the office of rural health and primary care within the Department of 
8 Health and Human Services; 
9 3. The following members appointed by the President of the Senate:
10 A. Two members from a statewide organization representing hospitals located in 
11 different regions of the State and that face different levels of financial stability;
12 B. One member from a statewide organization representing primary care providers 
13 working in federally qualified health centers; and
14 C. One member from a statewide organization representing physicians; and
15 4.  The following members appointed by the Speaker of the House:
16 A. One member from an organization that advocates for consumers of health care that 
17 use free clinic programs and other safety net programs in the State;
18 B. One member who is a provider of behavioral health services in a rural part of the 
19 State;
20 C. One member from a statewide organization that represents providers of home health 
21 services; and
22 D. One member from a statewide organization that represents long-term care providers.
23Sec. 3.  Meetings; chair; quorum. Resolved: That the Executive Director of the 
24 Office of Affordable Health Care or the executive director's designee shall call the first 
25 meeting of the task force. Notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the task force shall select a chair 
26 from among its members at the first meeting. A majority of the membership of the task 
27 force constitutes a quorum.
28Sec. 4.  Duties. Resolved: That the task force shall consider rural health care delivery 
29 in the State, including:
30 1. Evaluating the current system of rural health care delivery in the State, including the 
31 role of rural hospitals in the health care continuum;
32 2. Identifying how to ensure the sustainability of the rural health care system, including 
33 identifying the major financial, administrative and workforce barriers and providing 
34 technical assistance to support sustainability;
35 3. Proposing ways to overcome existing barriers to the sustainability of the rural health 
36 care system, including ideas to preserve access to health care services in rural parts of the 
37 State across the health care continuum;
38 4. Proposing ways to encourage and improve care coordination among institutional and 
39 community service providers; 
Page 2 - 131LR2248(01)1 5. Determining if alternative payment models would improve access to and quality of 
2 rural health care delivery; and 
3 6. Assessing the potential consequences of one or more rural hospitals in the State 
4 closing.
5Sec. 5.  Staff assistance. Resolved: That the Department of Health and Human 
6 Services and the Office of Affordable Health Care shall provide staff assistance to the task 
7 force.
8Sec. 6.  Report. Resolved: That the task force shall prepare and submit a written 
9 report to the Joint Standing Committee on Health and Human Services on its 
10 recommendations, including suggested legislation, no later than December 6, 2023.
11SUMMARY
12 This resolve establishes the Rural Health Services Task Force to study rural health 
13 care delivery in the State.12
