42-A.  Maintenance of voter registration information.  The registrar in each 
5 municipality shall keep the central voter registration system current at all times for the

10 Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2‑A.  A registrar may not cancel a voter's registration in 
11 the central voter registration system solely because the registered voter did not vote in 
12 previous elections. A voter’s registration record in the central voter registration system 
13 must be cancelled by either the registrar for the voter’s municipality or by the Secretary of 
14 State as follows:
15 A.  When it is determined that a voter has registered to vote in another jurisdiction in 
16 the State, the voter registration record from the former jurisdiction must be cancelled; 
17 and
18 B.  When it is determined that the voter has registered to vote in another jurisdiction 
19 outside of the State, the voter registration record in the State must be cancelled. ;
20 C.  When it is determined that the voter has moved into another jurisdiction, either in 
21 the State or outside of the State, regardless of whether the voter has registered to vote 
22 in that other jurisdiction, the voter registration record from the former jurisdiction must 
23 be cancelled; and
24 D.  When it is determined that the voter is deceased, the voter registration record must 
25 be cancelled.

2 This bill makes the following changes to the laws governing voting and voter 
3 registration.
4 1.  It requires a registrar of a municipality, when conducting maintenance of voter 
5 registration information, to cancel the voter registration record of a voter determined to 
6 have moved outside of that municipality.
7 2.  It requires a registrar of a municipality, when conducting maintenance of voter 
8 registration information, to cancel the voter registration record of a voter determined to be 
9 deceased.
10 3.  It prohibits an absentee ballot return envelope for use in any election from including 
11 any mark visible on the outside of the return envelope or a space designated for making a 
12 mark visible on the outside of the return envelope that indicates the political party of the 
13 voter to whom the absentee ballot is issued.
14 4.  It requires secured drop boxes for the return of absentee ballots to be monitored 
15 periodically and recorded continuously by a surveillance camera during the absentee voting 
16 period and requires the recorded video to be retained by the municipality for one year 
17 following the end of the absentee voting period.2
