34. Duties.  The committee shall review the training program developed as required by 
4 the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 20-A, section 1006, including the standards adopted 
5 under Title 20-A, section 1005, and determine the best way to disseminate the training 
6 program to school board members. The committee shall invite the participation of and 
7 comments from stakeholders, including but not limited to school administrators, educators 
8 and parents. The committee shall invite the participation of the Department of Education. 
9 The committee shall recommend how the dissemination of the training program should be 
10 incorporated into law in this State. The committee shall explore options, including the 
11 application of federal grant funds, to defray all or some of the initial and ongoing costs.
125. Staff assistance.  The Legislative Council shall provide necessary staffing services 
13 to the committee, except that Legislative Council staff support is not authorized when the 
14 Legislature is in regular or special session.

16 includes its findings and recommendations, including suggested legislation, to the Joint 
17 Standing Committee on Education and Cultural Affairs.  The committee may submit 
18 legislation based upon the report to the Second Regular Session of the 131st Legislature.
19SUMMARY
20 This bill requires the Department of Education to create a training program and meeting 
21 standards for school board members and establishes a legislative committee to review the 
22 training program and meeting standards and suggest means of disseminating the training 
23 program to school board members.  The bill provides for the removal from office of a 
24 school board member who violates the meeting standards.1
