2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, this resolve establishes the Commission to Study the Operations of the 
4 Maine Legislature; and
5Whereas, the study must be initiated before the 90-day period expires in order that 
6 the study may be completed and a report submitted in time for submission to the next 
7 legislative session; and
8Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
9 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
10 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
11 therefore, be it
12Sec. 1.  Commission established.  Resolved:  That the Commission to Study the 
13 Operations of the Maine Legislature, referred to in this resolve as "the commission," is 
14 established.
15Sec. 2.  Commission membership.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 
16 353, the commission consists of 12 members appointed as follows:
17 1.  Two members of the Senate appointed by the President of the Senate, including a 
18 member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature;
19 2.  Two members of the House of Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the 
20 House, including a member from each of the 2 parties holding the largest number of seats 
21 in the Legislature;
22 3.  Two members who are registered to lobby in the State, including one member 
23 appointed by the leaders of the party holding the largest number of seats in the Legislature 
24 and one member appointed by the leaders of the party holding the 2nd largest number of 
25 seats in the Legislature;
26 4.  Two public members recommended by a statewide organization that represents local 
27 labor unions, appointed by the Governor;
28 5.  Two public members recommended by a statewide organization that advocates for 
29 the creation and preservation of jobs that create economic value or bring money into the 
30 State, appointed by the Governor; and
31 6.  Two public members who represent the interests of small businesses and working 
32 families, appointed by the Governor.
33Sec. 3.  Chairs.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, the first-named 
34 public member recommended by a statewide organization that represents local labor unions 
35 and the first-named public member recommended by a statewide organization that 
36 advocates for the creation and preservation of jobs that create economic value or bring 
37 money into the State shall serve as cochairs of the commission.
38Sec. 4.  Appointments; convening of commission.  Resolved:  That all 
39 appointments must be made no later than 30 days following the effective date of this 
40 resolve.  The appointing authorities shall notify the Executive Director of the Legislative 
41 Council once all appointments have been completed.  After appointment of all members, 
Page 2 - 131LR1483(01)42 the chairs shall call and convene the first meeting of the commission.  If 30 days or more 
43 after the effective date of this resolve a majority of but not all appointments have been 
44 made, the chairs may request authority and the Legislative Council may grant authority for 
45 the commission to meet and conduct its business.
5Sec. 5.  Duties.  Resolved:  That the commission shall study the existing structure 
6 and operations of the Maine Legislature for the purposes of determining its adequacy and 
7 effectiveness and identifying changes to the Legislature that could improve efficiency, 
8 provide better information to inform decisions, help prioritize legislative activity and 
9 reinforce political trust and support in State Government.  In its work, the commission shall 
10 consider, but is not limited to considering, the following elements:
11 1.  The size of the Legislature;
12 2.  The number of terms for members of the Legislature;
13 3.  The frequency of elections for members of the Legislature;
14 4.  The number of bills introduced each session;
15 5.  The prioritization of legislation;
16 6.  The roles and responsibilities of legislative leadership;
17 7.  The determination of legislative districts during the reapportionment process;
18 8.  The training and education of Legislators and legislative staff;
19 9.  The committee assignment process;
20 10.  The rules for testimony;
21 11.  The use of policy research and data in the legislative process;
22 12.  The process for transparency in the Legislature;
23 13.  The adoption of a legislative strategic plan;
24 14.  The inclusion of economic impact statements on legislation; and
25 15.  The process for forming and approving the state budget.
26Sec. 6.  Staff assistance.  Resolved:  That the Legislative Council shall provide 
27 necessary staffing services to the commission, except that Legislative Council staff support 
28 is not authorized when the Legislature is in regular or special session.
29Sec. 7.  Report.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding Joint Rule 353, no later than 
30 December 6, 2023, the commission shall submit a report that includes its findings and 
31 recommendations, including suggested legislation, for presentation to the Second Regular 
32 Session of the 131st Legislature.  If the commission is unable to agree on all 
33 recommendations, the commission may issue both a majority report and a minority report.
34Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
35 takes effect when approved.
