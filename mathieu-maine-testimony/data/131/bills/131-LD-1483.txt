5ยง647. Rights of agricultural employees
61. Definitions. As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
7 following terms have the following meanings.
8 A. "Agricultural employee" means an individual employed:
9 (1) In agriculture as defined in the Employment Security Law and the Federal 
10 Unemployment Tax Act; or
11 (2) In the canning; processing; preserving; freezing; drying; marketing; storing; 
12 packing for shipment; or distributing of: 
13 (a) Agricultural produce;
14 (b) Meat and fish products; and
15 (c) Perishable foods.
16 B. "Agricultural employer" means a person or entity that directly or indirectly engages 
17 the services or permits the work of an individual employed in agriculture or an 
18 individual employed in certain activities related to agriculture.
19 C. "Key service provider" means a health care provider, an education provider, an 
20 attorney, a legal advocate, a government official, a member of the clergy and any other 
21 service provider to which an agricultural employee may need access.
222. Interference with reasonable access to key service providers and health care 
23providers. An agricultural employer may not interfere with reasonable access to a key 
24 service provider by an agricultural employee at any location during any time in which the 
25 agricultural employee is not performing work or during paid or unpaid rest and meal breaks. 
26 An agricultural employer may not interfere with access to a health care provider at any time 
27 by an agricultural employee whether or not that agricultural employee is working.
283. Person other than agricultural employee may not restrict access to residence. A 
29 person other than an agricultural employee may not prohibit, bar or interfere with, or 
30 attempt to prohibit, bar or interfere with the access to or egress from the residence of the 
31 agricultural employee, including by the erection or maintenance of any physical barrier, by 
32 physical force or the threat of physical force or violence, or by any order or notice given in 
33 any manner.
34SUMMARY
35 This bill provides that an agricultural employer may not interfere with reasonable 
36 access to a key service provider by agricultural employees during work time. It also 35
