41. In-state tuition for refugees, asylees and asylum seekers.  A refugee, asylee or 
5 asylum seeker under 8 United States Code, Chapter 12, Subchapter II, Part I who resides 
6 in the State and who meets the requirements of this section qualifies for in-state tuition at 
7 the University of Maine System, the Maine Community College System and the Maine 
8 Maritime Academy.
92. Criteria for in-state tuition. A person qualifies for in-state tuition under subsection 
10 1 if the person is not a citizen of the United States and:
11 A. Is a refugee and resettled in the State directly after arriving in the United States;
12 B. Is a refugee, resettled in another state directly after arriving in the United States prior 
13 to residing in the State and meets the same qualifications for in-state tuition as a citizen 
14 of the United States;
15 C. Is seeking asylum and meets the same qualifications for in-state tuition as a citizen 
16 of the United States; or
17 D. Has been granted asylum and meets the same qualifications for in-state tuition as a 
18 citizen of the United States.
193. Documentation. A person qualifying for in-state tuition under subsection 2 must 
20 provide appropriate documentation of residency and documentation from the United States 
21 Department of Homeland Security to establish the person's qualifications and immigration 
22 status under subsection 2.
23SUMMARY
24 This bill allows refugees, asylees and asylum seekers to qualify for in-state tuition at 
25 the University of Maine System, the Maine Community College System and the Maine 
26 Maritime Academy if they meet certain residency requirements and provide documentation 
27 for their qualifications and documentation regarding their immigration status from the 
28 United States Department of Homeland Security.24
