Page 1 - 131LR0491(01)1Emergency preamble.  Whereas, acts and resolves of the Legislature do not 
2 become effective until 90 days after adjournment unless enacted as emergencies; and
3Whereas, some municipal budgets are strained by efforts to prevent the residents of 
4 the municipality from losing heat or housing this winter; and
5Whereas, this Act provides immediate relief to those municipalities; and
6Whereas, in the judgment of the Legislature, these facts create an emergency within 
7 the meaning of the Constitution of Maine and require the following legislation as 
8 immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health and safety; now, 
9 therefore,
10Be it enacted by the People of the State of Maine as follows:
11Sec. 1. Reimbursement for eviction and heating shut-off assistance. The State 
12 shall reimburse a municipality for the full amount of money spent by that municipality from 
13 December 1, 2022 to March 31, 2023 for programs to prevent eviction of that municipality's 
14 residents or heating shut-off by utility companies serving that municipality's residents.  The 
15 State shall reimburse the municipality before the end of fiscal year 2022-23. 
16Emergency clause.  In view of the emergency cited in the preamble, this legislation 
17 takes effect when approved.
18SUMMARY
19 This bill requires the State to reimburse by the end of fiscal year 2022-23 a municipality

21 programs to prevent eviction of that municipality's residents or heating shut-off by utility 
22 companies serving that municipality's residents.19
