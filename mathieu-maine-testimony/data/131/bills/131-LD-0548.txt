41.  Sheriff's duties.  The sheriff's duties are as follows.  The sheriff is:
5 A. The chief law enforcement officer of the county in which the sheriff serves and is a 
6 constitutional officer under the Constitution of Maine, Article IX, Section 10;
7 B. Responsible for administering and directing the sheriff's department as authorized 
8 by the county budget for the county in which the sheriff serves; 
9 C. The department head of the sheriff's department, which consists of law enforcement, 
10 corrections, civil and court security with duties as follows.
11 (1) The law enforcement division is responsible for answering emergency and 
12 nonemergency calls, patrolling rural communities, investigating crimes, 
13 conducting traffic-related enforcement and working in conjunction with the county 
14 it serves.
15 (2) The corrections division is responsible for housing pretrial detainees and 
16 sentenced inmates who have been convicted of a Class A, B or C crime and 
17 sentenced to a term of imprisonment of 9 months or less or convicted of a Class D 
18 or E crime and sentenced to a term of imprisonment of 364 days or less.
19 (3) The civil division is responsible for serving civil subpoenas and other court-
20 related documents within the county it serves, including, but not limited to, 
21 documents related to family matters, civil suits and forcible entry and detainer; and
22 D.  Responsible for informing the county commissioners of sheriff's department 
23 activities on a regular basis.
24SUMMARY
25 This bill clarifies and expands the duties of sheriffs.25
