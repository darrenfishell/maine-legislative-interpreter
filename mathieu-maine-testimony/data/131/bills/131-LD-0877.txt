51.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
6 following terms have the following meanings.
7 A. "Company" means any type of legal business entity recognized under the laws of 
8 the State, including any subsidiaries, parent companies or affiliates of the company that 
9 exists for the purposes of making a profit.
10 B. "Government of China" means the government of the People's Republic of China.
11 C. "Subject company" means a company that is owned or operated by the government 
12 of China.
132.  Certification required.  A company that submits a bid or proposal for a contract 
14 with the State for goods or services shall certify that the company is not a subject company.
153. Prohibition.  A subject company is ineligible for and may not bid on or submit a 
16 proposal, directly or indirectly, for a contract with a public or private entity that directly or 
17 indirectly receives state funding.
184.  Civil violation; penalty.  A company that submits a false certification under 
19 subsection 2 commits a civil violation for which a fine may be adjudged in an amount that 
20 is the greater of:
21 A. Twice the amount of the contract for which the bid or proposal was submitted; or
22 B. $250,000.
23 The State shall terminate the contract with a company found to have violated subsection 
24 2 and the company is subject to the prohibition in subsection 3.
25SUMMARY
26 This bill requires that a company that submits a bid or proposal for a contract with the 
27 State for goods or services must certify that the company is not owned or operated by the 
28 government of the People's Republic of China.  It provides that a company owned or 
29 operated by the government of the People's Republic of China is ineligible for and may not 
30 bid on or submit a proposal, directly or indirectly, for a contract with a public or private 
31 entity that directly or indirectly receives state funding.26
