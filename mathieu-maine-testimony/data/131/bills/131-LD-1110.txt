2Sec. 1.  10 MRSA §2356 is enacted to read:
3§2356.  Calibration requirements for testing and measurement equipment
4 This section provides requirements for the calibration of certain testing and 
5 measurement equipment.
61.  Definitions.  As used in this section, unless the context otherwise indicates, the 
7 following terms have the following meanings.
8 A.  “Accredited laboratory” means a laboratory that is accredited pursuant to standard 
9 ISO/IEC 17025 of the International Organization for Standardization by a 3rd-party 
10 accrediting body.
11 B.  “NIST testing and measurement equipment” means any testing or measurement 
12 equipment that is subject to the requirements of this chapter and requires calibration to 
13 meet applicable standards published by the National Institute of Standards and 
14 Technology.
15 C.  “Publicly owned treatment works” has the same meaning as in Title 38, section 
16 361-A, subsection 3-D.
172.  Calibration required by accredited laboratory.  Notwithstanding any other 
18 provision of this chapter to the contrary, an agency or department of the State, a municipal 
19 or quasi-municipal water utility and a publicly owned treatment works shall ensure that any 
20 NIST testing and measurement equipment used by the agency, department, water utility or 
21 treatment works is calibrated by an accredited laboratory.
22SUMMARY
23 This bill amends the Maine Weights and Measures Law to require an agency or 
24 department of the State, a municipal or quasi-municipal water utility and a publicly owned 
25 treatment works to ensure that certain testing and measurement equipment used by the 
26 agency, department, water utility or treatment works is calibrated by an accredited 
27 laboratory.  The testing and measurement equipment subject to this calibration requirement 
28 includes any testing or measurement equipment that is subject to the requirements of the 
29 Maine Weights and Measures Law and requires calibration to meet applicable standards 
30 published by the National Institute of Standards and Technology.23
