7§1504.  Capital Fund
81.  Establishment.  The Capital Fund, referred to in this section as "the fund," is 
9 established as a dedicated, nonlapsing account within the Department of Transportation, 
10 referred to in this section as "the department."  The department shall administer the fund.  
112.  Revenue.  The following funds must be deposited into the fund:
12 A.  Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, all amounts appropriated to 
13 the department to fund the construction or repair of highways or bridges that at the end 
14 of any fiscal year would otherwise lapse to the General Fund; 
15 B.  Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, any amount in any dedicated 
16 account to fund the construction or repair of highways or bridges that at the end of any 
17 fiscal year would otherwise lapse to the General Fund; 
18 C.  Amounts transferred pursuant to Title 5, section 1536, subsection 1, paragraph G; 
19 and
20 D.  Any other funds available to the department that the department directs be deposited 
21 into the fund.
223. Use of fund.  The fund must be used to fund construction and repairs of highways 
23 and bridges and may be expended only on approval of the Legislature by a 2/3 vote of the 
24 members of each House.
25SUMMARY
26 This bill establishes the Capital Fund within the Department of Transportation.  The 
27 fund receives all amounts appropriated to the department to fund the construction or repair 
28 of highways or bridges that at the end of any fiscal year would otherwise lapse to the 
29 General Fund and any amount in any dedicated account to fund the construction or repair 
30 of highways or bridges that at the end of any fiscal year would otherwise lapse to the 
31 General Fund, as well as 20% of the unappropriated General Fund surplus at the end of 
32 each fiscal year and any other funds available to the department that the department directs 
33 be deposited into the fund.  The fund must be used to fund construction and repairs of 
34 highways and bridges as approved by the Legislature by a 2/3 vote of the members of each 
35 House.26
