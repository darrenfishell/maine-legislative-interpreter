11Sec. 2.  20-A MRSA §1003-A is enacted to read:
12§1003-A.  Background check required
131. Criminal history record check defined.  "Criminal history record check" means a 
14 fingerprint-based criminal history record check.
152. Background check requirement.  A newly elected school board member and a 
16 school board member upon reelection is required to submit to a criminal history record 
17 check in accordance with this section prior to commencing office under section 1003.  The 
18 department shall request a criminal history record check for each person who submits to a 
19 criminal history record check under this section.  The criminal history record check must 
20 include criminal history record information obtained from the Maine Criminal Justice 
21 Information System established in Title 16, section 631 and the Federal Bureau of 
22 Investigation.
23 A.  The criminal history record information obtained from the Maine Criminal Justice 
24 Information System must include a record of public criminal history record information 
25 as defined in Title 16, section 703, subsection 8.
26 B.  The criminal history record information obtained from the Federal Bureau of 
27 Investigation must include other state and national criminal history record information.
28 C.  An applicant shall submit to having fingerprints taken.  The State Police, upon 
29 payment of a fee established by the department by rule by the applicant, shall take or 
30 cause to be taken the applicant's fingerprints and shall immediately transmit the 
31 fingerprints to the State Bureau of Identification so that the bureau can conduct state 
32 and national criminal history record checks.  Except for the portion of the payment, if 
33 any, that constitutes the processing fee charged by the Federal Bureau of Investigation, 
34 all money received by the State Police for purposes of this paragraph must be paid over 
35 to the Treasurer of State.  The money must be applied to the expenses of administration 
36 incurred by the Department of Public Safety.  Any person who fails to transmit criminal 
37 fingerprint records to the State Bureau of Identification pursuant to this paragraph is 
38 subject to the provisions of Title 25, section 1550.
39 D.  The subject of a Federal Bureau of Investigation criminal history record check may 
40 obtain a copy of the criminal history record check by following the procedures outlined 
41 in 28 Code of Federal Regulations, Sections 16.32 and 16.33.  The subject of a state 
Page 2 - 131LR1028(01)42 criminal history record check may inspect and review the criminal history record 
43 information pursuant to Title 16, section 709.
3 E.  State and federal criminal history record information may be used by the department 
4 for the purpose of screening each applicant.  An action against an applicant under this 
5 subsection is subject to the provisions of Title 5, chapter 341.
6 F.  Information obtained pursuant to this subsection is confidential.  The results of 
7 criminal history record checks received by the department are for official use only and 
8 may not be disseminated to any other person or entity.
9 G.  An applicant whose term has expired and who is not running for reelection may 
10 request in writing that the State Bureau of Identification remove the applicant's 
11 fingerprints from the bureau's fingerprint file.  In response to a written request, the 
12 bureau shall remove the applicant's fingerprints from the fingerprint file and provide 
13 written confirmation of that removal.
143.  Rules.  The department, following consultation with the State Bureau of 
15 Identification, shall adopt rules to implement this section.  Rules adopted pursuant to this 
16 section relating to screening requirements must be identical to the screening requirements 
17 for sports coaches and substitute teachers.  Rules adopted pursuant to this subsection are 
18 routine technical rules as defined in Title 5, chapter 375, subchapter 2‑A.

26SUMMARY
27 This bill requires newly elected school board members and school board members upon 
28 reelection to submit to a fingerprint-based background check with screening requirements 
29 identical to those required for coaches and substitute teachers.1
