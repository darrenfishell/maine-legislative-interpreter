21 A full-time or part-time member of the Maine National Guard who reports that the

23 Maine National Guard may request paid leave during any pending investigation, and paid 
24 leave must be granted if the reporting party requests it. A full-time or part-time member of

27 investigation, unless the reporting party consents to an alternative arrangement after 
28 consultation with counsel for the Maine National Guard. If a reporting party takes paid 
29 leave but wishes to return to work prior to completion of the investigation, an evaluation 
30 of supervisory or reporting structures must be completed and modified as necessary to 
31 avoid further harassment, discrimination or other alleged conduct by the responding party.

7SUMMARY
8 This bill implements recommendations from the Governor's Advisory Council on 
9 Military Sexual Trauma. The bill does the following.
14 1.  It amends the duties of the Adjutant General to require an annual report to the joint 
15 standing committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over veterans affairs that 
16 includes: the aggregate number of new members of the Maine National Guard broken down 
17 by gender in the previous year; the aggregate discharge numbers of Maine National Guard 
18 members broken down by gender in the previous year; the aggregate promotion rates from 
19 the previous year of Maine National Guard members broken down by gender; the aggregate 
20 data or coded reason for discharge of all Maine National Guard members discharged in the 
21 previous year broken down by gender; the number of restricted and unrestricted reports of 
22 sexual harassment and sexual assault in the Maine National Guard by year broken down by 
23 gender of the reporting party; and the percentages of Maine National Guard members 
24 broken down by gender reporting sexual harassment and sexual assault as measured by an 
25 anonymous survey.
26 2.  It establishes paid leave for full-time or part-time members of the Maine National 
27 Guard who report being a victim of a sexual assault or sexual harassment by another 
28 member of the Maine National Guard and for the respondent.
29 3.  It amends requirements of the Victims' Compensation Fund statute to specify that 
30 for purposes of awarding compensation an unrestricted report of sexual assault or sexual 
31 harassment made to the Maine National Guard by a current or former member of the Maine 
32 National Guard is a crime reported to a law enforcement officer.
33 4.  It amends the date of the Adjutant General's annual report to the joint standing 
34 committee of the Legislature having jurisdiction over veterans affairs regarding sexual 
35 assault and sexual harassment in the Maine National Guard.
36 5.  It requires the Adjutant General to report to the Joint Standing Committee on 
37 Veterans and Legal Affairs on the activities of the Governor's Advisory Council on Military 
38 Sexual Trauma.
39 6.  It appropriates ongoing funds for a military sexual trauma liaison and programming 
40 costs.7
