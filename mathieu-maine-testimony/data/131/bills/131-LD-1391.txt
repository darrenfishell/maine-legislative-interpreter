9§305.  License restrictions
10 Notwithstanding any provision of this chapter to the contrary, the department may 
11 impose a moratorium on conditional cultivation facility licenses and renewal licenses for a 
12 tier with a greater area of authorized plant canopy under section 303 when the department 
13 determines that:
141. Price decrease. The average monthly price per gram of cannabis cultivated and sold 
15 by all cultivation facilities has decreased by more than 20% in any 2 consecutive fiscal 
16 quarters from the average monthly price per gram of cultivated cannabis from the prior 
17 year as calculated by the department from data reported pursuant to section 113, subsection 
18 2, paragraph F; or
192. Product demand decrease.  The current volume of cannabis cultivated by all 
20 cultivation facilities is at least 3 times the volume of cannabis sold by all cannabis stores 
21 in any 90-day period.
22 A moratorium imposed under this section must last for 4 consecutive months or until 
23 data received by the department indicates the conditions in subsections 1 and 2 no longer 
24 exist, whichever is later.
25SUMMARY
26 This bill allows the Department of Administrative and Financial Services to impose a 
27 moratorium on issuing a new conditional cultivation license or renewing a cultivation 
28 license for a tier with a greater area of authorized plant canopy if the department determines 
29 that: the average monthly price per gram of cannabis cultivated and sold by all licensed 
30 cultivation facilities has decreased by more than 20% in any 2 consecutive fiscal quarters 
31 from the average monthly price per gram of cultivated cannabis from the prior year as 
32 calculated by the department from data reported pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, 
33 Title 28-B, section 113, subsection 2, paragraph F; or the current volume of cannabis 
34 cultivated by all licensed cultivation facilities is at least 3 times the volume of cannabis 
35 sold by all licensed cannabis stores in any 90-day period.  A moratorium must last for 4 
36 consecutive months, or until data received by the department indicates the moratorium 
37 conditions no longer exist, whichever is later.26
