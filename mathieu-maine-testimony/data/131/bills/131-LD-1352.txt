27 (3)  At least once every 6 months during the 2-year supervision period, the 
28 supervising attorney submitted a report using a form or format prescribed by the 
29 board, if any, describing the number of hours the applicant studied each week 
30 during the law office's regular business hours; the number of hours the applicant 
31 was supervised by the supervising attorney; specific information on the books and 
32 other materials studied; and any other information required by the board; and
33 (4)  The supervising attorney did not personally supervise more than 4 applicants 
34 at any one time.
35SUMMARY
36 This bill provides that an applicant for admission to the bar who has studied law under 
37 the supervision of a licensed attorney for a minimum of 18 hours per week over a 2-year 
38 period need not attend law school prior to taking the bar examination.36
