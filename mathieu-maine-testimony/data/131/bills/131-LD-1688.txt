Page 1 - 131LR0296(01)1Sec. 1.  Authorization to sue State.  Resolved:  That, notwithstanding any 
2 provision of law to the contrary, Ronald Caron and Jennifer Berube, who claim to have 
3 suffered damages in connection with a motor vehicle accident involving the Department of 
4 Transportation, are authorized to bring a civil action against the Department of 
5 Transportation. This resolve is a waiver of the State's defense of immunity under the Maine 
6 Revised Statutes, Title 14, chapter 741.
7 Notwithstanding the application of any statute of limitations barring this action, this 
8 action may be brought in the Superior Court within one year from the effective date of this 
9 resolve. Liability and damages, including punitive damages, must be determined according 
10 to state law as in litigation between individuals. The action may be heard by a Justice of 
11 the Superior Court or by a jury.  The Maine Rules of Civil Procedure govern the conduct 
12 of the action. The Attorney General shall appear, answer and defend the action. To the 
13 extent possible, the Attorney General shall conduct negotiations in good faith to resolve 
14 this action by settlement.
15 The Treasurer of State shall pay any settlement determined by agreement of the parties 
16 or any judgment, including costs and interest, upon final process issued by the Superior 
17 Court or, if applicable, the Supreme Judicial Court.
18Sec. 2.  Waiver of statutory limitations.  Resolved:  That limitations on damages 
19 pursuant to the Maine Revised Statutes, Title 14, section 8105, subsection 1 are waived for 
20 the action described under section 1.
21SUMMARY
22 This resolve authorizes Ronald Caron and Jennifer Berube, who claim to have suffered 
23 damages in connection with a motor vehicle accident involving the Department of 
24 Transportation, to sue the Department of Transportation for damages resulting from the 
25 accident and waives the statutory limitations on damage recovery.22
