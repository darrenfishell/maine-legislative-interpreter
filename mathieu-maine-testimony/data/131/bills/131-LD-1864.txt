122.  Residential areas where municipal sewer and water available.  This subsection 
13 applies to residential areas where municipal sewer and water are available.
14 A.  If a housing development is served by a public, special district or other centrally

20 B.  A municipal ordinance may not establish dimensional standards for dwelling units

24 (1)  A road frontage requirement greater than 50 feet for dwelling units under this

27 under this subsection.
28 C.  A developer of a housing development under this subsection shall provide written

8SUMMARY
9 This bill provides restrictions on municipal ordinance requirements related to minimum 
10 lot size in areas where water and sewer infrastructure are available and in areas where water 
11 and sewer infrastructure are not available but that are within designated growth areas.  For 
12 a housing development served by a public, special district or other centrally managed water 
13 system and a public, special district or other comparable sewer system and that is located 
14 in an area in which dwelling units are allowed, a municipality must allow a dwelling unit 
15 on a lot with a minimum size of 5,000 square feet.  For a housing development located in 
16 a designated growth area that is not served by a public, special district or other centrally 
17 managed water system and a public, special district or other comparable sewer system, that 
18 complies with minimum lot size requirements in accordance with the Maine Revised 
19 Statutes, Title 12, chapter 423-A and that is located in an area in which dwelling units are 
20 allowed, a municipality must allow a dwelling unit on a lot with a minimum size of 20,000 
21 square feet.  The bill  also provides limits to ordinance provisions relating to lot coverage, 
22 road frontage and setback requirements.1
