2Sec. 1.  20-A MRSA §4015 is enacted to read:
3§4015.  Minimum hourly wage for school support staff
41.  Definition.  For the purposes of this section, “school support staff” means a public 
5 school employee who is not an administrator, as defined in section 13001-A, subsection 1, 
6 and is not a certified teacher or career and technical education teacher subject to the 
7 minimum salary requirement pursuant to section 13407.
82.  Minimum hourly wage.  Except as provided in subsection 3, for the school year 
9 starting after June 30, 2024 and for each subsequent school year, the minimum hourly wage 
10 for school support staff is an amount equal to 125% of the minimum hourly wage 
11 established in Title 26, section 664, subsection 1.
123.  Wage increases.  If, pursuant to the requirements of Title 26, section 664, 
13 subsection 1, the minimum hourly wage is increased on January 1st of any year, the 
14 minimum hourly wage for school support staff is increased beginning July 1st of the same 
15 year to an amount equal to 125% of the increased minimum hourly wage under Title 26, 
16 section 664, subsection 1.
17SUMMARY
18 This bill establishes a minimum hourly wage for school support staff in an amount 
19 equal to 125% of the state minimum hourly wage beginning in the school year starting after 
20 June 30, 2024. If the state minimum hourly wage is increased on January 1st of any year, 
21 the minimum hourly wage for school support staff is increased to 125% of that increased 
22 state minimum hourly wage beginning July 1st of the same year. Under this bill, "school 
23 support staff" means all public school employees who are not school administrators and 
24 are not certified teachers or career and technical education teachers subject to minimum 
25 salary requirements under statute.18
